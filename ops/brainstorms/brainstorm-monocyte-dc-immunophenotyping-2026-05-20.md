## Category: Novel Hypotheses

### Idea 1: CCR7 Trafficking Defect as Central ME/CFS Pathophysiology Driver
- **Type:** hypothesis
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Petrov shows impaired CCR7 on monocytes and DCs in ME/CFS (vs. Long COVID). CCR7 is essential for lymph node homing and immune surveillance. Defective trafficking could explain: (1) reduced DC frequency in blood (sequestered in tissues), (2) poor antigen presentation to T cells, (3) impaired immune coordination (Petrov: ME/CFS correlation networks less integrated), (4) psychiatric symptom link (neuro-immune axis disruption via failed perivascular trafficking). TRPM3 dysfunction in ME/CFS (existing hypothesis) could impair calcium signaling needed for CCR7-mediated migration.
- **Evidence link:** Petrov: ME/CFS shows impaired CCR7 on monocytes and DCs; reduced DC frequency; less coordinated correlation networks; inverse HLA-DR ↔ psychiatric symptoms. Existing: TRPM3 calcium channel hypothesis; neuro-immune axis evidence in ME/CFS.
- **Certainty:** 0.45
- **Falsifiable prediction:** CCR7-expressing monocytes in ME/CFS will show: (a) impaired chemotaxis to CCL19/CCL21 in transwell assays, (b) reduced lymph node homing in PET imaging, (c) restoration of CCR7 signaling via calcium modulators will improve trafficking in ex vivo assays. ME/CFS patients with CCR7 defects will have higher viral reservoir loads (EBV/HHV-6) due to failed surveillance.
- **Tier:** 1

### Idea 2: CD80 Suppression as Primary Adaptive Tolerance Mechanism in ME/CFS
- **Type:** hypothesis
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Petrov: ME/CFS shows reduced CD80 on M1-like monocytes (opposite to Long COVID's CD80 elevation). CD80 provides costimulatory signal for T cell activation via CD28. Suppression could be: (1) adaptive to prevent chronic hyperactivation (initial trigger: viral infection), (2) driven by PD-1/PD-L1 checkpoint exhaustion (existing ME/CFS finding), (3) maintained by IL-10 elevation from M2 skewing. This creates tolerance trap: low CD80 → poor priming → persistent infections → continued antigen load → sustained tolerance. Complement dysregulation in ME/CFS (existing) may amplify via C3a/C5a receptor cross-talk with CD80 pathway.
- **Evidence link:** Petrov: ME/CFS shows reduced CD80 on M1-like monocytes; Long COVID shows CD80 elevation (hyperactivation). Existing: PD-1/PD-L1 exhaustion in ME/CFS; complement system dysfunction; persistent viral reservoir hypothesis.
- **Certainty:** 0.50
- **Falsifiable prediction:** (a) ME/CFS monocytes will show elevated PD-L1 correlating inversely with CD80, (b) blockade of IL-10 signaling will restore CD80 expression ex vivo, (c) CD80 agonists will reverse T cell hyporesponsiveness in ME/CFS PBMC co-cultures. Longitudinal: acute infection patients who develop ME/CFS will show progressive CD80 loss during 3-6 month window.
- **Tier:** 1
