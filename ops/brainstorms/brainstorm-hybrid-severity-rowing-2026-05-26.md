## TOPIC C1: Rowing + Isometric Yoga Hybrid Protocol

### 1. Novel Hypotheses

**C1-H1: Parasympathetic "washout" window is truncated in ME/CFS**

- **Mechanistic rationale**: Healthy athletes show 8-10 min post-isometric parasympathetic dominance via HRV rebound; ME/CFS patients may have truncated recovery windows (2-4 min) due to autonomic rigidity, suggesting current 5-min isometric block may be too long relative to parasympathetic capacity.
- **Evidence link**: Studies showing blunted HRV recovery after exertion in ME/CFS; autonomic dysfunction literature on vagal withdrawal thresholds.
- **Preliminary certainty**: 0.35
- **Falsifiable prediction**: ME/CFS patients will show earlier reversion to sympathetic dominance during the 5-min isometric segment (measured by HRV LF/HF ratio crossing baseline) than matched healthy controls; truncating isometric block to 3 min will improve post-session PEM scores.
- **Tier**: T3 (Mechanistic speculation, testable)

**C1-H2: Metabolite redistribution is muscle-group selective, not global**

- **Mechanistic rationale**: Isometric holds in upper-body yoga (e.g., plank, downward dog) may not redistribute lower-body metabolites from rowing; active recovery benefit requires antagonistic muscle groups sharing metabolic pathways (e.g., arms vs legs both glycolytic but distinct pools).
- **Evidence link**: Metabolite compartmentalization studies; muscle-specific glycogen utilization patterns.
- **Preliminary certainty**: 0.30
- **Falsifiable prediction**: MRI spectroscopy will show elevated lower-leg intracellular lactate during isometric upper-body holds unchanged from rowing alone; only lower-body isometrics (e.g., wall sit) will reduce metabolite accumulation.
- **Tier**: T3 (Mechanistic speculation, testable)

**C1-H3: Breathing-mediated parasympathetic activation overrides metabolite-clearance benefit**

- **Mechanistic rationale**: Slow breathing (4-6 bpm) during isometrics may induce parasympathetic shift but reduce metabolic clearance by decreasing local blood flow (flow-metabolism coupling decoupling in ME/CFS); faster breathing with "bellows" mechanics may better flush metabolites.
- **Evidence link**: Respiratory pump effects on lymphatic drainage; exercise physiology on breathing rate and venous return.
- **Preliminary certainty**: 0.25
- **Falsifiable prediction**: Participants using faster breathing (12-15 bpm) during isometrics will report better PEM outcomes than slow-breathing groups, despite lower HRV parasympathetic metrics.
- **Tier**: T3 (Mechanistic speculation, testable)

## TOPIC C3: Severity-Stratified Rowing Protocol

### 1. Novel Hypotheses

**C3-H1: Severity-to-rowing-tolerance relationship is power-law, not linear**

- **Mechanistic rationale**: Energy envelope collapse in severe ME/CFS may be non-linear; 10% severity reduction may correspond to 50% rowing tolerance reduction at severe end vs 10% at mild end.
- **Evidence link**: Non-linear energy dynamics in ME/CFS; severity stratification studies.
- **Preliminary certainty**: 0.40
- **Falsifiable prediction**: Log-log plot of severity vs rowing tolerance will show power-law relationship with exponent >1 (severity amplification at low tolerance).
- **Tier**: T3 (Mechanistic speculation, testable)

**C3-H2: PEM history modulates progression speed more than baseline severity**

- **Mechanistic rationale**: Patients with frequent PEM may have "sensitized" energy envelope requiring slower progression regardless of baseline severity category.
- **Evidence link**: PEM sensitization theory; kindling model.
- **Preliminary certainty**: 0.35
- **Falsifiable prediction**: PEM frequency will predict progression speed independent of baseline severity; adding PEM-frequency bins to stratification will improve outcomes.
- **Tier**: T3 (Mechanistic speculation, testable)

**C3-H3: Missing intermediate categories: mild-moderate vs moderate-severe**
