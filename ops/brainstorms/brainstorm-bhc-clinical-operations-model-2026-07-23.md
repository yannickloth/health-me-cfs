# Brainstorm: BHC Clinical Operations Model as Integration Topic

**Source:** https://batemanhornecenter.org/ — Bateman Horne Center website comprehensive review
**Date:** 2026-07-23
**Gate source:** B — user-initiated exploratory scan, not scope-escalation

## Core Insight

BHC operates the most prominent US Center of Excellence for ME/CFS clinical care. Its operational model — membership fees, PCP partnership requirements, opioid policy, extended visit protocols, and patient engagement Terms of Engagement — represents a *care-delivery structure* topic distinct from the pharmacology and mechanism content already covered in the paper.

## What's Already in the Paper

- All BHC-authored research papers are already cited (RESTORE-ME, MCAM, NASA Lean Test, RECOVER, butyrate/microbiome, AI multi-omics, orthostatic hemodynamics, CPET publications)
- NASA Lean Test is used throughout (ch04, ch10, ch21, ch24, ch31, ch36, ch37)
- Oxaloacetate is deeply integrated (ch24, ch30, ch40, appendix I)
- CPET evidence is extensively covered including Mancini 2026 null replication
- van Campen CBF data already cited (91% CBF decline finding)
- "It's Not POTS" blog thesis (CBF deficits without POTS criteria) already covered by `@hyp:cbf-unifying-hub`

## Gap Identified

No section addresses *how clinical care is delivered* at the organizational level:
1. **Membership-based clinic model** — annual fee, comprehensive initial exams, PCP maintenance requirement
2. **Opioid policy** — explicit refusal to manage chronic daily opioids
3. **Patient engagement structure** — Terms of Engagement, athenahealth portal, pre-visit questionnaires, communication cards
4. **Care coordination model** — PCP + specialist division of labor
5. **Provider education infrastructure** — Project ECHO, medical education slides, CME offerings
6. **EMR customization** — athenahealth adapted for ME/CFS-specific fields (Good Day/Bad Day, HUA tracking)

## Assessment

**Priority:** Low. This is a healthcare-delivery topic, not a pathophysiological or pharmacological topic. The paper is a biomedical monograph, not a health-services research paper.

**Counter-argument for inclusion:** A section on "how expert centers structure care" could serve as a template for clinicians setting up ME/CFS services — actionable clinical guidance that complements the mechanism and treatment content. The BHC model (membership + PCP + specialist + ECHO education) is the closest thing to a proven care-delivery architecture for ME/CFS in the US.

**Recommendation:** Queue as low-priority. Integrate only if ch28 (integrative approaches) or a new appendix on care-delivery models is expanded to include organizational-level interventions.
