# Brainstorm: Fatigue Without Peripheral Inflammation — Omdal et al. 2026

**Source:** Omdal R, et al. Persistent fatigue in long-COVID is not associated with peripheral inflammatory or cellular stress biomarkers. *Brain Behav Immun Health*. 2026;54:101226. PMID 42004496.
**Date:** 2026-06-01
**Topic slug:** fatigue-no-inflammation-omdal-2026

## Study Summary

n=96, 48 Long COVID (NICE criteria) vs 48 age/sex-matched recovered controls. Severe fatigue (fVAS 63 vs 5, FACIT-F 21.5 vs 49) with zero peripheral biomarker differences: CRP, TNF-α, IL-6, HSP90α, Serpin F1, hemopexine, APOA4 all normal. ANA 10.4%, no correlation with fatigue. Conclusion: LC fatigue not sustained by systemic peripheral inflammation.

## Ideas Generated

### Category 1: Novel Hypotheses

1. **CNS-Confined Neuroinflammation Fatigue Hypothesis** (cert 0.55)
   Fatigue in LC/ME/CFS is driven by CNS-confined neuroinflammation (microglial activation, complement deposition, glymphatic dysfunction) that does not spill into peripheral circulation. The Omdal null is positive evidence for CNS compartmentalisation — not evidence against inflammation. Falsifiable prediction: CSF IL-1β, TNF-α, quinolinic acid, or complement proteins (C4d, sC5b-9) will be elevated in LC/ME/CFS despite normal peripheral cytokines. If CSF is also normal → CNS-confined neuroinflammation falsified.

2. **Epigenetic Fatigue Persistence Hypothesis** (cert 0.35)
   Fatigue persists because microglial and neuronal epigenetic marks (H3K4me1, DNA methylation at inflammatory loci) maintain a "fatigue setpoint" after the original inflammatory trigger resolves. The Mobius methylation model (97% accuracy distinguishing ME/CFS from controls) provides indirect support. Falsifiable prediction: DNA methylation signatures at inflammatory gene loci in PBMCs or sorted monocytes will correlate with fatigue severity independently of cytokine levels.

3. **Mitochondrial-Derived Danger Signal Hypothesis** (cert 0.40)
   Impaired mitophagy releases mtDNA/DAMPs into cytoplasm, activating cGAS-STING in microglia → trapped CNS inflammation. Peripherally, mtDNA is cleared by liver/macrophages; centrally, the BBB prevents clearance. The ccf-mtDNA–cognition correlation (Matits 2026) and the dissociation from CRP support mitochondrial, not inflammatory, causation. Falsifiable prediction: CSF mtDNA and ccf-mtDNA levels will be elevated in ME/CFS and correlate with fatigue severity, while plasma mtDNA and cytokines will not.

### Category 2: Research Directions

4. **Paired CSF-Plasma Cytokine Study in ME/CFS** (n/a)
   Direct test of CNS compartmentalisation: measure cytokines in simultaneously drawn CSF and plasma from ME/CFS patients. If CSF cytokines elevated while plasma normal → compartmentalisation confirmed. If both normal → fatigue mechanism is non-inflammatory.

5. **TSPO PET with BBB permeability control** (n/a)
   Re-run TSPO PET in ME/CFS using second-generation tracers + concurrent BBB permeability measurement (DCE-MRI). The Barzon 2026 finding that peripheral inflammation reduces TSPO tracer brain influx means previous null TSPO results cannot be interpreted without BBB control.

### Category 3: Mathematical Model Extensions

6. **Two-Compartment Fatigue ODE** (cert n/a)
   Add a CNS inflammation compartment to the existing energy model: CNS inflammatory signal → fatigue output; peripheral inflammation → CNS signal (via vagus, CVOs, BBB transport); CNS signal persists after peripheral signal normalizes (hysteresis from epigenetic/microglial memory). Parameterize from Omdal data: peripheral signal amplitude = 0 (null), CNS signal amplitude = fatigue severity.

### Category 4: Cross-Disease Bridges

7. **Sickness Behavior Model** (cert 0.50)
   Omdal's null directly supports the "locked sickness behavior" model: the hypothalamic sickness behavior program, once triggered, maintains fatigue via CNS circuits independently of ongoing peripheral immune activation. This parallels cancer-related fatigue (CRF), where fatigue persists after tumour resection and cytokine normalization. The CRF parallel provides a therapeutic framework: CNS-penetrant anti-inflammatories (minocycline, LDN) might work where peripheral anti-inflammatories don't.

### Category 5: Clinical/Differential Diagnosis

8. **The "Normal CRP Trap" as Diagnostic Feature** (cert 0.60)
   Omdal's finding that CRP is normal despite severe fatigue is not a diagnostic exclusion — it is characteristic of ME/CFS and LC. This should be explicitly stated to prevent clinicians from ruling out these diagnoses based on normal inflammatory markers. "Normal CRP with disabling fatigue" is itself a diagnostic signal.
