## CATEGORY 1: Novel Hypotheses (Mechanistic Connections)

### 1. Extrafollicular B Cell Sanctuary Hypothesis
**Mechanistic rationale:** Tatai's finding of IgM dominance (60% vs 36% in controls) with failed class switching indicates ongoing extrafollicular activation. ME/CFS may involve tissue-resident extrafollicular plasmablasts in bone marrow, gut-associated lymphoid tissue, or vascular adventitia that are protected from conventional immunoadsorption and daratumumab, explaining variable treatment responses. The polyreactive pattern (up to 8 bands) supports polyclonal extrafollicular activation rather than germinal center-mediated affinity maturation.

**Evidence link:** IgM dominance is hallmark of extrafollicular responses; tissue-resident plasma cells known sanctuary sites in autoimmune disease; failed immunoadsorption in subset suggests protected antibody sources.

**Certainty:** 0.6

**Falsifiable prediction:** Single-cell RNA-seq of bone marrow aspirates from ME/CFS patients will show enrichment of CD11c+ T-bet+ extrafollicular plasmablasts with tissue-residency markers (CD69, CXCR6) that correlate with autoantibody titers but not with peripheral B cell depletion markers.

### 2. Tissue-Specific Autoantibody Compartmentalization Hypothesis
**Mechanistic rationale:** Tatai found tissue-specific autoantibodies (cardiac 54%, vascular 34%, pulmonary 34%) in long COVID, undetectable by ANA HEp-2. ME/CFS symptoms may reflect compartmentalized autoantibody targeting of specific tissue microvascular beds (cerebral microvasculature, skeletal muscle microvasculature) that are functionally critical but antigenically distinct from systemic organs, explaining the diagnostic gap with conventional autoimmune panels.

**Evidence link:** ANA negativity despite tissue-specific reactivity; symptom heterogeneity suggests organ-specific targeting; neurovascular manifestations in long COVID parallel ME/CFS orthostatic intolerance and cognitive dysfunction.

**Certainty:** 0.55

**Falsifiable prediction:** Immunohistochemistry with ME/CFS patient IgG/IgM on human tissue microarrays will show binding patterns to microvascular endothelial cells in brain white matter, skeletal muscle, and gastrointestinal mucosa that correlate with individual symptom profiles but not with ANA status.

### 3. IgM-Mediated Endothelial Glycocalyx Disruption Hypothesis
**Mechanistic rationale:** Tatai's IgM dominance (failed class switching) may indicate pathogenic IgM autoantibodies targeting the endothelial glycocalyx (heparan sulfate, hyaluronic acid) rather than classical intracellular targets. IgM can efficiently activate complement via the classical pathway due to pentameric structure, causing localized glycocalyx shedding, increased vascular permeability, and impaired microvascular flow regulation—key features of ME/CFS pathophysiology.

**Evidence link:** Complement activation observed in ME/CFS; glycocalyx degradation markers (syndecan-1, hyaluronan) elevated in long COVID; IgM potent complement activator; CRP only marginally elevated in seropositive individuals suggests localized rather than systemic inflammation.

**Certainty:** 0.45

**Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS patients with IgM-dominant autoantibody profiles will have elevated plasma syndecan-1 and hyaluronan fragments, and their IgM will bind to purified glycocalyx components in ELISA, correlating with orthostatic intolerance severity and cerebral blood flow dysregulation.

### 4. Viral Antigen Molecular Mimicry-GPCR Hybrid Hypothesis
**Mechanistic rationale:** Tatai's tissue-specific autoantibodies may arise from molecular mimicry between SARS-CoV-2 epitopes and GPCR domains, explaining overlap between tissue-specific reactivity and GPCR autoantibody findings in ME/CFS literature. SARS-CoV-2 spike protein shares sequence similarity with β2-adrenergic receptor extracellular loops; initial tissue-specific responses may evolve toward GPCR cross-reactivity through epitope spreading, creating hybrid autoantibody populations.

**Evidence link:** Molecular mimicry established in post-viral autoimmunity; GPCR autoantibodies documented in both ME/CFS and long COVID; Tatai's polyreactive pattern supports epitope spreading; Wallukat 2021 documented GPCR autoantibodies in long COVID.

**Certainty:** 0.5

**Falsifiable prediction:** Peptide array screening with SARS-CoV-2 spike protein fragments and GPCR extracellular domains will identify cross-reactive IgM/IgG antibodies that bind both viral epitopes and GPCR targets, with cross-reactivity frequency higher in ME/CFS patients than controls.

### 5. B Cell Receptor (BCR) Intrinsic Signaling Hypothesis
**Mechanistic rationale:** Tatai's finding of persistent IgM and emergence of new autoantibodies over 141 days suggests ongoing BCR activation. ME/CFS may involve intrinsic BCR hyperresponsiveness due to gain-of-function mutations in BCR signaling pathway components (BLNK, SYK, BTK) or impaired negative regulation (CD22, FcγRIIB), lowering activation thresholds for autoreactive B cells and enabling continuous extrafollicular activation independent of T cell help.

**Evidence link:** Autoantibody persistence without antigen suggests intrinsic B cell dysregulation; similar mechanisms in other autoimmune diseases; failed class switching indicates T cell-independent activation; chronicity of ME/CFS fits self-sustaining B cell activation model.

**Certainty:** 0.4

**Falsifiable prediction:** Phosphoflow cytometry of ME/CFS patient B cells stimulated with anti-IgM will show enhanced and prolonged phosphorylation of SYK, BTK, and PLCγ2 compared to controls, correlating with autoantibody titers and disease duration.

## CATEGORY 2: Research Directions (Experiments to Resolve Uncertainties)

**Mechanistic rationale:** Tatai demonstrated significant diagnostic gap: tissue-specific autoantibodies NOT detected by ANA HEp-2. ME/CFS autoantibodies likely target non-nuclear, conformational, or membrane-bound antigens missed by conventional autoimmune panels. Systematic antigen discovery using protein arrays, phage display libraries, and immunoprecipitation-mass spectrometry is needed to identify novel targets.

**Evidence link:** Tatai's ANA-negative autoantibodies; Germain 2025 null finding may reflect wrong target antigens; GPCR antibodies validated by Freitag/Sotzny but orthogonal platforms disagree—suggests additional targets exist.

**Certainty:** 0.7

**Falsifiable prediction:** Proteomic antigen discovery using ME/CFS patient IgG/IgM immunoprecipitation from cardiac, vascular, and brain tissue lysates will identify 5+ novel autoantigen targets not covered by conventional autoimmune panels, with at least 2 targets reproducible across independent cohorts.

**Certainty:** 0.65

**Falsifiable prediction:** Single-cell multi-omics will reveal expanded CD11c+ T-bet+ extrafollicular plasmablasts with expanded clones and shared clonotypes between blood and tissue compartments, with epigenetic signatures of sustained activation correlating with disease severity.

## CATEGORY 3: Drug/Medication Ideas

**Evidence link:** BTK inhibitors reduce autoantibodies in rheumatoid arthritis and lupus; BCR signaling upregulated in extrafollicular B cells; IgM production dependent on BCR signaling; preclinical data supports BTK inhibition in autoimmunity.

**Certainty:** 0.5

**Falsifiable prediction:** In an open-label trial (n=20), acalabrutinib 100mg daily for 12 weeks will reduce IgM autoantibody titers by ≥30% and improve ME/CFS symptom scores (SF-36 physical component) by ≥15 points in 50% of patients with IgM-dominant profiles, with minimal toxicity.

**Certainty:** 0.45

**Certainty:** 0.55
