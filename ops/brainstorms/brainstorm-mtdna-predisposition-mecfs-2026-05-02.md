## 1. Novel Mechanistic Hypotheses

### B-1 — Heteroplasmy Threshold Sex Asymmetry
**Category:** Mechanism
**Rationale:** ME/CFS is ~3–4× more prevalent in females. Mitochondrial bottleneck during oogenesis fixes heteroplasmy ratios in offspring; female-biased X-linked nuclear-encoded mitochondrial proteins (e.g., NDUFA1, NDUFB11) interact with mtDNA-encoded subunits. A heteroplasmy load tolerated in males may exceed the symptom-modulation threshold in females due to additional X-linked nuclear-mitochondrial mismatch.
**Evidence link:** BR16 (heteroplasmy measured but low overall); V19 (population structure of variants)
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable prediction:** In a mixed-sex ME/CFS cohort with deep-coverage mtDNA sequencing (>1000×), female patients will show higher heteroplasmy variance at OXPHOS-relevant sites than male patients matched for severity.
**Tier:** 2

### B-2 — Purifying Selection Survivorship Bias
**Category:** Mechanism
**Rationale:** V19's anomaly (ME/CFS patients carry FEWER mildly deleterious mtDNA variants) may reflect survivorship: individuals with both high mtDNA variant load AND ME/CFS-triggering exposure die or develop overt mitochondrial disease before ME/CFS diagnosis. ME/CFS is then enriched for the "intermediate vulnerability" stratum — enough reserve to survive, not enough to recover from a Hit 2.
**Evidence link:** V19 (the anomaly); N14 (CDR reserve framing)
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable prediction:** A pediatric ME/CFS cohort sequenced for mtDNA will NOT show the "fewer deleterious variants" pattern, because survivorship filtering has not yet operated.
**Tier:** 1

### B-3 — Nuclear-Mitochondrial Mismatch Predisposition
**Category:** Mechanism
**Rationale:** Onset-null mtDNA results may obscure a real signal: the predisposition is not in mtDNA alone but in haplogroup × nuclear-genome compatibility. Recent admixture (e.g., European nuclear background + non-European mtDNA via maternal lineage) may reduce OXPHOS efficiency without any individual variant being deleterious.
**Evidence link:** BR16, V19 (both report only mtDNA, not nuclear-mtDNA interaction); A11 (heritability includes nuclear)
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS prevalence will be elevated in populations with high recent maternal-paternal continental admixture compared to populations with stable haplogroup-nuclear pairing, after adjusting for socioeconomic confounders.
**Tier:** 2

### B-4 — Haplogroup H "Exercise-Death" Phenotype as PEM Substrate
**Category:** Mechanism
**Rationale:** BR16 found haplogroup H associated with increased "feeling dead after exercise." Haplogroup H couples OXPHOS more tightly (less proton leak via UCPs) — efficient at rest but with narrower reserve when ATP demand spikes. PEM may be the clinical manifestation of a haplogroup-H-style coupling profile combined with acquired complex dysfunction.
**Evidence link:** BR16 (H-severity link); N14 (CDR exhaustion)
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable prediction:** In two-day CPET protocols, haplogroup H ME/CFS patients will show greater day-2 VO2max decrement than haplogroup U or J patients matched for baseline severity.
**Tier:** 1

### B-5 — Dopaminergic Demand Crossover Hypothesis
**Category:** Mechanism
**Rationale:** ADHD reflects PFC dopaminergic circuits failing under tonic demand; ME/CFS PEM reflects systemic energy circuits failing under phasic demand. Same low-reserve substrate, two failure modes determined by which circuit reaches its ceiling first. Predicts a developmental sequence: ADHD-like symptoms in childhood → ME/CFS susceptibility in adulthood after immune Hit 2.
**Evidence link:** C20, Ve16, G24 (ADHD mitochondrial); BR16 (severity modulation)
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable prediction:** Adult ME/CFS cohorts will show higher rates of childhood ADHD diagnosis or subclinical attention symptoms than matched controls, with the elevation concentrated in haplogroups OTHER than U/K (which are ADHD-protective).
**Tier:** 1

### B-6 — Haplogroup U Anti-Inflammatory Coupling
**Category:** Mechanism
**Rationale:** Haplogroup U's protective effect across ADHD (C20) and milder bloating in ME/CFS (BR16) may share an upstream mechanism: U-associated coupling efficiency reduces ROS leak at Complex I/III, dampening NLRP3 inflammasome activation. Mitochondrial ROS is the dominant DAMP for sterile inflammation.
**Evidence link:** C20, BR16
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable prediction:** PBMC ROS production under LPS challenge will be lower in haplogroup U carriers than in matched non-U controls, in both healthy donors and ME/CFS patients.
**Tier:** 2

### B-7 — Mitochondrial Unfolded Protein Response (UPRmt) Set-Point Inheritance
**Category:** Mechanism
**Rationale:** Inherited haplogroup variants may not affect baseline OXPHOS but may shift the UPRmt activation threshold. Lower threshold = chronic low-grade UPRmt = metabolic rewiring toward glycolysis = the lactate/pyruvate shift seen in ME/CFS. Onset-null but severity-modulating fits this model.
**Evidence link:** BR16, V19 (severity not onset); N14 (CDR includes mitohormesis)
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable prediction:** ATF5 target gene expression (HSP60, LONP1, CLPP) in ME/CFS PBMCs will correlate with haplogroup classification independent of disease severity scores.
**Tier:** 2

## 2. Research Directions

### B-8 — Trio mtDNA Sequencing in ME/CFS Families
**Category:** Research
**Rationale:** A11's RR=2.70 leaves the maternal-vs-paternal contribution unmeasured. Sequencing affected proband + mother + father in 100 families would partition heritability between mtDNA (maternal-only) and nuclear (biparental).
**Evidence link:** A11; OQ1 (already an open question — this is the experimental design to resolve it)
**Certainty:** N/A (study design)
**Falsifiable prediction:** If mtDNA contributes meaningfully to predisposition, maternal-side relatives of probands will show higher ME/CFS prevalence than paternal-side relatives at p<0.05 with n=100 trios.
**Tier:** 1

### B-9 — Long-Read mtDNA + Heteroplasmy Quantification
**Category:** Research
**Rationale:** Existing studies (BR16, V19) used short-read NGS that under-resolves low-frequency heteroplasmy and structural variants. Nanopore/PacBio with duplex sequencing detects heteroplasmy down to ~0.1%. May reveal the missed signal explaining onset.
**Evidence link:** BR16, V19, D20 (all short-read)
**Certainty:** N/A (study design)
**Falsifiable prediction:** Replication of BR16/V19 with long-read duplex sequencing on n=200 ME/CFS will identify at least one mtDNA structural variant or low-heteroplasmy site (≥1% AF) significantly enriched in cases.
**Tier:** 1

### B-10 — Tissue-Specific mtDNA Profiling
**Category:** Research
**Rationale:** Blood mtDNA poorly reflects muscle/brain mtDNA due to tissue-specific heteroplasmy drift. Muscle biopsy mtDNA from ME/CFS patients (paired with blood) would test whether tissue-restricted heteroplasmy explains the blood-based null findings.
**Evidence link:** BR16, V19 (blood only)
**Certainty:** N/A (study design)
**Falsifiable prediction:** Vastus lateralis mtDNA heteroplasmy in ME/CFS patients will exceed blood heteroplasmy by >2× at OXPHOS sites, with the difference larger than in controls.
**Tier:** 2

### B-11 — Reserve Capacity Phenotyping Battery
**Category:** Research / Biomarker
**Rationale:** "Mitochondrial reserve" is invoked but not measured. A standardized battery — Seahorse spare respiratory capacity in PBMCs + 31P-MRS PCr recovery + 2-day CPET — would operationalize the concept and enable haplogroup × reserve correlation.
**Evidence link:** N14 (CDR), BR16 (severity)
**Certainty:** N/A (study design)
**Falsifiable prediction:** A composite reserve score will correlate with ME/CFS severity (SF-36 PF) at r>0.4 and segregate by haplogroup (J/U vs H) within severity strata.
**Tier:** 1

## 3. Drug / Medication Ideas

### B-12 — Bezafibrate for PPAR-Mediated Reserve Expansion
**Category:** Drug (repurposed)
**Rationale:** Bezafibrate (PPAR-α/γ/δ agonist) increases mitochondrial biogenesis via PGC-1α and improves OXPHOS in primary mitochondrial disease trials. If ME/CFS reserve deficit is the rate-limiting variable, bezafibrate could raise the floor below which Hit 2 triggers symptom expression.
**Evidence link:** N14 (CDR reserve); BR16 (severity modulation)
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable prediction:** A 12-week bezafibrate trial in moderate ME/CFS will improve day-2 CPET VO2max by ≥10% vs placebo in haplogroup H subgroup.
**Tier:** 2

### B-13 — Dichloroacetate (DCA) for PDH Reactivation
**Category:** Drug (repurposed)
**Rationale:** ME/CFS shows pyruvate dehydrogenase suppression and elevated lactate. DCA inhibits PDK, reactivating PDH and restoring pyruvate entry into TCA. Haplogroup-stratified response expected: greatest benefit where coupling is intact but PDH is the bottleneck.
**Evidence link:** N14 (metabolic shift in CDR)
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Falsifiable prediction:** DCA at 12.5 mg/kg/day for 8 weeks will reduce post-exertional blood lactate AUC by ≥20% in ME/CFS patients without haplogroup H "tight coupling" phenotype.
**Tier:** 3

### B-14 — Methylene Blue as Alternative Electron Carrier
**Category:** Drug (repurposed)
**Rationale:** Methylene blue accepts electrons from NADH and donates to cytochrome c, bypassing Complex I/III blockade. If ME/CFS dysfunction is concentrated upstream of cytochrome c, low-dose MB could restore electron flux without targeting any specific haplogroup-defined complex.
**Evidence link:** Ve16, G24 (Complex I impairment in ADHD — same target tissue logic)
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Falsifiable prediction:** Low-dose methylene blue (0.5–4 mg/kg) will improve PBMC spare respiratory capacity by ≥15% in ME/CFS patients with documented Complex I deficit.
**Tier:** 3

### B-15 — Atomovetine/Modafinil Stratified by ADHD Comorbidity
**Category:** Drug (repurposed) / Stratification
**Rationale:** ME/CFS patients with comorbid ADHD may share the dopaminergic-PFC-energy bottleneck (B-5). Stimulants targeting this circuit (atomoxetine, modafinil) may help the cognitive/initiation symptoms specifically in this subgroup, without addressing PEM.
**Evidence link:** C20, Ve16, G24
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS patients with childhood ADHD history will report ≥30% greater cognitive symptom improvement on modafinil than ME/CFS patients without ADHD history, controlling for severity.
**Tier:** 2

## 4. Supplements / Nutraceuticals

### B-16 — Urolithin A for Mitophagy Activation
**Category:** Supplement
**Rationale:** Damaged mitochondria accumulate when mitophagy is impaired; chronic UPRmt (B-7) suggests this. Urolithin A (gut microbiome metabolite of ellagitannins) selectively induces mitophagy and improves muscle mitochondrial function in older adults. Predisposition + clearance defect = compounding reserve loss.
**Evidence link:** N14 (CDR mitochondrial turnover)
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable prediction:** 500 mg/day urolithin A for 16 weeks will improve PBMC mitochondrial membrane potential and reduce mtDNA copy number variance in moderate ME/CFS.
**Tier:** 2

### B-17 — MitoQ for Targeted Antioxidant Delivery to Inner Membrane
**Category:** Supplement
**Rationale:** Untargeted antioxidants (vitamin E, C) fail in ME/CFS trials. MitoQ (ubiquinol-TPP+ conjugate) accumulates 1000× in mitochondrial matrix, scavenging ROS at the source. Haplogroup-stratified expectation: greatest benefit in non-U haplogroups (more baseline ROS leak).
**Evidence link:** B-6 (U coupling efficiency); N14
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable prediction:** 20 mg/day MitoQ for 12 weeks will reduce urinary 8-OHdG by ≥25% in non-U-haplogroup ME/CFS patients but not in U-haplogroup carriers.
**Tier:** 2

### B-18 — Nicotinamide Riboside + Pterostilbene Stack
**Category:** Supplement
**Rationale:** NAD+ depletion is a documented ME/CFS feature. NR raises NAD+; pterostilbene activates SIRT1/3, which deacetylate mitochondrial proteins to improve OXPHOS efficiency. Combination targets both substrate pool and regulatory enzymes.
**Evidence link:** N14 (CDR redox); BR16 (severity modulation as compensation target)
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable prediction:** NR 300 mg + pterostilbene 50 mg daily for 12 weeks will raise whole-blood NAD+ by ≥40% and improve PROMIS fatigue scores by ≥0.5 SD in mild-moderate ME/CFS.
**Tier:** 2

### B-19 — Magnesium Threonate for Brain-Specific Bioavailability
**Category:** Supplement
**Rationale:** Mg2+ is required for ATP synthase function and crosses BBB poorly in standard forms. Magnesium threonate raises CSF Mg2+ and may specifically aid the dopaminergic-PFC bottleneck shared by ADHD-ME/CFS comorbidity (B-5).
**Evidence link:** Ve16, G24, C20
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Falsifiable prediction:** Magnesium threonate 2 g/day for 8 weeks will improve cognitive symptom scores in ME/CFS-ADHD comorbid patients more than equivalent elemental Mg as glycinate.
**Tier:** 3

### B-20 — Methylene-Blue-Sparing Riboflavin Loading
**Category:** Supplement
**Rationale:** Riboflavin is FAD precursor for Complex II and ETF dehydrogenase. High-dose riboflavin (400 mg) is established for migraine prevention via mitochondrial mechanism; ME/CFS shares migraine comorbidity. Cheap, safe, broadly accessible — important for severity-accessibility (B-23).
**Evidence link:** Ve16, G24 (Complex II/ETF in ADHD); N14
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable prediction:** Riboflavin 400 mg/day for 12 weeks will reduce monthly migraine days AND PEM frequency in ME/CFS by ≥30% vs placebo.
**Tier:** 1

## 5. Non-Pharmacological Interventions

### B-21 — Cold Exposure for Mitochondrial Biogenesis Without Exertion
**Category:** Non-pharma
**Rationale:** Cold activates UCP1 in BAT and induces PGC-1α-mediated biogenesis WITHOUT the exertion that triggers PEM. Brief cold-water face immersion (mammalian dive reflex) is feasible even for severe patients. Hypothesized to expand reserve without triggering Hit 2.
**Evidence link:** N14 (CDR); B-12 (PGC-1α target)
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Falsifiable prediction:** 4 weeks of daily 30-second cold-water face immersion will increase resting metabolic rate by ≥3% and HRV (RMSSD) by ≥10% in moderate ME/CFS patients.
**Tier:** 3

### B-22 — Heat Acclimation for Mitochondrial Hormesis
**Category:** Non-pharma
**Rationale:** Passive heat (sauna, warm bath) induces HSP60/70 and UPRmt in a controlled, exertion-free manner. Sub-symptom-threshold dosing tests the mitohormesis arm of N14's CDR model directly.
**Evidence link:** N14; B-7 (UPRmt)
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable prediction:** 8 weeks of 4×/week passive heat exposure (38°C bath, 20 min) will improve 6-minute walk distance in moderate ME/CFS without increasing PEM frequency.
**Tier:** 2

## 6. Combinations + Access

### B-23 — Severity-Tiered Reserve Protocol
**Category:** Protocol
**Rationale:** A single protocol is inaccessible to severe patients. Tier 1 (severe, bedbound): riboflavin + magnesium + horizontal heat. Tier 2 (moderate): add NR/pterostilbene + urolithin A + cold face immersion. Tier 3 (mild): add bezafibrate + structured pacing. Each tier additive when tolerated; mechanistic continuity preserved.
**Evidence link:** B-12, B-16, B-18, B-20, B-21, B-22
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable prediction:** Tier-matched protocol assignment will produce non-inferior outcomes vs expert-individualized treatment in a pragmatic n=120 trial across severity strata.
**Tier:** 1

### B-24 — Haplogroup-Guided Supplement Stack
**Category:** Protocol
**Rationale:** BR16's haplogroup-symptom links suggest haplogroup-tailored supplementation: H (tight coupling, exercise crashes) → MitoQ + uncoupler-sparing pacing; U (anti-inflammatory) → maintain coupling, target other deficits; J (joint protection, possibly antioxidant baseline) → minimal antioxidant addition. Cheap 23andMe-derived haplogroup makes this accessible.
**Evidence link:** BR16; B-4, B-6, B-17
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Falsifiable prediction:** Haplogroup-matched supplement assignment will outperform random-assignment of the same supplements in symptom-specific outcomes (joint pain in J, exercise-crash in H) at 16 weeks.
**Tier:** 2

## 7. Mathematical Model Extensions

### B-25 — Reserve-Threshold Variable in CDR ODE
**Category:** Modeling
**Rationale:** N14's CDR is qualitative. Add a state variable R(t) = mitochondrial reserve, with dR/dt = biogenesis(supply) − damage(stress) − age-decay. Disease onset = R drops below R_crit; haplogroup sets initial R(0) and damage-coefficient k. Predicts onset age distribution as a function of haplogroup and stress trajectory.
**Evidence link:** N14; BR16, V19, A11
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable prediction:** A fitted ODE will reproduce the bimodal ME/CFS onset age distribution (adolescent peak + 30s peak) using only haplogroup-stratified R(0) and population stressor frequency.
**Tier:** 1

### B-26 — Two-Hit DAG Extension with Haplogroup Modifier Edge
**Category:** Modeling
**Rationale:** Current DAGs treat predisposition as monolithic. Add haplogroup as a node with edges to (a) baseline reserve, (b) symptom-severity given onset, (c) ADHD comorbidity probability. Allows formal mediation analysis when phenotype + haplogroup data co-exist.
**Evidence link:** A11, BR16, C20
**Certainty:** 0.45
**Falsifiable prediction:** In a cohort with haplogroup + ADHD + ME/CFS phenotype data (n>500), mediation analysis will show haplogroup→ADHD→ME/CFS-severity is a non-zero indirect path independent of haplogroup→severity direct path.
**Tier:** 1

## 8. Cross-Disease Bridges

### B-27 — Migraine as Mitochondrial-Reserve Bridge
**Category:** Cross-disease
**Rationale:** Migraine shows mitochondrial dysfunction, responds to riboflavin/CoQ10/magnesium, and has elevated comorbidity with both ADHD and ME/CFS. Migraine may be a third clinical expression of the same reserve substrate, with cortical-spreading-depression as the rate-limiting failure mode.
**Evidence link:** B-20 rationale; G24
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable prediction:** Migraine prevalence in ME/CFS will be elevated specifically in haplogroup H (tight coupling, exercise-crash phenotype) and reduced in haplogroup U.
**Tier:** 1

### B-28 — Long COVID Subset Prediction
**Category:** Cross-disease
**Rationale:** If mitochondrial reserve gates ME/CFS-style outcomes after viral Hit 2, then Long COVID patients meeting ME/CFS criteria should show the same haplogroup distribution patterns as classic ME/CFS — and Long COVID patients NOT meeting ME/CFS criteria should not.
**Evidence link:** BR16, V19; two-hit framing
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable prediction:** Among Long COVID patients, those meeting ICC ME/CFS criteria will show haplogroup H over-representation in the severity-modulating direction predicted by BR16, while non-ME/CFS Long COVID will not.
**Tier:** 1

### B-29 — Primary Mitochondrial Disease Carriers as Natural Experiment
**Category:** Cross-disease
**Rationale:** Asymptomatic carriers of pathogenic mtDNA mutations (low heteroplasmy) live with reduced reserve their whole lives. If reserve-threshold model is correct, they should have elevated ME/CFS rates after viral triggers compared to non-carrier siblings.
**Evidence link:** N14; A11 (heritability); B-1 (heteroplasmy)
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable prediction:** Mitochondrial disease registries (MitoCohort, MMPOWER) will show post-viral ME/CFS-like illness incidence ≥3× general population in carrier-relatives, if surveyed.
**Tier:** 2

## 9. Diagnostic / Biomarker Ideas

### B-30 — Composite "Reserve Index" as Stratification Biomarker
**Category:** Biomarker
**Rationale:** Combine haplogroup (cheap, one-time) + Seahorse spare respiratory capacity (PBMCs) + heteroplasmy load (long-read mtDNA) into a single Reserve Index. Use to stratify trial arms — current ME/CFS trial heterogeneity may be partly reserve-stratum heterogeneity.
**Evidence link:** BR16, V19, N14, B-11
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable prediction:** Reserve Index will explain ≥15% of variance in 2-day CPET response in ME/CFS, exceeding any single component variable.
**Tier:** 1

### B-31 — Childhood ADHD History as Cheap ME/CFS-Risk Flag
**Category:** Biomarker (clinical)
**Rationale:** If B-5 holds, childhood ADHD diagnosis (or sub-clinical attention symptoms via WURS) is a no-cost, no-tech proxy for low mitochondrial reserve. Could enrich at-risk cohorts for prospective baseline studies (OQ2) without genetic testing.
**Evidence link:** C20, Ve16, G24; B-5
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable prediction:** WURS-25 score >36 in pre-illness adults will predict ME/CFS development after acute viral illness with OR ≥2.0 in a prospective post-viral cohort.
**Tier:** 1

### B-32 — Post-Exertional Plasma Lactate-to-Pyruvate Ratio as Coupling Proxy
**Category:** Biomarker
**Rationale:** L/P ratio rises with NADH/NAD+ imbalance — direct readout of OXPHOS bottleneck. Measured before and 24h post submaximal exertion, the delta should correlate with haplogroup-defined reserve. Cheap, repeatable, no genetic testing.
**Evidence link:** N14; BR16
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable prediction:** Δ(L/P ratio) at 24h post 6MWT will be ≥2× higher in haplogroup H ME/CFS than haplogroup U/J ME/CFS at matched severity.
**Tier:** 2
