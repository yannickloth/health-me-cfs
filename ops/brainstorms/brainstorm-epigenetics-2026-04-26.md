# Brainstorm: ME/CFS Epigenetics — PTPRN2 / miR-153-3p / PHB2 Axis

Generated: 2026-04-26

Context: Phase 3 brainstorm following Phase 1 literature scan; synthesizes the dual epigenetic finding (PTPRN2 hypomethylation + miR-153-3p reduction via PHB2) with prior ME/CFS methylation, miRNA, and 3D-genomic literature.

## Tier 1 — Strong Mechanistic Support, Plausibly Actionable

### T1.1 Hypothesis: PTPRN2 Hypomethylation Reflects Chronic Catecholamine Vesicle Stress

**Idea:** PTPRN2 hypomethylation in ME/CFS is not the cause but a *compensatory transcriptional response* to chronic depletion of dense-core vesicle machinery in catecholaminergic neurons and adrenal chromaffin cells. The hypomethylation transcriptionally upregulates PTPRN2 to restore vesicle biogenesis after sustained sympathoadrenal demand (chronic stress, orthostatic loading, infection-driven sympathetic surge).

**Mechanistic rationale:** PTPRN2 (IA-2β) is a transmembrane component of dense-core secretory vesicles required for monoamine packaging. Sustained sympathoadrenal activation (documented in ME/CFS via norepinephrine, autonomic dysfunction, POTS overlap) depletes vesicle pools. Promoter hypomethylation is a known compensatory response to chronic transcriptional demand. This makes hypomethylation a *biomarker of chronic autonomic stress*, not a primary lesion.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026 (PTPRN2 hypomethylation + male-only respiratory symptom association — respiratory drive depends on brainstem catecholaminergic nuclei); ch14 autonomic dysfunction; Boris 2026 (POTS overlap); known PTPRN2 biology (Cai 2011, Mziaut 2006 — vesicle biogenesis).

**Certainty:** 0.45.

**Falsifiable prediction:** PTPRN2 methylation should correlate inversely with 24h urinary catecholamine excretion and with tilt-table sympathetic indices (LF/HF ratio). Patients with the most severe orthostatic intolerance should show the most pronounced hypomethylation. If methylation is independent of autonomic load, the compensatory model fails.

### T1.2 Hypothesis: PHB2 Sex-Differential Regulation Drives Female Predominance via Estrogen-Mitochondrial Axis

**Idea:** PHB2 expression is estrogen-responsive (PHB2 is a repressor of estrogen receptor activity; ER signaling reciprocally regulates PHB2 transcription). The sex differences observed in PHB2/miR-153-3p/PTPRN2 regulation in ME/CFS are mediated by estrogen-cycle-dependent fluctuations in PHB2 levels, which destabilize miR-153-3p maturation specifically during low-estrogen windows. This contributes to the 4:1 female-to-male ratio and to perimenstrual symptom flares.

**Mechanistic rationale:** PHB2 is a known ER-alpha co-repressor (Kasashima 2006, He 2008). Estrogen withdrawal increases free PHB2 cytoplasmic pool. If cytoplasmic PHB2 sequesters or inhibits Dicer-stage processing of miR-153-3p, low-estrogen states (luteal phase, perimenstrual, perimenopause) should produce maximum miR-153-3p depletion and worst cognitive symptoms. This aligns with reported ME/CFS perimenstrual worsening and perimenopausal onset clusters.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026 (sex differences in PHB2 regulation); Cheema & Oltra 2020 (sex-differential miRNA in ME/CFS); known PHB2-ER biology; clinical observation of menstrual-cycle symptom modulation in ME/CFS.

**Certainty:** 0.40.

**Falsifiable prediction:** Within-subject longitudinal sampling of female ME/CFS patients across the menstrual cycle should show miR-153-3p nadir during late luteal/early follicular phase, correlating with peak cognitive symptom severity (PROMIS cognitive function, n-back). Postmenopausal patients should show stable low miR-153-3p. Males should show no cyclical pattern. If miR-153-3p is acyclic in cycling females, the estrogen-PHB2 model fails.

### T1.3 Hypothesis: miR-153-3p Reduction Causes Memory-Specific Cognitive Phenotype via BDNF-APP Co-dysregulation

**Idea:** The selective association of miR-153-3p reduction with *delayed memory recognition* (rather than global cognition) reflects loss of miR-153-3p's coordinated repression of APP and de-repression of BDNF in hippocampal CA1. This produces a "soft Alzheimer-like" memory consolidation deficit without the structural pathology — a functional epigenetic phenocopy.

**Mechanistic rationale:** miR-153-3p targets APP (lowering Aβ production) and indirectly modulates BDNF availability. In hippocampal long-term potentiation, both pathways converge on memory consolidation. Reduced miR-153-3p → elevated APP/Aβ + altered BDNF processing → impaired delayed recognition memory (the most LTP-dependent cognitive function). Acute and reversible — distinguishes ME/CFS brain fog from neurodegeneration.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026 (miR-153-3p inversely correlated with delayed memory recognition); Long 2012, Liang 2012 (miR-153 targets APP); rodent hippocampal memory consolidation studies; ME/CFS brain fog characterized by memory > attention deficits.

**Certainty:** 0.50.

**Falsifiable prediction:** In ME/CFS patients with low miR-153-3p, plasma soluble APP fragments (sAPPβ) should be elevated above controls but below Alzheimer thresholds. Cognitive testing should show a specific deficit on delayed recognition (Rey AVLT 30-min delay, Logical Memory II) with relative sparing of immediate recall, working memory, and processing speed. If the cognitive deficit pattern is global rather than memory-specific, the mechanism is upstream (cerebral hypoperfusion) not miR-153-3p-specific.

### T1.4 Research Direction: Multi-Tissue Methylation Concordance Validation Study

**Idea:** Test whether saliva PTPRN2 methylation (Chalder 2026 proxy) reflects blood, buccal, and (where ethically obtainable) post-mortem brain methylation in matched ME/CFS samples. Without this concordance, all saliva-based epigenetic ME/CFS findings remain interpretively limited.

**Mechanistic rationale:** Tissue specificity of methylation is the central limitation of saliva-based EWAS. PTPRN2 is expressed in pancreas, brain, adrenal — not salivary gland. Saliva methylation reflects buccal epithelium + leukocytes. Validation requires demonstrating that the PTPRN2 saliva signal tracks the leukocyte signal (proxy for systemic) and ideally that immune cells from ME/CFS patients show parallel changes.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026 (acknowledges saliva proxy limitation); Apostolou 2024 (immune cell methylation); established multi-tissue concordance methodology (BECon, IMAGE-CpG databases).

**Certainty:** 0.65 (the study itself is feasible; the prediction is moderate).

**Falsifiable prediction:** In matched saliva-blood pairs (n ≥ 60), PTPRN2 CpG methylation should correlate r > 0.5 across tissues at the same patients-vs-controls direction. If saliva and blood disagree on direction at PTPRN2 in > 30% of subjects, the saliva finding cannot be generalized to systemic biology.

### T1.5 Diagnostic Idea: Composite Epi-Score (PTPRN2 + miR-153-3p + EpiSwitch 3D)

**Idea:** Combine three orthogonal epigenetic measurements into a single discriminatory ME/CFS panel: (a) PTPRN2 saliva methylation (Chalder 2026), (b) blood miR-153-3p (Chalder 2026), (c) EpiSwitch 3D chromatin conformation panel (Pshezhetskiy 2025). Each captures a different layer of the regulatory hierarchy — promoter methylation, post-transcriptional miRNA, 3D genome architecture — and their combination should outperform any single marker.

**Mechanistic rationale:** Epigenetic regulation is hierarchical: 3D chromatin determines accessibility, methylation determines promoter activity, miRNA determines protein-level fine-tuning. Diagnostic signal at all three layers in concordant directions provides triangulated evidence and reduces single-platform false positives.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026; Pshezhetskiy 2025; Nepotchatykh 2020/2023 (miRNA panel discrimination of ME/CFS vs FM).

**Certainty:** 0.55.

**Falsifiable prediction:** A composite score combining all three should achieve AUC > 0.85 for ME/CFS vs sedentary controls in an independent cohort, exceeding any single component (Chalder reported ~AUC 0.7-0.75 range for PTPRN2 alone; EpiSwitch reported ~0.8). If the composite does not exceed the best single component by ≥ 0.05 AUC, the layers are redundant and the panel offers no advantage.

## Tier 2 — Moderate Support, Testable

### T2.1 Hypothesis: Mitochondrial PHB2-Mitophagy Failure Connects Epigenetic Axis to Energy Phenotype

**Idea:** PHB2 has dual cytoplasmic (miRNA processing) and mitochondrial (inner membrane scaffold, mitophagy receptor) roles. Sex-differential PHB2 dysregulation in ME/CFS may simultaneously cause miR-153-3p maturation failure *and* impaired mitophagy. The same PHB2 lesion thus links the epigenetic finding to the well-established ME/CFS mitochondrial dysfunction phenotype, providing a unified upstream cause.

**Mechanistic rationale:** PHB2 is required for PINK1/Parkin-mediated mitophagy (Wei 2017). PHB2 dysfunction → accumulation of damaged mitochondria → ROS, reduced ATP, sensory neuron + microglial activation. If a single PHB2 perturbation explains both miR-153-3p loss (cognitive phenotype) and mitophagy failure (energy phenotype), it is a parsimonious upstream node.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026 (PHB2 sex differences); Wei 2017 (PHB2 mitophagy receptor); Booth 2012, Tomas 2017 (ME/CFS mitochondrial dysfunction).

**Certainty:** 0.35.

**Falsifiable prediction:** PBMC mitophagy flux assays (e.g., mt-Keima, MitoSOX + LC3) in ME/CFS should show reduced mitophagy proportional to reduced miR-153-3p in matched patients. If mitophagy and miRNA defects are uncorrelated, PHB2 has dissociated functions.

### T2.2 Drug Idea: Metformin (Low-Dose) as PHB2-Mitophagy Modulator

**Idea:** Low-dose metformin (250-500 mg/day, well below diabetic doses) activates AMPK, indirectly enhances PHB2-mediated mitophagy, and may rescue both energy and cognitive phenotypes via the proposed PHB2 axis.

**Mechanistic rationale:** Metformin → AMPK → ULK1 phosphorylation → mitophagy initiation. Independent of glycemia. Already shown neuroprotective in pre-clinical AD/PD models (a disease space sharing miR-153-3p targets with ME/CFS).

**Evidence link:** T2.1 above; metformin neuroprotection literature; emerging Long COVID metformin trials (Bramante 2023 — reduced incidence).

**Certainty:** 0.30.

**Falsifiable prediction:** A 12-week pilot of metformin 500 mg/day in ME/CFS (n = 40, randomized vs placebo) should produce ≥ 0.3 SD improvement on delayed recognition memory and ≥ 5-point FUNCAP improvement compared to placebo. Negative trial → metformin does not access this axis at safe doses.

---

### T2.3 Supplement Idea: Urolithin A — Mitophagy Activator

**Idea:** Urolithin A (a gut-microbiome metabolite of ellagitannins; available as supplement Mitopure®) directly induces mitophagy via PHB2-dependent pathway and is the most specific mitophagy enhancer currently available OTC.

**Mechanistic rationale:** Urolithin A induces PHB2-mediated mitophagy in muscle and brain (Ryu 2016, Andreux 2019). If PHB2 dysfunction is upstream of ME/CFS energy + cognitive phenotype, supplementing the most direct PHB2-pathway activator may bypass the lesion.

**Evidence link:** Ryu 2016 (Nat Med — urolithin A mitophagy via PHB2); Andreux 2019 (human RCT, safe and improves muscle endurance in elderly); T2.1 above.

**Certainty:** 0.30.

**Falsifiable prediction:** RCT of urolithin A 500 mg/day × 16 weeks in ME/CFS should improve handgrip endurance and post-exertional recovery time vs placebo. Mechanistic biomarker: increased PBMC mitophagy flux. Negative trial → urolithin A does not reach therapeutic levels in ME/CFS-relevant tissues.

### T2.4 Hypothesis: Vagal Afferent Catecholamine Loop Couples PTPRN2 Methylation to Brain Fog

**Idea:** Hypomethylation-driven PTPRN2 upregulation in vagal nuclei (NTS, dorsal motor nucleus) alters dense-core vesicle dynamics in vagal afferents, distorting interoceptive signaling. The resulting mismatch between predicted and actual visceral state is experienced as brain fog (cognitive-interoceptive incoherence model of fatigue, Stephan 2016).

**Mechanistic rationale:** Brain fog has been mechanistically modeled as a Bayesian inference failure where interoceptive priors no longer match afferent signals. Vagal afferent vesicle dynamics depend on PTPRN2-family proteins. If PTPRN2 methylation perturbs vagal monoamine packaging, interoceptive precision collapses → "foggy" subjective state without measurable cognitive task deficit.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026 (PTPRN2 ↔ cognitive symptoms); Stephan 2016 (allostatic-interoceptive fatigue model); ch15 vagal/interoceptive content (if present).

**Certainty:** 0.25.

**Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS patients with PTPRN2 hypomethylation should show worse heartbeat-tracking task accuracy (interoceptive precision metric) than methylation-normal ME/CFS patients, even when matched on objective cognitive task performance. If interoceptive precision is independent of methylation status, the model fails.

### T2.5 Non-pharmacological Idea: Transcutaneous Auricular Vagus Nerve Stimulation (taVNS)

**Idea:** taVNS may downregulate sympathetic-driven PTPRN2 transcriptional demand by directly engaging parasympathetic tone, allowing PTPRN2 methylation to renormalize over weeks-months. Caregiver-implementable, suitable for severe/very-severe patients.

**Mechanistic rationale:** If PTPRN2 hypomethylation is a compensatory response to chronic sympathetic overdrive (T1.1), reducing sympathetic load via vagal stimulation should reduce transcriptional demand and permit methylation re-establishment. taVNS has documented effects on HRV, inflammation, and is well-tolerated.

**Evidence link:** T1.1; emerging taVNS trials in Long COVID (Verbanck 2024); ch14 autonomic content.

**Certainty:** 0.30.

**Falsifiable prediction:** 12-week taVNS protocol (NEMOS or equivalent, 30 min/day) in ME/CFS should produce (a) HRV improvement (RMSSD +10%), (b) symptom improvement (FUNCAP, fatigue), and (c) trend toward PTPRN2 methylation normalization in saliva sampled at baseline and end-of-treatment. Methylation change is the strongest test — if methylation is unchanged despite symptom improvement, the autonomic-epigenetic causal link is wrong.

### T2.6 Cross-Disease Bridge: Long COVID Methylation Convergence

**Idea:** Long COVID patients with cognitive phenotype should show PTPRN2 hypomethylation and miR-153-3p reduction overlapping with ME/CFS. Direct comparison would test whether the Chalder finding is post-infectious-syndrome-specific or ME/CFS-specific.

**Mechanistic rationale:** ME/CFS and Long COVID share post-infectious onset, autonomic dysfunction, cognitive phenotype. If they share the PTPRN2/miR-153-3p signature, the epigenetic axis is downstream of generalized post-infectious dysregulation, not ME/CFS-pathognomonic.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026; ch31 Long COVID overlap; Long COVID EWAS literature (limited but growing).

**Certainty:** 0.50.

**Falsifiable prediction:** Saliva methylation + blood miR-153-3p in n = 50 Long COVID patients with PEM should show > 70% directional concordance with the Chalder ME/CFS signature. If discordant, the signature is ME/CFS-specific (good diagnostic) but does not generalize across post-infectious syndromes.

### T2.7 Hypothesis: Dopamine-Norepinephrine Balance Determines Cognitive vs Respiratory Phenotype

**Idea:** PTPRN2 hypomethylation primarily affects cortical dopaminergic vesicle function in females (cognitive phenotype) and brainstem noradrenergic respiratory drive in males (respiratory phenotype). Sex-divergent neural distribution of PTPRN2-dependent vesicles, modulated by sex hormones, explains the sex-stratified symptom association observed by Chalder.

**Mechanistic rationale:** PTPRN2 expression is region-specific; sex hormones modulate regional catecholamine system development and maintenance. Female brains have higher cortical dopaminergic density; male brains higher locus coeruleus/RVLM noradrenergic drive. A common upstream lesion can produce sex-divergent phenotypes via differential neural substrate.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026 (male-only respiratory association is exploratory but striking); known sex differences in catecholamine system; T1.2.

**Certainty:** 0.30.

**Falsifiable prediction:** In a replication cohort, PTPRN2 hypomethylation should predict cognitive symptoms in females (OR > 2) and respiratory symptoms (dyspnea, breathing pattern disorder) in males (OR > 2), with sex-by-genotype interaction p < 0.05. If the male respiratory association does not replicate, the Chalder exploratory finding was a chance result.

### T2.8 Mathematical Model Extension: Add Epigenetic State Variable to Allostatic ODE

**Idea:** Existing allostatic-load ME/CFS ODE models (ch32 attractor model) treat the patient as a dynamical system with energy, inflammation, autonomic state. Add a slow-timescale epigenetic state variable E (representing PTPRN2 methylation or composite epi-score) with dynamics dE/dt = -α(E - E_demand) where E_demand is set by sympathoadrenal load. This couples fast (autonomic) and slow (epigenetic) timescales and predicts hysteresis in recovery.

**Mechanistic rationale:** Methylation responds to demand on weeks-months timescale; symptoms can shift in hours. Coupled-timescale dynamical systems exhibit hysteresis: even when acute trigger removes, the slow variable E remains shifted, biasing the system toward relapse. Quantifies why ME/CFS is "easy to enter, hard to leave."

**Evidence link:** ch32 attractor model; Chalder 2026 (methylation as state variable); general slow-fast dynamical systems theory.

**Certainty:** 0.40 (the model can be written; whether it predicts useful clinical phenomena is uncertain).

**Falsifiable prediction:** Model should predict that recovery time scales with the integral of past sympathoadrenal load (∫ stress dt over months prior). Empirical test: time-to-remission in ME/CFS recovery cohorts should correlate with cumulative pre-illness allostatic load metrics. If recovery time is independent of pre-illness load history, the slow-variable model is over-specified.

### T2.9 Diagnostic Idea: Methylation-Based Subgrouping for Treatment Stratification

**Idea:** PTPRN2 methylation status defines two ME/CFS subgroups with potentially different treatment response profiles. Hypomethylated subgroup may preferentially respond to autonomic/catecholamine-targeted treatments (pyridostigmine, ivabradine, low-dose clonidine); methylation-normal subgroup may preferentially respond to immune-targeted treatments (LDN, IVIG).

**Mechanistic rationale:** Chalder 2026 explicitly demonstrates that PTPRN2 methylation enables patient subgrouping. If the subgroup reflects autonomic-dominant pathophysiology (T1.1), it should predict differential treatment response.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026 (subgrouping); ch14 mechanism-treatment map.

**Certainty:** 0.35.

**Falsifiable prediction:** In retrospective analysis of pyridostigmine-treated ME/CFS cohorts, response rate should be ≥ 20 percentage points higher in PTPRN2-hypomethylated patients than in methylation-normal patients. A prospective stratified trial would be definitive. Null differential → methylation does not predict treatment response.

## Tier 3 — Speculative, Cross-Disease Analogy, Lower Certainty

### T3.1 Hypothesis: PTPRN2 Hypomethylation Reflects Reactivated Endogenous Retrovirus Adjacent to Locus

**Idea:** PTPRN2 lies in a chromosomal region known to harbor HERV-W and HERV-K elements. Chronic inflammation/infection can demethylate HERV LTRs, and adjacent gene methylation often shifts together. The Chalder PTPRN2 hypomethylation may be a bystander effect of HERV reactivation, not a primary PTPRN2 lesion.

**Mechanistic rationale:** HERV reactivation has been proposed in ME/CFS (de la Hera 2013) and is established in ALS, MS. ERV LTRs influence neighboring methylation domains. If true, the therapeutic target is HERV (antiretroviral candidate; e.g., temelimab for HERV-W in MS) rather than PTPRN2 itself.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026; HERV-W/K literature; emerging temelimab MS trials.

**Certainty:** 0.20.

**Falsifiable prediction:** Targeted bisulfite sequencing of HERV LTRs near PTPRN2 (chr7q36) in Chalder cohort samples should show co-directional hypomethylation. If HERV LTRs are normally methylated despite PTPRN2 hypomethylation, the HERV-bystander model fails.

### T3.2 Cross-Disease Bridge: Autism PTPRN2 Variant Convergence

**Idea:** PTPRN2 is among autism-associated CNV/SNV genes. Genetic disruption (autism) and epigenetic disruption (ME/CFS) of the same gene may produce overlapping interoceptive/sensory features. ME/CFS sensory hypersensitivity may share substrate with autism sensory differences.

**Mechanistic rationale:** Convergent gene disruption from different mechanisms suggests common downstream phenotype. Both conditions show sensory processing differences, autonomic atypicality, fatigue susceptibility.

**Evidence link:** PTPRN2 in autism literature (SFARI gene database); sensory hypersensitivity overlap clinically observed.

**Certainty:** 0.20.

**Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS patients with PTPRN2 hypomethylation should score higher on sensory profile measures (Sensory Profile Adolescent/Adult) than methylation-normal ME/CFS, with effect size cohen's d > 0.4. Null → PTPRN2 disruption does not produce a unified sensory phenotype.

### T3.3 Supplement Idea: SAM-e (S-Adenosylmethionine) for Methylation Support

**Idea:** SAM-e is the universal methyl donor. If ME/CFS shows methylation depletion at PTPRN2, supporting global methylation capacity may reduce hypomethylation. Counterintuitive: SAM-e is also a depression supplement, addressing an overlapping symptom domain.

**Mechanistic rationale:** Methylation reactions depend on methyl donor availability (SAM-e from methionine via folate-B12 cycle). If a methyl donor deficiency contributes to PTPRN2 hypomethylation, supplementation may rescue it. Note: hypomethylation is more likely *demand-driven* than *supply-driven*, so this may be ineffective unless combined with demand reduction.

**Evidence link:** General methylation biochemistry; SAM-e in depression (modest evidence); reported folate-B12 issues in some ME/CFS patients.

**Certainty:** 0.15.

**Falsifiable prediction:** SAM-e 800 mg/day × 12 weeks should not normalize PTPRN2 methylation as monotherapy (prediction of *failure*). If it does, supply-side methylation support is sufficient. Combined with stress reduction, may help; alone, unlikely.

### T3.4 Drug Idea: Bromodomain (BET) Inhibitors as Epigenetic Resetters

**Idea:** BET inhibitors (e.g., apabetalone, JQ1) reshape epigenetic landscapes and have been explored in cardiovascular, oncologic, autoimmune disease. Speculative use in severe ME/CFS to reset aberrant methylation/chromatin patterns.

**Mechanistic rationale:** BET inhibitors disrupt BRD4-mediated transcription at hyperacetylated loci, allowing epigenetic re-configuration. Apabetalone has favorable safety profile in CV trials.

**Evidence link:** General BET inhibitor literature; no ME/CFS evidence.

**Certainty:** 0.10.

**Falsifiable prediction:** Pre-clinical (PBMC ex vivo) test: apabetalone treatment of ME/CFS patient PBMCs should normalize aberrant gene expression patterns toward control profiles (RNA-seq endpoint). If no normalization in vitro, no rationale to proceed.

### T3.5 Combination Protocol: Tier-Stratified Multi-Component Intervention

**Idea:** Sequential, severity-stratified protocol drawing on epigenetic axis ideas:
- **All patients (mild-very severe):** taVNS (caregiver-applied for severe), urolithin A 500 mg/day, B12 + folate optimization, light/sound environment control.
- **Mild-moderate add:** Low-dose metformin 500 mg/day, autonomic rehabilitation (within energy envelope), cognitive pacing.
- **PTPRN2-hypomethylated subgroup (any severity):** Add pyridostigmine titration if orthostatic intolerance present; consider low-dose clonidine for sympathoadrenal load reduction.
- **Female cycling patients:** Symptom-cycle tracking; consider luteal-phase support strategies pending T1.2 validation.

**Rationale:** Combines mitophagy support (urolithin A), autonomic load reduction (taVNS, optional clonidine), methyl donor support (B12/folate), severity-appropriate accessibility, and methylation-stratified add-ons.

**Certainty:** 0.25 (as a coherent untested protocol).

**Falsifiable prediction:** Real-world evidence cohort following this protocol vs treatment-as-usual should show ≥ 8-point FUNCAP improvement at 6 months in protocol group. Null → multicomponent strategy adds nothing over individual components.

### T3.6 Research Direction: Single-Cell Methylation Atlas of ME/CFS Saliva and Blood

**Idea:** Bulk methylation studies (Chalder 2026) cannot localize PTPRN2 hypomethylation to specific cell types. Single-cell methylation (snmC-seq, sci-MET) on ME/CFS saliva and blood would identify which immune/epithelial subset drives the signal.

**Mechanistic rationale:** Bulk averaging dilutes cell-type-specific signals. If PTPRN2 hypomethylation is restricted to a single immune subset (e.g., NK cells, plasmacytoid DCs), this both clarifies mechanism and provides a more specific biomarker.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026; Apostolou 2024 (immune cell methylation); single-cell methylation methodology.

**Certainty:** 0.55 (study is feasible; outcome interpretation uncertain).

**Falsifiable prediction:** snmC-seq should localize PTPRN2 hypomethylation to ≤ 3 cell types out of ~15 detectable. If hypomethylation is uniformly distributed across all cell types, the bulk signal reflects systemic regulation rather than cell-type-specific dysfunction.

### T3.7 Hypothesis: miR-153-3p Functions as a Sleep-Wake Regulator

**Idea:** miR-153-3p has circadian variation in expression. Its reduction in ME/CFS may contribute to the unrefreshing sleep / circadian dysregulation phenotype, independent of memory function.

**Mechanistic rationale:** miRNAs regulate circadian gene networks. miR-153 targets include genes in hippocampal-suprachiasmatic communication. Sleep dysregulation is core ME/CFS, distinct from cognitive symptoms.

**Evidence link:** Limited circadian miRNA literature; ME/CFS sleep architecture studies (Jackson 2016).

**Certainty:** 0.15.

**Falsifiable prediction:** Time-of-day sampling of miR-153-3p should show reduced amplitude (flattened diurnal variation) in ME/CFS, correlating with actigraphy-measured sleep fragmentation. Null → miR-153-3p is sleep-irrelevant.

### T3.8 Diagnostic Idea: Saliva-Based Self-Sampling Kit for Severe Patients

**Idea:** Methylation analysis from saliva is a major accessibility advantage for very-severe and bedbound ME/CFS patients who cannot reach blood-draw services. A validated home self-sampling protocol (Oragene-style kit + mailed return) is the path to including the most severely ill in epigenetic research and biomarker stratification.

**Mechanistic rationale:** Equity and representativeness. Severe-ME/CFS literature is systematically underpowered because severe patients can't attend clinics. Saliva home kits eliminate this bias.

**Evidence link:** Chalder 2026 (saliva methodology); 25%MEGroup advocacy on severe-patient research exclusion.

**Certainty:** 0.70 (operationally feasible; the bottleneck is funding and IRB approval, not science).

**Falsifiable prediction:** Self-collected saliva methylation should show < 10% technical variance compared to clinic-collected samples in a methodological validation study (paired-design, n = 30). If variance > 20%, home sampling is not interchangeable with clinic sampling.

### T3.9 Cross-Disease Bridge: Parkinson's Disease miR-153-3p Convergence

**Idea:** miR-153-3p targets SNCA (alpha-synuclein) in Parkinson's models. PD patients show altered miR-153 levels. ME/CFS reduction of miR-153-3p could reflect a shared "neurovesicular dysregulation" axis with PD, suggesting that ME/CFS patients may have elevated subclinical risk for later-life parkinsonian features.

**Mechanistic rationale:** Shared substrate (PTPRN2 = vesicle protein; miR-153 → SNCA = vesicle-associated protein) suggests both diseases impinge on dense-core vesicle biology. ME/CFS epidemiology has noted elevated long-term neurodegenerative comorbidity in some cohorts (uncertain).

**Evidence link:** Doxakis 2010 (miR-153 → SNCA); Chalder 2026; weak ME/CFS-neurodegeneration epidemiology.

**Certainty:** 0.15.

**Falsifiable prediction:** Long-term follow-up cohorts (≥ 20 years) of ME/CFS patients should show modestly elevated PD/Lewy body dementia incidence vs population baseline (HR > 1.3). Null → ME/CFS miR-153 reduction does not translate to elevated synucleinopathy risk.

### T3.10 Mathematical Model Extension: Stochastic Methylation-Phenotype Map

**Idea:** Treat methylation at PTPRN2 not as a single value but as a probability distribution across CpG sites; phenotype severity reflects the proportion of dysregulated sites crossing a threshold. Useful for explaining within-patient symptom variability and crash-dynamics.

**Mechanistic rationale:** CpG-level stochasticity is biologically real. Methylation is a population property, not a deterministic value. Phenotype thresholds in stochastic systems explain hysteresis, episodic worsening, and partial recovery.

**Evidence link:** Methylation noise literature (Landan 2012); ch32 attractor model.

**Certainty:** 0.30.

**Falsifiable prediction:** Within-patient longitudinal CpG methylation distributions should show greater variance in severe vs mild ME/CFS, with variance correlating with day-to-day symptom variability. Null → CpG-level stochasticity does not contribute to clinical heterogeneity.

## Cross-Cutting Notes

- **Replication priority:** T1.3 (memory-specific deficit), T1.5 (composite epi-score), T2.6 (Long COVID convergence) are the highest-value replication studies.
- **Lowest-risk actionable items:** T2.3 (urolithin A), T2.5 (taVNS), T3.8 (saliva home kit) — all currently feasible with low risk.

## Caveats & Limitations

- All mechanistic chains here extrapolate from a single primary study (Chalder 2026, n = 54 vs 21, saliva proxy, cross-sectional).
- Sex-stratified findings in Chalder are exploratory and not multiple-testing corrected.
- Treatment ideas require medical supervision; none are clinical recommendations.
- Mechanistic plausibility ≠ clinical efficacy. Several Tier 1/2 ideas could fail despite tight logic.
- Cross-disease bridges (autism, PD) are analogical and weakest of the categories.
- Replication of the Chalder findings is the prerequisite to most downstream work proposed here.
