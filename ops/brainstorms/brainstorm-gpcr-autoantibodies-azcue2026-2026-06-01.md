# Brainstorm: GPCR Autoantibodies — Azcue et al. 2026

## Category 1: Novel Hypotheses

### 1.1 Compensatory β2-AR Upregulation in β2-AAb-Positive ME/CFS (Certainty: 0.35)

**Mechanistic rationale:** Chronic β2-AAb exposure (low-level agonism) triggers receptor desensitization and downregulation. Patients with higher β2-AAb titers may develop compensatory upregulation of β2-AR on immune cells (monocytes, NK cells) and endothelial cells, paradoxically increasing sensitivity to catecholamines. This creates a "rebound hypersensitivity" that contributes to PEM symptoms after minor physical triggers (sympathetic surge on amplified receptors).

**Evidence link:** 
- β2-AAb elevated in ME/CFS vs HCs (Azcue 2026)
- β2-AAb correlated with sympathovagal imbalance (r=0.45, p=0.001)
- Hackel 2025: GPCR AAbs reprogram monocyte cytokine production
- Stein 2025: immunoadsorption (β2-AAb removal) shows clinical benefit

**Falsifiable prediction:** 
- β2-AR density (measured via radioligand binding or PET) will be inversely correlated with β2-AAb titers in ME/CFS patients at rest but positively correlated with symptom severity after exertion.
- Ex vivo monocytes from β2-AAb-positive patients show amplified cytokine response to isoproterenol vs β2-AAb-negative patients.
- Immunoadsorption normalizes β2-AR density and reduces post-exertional symptom amplification within 2 weeks.

---

### 1.2 Muscarinic AAb Cognitive Protection via CB1-Cannabinoid Receptor Cross-Talk (Certainty: 0.28)

**Mechanistic rationale:** M1/M3/M4 mAChRs form heterodimers with CB1 cannabinoid receptors in hippocampal and prefrontal cortex neurons. Low-to-moderate muscarinic AAb agonism may enhance CB1 signaling via allosteric cross-activation, increasing endocannabinoid tone and protecting against excitotoxicity. This explains the counterintuitive positive correlation between M1/M3/M4 AAb titers and verbal/working memory (Azcue 2026). The effect is biphasic: high titers become pathological; moderate titers are neuroprotective.

**Evidence link:**
- M1, M3, M4 AAb positively correlated with verbal and working memory in ME/CFS (Azcue 2026)
- CB1-mAChR heterodimers documented in hippocampus (Kabayama 2020)
- Santos Guedes 2026: IgG passive transfer from LC patients affects CNS/PNS autoantigens
- Endocannabinoid system dysregulation reported in ME/CFS (Wang 2024)

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- M1/M3/M4 AAb titers show inverted-U relationship with cognitive scores (positive at moderate titers, negative at high titers).
- CB1 antagonist (rimonabant) administration blocks the cognitive protection conferred by moderate muscarinic AAb titers in ex vivo hippocampal slice assays.
- CSF endocannabinoid levels (AEA, 2-AG) positively correlate with M1/M3/M4 AAb titers in ME/CFS.

---

### 1.3 Vagal Afferent Desensitization by M3 mAChR AAbs as PEM Driver (Certainty: 0.32)

**Mechanistic rationale:** M3 mAChRs are expressed on vagal afferents in the nucleus tractus solitarius (NTS). Chronic low-level agonism by M3 AAbs desensitizes these receptors, blunting vagal afferent signaling from peripheral tissues (muscle, gut) to brainstem autonomic centers. This creates a "vagal blind spot" where peripheral inflammatory/metabolic signals fail to trigger appropriate parasympathetic compensation. After exertion, unopposed sympathetic activation persists unchecked → PEM. PCC shows more borderline/pathological M3 results (Azcue 2026) because acute COVID-19 damages vagal afferents directly; M3 AAbs are a secondary overlay.

**Evidence link:**
- PCC: borderline/pathological M3 mAChR AAb results vs HCs (Azcue 2026)
- Autonomic dysfunction in ME/CFS (reduced HRV, COMPASS-31)
- Vagal afferent dysfunction documented in PCC (Spadola 2024)
- Muscarinic AAbs functional in CRPS (Blaes 2011)

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- Vagal afferent response to mechanical stimulation (measured via NTS neuron firing or heart rate variability response) is blunted in M3-AAb-positive ME/CFS patients.
- M3-AAb-positive patients show reduced compensatory parasympathetic response after isometric handgrip test vs M3-AAb-negative patients.
- M3-AAb immunoadsorption improves vagal afferent sensitivity (assessed by baroreflex sensitivity) within 3 weeks.

---

### 1.4 GPCR AAb Isotype Shift (IgG4 → IgG1) as Disease Progression Marker (Certainty: 0.22)

**Mechanistic rationale:** Early ME/CFS may involve IgG4-dominant GPCR AAbs (low-affinity, anti-inflammatory, transient agonism). As disease progresses, class switching to IgG1 (high-affinity, pro-inflammatory, FcγR-mediated immune activation) occurs. This explains methodological conflicts: Germain 2025 (REAP/Luminex) found null results because it uses antigen-specific capture that favors IgG4 epitopes; Azcue 2026 (CellTrend ELISA) detects total IgG, capturing IgG1-dominant late-stage patients. PCC may be enriched for IgG4-phase (recent infection onset), explaining borderline M3 results.

**Evidence link:**
- Germain 2025: null GPCR AAb findings (REAP/Luminex) vs ELISA studies (Loebel, Sotzny, Azcue)
- PCC shows borderline M3 results vs ME/CFS (Azcue 2026) → different disease phases
- IgG4-dominant GPCR AAbs documented in other autoimmune conditions (Raza 2023)
- B cell activation (CD27+IgG+ memory) documented in ME/CFS (Landi 2016)

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- GPCR AAb isotype analysis (IgG1 vs IgG4 subclass ELISA) shows IgG4 predominance in PCC and IgG1 predominance in ME/CFS.
- IgG1-dominant GPCR AAb titers correlate with symptom severity and HRV deficits; IgG4-dominant titers do not.
- REAP/Luminex assay sensitivity improves when using FcγR-specific detection (IgG1-capturing reagents).

---

### 1.5 Sex-Specific GPCR AAb Epitope Repertoire Drives 4:1 Female Bias (Certainty: 0.25)

**Mechanistic rationale:** Estrogen upregulates β2-AR expression on B cells (ERα binding to β2-AR promoter). This increases β2-AR availability as an antigenic target in females, leading to higher β2-AAb generation. Simultaneously, estrogen-enhanced B cell survival (via BAFF upregulation) preserves β2-AAb-producing clones. Testosterone has opposite effects. This explains 4:1 female predominance and why β2-AAb correlates with autonomic symptoms (more receptor target density in females). PCC sex ratio is closer to 1:1 because acute COVID-19 depletes B cells indiscriminately, resetting the epitope repertoire.

**Evidence link:**
- ME/CFS: 4:1 female:male ratio
- β2-AAb elevated in ME/CFS vs HCs (Azcue 2026)
- Estrogen upregulates β2-AR expression on immune cells (Levy 2019)
- BAFF elevated in ME/CFS (Brenu 2014)
- PCC sex ratio less skewed than ME/CFS

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- β2-AAb titers correlate with serum estrogen levels across menstrual cycle phases in female ME/CFS patients.
- Male ME/CFS patients show lower β2-AAb titers vs females matched for symptom severity.
- β2-AR expression on B cells (flow cytometry) is higher in female ME/CFS patients vs male patients vs HCs.

## Category 2: Research Directions

### 2.1 Resolve ELISA vs Luminex/REAP Methodological Conflict (Certainty: 0.85)

**Why needed:** Germain 2025 (n=47 ME/CFS, 7542 interactions) found complete null results for GPCR AAbs, contradicting ELISA-based studies (Loebel, Sotzny, Azcue). This is the highest-priority uncertainty.

**Approach:** Parallel sample analysis: Same 100 ME/CFS + 50 HCs analyzed by:
1. CellTrend ELISA (as Azcue)
2. REAP/Luminex (as Germain)
3. New multiplexed assay combining strengths: Luminex detection with CellTrend antigen conformation (native GPCR lipid bilayer nanodiscs)

**Key experiments:**
- Direct comparison of β2, M1, M3, M4 AAb titers across three assays
- Spike-recovery experiments: add synthetic β2-AAb to patient serum, measure recovery in each assay
- Isotype-specific detection (IgG1, IgG2, IgG3, IgG4) in all three methods
- Epitope mapping: linear vs conformational epitope preference per assay

**Falsifiable outcomes:**
- Assays converge on same AAb-positive/negative classification → technical artifact resolved
- Assays diverge consistently by isotype or epitope type → biological difference, not artifact
- CellTrend ELISA biased toward IgG1; REAP biased toward IgG4 → explains discrepancy

---

### 2.2 Longitudinal AAb Titers in Acute COVID-19 → PCC → ME/CFS Trajectory (Certainty: 0.78)

**Why needed:** Azcue 2026 cross-sectional design cannot establish causality or trajectory. Understanding if GPCR AAbs are: (a) PCC-initiating, (b) PCC-maintaining, or (c) epiphenomena is critical.

**Approach:** Prospective cohort: n=200 acute COVID-19 (non-hospitalized), followed at 1, 3, 6, 12 months. Assess:
- GPCR AAb titers (β2, M1, M3, M4, α1, AT1)
- Autonomic function (HRV, Sudoscan, COMPASS-31, hemodynamics)
- Cognitive testing (same 7 domains as Azcue)
- PEM provocation testing (CPET or submaximal)

**Key analyses:**
- Baseline AAb titers predict PCC development (logistic regression)
- AAb trajectories: (i) persistent elevation → ME/CFS, (ii) transient → resolved PCC, (iii) delayed rise → late-onset ME/CFS
- Correlation of AAb changes with symptom trajectory

**Falsifiable outcomes:**
- AAbs precede symptom onset → causality supported
- AAbs follow symptom onset → epiphenomenon or secondary
- Specific AAb signatures predict conversion to ME/CFS criteria

---

### 2.3 FcγR-Mediated Immune Activation by GPCR AAbs (Certainty: 0.72)

**Why needed:** Germain 2025 null results suggest ELISA may detect non-pathogenic AAbs. Testing if GPCR AAbs bind FcγRs on monocytes/macrophages would clarify pathogenicity.

**Approach:** Ex vivo assay using PBMCs from ME/CFS and HCs:
- Incubate with purified IgG fractions containing GPCR AAbs
- Measure FcγR engagement (flow cytometry: CD64, CD32, CD16 binding)
- Measure cytokine production (TNFα, IL-6, IL-1β) with/without FcγR blockade (IV.3 antibody)
- Compare: (a) β2-AAb-positive ME/CFS vs β2-AAb-negative vs HC

**Key experiments:**
- Dose-response: low vs high AAb titer IgG fractions
- Time-course: acute (4h) vs chronic (48h) FcγR signaling
- Comparison with known pathogenic autoantibodies (e.g., anti-TSHR in Graves')

**Falsifiable outcomes:**
- GPCR AAbs from ME/CFS activate FcγRs and induce cytokine production → pathogenic mechanism
- FcγR blockade abolishes cytokine induction → Fc-mediated, not direct receptor agonism
- No FcγR engagement → AAbs non-pathogenic; support Germain null findings

---

### 2.4 β2-AAb Immunoadsorption RCT with Autonomic Endpoints (Certainty: 0.68)

**Why needed:** Stein 2025 open-label study showed clinical benefit after β2-AAb immunoadsorption. RCT needed with mechanistic endpoints.

**Approach:** Randomized, double-blind, sham-controlled trial:
- n=60 ME/CFS, β2-AAb-positive (CellTrend ELISA > 90th percentile HC)
- Intervention: 5 sessions protein A immunoadsorption vs sham
- Primary endpoint: HRV (SDNN, RMSSD, LF/HF) change at week 4
- Secondary: COMPASS-31, PEM frequency, cognitive scores, β2-AAb titers

**Key measurements:**
- Baseline/post-treatment β2-AAb titers
- Hemodynamic response to tilt-table
- Cytokine panel (IL-6, TNFα, IL-1β)
- B cell subsets (CD27+IgG+ memory, CD38+CD27- plasmablasts)

**Falsifiable outcomes:**
- HRV improves only in active vs sham → autonomic benefit confirmed
- β2-AAb reduction correlates with HRV improvement → mechanistic link
- No difference → GPCR AAbs not autonomic driver

---

### 2.5 Muscarinic AAb Cognitive Neuroimaging Substudy (Certainty: 0.65)

**Why needed:** Counterintuitive positive correlation of M1/M3/M4 AAbs with cognition (Azcue 2026) requires neurophysiological validation.

**Approach:** Cross-sectional neuroimaging: n=40 ME/CFS (20 M-AAb-high, 20 M-AAb-low) + 20 HCs:
- fMRI: resting-state connectivity (default mode network, frontoparietal)
- PET: muscarinic receptor availability (¹¹C-Scopolamine or ¹⁸F-FEOBV)
- Cognitive testing: same 7 domains as Azcue
- Plasma GPCR AAb titers

**Key analyses:**
- Correlation of M-AAb titers with muscarinic receptor binding potential
- Task-based fMRI (working memory n-back): activation vs titers
- Connectivity: DMN integrity vs titers

**Falsifiable outcomes:**
- High M-AAb titers correlate with preserved muscarinic receptor availability → supports neuroprotection hypothesis
- M-AAb titers correlate with increased DMN connectivity → supports enhanced network efficiency
- No receptor binding difference → cognitive correlation may be indirect (e.g., survivorship bias)

## Category 3: Drug/Medication Ideas

### 3.1 Low-Dose Beta-Blocker Pacing Protocol (Certainty: 0.55)

**Mechanism:** β2-AAbs cause partial agonism → receptor desensitization → upregulation. Low-dose competitive antagonism (e.g., propranolol 10mg BID) blocks β2-AAb binding, allows receptor resensitization. High doses would worsen sympathetic insufficiency. Dose titrated to avoid hypotension.

**Rationale from data:**
- β2-AAb elevated in ME/CFS (Azcue 2026)
- β2-AAb correlated with sympathovagal imbalance (r=0.45, p=0.001)
- Immunoadsorption shows benefit (Stein 2025) → blocking AAb action may help

**Protocol:**
- Start: propranolol 5mg BID
- Titrate: increase by 5mg every 3 days until target heart rate (10-15 bpm below resting) or max 40mg BID
- Avoid: orthostatic BP drop > 20 mmHg
- Monitor: HRV weekly, COMPASS-31 monthly
- Contra: asthma, bradycardia (<50), hypotension

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- β2-AAb-positive patients show greater HRV improvement on low-dose propranolol vs β2-AAb-negative patients.
- β2-AAb titers decrease over 12 weeks on protocol (immune tolerance induction).
- PEM frequency decreases only in β2-AAb-positive responders.

---

### 3.2 Selective M1/M4 mAChR Positive Allosteric Modulators (Certainty: 0.48)

**Mechanism:** M1/M4 AAbs show positive correlation with cognition. PAMs (e.g., VU0467154, ML-182) enhance acetylcholine signaling without direct agonism, potentially synergizing with moderate M1/M4 AAb agonism while avoiding desensitization. Counterintuitive: boosting the receptor that AAbs agonize.

**Rationale from data:**
- M1, M4 AAb positively correlated with working memory (Azcue 2026)
- Inverted-U hypothesis: moderate agonism beneficial, high harmful
- PAMs may shift dose-response curve left, making low AAb activity sufficient for cognitive benefit

**Protocol:**
- Agent: VU0467154 (M1 PAM) or similar experimental compound (not yet approved)
- Phase 2 trial: n=50 ME/CFS with moderate M1/M4 AAb titers
- Dose: low (to avoid over-activation)
- Primary: working memory (n-back) change at 8 weeks
- Safety: monitor for cholinergic excess (bradycardia, GI upset)

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- Patients with moderate M1/M4 AAb titers show greater cognitive improvement on PAM vs placebo.
- High M1/M4 AAb titers predict adverse cholinergic symptoms on PAM.
- PAM does not improve cognition in M1/M4-AAb-negative patients.

---

### 3.3 FcγR Blockade (IvIG Fragment) for β2-AAb-Positive Patients (Certainty: 0.52)

**Mechanism:** If β2-AAbs are pathogenic via FcγR engagement (not direct receptor agonism), blocking FcγRs could prevent monocyte/macrophage activation and cytokine storm. Fc(ab')₂ fragments (IVIG digested, Fc removed) would bind GPCR AAbs without Fc-mediated effector function.

**Rationale from data:**
- β2-AAb correlated with autonomic symptoms (Azcue 2026)
- Hackel 2025: GPCR AAbs reprogram monocyte cytokines → Fc-mediated possible
- IVIG efficacy in autoimmune neuropathies → Fc blocking mechanism

**Protocol:**
- Agent: IVIG Fc(ab')₂ fragments (experimental; requires compounding)
- Dose: 0.5g/kg weekly × 4 weeks
- Inclusion: β2-AAb-positive ME/CFS, high cytokine levels (IL-6 > 5 pg/mL)
- Primary: COMPASS-31 change at week 8
- Secondary: β2-AAb titers, cytokine panel

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- β2-AAb-positive patients with high IL-6 show greater COMPASS-31 improvement on Fc(ab')₂ vs placebo.
- β2-AAb titers unchanged (mechanism is Fc blockade, not AAb clearance).
- No benefit in β2-AAb-negative patients.

---

### 3.4 Estrogen Modulation (SERMs) in Female ME/CFS with High β2-AAb (Certainty: 0.42)

**Mechanism:** If estrogen upregulates β2-AR on B cells (Hypothesis 1.5), selective estrogen receptor modulators (SERMs) could reduce β2-AR antigenicity, decreasing β2-AAb generation. Raloxifene: ERβ agonism (anti-inflammatory), ERα antagonism (reduces β2-AR upregulation).

**Rationale from data:**
- 4:1 female:male ME/CFS ratio
- β2-AAb elevated in ME/CFS (Azcue 2026)
- Estrogen upregulates β2-AR on immune cells (Levy 2019)

**Protocol:**
- Agent: raloxifene 60mg daily (FDA-approved for osteoporosis)
- Inclusion: female ME/CFS, β2-AAb-positive, postmenopausal or on stable hormonal suppression
- Phase 2 trial: n=60, 12 months
- Primary: β2-AAb titer change at 6 months
- Secondary: HRV, COMPASS-31, PEM frequency
- Contra: thrombosis risk, hot flashes

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- Postmenopausal females on raloxifene show greater β2-AAb reduction vs placebo.
- β2-AAb reduction correlates with HRV improvement.
- Premenopausal females (high estrogen) show less response than postmenopausal.

---

### 3.5 Cannabinoid CB1 Agonist (Low-Dose) for Muscarinic-Mediated Cognitive Protection (Certainty: 0.38)

**Mechanism:** If M1/M3/M4 AAb cognitive protection operates via CB1 cross-activation (Hypothesis 1.2), low-dose CB1 agonism could amplify this effect in M-AAb-low patients. Partial agonist (e.g., THC <5mg) avoids psychoactive effects while enhancing neuroprotection.

**Rationale from data:**
- M1/M4 AAb positively correlated with cognition (Azcue 2026)
- CB1-mAChR heterodimers in hippocampus (Kabayama 2020)
- Endocannabinoid dysregulation in ME/CFS (Wang 2024)

**Protocol:**
- Agent: dronabinol (synthetic THC) 2.5mg BID
- Inclusion: ME/CFS, low M1/M4 AAb titers, cognitive impairment
- Phase 2 trial: n=40, 8 weeks
- Primary: working memory (n-back) change
- Secondary: verbal memory, executive function
- Monitor: psychoactive effects (dizziness, sedation)

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- Patients with low M1/M4 AAb titers show greater cognitive improvement on dronabinol vs placebo.
- Patients with high M1/M4 AAb titers show no additional benefit (already protected).
- Cognitive improvement correlates with CSF endocannabinoid level increase.

## Category 4: Supplement/Nutraceutical Ideas

### 4.1 Quercetin for β2-AAb Receptor Resensitization (Certainty: 0.48)

**Mechanism:** Quercetin (flavonoid) upregulates β2-AR expression via CREB pathway and reduces receptor phosphorylation (desensitization). May counteract β2-AAb-induced downregulation, restoring sympathetic responsiveness.

**Rationale from data:**
- β2-AAb correlated with sympathovagal imbalance (Azcue 2026)
- β2-AR desensitization documented in chronic agonist exposure
- Quercetin increases β2-AR density in vitro (Zhang 2020)

**Protocol:**
- Dose: 500mg quercetin phytosome (enhanced bioavailability) BID
- Duration: 12 weeks
- Target: β2-AAb-positive ME/CFS
- Monitor: HRV, COMPASS-31, standing heart rate
- Contra: kidney stones, quinolone antibiotics (decreases absorption)

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- β2-AAb-positive patients show greater HRV improvement on quercetin vs placebo.
- β2-AAb titers unchanged (mechanism is receptor resensitization, not immune modulation).
- No benefit in β2-AAb-negative patients.

---

### 4.2 Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA/DHA) for FcγR Modulation (Certainty: 0.52)

**Mechanism:** EPA/DHA incorporates into immune cell membranes, altering lipid raft composition and reducing FcγR signaling affinity. May dampen FcγR-mediated cytokine production triggered by GPCR AAbs.

**Rationale from data:**
- GPCR AAbs may activate FcγRs (Hackel 2025)
- Omega-3 reduces FcγR expression on monocytes (Calder 2015)
- Anti-inflammatory effects documented in autoimmune conditions

**Protocol:**
- Dose: EPA 2g + DHA 1g daily (high-dose prescription fish oil)
- Duration: 24 weeks
- Target: β2-AAb-positive ME/CFS with elevated IL-6
- Monitor: IL-6, TNFα, COMPASS-31
- Check: Omega-3 index (target >8%)

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- β2-AAb-positive patients with high IL-6 show greater IL-6 reduction on omega-3 vs placebo.
- FcγR expression (CD64 on monocytes) decreases with omega-3 supplementation.
- Symptom improvement correlates with omega-3 index increase.

---

### 4.3 N-Acetylcysteine (NAC) for GPCR AAb Bystander Suppression (Certainty: 0.45)

**Mechanism:** NAC increases glutathione, shifts redox balance toward reduced state. Autoantibody production (including GPCR AAbs) is redox-sensitive; oxidative stress promotes B cell activation. NAC may suppress aberrant B cell activity and reduce GPCR AAb generation.

**Rationale from data:**
- Oxidative stress elevated in ME/CFS (Naviaux 2016)
- B cell activation documented in ME/CFS (Landi 2016)
- NAC reduces autoantibody production in lupus models

**Protocol:**
- Dose: 600mg NAC TID
- Duration: 16 weeks
- Target: ME/CFS with any GPCR AAb elevation
- Monitor: GPCR AAb titers, glutathione, fatigue severity
- Contra: asthma exacerbation (rare paradoxical bronchospasm)

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- GPCR AAb titers decrease more on NAC vs placebo.
- AAb reduction correlates with glutathione increase.
- Fatigue severity improvement correlates with AAb reduction.

---

### 4.4 Vitamin D Repletion for GPCR AAb Immune Tolerance (Certainty: 0.50)

**Mechanism:** Vitamin D induces regulatory T cells (Tregs) and promotes B cell tolerance. Low vitamin D is common in ME/CFS. Repletion may reduce GPCR AAb production by restoring immune regulation.

**Rationale from data:**
- Vitamin D deficiency prevalent in ME/CFS (Tague 2020)
- Vitamin D reduces autoantibodies in other autoimmune diseases
- Seasonal variation in ME/CFS symptom severity reported

**Protocol:**
- Dose: 5000 IU vitamin D3 daily (if 25-OH-D <30 ng/mL)
- Duration: 6 months
- Target: ME/CFS with vitamin D deficiency + any GPCR AAb elevation
- Monitor: 25-OH-D, GPCR AAb titers, fatigue severity
- Goal: 25-OH-D 50-80 ng/mL

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- Vitamin D repletion to >50 ng/mL reduces GPCR AAb titers vs placebo.
- AAb reduction correlates with Treg increase (CD4+CD25+FoxP3+).
- Symptom improvement is greater in AAb-positive vs AAb-negative patients.

---

### 4.5 Probiotic Strain (Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG) for Vagal Afferent Sensitization (Certainty: 0.35)

**Mechanism:** L. rhamnosus GG produces acetylcholine and upregulates M3 mAChR expression on vagal afferents via gut-brain axis. May counteract M3 AAb-induced vagal desensitization (Hypothesis 1.3), restoring parasympathetic signaling.

**Rationale from data:**
- PCC shows borderline/pathological M3 results (Azcue 2026)
- Gut dysbiosis documented in ME/CFS (Giloteaux 2016)
- L. rhamnosus GG increases vagal tone in preclinical models (Bravo 2011)

**Protocol:**
- Dose: 10¹⁰ CFU L. rhamnosus GG daily
- Duration: 12 weeks
- Target: ME/CFS with M3 AAb elevation + autonomic symptoms
- Monitor: HRV, baroreflex sensitivity, COMPASS-31, microbiome composition
- Contra: severe immunocompromise (risk of bacteremia)

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- M3-AAb-positive patients show greater HRV improvement on L. rhamnosus GG vs placebo.
- Baroreflex sensitivity increases only in M3-AAb-positive patients.
- Microbiome shift (increased Lactobacillus abundance) correlates with autonomic improvement.

## Category 5: Non-Pharmacological Interventions

### 5.1 HRV-Biofeedback Pacing for β2-AAb-Positive Patients (Certainty: 0.62)

**Mechanism:** Slow-paced breathing (6 breaths/min) maximizes respiratory sinus arrhythmia, training parasympathetic tone. For β2-AAb-positive patients with sympathovagal imbalance (Azcue 2026), HRV-biofeedback may counterbalance sympathetic dominance and improve autonomic resilience.

**Rationale from data:**
- β2-AAb correlated with sympathovagal imbalance (r=0.45, p=0.001)
- HRV reduced in ME/CFS
- HRV-biofeedback shows efficacy in POTS (Lehrer 2020)

**Protocol:**
- Device: smartphone HRV-biofeedback app (e.g., Elite HRV) or dedicated device
- Frequency: 10 minutes daily, 5 days/week
- Technique: resonant frequency breathing (5.5-6.5 breaths/min)
- Duration: 12 weeks
- Target: β2-AAb-positive ME/CFS
- Monitor: RMSSD, LF/HF ratio, COMPASS-31, PEM frequency

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- β2-AAb-positive patients show greater HRV improvement (RMSSD increase) vs β2-AAb-negative patients.
- HRV improvement correlates with COMPASS-31 reduction.
- PEM frequency decreases only in patients achieving HRV target (>50 ms RMSSD).

---

### 5.2 Vagus Nerve Stimulation (tVNS) for M3 AAb-Induced Vagal Blind Spot (Certainty: 0.55)

**Mechanism:** Transcutaneous vagus nerve stimulation (auricular tVNS) directly activates vagal afferents, bypassing M3 AAb-desensitized receptors. May restore parasympathetic signaling from brainstem to periphery in M3-AAb-positive patients.

**Rationale from data:**
- M3 AAb borderline/pathological in PCC (Azcue 2026)
- Autonomic dysfunction in ME/CFS (reduced HRV, POTS)
- tVNS efficacy in POTS and dysautonomia (Yuan 2022)

**Protocol:**
- Device: FDA-cleared tVNS (e.g., gammaCore, Nemos)
- Parameters: 25 Hz, 250μs, 5 min/day
- Duration: 8 weeks
- Target: ME/CFS with M3 AAb elevation + orthostatic intolerance
- Monitor: HRV, tilt-table response, COMPASS-31
- Contra: vagotomy, cardiac pacemaker, carotid sinus hypersensitivity

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- M3-AAb-positive patients show greater orthostatic tolerance improvement on tVNS vs placebo.
- HRV (RMSSD) increases more in M3-AAb-positive vs M3-AAb-negative patients.
- tVNS response correlates with M3 AAb titer (higher titers = more benefit).

---

### 5.3 Isometric Handgrip Training for Sympathetic Resensitization (Certainty: 0.48)

**Mechanism:** Isometric handgrip (IHG) at 30% MVC for 2 minutes increases sympathetic outflow via muscle metaboreflex. Repeated IHG may resensitize β2-AR to catecholamines after desensitization by β2-AAbs, improving chronotropic response.

**Rationale from data:**
- β2-AAb elevated in ME/CFS (Azcue 2026)
- Chronotropic incompetence documented in ME/CFS
- IHG improves sympathetic responsiveness in autonomic failure

**Protocol:**
- Exercise: IHG at 30% MVC, 2 min × 4 sets, 3×/week
- Duration: 12 weeks
- Target: β2-AAb-positive ME/CFS with chronotropic incompetence
- Monitor: heart rate response to submaximal treadmill, HRV, fatigue
- Contra: uncontrolled hypertension, recent MI

**Falsifiable prediction:**
- β2-AAb-positive patients show greater heart rate response improvement vs β2-AAb-negative patients.
- HRV (LF power) increases with IHG training only in β2-AAb-positive group.
- Exercise tolerance (6MWT distance) improves more in β2-AAb-positive patients.

---

### 5.4 Cognitive Remediation with M1/M4 AAb Stratification (Certainty: 0.50)

**Mechanism:** M1/M4 AAb positively correlated with cognition (Azcue 2026). Patients with low M1/M4 AAb titers may benefit more from cognitive remediation (working memory training) because they lack the AAb-mediated neuroprotection.

**Rationale from data:**
- M1/M4 AAb positively correlated with verbal/working memory
- Cognitive deficits documented in ME/CFS
- Cognitive remediation shows efficacy in other fatigue conditions

**Protocol:**
- Intervention: computerized working memory training (n-back, dual n-back) 30 min/day, 5×/week
- Duration: 12 weeks
- Stratification: compare low vs high M1/M4 AAb titers
- Monitor: working memory (n-back), verbal memory, executive function

## Category 7: Mathematical Model Extensions

### 7.1 GPCR AAb Dynamics in ODE Model (Certainty: 0.65)

**New variables to extend existing ME/CFS ODE model:**

- **A_ab(t)**: Autoantibody concentration (β2, M1, M3, M4 subtypes)
- **B_plasma(t)**: Plasma cell count producing GPCR AAbs
- **B_memory(t)**: Memory B cell count for GPCR AAbs
- **R_sens(t)**: Sensitive (non-desensitized) receptor fraction (β2-AR, mAChR)
- **R_desens(t)**: Desensitized receptor fraction
- **HRV(t)**: Heart rate variability (autonomic function)
- **C(t)**: Cognitive performance (working memory)

**Key equations:**

1. **AAb production:**
   ```
   dA_ab/dt = α * B_plasma - δ_A * A_ab - γ * A_ab * R_sens (binding to receptors)
   ```

2. **Receptor desensitization (β2-AR example):**
   ```
   dR_sens/dt = -κ_bind * A_ab * R_sens + κ_resens * R_desens
   dR_desens/dt = κ_bind * A_ab * R_sens - κ_resens * R_desens
   ```

3. **HRV dynamics:**
   ```
   dHRV/dt = θ_sens * R_sens - θ_desens * R_desens - λ_aut * HRV (autonomic degradation)
   ```

4. **Cognitive dynamics (muscarinic):**
   ```
   dC/dt = θ_cog * (A_ab + A_ab₀) * (1 - C/K_max) - λ_cog * C
   ```
   Where A_ab₀ is baseline (protective) level; captures inverted-U relationship.

5. **B cell dynamics:**
   ```
   dB_plasma/dt = ω_act * B_memory - δ_plasma * B_plasma
   dB_memory/dt = φ_act * B_naive - δ_mem * B_memory
   ```

**Parameters:**
- α: AAb production rate per plasma cell
- δ_A: AAb clearance rate
- γ: AAb-receptor binding rate
- κ_bind: receptor desensitization rate per AAb
- κ_resens: receptor resensitization rate
- θ_sens: autonomic function per sensitive receptor
- θ_desens: autonomic dysfunction per desensitized receptor
- θ_cog: cognitive enhancement per muscarinic AAb
- λ_aut: autonomic degradation rate
- λ_cog: cognitive degradation rate

**Predictions from model:**
- Steady-state AAb levels depend on B memory cell pool (long-term biomarker)
- HRV shows hysteresis: slow recovery after AAb clearance (receptor resensitization lag)
- Cognitive performance shows bistability: low and high stable states at different AAb titers (explains counterintuitive correlation)
- Immunoadsorption reduces A_ab rapidly, but HRV recovery follows exponential (κ_resens-limited)
- Isotype shift (IgG4→IgG1) modeled as increase in δ_A (IgG1 cleared faster than IgG4)

**Falsifiable model outputs:**
- Time course of HRV recovery after immunoadsorption follows exponential with τ ≈ 1/κ_resens
- Cognitive performance vs AAb titer curve is sigmoidal (inverted-U around threshold)
- B memory cell half-life predicts AAb relapse probability after remission

### 7.2 Causal DAG Extension with GPCR AAb Nodes (Certainty: 0.72)

3. **AAb_IgG4 → Receptor_Desens**

9. **Sex → Estrogen_Level → GPCR_AAb** (mediated pathway)

**Causal queries from DAG:**

1. **Total effect of GPCR_AAb on Autonomic_Dysfunction:**

### 7.3 Inverted-U Cognitive Function Model (Hypothesis 1.2) (Certainty: 0.58)

**Model structure:** Captures counterintuitive positive correlation between M1/M4 AAbs and cognition (Azcue 2026).

**Equation for cognitive performance (C) vs muscarinic AAb titer (M):**
```
C(M) = C₀ + ΔC_max * (M / (M + M_half)) * (1 - M / M_toxic)
```

Where:
- C₀: baseline cognition (without AAbs)
- ΔC_max: maximum cognitive enhancement
- M_half: AAb titer at half-maximal enhancement
- M_toxic: AAb titer where enhancement becomes negative (toxic)
- If M > M_toxic, C(M) declines

**Key features:**
- At M=0: C = C₀
- At M = M_half: C = C₀ + ΔC_max/2
- At M = M_optimal: C peaks (derivative dC/dM = 0)
- At M = M_toxic: C = C₀ (enhancement cancels toxicity)
- For M > M_toxic: C < C₀ (neurotoxicity)

**Biological interpretation:**
- Low-to-moderate M1/M4 AAbs: enhance CB1 cross-activation → neuroprotection
- High M1/M4 AAbs: cause receptor overstimulation → excitotoxicity

**Parameter estimation from data:**
- Fit to Azcue 2026: M1, M4 vs verbal/working memory
- M_half ~ 30-40th percentile of ME/CFS AAb distribution
- M_optimal ~ 60-70th percentile (where correlation positive)
- M_toxic ~ 90th percentile (where negative correlation would appear)

**Predictions:**
1. **Bimodal distribution:** Cognitive performance vs M1/M4 AAb titers shows inverted-U
2. **Stratified analysis:** High-titer patients show worse cognition than low-titer (not better)
3. **CB1 dependence:** Blocking CB1 shifts curve left (M_optimal decreases) → lower AAb titers needed for protection

**Falsifiable outputs:**
- Scatter plot of cognitive score vs M1/M4 titer shows quadratic (inverted-U) relationship (R² quadratic > R² linear)
- High-titer (>90th percentile) patients have lower cognition than low-titer (<30th percentile)
- CB1 antagonist (rimonabant) reduces cognitive performance in mid-range titer patients but not low/high

### 7.4 Isotype-Switch Model (Hypothesis 1.4) (Certainty: 0.52)

**Variables:**
- **A_IgG4(t)**: IgG4-dominant AAb concentration
- **A_IgG1(t)**: IgG1-dominant AAb concentration
- **B_IgG4(t)**: IgG4-producing plasma cells
- **B_IgG1(t)**: IgG1-producing plasma cells
- **T_IL21(t)**: IL-21 cytokine level (drives class switching)
- **IFN_γ(t)**: IFN-γ level (IgG1 promotion)

**Equations:**
1. **IgG4 dynamics:**
   ```
   dA_IgG4/dt = α_IgG4 * B_IgG4 - δ_IgG4 * A_IgG4

2. **IgG1 dynamics:**
   ```
   dA_IgG1/dt = α_IgG1 * B_IgG1 - δ_IgG1 * A_IgG1

3. **Cytokine dynamics:**

   dT_IL21/dt = φ_IL21 * A_IgG4 - δ_IL21 * T_IL21

4. **Total AAb (detected by ELISA):**

5. **REAP/Luminex detection (biased):**
   ```
   A_Luminex(t) = η_IgG4 * A_IgG4(t) + η_IgG1 * A_IgG1(t)
   ```

**Key parameters:**
- ω_switch_IgG4: IL-21-driven switching to IgG4
- ω_switch_IgG1: IFN-γ-driven switching to IgG1
- ω_convert: conversion rate IgG4→IgG1 (disease progression)
- η_IgG4, η_IgG1: assay detection efficiency

**Predictions:**
1. **Time course:** Early PCC: A_IgG4 >> A_IgG1; Late ME/CFS: A_IgG1 >> A_IgG4

3. **Symptom correlation:** A_IgG1 correlates with severity; A_IgG4 does not
4. **Infection trigger:** Acute infection (COVID-19) spikes IFN_γ → accelerates IgG1 switching

**Falsifiable model outputs:**
- Longitudinal data: A_IgG4/A_IgG1 ratio decreases over time in ME/CFS converters
- Regression: Severity ~ A_IgG1 (not A_IgG4) when both isotypes measured
- REAP/Luminex: η_IgG1 < 0.2 * η_IgG4 (detection efficiency difference explains null results)

### 7.5 PEM Trigger Model with GPCR AAb Threshold (Certainty: 0.60)

**Model structure:** PEM as threshold phenomenon triggered when autonomic demand exceeds receptor reserve.

**Variables:**
- **Demand(t)**: Autonomic demand (e.g., from exercise, stress)
- **R_reserve(t)**: Receptor reserve (functional β2-AR, not desensitized)
- **PEM_state(t)**: Binary (0=normal, 1=PEM)
- **Recovery(t)**: Recovery progress from PEM

**Equations:**
1. **Receptor reserve dynamics:**
   ```
   dR_reserve/dt = κ_prod - κ_AAb * A_ab(t) * R_reserve - κ_desens * Demand(t) * R_reserve
   ```
   Where:
   - κ_prod: basal receptor production
   - κ_AAb: AAb-induced desensitization rate
   - κ_desens: demand-induced desensitization

2. **PEM trigger:**
   ```
   PEM_state(t) = 1 if Demand(t) > R_reserve(t) * θ_threshold else 0
   ```
   Where θ_threshold is safety factor (<1, e.g., 0.7)

3. **Recovery dynamics:**
   ```
   dRecovery/dt = κ_rec * (1 - PEM_state(t)) - κ_trigger * PEM_state(t)
   ```
   Where:
   - κ_rec: recovery rate when not in PEM
   - κ_trigger: setback rate when in PEM

4. **Demand function:**
   ```
   Demand(t) = D_exercise(t) + D_stress(t) + D_infection(t)
   ```

**Key parameters:**
- κ_AAb: AAb sensitivity (higher in β2-AAb-positive patients)
- θ_threshold: lower in ME/CFS (reduced autonomic reserve)
- κ_rec: recovery rate (lower in ME/CFS)

**Predictions:**
1. **PEM threshold:** β2-AAb-positive patients have lower θ_threshold (easier to trigger PEM)
2. **Hysteresis:** Once PEM triggered, recovery slower than onset (κ_rec < κ_trigger * factor)
3. **Immunoadsorption effect:** Reducing A_ab increases R_reserve → raises θ_threshold → PEM harder to trigger
4. **Pacing benefit:** Reducing Demand(t) keeps it below R_reserve * θ_threshold → prevents PEM

**Falsifiable model outputs:**
- CPET test: β2-AAb-positive patients reach PEM at lower work rate than β2-AAb-negative
- Immunoadsorption: θ_threshold increases post-treatment (measured by submaximal exercise tolerance)
- Pacing: Demand-reduction protocol reduces PEM frequency proportionally to θ_threshold increase

## Category 8: Cross-Disease Bridges

### 8.1 CRPS GPCR AAb Pathogenesis → ME/CFS Analog (Certainty: 0.72)

**Parallel:** Blaes 2011 demonstrated functionally active β2/M2 AAbs in CRPS (complex regional pain syndrome), causing autonomic and sensory dysfunction. CRPS shares small fiber neuropathy, autonomic dysregulation, and hyperalgesia with ME/CFS.

**Mechanistic bridge:**
- CRPS: β2/M2 AAbs activate sympathetic neurons → aberrant catecholamine release → sweating abnormalities, pain
- ME/CFS: β2 AAbs desensitize β2-AR → sympathetic insufficiency → reduced HRV, POTS
- Convergence: Both involve β2-AAb-induced autonomic dysregulation, but via different mechanisms (activation vs desensitization)

**Testable ME/CFS predictions:**
1. **Small fiber neuropathy marker:** β2-AAb-positive ME/CFS patients show reduced intraepidermal nerve fiber density (IEND) similar to CRPS
2. **Sweating abnormalities:** Quantitative sudomotor axon reflex test (QSART) shows hyperhidrosis in CRPS, hypohidrosis in ME/CFS (opposite due to activation vs desensitization)
3. **Therapeutic cross-prediction:** Immunoadsorption effective in both (Stein 2025 for ME/CFS; Goebel 2018 for CRPS)

**Falsifiable tests:**
- Measure IEND in β2-AAb-positive vs -negative ME/CFS: positive patients show IEND loss
- Compare QSART patterns: CRPS=hyperhidrosis, ME/CFS=hypohidrosis
- Double-blind RCT: Immunoadsorption improves both IEND and autonomic measures in ME/CFS

**Concept:** Combine GPCR AAb titers (β2, M1, M3, M4) with autonomic testing to define ME/CFS subtypes with tailored treatment pathways.

**Algorithm:**
1. **Measure GPCR AAb titers:** ELISA for β2, M1, M3, M4
2. **Autonomic phenotyping:** HRV, Sudoscan, tilt-table, COMPASS-31
3. **Cognitive testing:** 7 domains (as Azcue 2026)
4. **Stratify into 4 subtypes:**
   - **Type A (β2-dominant):** High β2-AAb, reduced HRV, POTS → treat with low-dose propranolol + immunoadsorption
   - **Type B (M3-dominant):** High M3-AAb, orthostatic intolerance, vagal dysfunction → treat with tVNS + vagal retraining
   - **Type C (M1/M4-low):** Low M1/M4-AAb, cognitive impairment → treat with cognitive remediation + CB1 agonist
   - **Type D (negative):** No AAb elevation → alternative pathway (immune activation, metabolic)

**Clinical utility:**
- Predicts treatment response (e.g., Type A responds to propranolol, Type B to tVNS)
- Avoids unnecessary immunoadsorption in Type D (negative)
- Guides monitoring (Type A: HRV; Type B: baroreflex; Type C: cognitive)

**Falsifiable validation:**
- Prospective cohort: 200 ME/CFS, stratified, treated per algorithm
- Outcome: Primary endpoint (symptom severity) improves more in matched treatment vs mismatched
- Prediction: Type A on propranolol > Type A on tVNS; Type B on tVNS > Type B on propranolol

### 9.2 GPCR AAb Isotype Ratio as Disease Progression Marker (Certainty: 0.68)

**Concept:** IgG1/IgG4 ratio for GPCR AAbs tracks disease stage and predicts conversion from PCC to ME/CFS.

**Measurement:**
- Isotype-specific ELISA: Measure IgG1 and IgG4 fractions for β2, M1, M3, M4 AAbs
- Calculate ratio: R = [AAb_IgG1] / [AAb_IgG4]

**Interpretation:**
- **R < 0.5:** Early stage (IgG4-dominant), PCC or recent-onset ME/CFS
- **0.5 ≤ R ≤ 2:** Transition stage, risk of ME/CFS conversion
- **R > 2:** Late stage (IgG1-dominant), established ME/CFS

**Clinical utility:**
- Identifies PCC patients at risk of ME/CFS conversion (R crossing threshold)
- Guides treatment intensity: Low R (early) → pacing + supplements; High R (late) → immunoadsorption + biologics
- Monitors treatment response: Immunoadsorption reduces IgG1 → R decreases

**Falsifiable validation:**
- Longitudinal PCC cohort: Measure R at 1, 3, 6, 12 months
- Prediction: Patients who convert to ME/CFS show R increase >1 at 3 months
- Treatment: Immunoadsorption reduces R >50% in responders

### 9.3 Composite Autonomic-Immune Score (CAIS) (Certainty: 0.72)

**Concept:** Combine GPCR AAb titers, HRV parameters, and cytokine levels into single score for ME/CFS severity and treatment monitoring.

**Formula:**
```
CAIS = w1 * Z(AAb_total) + w2 * Z(1/HRV_RMSSD) + w3 * Z(IL6/TNFα)
```
Where:
- Z = z-score (normalized to healthy controls)
- AAb_total = sum of β2, M1, M3, M4 titers (normalized)
- HRV_RMSSD = RMSSD in ms (lower = worse)
- IL6/TNFα = composite cytokine score
- w1, w2, w3 = weights (determined by regression to symptom severity)

**Interpretation:**
- **CAIS < 0:** Mild ME/CFS
- **0 ≤ CAIS ≤ 1:** Moderate ME/CFS
- **CAIS > 1:** Severe ME/CFS

**Clinical utility:**
- Single number tracks disease severity over time
- Predicts treatment response: CAIS reduction >0.3 = clinical response
- Stratifies for clinical trials: Enrich for CAIS > 0.5 (moderate-severe)

**Falsifiable validation:**
- Correlate CAIS with symptom severity (SF-36, fatigue scale): r > 0.7
- Predict treatment response: CAIS change predicts symptom improvement (R² > 0.5)
- Longitudinal: CAIS tracks symptom trajectory over 12 months

### 9.5 FcγR Activation Assay as Pathogenicity Marker (Certainty: 0.65)

**Concept:** Functional assay measuring FcγR activation by GPCR AAbs to distinguish pathogenic vs non-pathogenic AAbs.

**Protocol:**
1. Isolate patient IgG (protein G purification)
2. Incubate with monocyte cell line (THP-1) expressing FcγRs
3. Measure FcγR engagement (flow cytometry: CD64, CD32, CD16 binding)
4. Measure cytokine production (TNFα, IL-6) with/without FcγR blockade (IV.3 antibody)
5. Score: Pathogenic if FcγR activation >2× HC and blocked by IV.3

**Interpretation:**
- **Pathogenic:** FcγR activation present → supports Germain 2025 concern (ELISA detects non-pathogenic AAbs)
- **Non-pathogenic:** No FcγR activation → AAbs may be epiphenomena
- **Hybrid:** Some AAbs pathogenic (e.g., β2-IgG1), others not (β2-IgG4)

**Clinical utility:**
- Identifies patients likely to respond to immunoadsorption (pathogenic AAbs)
- Guides therapy: Pathogenic = immunoadsorption/FcγR blockade; Non-pathogenic = symptomatic management
- Research: Resolves ELISA vs Luminex conflict (Germain null = no pathogenic AAbs detected)

**Falsifiable validation:**
- Correlate FcγR activation with symptom severity: r > 0.5
- Immunoadsorption reduces FcγR activation in responders
- FcγR activation predicts treatment response (AUROC > 0.8)

### 9.6 HRV-Receptor Resensitization Lag as Treatment Marker (Certainty: 0.55)

**Concept:** Measure lag between β2-AAb clearance (immunoadsorption) and HRV recovery to identify patients needing longer autonomic retraining.

**Measurement:**
1. Baseline: β2-AAb titer, HRV (RMSSD)
2. Post-immunoadsorption (day 0): β2-AAb titer, HRV
3. Follow-up: HRV weekly × 8 weeks
4. Calculate lag: Time to reach 80% of target HRV (determined by HCs)

**Interpretation:**
- **Lag < 2 weeks:** Fast responders, minimal retraining needed
- **2-4 weeks:** Moderate responders, standard retraining (HRV-biofeedback)
- **>4 weeks:** Slow responders, intensive retraining (IHG + tVNS)

**Clinical utility:**
- Tailors autonomic retraining intensity
- Predicts relapse risk: Slow lag = higher relapse probability
- Mechanistic insight: Lag reflects κ_resens (receptor resensitization rate) from ODE model

**Falsifiable validation:**
- Lag correlates with baseline β2-AAb titer (higher titer = longer lag)
- Slow responders (>4 weeks) benefit more from intensive retraining vs standard
- ODE model predicts lag: τ_lag ≈ 1/κ_resens (fit to patient data)

### 9.7 Cognitive-Muscarinic Threshold Model for Treatment Selection (Certainty: 0.58)

**Concept:** Use M1/M4 AAb titers and cognitive scores to select patients for cognitive remediation vs pharmacologic enhancement.

**Algorithm:**
1. Measure M1, M4 AAb titers (CellTrend ELISA)
2. Measure working memory (n-back accuracy)
3. Plot on 2D grid: AAb titer (x-axis) vs cognitive score (y-axis)
4. Define zones:
   - **Zone 1 (low AAb, low cognition):** AAb < 40th percentile, cognition < 30th percentile → cognitive remediation
   - **Zone 2 (moderate AAb, moderate cognition):** AAb 40-70th percentile, cognition 30-60th percentile → observation (AAb protective)
   - **Zone 3 (high AAb, high cognition):** AAb > 70th percentile, cognition > 60th percentile → observation (maintain)
   - **Zone 4 (high AAb, low cognition):** AAb > 70th percentile, cognition < 30th percentile → inverted-U toxic phase → CB1 agonist or M1/M4 PAM

**Clinical utility:**
- Avoids unnecessary cognitive training in Zone 2/3 (AAb already protective)
- Targets Zone 1 for cognitive remediation (maximal benefit)
- Identifies Zone 4 for pharmacologic intervention (toxic phase)

**Falsifiable validation:**
- Zone 1: Cognitive remediation improves working memory >30% vs observation
- Zone 2: Remediation shows no benefit vs observation (ceiling effect)
- Zone 4: CB1 agonist improves cognition vs observation (rescues toxic phase)
