# Brainstorming: Salivary Metabolomic Biomarkers of Sleep Deprivation and ME/CFS Unrefreshing Sleep

**Date:** 2026-06-04
**Research Stream:** sleep-deprivation-saliva
**Phase 2: Scientific Insight Generation**

---

## Executive Summary

This document synthesizes Phase 1 literature findings with the existing ME/CFS paper to generate creative, evidence-grounded hypotheses. The core challenge is bridging two critical gaps:

1. **Acute-vs-chronic dissociation**: Scholz 2026 shows 24h acute sleep deprivation produces detectable salivary metabolic fingerprint (94% accuracy), but 4-night chronic sleep restriction produces NO exploitable metabolic changes.

2. **Subjective-objective disconnect**: ME/CFS patients report 91-100% unrefreshing sleep prevalence, yet PSG shows near-normal sleep architecture.

Ideas that bridge these gaps are prioritized.

## Novel Hypotheses

### 1. Metabolic Sleep Disorder Hypothesis

**Category:** Novel Hypotheses
**Tier:** 1
**Certainty:** 0.60

**Mechanistic Rationale:**
Unrefreshing sleep in ME/CFS represents a *qualitatively different metabolic state* than both acute sleep deprivation and chronic sleep restriction. The metabolic fingerprint may show a *reversed* pattern where acute deprivation metabolites are paradoxically decreased or absent, while chronic restriction metabolites remain normal.

**Evidence Links:**
- Scholz 2026: 4-night restriction produces no metabolic changes
- Oishi 2025: Chronic poor sleep produces different metabolites (2HB +36%) than acute deprivation
- Aho 2016: Chronic restriction produces cholesterol/inflammatory changes, different from acute deprivation

**Falsifiable Prediction:**
ME/CFS patients will show (a) absence of acute deprivation metabolites (Scholz's 12 features), (b) absence of chronic restriction metabolites (Oishi's 2HB pattern), AND (c) presence of a third, distinct metabolomic signature correlating with subjective unrefreshing sleep.

### 2. Salivary Circadian Phase Locking Failure Hypothesis

**Category:** Novel Hypotheses
**Tier:** 1
**Certainty:** 0.55

**Mechanistic Rationale:**
ME/CFS unrefreshing sleep results from *circadian metabolite phase locking failure*. Scholz 2026 found 7/12 acute deprivation features show 24h rhythmicity. In ME/CFS, these circadian-phase metabolites may remain *out of phase* with sleep, causing the brain to interpret sleep as "non-restorative" despite normal architecture.

**Evidence Links:**
- Scholz 2026: 7/12 features exhibit 24h circadian rhythmicity
- ME/CFS paper: orexin deficiency, glymphatic impairment proposed mechanisms
- SleepFM 2026: AI detection of cross-modal sleep decoupling

**Falsifiable Prediction:**
ME/CFS patients will show normal concentrations of circadian metabolites but with *phase-shifted timing* relative to sleep onset/wake time compared to healthy controls. Salivary samples collected every 2-4h will reveal delayed/advanced peaks.

### 3. Salivary Glymphatic Clearance Signature Hypothesis

**Category:** Novel Hypotheses
**Tier:** 1
**Certainty:** 0.50

**Mechanistic Rationale:**
Salivary metabolites reflect *glymphatic clearance efficiency*. During normal sleep, brain metabolites (e.g., amyloid-beta, tau) are cleared via glymphatic system and may appear in saliva. ME/CFS patients may show *reduced clearance signature* — lower morning levels of brain-derived metabolites — despite normal sleep architecture.

**Evidence Links:**
- ME/CFS paper: Glymphatic impairment proposed mechanism
- ME/CFS paper: Exosome HSP70 proposed as sleep phenotype biomarker
- Davies 2014: 171 metabolites altered by acute deprivation (many brain-derived)
- Normal PSG despite unrefreshing sleep (Maksoud 2021)

**Falsifiable Prediction:**
ME/CFS patients will show reduced morning (08:00-12:00) salivary levels of brain-derived metabolites (e.g., neurotransmitter breakdown products, lipophilic compounds) compared to healthy controls, while evening levels are similar.

### 4. Chronic Low-Grade Sleep Restriction Hypothesis

**Category:** Novel Hypotheses
**Tier:** 2
**Certainty:** 0.45

**Mechanistic Rationale:**
ME/CFS patients experience *subtle, chronic sleep restriction below PSG detection threshold* — micro-arousals or sleep fragmentation that don't register as "reduced sleep time" but accumulate to chronic sleep debt. This produces a "stalled" metabolic signature: too advanced for normal sleep, too mild for acute deprivation.

**Evidence Links:**
- Maksoud 2021: Increased micro-arousal index in all 5 ME/CFS studies
- Scholz 2026: 4-night restriction at 6h produces no signal
- Davies 2014: 171 metabolites altered by acute deprivation
- Subjective-objective disconnect (Maksoud 2021)

**Falsifiable Prediction:**
ME/CFS patients will show *intermediate* metabolic signatures: metabolite levels between acute deprivation and rested states. Correlation with high-frequency EEG spectral power (30-80Hz) during sleep, not standard sleep architecture metrics.

---

### 5. Metabolic Signature Hysteresis Hypothesis

**Category:** Novel Hypotheses
**Tier:** 2
**Certainty:** 0.40

**Mechanistic Rationale:**
ME/CFS patients exhibit *metabolic hysteresis* — metabolic fingerprints fail to reset after sleep, remaining in a "post-deprivation" state despite normal sleep architecture. This creates a persistent unrefreshing sleep perception: the brain detects the metabolic mismatch.

**Evidence Links:**
- Jason 2021: Salivary fatigue biomarker distinguishes severe ME/CFS
- Scholz 2026: Acute deprivation fingerprint clears after normal sleep (implied)
- Chronic unrefreshing sleep in ME/CFS (95-100% prevalence)

**Falsifiable Prediction:**
ME/CFS patients will show acute deprivation-like metabolic signatures *at all times* regardless of recent sleep quality. Acute sleep challenge (24h deprivation) will produce *minimal additional metabolic changes* in ME/CFS vs. large changes in controls.

## Research Directions

### 6. Circadian Metabolite Phase Mapping Study

**Category:** Research Directions
**Tier:** 1
**Certainty:** 0.70

**Mechanistic Rationale:**
Test the Circadian Phase Locking Failure hypothesis using dense time-series sampling. Scholz 2026 found time-dependent fingerprint (stronger 08:00-12:00); this design leverages that finding.

**Evidence Links:**
- Scholz 2026: Time-dependent fingerprint, 7/12 features rhythmic
- Jason 2021: Salivary fatigue biomarker correlates with fatigue severity

**Falsifiable Prediction:**
ME/CFS patients show phase-shifted circadian metabolite peaks relative to sleep/wake times. Cross-correlation analysis of metabolite levels vs. sleep timing will reveal significant phase delays (≥2h) in ME/CFS.

**Study Design:**
- Sample: 50 ME/CFS, 50 healthy controls
- Collection: Saliva every 2h for 48h (home collection, notched cards)
- Analysis: LC-HRMS for Scholz's 12 features + Oishi's 13 metabolites
- Timing: Fixed sleep schedule enforced for 7 days pre-collection
- Primary outcome: Phase offset of circadian metabolites vs. sleep onset

### 29. Metabolic Hysteresis Model

**Category:** Mathematical Model Extensions
**Tier:** 2
**Certainty:** 0.55

**Mechanistic Rationale:**
Model metabolic fingerprint as state-dependent with hysteresis, explaining why normal sleep fails to reset metabolic state in ME/CFS.

**Evidence Links:**
- Metabolic Hysteresis hypothesis (Hypothesis 5)
- Scholz 2026: Acute fingerprint clears after normal sleep (healthy)
- ME/CFS: Persistent unrefreshing sleep

**Falsifiable Prediction:**
Hysteresis model predicts metabolite time-courses in ME/CFS vs. healthy controls with R² > 0.8. Hysteresis parameter (H) correlates with disease severity and unrefreshing sleep scores.

**Model Structure:**
```
dS/dt = α * (S_target - S) + H * f(previous states)
```
where:
- S = metabolic state vector
- S_target = target state for current sleep condition
- α = convergence rate
- H = hysteresis parameter (0 = no hysteresis, 1 = full hysteresis)
- f(previous states) = function of state history

**Parameters to Estimate:**
- Convergence rate (α) - may be slowed in ME/CFS
- Hysteresis parameter (H) - may be elevated in ME/CFS
- State history function (f)

**Applications:**
- Predicting time-course of metabolic responses
- Identifying ME/CFS subtypes (high vs. low hysteresis)
- Optimizing treatment timing (interventions during low-hysteresis windows)
