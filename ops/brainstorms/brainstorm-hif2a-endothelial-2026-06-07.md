# Brainstorm: HIF-2α-Mediated Endothelial Dysfunction as Post-Viral ME/CFS Mechanism

**Date:** 2026-06-07
**Context:** 16 papers (Ribeiro2026 anchor). Spike S1 → sustained HIF-2α → VEGF → barrier disruption. Belzutifan rescues. No direct ME/CFS HIF-2α data. ED confirmed in ME/CFS (Sandvik2023, Sørland2021) but mechanism in β2AR-negative ~70% unknown.

---

## 1. Novel Hypotheses

### H1.1: HIF-2α Persistence as "Molecular Memory" — Epigenetic Lock-In
**Mechanism:** Viral trigger (spike, EBV, HHV-6) → ROS → sustained HIF-2α via epigenetic modifications (HIF-2α promoter demethylation, H3K4me3 at HREs). Once established, HIF-2α autoregulates via positive feedback (EPAS1 has HRE in its own promoter), creating self-sustaining loop independent of initial trigger. This explains why viral clearance does not resolve ED.
**Evidence:** Ribeiro2026 (sustained HIF-2α persists beyond spike removal), Zhang2026 (oxidative stress → HIF switch), Deng2026 (IRF3 retention links viral signaling to HIF)
**Certainty:** 0.45
**Falsifiable:** HIF-2α ChIP-qPCR in post-viral ECs (spike-pulsed then 7d recovery) shows sustained promoter occupancy and activating histone marks vs control ECs
**Tier:** 2

### H1.2: β2AR-Negative ED Paradox — HIF-2α Replaces Autoantibody Vasoconstriction
**Mechanism:** Stein2025 — ~30% ME/CFS have β2AR autoantibodies → vasoconstriction. Remaining ~70% have ED (Sandvik2023) without known mechanism. HIF-2α → VEGF → microvascular barrier disruption → interstitial edema → impaired O2 diffusion → same net tissue hypoxia as vasoconstriction but via permeability rather than constriction. Two different proximal causes, same distal consequence.
**Evidence:** Ribeiro2026 (HIF-2α→VEGF→permeability), Stein2025 (30% autoantibody+, 70% unknown), Sandvik2023 (ED confirmed, no autoantibody measure), Wirth2024 (microvascular→mitochondrial cascade)
**Certainty:** 0.55
**Falsifiable:** β2AR-autoantibody-negative ME/CFS patients have elevated plasma VEGF, EPO, and vWF compared to β2AR-autoantibody-positive and healthy controls
**Tier:** 1

### H1.3: HIF-2α-Driven Immunothrombosis as Microclot Origin
**Mechanism:** HIF-2α → VEGF → Weibel-Palade body exocytosis → ultra-large vWF multimers → platelet adhesion + microclot formation. Provides direct mechanism for Kell-type microclots without requiring autoantibodies. Explains why microclots are found in post-COVID and post-viral ME/CFS.
**Evidence:** Ribeiro2026 (VEGF from HIF-2α), Wirth2024 (microclots + RBC deformation), Romanowska-Kocejko2025 (sustained microvascular impairment in long COVID)
**Certainty:** 0.50
**Falsifiable:** Plasma vWF antigen and vWF propeptide (marker of acute endothelial release) are elevated in post-viral ME/CFS and correlate with HIF-2α target gene expression (EDN1, VEGFA) and microclot burden
**Tier:** 2

### H1.4: NRF2 Exhaustion Permits HIF-2α Dominance in Post-Viral ECs
**Mechanism:** Acute viral infection depletes NRF2 antioxidant reserve (GSH consumption, lipid peroxidation). NRF2 normally competes with HIF-2α for transcriptional co-activators (CBP/p300) and maintains EC junction integrity (Shen2024). NRF2 exhaustion removes this brake, allowing unchecked HIF-2α activation. Bimodal: moderate stress → NRF2 protects; severe/prolonged stress → NRF2 depleted → HIF-2α runs unchecked.
**Evidence:** Shen2024 (NRF2-HIF2α axis protects EC junctions), Zhang2026 (oxidative stress governs HIF-1α vs HIF-2α dominance), Morris2025 (RSV stabilizes HIF under normoxia via ROS)
**Certainty:** 0.50
**Falsifiable:** NRF2 target genes (NQO1, HO-1, GCLC) are suppressed in post-viral ME/CFS ECs relative to HIF-2α targets; sulforaphane (NRF2 activator) rescues barrier integrity in spike-stimulated ECs
**Tier:** 2

### H1.5: Retinal ECs as Sentinel Window to Systemic/Cerebral Endothelium
**Mechanism:** Retinal ECs share developmental origin (neural crest) with brain ECs (BBB). Ribeiro2026 shows retinal EC vulnerability to spike→HIF-2α. Retinal microvascular changes may parallel BBB disruption, providing non-invasive window into cerebral microvascular health. "Eye as window to brain" applied to ED.
**Evidence:** Ribeiro2026 (retinal EC model), Pasupneti2020 (pulmonary EC-HIF-2α — different bed, same principle), Reiterer2019 (bed-specific HIF-2α effects)
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable:** Retinal capillary density (OCT angiography foveal avascular zone area) correlates with cognitive dysfunction (processing speed) and serum NfL in ME/CFS, and with BBB Ktrans on DCE-MRI
**Tier:** 2

### H1.6: Complement C3 Deposition via HIF-2α/VEGF Permeability Amplifies Injury
**Mechanism:** HIF-2α→VEGF→EC permeability → complement C3 extravasates and deposits perivascularly → local C3a/C5a generation → immune cell recruitment → more ROS → further HIF-2α stabilization. Positive feedback loop explaining progressive post-viral trajectory.
**Evidence:** Ribeiro2026 (permeability from HIF-2α), no direct complement+HIF-2α studies (gap identified), general complement-mediated endothelial injury literature
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable:** C3d deposition on dermal/muscle capillary endothelium (biopsy) correlates with plasma VEGF levels in post-viral ME/CFS; belzutifan reduces C3d in vitro
**Tier:** 3

## 2. Research Directions

### R2.1: HIF-2α Immunohistochemistry on Skin/Muscle Biopsies in ME/CFS
**Design:** Cross-sectional, n=40 (20 post-viral onset ME/CFS, 10 gradual onset, 10 healthy controls). 4mm punch skin biopsy + optional vastus lateralis needle biopsy. IHC for nuclear HIF-2α, CD31 (EC marker), ZO-1/occludin (junctions), VEGF. Automated quantification.
**Primary:** Nuclear HIF-2α intensity in dermal/muscle ECs (post-viral vs controls)
**Secondary:** Junction protein intensity correlation with nuclear HIF-2α
**Certainty:** 0.55
**Falsifiable:** Nuclear HIF-2α in dermal ECs is ≥2-fold higher in post-viral ME/CFS vs controls, with inverse correlation to ZO-1 intensity (r < -0.5)
**Tier:** 1

### R2.2: Belzutifan Single-Dose PK/PD Challenge in Post-Viral ME/CFS
**Design:** Open-label, single 40mg dose belzutifan in n=10 post-viral ME/CFS (confirmed ED by FMD). Measure plasma VEGF, EPO, vWF, PAI-1, sFlt-1 at 0, 1, 2, 6, 24, 48h. Also retinal OCT-A and cognitive battery (N-back) at 0 and 6h.
**Primary:** ΔVEGF at 6h post-dose (expected: 30-50% reduction based on VHL data)
**Safety:** Hemoglobin, blood pressure monitoring
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable:** ≥8/10 patients show VEGF reduction >30% at 6h with concurrent improvement in retinal capillary density on OCT-A
**Tier:** 2

### R2.3: EBV/HHV-6 Infection Model for HIF-2α in Brain ECs
**Design:** In vitro. Infect hCMEC/D3 (human brain microvascular EC line) with EBV (B95-8 strain) or HHV-6A in normoxia (21% O2). Measure at 1, 3, 7, 14 days: nuclear HIF-1α/HIF-2α protein (fractionation + ELISA), target gene expression (VEGFA, EDN1, PDK1), TEER (barrier resistance), ZO-1/occludin IF, viral DNA copies (qPCR). Include belzutifan rescue arm.
**Certainty:** 0.55
**Falsifiable:** EBV/HHV-6 infection induces sustained HIF-2α nuclear localization in brain ECs from day 3-14, progressive TEER decline ≥30%, rescued by belzutifan from day 7
**Tier:** 1

### R2.4: Complement C5a + Spike Synergy for HIF-2α in ECs
**Design:** In vitro. HRECs or HUVECs stimulated with: (1) S1 spike alone, (2) C5a alone, (3) S1 + C5a, (4) S1 + C5a + belzutifan, (5) S1 + C5a + C5aR1 antagonist. Measure HIF-2α nuclear translocation (0-48h), ROS (DCFDA), VEGF secretion, TEER, junction protein IF.
**Primary:** Synergy index for HIF-2α (observed/expected under additivity)
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable:** C5a + spike synergistically increase HIF-2α beyond additive (synergy index >1.3); C5aR1 antagonist partially but not fully rescues barrier
**Tier:** 2

### R2.5: HIF-2α Dynamics During CPET in ME/CFS — Serial Nuclear Fraction
**Design:** n=20 ME/CFS (10 post-viral, 10 gradual onset), n=10 sedentary controls. Serial blood at baseline, peak exercise, 2h, 6h, 24h, 48h post-CPET. PBMC nuclear fraction for HIF-1α/HIF-2α protein. Also VEGF, EPO, vWF, sFlt-1, lactate.
**Primary:** HIF-2α nuclear accumulation trajectory (AUC 0-48h): post-viral vs gradual vs control
**Certainty:** 0.55
**Falsifiable:** Controls show transient HIF-1α nuclear peak at 0h post-CPET, returning to baseline by 2h; post-viral ME/CFS shows delayed HIF-2α nuclear rise at 24-48h post-CPET, persisting at 48h; gradual-onset ME/CFS shows intermediate pattern
**Tier:** 1

### R2.6: Retrospective Transcriptome Mining for HIF-2α Signature in ME/CFS
**Design:** Re-analyze publicly available ME/CFS PBMC transcriptome datasets (GEO: GSE145773, GSE150473, etc.) for EPAS1 and HIF-2α target gene meta-signature (VEGFA, EDN1, SERPINE1, ADM, CA9, GLUT1, PDK1). Stratify by onset type (viral vs gradual), illness duration, severity. Compare to HIF-1α signature.
**Certainty:** 0.50
**Falsifiable:** Post-viral ME/CFS samples cluster separately on HIF-2α target gene PCA (but not HIF-1α), with higher EPAS1 signature score vs controls (FDR < 0.05 in ≥3 datasets)
**Tier:** 1

### R2.7: VEGF Trap (Aflibercept) Effect on ED in Post-Viral ME/CFS — Proof-of-Concept
**Design:** N-of-1 series (n=6). Open-label, single intravitreal aflibercept injection in post-viral ME/CFS patients already receiving anti-VEGF for retinal comorbidity (diabetic macular edema, retinal vein occlusion, etc.). No additional risk — standard care. Endothelial function (FMD + EndoPAT) and cognitive battery at baseline, 1, 4, 12 weeks post-injection. Measure plasma VEGF, vWF, sFlt-1.
**Primary:** ΔFMD at 4 weeks post-injection vs baseline
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Falsifiable:** ≥4/6 patients show FMD improvement >20% at 4 weeks post-aflibercept, correlating with plasma total VEGF reduction
**Tier:** 3

## 3. Drug/Medication Ideas

### D3.1: Belzutifan (HIF-2α Inhibitor) — Expanded Indication for Post-Viral ME/CFS
**Mechanism:** Selective allosteric HIF-2α inhibitor. Blocks HIF-2α/ARNT heterodimerization → prevents transcriptional activation of VEGF, EPO, EDN1, etc. FDA-approved for VHL disease. Directly targets anchor mechanism (Ribeiro2026 rescue).
**Selection criteria:** Post-viral onset, elevated VEGF/vWF, β2AR-autoantibody-negative preferred
**Dosing:** Start 40mg daily, monitor hemoglobin (risk of anemia, HIF-2 inhibition reduces renal EPO), blood pressure, VEGF q2wks
**Risks:** Anemia (15-20%), fatigue, edema (paradoxical). Brain penetration unknown — may not reach BBB. Peripheral ED improvement expected even without CNS penetration.
**Certainty:** 0.45 (up from 0.25 in prior round — stronger evidence base)
**Falsifiable:** 8wk belzutifan (40mg daily) in post-viral ME/CFS with elevated VEGF reduces FMD-ED and PEM severity by ≥30% vs placebo in randomized crossover n=30
**Tier:** 1

### D3.2: Darunavir/Ritonavir (HIV Protease Inhibitor, HIF-2α Blocker)
**Mechanism:** Inhibits HIF-2α activation independent of protease inhibition (Sales2026: darunavir preserves ZO-1/occludin in esophageal barrier via HIF-2α blockade). Long safety track record (HIV population since 2006). Low CNS penetration — primarily peripheral EC protection.
**Dosing:** 600/100mg BID with ritonavir booster
**Risks:** Hepatotoxicity, rash, GI intolerance, drug interactions (CYP3A4). Full HIV dosing in HIV-negative patients requires justification.
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable:** 4wk darunavir/ritonavir in post-viral ME/CFS reduces plasma VEGF and vWF by ≥25% and improves FMD vs placebo (double-blind, n=20)
**Tier:** 2

### D3.3: Sulforaphane (NRF2 Activator) — Restore Brake on HIF-2α
**Mechanism:** NRF2 activation restores antioxidant reserve, competes with HIF-2α for CBP/p300, maintains EC junction integrity (Shen2024). Targets H1.4 (NRF2 exhaustion hypothesis). Available as standardized broccoli sprout extract.
**Dosing:** Sulforaphane glucosinolate 50mg daily (≈150 μmol SFN), or myrosinase-active broccoli sprout extract
**Risks:** Well-tolerated. GI gas/bloating. No known serious AEs.
**Certainty:** 0.45
**Falsifiable:** 12wk sulforaphane in post-viral ME/CFS increases NQO1 expression (PBMC, ≥2-fold), reduces plasma VEGF by ≥20%, improves FMD by ≥15%
**Tier:** 2

### D3.4: Dimethyl Fumarate (DMF, Tecfidera) — NRF2 Activator + HIF-1α Inhibition
**Mechanism:** DMF activates NRF2 (same as D3.3) AND directly inhibits HIF-1α by modifying cysteine residues (unlike sulforaphane). Dual mechanism: restores NRF2 brake, directly suppresses HIF-1α in immune cells. Approved for MS. May benefit autoantibody-positive subgroup via immune modulation.
**Dosing:** 120mg BID → 240mg BID after 1 week (MS dosing)
**Risks:** Flushing, GI upset, lymphopenia (monitor), PML risk (very low with normal lymphocyte count). Requires monitoring.
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable:** 12wk DMF in ME/CFS reduces PBMC HIF-1α protein and granzyme B (T cell cytotoxicity) by ≥30%, improves FMD and fatigue in autoantibody-positive subgroup
**Tier:** 3

### D3.5: Saracatinib (Src Inhibitor, HIF Pathway Modulator)
**Mechanism:** Src kinase inhibitor that targets HIF signaling in ECs (Qiang2026, retinopathy model). Originally developed for cancer, tested in Alzheimer's (failed phase 2 for cognition but safe). Targets both HIF-1α and HIF-2α in ECs.
**Dosing:** 100mg daily (from AD trials, 175mg MTD)
**Risks:** GI upset, fatigue, rash. Generally well-tolerated at 100mg.
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable:** Saracatinib (100mg daily, 4wk) reduces spike-induced HIF-2α nuclear localization and restores TEER in vitro; no human ME/CFS data yet
**Tier:** 3

### D3.6: Propranolol (for HIF-2α Stratified Subgroup Selection)
**Mechanism:** Non-selective β-blocker. β2AR blockade reduces cAMP → reduces PKA → reduces HIF-1α stabilization. May also affect HIF-2α in ECs indirectly via hemodynamic effects. Already used in ME/CFS pilot studies. Key refinement: use pre-treatment VEGF to predict response.
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable:** Propranolol responders (composite symptom improvement ≥30%) have lower baseline VEGF than non-responders (cutoff ≤150 pg/mL identifies responders with sensitivity >80%), suggesting responders have β2AR-driven rather than HIF-2α-driven ED
**Tier:** 2

### D3.7: Acetazolamide (Hypoxia-Mimetic, HIF Stabilizer — Paradoxical Strategy)
**Mechanism:** Carbonic anhydrase inhibitor → metabolic acidosis → PHD inhibition → HIF-1α stabilization. Paradoxically, could "train" HIF pathway responsiveness if REVERSIBLE HIF-2α blockade (D3.1) followed by HIF-PH activation. Sequential approach: Phase 1 belzutifan (suppress pathological HIF-2α), Phase 2 acetazolamide (restore physiological HIF responsiveness).
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Falsifiable:** In spike-exposed ECs, acetazolamide alone worsens barrier (more HIF-2α), but belzutifan pretreatment → washout → acetazolamide restores normal HIF response without pathological persistence
**Tier:** 3

## 4. Supplement/Nutraceutical Ideas

### S4.1: Pycnogenol (French Maritime Pine Bark Extract)
**Mechanism:** Stimulates NRF2, inhibits VEGF signaling, directly protects EC junctions (increases ZO-1/occludin expression), reduces endothelial permeability. Anti-inflammatory. Multiple clinical trials in venous insufficiency, diabetic retinopathy, hypertension show endothelial improvement.
**Dosing:** 150-300mg/day
**Risks:** Very well-tolerated. Mild GI upset. Theoretical anticoagulation (negligible at this dose).
**Certainty:** 0.50
**Falsifiable:** 8wk Pycnogenol (150mg/day) in ME/CFS improves FMD by ≥20% and reduces PEM severity (VISUAL) by ≥25% vs placebo
**Tier:** 1

### S4.2: Quercetin
**Mechanism:** Direct HIF-1α/HIF-2α inhibitor (iron chelation + prolyl hydroxylase modulation), antioxidant, mast cell stabilizer, antiviral (inhibits viral replication including SARS-CoV-2). Multiple mechanisms converge on endothelial protection.
**Dosing:** 500mg BID (optimally with bromelain or phosphatidylcholine for absorption)
**Risks:** Well-tolerated. Mild HA, GI upset. Theoretical renal effects at high dose.
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable:** 8wk quercetin (500mg BID) in post-viral ME/CFS reduces plasma VEGF by ≥20% and improves FMD vs placebo (n=30)
**Tier:** 2

### S4.3: Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA/DHA, 4g/day)
**Mechanism:** Specialized pro-resolving mediators (resolvins, protectins) from EPA/DHA actively resolve endothelial inflammation, reduce adhesion molecule expression, improve NO bioavailability, and may suppress HIF-2α signaling via PPARγ. Multiple vascular benefits.
**Dosing:** 4g/day (≥60% EPA+DHA). High-dose needed for measurable endothelial effect.
**Risks:** Fishy burps, mild anticoagulation. Very safe.
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable:** 12wk high-dose omega-3 (4g/day) in ME/CFS reduces plasma VCAM-1, ICAM-1, and VEGF by ≥15%, improves NVC during cognitive task on fMRI BOLD lag time
**Tier:** 2

### S4.4: Vitamin D (Correction of Deficiency)
**Mechanism:** VDR directly regulates HIF-1α stability via interaction with PHD2. Vitamin D deficiency (common in ME/CFS — limited sun exposure, indoor lifestyle) removes this regulatory brake, permissive for HIF-2α. Correction restores pathway balance.
**Dosing:** 2000-5000 IU/day to target 50-80 ng/mL
**Risks:** Very safe. Monitor 25(OH)D at 3 months to avoid toxicity.
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable:** Correction of vitamin D deficiency in ME/CFS (target >50 ng/mL) lowers plasma VEGF and vWF by ≥15%, with greater effect in post-viral subtype
**Tier:** 2

### S4.5: L-Arginine/L-Citrulline + BH4 (Tetrahydrobiopterin) — NO Support Strategy
**Mechanism:** HIF-2α upregulates eNOS transcriptionally, but in pathological activation, eNOS may become uncoupled (BH4 deficiency → superoxide instead of NO). Supplementation restores eNOS coupling → NO production → vasodilation → improved O2 delivery. Counteracts ED. The arginine paradox: works only if BH4 is sufficient.
**Dosing:** L-citrulline 6g/day + BH4 (sapropterin) 10mg/kg/day
**Risks:** Citrulline: very safe. BH4: expensive, approved for PKU; safety in non-PKU populations not fully established.
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable:** L-citrulline + BH4 improves FMD in ME/CS only in those with low baseline ADMA (<0.6 μmol/L); worsens FMD in those with high ADMA (>0.8 μmol/L, indicates uncoupled eNOS)
**Tier:** 3

### S4.6: Green Tea EGCG
**Mechanism:** Direct HIF-1α inhibitor (prevents HIF-1α protein accumulation), iron chelator, antioxidant. Modulates VEGF signaling.
**Dosing:** 400-800mg/day (standardized to ≥50% EGCG)
**Risks:** Hepatotoxicity at very high doses (>800mg) or on empty stomach. Caffeine content (use decaffeinated). Take with food.
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Falsifiable:** 12wk EGCG (400mg BID) in ME/CFS reduces plasma VEGF and HIF-1α target gene expression in PBMCs, improves fatigue score
**Tier:** 3

## 5. Non-Pharmacological Interventions

### N5.1: Graduated Compression Therapy (Lower Body, 20-30 mmHg)
**Mechanism:** Counteracts HIF-2α-mediated capillary permeability → reduces interstitial fluid accumulation → improves O2 diffusion from capillary to mitochondria, reduces orthostatic venous pooling, improves cerebral perfusion. Addresses both ED mechanism + symptom expression.
**Protocol:** Daily below-knee graduated compression stockings (20-30 mmHg, thigh-high if orthostatic intolerance severe), worn during upright activity. 10-12h/day.
**Certainty:** 0.50
**Falsifiable:** 4wk daily compression in ME/CFS improves orthostatic tolerance (NASA lean test symptoms by ≥40%) and reduces plasma VEGF vs baseline (ED biomarker); effect size larger in post-viral subgroup
**Tier:** 1

### N5.2: Pacing Protocol Targeting Microvascular Perfusion (Avoid Prolonged Immobility)
**Mechanism:** Prolonged sitting/standing (immobile upright posture) → venous pooling → capillary stasis → local hypoxia → HIF pathway activation → cycle perpetuation. Frequent micro-movements maintain capillary shear stress → NO release → preserves EC function. Pacing as endothelial protection, not just activity limitation.
**Protocol:** Hourly 2-min walking or seated leg exercises (ankle pumps, heel raises). Avoid >30min continuous sitting. Timer-based prompting. Compare to conventional energy-limiting pacing.
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable:** Microvascular pacing protocol (hourly movement) reduces PEM incidence by ≥30% vs conventional pacing (activity-limiting) over 8wk, with improved FMD and reduced VEGF
**Tier:** 2

### N5.3: Nocturnal CPAP for Mild Sleep-Disordered Breathing (Even Without OSA Diagnosis)
**Mechanism:** Even subclinical nocturnal desaturation (SpO2 88-92%, not meeting OSA criteria) causes intermittent HIF-1α activation. Accumulated nightly HIF-1α→HIF-2α switch (Zhang2026). Preventing nocturnal hypoxia may prevent the cumulative shift to pathological HIF-2α.
**Protocol:** Screening: home sleep test. Treat: auto-CPAP for AHI ≥5 AND nocturnal SpO2 <90% >5% of night, or CPAP for upper airway resistance syndrome (RDI ≥5). 8wk trial.
**Certainty:** 0.45
**Falsifiable:** ME/CFS patients with nocturnal SpO2 <90% >5% of night (even without OSA diagnosis) have higher plasma VEGF and worse FMD than those with normal nocturnal oxygenation; 8wk CPAP normalizes VEGF and improves fatigue
**Tier:** 2

### N5.4: Red/Near-Infrared Photobiomodulation (660nm/850nm)
**Mechanism:** Cytochrome c oxidase activation → improved mitochondrial ETC efficiency → reduced ROS → restored NRF2/HIF balance. Near-infrared penetrates to capillary level. Preclinical evidence for EC protection.
**Protocol:** LED panel (660nm + 850nm), 20 J/cm², 3×/week, 10 min per session to lower abdomen (large vascular bed exposure) or neck (cerebral circulation). 8wk.
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable:** 8wk photobiomodulation in ME/CFS improves FMD by ≥15% and reduces plasma VEGF and F2-isoprostanes vs sham (identical device, light off)
**Tier:** 3

### N5.5: Contrast Hydrotherapy (Alternating Warm/Cold Water)
**Mechanism:** Alternating vasodilation (warm) and vasoconstriction (cold) → hypoxic conditioning stimulus → improves EC responsiveness and NO bioavailability. May train HIF pathway to respond appropriately rather than persistently.
**Protocol:** 3 min warm (38-40°C) → 1 min cool (15-20°C) → repeat 3 cycles. 2×/day. Lower body only. Avoid if orthostatic instability severe.
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable:** 8wk contrast hydrotherapy in mild-moderate ME/CFS improves PORH (small vessel endothelial function) by ≥25% vs baseline
**Tier:** 3

## 6. Combinations + Access

### C6.1: Triple Pathway Protocol — HIF-2α Inhibition + NRF2 Activation + NO Support
**Components:** Belzutifan 40mg daily (HIF-2α inhibitor) + sulforaphane 50mg daily (NRF2 activator) + L-citrulline 6g daily (NO substrate)
**Rationale:** Block pathological HIF-2α at source, restore antioxidant brake, support compensatory vasodilation. Three independent mechanisms targeting converging pathway.
**Cost:** Belzutifan ~$15,000/month (limited access). Sulforaphane + citrulline <$100/month.
**Risk:** Belzutifan anemia + citrulline hypotension potential. Monitor Hb + BP.
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable:** Triple combination improves FMD more than belzutifan alone (ΔFMD 40% vs 25%) and permits lower belzutifan dose (20mg) to achieve same effect
**Tier:** 2

### C6.2: Sequential Protocol — Acute Suppression Then Conditioning
**Phase 1 (4wk):** Belzutifan + compression stockings (rapidly restore barrier integrity, break HIF-2α positive feedback)
**Phase 2 (8wk):** IHT (intermittent hypoxic training, FiO2 0.14-0.15, 3×/wk) + tapered belzutifan (restore physiological HIF responsiveness without pathological persistence)
**Rationale:** Break maladaptive cycle first, then re-train adaptive pathway. Sepsis-like approach: barrier rescue → conditioning.
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable:** Phase 2 outcomes (6MWT, FMD, VEGF) are superior to belzutifan-alone or IHT-alone, and belzutifan can be withdrawn without VEGF rebound
**Tier:** 2

### C6.3: Affordable HIF-2α Protocol (No Prescription Drug)
**Components:** Pycnogenol 150mg/day + quercetin 500mg BID + omega-3 4g/day + vitamin D (to target)
**Rationale:** All OTC, total cost <$150/month. Targets: VEGF inhibition (pycnogenol + quercetin), direct HIF inhibition (quercetin), anti-inflammatory/resolving (omega-3), pathway regulation (vitamin D). Four orthogonal mechanisms.
**Risk:** Bleeding if on anticoagulants (quercetin + omega-3). GI tolerance.
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable:** 12wk affordable protocol reduces plasma VEGF by ≥25% and improves FMD by ≥15% in post-viral ME/CFS (within-subject, n=20 open-label)
**Tier:** 2

### C6.4: Accessibility Tier
**Tier 1 (Immediate, no prescription):** Pycnogenol + quercetin + omega-3 + compression stockings
**Tier 2 (GP/prescription):** Darunavir/ritonavir (if accessible) or propranolol (stratified by VEGF) + above
**Tier 3 (Specialist):** Belzutifan (compassionate use/clinical trial) + sulforaphane + rehabilitation
**Certainty:** 0.45 (tier concept based on existing access pathways)
**Falsifiable:** Each successive tier adds ≥15% improvement in FMD over previous tier in sequential escalation protocol
**Tier:** 1 (access framework)

## 7. Mathematical Model Extensions

### M7.1: HIF-1α/HIF-2α Switch ODE with Viral Trigger
**New variables:**
- [VT] — viral trigger (spike antigen persistence, dimensionless)
- [ROS] — reactive oxygen species (normalized to baseline)
- [NRF2] — NRF2 nuclear activity (0-1, 1=fully active)
- [H1n] — nuclear HIF-1α
- [H2n] — nuclear HIF-2α
- [V] — VEGF (extracellular)
- [P] — endothelial permeability (TEER-normalized)

**Core dynamics:**
d[H1n]/dt = k1·[ROS]/(Kd1+[ROS])·f([NRF2]) − k2·[H1n]
d[H2n]/dt = k3·[ROS]^2/(Kd2^2+[ROS]^2)·g([NRF2]) − k4·[H2n]
f([NRF2]) = 1/(1+α·[NRF2])   (NRF2 inhibits HIF-1α)
g([NRF2]) = 1/(1+β·[NRF2]^2) (NRF2 inhibits HIF-2α more steeply)

**Bifurcation:** When [NRF2] falls below threshold θ_NRF2 (NRF2 exhaustion), [ROS]→[H2n] gain increases nonlinearly, and HIF-2α enters self-sustaining regime (positive feedback: H2n → VEGF → EC activation → more ROS → more H2n).

**ME/CFS parameter:** Baseline [NRF2] lower (genetic/predisposition), k2/k4 lower (reduced PHD activity), making switch more likely from the same viral trigger.

**Certainty:** 0.50
**Falsifiable:** Model predicts critical NRF2:ROS ratio threshold (NRT ≥ 2.3) below which HIF-2α becomes self-sustaining; in vitro experiments confirm irreversible switch when NRF2 depleted >60%
**Tier:** 1

### M7.2: DAG Extension — Endothelial-Centric Causal Network
**Add nodes to existing causal DAG:**

1. `NRF2_reserve` — individual antioxidant capacity (latent, influenced by genetics + nutrition + prior stress)
2. `EC_HIF2a_activation` — sustained HIF-2α nuclear localization
3. `VEGF_production` — vascular permeability factor
4. `EC_perm` — endothelial barrier permeability

**Certainty:** 0.55

**Tier:** 1

### M7.3: Compartment O2 Delivery Model with Capillary Leak
**Compartments:**
1. **Blood (arterial):** [Hb], pO2, CBF
2. **Interstitium:** O2 partial pressure (pO2_is), volume (V_is)
3. **Cell:** O2 consumption (VO2), pO2_cell

**Key innovation:** Endothelial permeability (P_endo) adds an effective inter-compartment resistance term. Normally, O2 diffuses rapidly from blood → interstitium → cell. When P_endo is high (HIF-2α-mediated), capillary filtrate increases V_is (interstitial edema), creating longer O2 diffusion distance and reduced pO2_cell for the same CBF and [Hb].

d[O2_is]/dt = k1·([Hb]·SO2(pO2_blood) · CBF) − k2·(pO2_is − pO2_cell)·A_eff − k3·VO2
where A_eff = A0 / (1 + γ·P_endo) — available surface area reduced by perivascular edema

**Prediction:** Same workload (same VO2) requires proportionally higher CBF when P_endo is elevated. When CBF cannot increase proportionally (impaired autoregulation, Badhwar2025), pO2_cell drops → anaerobic metabolism → lactate → PEM.

**Certainty:** 0.45
**Falsifiable:** Model predicts that for ME/CFS (high P_endo + low CBF reserve), the O2 supply-demand mismatch during exercise is 30-50% worse than controls at same workload; CPET data (VO2 kinetics) should show slowed VO2 on-kinetics and early lactate threshold consistent with this prediction
**Tier:** 2

### M7.4: Bayesian Hierarchical Model for EC HIF-2α Subtype Classification
**Model:** Latent class analysis using biomarkers (VEGF, vWF, sFlt-1, EPO, FMD) to identify an "HIF-2α-activated" latent class. Incorporate prior from Ribeiro2026 (expected 50-60% of post-viral, 0-10% of controls). Class membership probability output for each patient.

**Features:**
- Latent class 1: "HIF-2α active" — high VEGF, vWF, EPO; low FMD
- Latent class 2: "Autoantibody vasoconstriction" — normal VEGF, low FMD, +β2AR ab
- Latent class 3: "Intact" — normal biomarkers, normal FMD

**Certainty:** 0.45
**Falsifiable:** In a mixed ME/CFS cohort (n=200), ≥3 latent classes provide better fit than 2 (LMR-LRT p<0.05); class 1 patients preferentially have post-viral onset and respond to compression therapy in prospective follow-up
**Tier:** 2

## 8. Cross-Disease Bridges

### B8.1: ME/CFS ↔ Diabetic Retinopathy (DR) — Shared HIF-2α→VEGF→Permeability Axis
**Observation:** DR: chronic hyperglycemia → oxidative stress → HIF-2α activation → VEGF → retinal capillary permeability → vision loss. Anti-VEGF therapy (ranibizumab, aflibercept) is first-line DR treatment. ME/CFS may share the same pathway triggered by viral (not glycemic) stress.
**Bridge prediction:** ME/CFS patients show subclinical retinal microvascular changes (capillary non-perfusion, foveal avascular zone enlargement) similar to early non-proliferative DR, despite normal HbA1c.
**Test:** OCT angiography in ME/CFS vs age/BMI-matched controls. Compare retinal capillary density.
**Certainty:** 0.55
**Falsifiable:** ME/CFS patients have reduced superficial capillary plexus density (≥10% vs controls) that correlates with FMD (r>0.5) and cognitive processing speed (r>0.4), independent of HbA1c and blood pressure
**Tier:** 1

### B8.2: ME/CFS ↔ Preeclampsia — sFlt-1-Mediated Anti-Angiogenic State
**Observation:** Preeclampsia features HIF-2α activation → excessive sFlt-1 (soluble VEGFR1, anti-angiogenic) → systemic endothelial dysfunction → hypertension, proteinuria. ME/CFS may have analogous sFlt-1 elevation without reaching preeclamptic levels. sFlt-1 traps VEGF and PlGF, impairing angiogenesis and EC health.
**Bridge prediction:** Post-viral ME/CFS patients have elevated sFlt-1:PlGF ratio, indicating functional VEGF deficiency despite normal total VEGF (or even elevated VEGF neutralized by excess sFlt-1).
**Test:** Plasma sFlt-1, PlGF, VEGF, sFlt:PlGF ratio in ME/CFS vs controls.
**Certainty:** 0.45
**Falsifiable:** sFlt-1:PlGF ratio is elevated in post-viral ME/CFS vs controls (mean ratio >85 vs <38 in controls), correlating with PEM severity and anti-angiogenic markers (reduced circulating endothelial progenitor cells)
**Tier:** 2

### B8.3: ME/CFS ↔ CAR-T Neurotoxicity — BBB HIF-2α Disruption
**Observation:** CAR-T cell therapy → cytokine release → systemic inflammation → HIF-2α activation in BBB endothelium → BBB disruption → neurotoxicity. Same mechanism at lower intensity from post-viral immune activation may explain ME/CFS cognitive dysfunction.
**Bridge prediction:** ME/CFS cognitive dysfunction involves BBB disruption measurable by DCE-MRI and serum S100B.
**Test:** Serum S100B, NfL, albumin ratio, and DCE-MRI BBB Ktrans in ME/CFS vs controls.
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Falsifiable:** ME/CFS patients with cognitive dysfunction show elevated Ktrans in hippocampus and brainstem vs healthy controls, and serum S100B correlates with VEGF (r>0.5)
**Tier:** 3

### B8.4: ME/CFS ↔ COVID-19 Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS) — HIF-2α in Pulmonary ECs
**Observation:** COVID-19 ARDS involves massive pulmonary EC dysfunction with HIF-2α activation (SARS-CoV-2 infects ECs directly). Survivors may have persistent pulmonary HIF-2α activation → exercise limitation. ME/CFS may generalize this: non-pulmonary EC beds (systemic, cerebral) with persistent HIF-2α.
**Bridge prediction:** Post-COVID ME/CFS patients without ARDS history show pulmonary EC dysfunction on cardiopulmonary exercise testing (reduced DLCO, increased Vd/Vt) despite normal spirometry, suggesting subclinical pulmonary microvascular impairment.
**Test:** CPET with DLCO, dead space fraction, and alveolar-arterial O2 gradient in post-COVID ME/CFS vs non-COVID ME/CFS vs controls.
**Certainty:** 0.45
**Falsifiable:** Post-COVID ME/CFS shows reduced DLCO and increased Vd/Vt on CPET compared to non-COVID ME/CFS and controls, correlating with plasma VEGF (r>0.5), indicating persistent pulmonary microvascular impairment
**Tier:** 2

### B8.5: ME/CFS ↔ Hereditary Hemorrhagic Telangiectasia (HHT) — ALK1/VEGF Pathway Overlap
**Observation:** HHT: mutations in ENG (endoglin) or ALK1 → TGF-β signaling disruption → VEGF pathway dysregulation → vascular malformations. ALK1 signaling modulates HIF-2α. Heterozygous carriers of HHT-associated polymorphisms may have subtle vascular phenotype predisposing to post-viral ED.
**Bridge prediction:** Common ENG or ACVRL1 (ALK1) polymorphisms are enriched in post-viral ME/CFS, and carriage is associated with more severe ED (lower FMD, higher VEGF).
**Test:** Candidate gene association study (ENG, ACVRL1, SMAD4) in post-viral ME/CFS vs controls (n≥500).
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Falsifiable:** ENG rs10915891 or ACVRL1 rs11169953 are enriched in post-viral ME/CFS (OR >1.5, p<0.01 after Bonferroni correction for 10 SNPs tested)
**Tier:** 3

### B8.6: ME/CFS ↔ VHL Disease — Differential HIF-2α Activation Pattern
**Observation:** VHL disease: constitutive HIF-2α activation (VHL loss) → hemangioblastomas, RCC, polycythemia. Post-viral ME/CFS: stimulus-dependent HIF-2α activation (viral trigger without VHL mutation). Contrast in downstream profiles reveals ME/CFS-specific HIF-2α program.
**Bridge prediction:** Post-viral ME/CFS shows select HIF-2α targets (VEGF, EDN1, SERPINE1) but NOT erythrocytosis-inducing targets (EPO) — pattern consistent with "partial" HIF-2α activation in ECs without renal EPO production.
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable:** ME/CFS patients show elevated EC-specific HIF-2α targets (VEGF, EDN1) but normal hemoglobin and normal EPO relative to VHL patients, indicating EC-confined HIF-2α activation pattern
**Tier:** 2

## 9. Diagnostic/Biomarker Ideas

### Dx9.1: VEGF/sFlt-1 Ratio as ED Subtype Classifier
**Rationale:** VEGF = direct HIF-2α transcriptional target. sFlt-1 = endogenous VEGF inhibitor (soluble VEGFR1), also hypoxia-regulated. Ratio captures net angiogenic balance. Low ratio = anti-angiogenic state (sFlt-1 consuming VEGF), high or normal ratio = pro-permeability state (free VEGF).
**Protocol:** Fasting plasma VEGF, sFlt-1, PlGF. Calculate ratio. Use cutoffs from preeclampsia literature (sFlt-1:PlGF >38 = abnormal).
**Prediction:** Low VEGF/sFlt-1 ratio identifies an ED subgroup with post-viral onset, elevated vWF, and preferential response to compression therapy.
**Certainty:** 0.50
**Falsifiable:** VEGF/sFlt-1 ratio <0.5 (arbitrary unit) defines a ME/CFS subgroup with ≥2× higher odds of post-viral onset, worse PEM severity, and greater FMD improvement with compression vs ratio >0.5 subgroup
**Tier:** 1

### Dx9.2: Retinal OCT Angiography (OCT-A) — Non-Invasive ED Assessment
**Rationale:** Retinal microvasculature mirrors cerebral and systemic. HIF-2α activation → capillary dropout, pericyte loss, increased tortuosity. OCT-A is painless, non-invasive, 5-min scan, widely available in ophthalmology clinics.
**Protocol:** 3mm × 3mm macular scan. Automated quantification: superficial/deep capillary plexus density, foveal avascular zone (FAZ) area, vessel tortuosity index.
**Prediction:** ME/CFS patients show reduced capillary density and increased FAZ area vs controls.
**Certainty:** 0.55
**Falsifiable:** ME/CFS patients (particularly post-viral) have parafoveal capillary density <38% (vs >42% in controls) on OCT-A. Density correlates with FMD (r>0.5) and cognitive processing speed (r>0.4). Sensitivity >75%, specificity >80% for ME/CFS diagnosis using machine learning on OCT-A features.
**Tier:** 1

### Dx9.3: Circulating Endothelial Microparticles (EMPs) — CD62E+ EMP Subset
**Rationale:** EMPs = membrane fragments shed from activated/damaged ECs. CD62E (E-selectin) specifically indicates EC activation (not apoptosis). Size 0.1-1.0 μm, quantified by flow cytometry. CD62E+ EMPs are elevated in vasculopathies and respond to treatment.
**Protocol:** Platelet-poor plasma, flow cytometry using CD31+CD42b−CD62E+ gate (endothelial-derived, not platelet-contaminated, activation-specific).
**Prediction:** CD62E+ EMPs are elevated in post-viral ME/CFS and correlate with VEGF.
**Certainty:** 0.50
**Falsifiable:** CD62E+ EMP count is ≥2-fold higher in post-viral ME/CFS vs controls and vs gradual-onset ME/CFS. After CPET, EMP count increases in ME/CFS but not controls at 24h. EMP correlates with VEGF (r>0.5) and PEM severity (r>0.6).
**Tier:** 1

### Dx9.4: Exercise-Induced vWF Release as HIF-2α Functional Test
**Rationale:** vWF stored in Weibel-Palade bodies, released upon EC activation. VEGF (HIF-2α target) enhances vWF exocytosis. Exercise triggers vWF release as physiological response. In HIF-2α-mediated ED, vWF release may be exaggerated, prolonged, or incompletely regulated.
**Protocol:** CPET. vWF:Ag and vWF propeptide (marker of acute release) at baseline, peak exercise, 2h, 6h, 24h, 48h. Compare to controls.
**Prediction:** ME/CFS shows elevated baseline vWF, excessive release during exercise, and delayed return to baseline (>24h).
**Certainty:** 0.50
**Falsifiable:** ME/CFS patients show vWF:Ag elevation at 24h post-CPET >30% above baseline (vs return to baseline by 6h in controls). AUC of vWF:Ag(0-48h) correlates with PEM-VAS at 48h (r>0.6). Sensitivity >80% for distinguishing post-viral ME/CFS from controls.
**Tier:** 1

### Dx9.5: EndoPAT + VEGF Dual Test — Point-of-Care ED Screening
**Rationale:** EndoPAT (FDA-cleared) measures reactive hyperemia index (RHI) via digital pulse amplitude. Already validated for ED detection. Adding VEGF blood test specifically identifies HIF-2α-driven ED.
**Protocol:** EndoPAT (15-min, non-invasive) + fingerstick VEGF (ELISA, 30-min). Combine: RHI <1.67 + VEGF >75th percentile → "HIF-2α-mediated ED" phenotype.
**Prediction:** This combined phenotype defines a treatment-responsive subgroup.
**Certainty:** 0.50
**Falsifiable:** Combined EndoPAT+VEGF screening identifies a ME/CFS subgroup (~40% of post-viral) where compression therapy improves RHI by ≥20% (vs ≤5% in RHI-low+VEGF-normal subgroup)
**Tier:** 1

### Dx9.6: Urinary VEGF:Creatinine Ratio — Accessible Home Monitoring
**Rationale:** VEGF is filtered and measured in urine, normalized to creatinine. Correlates with plasma VEGF. Non-invasive, home collection, mail-in assay. Enables serial monitoring of HIF-2α activity without clinic visits.
**Protocol:** Morning void, spot urine. ELISA for VEGF, creatinine correction. Normal: <30 pg/mg Cr. Elevated: >50 pg/mg Cr.
**Prediction:** Urinary VEGF:Cr is elevated in post-viral ME/CFS, decreases with successful intervention, and increases before/during PEM episodes.
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Falsifiable:** In a prospective 4-week diary study, urinary VEGF:Cr rises ≥50% from individual baseline on day preceding reported PEM (24-48h lead time), with sensitivity >70% for PEM prediction
**Tier:** 3

### Dx9.7: Systemic Capillary Permeability (Landis Test) — Direct ED Measurement
**Rationale:** The Landis test measures capillary filtration rate (CFR) via venous occlusion plethysmography. Directly assesses permeability, the functional endpoint of HIF-2α-mediated ED. If HIF-2α→VEGF→permeability, CFR should be elevated.
**Protocol:** Strain-gauge plethysmography on forearm. Venous occlusion at 40 mmHg. Measure CFR (mL/min/100g tissue). Validated in diabetes, sepsis.
**Prediction:** ME/CFS patients have elevated CFR at rest, increasing further during mild exercise.
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Falsifiable:** Baseline CFR is elevated ≥50% in post-viral ME/CFS vs controls. After 5 min of mild handgrip exercise, CFR increases disproportionately in ME/CFS (+80% from baseline) vs controls (+30%). CFR correlates with PEM severity.
**Tier:** 2
