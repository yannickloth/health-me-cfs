## Ranked Idea List

### 1. Local-Tissue-Sequestered IgE (Not Reflected in Serum)
- **Category:** 1 — Novel hypothesis
- **Rationale:** The Th2-bias (skowera2004type2) without serum-total-IgE elevation (reparramirez2001igelevels) is the central dissociation. It dissolves if antigen-specific IgE is produced and retained locally — in perivascular mast cells, nasal/respiratory mucosa, dural perivascular niches, or the gut lamina propria — at concentrations too low to raise serum totals. Tissue-resident mast cells can bind IgE locally without systemic spillover. This reconciles a Th2-competent immune system (skowera) with two nulls (reparramirez, kowal2002prevalence). Matches the tissue-level pathology pattern in ME/CFS (localized, not systemic).
- **Evidence:** skowera2004type2, reparramirez2001igelevels, kowal2002prevalence; mast-cell biology (tissue-resident IgE binding well-established outside ME/CFS); lakatos2025bidirectional (perivascular mast cell location at BBB).
- **Certainty:** 0.15 — entirely speculative. No direct ME/CFS tissue IgE data. A rescue hypothesis for the dissociation, not evidence-based.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** IgE+ mast cells or IgE deposition in gut/nasal/CSF compartments of ME/CFS patients, in the absence of elevated serum IgE. Assay: flow cytometry on endoscopic biopsies or nasal brushings for IgE+ cells; paired serum IgE comparison.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** If true, a subset of patients may have a localized allergic-type process in specific tissues despite normal allergy blood tests — meaning routine serum IgE panels miss the relevant signal.
- **Suggested environment:** #speculation

### 2. IgE-Independent FcεRI Sensitization in ME/CFS
- **Category:** 1 — Novel hypothesis
- **Rationale:** FcεRI (the high-affinity IgE receptor) can be upregulated and primed by Th2 cytokines (IL-4, IL-13) even in the absence of antigen-specific IgE. In ME/CFS, the documented Th2 bias (skowera2004type2) could drive FcεRI over-expression on mast cells and basophils, lowering the activation threshold for any IgE (including low-level environmental specificities within normal range). This explains: (a) enhanced mast-cell reactivity, (b) normal total IgE (no hyper-IgE needed for hyper-reactivity), (c) perceived allergy symptoms without serological confirmation (kowal2002prevalence, conti1996). The mechanism is IgE-*dependent* in the sense of requiring the receptor but not requiring elevated IgE. Relevant to the MCAS literature: theoharides2019 non-IgE paradigm may coexist with FcεRI-upregulated IgE-hypersensitivity.
- **Evidence:** skowera2004type2; reparramirez2001igelevels; kowal2002prevalence; conti1996; theoharides2019 (partially — this idea bridges IgE and non-IgE). **Sub-research candidate:** need IL-4/IL-13 → FcεRI upregulation evidence from non-ME/CFS immunology literature (well-established in allergy field; not in Phase 1).
- **Certainty:** 0.12 — speculative translation of Th2-biology to ME/CFS. Requires FcεRI density data in ME/CFS.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** FcεRI density on basophils or tissue mast cells is higher in ME/CFS vs controls, independent of serum IgE levels. Assay: flow-cytometric FcεRIα quantification on peripheral basophils.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** Would mean ME/CFS patients' mast cells could be "tuned" to react more easily to environmental triggers — like a smoke alarm set too sensitively — without high IgE levels in the blood.
- **Suggested environment:** #speculation
- **origin:** brainstorm

### 3. Alpha-Gal IgE as a Tick-Exposure Marker, Not a Causal Mediator in ME/CFS
- **Category:** 1 — Novel hypothesis
- **Rationale:** The LinkedIn hypothesis invokes tick-borne pathogen antigens generating specific IgE that drives ME/CFS. An alternative: alpha-gal IgE (plattsmills2025immunology, wilson2024ticksalpha) is a marker of tick exposure but the actual ME/CFS trigger is a tick-borne pathogen (Borrelia, Ehrlichia, Babesia) independent of the IgE response. Alpha-gal IgE would then be an *epidemiological confounder* — associated with tick-borne-pathogen-triggered ME/CFS but not causative. This separates two linked but mechanistically distinct consequences of tick bites: (a) alpha-gal IgE → mammalian meat allergy, (b) tick-borne pathogen → post-infectious ME/CFS. Zychowski 2024 (alpha-gal IgE + knee pain, NOT tick-borne-pathogen seropositivity) already shows alpha-gal IgE does not track pathogen-mediated symptoms.
- **Evidence:** plattsmills2025immunology, wilson2024ticksalpha, vanrunen2015tickinduced, caberascruz2021tickhuman, zychowski2024 (alpha-gal → pain but NOT → tick-pathogen seropositivity).
- **Certainty:** 0.25 — limited data. Zychowski 2024 provides suggestive support but is a single study on knee OA, not ME/CFS.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In tick-triggered ME/CFS, seropositivity for tick-borne pathogens (Borrelia, Ehrlichia, Babesia) is associated with disease status independent of alpha-gal IgE levels; alpha-gal IgE alone (without pathogen seropositivity) does not associate with ME/CFS.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** If this is right, then tick bites could trigger ME/CFS through the infection itself, not through the allergic response to tick-bite chemicals — an important distinction for how we think about prevention and testing.
- **Suggested environment:** #open-question
- **origin:** brainstorm

### 4. Anti-Spike IgE in Post-COVID ME/CFS: COVID-Exposure Marker vs Disease-Driver
- **Category:** 2 — Research direction
- **Rationale:** Giménez-Orenga 2025 found anti-spike IgE higher in post-COVID condition than prepandemic ME/CFS — suggesting anti-spike IgE tracks COVID exposure history, not ME/CFS disease state. A definitive serosurvey is needed: measure anti-spike IgE (S1, S2, RBD), anti-nucleocapsid IgE, and IgG4 in three groups: (a) post-COVID ME/CFS, (b) post-COVID recovered (no ME/CFS), (c) prepandemic ME/CFS. Primary endpoint: anti-spike IgE titer by group. Secondary: correlation with clinical severity, mast-cell activation markers (tryptase, histamine, PGD2), and neurocognitive battery. Design must control for: time since infection, COVID severity, atopic background, and vaccination history. tan2022hypersensitivity and meltendorf2022iligespike established anti-spike IgE assays; this merely ports them to the right populations.
- **Evidence:** gimenezorenga2025blood, tan2022hypersensitivity, meltendorf2022iligespike, portilho2024igeanti; oh2024incident (infection→allergic sensitization).
- **Certainty:** 0.30 — assay exists, populations exist, study is logistically straightforward. Low cost, high informativeness. But uncertainty about whether signal would distinguish groups.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Anti-spike IgE titers are not significantly different between post-COVID ME/CFS and post-COVID recovered groups after controlling for acute COVID severity and time-since-infection.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** A simple blood test could determine whether the allergic-type response to the COVID virus is a cause of ongoing illness or just a scar of past infection — directly informing whether antihistamine-type treatments would help.
- **Suggested environment:** #speculation (before data exists); would upgrade to #open-question after study
- **origin:** brainstorm

### 5. CSF IgE Profiling in ME/CFS: Proof-of-Concept for CNS-Compartment IgE
- **Category:** 2 — Research direction
- **Rationale:** If antigen-specific IgE is relevant to neuroinflammation, it should be detectable in CSF — the compartment bathing the BBB-adjacent mast cells whose activation drives microglial activation (lakatos2025bidirectional, theoharides1995stressinduced). A pilot: measure total IgE and anti-spike/anti-nucleocapsid/anti-tick IgE in paired serum-CSF from 20 ME/CFS (10 post-COVID, 10 prepandemic) vs 10 healthy controls. Primary: IgE detection rate in CSF by group. Secondary: CSF/serum IgE ratio (intrathecal synthesis index), correlation with CSF tryptase, histamine, and neurofilament light. Even a single positive would be transformative. Even a clean null would constrain the hypothesis.
- **Evidence:** lakatos2025bidirectional (mast cell at BBB), theoharides1995stressinduced, rocha2016histamine; gimenezorenga2025blood (anti-spike IgE in serum).
- **Certainty:** 0.20 — high-risk pilot. CSF IgE is typically undetectable in healthy people; even in CNS allergy (e.g., neuroborreliosis) detection is inconsistent. But the payoff (or constraint) justifies the risk.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Total IgE is detectable in CSF of >0% of ME/CFS patients vs 0% of controls, and/or anti-spike IgE is detectable in CSF of post-COVID ME/CFS patients.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** If IgE shows up in spinal fluid of ME/CFS patients, it would strongly suggest an allergic-type process is happening inside the brain — a completely new way to understand ME/CFS brain symptoms.
- **Suggested environment:** #speculation
- **origin:** brainstorm

### 6. Histamine-H1R Blockade Crossover Trial in IgE-Sensitized ME/CFS Subset: Cognitive Endpoint
- **Category:** 2 — Research direction
- **Rationale:** The final mechanistic link (histamine→H1R→microglia→neurotoxicity: rocha2016histamine) is well-established in vitro/in vivo but never tested as a translational cognitive intervention in ME/CFS. Design: prescreen for putative IgE-sensitization markers (elevated total IgE OR anti-spike/anti-tick IgE OR FcεRI density). Enroll IgE-high vs IgE-low tertiles. Crossover: cetirizine 10 mg BID vs placebo, 4 weeks each. Primary: cognitive processing speed (Cogstate battery). Secondary: PEM diary, orthostatic tolerance, mast cell mediator panel. If IgE-high subgroup shows cognitive improvement vs IgE-low, this would (a) validate IgE-relevance, (b) identify a treatable subgroup, (c) close the mechanism→clinical loop. If negative in both groups, would disconfirm the IgE→neurocognitive pathway.
- **Evidence:** rocha2016histamine (H1R→microglia), lakatos2025bidirectional, theoharides1995stressinduced; reparramirez2001igelevels, kowal2002prevalence (nulls that define the expected non-IgE majority).
- **Certainty:** 0.18 — speculative and logistically complex. Drug is cheap and safe, but the IgE-sensitization screen may identify very few patients (given the nulls). Power is a concern.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In IgE-high ME/CFS patients, cetirizine improves cognitive processing speed by ≥0.3 SD vs placebo; no effect in IgE-low patients.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** If it works, a common over-the-counter allergy pill could improve thinking and concentration in the specific ME/CFS patients who have elevated IgE markers.
- **Suggested environment:** #speculation
- **origin:** brainstorm

### 7. The Nulls Are the Signal: Normal Total IgE Trumps the IgE Hypothesis
- **Category:** 10 — Reasons this mechanism may not be relevant
- **Rationale:** Two independent studies (reparramirez2001igelevels, kowal2002prevalence) found normal total IgE and normal allergen-specific IgE in ME/CFS — the only direct ME/CFS-specific data on IgE. While antigen-specific IgE to spike/tick is *unstudied*, the burden of proof is on the hypothesis to show that a novel antigen-specific IgE exists in the face of normal total IgE. Serum total IgE is a highly sensitive screen for atopy/elevated IgE states; if it is normal, any antigen-specific IgE hypothesis requires a mechanism by which specific IgE exists without raising the total. The rescue hypotheses (local sequestration, FcεRI upregulation) are possible but multiply assumptions. Parsimony: the nulls are correct, IgE is irrelevant in ME/CFS, and the Th2 bias (skowera2004type2) operates through IgE-independent pathways (non-IgE MCAS: theoharides2019). This remains the default and strongest position until direct evidence overturns it.
- **Evidence:** reparramirez2001igelevels; kowal2002prevalence; skowera2004type2; conti1996 (normal ECP); theoharides2019 (non-IgE paradigm).
- **Certainty:** 0.55 — this is the evidence-anchored default position, not speculation. But in absolute terms still moderate because antigen-specific IgE hasn't been directly tested.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** If anti-spike IgE or anti-tick IgE is detected in >10% of ME/CFS patients above a validated cutoff, and titers correlate with disease severity, then the null-is-correct position would be falsified. This remains possible and worth testing.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** ME/CS patients' normal allergy blood tests are not a fluke — they likely mean the classical allergic pathway is simply not part of the disease for most patients.
- **Suggested environment:** #limitation
- **origin:** brainstorm

### 8. Non-IgE MCAS Explains All Observations Without Invoking Antigen-Specific IgE
- **Category:** 10 — Reasons this mechanism may not be relevant
- **Rationale:** Theoharides 2019 comprehensively argues that mast cell activation in chronic inflammatory conditions is predominantly non-IgE-driven via substance P, CRH, IL-33, and MRGPRX2. This framework explains: Th2 bias (skowera2004type2) — IL-33/TSLP drive ILC2/Th2 independent of IgE; normal IgE (reparramirez2001igelevels, kowal2002prevalence) — no IgE needed for activation; high self-reported allergy (conti1996) — symptoms mimic allergy without IgE; stress-exacerbation (theoharides1995stressinduced) — CRH directly activates mast cells; mast cell neuroinflammation (lakatos2025bidirectional, rocha2016histamine) — occurs downstream of any MC trigger, IgE not required. The parsimony argument is strong: every observation in the IgE-to-spike/tick hypothesis chain is equally explained by non-IgE MCAS, and the MCAS framework has the advantage of matching the replicated IgE nulls. The IgE hypothesis would need to demonstrate something *uniquely* explained by IgE (e.g., a seasonal/trigger-specific pattern, a food-trigger pattern, or specific IgE measurement) to be worth favoring over the simpler MCAS model.
- **Evidence:** theoharides2019; skowera2004type2; reparramirez2001igelevels; kowal2002prevalence; conti1996; theoharides1995stressinduced; lakatos2025bidirectional; rocha2016histamine.
- **Certainty:** 0.60 — the competing paradigm is well-evidenced and directly published by the group that discovered stress→mast-cell activation (theoharides). This is not a fringe alternative.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** If the IgE hypothesis is correct, then IgE-positive ME/CFS patients should show distinct symptom triggers (e.g., specific food or aeroallergen-driven flares) not seen in IgE-negative patients, and non-IgE MC mediators (substance P, CRH) should not fully account for the activation. Absence of such a distinction would favor the parsimonious non-IgE model.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** Most mast-cell-related symptoms in ME/CFS are likely caused by stress, inflammatory signals, and nerve-fiber activation — not by allergies — which means allergy tests and antihistamines may miss the real mechanism.
- **Suggested environment:** #limitation
- **origin:** brainstorm

### 9. Acute-COVID Anti-Spike IgE Is a Transient Epiphenomenon
- **Category:** 10 — Reasons this mechanism may not be relevant
- **Rationale:** The three papers documenting anti-spike IgE (tan2022hypersensitivity, meltendorf2022iligespike, portilho2024igeanti) all measure acute or convalescent COVID-19 patients — not chronic post-infectious illness. In acute infection, IgE class-switching to viral antigens is a known but typically transient phenomenon (the immune system produces short-lived IgE plasmablasts during acute Th2-skewed responses; these decline with antigen clearance). By the time chronic ME/CFS is established (≥6 months post-infection), the original antigenic stimulus may be cleared and anti-spike IgE may have decayed to baseline. gimenezorenga2025blood found anti-spike IgE higher in post-COVID condition than prepandemic ME/CFS — consistent with a decaying post-infection signal that tracks exposure history, not ongoing disease. Without evidence of persistent antigen (e.g., spike persistence in tissue reservoirs) or ongoing class-switching, the transient-antibody model is the default expectation. This renders spike-specific IgE irrelevant to chronic ME/CFS pathophysiology, even if it played a role in the acute triggering phase.
- **Evidence:** tan2022hypersensitivity, meltendorf2022iligespike, portilho2024igeanti; gimenezorenga2025blood.
- **Certainty:** 0.45 — moderate. The basic immunology of short-lived IgE responses is well-established. What's uncertain is whether antigen persists in ME/CFS (a separate, unstudied question).
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Anti-spike IgE titers in post-COVID ME/CFS decay to control levels within 12 months post-infection, and do not correlate with ME/CFS symptom severity or duration when controlled for time-since-infection.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** The allergic-type antibody that some people make against the COVID virus during acute illness may simply fade away afterward, making it irrelevant to ongoing ME/CFS symptoms months or years later.
- **Suggested environment:** #limitation
- **origin:** brainstorm

### 10. Alpha-Gal Syndrome Symptoms Do Not Overlap ME/CFS, Weakening the Tick→IgE→ME/CFS Logic
- **Category:** 10 — Reasons this mechanism may not be relevant
- **Rationale:** Alpha-gal syndrome (plattsmills2025immunology, wilson2024ticksalpha, vanrunen2015tickinduced) presents as delayed anaphylaxis 2–6 hours after mammalian meat ingestion — urticaria, angioedema, gastrointestinal distress, anaphylaxis. This symptom profile is entirely distinct from ME/CFS (PEM, orthostatic intolerance, cognitive dysfunction, unrefreshing sleep, pain). If tick-induced IgE to alpha-gal caused mast-cell mediator release sufficient to drive ME/CFS, we would expect alpha-gal patients to report ME/CFS-like symptoms during their reactions — but alpha-gal literature (extensive clinical experience: plattsmills2025immunology, wilson2024ticksalpha, vanrunen2015tickinduced) does not describe PEM, OI, or the characteristic ME/CFS cognitive pattern. The mechanistic gap: tick→IgE→mast-cell activation is proven, but the *type and location* of mast cell activation differs qualitatively between alpha-gal (systemic degranulation with IgE to a lipid-carbohydrate antigen) and whatever pattern would be needed for ME/CFS (perivascular, BBB-adjacent, chronic low-grade). The tick→IgE link may be mechanistically insufficient.
- **Evidence:** plattsmills2025immunology, wilson2024ticksalpha, vanrunen2015tickinduced, caberascruz2021tickhuman.
- **Certainty:** 0.50 — moderate. Clinical phenotype mismatch is a strong argument. It doesn't disprove the tick→IgE chain but shifts it from plausible to incomplete.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** If the tick→IgE→ME/CFS pathway is correct, alpha-gal syndrome patients should have higher-than-expected rates of ME/CFS diagnosis or ME/CFS-like symptoms. This is testable with a cross-sectional survey of alpha-gal cohorts.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** People with tick-bite-related meat allergy don't seem to develop ME/CFS symptoms during their allergic reactions, which is a clue that tick bites probably don't cause ME/CFS through the allergic pathway.
- **Suggested environment:** #limitation
- **origin:** brainstorm

### 11. If Antigen-Specific IgE Has No Role: Null Hypothesis Assessment
- **Category:** 11 — Null hypothesis assessment
- **Rationale:** The null — ME/CFS involves no clinically significant antigen-specific IgE response — has practical implications:
  (a) No existing paper claims would require revision. The 4 null studies (reparramirez2001igelevels, kowal2002prevalence, conti1996, and by extension gimenezorenga2025blood showing anti-spike IgE tracks COVID exposure not disease) would simply be confirmed as correct descriptions of ME/CFS IgE economy.
  (b) Integration into the paper would be a **negative finding section** — making explicit that despite biological plausibility and public interest (LinkedIn hypothesis), the weight of direct ME/CFS evidence rejects general IgE involvement. This is valuable: a clear negative on a plausible hypothesis prevents future researchers from pursuing it as confidently.
  (c) The Th2 bias (skowera2004type2) would need an IgE-independent interpretation — which already exists: IL-4/IL-13 from ILC2 and Th2 cells drive mast-cell proliferation, eosinophil chemotaxis, and B-cell class-switching to IgG4 rather than IgE (consistent with me/cfs IgG4 elevation findings). This reinterpretation is already compatible with the MCAS literature.
  (d) The infection→allergic-sensitization epidemiological data (oh2024incident, clarion2026postcovid) would need to be interpreted as non-IgE allergy (eosinophilic, neurogenic, or mast-cell-mediated without IgE). This is mechanistically plausible (IL-33/TSLP/ILC2 axis drives symptoms often labeled "allergic" without IgE).
  (e) The practical consequence: no therapeutic pathway involving IgE blockade (omalizumab, ligelizumab) or IgE-desensitization would be indicated for ME/CFS outside the atopic-comorbid subgroup — aligning with current practice.
- **Evidence:** reparramirez2001igelevels, kowal2002prevalence, conti1996, gimenezorenga2025blood (all supporting null); skowera2004type2 (requires reinterpretation under null); theoharides2019 (non-IgE framework).
- **Certainty:** 0.50 — this is the default scientific position (null favored until disproven), not a speculative one.
- **Falsifiable prediction (for the null itself):** If even under the null, at least one specific IgE (anti-spike, anti-alpha-gal, or anti-tick) is detected at significantly higher rates in ME/CFS vs controls, and correlates with clinical severity, the null is falsified. No current data meet this bar.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** Accepting that IgE is irrelevant in ME/CFS would mean redirecting research energy toward other mast-cell and immune pathways, not allergy treatments.
- **Suggested environment:** #speculation (the null itself is a fact, but its implications for paper integration are speculative)
- **origin:** brainstorm

### 12. Self-Reported Allergy vs Serological Mismatch: Measurement-Utility Concern
- **Category:** 12 — Evidence quality concern
- **Rationale:** ME/CFS patients consistently self-report higher allergy rates than controls, but serological markers (total IgE: reparramirez2001igelevels; allergen-specific IgE: kowal2002prevalence; eosinophil cationic protein: conti1996) are normal. This mismatch creates a problem for any study using self-reported atopy as a covariate or enrichment strategy. If self-reported allergy is driven by non-IgE mechanisms (substance P, CRH, MRGPRX2 acting on mast cells and producing histamine-release symptoms that patients label as "allergy"), then studies that stratify by self-reported allergy will systematically misclassify non-IgE mast-cell-activation as allergic. This confound would: (a) dilute any true IgE signal if present, (b) inflate apparent IgE relevance if studied only by self-report, (c) make it difficult to distinguish the IgE-hypothesis from the non-IgE-MCAS hypothesis. Any future serosurvey must include objective IgE measurements AND validated MC-mediator panels, not rely on patient-reported allergy history.
- **Evidence:** conti1996 (self-reported allergy high, ECP normal); reparramirez2001igelevels (total IgE normal with stratification); kowal2002prevalence (specific IgE normal); skowera2004type2 (Th2 bias, IgE-independent).
- **Certainty:** 0.70 — well-established pattern in ME/CFS literature. Mismatch is real and has been replicated over 25 years.
- **Specific concern:** Self-reported allergy confounds all existing epidemiological studies linking ME/CFS to atopy; the majority of "atopic" ME/CFS patients may have non-IgE mast-cell activation, not classical allergy. This undermines using self-report as a proxy for IgE-mediated disease.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** When ME/CFS patients say they have allergies, it may be their mast cells acting up from stress or inflammation rather than true IgE allergies — which means standard allergy tests and treatments may not work for them.
- **Suggested environment:** #limitation
- **origin:** brainstorm

### 13. Denatured Antigen Artifacts in Anti-Spike IgE Assays
- **Category:** 12 — Evidence quality concern
- **Rationale:** Many commercial and research anti-spike IgE assays use recombinant spike protein produced in E. coli or insect cells, which may present denatured/linear epitopes rather than native conformational ones. IgE detected against linear epitopes may not bind native spike on intact virions or infected cells — and therefore may not trigger mast-cell degranulation via FcεRI crosslinking. This is a known issue in food allergy diagnostics (e.g., peanut IgE to linear vs conformational Ara h 2). If tan2022hypersensitivity, meltendorf2022iligespike, or portilho2024igeanti used denatured antigen, their reported anti-spike IgE prevalence may overestimate functionally relevant IgE. Further, Western-blot-based detection (denaturing + reducing) is particularly susceptible. Any future ME/CFS serosurvey must use native, trimeric spike protein in a conformationally intact format (e.g., lentiviral pseudotype display or cell-surface expression) for IgE detection to ensure functional relevance.
- **Evidence:** General immunology (not in Phase 1). **Sub-research candidate:** a methodological review comparing denatured vs native antigen IgE detection in viral systems.
- **Certainty:** 0.30 — a recognized technical concern in allergy diagnostics, but the extent to which the specific Phase 1 anti-spike IgE papers are affected is unknown without reviewing their exact assay methods.
- **Specific concern:** If anti-spike IgE prevalence from existing literature is inflated by denatured-antigen artifacts, the entire evidentiary foundation for the spike→IgE→ME/CFS hypothesis is weakened proportionally.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** Some positive allergy blood tests may be laboratory artifacts, detecting antibodies that react with damaged virus protein pieces but wouldn't cause allergic reactions in the body.
- **Suggested environment:** #limitation
- **origin:** brainstorm
