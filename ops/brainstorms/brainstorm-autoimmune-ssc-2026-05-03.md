## Category 1 — Novel Hypotheses

### H1 — Complex III as Convergent Bottleneck in Post-Viral and Autoimmune Fatigue
**Category:** 1
**Target chapter:** ch06 (energy metabolism); secondary ch14d
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Rationale:** van Eeden 2023 found reduced CyB (Complex III) expression specifically in SSc patients meeting ME/CFS criteria, independent of fibrosis burden. ME/CFS literature shows mitochondrial dysfunction with variable site localization (Complex I, IV, V reported). Complex III sits at a Q-cycle bottleneck where reverse electron transport generates ROS. Hypothesis: when *any* upstream insult (post-viral, autoimmune, inflammatory) chronically elevates electron pressure on the ETC, Complex III becomes the rate-limiting failure point because CyB is mitochondrially encoded and translation is uniquely sensitive to mtDNA damage and inflammatory mtROS feedback. SSc-ME/CFS would then be a "convergent endpoint" model rather than a distinct disease.
**Evidence link:** van Eeden 2023; ME/CFS mitochondrial literature (Tomas, Missailidis, Booth)
**Falsifiable prediction:** In a head-to-head bioenergetic study (Seahorse + RNA-seq) of post-viral ME/CFS, SSc-ME/CFS, and Long COVID-ME/CFS patients, Complex III-specific deficit (CyB expression, antimycin-sensitive respiration fraction) will be present in ≥60% of all three groups despite differing upstream triggers — and absent in fatigued SSc patients NOT meeting ME/CFS criteria.

### H2 — ADRA2A Vasospasm as Autonomic Tone Driver in ME/CFS Cold Sensitivity and OI
**Category:** 1
**Target chapter:** ch10 (cardiovascular); secondary ch08
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Rationale:** Hartmann 2023 and Tervi 2024 both pin ADRA2A (α2A-adrenoreceptor) as the strongest Raynaud's risk locus. α2A is presynaptic on sympathetic nerve terminals AND postsynaptic on vascular smooth muscle — gain-of-function variants would cause both excess vasoconstriction AND impaired baroreflex feedback. ME/CFS shows cold extremities, Raynaud-like phenomena, and orthostatic intolerance. Hypothesis: a subset of ME/CFS patients (perhaps the cold-intolerant/Raynaud-positive cluster) carry ADRA2A risk variants that contribute mechanistically to both peripheral vasospasm AND impaired postural blood pressure regulation.
**Evidence link:** Hartmann 2023, Tervi 2024; ME/CFS Raynaud comorbidity reports
**Falsifiable prediction:** Genotyping ME/CFS patients at the ADRA2A lead Raynaud variants will show enrichment (OR ≥1.5) in patients with both Raynaud-positive history AND head-up tilt-positive orthostatic intolerance, vs ME/CFS patients with neither.

### H3 — eNOS/NOS3 Loss-of-Function as Shared Endothelial Substrate for SSc-Raynaud and ME/CFS Endothelial Dysfunction
**Category:** 1
**Target chapter:** ch10; secondary ch20
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Rationale:** Tervi 2024 identified NOS3 as a Raynaud's risk gene. ME/CFS literature (Scherbakov, Sandvik, Newton) reports impaired flow-mediated dilation and endothelial dysfunction. NOS3 produces endothelial NO; reduced NO causes both vasospasm and impaired microvascular reactivity during exercise (a candidate mechanism for PEM-associated muscle deoxygenation). Hypothesis: NOS3 hypofunction (genetic + acquired via BH4 depletion, ADMA accumulation, oxidative uncoupling) is a shared substrate linking SSc microvascular disease to ME/CFS exercise intolerance.
**Evidence link:** Tervi 2024; ME/CFS endothelial dysfunction literature
**Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS patients will show elevated plasma ADMA/SDMA and reduced post-occlusive reactive hyperaemia compared to controls, with the deficit correlating with PEM severity and partially rescuing with sapropterin (BH4) or L-citrulline supplementation in a controlled trial.

### H4 — Anti-Mitochondrial Antibody (AMA) Subset Bridges SSc, PBC, and a Subgroup of ME/CFS
**Category:** 1
**Target chapter:** ch14d; secondary ch20
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Rationale:** PBC and a SSc subset share AMA positivity; PBC-associated fatigue is severe and clinically resembles ME/CFS (Newton's group has long noted this). Hypothesis: a sub-cohort of ME/CFS patients (perhaps those with female predominance, autonomic features, and unexplained mild cholestasis) carry low-titer AMA that has been missed because it is not part of standard ME/CFS workup. The AMA target (PDC-E2) is on the inner mitochondrial membrane near Complex III — a mechanistic link to H1.
**Evidence link:** van Eeden 2022 (review of mitochondrial overlap); PBC-fatigue literature (Jones, Newton)
**Falsifiable prediction:** Screening 200 unselected ME/CFS patients for AMA-M2 will identify ≥3% positivity (vs ~0.5% population baseline), and AMA-positive ME/CFS patients will show worse autonomic scores (COMPASS-31) and lower CyB expression than AMA-negative ME/CFS.

### H5 — Cardiac Autonomic Neuropathy (CAN) as Hidden Phenotype in ME/CFS
**Category:** 1
**Target chapter:** ch10; secondary ch08
**Certainty:** 0.45
**Rationale:** Masini 2021 found 50% CAN prevalence in SSc, correlated with microvascular damage pattern (nailfold capillaroscopy). ME/CFS HRV studies show autonomic dysfunction but rarely apply formal CAN criteria (Ewing battery, MIBG scintigraphy). Hypothesis: a substantial fraction of ME/CFS patients meet formal CAN criteria when properly tested, and CAN burden correlates with severity and PEM threshold.
**Evidence link:** Masini 2021; ME/CFS HRV literature
**Falsifiable prediction:** Applying the Ewing 5-test battery to 100 ME/CFS patients will identify ≥30% with definite CAN (≥2 abnormal tests), with CAN status predicting CPET-2 day VO2 drop magnitude.

### H6 — Nailfold Capillaroscopy as Underused ME/CFS Microvascular Biomarker
**Category:** 1
**Target chapter:** ch20; secondary ch10
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Rationale:** Nailfold capillaroscopy is cheap, non-invasive, and routine in SSc — Masini 2021 used it to grade microvascular damage. ME/CFS endothelial dysfunction literature has not systematically applied this tool. Hypothesis: ME/CFS patients show subtle but reproducible nailfold capillary abnormalities (reduced density, dilated loops, microhaemorrhages) compared to controls, providing a bedside microvascular biomarker.
**Evidence link:** Masini 2021; ME/CFS endothelial literature
**Falsifiable prediction:** Blinded nailfold capillaroscopy of 50 ME/CFS vs 50 matched controls will show capillary density reduction ≥15% in the ME/CFS group, with a sensitivity ≥40% and specificity ≥80% for caseness.

## Category 2 — Research Directions

### R1 — SSc-ME/CFS as a Tractable "Pre-Existing Disease + ME/CFS" Cohort
**Category:** 2
**Target chapter:** ch14d
**Certainty:** 0.55
**Rationale:** SSc patients are followed in dedicated registries (SPIN n=2,385, EUSTAR), serially phenotyped, and have biobanked samples. A subset develops ME/CFS-criterion fatigue with PEM. This is one of the few settings where pre-onset baseline samples exist. Direction: prospective sub-study within SPIN/EUSTAR applying CCC/IOM criteria + 2-day CPET to identify and serially sample SSc-ME/CFS converters.
**Evidence link:** Kwakkenbos 2025, van Eeden 2023
**Falsifiable prediction:** A 5-year prospective sub-study will identify pre-conversion immunometabolic signatures (e.g., IFN-I score elevation, declining CyB, rising ADMA) detectable ≥6 months before CCC-criterion ME/CFS onset.

### R2 — PEM Testing in SSc Cohorts (Long Overdue)
**Category:** 2
**Target chapter:** ch14d
**Certainty:** 0.60
**Rationale:** van Eeden 2022 explicitly noted PEM has not been systematically tested in SSc. This is a gap that can be closed quickly with validated instruments (DSQ-PEM) or 2-day CPET.
**Evidence link:** van Eeden 2022
**Falsifiable prediction:** Administering DSQ-PEM to a SPIN sub-cohort (n≥500) will identify a PEM-positive subgroup of 15–25%, and that subgroup will be enriched for the immunometabolic markers in van Eeden 2023.

### R3 — Mendelian Randomization: Raynaud Loci → ME/CFS
**Category:** 2
**Target chapter:** ch20; secondary ch10
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Rationale:** Tervi 2024 and Hartmann 2023 provide well-powered Raynaud GWAS summary statistics. ME/CFS GWAS (DecodeME) is underway. Two-sample MR can test whether Raynaud genetic liability causally raises ME/CFS risk — distinguishing shared cause from coincidence.
**Evidence link:** Hartmann 2023, Tervi 2024
**Falsifiable prediction:** Two-sample MR using Raynaud GWAS as exposure and DecodeME as outcome will yield IVW OR >1.1 with p<0.01 for ME/CFS risk, robust to MR-Egger and weighted-median sensitivity tests.

### R4 — Single-Cell Mitochondrial Phenotyping in PBMCs Across Fatigue Diseases
**Category:** 2
**Target chapter:** ch06
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Rationale:** Single-cell mitochondrial respirometry (e.g., Resipher, Seahorse XFp on sorted populations) plus single-cell RNA-seq for ETC subunits would resolve which cell types carry the Complex III deficit. Currently CyB findings are bulk-tissue.
**Evidence link:** van Eeden 2023
**Falsifiable prediction:** sc-RNA-seq of PBMCs from SSc-ME/CFS, ME/CFS, and SSc-no-fatigue will show CyB deficit concentrated in CD8+ T cells and monocytes, not B cells.

### R5 — Vibration-Sensitive Cold Provocation Protocol
**Category:** 2
**Target chapter:** ch10
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Rationale:** Standardized cold-provocation tests with laser Doppler imaging are routine in SSc. Applying them to ME/CFS would quantify Raynaud-spectrum vasoreactivity in a comparable framework.
**Evidence link:** Hartmann 2023, Tervi 2024
**Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS patients will show prolonged rewarming time (>15 min, vs <8 min controls) in ≥35% of cases, correlating with self-reported cold intolerance.

## Category 3 — Drug/Medication Ideas

### D1 — α2A-Antagonist Repurposing (Yohimbine, Idazoxan) for Raynaud-Phenotype ME/CFS
**Category:** 3
**Target chapter:** ch14d (research-stage); secondary ch10
**Certainty:** 0.15 (speculative)
**Rationale:** ADRA2A as Raynaud risk gene (Hartmann 2023, Tervi 2024) suggests α2A blockade could reduce vasospasm. Yohimbine has been used in OI and erectile dysfunction; idazoxan has historical autonomic-research use. WARNING: α2A antagonism can worsen orthostatic hypotension and anxiety — this is a high-risk repurposing requiring careful selection.
**Evidence link:** Hartmann 2023, Tervi 2024
**Falsifiable prediction:** Low-dose yohimbine (2.5–5 mg) in a Raynaud-positive ME/CFS subgroup with normal supine BP will improve digital rewarming time without worsening orthostatic symptoms, in a randomized crossover trial (n≥30).

### D2 — Sapropterin (BH4) Replacement for eNOS Uncoupling
**Category:** 3
**Target chapter:** ch10
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Rationale:** NOS3 GWAS hit (Tervi 2024) + ME/CFS endothelial dysfunction → BH4 depletion is a candidate mechanism (eNOS uncoupling produces superoxide rather than NO). Sapropterin is FDA-approved for PKU; off-label use in cardiovascular disease has mixed results.
**Evidence link:** Tervi 2024
**Falsifiable prediction:** A 12-week sapropterin trial (10 mg/kg/day) in ME/CFS patients with documented impaired flow-mediated dilation will improve FMD by ≥2% absolute and reduce orthostatic symptom scores, vs placebo.

### D3 — Low-Dose Naltrexone in SSc-ME/CFS (Targeted Subgroup Trial)
**Category:** 3
**Target chapter:** ch14d
**Certainty:** 0.20
**Rationale:** LDN has anecdotal benefit in both SSc (small case series) and ME/CFS. SSc-ME/CFS is a uniquely well-phenotyped overlap cohort where LDN response could be tested with biomarker readouts (CyB, IFN-I score).
**Evidence link:** van Eeden 2022, 2023
**Falsifiable prediction:** A randomized SSc-ME/CFS LDN trial (n≥40, 16 weeks, 4.5 mg) will show ≥30% PEM-frequency reduction in responders, enriched in patients with high baseline IFN-I score.

### D4 — Riociguat (sGC Stimulator) — High-Risk High-Reward
**Category:** 3
**Target chapter:** ch10 (research-stage)
**Certainty:** 0.10 (speculative)
**Rationale:** Riociguat is approved for SSc-associated PAH; it bypasses NO-dependent sGC activation. If ME/CFS endothelial dysfunction is partly NO/cGMP-pathway downstream, riociguat could rescue. WARNING: hypotension risk; only justifiable in severely affected, OI-tolerant subset with documented endothelial dysfunction.
**Evidence link:** Tervi 2024 (NOS3); ME/CFS endothelial literature
**Falsifiable prediction:** A small open-label pilot (n=10) in ME/CFS patients with impaired FMD and no orthostatic hypotension will improve 6-min walk distance by ≥30 m at 12 weeks.

### D5 — Mitochondrial Cofactor Stack Targeting Complex III (CoQ10 + Riboflavin + Idebenone)
**Category:** 3
**Target chapter:** ch06; secondary ch14d
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Rationale:** CyB deficit (van Eeden 2023) sits at Complex III where CoQ10 is the substrate. CoQ10 deficiency phenocopies Complex III dysfunction. Idebenone is a synthetic CoQ analogue with better bioavailability and is approved for LHON (a Complex I disorder, but rationale extends).
**Evidence link:** van Eeden 2023
**Falsifiable prediction:** A CoQ10 (300 mg) + riboflavin (400 mg) trial in ME/CFS patients with documented low plasma CoQ10 will improve fatigue scale scores by ≥0.5 SD vs placebo at 12 weeks.

## Category 4 — Supplement/Nutraceutical Ideas

### S1 — L-Citrulline (NO Substrate) for Endothelial Phenotype
**Category:** 4
**Target chapter:** ch10
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Rationale:** L-citrulline raises plasma arginine more reliably than oral arginine (which is gut-metabolized) and supports eNOS substrate availability. Cheap, well-tolerated. Direct rationale from NOS3 hit.
**Evidence link:** Tervi 2024
**Falsifiable prediction:** L-citrulline 6 g/day for 8 weeks will improve flow-mediated dilation by ≥1.5% absolute in ME/CFS patients with baseline FMD <6%, in a placebo-controlled crossover (n≥30).

### S2 — Coenzyme Q10 (Ubiquinol form) Targeted at CyB-Deficit Subgroup
**Category:** 4
**Target chapter:** ch06
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Rationale:** Ubiquinol (reduced CoQ10) bypasses the reductase step and is more bioavailable. The CyB deficit specifically implicates Complex III, where CoQ is the substrate. This is a more targeted rationale than generic "mitochondrial support."
**Evidence link:** van Eeden 2023
**Falsifiable prediction:** Stratified trial: CoQ10 effect size will be ≥2× larger in ME/CFS patients with low baseline plasma CoQ10 (<0.7 µg/mL) than those with normal levels.

### S3 — Glycine + N-Acetylcysteine (GlyNAC) for mtROS / GSH Restoration
**Category:** 4
**Target chapter:** ch06
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Rationale:** Sekhar's group has shown GlyNAC restores GSH and improves mitochondrial function in aging and HIV. ME/CFS mtROS and oxidative stress literature suggests similar deficit. Cheap, safe.
**Evidence link:** Indirect (van Eeden 2023 mitochondrial pattern)
**Falsifiable prediction:** GlyNAC (Gly 100 mg/kg + NAC 100 mg/kg) for 12 weeks will raise erythrocyte GSH by ≥20% and reduce 8-isoprostane by ≥15% in ME/CFS, with fatigue benefit in the responder subgroup.

### S4 — Niacinamide / NMN for NAD+ Restoration in Complex III Bottleneck
**Category:** 4
**Target chapter:** ch06
**Certainty:** 0.20
**Rationale:** Complex III dysfunction backs up NADH→NAD+ recycling, lowering cytosolic NAD+ and inhibiting glycolysis. Restoring NAD+ pool with NMN/NR/niacinamide may partially compensate. Evidence in ME/CFS is limited (one small NMN trial).
**Evidence link:** van Eeden 2023
**Falsifiable prediction:** NMN 500 mg/day for 12 weeks will raise whole-blood NAD+ by ≥30% and improve handgrip endurance by ≥10% in ME/CFS patients, with effect size correlated with baseline lactate/pyruvate ratio.

### S5 — Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin) at Migraine Doses for Flavoprotein Support
**Category:** 4
**Target chapter:** ch06
**Certainty:** 0.20
**Rationale:** Riboflavin 400 mg/day is established for migraine prophylaxis with mitochondrial rationale. FAD/FMN cofactor demand rises when ETC is stressed. Very safe.
**Evidence link:** Indirect
**Falsifiable prediction:** Riboflavin 400 mg/day for 12 weeks will reduce headache and brain-fog days by ≥20% in the ME/CFS-with-migraine subgroup.

## Category 5 — Non-Pharmacological Interventions

### N1 — Standardized Heat-Therapy Protocol (Inverse of Cold Provocation)
**Category:** 5
**Target chapter:** ch14d; secondary ch10
**Certainty:** 0.20
**Rationale:** SSc patients use thermoregulation protocols. Mild repeated sauna/heat (Waon therapy in CHF literature) improves endothelial function and may help cold-phenotype ME/CFS — but heat intolerance is also common in ME/CFS, so individualization is essential.
**Evidence link:** Indirect; SSc clinical practice
**Falsifiable prediction:** 4 weeks of low-temperature far-infrared sauna (40°C, 15 min, 3×/week) in cold-intolerant non-heat-intolerant ME/CFS patients will improve digital rewarming time and self-reported cold symptoms, without worsening PEM, in ≥50% of participants.

### N2 — Slow Diaphragmatic Breathing (6 breaths/min) for Vagal-Sympathetic Rebalancing
**Category:** 5
**Target chapter:** ch10; secondary ch08
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Rationale:** Resonant-frequency breathing raises HRV and shifts autonomic balance. Given Masini 2021's finding of 50% CAN in SSc and likely substantial CAN in ME/CFS, a low-cost, caregiver-implementable, severity-accessible intervention is valuable.
**Evidence link:** Masini 2021 + ME/CFS HRV literature
**Falsifiable prediction:** 6 breaths/min for 10 min twice daily over 8 weeks will increase RMSSD by ≥10 ms in ME/CFS patients with low baseline HRV (<25 ms), and a subset will show OI symptom reduction.

### N3 — Compression Garments at Calibrated Pressures for OI
**Category:** 5
**Target chapter:** ch10
**Certainty:** 0.45
**Rationale:** Already used clinically; SSc literature has compression data. Calibrating pressure (20-30 mmHg vs 30-40 mmHg) and length (knee-high vs thigh-high vs abdominal) by individual response is under-explored.
**Evidence link:** ME/CFS OI literature; Masini 2021 microvascular finding
**Falsifiable prediction:** A pressure-titration protocol (sequential 15→20→30 mmHg) in OI-positive ME/CFS will identify a per-patient optimum that improves NASA Lean Test tolerance by ≥3 minutes vs uncompressed baseline.

### N4 — Nailfold Capillaroscopy-Guided Pacing Calibration
**Category:** 5
**Target chapter:** ch20; secondary ch12 (pacing)
**Certainty:** 0.15 (speculative)
**Rationale:** If H6 holds, nailfold capillary density tracks microvascular reserve and may correlate with daily energy envelope. A bedside test usable for severity-monitoring without exertion would be valuable for very-severe patients.
**Evidence link:** Masini 2021
**Falsifiable prediction:** In a 6-month longitudinal study, week-to-week changes in nailfold capillary density (or microhaemorrhage count) will correlate (r ≥0.3) with weekly PEM frequency.

### N5 — Cold-Avoidance + Heated Microenvironment as Caregiver-Implementable Severe-ME Protocol
**Category:** 5
**Target chapter:** ch12 (severe ME care); secondary ch14d
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Rationale:** Cold triggers vasospasm in Raynaud-phenotype patients; severe ME patients cannot self-thermoregulate effectively. Caregiver-implementable: heated bedding, hand warmers, room-temperature stability, cold-avoidance routines. Low-cost, accessible.
**Evidence link:** Hartmann 2023, Tervi 2024 + clinical Raynaud care
**Falsifiable prediction:** A standardized caregiver protocol (room ≥22°C, heated extremity wraps during cold weather) will reduce reported "crash" frequency by ≥20% in severe-ME patients with documented cold sensitivity, in a 12-week within-subject design.

## Category 6 — Combinations and Access

### C1 — "Endothelial Rescue Stack": L-Citrulline + CoQ10 + Slow Breathing
**Category:** 6
**Target chapter:** ch10
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Rationale:** Three low-cost, low-risk interventions targeting NO substrate, Complex III bypass, and autonomic balance. All implementable at any severity level. Combined effect plausibly synergistic (substrate + cofactor + neural modulation).
**Evidence link:** Tervi 2024, van Eeden 2023, Masini 2021
**Falsifiable prediction:** A factorial 2×2×2 trial will show super-additive interaction (interaction term p<0.1) for FMD improvement vs single-component arms.

### C2 — Severity-Tiered SSc-ME/CFS Protocol
**Category:** 6
**Target chapter:** ch12; secondary ch14d
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Rationale:** Mild–moderate: sapropterin or citrulline + CoQ10 + paced breathing + compression. Severe: cold-avoidance + heated microenvironment + caregiver-administered citrulline + CoQ10. Very severe: thermal stability + breathing audio guidance only (no active interventions). Tiering matches access reality.
**Evidence link:** Composite
**Falsifiable prediction:** A registry comparing tiered-protocol uptake vs ad-hoc care will show ≥15% improvement in 12-month functional stability in the tiered cohort.

### C3 — IFN-I Score-Stratified Treatment Allocation
**Category:** 6
**Target chapter:** ch14d; secondary ch20
**Certainty:** 0.20
**Rationale:** If IFN-I-high ME/CFS responds differently to LDN, JAK inhibitors, or anti-IFNAR (anifrolumab in lupus), stratification by IFN-I score before allocation could massively improve trial efficiency.
**Evidence link:** Already-integrated `spec:ssc-ifn-fatigue-model`
**Falsifiable prediction:** In a basket trial, IFN-I-high ME/CFS will show ≥2× greater response rate to anti-IFN intervention than IFN-I-low.

## Category 7 — Mathematical Model Extensions

### M1 — Add Microvascular Perfusion Compartment to ODE Energy Model
**Category:** 7
**Target chapter:** ch06; secondary ch10
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Rationale:** Existing ODE models of ME/CFS energy metabolism rarely include a microvascular oxygen-delivery compartment. Adding a perfusion variable (P) with eNOS-dependent dilation (NO term) and α2A-dependent constriction (sympathetic term) would couple the autonomic and metabolic submodels and predict why patients with NOS3/ADRA2A risk show steeper PEM curves.
**Evidence link:** Tervi 2024, Hartmann 2023
**Falsifiable prediction:** The extended model with perfusion compartment will reproduce the observed lag between exertion and PEM peak (24–72 h) only when perfusion-recovery time constants exceed metabolic-recovery time constants, predicting that NO-substrate restoration shortens PEM lag by ≥6 h.

### M2 — Complex III Saturation Term in ETC Submodel
**Category:** 7
**Target chapter:** ch06
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Rationale:** If CyB is the convergent bottleneck (H1), an explicit Vmax/Km term for Complex III throughput predicts threshold behavior: below a critical CyB level, small additional electron load causes disproportionate ROS and energy collapse — a candidate mechanism for "PEM threshold."
**Evidence link:** van Eeden 2023
**Falsifiable prediction:** Patients with the lowest decile of CyB expression will show the steepest dose-response curve (slope ≥2× cohort median) between CPET workload and 48-h symptom score.

### M3 — DAG Extension: ADRA2A and NOS3 as Upstream Genetic Nodes
**Category:** 7
**Target chapter:** ch20
**Certainty:** 0.30
**Rationale:** Causal DAG of ME/CFS biomarkers should include genetic priors. Adding ADRA2A → vasospasm → cold/OI and NOS3 → endothelial NO → FMD/PEM as upstream nodes makes the model testable via Mendelian randomization (R3).
**Evidence link:** Hartmann 2023, Tervi 2024
**Falsifiable prediction:** d-separation tests on ME/CFS GWAS + biomarker data will show ADRA2A independent of NOS3 conditional on phenotype, consistent with two distinct upstream causes.

## Category 8 — Cross-Disease Bridges

### B1 — PBC Fatigue Literature as ME/CFS Translational Source
**Category:** 8
**Target chapter:** ch14d
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Rationale:** PBC fatigue is severe, AMA-positive, and has been studied for decades (Newton, Jones). Many findings (autonomic dysfunction, cognitive symptoms, pacing benefit, modafinil mixed results) prefigure ME/CFS literature. Mining PBC interventional trial data for transferable insights is underused.
**Evidence link:** van Eeden 2022 (mitochondrial/autonomic overlap)
**Falsifiable prediction:** Re-analysis of PBC fatigue RCTs (modafinil, rituximab, ondansetron) will identify intervention-responder subgroups whose biomarker profile matches an ME/CFS subgroup.

### B2 — POTS-Raynaud Co-occurrence as Autonomic-Vascular Bridge
**Category:** 8
**Target chapter:** ch10
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Rationale:** Raynaud's prevalence in POTS cohorts is reportedly elevated. If both share ADRA2A genetic background (Hartmann 2023), this is a testable cross-disease bridge.
**Evidence link:** Hartmann 2023; POTS-Raynaud comorbidity reports
**Falsifiable prediction:** ADRA2A risk-allele frequency will be elevated (OR ≥1.3) in POTS+Raynaud co-occurring patients vs POTS-only.

### B3 — SSc Microvascular Damage Trajectory as Predictive Template for Severe ME/CFS
**Category:** 8
**Target chapter:** ch12; secondary ch20
**Certainty:** 0.20
**Rationale:** SSc has a well-characterized progression (early → active → late nailfold pattern; Cutolo). If ME/CFS shows a parallel microvascular trajectory, SSc staging tools could be repurposed for ME/CFS severity stratification.
**Evidence link:** Masini 2021
**Falsifiable prediction:** A 5-year longitudinal nailfold study in 100 ME/CFS patients will reveal ≥3 distinguishable microvascular stages, with stage transitions correlating with self-reported severity changes.

## Category 9 — Diagnostic / Biomarker Ideas

### Bk1 — IFN-I Score + CyB Expression as Two-Axis Stratifier
**Category:** 9
**Target chapter:** ch20; secondary ch14d
**Certainty:** 0.40
**Rationale:** Two orthogonal axes (immune activation vs metabolic deficit) define a 2×2 stratification: high-IFN/low-CyB (autoimmune-metabolic), high-IFN/normal-CyB (autoimmune-only), low-IFN/low-CyB (post-viral metabolic), low-IFN/normal-CyB (other). This yields four candidate ME/CFS subtypes with distinct treatment implications.
**Evidence link:** van Eeden 2023, `spec:ssc-ifn-fatigue-model`
**Falsifiable prediction:** Cluster analysis of IFN-I score + CyB on a ME/CFS cohort (n≥150) will yield ≥3 reproducible clusters with distinct symptom profiles (PEM severity, autonomic burden, OI), validated in an independent cohort.

### Bk2 — Plasma ADMA / SDMA / Arginine Ratio as Endothelial Reserve Index
**Category:** 9
**Target chapter:** ch20; secondary ch10
**Certainty:** 0.35
**Rationale:** ADMA inhibits eNOS; arginine/ADMA ratio is an established cardiovascular biomarker. Cheap LC-MS assay. Direct mechanistic link to NOS3 hit (Tervi 2024).
**Evidence link:** Tervi 2024
**Falsifiable prediction:** Arginine/ADMA ratio will be ≥20% lower in ME/CFS vs matched controls and correlate negatively with PEM frequency (r ≤ -0.3).

### Bk3 — Composite Autonomic Score (Ewing Battery + COMPASS-31 + HRV)
**Category:** 9
**Target chapter:** ch20; secondary ch08, ch10
**Certainty:** 0.50
**Rationale:** Borrowed directly from SSc autonomic workup (Masini 2021). Already-validated instruments. Implementation is largely a clinical-protocol issue, not a discovery issue.
**Evidence link:** Masini 2021
**Falsifiable prediction:** A composite Z-score from these three measures will discriminate ME/CFS from healthy controls with AUC ≥0.85 and predict 2-day CPET VO2 drop magnitude (r ≥ 0.4).

### Bk4 — Nailfold Capillaroscopy Density Index
**Category:** 9
**Target chapter:** ch20
**Certainty:** 0.25
**Rationale:** Bedside, no exertion, non-invasive, suitable for very-severe patients. See H6/N4.
**Evidence link:** Masini 2021
**Falsifiable prediction:** See H6.

### Bk5 — Anti-Mitochondrial Antibody Panel as Add-On to ME/CFS Workup
**Category:** 9
**Target chapter:** ch20
**Certainty:** 0.20
**Rationale:** Cheap, widely available immunoassay. May identify a treatable subset (UDCA-responsive cholestatic-fatigue overlap).
**Evidence link:** van Eeden 2022
**Falsifiable prediction:** See H4.

### Bk6 — Two-Day CPET + Genetic Risk Score Composite Severity Index
**Category:** 9
**Target chapter:** ch20
**Certainty:** 0.20
**Rationale:** Combine 2-day CPET VO2 drop with a Raynaud-locus polygenic risk score (ADRA2A + NOS3 + others from Tervi 2024). Genetic axis adds stable trait information; CPET adds state information.
**Evidence link:** Tervi 2024, Hartmann 2023
