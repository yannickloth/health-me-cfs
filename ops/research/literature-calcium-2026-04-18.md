## Papers Found and Added (8 new)

### 1. Nguyen et al. 2017 — First TRPM3 Impairment
**Key:** `Nguyen2017trpm3` | **PMID:** 27727448 | **Certainty:** 0.55
**Journal:** Clin Exp Immunol | **n:** 15 CFS/ME, 25 HC
**Finding:** First study linking TRPM3 dysfunction to impaired Ca2+ mobilization in ME/CFS NK cells. CD56bright NK cells: reduced TRPM3 surface expression; elevated Ca2+ flux after PregS stimulation in patients.
**Stream relevance:** Foundation of TRPM3-channelopathy hypothesis; replicated by 5+ subsequent studies.

### 2. Cabanas et al. 2019a — Patch-Clamp Validation
**Key:** `Cabanas2019trpm3val` | **PMID:** 31014226 | **Certainty:** 0.55
**Journal:** Molecular Medicine | **n:** 12 matched pairs
**Finding:** Whole-cell patch-clamp confirms significantly reduced TRPM3 current amplitude in CFS/ME NK cells using multiple pharmacological probes (PregS, nifedipine, ononetin).
**Stream relevance:** Gold-standard electrophysiology validation; cited as validation study by Sasso et al. 2026.

### 3. Cabanas et al. 2019b — Naltrexone Restores TRPM3 In Vitro
**Key:** `Cabanas2019trpm3ldnFront` | **PMID:** 31736966 | **Certainty:** 0.50
**Journal:** Frontiers in Immunology | **n:** 8/8
**Finding:** 24h NTX incubation fully restores TRPM3 channel activity in IL-2-stimulated ME/CFS NK cells. Mechanism: opioid receptor antagonism removes inhibitory block on TRPM3.
**Stream relevance:** First pharmacological reversal of TRPM3 dysfunction; mechanistic basis for LDN clinical use.

### 4. Eaton-Fitch et al. 2021 — TRPM3/PIP2 and NK Degranulation
**Key:** `EatonFitch2021trpm3pip2` | **PMID:** 34266470 | **Certainty:** 0.50
**Journal:** J Translational Medicine | **n:** 15/15
**Finding:** TRPM3/PIP2 co-localization significantly reduced in ME/CFS NK cells. PIP2 pathway required for granule polarization, perforin pore formation, granzyme-dependent apoptosis. IL-2 partially compensates.
**Stream relevance:** Mechanistic link between TRPM3 Ca2+ dysfunction and impaired NK degranulation; explains reduced cytotoxic killing.

### 5. Eaton-Fitch et al. 2022 — TRPM3 Ca2+ Influx + NTX (Expanded)
**Key:** `EatonFitch2022trpm3ntx` | **PMID:** 35172836 | **Certainty:** 0.55
**Journal:** J Translational Medicine | **n:** 10/10
**Finding:** Ca2+ amplitude and T1/2 significantly reduced at baseline (p<0.0001). Overnight NTX fully restores TRPM3-dependent Ca2+ influx. Confirms and expands Cabanas 2019b.
**Stream relevance:** Larger replication of NTX restoration finding; strengthens therapeutic case for LDN.

### 6. du Preez et al. 2023 — TRPM7 Calcium Dysregulation
**Key:** `DuPreez2023trpm7` | **PMID:** 37509075 | **Certainty:** 0.45
**Journal:** Biomolecules | **n:** 9/9
**Finding:** TRPM7-dependent Ca2+ influx slope significantly reduced in ME/CFS following TRPM7 activation. Suggests broader TRP channelopathy involving TRPM7 in addition to TRPM3.
**Stream relevance:** Expands channelopathy model from TRPM3-specific to multi-channel TRP dysfunction; supports subtype B systemic channelopathy hypothesis.

### 7. Vijayan et al. 2015 — Thalamic Alpha-Delta Sleep Model
**Key:** `Vijayan2015alphaDelta` | **PMID:** 26245315 | **Certainty:** 0.50
**Journal:** J Neurophysiology | **n:** Mathematical model
**Finding:** Alterations in GABAB, Ih, and K+ leak currents transform delta oscillations into alpha-delta pattern. CaV3 T-type channels prevalent in thalamus as substrate. Predicts that reducing Ih conductance may restore delta sleep.
**Stream relevance:** Mechanistic basis for thalamic Ca2+ channel hypothesis in non-restorative sleep; applicable to ME/CFS via fibromyalgia overlap.

### 8. Crunelli et al. 2006 — Thalamic T-Type Ca2+ and NREM Sleep
**Key:** `Crunelli2006thalamicTtype` | **PMID:** 16777223 | **Certainty:** 0.65
**Journal:** Cell Calcium | **n:** CaV3.1 KO mice + review
**Finding:** T-type Ca2+ channels (CaV3.1) generate delta waves and sleep spindles. CaV3.1 KO mice show reduced NREM sleep. Establishes CaV3.1 as most crucial conductance for thalamic sleep activity.
**Stream relevance:** Foundational basic science for thalamic Ca2+ channel hypothesis; explains how VGCC disruption impairs delta sleep quality.

## Null Results / Gaps

- "Subtype A / Subtype B" nomenclature for calcium channelopathy subtypes not found in peer-reviewed literature (user-proposed framework, not yet in published papers)
- No independent replication of TRPM7 findings (du Preez 2023) yet
- No in vivo demonstration of TRPM3 dysfunction in non-NK cell tissues (brain neurons, muscle) in ME/CFS patients — proposed but not yet shown
- Thalamic alpha2delta subunit specific to ME/CFS: no direct ME/CFS study found; Vijayan 2015 model is for fibromyalgia (with pain overlap)
- Timofeev (mentioned in request): the relevant 2015 paper is Vijayan et al. (J Neurophysiol 2015); Timofeev himself has separate thalamocortical work but no ME/CFS-specific alpha-delta model found under that name
