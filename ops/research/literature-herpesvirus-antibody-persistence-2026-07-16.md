# Literature Summary: Herpesvirus Antibody Persistence Decades After Infection

## Papers Found: 15

### Paper 1: Amanna et al. (2007) — Duration of Humoral Immunity to Common Viral and Vaccine Antigens
- **DOI:** 10.1056/NEJMoa066092
- **Certainty:** 0.85
- **Type:** primary (longitudinal cohort)
- **Key findings:** 45 subjects followed up to 26 years. Antiviral antibody responses remarkably stable: VZV half-life ~50 yr; EBV, measles, mumps >200 yr. Tetanus/diphtheria waned faster (11–19 yr). Memory B-cell numbers did NOT correlate with serum antibody titers for 5/8 antigens, suggesting plasma cells and memory B cells are independently regulated.
- **Relevance to question:** Foundational demonstration that antiviral antibodies (including EBV, VZV) persist for decades via LLPCs, NOT via ongoing antigenic stimulation. Directly challenges the assumption that elevated titres require active viral replication.
- **Limitations:** n=45; single US cohort; healthy subjects only

### Paper 2: Hammarlund et al. (2017) — Plasma Cell Survival in the Absence of B Cell Memory
- **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-017-01901-w
- **Certainty:** 0.80
- **Type:** primary (rhesus macaque, decade-long)
- **Key findings:** Antibody responses and bone marrow plasma cells persisted for years after sustained memory B cell depletion. BrdU+ plasma cells detected 10 years after vaccination without cell division. LLPCs alone suffice for long-term serological memory — B cell memory is not required.
- **Relevance to question:** Direct experimental proof that LLPCs maintain antibody titres antigen-independently. If this holds for herpesviruses, elevated titres in ME/CFS need not indicate reactivation.
- **Limitations:** Non-human primate model (n=12); vaccination not herpesvirus infection; but the principle applies generically to any LLPC-derived antibody

### Paper 3: Robinson et al. (2022) — Long-Lived Plasma Cells Accumulate in Bone Marrow at a Constant Rate
- **DOI:** 10.1126/sciimmunol.abm8389
- **Certainty:** 0.75
- **Type:** primary (mouse genetic timestamping)
- **Key findings:** PCs accrue in bone marrow at ~1 cell/hour over weeks after immunization. LLPC lifespan is partly intrinsic/preprogrammed. Final number relates to duration of germinal centre response. Affinity-based selection evident but threshold is dynamic.
- **Relevance to question:** Mechanism by which LLPC pools are established. Suggests that initial infection magnitude (not just ongoing reactivation) determines long-term antibody levels.
- **Limitations:** Mouse model; single immunization; may not capture human herpesvirus latency dynamics

### Paper 4: Chackerian & Peabody (2020) — Factors That Govern the Induction of Long-Lived Antibody Responses
- **DOI:** 10.3390/v12010074
- **Certainty:** 0.70
- **Type:** review
- **Key findings:** LLPCs persist for decades. Multivalent, dense antigen arrays preferentially drive LLPC differentiation. The structural properties of the antigen — not just antigen persistence — determine antibody longevity.
- **Relevance to question:** Herpesviruses present highly multivalent, repetitive epitopes (viral capsids, envelope glycoproteins), which are precisely the type of antigen that maximally drives LLPC generation. This predicts that herpesvirus infections should produce exceptionally durable antibody responses.
- **Limitations:** Review; no original data

### Paper 5: Ionescu & Urschel (2019) — Memory B Cells and Long-Lived Plasma Cells
- **DOI:** 10.1097/TP.0000000000002594
- **Certainty:** 0.65
- **Type:** review
- **Key findings:** PCs can persist independently in bone marrow for years or decades without needing replacement from differentiating B cells. Reviews evidence that some LLPCs undergo self-renewal without proliferation.
- **Relevance to question:** Confirms the independent LLPC survival paradigm from a transplantation immunology perspective.
- **Limitations:** Transplant-focused; not virus-specific

### Paper 6: Slifka & Amanna (2019) — Role of Multivalency and Antigenic Threshold
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fimmu.2019.00956
- **Certainty:** 0.75
- **Type:** review
- **Key findings:** Highly multivalent antigens trigger qualitatively different B cell responses (lower activation threshold, preferential LLPC induction). Extended review of principles from Amanna 2007 and Hammarlund 2017.
- **Relevance to question:** Provides mechanistic framework: herpesviruses (enveloped, repetitive capsid) are structurally optimized for durable antibody responses.
- **Limitations:** Review; draws on vaccine literature

### Paper 7: Palomo et al. (2026) — Chronic Reactivation of EBV, HHV-6, VZV in ME/CFS
- **DOI:** 10.1002/jmv.70769
- **Certainty:** 0.60
- **Type:** primary (case-control)
- **Key findings:** 873 longitudinal serum samples (40 ME/CFS, 16 controls). Significant increase in dUTPase IgG to EBV, HHV-6, VZV in ME/CFS (p<0.001). 72.5% of ME/CFS co-expressed multiple herpesvirus antibodies vs 31% controls. Antibody levels clustered with moderate-severe fatigue.
- **Relevance to question:** Supports herpesvirus antibody elevation in ME/CFS. However, dUTPase is an early lytic protein — antibodies to it may reflect recent reactivation events, not just stable LLPC output.
- **Limitations:** Small control group (n=16); cross-sectional analysis of longitudinal samples; dUTPase specificity means this may detect reactivation events rather than steady-state antibody levels

### Paper 8: Blomberg et al. (2019) — Antibodies to Human Herpesviruses in ME/CFS — Null Result
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fimmu.2019.01946
- **Certainty:** 0.65
- **Type:** primary (case-control) — **null**
- **Key findings:** Swedish ME/CFS cohort (Canada criteria) vs blood donors. Suspension multiplex immunoassay for HHV-1-7 IgG. **Overall, IgG anti-herpes-viral reactivities did NOT show significant differences.** Minor relative differences in HHV-1 whole virus and EBV EBNA6/EA antigens, but authors caution against over-interpretation.
- **Relevance to question:** Critical null finding — at least one well-conducted study found no overall herpesvirus antibody elevation. Contradicts the Palomo 2026 narrative. Suggests study design, antigen selection, and population differences matter enormously.
- **Limitations:** Controls were blood donors (possible healthy volunteer bias); cross-sectional; Swedish population only

### Paper 9: Domingues et al. (2023) — Herpesvirus IgG and Symptoms in ME/CFS
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.heliyon.2023.e18250
- **Certainty:** 0.60
- **Type:** primary (re-analysis, n=222 ME/CFS)
- **Key findings:** UK ME/CFS Biobank. Six herpesviruses (CMV, EBV, HHV-6, HSV-1/2, VZV) IgG vs symptomology in 222 ME/CFS patients, 46 MS controls. **Most significant association: increasing HSV1 IgG and brain fog.** More antibody-symptom associations in MS than ME/CFS. IgG data did NOT distinguish ME/CFS subgroups from each other well.
- **Relevance to question:** Large well-characterised cohort. Herpesvirus IgG explains more symptomology in MS than ME/CFS. Modest support for HSV1 cognitive effects. EBV/CMV/HHV-6/VZV associations weaker.
- **Limitations:** Cross-sectional; antibody-symptom association only (not causal); multiple comparisons

### Paper 10: Loebel et al. (2017) — Serological Profiling of EBV Immune Response in CFS
- **DOI:** 10.1371/journal.pone.0179124
- **Certainty:** 0.65
- **Type:** primary (case-control)
- **Key findings:** Peptide microarray (3054 peptides from 14 EBV proteins) comparing 92 CFS patients vs 50 controls. **Overall EBV IgG response pattern quite similar** between CFS and controls. Significantly enhanced IgG to EBNA-6 repeat region in CFS; correlated with EBNA-6 protein. EBNA-6 repeat shows homology to human proteins (antigenic mimicry candidate).
- **Relevance to question:** Most comprehensive EBV seroprofiling in CFS. "Quite similar" overall pattern argues against dramatic EBV reactivation — the differences are specific (EBNA-6 repeat) rather than broad. Could reflect epitope spreading or cross-reactivity.
- **Limitations:** Peptide arrays miss conformational epitopes; CFS subset (those with EBV seropositivity); single German cohort

### Paper 11: Apostolou et al. (2022) — Saliva Antibody Fingerprint of Reactivated Latent Viruses in ME/CFS
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fimmu.2022.949787
- **Certainty:** 0.55
- **Type:** primary (case-control)
- **Key findings:** 95 ME/CFS, 110 healthy donors — saliva antibody profiling. SARS-CoV-2 triggered reactivation of EBV, HHV-6, HERV-K in both groups. In ME/CFS, EBNA-1 IgG significantly stronger, EBV-VCA IgG elevated at baseline. **Antibody elevation detected locally in saliva, not systemically.**
- **Relevance to question:** Novel finding that local (mucosal) antibody may capture reactivation missed by plasma. Suggests ME/CFS patients have "chronically aroused" anti-viral profile against latent viruses.
- **Limitations:** Non-vaccinated cohort (hard to find post-2021); saliva sampling less standardised than plasma; antibody fingerprint measures viral reactivation indirectly

### Paper 12: Liu et al. (2023) — Fibronectin, Natural IgM Depletion, EBV/HSV-1 Reactivation in ME/CFS
- **DOI:** 10.1101/2023.06.23.23291827
- **Certainty:** 0.40
- **Type:** preprint (medRxiv)
- **Key findings:** Antibody response to herpesvirus dUTPases, increased serum fibronectin, depletion of natural IgM against fibronectin common to severe ME/CFS and long COVID. dUTPase-mediated cytoskeletal/mitochondrial dysfunction.
- **Relevance to question:** Links herpesvirus antibody responses to mechanistic cellular dysfunction. However, preprint — not yet peer-reviewed.
- **Limitations:** Preprint; mechanistic claims based on in vitro dUTPase experiments; clinical-translational gap

### Paper 13: Hannestad et al. (2025) — EBV, HHV-6, HCMV in Sputum from ME/CFS
- **DOI:** 10.3390/v17030422
- **Certainty:** 0.50
- **Type:** primary (case-control)
- **Key findings:** 13 ME/CFS, 16 controls (including immunosuppressed). Higher EBV viral load in sputum of ME/CFS (p=0.0256). HHV-6 present in ~50% of all participants at same level. Anti-IFN-I autoAbs not different except in severe case.
- **Relevance to question:** Direct viral detection (sputum EBV load) supports active EBV replication in ME/CFS, not just antibody elevation. Small sample but corroborates reactivation hypothesis.
- **Limitations:** n=13 ME/CFS only; sputum may not reflect systemic viral activity; small control group

### Paper 14: Cliff et al. (2019) — Cellular Immune Function in ME/CFS — Null Herpesvirus Serology
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fimmu.2019.00796
- **Certainty:** 0.70
- **Type:** primary (case-control) — **null**
- **Key findings:** UK ME/CFS Biobank — 251 ME/CFS (54 severe), 107 healthy, 46 MS. **No differences in seroprevalence for six human herpesviruses** between ME/CFS and controls (despite elevated EBV in MS). T cell compartment altered (effector memory CD8+ increased, terminally differentiated decreased), but NK cell numbers/function normal.
- **Relevance to question:** Largest herpesvirus seroprevalence study in ME/CFS (n=251). Null finding for IgG seroprevalence is strong counter-evidence to the hypothesis that ME/CFS patients have elevated herpesvirus antibodies. Supports the view that antibody elevation is not a universal ME/CFS feature.
- **Limitations:** UK cohort; seroprevalence (yes/no) not quantitative titre; cross-sectional

### Paper 15: Sun et al. (2024) — Immunometabolic Changes in CFS by scRNA-seq
- **DOI:** 10.1186/s12967-024-05710-w
- **Certainty:** 0.45
- **Type:** primary (scRNA-seq, n=4 patients)
- **Key findings:** Memory B cells in ME/CFS show unique subtype early in pseudotime, with increased differentiation to plasma cells suggesting B cell overactivity. T cells show suppressed differentiation. NK cells show reduced cytotoxicity.
- **Relevance to question:** Indirect biochemical link: if memory B cells are overactive and preferentially differentiate to plasma cells, this could drive elevated antibody production even without antigenic stimulation. Potential mechanistic basis for elevated antibody titres without reactivation.
- **Limitations:** n=4 patients, n=4 controls — very small; single time point; peripheral blood only

## Summary

**Strength of evidence:** The core immunology is very well established (Amanna 2007, Hammarlund 2017, Certainty 0.75–0.85): LLPCs maintain antibody titres for decades without antigen, and herpesviruses (as multivalent antigens) are especially potent at inducing LLPCs.

**ME/CFS evidence is mixed:**
- **Supporting elevation:** Palomo 2026 (dUTPase IgG), Apostolou 2022 (saliva EBNA-1), Hannestad 2025 (sputum EBV load), Loebel 2017 (EBNA-6 enhanced)
- **Null/no difference:** Blomberg 2019 (overall HHV-1-7 IgG no difference), Cliff 2019 (seroprevalence no difference)
- **Mixed/partial:** Domingues 2023 (HSV1 symptom association but EBV/CMV/HHV-6 weaker)

**Key unresolved issue:** Antibody elevation in ME/CFS may be specific to certain antigen types (dUTPase, EBNA-6 repeat) rather than a global increase. This argues for a qualitative rather than quantitative difference in the antibody response — possibly epitope spreading, cross-reactivity, or altered B cell regulation rather than active viral reactivation. The Sun 2024 scRNA-seq finding (increased memory B cell to plasma cell differentiation) provides a potential mechanism for elevated antibody without viral reactivation.
