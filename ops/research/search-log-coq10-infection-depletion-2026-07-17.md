## Inclusion Criteria

1. Directly measures or discusses CoQ10 in ME/CFS (primary)
2. Provides biochemical mechanism linking infection/ROS to antioxidant depletion (mechanism)
3. Demonstrates viral reactivation causing mitochondrial dysfunction/ROS (mechanism)
4. Shows post-viral oxidative damage and antioxidant depletion (analogous)
5. Addresses competing non-infectious mechanisms of CoQ10 deficiency (competing)
6. Published in peer-reviewed journal
7. English language

## Exclusion Criteria

1. CoQ10 supplementation trials/outcomes (separate topic cycle — do not duplicate)
2. ME/CFS oxidative stress papers without CoQ10 component
3. Non-English
4. Conference abstracts without follow-up publication

## Key Search Gaps Identified

1. **No paper directly links EBV/enterovirus load with CoQ10 levels** — this is the critical missing link
2. **No longitudinal study** of CoQ10 from pre- to post-infection
3. **No COQ gene polymorphism study** in ME/CFS to distinguish consumption from biosynthesis defect
4. **CoQ10 consumption rate** has never been measured in any human disease — only inferred from product accumulation
5. **Tissue CoQ10** levels in ME/CFS (muscle, brain) completely unstudied
