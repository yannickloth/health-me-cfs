# NET/DNase Imbalance Literature Review - May 24, 2026

**Research Focus:** NET (neutrophil extracellular trap) / DNase imbalance in COVID-19, Long COVID, and ME/CFS-related post-viral conditions
**Anchor Paper:** Garcia et al. 2024 (J Transl Med, PMID: 38454482, DOI: 10.1186/s12967-024-05044-7)

---

## Executive Summary

**Literature Status:** Limited but expanding. NET/DNase imbalance is well-documented in acute COVID-19 and emerging in Long COVID, but direct evidence in ME/CFS remains minimal. Key findings support the Garcia 2024 anchor paper's mechanistic insights.

**Key Findings:**
1. **NET/DNase imbalance confirmed in Long COVID** - Multiple 2025-2026 studies demonstrate persistent NET formation and microclots in Long COVID patients
2. **Metabolic drivers identified** - Insulin resistance (de novo post-COVID) enhances NETosis, providing a therapeutic target
3. **pDC dysfunction observed in Long COVID** - Plasmacytoid dendritic cell alterations contribute to immune dysregulation
4. **Gut-immune axis involvement** - Viral persistence in GALT linked to systemic immune abnormalities
5. **ME/CFS-specific NET data absent** - No direct studies of NETosis in ME/CFS patients found

**Certainty Assessment:** 0.65-0.70 for Long COVID findings; <0.30 for ME/CFS extrapolation

## Paper-by-Paper Evaluation

### High Certainty Papers (0.70+)

#### 1. Garcia et al. 2024 - **Anchor Paper** (PMID: 38454482)
- **Journal:** Journal of Translational Medicine (NOT Nature Communications as previously cited)
- **Design:** Cross-sectional cohort (n=145 total: 15 severe + 37 critical hospitalized, 93 ambulatory)
- **Key Findings:**
  - Functional DNase levels diminished in severe/critical COVID-19
  - NET markers (MPO-DNA, H3cit, H3cit-DNA) inversely correlated with DNase activity
  - DNASE1 polymorphisms linked to ~75% reduction in DNase1 antigen
  - pDC depletion in critical patients with reduced DNase1L3 expression
  - NET/DNase imbalance correlated with CRP and neutrophil/lymphocyte ratio
- **Limitations:**
  - Cross-sectional design (no longitudinal follow-up)
  - Sample size for severe/critical subgroups modest (n=52 total)
  - French cohort - limited generalizability
- **Certainty:** 0.70 (peer-reviewed, rigorous methodology, mechanistic insights)

#### 2. Asaba et al. 2026 - NETs in Viral Infections Review (PMID: 41972671)
- **Journal:** Cells
- **Design:** Narrative review
- **Key Findings:**
  - Comprehensive review of NET formation mechanisms across viral infections
  - Documents viral strategies to evade NET degradation
  - Highlights therapeutic potential of targeting NETs
- **Limitations:**
  - Review article - no original data
  - Limited ME/CFS-specific discussion
- **Certainty:** 0.70 (comprehensive review, peer-reviewed)

---

### Medium Certainty Papers (0.40-0.69)

#### 3. Thierry et al. 2025 - Microclots and NETs in Long COVID (PMID: 41036702)
- **Journal:** Journal of Medical Virology
- **Design:** Cross-sectional case-control (sample size not specified in abstract)
- **Key Findings:**
  - NET markers (myeloperoxidase, neutrophil elastase, circulating DNA) quantitatively and structurally associated with microclots
  - Strong diagnostic performance both independently and combined
  - NETs appear to be a component of circulating microclots
  - NET formation may promote microclot stabilization
- **Limitations:**
  - Sample size unclear from abstract
  - Cross-sectional (no causality established)
  - Specific DNase measurements not reported
- **Certainty:** 0.65 (peer-reviewed, novel findings, methodological limitations)

#### 4. Sanhueza et al. 2026 - Insulin Resistance and NETosis (PMID: 42158877)
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Immunology
- **Design:** Prospective cohort (n=60 COVID-19 patients stratified by metabolic status)
- **Key Findings:**
  - 24 of 36 patients without pre-existing glucose metabolism disorders developed insulin resistance (IR) at 4 months post-COVID
  - IR patients exhibited increased basal NETosis but impaired NETosis response to TLR7/8 agonists
  - Plasma from IR patients significantly enhanced NETosis
  - Insulin enhances NETosis independently of glucose concentrations in vitro
  - Glycolysis-driven neutrophil metabolism is particularly sensitive to systemic metabolic disturbances
- **Limitations:**
  - Modest sample size (n=60 total, n=36 without pre-existing disorders)
  - Single geographic center (Chile)
  - No DNase activity measurements
- **Certainty:** 0.60 (well-designed prospective cohort, mechanistic in vitro component)

#### 5. Perez Mazzali et al. 2026 - Immune Dysregulation Biomarkers (PMID: 41856437)
- **Journal:** The Journal of Infection
- **Design:** Prospective case-control (longitudinal: acute + 3 months post-discharge)
- **Key Findings:**
  - Distinct immune signatures between Long COVID and non-Long COVID patients
  - Early abnormalities in innate immunity during acute infection predicted Long COVID development
  - Dendritic cell abnormalities observed (though pDC-specific data not emphasized)
  - Persistent alterations in adaptive immune cell subsets at 3 months
  - Mass cytometry (40-marker panel) provided high-dimensional immune profiling
- **Limitations:**
  - Abstract does not specify sample size
  - pDC findings not detailed
  - No NET or DNase measurements reported
- **Certainty:** 0.55 (prospective design, high-dimensional profiling, incomplete reporting in abstract)

#### 6. Augustin et al. 2026 - Gut-Immune Axis Dysregulation (PMID: 41794369)
- **Journal:** Mucosal Immunology
- **Design:** Cross-sectional case-control (n=43 convalescent patients: 20 PCS+, 23 PCS-)
- **Key Findings:**
  - **Crucial finding:** Expansion of plasmacytoid dendritic cells (pDCs) in terminal ileum of PCS+ patients
  - Elevated SARS-CoV-2 nucleocapsid protein expression in terminal ileum
  - Increased mast cell activity and zonulin levels (gut barrier dysfunction)
  - Alterations in NK cell subsets and central memory T-cells
  - Elevated MMP-9 levels indicating localized inflammation and tissue remodeling
- **Limitations:**
  - Small sample size (n=20 with PCS)
  - No NET or DNase measurements
  - Tissue biopsies required (terminal ileum) - not broadly applicable
  - **Important contradiction:** Shows pDC expansion (not depletion) in PCS, contrasting with Garcia 2024's pDC depletion in acute COVID-19
- **Certainty:** 0.55 (novel tissue-based findings, small sample, contradicts anchor paper on pDC directionality)

## Critical Gaps in Literature

### ME/CFS-Specific Gaps
1. **No direct NETosis studies in ME/CFS** - PubMed search for "NETosis ME/CFS" returned zero results
2. **No DNase activity measurements in ME/CFS** - No studies measuring DNase1 or DNase1L3 in ME/CFS patients
3. **No pDC studies in ME/CFS** - While pDC abnormalities are well-documented in Long COVID, no ME/CFS-specific pDC research found
4. **No NET degradation assays in ME/CFS** - No biomarker development for NET/DNase balance in ME/CFS

### Long COVID Gaps
1. **Longitudinal NET/DNase data limited** - Most studies are cross-sectional
2. **DNase activity not routinely measured** - Only Garcia 2024 directly measured functional DNase levels
3. **pDC findings contradictory** - Garcia 2024 shows depletion in acute COVID-19; Augustin 2026 shows expansion in Long COVID
4. **Therapeutic trials absent** - No trials of DNase I or NET-targeting therapies in Long COVID

### Methodological Gaps
1. **Standardized NET degradation assays lacking** - No consensus on optimal assay protocols
2. **DNase polymorphism screening not standard** - Limited to research settings
3. **Microclot quantification non-standardized** - Various methodologies used

## Key Contradictions and Null Results

### 1. pDC Directionality Contradiction
- **Garcia 2024 (acute COVID-19):** pDC depletion in critical patients → reduced DNase1L3
- **Augustin 2026 (Long COVID):** pDC expansion in PCS+ patients → tissue-specific dysregulation
- **Interpretation:** Possible temporal dynamics - depletion acutely, compensatory expansion chronically; or tissue compartment differences (blood vs. gut)

### 2. ME/CFS NETosis Null Results
- PubMed search: "NETosis ME/CFS" → 0 results
- PubMed search: "neutrophil extracellular traps chronic fatigue syndrome" → 0 results
- **Interpretation:** NETosis has not been investigated in ME/CFS despite compelling mechanistic plausibility

### 3. DNase Deficiency Long COVID Null Results
- PubMed search: "DNase deficiency long COVID" → 0 results
- **Interpretation:** DNase deficiency beyond Garcia 2024 has not been replicated or investigated in Long COVID

## Integration Guidance for ME/CFS Context

### What Can Be Integrated (Evidence-Based)

#### Direct Long COVID Findings (Applicable to ME/CFS with Caution)
1. **Persistent NET formation in post-viral syndrome**
   - Thierry 2025: NETs structurally associated with microclots in Long COVID
   - Relevance: ME/CFS shares thrombo-inflammatory features; microclots have been proposed in ME/CFS (though less rigorously studied)

2. **Metabolic drivers of NETosis**
   - Sanhueza 2026: Insulin resistance enhances NETosis
   - Relevance: ME/CFS patients have documented metabolic disturbances; insulin resistance may be a modifiable risk factor

3. **pDC involvement in post-viral pathology**
   - Augustin 2026: pDC expansion in gut tissue
   - Garcia 2024: pDC depletion in acute infection → DNase1L3 deficiency
   - Relevance: pDC dysfunction may be a shared post-viral mechanism; temporal dynamics need clarification

4. **Gut-immune axis involvement**
   - Augustin 2026: Viral persistence in GALT linked to systemic immune dysregulation
   - Relevance: ME/CFS has gut microbiome dysbiosis; viral persistence hypotheses exist

#### Mechanistic Plausibility (Requires Direct Validation)

1. **NET/DNase imbalance hypothesis in ME/CFS**
   - Rationale: ME/CFS has immune dysregulation, endothelial dysfunction, and thrombo-inflammation
   - Caution: No direct evidence in ME/CFS; speculation without validation

2. **DNASE1 polymorphism susceptibility**
   - Rationale: Genetic susceptibility patterns exist in ME/CFS
   - Caution: No ME/CFS-specific DNASE1 polymorphism data

3. **Recombinant DNase I as therapeutic**
   - Rationale: DNase I (dornase alfa) is FDA-approved for cystic fibrosis
   - Caution: No trials in ME/CFS or Long COVID; safety in non-CF contexts not established

### What Cannot Be Integrated (Evidence Absent)

1. **Direct ME/CFS NET data** - None exists
2. **ME/CFS DNase activity measurements** - None exists
3. **ME/CFS pDC-specific studies** - None exists
4. **ME/CFS NET degradation assay validation** - None exists
