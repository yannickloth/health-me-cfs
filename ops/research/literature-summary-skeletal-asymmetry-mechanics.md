## Path C Certainty Assessment (post the full-indirect-load analysis)

The strongest direct mechanistic paper is **Schulte2010** (0.45): sympathetic chain lesions documented from mechanical disruption of the anterolateral thoracic spine. This establishes that the sympathetic chain *can* be injured by spinal pathology — a necessary precondition for the hypothesis. **He2014** (0.50) establishes the reverse direction: ANS affects bone, meaning bidirectional ANS-skeletal interaction is established.

However:
- Zero papers test the forward chain: skeletal asymmetry → autonomic dysfunction → fatigue
- The only paper testing a related asymmetry→fatigue pathway in a neurological disease (**Proessl2018**) found null results (MS, n=15)
- The hypothesis depends on a chain of 4+ inferential steps with no direct evidence at any link

## Overall Assessment

**10 papers included. Quality range: 0.15 (case report) to 0.70 (Rett natural history).**

**Strongest:** Killian2017 (n=913, multicenter, longitudinal; but Rett-specific)

**Weakest:** Chu2023 (n=1, case report)

**What the evidence establishes:**
1. Sympathetic chain is anatomically vulnerable to thoracic spine pathology (Schulte2010)
2. Scoliosis co-occurs with autonomic dysfunction in neurological diseases (Rett syndrome; Killian2017)
3. Leg length discrepancy is treated with shoe lifts but fatigue outcomes are unstudied (Campbell2019)
4. Compensatory-overuse fatigue has precedent in post-polio syndrome (Jubelt2004)
5. A null signal: leg asymmetry does NOT predict fatigue in MS (Proessl2018)

**What the evidence does NOT establish:**
- Any direct link between idiopathic skeletal asymmetry and ME/CFS
- Any mechanism by which rotoscoliosis would produce multi-system metabolic failure
- Any treatment effect of postural correction on fatigue
- Any evidence for DAMI/ILMI as clinical entities (zero PubMed results)
