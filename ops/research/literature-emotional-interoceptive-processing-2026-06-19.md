# Literature Summary: Emotional Processing, Interoceptive Processing, and Illness Cognitions in ME/CFS

**Date:** 2026-06-19
**Topic slug:** emotional-interoceptive-processing

## Included Papers Summary

### Core Interoception Framework

**1. Garfinkel et al. 2015 — Knowing Your Own Heart: Distinguishing Interoceptive Accuracy from Interoceptive Awareness**
- *Journal:* Biological Psychology
- *DOI:* 10.1016/j.biopsycho.2014.11.004
- *Design:* Experimental (heartbeat detection + self-report)
- *Sample:* Healthy volunteers (n=108)
- *Key contribution:* Established tripartite model: interoceptive accuracy (objective performance), interoceptive sensibility (self-reported), interoceptive awareness (metacognitive insight). The accuracy-sensibility dissociation is foundational — these dimensions are frequently dissociated in clinical populations
- *Certainty:* 0.75 (well-replicated framework; n=108; >1500 citations; independent replication by multiple groups)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* The accuracy-sensibility dissociation provides the critical framework for understanding why patients may report heightened body awareness while showing objective interoceptive deficits — key for interpreting CFS interoception findings

**2. Quadt, Critchley & Garfinkel 2018 — The Neurobiology of Interoception in Health and Disease**
- *Journal:* Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences
- *DOI:* 10.1111/nyas.13915
- *Design:* Narrative review
- *Key contribution:* Comprehensive review establishing interoception as transdiagnostic mechanism in psychiatric and neurological disorders. Insula as hub; interoceptive predictive coding framework. Links interoceptive dysfunction to anxiety, depression, autism, functional disorders
- *Certainty:* 0.80 (highly cited; authoritative review)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Establishes the theoretical basis for applying interoceptive framework to chronic fatigue states. Directly relevant to the paper's existing interoceptive predictive processing content in ch14d

**3. Seth, Suzuki & Critchley 2011 — An Interoceptive Predictive Coding Model of Conscious Presence**
- *Journal:* Frontiers in Psychology
- *DOI:* 10.3389/fpsyg.2011.00395
- *Design:* Theoretical/model paper
- *Key contribution:* Foundational predictive coding model of interoception. Proposes that conscious presence emerges from prediction error minimization in interoceptive processing. Introduces hierarchical interoceptive inference with precision weighting
- *Certainty:* 0.70 (highly influential; >900 citations)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Directly relevant to the paper's existing free energy/interoceptive inference framework. Provides the theoretical basis for understanding altered presence/interoception in chronic illness

### Interoceptive Processing in Fatigue States

**4. Greenhouse-Tucknott et al. 2022 — Toward the Unity of Pathological and Exertional Fatigue: A Predictive Processing Model**
- *Journal:* Cognitive, Affective, & Behavioral Neuroscience
- *DOI:* 10.3758/s13415-021-00958-x
- *Authors:* Greenhouse-Tucknott A, Butterworth JB, Wrightson JG, Smeeton NJ, Critchley HD, Dekerle J, Harrison NA
- *Design:* Theoretical review
- *Key contribution:* Proposes unified predictive coding model bridging pathological fatigue (chronic illness) and exertional fatigue (healthy). Fatigue = increased prior precision for effort costs → reduced willingness to exert. Concept of "precision-weighted effort estimation"
- *Certainty:* 0.60 (well-argued theoretical model; not empirically tested; moderate citations)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Directly applicable — provides computational mechanism for fatigue perception unifying peripheral and central contributions. Among the few papers explicitly bridging exercise physiology and predictive coding

**5. Manjaly, Harrison, Critchley et al. 2019 — Pathophysiological and Cognitive Mechanisms of Fatigue in Multiple Sclerosis**
- *Journal:* Journal of Neurology, Neurosurgery & Psychiatry
- *DOI:* 10.1136/jnnp-2018-320050
- *Design:* Narrative review + computational model proposal
- *Key contribution:* Links interoceptive processing, inflammation, and predictive coding models of fatigue in MS. Proposes that fatigue arises from dysregulated interoceptive prediction error signaling involving anterior cingulate and insula
- *Certainty:* 0.75 (high-impact journal; well-cited; multi-author expertise across neuroimmunology, computational psychiatry)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* The MS fatigue model directly parallels ME/CFS — inflammation-driven interoceptive predictive processing changes leading to pathological fatigue. Establishes cross-disease mechanism

**6. Danciut, Garfinkel et al. 2024 — Understanding the Mechanisms of Fatigue in MS: Linking Interoception, Metacognition and White Matter Dysconnectivity**
- *Journal:* Brain Communications
- *DOI:* 10.1093/braincomms/fcae292
- *Design:* Cross-sectional behavioral + DTI study
- *Sample:* n=33 MS patients + 33 healthy controls
- *Key contribution:* Empirical link between interoceptive accuracy, metacognitive insight (interoceptive awareness), and fatigue severity in MS. White matter dysconnectivity in interoceptive network correlates with both interoceptive deficits and fatigue
- *Certainty:* 0.55 (moderate sample; MS-specific; well-controlled; single study; no replication yet)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* First empirical demonstration that interoceptive accuracy deficits correlate with fatigue severity — provides template for ME/CFS studies. White matter dysconnectivity in insular tracts as mechanism

### Barrett/Lisa Feldman Barrett — Constructed Emotion/Allostasis Framework

**7. Barrett et al. 2025 — The Theory of Constructed Emotion: More Than a Feeling**
- *Journal:* Perspectives on Psychological Science
- *DOI:* 10.1177/17456916251319045
- *Design:* Theoretical review and response to critics
- *Key contribution:* Comprehensive defense and update of the Theory of Constructed Emotion. Emotions = predictions constructed from interoceptive and exteroceptive sensory arrays, categorized using prior experience. Core brain networks (salience, default mode, frontoparietal) instantiate emotion categories from interoceptive predictions
- *Certainty:* 0.75 (high-impact journal; extensively cited; major theoretical framework)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Constructed emotion framework predicts that chronic interoceptive prediction error (from inflammation, autonomic dysfunction) would fundamentally alter emotional experience. Directly relevant to understanding ME/CFS emotional processing changes

### Wager — Brain-Based Predictive Models of Interoception/Emotion

**8. Zhang, Wager et al. 2025 — Cortical and Subcortical Mapping of the Human Allostatic-Interoceptive System Using 7 Tesla fMRI**
- *Journal:* Nature Neuroscience
- *DOI:* 10.1038/s41593-025-02087-x
- *Design:* 7T fMRI study (n=200+)
- *Key contribution:* Comprehensive mapping of the allostatic-interoceptive system at high resolution. Identifies widespread cortical (anterior insula, ACC, mPFC) and subcortical (hypothalamus, PAG, amygdala, brainstem nuclei) network for interoception + allostasis. Replicates and extends previous findings
- *Certainty:* 0.80 (Nat Neurosci; large sample; 7T resolution; replication study)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Provides the neural reference map for the interoceptive-allostatic system that would be dysregulated in ME/CFS. Key brain regions overlap with known ME/CFS abnormalities (insula, ACC, brainstem autonomic nuclei)

### Inflammation-Sickness Behavior-Interoception Link

**9. Harrison et al. 2009 — Neural Origins of Human Sickness in Interoceptive Responses to Inflammation**
- *Journal:* Biological Psychiatry
- *DOI:* 10.1016/j.biopsych.2009.03.007
- *Design:* Experimental (typhoid vaccination inflammatory challenge)
- *Sample:* n=16 healthy volunteers
- *Key contribution:* Landmark study showing typhoid vaccination-induced inflammation alters brain activity in interoceptive regions (insula, ACC). Inflammatory cytokines modulate interoceptive cortex, linking immune activation to altered interoceptive-emotional processing
- *Certainty:* 0.65 (modest sample; well-replicated paradigm; landmark paper; >400 citations)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Direct experimental evidence for the inflammation→interoceptive processing pathway. Foundational for cytokine-induced emotional processing changes in chronic inflammatory states

**10. Savitz & Harrison 2018 — Interoception and Inflammation in Psychiatric Disorders**
- *Journal:* Biological Psychiatry: Cognitive Neuroscience and Neuroimaging
- *DOI:* 10.1016/j.bpsc.2017.12.011
- *Design:* Narrative review
- *Key contribution:* Systematic review of the inflammation-interoception link. Humoral immune signals (cytokines) directly modulate interoceptive brain regions via neural (vagal) and humoral (circumventricular organ) pathways. Proposes that inflammation-induced interoceptive changes contribute to emotional symptoms in psychiatric disorders
- *Certainty:* 0.70 (well-cited; authoritative; integrates immunology + interoception)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Provides the mechanistic bridge between inflammation (well-established in ME/CFS) and interoceptive/emotional processing changes. Strongly supportive of the bidirectional immune-interoceptive axis in ME/CFS

### Alexithymia and Emotional Processing in ME/CFS

**11. Maroti, Molander & Bileviciute-Ljungar 2017 — Differences in Alexithymia and Emotional Awareness in Exhaustion Syndrome and Chronic Fatigue Syndrome**
- *Journal:* Scandinavian Journal of Psychology
- *DOI:* 10.1111/sjop.12332
- *Design:* Cross-sectional
- *Sample:* n=43 CFS, n=34 exhaustion syndrome, n=43 healthy controls
- *Key findings:* Elevated alexithymia in both CFS and exhaustion syndrome compared to controls (TAS-20). Difficulty Identifying Feelings factor most elevated. No difference in externally-oriented thinking. CFS patients showed lower emotional awareness than exhaustion syndrome
- *Certainty:* 0.50 (moderate sample; single study; adequate measures; no replication in independent sample)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Direct evidence for alexithymia in ME/CFS. Difficulty identifying feelings may reflect interoceptive deficits — consistent with interoceptive accuracy-sensibility dissociation. Distinguishes CFS from stress-related exhaustion

**12. Bileviciute-Ljungar & Friberg 2020 — Emotional Awareness Correlated With Number of Awakenings From PSG in ME/CFS**
- *Journal:* Frontiers in Psychiatry
- *DOI:* 10.3389/fpsyt.2020.00222
- *Design:* Cross-sectional (polysomnography + questionnaires)
- *Sample:* n=30 ME/CFS patients
- *Key findings:* Lower emotional awareness (TAS-20) correlated with more frequent nocturnal awakenings on PSG. No significant correlation with other sleep parameters. Suggests emotional-interoceptive processing linked to sleep fragmentation in ME/CFS
- *Certainty:* 0.40 (modest sample; pilot study; no control group; single study)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Links emotional awareness deficits with objective sleep disruption in ME/CFS. Small but specific finding connecting interoceptive-emotional processing to sleep architecture — one of the few studies doing so

**13. Brooks, Chalder & Rimes 2017 — CFS: Cognitive, Behavioural and Emotional Processing Vulnerability Factors**
- *Journal:* Behavioural and Cognitive Psychotherapy
- *DOI:* 10.1017/S1352465816000631
- *Design:* Cross-sectional behavioral measures
- *Sample:* n=67 CFS patients (UK tertiary care)
- *Key findings:* Higher emotional processing difficulties (ambivalence over emotional expression, thought suppression) associated with CFS. These factors correlated with fatigue severity. Emotional processing vulnerability distinguished CFS patients from population norms
- *Certainty:* 0.45 (moderate CFS sample; single-center; cross-sectional; no control group)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Identifies emotional processing factors specifically in CFS population. Relevance limited by cognitive-behavioural framing but provides empirical data on emotional regulation differences

**14. Rimes, Ashcroft, Bryan & Chalder 2016 — Emotional Suppression in CFS: Experimental Study**
- *Journal:* Health Psychology
- *DOI:* 10.1037/hea0000341
- *Design:* Experimental (emotion suppression task)
- *Sample:* n=80 (40 CFS, 40 controls)
- *Key findings:* CFS patients showed greater self-reported emotional suppression. During experimental suppression task, CFS patients had lower EDA response (reduced autonomic reactivity) but higher self-reported distress. Dissociation between physiological and subjective emotional response
- *Certainty:* 0.55 (moderate sample; experimental design; well-controlled; single study)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Experimental evidence of emotion-autonomic dissociation in CFS. The gap between blunted autonomic reactivity and heightened subjective distress parallels interoceptive accuracy-sensibility dissociation. Directly relevant to understanding interoceptive-emotional processing in ME/CFS

**15. Wortinger et al. 2017 — Emotional Conflict Processing in Adolescent CFS: A Pilot fMRI Study**
- *Journal:* Journal of Clinical and Experimental Neuropsychology
- *DOI:* 10.1080/13803395.2016.1230180
- *Design:* fMRI pilot study
- *Sample:* n=20 adolescents (10 CFS, 10 controls)
- *Key findings:* Altered neural activation in anterior cingulate and prefrontal cortex during emotional conflict processing in CFS. Behavioural performance did not differ groups, but brain activation patterns diverged — suggests compensatory neural recruitment
- *Certainty:* 0.40 (very small sample; adolescent-specific; pilot; single study)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Only fMRI study of emotional conflict processing in ME/CFS. Provides neural evidence for altered emotion processing circuitry despite preserved behavioral performance. ACC/insula findings converge with interoceptive processing literature

### Negative Affect — Somatic Symptom Amplification

**16. Van Den Houte et al. 2017 — Inducing Somatic Symptoms in Functional Syndrome Patients: Effects of Manipulating State Negative Affect**
- *Journal:* Psychosomatic Medicine
- *DOI:* 10.1097/PSY.0000000000000527
- *Design:* Experimental (negative affect induction)
- *Sample:* n=35 FM patients, n=29 CFS patients, n=30 healthy controls
- *Key findings:* Inducing negative affect amplified somatic symptom reporting in both FM and CFS patients. Effect significantly stronger than in controls. Symptom amplification mediated by negative affect — but does not imply psychological causation
- *Certainty:* 0.60 (Psychosom Med; adequate sample; experimental design; well-controlled)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Important for understanding affect-symptom amplification pathway without falling into psychogenic causation fallacy. Shows that negative affect genuinely increases symptom perception, but this is a normal psychophysiological pathway that is exaggerated in CFS — not evidence of psychological etiology

### Catastrophizing — Null and Negative Results

**17. Sohl & Friedberg 2008 — Memory for Fatigue in CFS: Relationships to Fatigue Variability, Catastrophizing, and Negative Affect**
- *Journal:* Behavioral Medicine
- *DOI:* 10.3200/BMED.34.1.29-38
- *Design:* Cross-sectional (ecological momentary + retrospective recall)
- *Sample:* n=43 CFS patients
- *Key findings:* Catastrophizing correlated with retrospective fatigue recall bias, not with concurrent fatigue ratings. Patients who catastrophized overestimated past fatigue. Catastrophizing did not predict objective activity levels or momentary fatigue
- *Certainty:* 0.40 (modest CFS sample; single study; limited catastrophizing measure)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Important null-context finding: catastrophizing affects fatigue memory but not real-time fatigue experience or behavior. This limits the clinical significance of catastrophizing in CFS — it may primarily distort recall rather than drive symptoms

**18. Poort et al. 2021 — Condition or Cognition? Mechanism of Change in Fatigue RCT**
- *Journal:* Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology
- *DOI:* 10.1037/ccp0000670
- *Design:* RCT mediation analysis (GET vs CBT for cancer fatigue)
- *Sample:* n=134 (advanced cancer patients with severe fatigue)
- *Key findings:* Changes in catastrophizing did NOT mediate fatigue improvement in either GET or CBT. Fatigue reduction was mediated by changes in physical conditioning (GET) and perceived activity (CBT), not by cognitive variables including catastrophizing
- *Certainty:* 0.65 (RCT; formal mediation analysis; adequate sample; published in top clinical psychology journal)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Null result for catastrophizing as mediator of treatment outcome. If catastrophizing does not drive fatigue improvement even in CBT for fatigue, its role as a treatment target in CFS is questionable. Supports the paper's existing critique of catastrophizing as explanatory construct

### Competing Mechanisms

**19. Kang et al. 2026 — Chronic Stress and Cognitive Dysfunction in ME/CFS: HPA Axis Dysregulation and Hippocampal Plasticity**
- *Journal:* Frontiers in Neuroscience
- *DOI:* 10.3389/fnins.2026.1814098
- *Design:* Narrative review
- *Key contribution:* Reviews HPA axis dysregulation in ME/CFS as alternative mechanism for cognitive and emotional changes. Proposes hippocampal plasticity as key mediator. Cortisol dysregulation → hippocampal atrophy → cognitive deficits + emotional dysregulation
- *Certainty:* 0.50 (review-level; not systematic; published 2026)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Provides competing mechanism: emotional/interoceptive changes may be driven primarily by HPA axis dysregulation and cortisol, rather than interoceptive predictive processing. Both may coexist. Useful for framing alternative explanations

### Body Awareness in Chronic Pain/Fatigue

**20. Valenzuela-Moguillansky, Reyes-Reyes & Gaete 2017 — Exteroceptive and Interoceptive Body-Self Awareness in Fibromyalgia Patients**
- *Journal:* Frontiers in Human Neuroscience
- *DOI:* 10.3389/fnhum.2017.00117
- *Design:* Cross-sectional behavioral study
- *Sample:* n=34 FM patients, n=36 healthy controls
- *Key findings:* FM patients showed altered body awareness: increased attention to bodily sensations but decreased accuracy in tactile localization. Dissociation between body awareness dimensions — elevated subjective body focus with impaired objective body processing
- *Certainty:* 0.50 (moderate sample; FM-specific; single study; no CFS comparison)
- *ME/CFS relevance:* Fibromyalgia (highly comorbid with ME/CFS) shows the interoceptive accuracy-sensibility dissociation pattern. Provides indirect evidence for similar dissociation in ME/CFS. Body awareness measures applicable to CFS populations

## Key Findings Across All Papers

1. **Interoceptive accuracy-sensibility dissociation** (Garfinkel framework) is well-established in health and disease but never directly tested in ME/CFS — a critical gap
2. **Predictive coding models of fatigue** (Greenhouse-Tucknott 2022; Manjaly 2019; Seth 2011) provide computational mechanisms linking interoceptive processing to fatigue perception
3. **Inflammation directly modulates interoceptive brain regions** (Harrison 2009; Savitz/Harrison 2018) providing a causal pathway from ME/CFS immune abnormalities to altered interoceptive-emotional processing
4. **Alexithymia is elevated in ME/CFS** (Maroti 2017; Bileviciute-Ljungar 2020) specifically in difficulty identifying feelings — consistent with interoceptive accuracy deficit
5. **Emotional-autonomic dissociation** occurs in CFS (Rimes 2016) — blunted peripheral reactivity with heightened subjective distress
6. **Catastrophizing affects fatigue recall but not real-time experience** (Sohl & Friedberg 2008) and does NOT mediate treatment outcomes (Poort 2021) — limiting its clinical significance
7. **Negative affect amplifies somatic symptom reporting** (Van Den Houte 2017) but this is a normal psychophysiological mechanism exaggerated in CFS, not evidence of psychogenesis
8. **HPA axis dysregulation** (Kang 2026) provides a competing pathway for emotional/cognitive changes that may coexist with interoceptive predictive processing alterations
9. **The allostatic-interoceptive network** has now been comprehensively mapped (Zhang/Wager 2025) — ME/CFS dysregulation can be mapped onto specific nodes (insula, ACC, brainstem)

## Contradictions Identified

1. **Catastrophizing role**: Poort 2021 (catastrophizing does not mediate treatment outcome in fatigue) contradicts the broader CBT literature that assumes catastrophizing is a key mechanism. Supports the paper's existing catastrophizing critique
2. **Interoceptive accuracy direction**: The interoceptive deficit model (patients have reduced accuracy) vs heightened interoceptive attention (patients are hypervigilant to body) are both supported by different studies. Garfinkel's tripartite framework resolves this: accuracy and sensibility can dissociate
3. **Emotional reactivity**: Rimes 2016 (blunted autonomic reactivity + increased subjective distress) vs Van Den Houte 2017 (negative affect induction amplifies symptoms) — these are not contradictory but reflect different levels: autonomic blunting vs cognitive-emotional amplification
4. **Alexithymia specificity**: Whether alexithymia in CFS is a cause, consequence, or correlate of interoceptive deficits is unresolved. The direction of causality cannot be determined from cross-sectional studies

## Evidence Gaps Noted

1. **No direct interoception study in ME/CFS** using the Garfinkel tripartite framework — this is the most critical gap. We have FM studies (Valenzuela 2017) but not CFS-specific
2. **No interoceptive training/intervention trials** in ME/CFS (searches returned 0 relevant results) — the ADIE trial (Quadt 2021) was in autism, not chronic fatigue
3. **Heartbeat detection studies** in CFS: only 1-2 small studies, none using modern methods
4. **Longitudinal studies** of emotional processing during/after exercise in CFS: absent
5. **Mechanistic studies** linking known ME/CFS immune abnormalities (elevated cytokines) to specific interoceptive processing changes: none
6. **Neuroimaging of emotional processing** in ME/CFS: only 1 pilot fMRI study (Wortinger 2017, n=10 per group)
7. **Catastrophizing measures** validated specifically for fatigue (not pain) in CFS populations: none

## Overall Assessment

**Strong evidence in favor of:** interoceptive predictive processing framework and inflammation-interoception link (well-established in general populations and cross-disease, but NOT tested in ME/CFS directly).

**Moderate evidence in favor of:** alexithymia and emotional processing differences in ME/CFS (consistent findings from small-to-moderate studies, but all cross-sectional and mostly from single groups).

**Weak/contradictory evidence against:** catastrophizing as clinically meaningful mechanism in CFS fatigue (null mediation findings, recall bias interpretation).

**Critical gap:** No study has directly tested the Garfinkel tripartite interoceptive model in ME/CFS — this is the single most important finding for the paper.
