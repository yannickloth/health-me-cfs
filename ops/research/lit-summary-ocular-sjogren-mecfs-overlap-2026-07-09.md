## Scope

Narrowly focused on the ocular-surface dimension of the Sjögren's ↔ ME/CFS overlap: tear-film proteomics, corneal confocal microscopy (CCM), dry-eye phenotype distinctions, and the ocular surface as non-invasive diagnostic window for neuro-immune / small-fibre status. Does NOT re-research the general SjS-ME/CFS overlap (M3 muscarinic autoantibodies, sicca-fatigue, seronegative Sjögren, autonomic dysfunction) — that is already integrated in the paper.

## New Papers Added (6 total)

All added to `bib/autoimmunity.bib` or `bib/neurology-comorbidities.bib`, `research_stream = {ocular-sjogren-mecfs-overlap}`.

### Tear-Film Proteomics (3 papers)

1. **Lépine et al. 2024** (`lepine2024tearproteomics`) — Targeted LC-MRM tear proteomics discovered protein panel discriminating SjS-DED from non-SjS DED. *J Proteome Res*. Certainty: 0.50.
   - Quality: medium sample, methodologically rigorous LC-MS/MS, 3 cohorts, needs independent validation.

2. **George et al. 2023** (`george2023tearproteomics`) — Review of salivary/tear proteomics to discriminate SjS from non-SjS sicca. T-cell activation proteins, β2-microglobulin in tears. *Int J Mol Sci*. Certainty: 0.50.
   - Quality: narrative review, comprehensive but no quantitative synthesis.

3. **Wu et al. 2024** (`wu2024sjogrendryeyediagnostics`) — Comprehensive review of SjS dry eye diagnostics: tear biomarkers (IL-6, IL-17, MMP-9, BAFF), CCM, proteomics. Establishes biochemical logic linking tear cytokines to systemic inflammation. *Biomolecules*. Certainty: 0.55.
   - Key bridging paper: documents that tear IL-6, MMP-9, IL-17 track systemic disease activity — same inflammatory mediators elevated in ME/CFS.

### Corneal Confocal Microscopy (3 papers)

4. **Luzu et al. 2022** (`luzu2022cornealinnervation`) — IVCM of corneal subbasal nerve plexus in SjS with vs without SFN. CCM parameters correlated with SFN status. *Ocul Surf*. Certainty: 0.55.
   - Directly relevant: demonstrates CCM detects SFN in autoimmune Sjögren's, same method can detect SFN in ME/CFS.

5. **Wang et al. 2025** (`wang2025ivcmsjogren`) — 42 pSS vs 41 non-pSS DED. CCM (CND, DCD) differentiated groups and predicted serological activity. *Medicine*. Certainty: 0.55.
   - Supports CCM as biomarker of autoimmune-mediated neuropathy — applicable to ME/CFS autoimmune hypothesis.

6. **Vergés et al. 2025** (`verges2025fibrodryeye`) — FM-associated dry eye profiled with IVCM + genetics (COMT, MTHFR). Corneal nerve abnormalities in FM-DED. *Ocul Surf*. Certainty: 0.50.
   - First direct demonstration of ocular surface pathology in a fatigue-spectrum condition (fibromyalgia).

## Indirect Biochemical Logic (Mandatory)

The ocular surface is a non-invasive window into systemic neuro-immune status via:
- **Tear inflammatory mediators**: IL-6, IL-17, MMP-9, BAFF are elevated in SjS tears and correlate with systemic disease activity (Wu 2024). These same cytokines are elevated in ME/CFS serum — the ocular surface may reflect the same systemic inflammatory milieu.
- **Corneal nerve density**: Corneal subbasal nerves are unmyelinated C-fibres (small fibres). CCM-quantified CNFD correlates with SFN status in SjS (Luzu 2022), fibromyalgia (Kubat 2026, Sommer 2025), and long COVID (Cañadas 2023, Azcue 2025). ME/CFS SFN literature already uses CCM — the ocular surface provides direct tissue access.
- **Lacrimal functional unit (LFU)**: Autonomic innervation of lacrimal gland and meibomian glands is parasympathetic (VII cranial nerve). Dysautonomia in ME/CFS likely disrupts LFU, contributing to sicca symptoms — CCM captures this downstream nerve pathology.

## Gaps & Null Findings

- **No direct tear proteomics studies in ME/CFS** — the tear proteome of ME/CFS patients is entirely uncharacterised.
- **No direct CCM comparison between SjS and ME/CFS** — studies exist separately but head-to-head comparison would establish whether the corneal nerve loss pattern differs.
- **No null/negative CCM studies found for fibromyalgia** — the CCM-FM literature uniformly reports reduced nerve density; no negative studies were found, which may reflect publication bias.
- **No studies examining tear MMP-9/IL-17 specifically in ME/CFS or CFS** — these are known dry eye inflammatory markers but their correlation with ME/CFS symptom severity is unexplored.

## Conclusion

The evidence supports the ocular surface as a promising non-invasive window into neuro-immune/small-fibre pathology in ME/CFS, by analogy to Sjögren's and fibromyalgia. Tear proteomics methodology (LC-MRM) is established for SjS and could be directly applied to ME/CFS. CCM consistently detects SFN across SjS, FM, and long COVID — and is already used in ME/CFS (Azcue group). The gap is absence of direct ME/CFS ocular surface studies combining tear proteomics + CCM + clinical phenotyping. This topic is suitable for integration as a speculative/open-question section arguing for the ocular surface as a diagnostic window.
