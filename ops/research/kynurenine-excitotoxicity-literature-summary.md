# Kynurenine Pathway-Sleep-Mitochondria-Excitotoxicity Literature Summary

## Overview
Research collected for integration into multi-system feedback loop hypothesis in ch14c-multi-lock-integration.typ. Focus: kynurenine pathway activation, sleep/glymphatic disruption, mitochondrial dysfunction, and EAAT2/glutamate excitotoxicity in ME/CFS.

## Papers Found and Processed

### High-Certainty Papers (certainty 0.7-1.0)

#### 1. Cervenka2017 - Science
**BibTeX key:** `Cervenka2017`
**Paper:** Kynurenines: Tryptophan's metabolites in exercise, inflammation, and mental health
**Journal:** Science. 2017;357(6349):eaaf9794
**DOI:** 10.1126/science.aaf9794
**PMID:** 28751584

**Key Findings:**
- Kynurenine pathway integrates immune, metabolic, and neurological signaling across organs
- Peripheral inflammation drives kynurenine accumulation in brain
- Exercise activates kynurenine clearance in skeletal muscle
- Different metabolites have opposing effects: KYNA (neuroprotective), QA (neurotoxic)
- Interorgan cross-talk via kynurenine metabolites connects exercise to mental health

**Relevance to ME/CFS:**
- Explains mechanism for immune activation causing neurological symptoms
- Provides basis for kynurenine-mediated NAD+ depletion
- Shows how exercise intolerance may relate to impaired kynurenine clearance

**Certainty:** 0.95 (Science review, extensively replicated)

---

#### 2. Schwarcz2012 - Nature Reviews Neuroscience
**BibTeX key:** `Schwarcz2012`
**Paper:** Kynurenines in the mammalian brain: when physiology meets pathology
**Journal:** Nat Rev Neurosci. 2012 Jul;13(7):465-77
**DOI:** 10.1038/nrn3257
**PMID:** 22678511

**Key Findings:**
- Tryptophan degraded to neuroactive compounds via kynurenine pathway (KYNA, 3-HK, QA)
- Pathway tightly controlled by immune system via IDO activation
- Dysregulation causes hyper/hypofunction of active metabolites
- Associated with neurodegenerative and psychiatric disorders
- QA is NMDA receptor agonist causing excitotoxicity; KYNA is NMDA antagonist

**Relevance to ME/CFS:**
- Establishes QA as NMDA agonist causing glutamate excitotoxicity
- Provides mechanism for immune-to-brain signaling via kynurenine pathway
- Supports kynurenine pathway as therapeutic target

**Certainty:** 0.95 (Nature Reviews Neuroscience, foundational review)

#### 3. Xie2013 - Science (ALREADY IN BIBLIOGRAPHY)
**BibTeX key:** `Xie2013glymphatic`
**Paper:** Sleep drives metabolite clearance from the adult brain
**Journal:** Science. 2013 Oct 18;342(6156):373-7
**DOI:** 10.1126/science.1241224
**PMID:** 24136970

**Key Findings:**
- Sleep increases brain interstitial space by 60%
- Enhanced CSF-ISF convective exchange during sleep
- Accelerated β-amyloid clearance during sleep via glymphatic system
- Restorative function of sleep linked to neurotoxic waste removal

**Relevance to ME/CFS:**
- Explains how unrefreshing sleep causes metabolite accumulation
- Glymphatic dysfunction could contribute to cognitive symptoms
- Provides mechanism for sleep disruption as upstream event

**Certainty:** 0.90 (Science, foundational discovery, extensively replicated)

### Medium-Certainty Papers (certainty 0.4-0.69)

#### 4. Che2025 - NPJ Metabolic Health and Disease

**Paper:** Heightened innate immunity may trigger chronic inflammation, fatigue and post-exertional malaise in ME/CFS
**Journal:** NPJ Metab Health Dis. 2025 Sep 3;3(1):34
**DOI:** 10.1038/s44324-025-00079-w
**PMID:** 40903540

- Multi-omics analysis of ME/CFS patients before/after exercise
- Exaggerated innate immune response after microbial antigen exposure
- Impaired energy production (citric acid cycle, β-oxidation, urea cycle)

- Abnormalities worsened after exercise, correlated with symptom intensity

**Relevance to ME/CFS:**
- Direct ME/CFS study showing kynurenine pathway dysregulation
- Links immune activation to energy metabolism impairment
- Shows exercise-induced exacerbation of metabolic abnormalities

**Certainty:** 0.65 (peer-reviewed ME/CFS study, multi-omics, single cohort)

#### 5. Chojnacki2026 - Nutrients
**BibTeX key:** `Chojnacki2026`
**Paper:** Multi-Strain Probiotic Improves Tryptophan Metabolism and Symptoms in Chronic Fatigue Syndrome Patients with Co-Occurring Irritable Bowel Syndrome
**Journal:** Nutrients. 2026 Jan 5;18(1):174
**DOI:** 10.3390/nu18010174
**PMID:** 41515290

**Key Findings:**
- 40 female CFS+IBS patients with dysbiosis treated with CDS22 probiotic (450B CFU/day, 12 weeks)
- Baseline: elevated bacterial proteolytic markers (3-IS), low tryptophan, high QA, low KYNA
- Post-treatment: fatigue scores declined 40.3%, 97.5% reached remission threshold
- 3-IS decreased to healthy levels, KYNA/QA ratio increased 45%
- Increased tryptophan availability correlated with symptom reduction

**Relevance to ME/CFS:**
- Clinical study showing kynurenine pathway dysregulation in ME/CFS
- Demonstrates gut-kynurenine axis as modifiable therapeutic target
- Shows neurotoxic QA elevation and neuroprotective KYNA depletion

**Certainty:** 0.60 (clinical study, n=40, open-label, single center)

#### 9. Zhang2026 - Molecular Neurobiology
**BibTeX key:** `Zhang2026MitochondriaFatigue`

**Journal:** Mol Neurobiol. 2026 Apr 29;63(1):596
**DOI:** 10.1007/s12035-026-05896-8

- **Limitation:** General chronic fatigue, not ME/CFS-specific

**Certainty:** 0.50 (genetic study, large sample, but general fatigue not ME/CFS-specific)

## Evidence Gaps

### 1. Direct ME/CFS Studies on EAAT2
- **Gap:** No direct studies of EAAT2 expression/function in ME/CFS
- **Need:** Post-mortem brain studies, CSF glutamate transporter measurements, in vivo imaging
- **Status:** Must extrapolate from Alzheimer's/Parkinson's/ALS literature

### 2. Sleep Architecture Studies in ME/CFS
- **Gap:** Limited quantitative sleep architecture studies (slow-wave sleep, REM sleep)
- **Need:** Polysomnography studies with glymphatic function assessment
- **Status:** Some sleep studies exist, but glymphatic connection not directly studied in ME/CFS

### 3. Kynurenine Metabolite Measurements in ME/CFS
- **Partial:** Che2025 and Chojnacki2026 show pathway dysregulation
- **Gap:** Limited comprehensive metabolite profiling (KYNA, QA, 3-HK, kynurenine/tryptophan ratio)
- **Need:** Larger cohort studies with pre/post-exercise measurements

### 4. Na+/K+ Gradient Studies in ME/CFS
- **Gap:** No direct studies of Na+/K+ ATPase function in ME/CFS neurons/astrocytes
- **Need:** In vitro studies of ion pump function, ATP availability effects
- **Status:** Theoretical extrapolation from mitochondrial dysfunction evidence

### 5. Glymphatic Function Assessment in ME/CFS
- **Gap:** No direct glymphatic flow measurements in ME/CFS patients
- **Need:** MRI-based glymphatic function assessment (DTI-ALPS index)
- **Status:** Extrapolation from sleep studies in other conditions

## Contradictions and Controversies

### 1. Kynurenine Pathway Direction
- **Question:** Does chronic fatigue show kynurenine pathway activation or inhibition?
- **Evidence:**
  - Activation: Che2025 (ME/CFS multi-omics), Cervenka2017 (inflammation drives kynurenine)
  - Inhibition: Yan2026 (rat fatigue model showed inhibited kynurenine pathway)
- **Possible resolution:** Acute activation → chronic depletion; different study timepoints

### 2. Mitochondrial Dysfunction Specificity
- **Question:** Are mitochondrial defects primary or secondary in ME/CFS?
- **Evidence:**
  - Primary: Zhang2026 (genetic evidence), Fano2026 (review)
  - Secondary: Che2025 (energy defects worsen post-exercise)
- **Possible resolution:** Both - genetic predisposition + environmental trigger

### 3. Glutamate Excitotoxicity in ME/CFS
- **Question:** Is glutamate excitotoxicity documented in ME/CFS?
- **Evidence:**
  - Indirect: Chojnacki2026 (elevated QA = NMDA agonist)
  - Missing: Direct measurements of extracellular glutamate, NMDA receptor activity
- **Status:** Hypothetical, requires direct measurement
