## Inclusion Criteria

- Direct measurement or review of brain glucose metabolism in ME/CFS
- Astrocyte, glial, or neuroglial dysfunction in ME/CFS
- Brain energy metabolism, cerebral bioenergetics in ME/CFS
- FDG-PET or other neuroimaging of brain metabolism in ME/CFS
- Lactate shuttle / brain lactate in ME/CFS
- Glycolysis or OXPHOS dysregulation relevant to brain in ME/CFS
- Mitochondrial dysfunction with brain involvement in ME/CFS
- Null/negative findings for brain glucose metabolism in ME/CFS
- Mechanistic reviews linking neuroinflammation to energy-dependent cognitive dysfunction
- Peer-reviewed journal articles (all study types); preprints excluded
- English language or English abstract

## Exclusion Criteria

- Peripheral-only glycolysis/metabolism without CNS relevance
- Animal models without direct human relevance to ME/CFS
- Herbal/traditional medicine intervention studies tangential to mechanism
- IBS/gut-only papers without brain metabolism connection
- Papers on GLUT1 deficiency syndrome (developmental, unrelated to ME/CFS)
- Glycogen storage diseases without ME/CFS connection

## Null/Negative Findings

- Siessmeier et al. (2003) found no global differences in FDG-PET between CFS and controls using observer-independent analysis, but found regional abnormalities in right caudate nucleus and brainstem — mixed results (some null, some positive)
- No studies found reporting negative or null results for the overall brain glucose metabolism hypothesis — reflects literature gap rather than evidence of absence
- No direct searches for "executive function + glucose + CFS" or "prefrontal cortex + glucose + CFS" returned any results — underscores the gap between the ASD model's predictions and existing ME/CFS literature
