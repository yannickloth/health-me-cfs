## Thematic Synthesis

### 1. ME/CFS: Peripheral Serotonin Evidence
- **Direct measurement** (Raij 2024): Lower serum serotonin correlates with fatigue in ME/CFS (n=38)
- **Metabolomics** (Simonato 2021, Abujrais 2024): Impaired tryptophan-to-serotonin conversion
- **Immune-driven** (Che 2025, Maes 2013): Innate immune activation diverts tryptophan; autoantibodies against 5-HT
- **Compartment paradox**: Central serotonin hyperactivity (Lee 2024 mouse model) coexists with peripheral depletion — distinct CNS vs peripheral pools

### 2. Long COVID: Mechanistic Convergence
- **Wong 2023 (Cell)**: 3 converging mechanisms: IFN-gamma-IDO tryptophan diversion, enterochromaffin cell damage → impaired absorption, platelet storage defects
- **Critique**: Anderson 2024 questions platelet serotonin methodology; Mathé 2025 found no serotonin reduction (null result)
- **Net assessment**: Mechanism likely real but variable; depends on severity, timing, measurement standardization

### 3. POTS: Platelet Serotonin Deficiency
- **Gunning 2016**: 81% of POTS patients have delta-granule storage pool deficiency with low platelet serotonin (n=181)
- **Raziq 2021**: Lower serotonin in POTS and VVS
- **Mar 2014 (RCT)**: SSRI worsens POTS symptoms — clinically consistent with low serotonin state
- **Mechanism**: Platelet serotonin = peripheral serotonin reservoir; deficiency in POTS links autonomic dysfunction to serotonin

### 4. Fibromyalgia (high comorbidity with ME/CFS)
- **Loçasso 2024**: IL-6 inversely correlated with serotonin in FM; lower serotonin → higher pain
- **Paredes 2019**: Review: serotonin modulates pain via 5-HT1A/2A/3 receptors; estrogen modulates signaling → sex differences

### 5. Null/Contradictory Evidence
- **Mathé 2025**: Failed replication of serotonin depletion in PASC — no difference from controls
- **Anderson 2024**: Methodological critique — platelet serotonin is labile, sample handling critical, clinical significance uncertain

### 6. Indirect Biochemical Links (Integrative)
| Function | Serotonin role | ME/CFS relevance |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Mitochondrial | Serotonin regulates mitochondrial biogenesis via 5-HT receptors; L-carnitine correlates (Raij 2024) | Energy metabolism failure |
| Vagal tone | Enterochromaffin serotonin activates 5-HT3 receptors on vagal afferents | Autonomic dysfunction |
| Immune modulation | 5-HT receptors on immune cells; anti-5-HT autoantibodies (Maes 2013) | Neuroimmune dysregulation |
| Platelet function | Platelet storage pool deficiency (Gunning 2016) | Vascular/hemodynamic |
| Gut motility | Enterochromaffin serotonin regulates peristalsis | GI comorbidity in ME/CFS |
| Vasoconstriction | 5-HT2B receptors on endothelial cells | Orthostatic intolerance |

### 7. Integration with Existing Kynurenine Pathway Coverage
The paper already extensively covers IDO/TDO-driven tryptophan diversion toward kynurenine. These new entries provide the **consequence data**: peripheral serotonin is what decreases when tryptophan is diverted. The pathway is:
Inflammation → IDO activation → tryptophan → kynurenine (not serotonin) → peripheral serotonin depletion
