## Papers Added

### 1. van Campen 2024 — Two Hemodynamic Responses in ME/CFS+POTS
- **PMID:** 39768649
- **Journal:** J Clin Med
- **Type:** Original research
- **Key Finding:** ME/CFS+POTS patients show TWO distinct hemodynamic responses during HUT: (1) hypovolemic-like with reduced stroke volume, (2) hyperadrenergic-like with increased heart rate. Subtyping may guide treatment.
- **Certainty:** Medium (n=~75, single center)
- **Novelty:** High — first to characterize two POTS hemodynamic subtypes specifically in ME/CFS

### 2. Malik 2026 — Brain Tissue Changes & Cerebral Hemodynamic Deficits in POTS
- **PMID:** 41740821
- **Journal:** Heart Rhythm
- **Type:** Original research
- **Key Finding:** POTS patients show gray matter volume reductions, altered brain network connectivity, and cerebral hemodynamic deficits on MRI. Structural brain changes correlate with symptom severity.
- **Certainty:** Medium (moderate n, first report)
- **Novelty:** Very high — first demonstration of structural brain changes in POTS

### 3. Miranda-Hurtado 2026 — Stroke Volume Reduction Impairs CBF Regulation via ETCO2 in POTS
- **PMID:** 41398115
- **Journal:** Clin Auton Res (Raj lab)
- **Type:** Original research
- **Key Finding:** Reduced stroke volume in POTS → lower ETCO2 → impaired cerebral autoregulation. Establishes mechanistic link between low SV and cognitive symptoms via hypocapnic cerebral vasoconstriction.
- **Certainty:** Medium-High (well-controlled physiology, Raj lab)
- **Novelty:** High — mechanistic pathway from hypovolemia/SV → CO2 → CBF

### 4. Seeley 2025 — Brain SPECT Shows Abnormal Cerebral Perfusion in POTS with Cognitive Dysfunction
- **PMID:** 39875497
- **Journal:** Sci Rep
- **Type:** Original research
- **Key Finding:** Novel brain SPECT imaging reveals region-specific hypoperfusion in POTS patients with cognitive complaints. Perfusion deficits correlate with cognitive scores.
- **Certainty:** Medium (pilot, novel imaging)
- **Novelty:** High — first brain SPECT in POTS cognitive dysfunction

### 5. Kwok 2026 — Midodrine for POTS: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
- **PMID:** 41949563
- **Journal:** J Cardiovasc Pharmacol
- **Type:** Systematic review + meta-analysis, 14 studies, n=968
- **Key Finding:** Midodrine significantly increases symptom response vs placebo in pediatric POTS (RR 1.52, p=0.01). Evidence in adults is limited. Hypertension in 8.2%. Second/third-line therapy.
- **Certainty:** Medium-High (systematic review, heterogeneity I²=78%)
- **Novelty:** Medium — first dedicated midodrine meta-analysis

### 6. Hedge 2026 — Test-Retest Reliability of Supine-to-Stand Tests in POTS: A Cautionary Tale
- **PMID:** 41887448
- **Journal:** Heart Rhythm (Vernino/Levine lab)
- **Type:** Original research
- **Key Finding:** Clinical supine-to-stand tests show poor test-retest reliability in POTS patients. Day-to-day HR variability may lead to misdiagnosis or misclassification.
- **Certainty:** Medium-High (reliability study, robust methods)
- **Novelty:** Very high — directly challenges reliability of the diagnostic test itself; important for clinical trials

### 7. Marchetta 2025 — Ivabradine and Symptom Burden in POTS
- **PMID:** 40294227
- **Journal:** J Cardiovasc Pharmacol
- **Type:** Original research
- **Key Finding:** Ivabradine reduces HR in POTS but symptom burden improvement does not correlate with HR reduction. Suggests tachycardia is compensatory, not causal.
- **Certainty:** Medium (moderate n)
- **Novelty:** High — dissociates HR reduction from symptom improvement; supports compensatory tachycardia hypothesis

### 8. Ekman 2025 — Peripheral Sensory Neuropathy in POTS Correlates with GI Symptoms
- **PMID:** 40608783
- **Journal:** PLoS One
- **Type:** Original research
- **Key Finding:** Objective sensory neuropathy signs present in POTS using quantitative sensory testing. Neuropathy severity correlates with gastrointestinal symptom burden.
- **Certainty:** Medium (n=moderate, objective measures)
- **Novelty:** Medium-High — links SFN to GI symptoms in POTS

### 9. Mathew 2026 — Central Sensitization in POTS
- **PMID:** 41528744
- **Journal:** JAMA Netw Open
- **Type:** Original research (Novak lab)
- **Key Finding:** 67% of POTS patients meet criteria for central sensitization using validated questionnaires. Central sensitization prevalence far exceeds general population.
- **Certainty:** Medium (validated instruments, single center)
- **Novelty:** High — first report of central sensitization prevalence in POTS

### 10. Chopra 2026 — Compensatory Orthostatic Tachycardia: A Mechanistic Argument
- **PMID:** 42037722
- **Journal:** Front Neurol
- **Type:** Review/opinion
- **Key Finding:** Argues POTS tachycardia is compensatory (not pathological per se). Tachycardia defends cerebral perfusion when stroke volume drops. Treatment should target root cause (hypovolemia, venous pooling), not HR.
- **Certainty:** Low-Medium (review/opinion, but biologically plausible)
- **Novelty:** High — conceptual reframing with therapeutic implications

### 11. Lukáčová 2025 — Autoimmune Antibodies in Orthostatic Intolerance Syndromes
- **PMID:** 40432440
- **Journal:** Physiol Res
- **Type:** Original research
- **Key Finding:** Autoantibodies against α1- and β1-adrenergic receptors detected in POTS patients. No correlation with clinical severity. Adds to mixed evidence on GPCR autoantibodies.
- **Certainty:** Medium (n=moderate, single center)
- **Novelty:** Medium — adds to autoantibody literature; independent replication attempt

### 12. Uppal 2026 — Do Medications Actually Help in POTS? A Qualitative Study
- **PMID:** 42146286
- **Journal:** CJC Open (Raj lab)
- **Type:** Qualitative research
- **Key Finding:** Patient-reported medication effectiveness is modest. Most patients try multiple medications with limited benefit. Non-pharmacological strategies (salt, compression, exercise) are valued.
- **Certainty:** Medium (qualitative, but rigorous methods, Raj lab)
- **Novelty:** High — rare qualitative study on patient perspectives; important for clinical realism
