## Genuinely New Papers

### 1. Girgis2025PTLDTcellPhenotypes — Aberrant T-cell Phenotypes in PTLD

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Authors | Girgis AA, Cimbro R, Yang T, Rebman AW, … Aucott J |
| Journal | Front Immunol. 2025;16:1607619 |
| DOI | 10.3389/fimmu.2025.1607619 |
| PMID | 40703523 |
| Design | Cross-sectional immunophenotyping, n=272 PTLD + 28 HC |
| Certainty | Medium-High (0.65) — well-powered cohort, Hopkins Lyme Center, 19-parameter flow cytometry |
| Research stream | tick-borne-symptom-domains |

**Key Findings:**
- PTLD cohort had fewer circulating CXCR5+ CD4+ naïve T cells vs HC (5.2% vs 8.3%, Padj<0.001)
- Increased CXCR3+CCR4-CCR6- CD8 T cells (43.1% vs 25.7%, Padj<0.01)
- Elastic net classifier identified PTLD with AUC 0.83
- Cytokines alone did not distinguish PTLD from HC
- Female-specific increase in central memory CD8 T cells restricted to a high-fatigue subgroup
- Factor analysis linked specific immune cell frequencies to specific symptom severity (pain, fatigue, cognitive domains)

**Symptom-domain mapping:**
- Fatigue domain: associated with female-specific central memory CD8 T cell expansion
- Musculoskeletal pain: associated with CXCR5+ CD4+ naïve T cell abundance
- Cognitive: not specifically modeled but within factor structure

**Gap covered:** ch07 mentions PTLDS immune dysregulation broadly. This paper provides the first detailed T-cell subset mapping and symptom-domain linking in PTLDS, directly comparable to ME/CFS immunophenotyping studies.

**Limitations:** Cross-sectional, no ME/CFS comparator arm within same study.

### 3. Yakubovsky2026CatScratchEncephalitis — Bartonella CNS Infection

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Authors | Yakubovsky M, Kadar L, Katchman E, … Giladi M |
| Journal | Int J Infect Dis. 2026;164:108395 |
| DOI | 10.1016/j.ijid.2026.108395 |
| PMID | 41544843 |
| Design | National retrospective cohort, Israeli CSD surveillance |
| Certainty | Medium-High (0.65) — multi-center national cohort |
| Research stream | tick-borne-symptom-domains |

**Key Findings:**
- Largest systematic description of B. henselae encephalitis
- Neurological involvement rates, long-term cognitive outcomes quantified
- Demonstrates that Bartonella CNS infection produces measurable long-term cognitive deficits

**Symptom-domain mapping:**
- Cognitive domain: Bartonella neuroinvasion → encephalitis with persistent cognitive sequelae

**Gap covered:** ch07 mentions Bartonella neuropsychiatric symptoms but cites only Breitschwerdt chronic fatigue data. This provides independent epidemiological evidence of Bartonella CNS morbidity from a completely different population (Israeli CSD national surveillance).

**Limitations:** Retrospective, CSD cases only (B. henselae), not directly ME/CFS.

### 7. Nawrocki2025LymeNonspecificSymptoms — CDC Large-Scale Symptom Study

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Authors | Nawrocki CC, Delorey MJ, Earley AR, … Mead PS, Hinckley AF |
| Journal | Emerg Infect Dis. 2025;31(14):30-37 |
| DOI | 10.3201/eid3114.250459 |
| PMID | 41570190 |
| Design | Retrospective claims cohort, n=24,503 LD + 122,095 matched controls |
| Certainty | High (0.75) — very large n, CDC, commercial claims |
| Research stream | tick-borne-symptom-domains |

**Key Findings:**
- Pain/fatigue/cognitive codes occurred 5.0% more frequently in LD patients vs controls
- Symptom codes comprised ~11% of total symptom codes among LD patients
- Symptom frequency declined significantly at 6–12 months
- **Exception: fatigue persisted beyond 1 year**
- Fatigue is the most persistent post-Lyme symptom in this population-level analysis

**Symptom-domain mapping:**
- Fatigue: specifically found to persist beyond 12 months — the only symptom domain that did not normalize
- Pain and cognitive: declined to control levels by 12 months

**Gap covered:** ch07 cites PTLDS prevalence as 10–20% from smaller studies. This is the largest population-level estimate of post-Lyme symptom burden from CDC, with domain-specific persistence data.

**Limitations:** Claims-based (ICD codes, not validated symptom questionnaires), may undercount. No ME/CFS comparator.

### 8. Georgopoulos2025HLAPathogens — HLA Bridge ME/CFS ↔ PTLDS

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Authors | Georgopoulos AP, James LM, Peterson PK |
| Journal | Sci Rep. 2025;15:37303 |
| DOI | 10.1038/s41598-025-21230-z |
| PMID | 41136524 |
| Design | In silico HLA binding affinity prediction |
| Certainty | Medium (0.50) — in silico, requires validation, but published in peer-reviewed journal |
| Research stream | tick-borne-symptom-domains |

**Key Findings:**
- ME/CFS susceptibility HLA alleles (C*07:04, DQB1*03:03) have WEAK binding to Borrelia burgdorferi antigens
- ME/CFS protective HLA alleles (B*08:01, DPB1*02:01) have STRONG binding
- Same pattern for HHV antigens and SARS-CoV-2
- Hypothesis: weak HLA-antigen binding → persistent antigens → ME/CFS, PTLDS, Long COVID share HLA-mediated vulnerability

**Gap covered:** ch07/ch35 discuss PTLDS/ME/CFS overlap phenomenologically. This provides a genetic/immunological mechanism — shared HLA susceptibility — for why the same individual might develop chronic illness after either tick-borne or viral trigger.

**Limitations:** In silico only, no wet-lab validation. Small number of HLA alleles tested. Binding affinity does not guarantee functional immune outcome.

## Null/Negative/Contradicting Evidence

- **Nawrocki2025** (above): Population-level data show only 5% excess symptom burden in LD patients vs controls at 12 months — suggests the absolute risk of PTLDS-like symptoms may be lower than commonly cited 10–20% for the general LD population (though those with prolonged symptoms may be a distinct subset). Exception: fatigue specifically persists.
- **No new RCTs for extended antibiotics** were found (2024–2026). The Klempner paradigm remains unreplicated with improved design.
- **Bartonella DNA prevalence in healthy controls**: No large-scale 2024–2026 study resolves this controversy. The Guirguis pilot (n=30 controls) is too small.
- **No Babesia-specific 2024–2026 studies** found that link to ME/CFS symptom domains (zero PMIDs from Babesia+chronic+fatigue+autonomic search).

## Gap Assessment

Tick-borne literature from 2024–2026 yields 11 genuinely new papers not captured in existing ch07/ch35 coverage. The most impactful contributions are: (1) Girgis2025 — first detailed T-cell immunophenotyping of PTLDS with symptom-domain linking (directly comparable to ME/CFS immunophenotyping); (2) Nawrocki2025 — CDC population-level estimate confirming fatigue as the most persistent post-Lyme symptom; (3) Georgopoulos2025 — HLA-based mechanistic hypothesis unifying ME/CFS, PTLDS, and Long COVID via weak antigen binding; (4) Bush2024 — comprehensive neurobartonellosis framework; (5) Marques2026 — novel epitope-based diagnostic approach. Major gaps remain: zero new Babesia studies linking to chronic fatigue/cognition; no resolved Bartonella DNA prevalence in healthy controls; no PTLDS-specific treatment RCTs. The overall pattern reinforces the ch07 narrative but adds immunological specificity (T-cell subsets, HLA genetics, epitope diagnostics) that could strengthen the PTLDS/ME/CFS overlap argument.
