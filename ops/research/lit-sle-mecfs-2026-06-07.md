## Paper Summaries

### Schwarting et al. 2019 — Fatigue in SLE: diagnostic and pathogenic impact of anti-N-methyl-D-aspartate receptor (NMDAR) autoantibodies

- *Full Citation*: Schwarting A, Möckel T, Lütgendorf F, Triantafyllias K, Grella S, et al. Fatigue in SLE: diagnostic and pathogenic impact of anti-N-methyl-D-aspartate receptor (NMDAR) autoantibodies. _Annals of the Rheumatic Diseases_. 2019;78:1226–1233.
- *DOI*: 10.1136/annrheumdis-2019-215098
- *PMID*: 31186256
- *Key Findings*:
    - Anti-NR2 (NMDAR subunit) antibodies closely correlate with fatigue severity in SLE (n=426)
    - Correlation independent of neuropsychiatric lupus manifestations
    - Anti-NR2 antibodies detected in CSF, bind to hippocampus in situ
    - Functional in vitro assays: anti-NR2 downregulates neuronal energy metabolism WITHOUT increased cytotoxicity — subtle functional impairment, not cell death
    - Belimumab therapy (n=86) ≥6 months reduces both fatigue and anti-NR2 levels
- *Conclusion*: Anti-NR2 antibodies are a diagnostic tool and therapeutic target for SLE fatigue; mechanism involves neuronal metabolic suppression rather than cytotoxicity — directly parallels ME/CFS energy metabolism hypotheses
- *Limitations*: Cross-sectional for correlation; treatment subgroup not randomized; hippocampal volume correlation in small subgroup
- *Certainty*: 0.85
- *Research Stream*: SLE fatigue mechanisms (autoantibody)

### Fluder et al. 2026 — Mitochondrial dysfunction drives natural killer cell dysfunction in systemic lupus erythematosus

- *Full Citation*: Fluder N, Humbel M, Recazens E, Jourdain AA, Ribi C, et al. Mitochondrial dysfunction drives natural killer cell dysfunction in systemic lupus erythematosus. _JCI Insight_. 2026. doi:10.1172/jci.insight.195170
- *DOI*: 10.1172/jci.insight.195170
- *PMID*: 41628153
- *Key Findings*:
    - SLE NK cells accumulate enlarged, dysfunctional mitochondria
    - Impaired mitophagy and lysosomal acidification
    - Defective NK cell cytotoxicity and cytokine production
    - Structural, metabolic, and proteomic analyses converge on mitochondrial dysfunction
- *Conclusion*: Mitochondrial dysfunction is a key driver of NK cell impairment in SLE, mirroring the well-established NK cell dysfunction in ME/CFS — suggests a shared mechanistic node
- *Limitations*: Preclinical/mechanistic study; sample size not specified in abstract; causal direction (mitochondrial vs immune trigger) not established
- *Certainty*: 0.75
- *Research Stream*: SLE fatigue mechanisms (mitochondrial, NK cell)

### Zheng et al. 2024 — Exploring the shared mechanism of fatigue between systemic lupus erythematosus and myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome: monocytic dysregulation and drug repurposing

- *Full Citation*: Zheng D, Li X, Wang P, Zhu Q, Huang Z, et al. Exploring the shared mechanism of fatigue between systemic lupus erythematosus and myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome: monocytic dysregulation and drug repurposing. _Frontiers in Immunology_. 2024;15:1440922.
- *DOI*: 10.3389/fimmu.2024.1440922
- *PMID*: 39845969
- *Key Findings*:
    - 58 overlapping genes between SLE and ME/CFS from disease target databases
    - Five key shared targets: IL1β, CCL2, TLR2, STAT1, IFIH1
    - Monocytes enriched with these targets at single-cell level
    - RT-qPCR confirmed upregulation in activated THP-1 monocyte model
    - Drug prediction: suloctidil, N-acetyl-L-cysteine, simvastatin, camptothecin
- *Conclusion*: Classical monocytes may be crucial in linking inflammation and fatigue in both SLE and ME/CFS; NAC and simvastatin predicted as repurposable therapies
- *Limitations*: Bioinformatics + in vitro only; no patient validation of predicted drugs; ME/CFS gene targets from databases (heterogeneous criteria)
- *Certainty*: 0.60
- *Research Stream*: Shared mechanisms SLE/ME/CFS

### Parodis et al. 2025 — Poor health-related quality of life despite Lupus Low Disease Activity State or Definitions of Remission in SLE

- *Full Citation*: Parodis I, Lindblom J, Palazzo L, Tsoi A, Cetrez N, et al. Poor health-related quality of life despite Lupus Low Disease Activity State or Definitions of Remission in SLE: results from a clinical trial setting. _RMD Open_. 2025;11:e006061.
- *DOI*: 10.1136/rmdopen-2025-006061
- *PMID*: 41173511
- *Key Findings*:
    - Pooled analysis of 4 phase III belimumab trials (BLISS-52, BLISS-76, BLISS-SC, EMBRACE; n=2406)
    - 15.7% in LLDAS and 13.6% in DORIS remission still report poor physical QoL
    - 18.5-26.2% report FACIT-Fatigue scores <30 (clinically significant fatigue) despite remission
    - Pain/discomfort worst EQ-5D dimension (27.9-28.7%)
    - PCS scores remained below population norms even after 52 weeks of treatment
- *Conclusion*: Current therapeutic goal definitions (LLDAS, DORIS) do not capture patient-reported fatigue; fatigue persists as an unmet need even when inflammation is controlled
- *Limitations*: Post-hoc analysis; clinical trial population may not generalize; FACIT-F threshold <30 validated but arbitrary
- *Certainty*: 0.80
- *Research Stream*: Null/negative — SLE fatigue independent of disease activity

### Arcani et al. 2023 — Categorization of SLE patients using disease activity, patient-reported outcomes, and transcriptomic signatures

- *Full Citation*: Arcani R, Jouve E, Chiche L, Jourde-Chiche N. Categorization of patients with systemic lupus erythematosus using disease activity, patient-reported outcomes, and transcriptomic signatures. _Clinical Rheumatology_. 2023;42:2061-2070.
- *DOI*: 10.1007/s10067-023-06525-8
- *PMID*: 36759402
- *Key Findings*:
    - Developed a type 2 (fatigue/pain/depression) score from SF-36 for SLE (n=50, 100 visits)
    - Four clinical groups: minimal (39%), type 1/inflammatory (37%), type 2 (9%), mixed (15%)
    - Type 2 patients were older with longer disease duration
    - CRITICAL: Immunological data and modular interferon signatures did NOT differ between type 1 and type 2 groups
- *Conclusion*: SLE fatigue (type 2 symptoms) is a biologically distinct dimension, not merely a reflection of inflammatory activity or interferon signature
- *Limitations*: Small cohort (n=50); single center; SF-36 derived score not independently validated
- *Certainty*: 0.60
- *Research Stream*: Null/negative — SLE fatigue independent of inflammation/IFN

### Weissman-Tsukamoto et al. 2025 — Diffuse neuropsychiatric lupus: Clinical evidence, immune-mediated mechanisms, and therapeutic insights

- *Full Citation*: Weissman-Tsukamoto R, Carroll KR, Diamond B. Diffuse neuropsychiatric lupus: Clinical evidence, immune-mediated mechanisms, and therapeutic insights. _Seminars in Immunology_. 2025;78:101981.
- *DOI*: 10.1016/j.smim.2025.101981
- *PMID*: 40818249
- *Key Findings*:
    - NPSLE affects >50% of SLE patients; fatigue is among most debilitating symptoms
    - Symptoms often present at diagnosis and can be insidious
    - Brain-reactive autoantibodies cross compromised BBB → neuronal dysfunction
    - Neuroimaging confirms structural and functional brain alterations
    - Mechanistic progress leads to testable therapeutic strategies
- *Conclusion*: Diffuse NPSLE (including fatigue) has identifiable immune-mediated mechanisms, particularly brain-reactive autoantibodies and BBB injury; treatable
- *Limitations*: Review; primarily animal model data for mechanisms; limited human trial data
- *Certainty*: 0.75
- *Research Stream*: SLE fatigue mechanisms (neuropsychiatric)

### Morand et al. 2020 — Trial of Anifrolumab in Active Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (TULIP-2)

- *Full Citation*: Morand EF, Furie R, Tanaka Y, Bruce IN, Askanase AD, et al. Trial of Anifrolumab in Active Systemic Lupus Erythematosus. _New England Journal of Medicine_. 2020;382:211-221.
- *DOI*: 10.1056/NEJMoa1912196
- *PMID*: 31851795
- *Key Findings*:
    - Phase 3 RCT of anifrolumab (anti-IFNAR) vs placebo in moderate-to-severe SLE
    - Met primary endpoint (BILAG-based composite response) at week 52
    - Fatigue assessed as secondary endpoint; improvement with anifrolumab
    - IFN blockade reduces IFN gene signature and clinical disease activity
- *Conclusion*: Type I IFN blockade is effective in SLE; fatigue improvement accompanies reduced disease activity, but the dissociation between IFN signature and type 2 symptoms (per Arcani 2023) suggests fatigue improvement may be indirect
- *Limitations*: Fatigue was secondary endpoint not primary; mechanism of fatigue improvement not directly studied; TULIP-1 (companion trial) did not meet primary endpoint
- *Certainty*: 0.90
- *Research Stream*: Biologic treatment effects on SLE fatigue

### Mertz et al. 2020 — Towards a practical management of fatigue in systemic lupus erythematosus

- *Full Citation*: Mertz P, Schlencker A, Schneider M, Gavand PE, Martin T, et al. Towards a practical management of fatigue in systemic lupus erythematosus. _Lupus Science & Medicine_. 2020;7:e000441.
- *DOI*: 10.1136/lupus-2020-000441
- *PMID*: 33214160
- *Key Findings*:
    - 2/3 of SLE patients report significant fatigue, 1/3 severe
    - Fatigue occurs both with and without disease activity
    - Algorithm: rule out non-SLE causes first, then assess disease activity
    - In active disease: treat to remission is primary strategy
    - In remission with persistent fatigue: non-pharmacological approaches (exercise, psychological)
- *Conclusion*: Fatigue management in SLE requires separating inflammatory from non-inflammatory drivers — directly parallel to ME/CFS differential diagnosis
- *Limitations*: Expert opinion/algorithm; limited trial evidence for fatigue-specific treatments
- *Certainty*: 0.50
- *Research Stream*: SLE fatigue epidemiology and management

### Paroli & Sirinian 2025 — Pathogenic crosstalk between peripheral and central nervous system in rheumatic diseases

- *Full Citation*: Paroli M, Sirinian MI. Pathogenic crosstalk between the peripheral and central nervous system in rheumatic diseases: emerging evidence and clinical implications. _International Journal of Molecular Sciences_. 2025;26:6036.
- *DOI*: 10.3390/ijms26136036
- *PMID*: 40649815
- *Key Findings*:
    - Pain, fatigue, cognitive dysfunction, and autonomic disturbances in SARDs (SLE, RA, Sjögren's) not fully attributable to peripheral inflammation
    - Glial cell activation and neuroinflammation within CNS
    - Small fiber neuropathy (SFN) affects peripheral nociceptive pathways
    - Central sensitization and ANS dysfunction contribute
    - Cytokines, neuropeptides, and microRNAs as mediators
- *Conclusion*: Neuroimmune dysregulation in SARDs produces fatigue and cognitive symptoms through mechanisms distinct from peripheral inflammation — directly relevant to ME/CFS
- *Limitations*: Narrative review; broad scope limits depth on any single mechanism
- *Certainty*: 0.55
- *Research Stream*: Cross-disease neuroimmune mechanisms

### Urbańska-Krawiec & Hrycek 2010 — Chronic fatigue syndrome with special focus on systemic lupus erythematosus

- *Full Citation*: Urbańska-Krawiec D, Hrycek A. Chronic fatigue syndrome with special focus on systemic lupus erythematosus. _Polski Merkuriusz Lekarski_. 2010;29(172):260-264.
- *DOI*: N/A
- *PMID*: 21268918
- *Key Findings*:
    - CFS diagnostic criteria reviewed; SLE as a model for CFS
    - Fatigue observed in 80% of SLE patients
    - Immune, hormonal, and neurotransmitter mechanisms implicated in both
    - Suggests CFS may be a primary disorder, not secondary to psychiatric illness
- *Conclusion*: SLE and CFS share fatigue as central symptom; immune and neurohormonal mechanisms overlap; SLE can serve as a model for understanding CFS pathophysiology
- *Limitations*: Polish-language journal; narrative review, not systematic; 2010 publication date (pre-dates much ME/CFS mechanistic research)
- *Certainty*: 0.25
- *Research Stream*: SLE/ME/CFS overlap

### Rubio & Kyttaris 2023 — Undifferentiated Connective Tissue Disease: Comprehensive Review

- *Full Citation*: Rubio J, Kyttaris VC. Undifferentiated connective tissue disease: comprehensive review. _Current Rheumatology Reports_. 2023;25:98-106.
- *DOI*: 10.1007/s11926-023-01099-5
- *PMID*: 36884206
- *Key Findings*:
    - UCTD: clinical + lab autoimmunity without fulfilling SLE/SSc criteria
    - Subcategorized as evolving vs stable UCTD
    - Fatigue is common in UCTD, often comparable to SLE
    - 18% evolve to defined CTD; stable UCTD may remain undifferentiated for years
- *Conclusion*: UCTD represents a large population of patients with autoimmune fatigue who fall below diagnostic thresholds — potential overlap with ME/CFS
- *Limitations*: Systematic review but UCTD definition varies; fatigue not primary focus
- *Certainty*: 0.65
- *Research Stream*: SLE subtypes without organ involvement

### Spinelli et al. 2023 — Exercise-induced modulation of Interferon-signature: a therapeutic route toward management of SLE

- *Full Citation*: Spinelli FR, Berti R, Farina G, Ceccarelli F, Conti F. Exercise-induced modulation of Interferon-signature: a therapeutic route toward management of Systemic Lupus Erythematosus. _Autoimmunity Reviews_. 2023;22:103412.
- *DOI*: 10.1016/j.autrev.2023.103412
- *PMID*: 37597604
- *Key Findings*:
    - IFNα-signature is central to SLE pathogenesis and immunopathology
    - Exercise can modulate IFN signature and improve fatigue
    - Physical activity reduces pro-inflammatory cytokine production
    - Exercise improves QoL and fatigue perception in SLE
- *Conclusion*: Exercise may reduce IFN signature in SLE, providing a mechanistic basis for fatigue improvement; parallels exercise studies in ME/CFS (but PEM must be considered)
- *Limitations*: Narrative review; limited direct evidence of IFN modulation by exercise in SLE specifically; does not address post-exertional malaise
- *Certainty*: 0.65
- *Research Stream*: SLE fatigue mechanisms (interferon)

### Andreoli & Tincani 2017 — Undifferentiated connective tissue disease, fibromyalgia and the environmental factors

- *Full Citation*: Andreoli L, Tincani A. Undifferentiated connective tissue disease, fibromyalgia and the environmental factors. _Current Opinion in Rheumatology_. 2017;29:380-386.
- *DOI*: 10.1097/BOR.0000000000000392
- *PMID*: 28368979
- *Key Findings*:
    - UCTD and fibromyalgia share undefined clinical and laboratory features
    - Both recognize environmental exposures as triggering factors
    - May both fit under ASIA (Autoimmune Syndrome Induced by Adjuvants) framework
    - Fatigue is bridging symptom between autoimmunity and functional syndromes
- *Conclusion*: UCTD and fibromyalgia (and by extension ME/CFS) may share environmental triggers and pathophysiological features; fatigue as common endpoint
- *Limitations*: Opinion review; ASIA framework controversial (see Ameratunga 2017 refuting ASIA)
- *Certainty*: 0.55
- *Research Stream*: SLE subtypes / UCTD

## Key Themes

1. **Fatigue in SLE is dissociable from inflammation** — Multiple studies (Arcani 2023, Parodis 2025, Mertz 2020) demonstrate that fatigue persists even when disease activity is controlled and does not correlate with interferon signatures or inflammatory biomarkers.

2. **Shared autoantibody mechanisms** — Anti-NR2 (NMDAR) antibodies in SLE fatigue directly downregulate neuronal energy metabolism without cytotoxicity (Schwarting 2019), paralleling proposed mechanisms in ME/CFS.

3. **Monocyte/macrophage dysregulation as shared pathway** — Zheng 2024 identifies 58 shared genes enriched in monocytes; IL1β, CCL2, TLR2 pathways link both diseases.

4. **NK cell and mitochondrial dysfunction converge** — Fluder 2026 demonstrates mitochondrial dysfunction drives NK cell impairment in SLE, mirroring the well-characterized NK cell dysfunction in ME/CFS (TRPM3 impairment, reduced cytotoxicity).

5. **Neuroimmune crosstalk** — Paroli 2025 and Weissman-Tsukamoto 2025 describe glial activation, BBB breakdown, and brain-reactive autoantibodies producing fatigue and cognitive symptoms via mechanisms independent of peripheral inflammation.

6. **UCTD as a diagnostic bridge** — Rubio 2023 and Andreoli 2017 suggest that patients with autoimmune fatigue below SLE diagnostic thresholds (UCTD, incomplete lupus) may overlap significantly with ME/CFS populations.

## Critical Evidence Gaps

- **No direct comparative studies of SLE fatigue vs ME/CFS fatigue using objective measures** (CPET, actigraphy, metabolomics) in a head-to-head design.
- **No studies examining post-exertional malaise in SLE** — the cardinal ME/CFS symptom has not been systematically assessed in SLE populations.
- **No microbiome or metabolomic studies comparing SLE and ME/CFS directly** despite shared immune pathways.
- **NK cell dysfunction in SLE** (Fluder 2026) has not been connected to the specific TRPM3 channelopathy established in ME/CFS NK cells.
- **Belimumab and anifrolumab effects on fatigue** are secondary endpoints; no RCT has primarily targeted fatigue in SLE.
- **Prevalence of ME/CFS in SLE populations** is unknown; no epidemiological studies address this overlap.
- **Contradictory evidence**: Arcani 2023 shows type 2 symptoms are independent of IFN signature, yet Morand 2020 shows IFN blockade improves fatigue — suggesting multiple fatigue subtypes within SLE.
