## 1. Primary Finding (Direct)

### Liu2026VMAT2longcovid — Liu YK et al. 2026, *eBioMedicine*
- **Design:** Case-control PET study, n=24 long COVID vs 24 age-matched controls (+19 extended controls)
- **Tracer:** (+)[11C]DTBZ BPND (VMAT2, dopaminergic terminal marker)
- **Finding:** 16-20% reduction across ventral striatum, dorsal putamen, dorsal caudate (LME P=4×10⁻⁵)
- **Symptom correlations:** Apathy r=-0.54 (ventral striatum), motor slowing r=0.51 (dorsal putamen), memory r=0.58 (dorsal caudate)
- **Recovery control:** COVID-recovered healthy controls showed no VMAT2 reduction vs never-COVID (P=0.77) — strengthens specificity to long COVID
- **Limitations:** Young sample (mean 32.2), apathy-predominant phenotype, n=24, no pre-COVID baseline
- **Certainty:** 0.72
- **Bib key:** `Liu2026VMAT2longcovid`
- **File:** Literature/neurological/PIIS2352396426002227.pdf

## 2. TSPO PET / Neuroinflammation in Long COVID (Supporting, same cohort)

### Braga2023neuroCOVID — Braga et al. 2023, *JAMA Psychiatry*
- **Design:** TSPO PET [18F]-FEPPA in n=20 COVID-DC vs 20 controls
- **Finding:** Elevated TSPO binding (microglial activation) in striatum, prefrontal cortex, ACC — correlated with depressive symptoms
- **Relevance:** Same CAMH/Toronto cohort as Liu 2026. Demonstrates striatal neuroinflammation as potential upstream driver of VMAT2 loss
- **Certainty:** 0.72
- **Bib key:** `Braga2023neuroCOVID`

### Braga2025astrogliosis — Braga et al. 2025, *Biological Psychiatry*
- **Design:** [11C]SL25.1188 PET (MAO-B, astrogliosis marker) in n=21 COVID-DC vs 22 controls
- **Finding:** Elevated astrogliosis in striatum, prefrontal cortex, ACC, insula
- **Relevance:** Dual evidence (microglial + astroglial) for striatal gliosis driving dopaminergic terminal damage
- **Certainty:** 0.70
- **Bib key:** `Braga2025astrogliosis`

### VanElzakker2024PASCneuro — VanElzakker et al. 2024, *Brain Behav Immun*
- **Design:** [11C]PBR28 TSPO PET in n=12 PASC vs 13 controls
- **Finding:** TSPO binding correlated with vascular disease measures, not uniform neuroinflammation
- **Relevance:** Mixed results — TSPO signal in PASC may be vascular-linked, not purely glial
- **Certainty:** 0.58
- **Bib key:** `VanElzakker2024PASCneuro`

### Visser2025TSPOfatigue — Visser et al. 2025, *J Nucl Med*
- **Design:** TSPO PET in n=27 post-COVID fatigue vs 18 controls
- **Finding:** Variable TSPO binding — some increase, some decrease, highlighting heterogeneity in long COVID neuroinflammation
- **Certainty:** 0.55
- **Bib key:** `Visser2025TSPOfatigue`

## 3. Postmortem Evidence: DA Neuron Vulnerability to SARS-CoV-2

### Yang2024dopaminesenescence — Yang et al. 2024, *Cell Stem Cell*
- **Design:** Human iPSC-derived DA neurons + postmortem SN tissue from acute COVID-19 (n=13)
- **Finding:** SARS-CoV-2 directly infects DA neurons via ACE2, induces senescence (not acute cell death); detectable viral transcripts in SN postmortem
- **Relevance:** Mechanistic precedent for DA neuron vulnerability in COVID. However: elderly sample (mean 69.7), acute COVID not long COVID, some had dementia/Parkinson's
- **Certainty:** 0.65
- **Bib key:** `Yang2024dopaminesenescence`

### Eidhof2024dopaminecomment — Eidhof et al. 2024, *Cell Stem Cell* (Commentary on Yang)
- Contextualizes Yang findings: DA neuron senescence may explain chronic neuropsychiatric symptoms
- **Certainty:** 0.50

## 4. Basal Ganglia / Striatal Dysfunction in Post-COVID

### Rudroff2024basalganglia — Rudroff 2024, *Neurology International*
- **Design:** Narrative review + FDG-PET data (n=16 LC, n=28 MS, n=33 recovered)
- **Finding:** Reduced frontal-striatal glucose metabolism in long COVID vs recovered controls; basal ganglia fatigue circuit model
- **Certainty:** 0.50 (narrative review)
- **Bib key:** `Rudroff2024basalganglia`

### Zhao2024cognitiveslow — Zhao et al. 2024, *EClinicalMedicine*
- **Design:** Multicentre cross-sectional, n=?
- **Finding:** Severe cognitive slowing in long COVID — objective processing speed deficits
- **Relevance:** Cognitive slowing component may have dopaminergic basis (confirmed by Liu 2026 motor correlation)
- **Certainty:** 0.65
- **Bib key:** `Zhao2024cognitive` (already in bib: pathophysiology-general.bib)

### Fujimoto2025AMPA — Fujimoto et al. 2025, *Brain Commun*
- Systemic increase of AMPA receptors in long COVID cognitive impairment
- **Competing:** AMPAR upregulation offers non-dopaminergic mechanism for cognitive symptoms
- **Certainty:** 0.55

## 5. Dopamine and ME/CFS — Direct Studies

### Nakatomi2014neuroinflammation — Nakatomi et al. 2014, *J Nucl Med*
- **Design:** TSPO PET [11C]-PK11195 in n=9 CFS vs 10 controls
- **Finding:** Increased TSPO binding in thalamus, midbrain, amygdala, cingulate — but **not striatum**
- **Relevance:** Supports neuroinflammation model, but striatum not implicated. Null for the striatal hypothesis
- **Certainty:** 0.50 (small n, first-gen tracer)
- Already in bib.

### Raijmakers2022TSPOnull — Raijmakers et al. 2022, *Neurol Neuroimmunol Neuroinflamm*
- **Design:** TSPO PET [11C]-PK11195 in n=9 CFS, n=10 QFS, n=9 controls
- **Finding:** **No significant TSPO difference** between CFS and controls. Negative correlation between TSPO and symptom severity
- **Null/Contradicting:** Directly challenges neuroinflammation model in ME/CFS (non-COVID)
- **Limitations:** First-gen tracer, small n, women only
- **Certainty:** 0.45
- **Bib key:** `Raijmakers2022TSPOnull`

### Ferrero2017CNSautopsy — Ferrero et al. 2017, *J Investig Med*
- **Design:** Literature review + 1 case report, CFS autopsy
- **Finding:** Gliosis, white matter changes in CFS brain; no striatal-specific findings
- **Certainty:** 0.35 (single case report + review)
- **Bib key:** `Ferrero2017CNSautopsy`

## 6. DA Augmentation Treatments in ME/CFS and Long COVID

### Aregawi2026Noradrenergic — Aregawi et al. 2026, *Brain Commun*
- **Design:** CSF catecholamine study, n=16 PI-ME/CFS, n=34 PASC, n=32 PD, n=40 HV
- **Finding:** Selective **noradrenergic** deficiency (NE pathway low). **DA pathway (DOPAC+HVA) normal** — contradicts expectation of DA deficiency
- **Null/Contradicting:** If replicated, argues against DA augmentation strategy; suggests NE > DA in PI-ME/CFS
- **Limitations:** Post-infectious only, small PI-ME/CFS subgroup
- **Certainty:** 0.75
- Already in bib (neuroinflammation.bib)

### Theoharides2021COVIDdopamine — Theoharides et al. 2021, review
- Long COVID neuroinflammation-driven neurotransmitter depletion: increased MAO-B → DA degradation, serotonin depletion via IDO
- Supports MAO inhibition as treatment strategy (consistent with Liu 2026's proposal)
- **Bib key:** Already in bib (neuroinflammation.bib, line 1358)

### Liu2026VMAT2longcovid (treatment proposal)
- Suggests L-dopa or MAOIs to augment DA release
- Patent filed (rasagiline + tyramine) by senior author Meyer

## 7. Competing Mechanisms

### Barzon2026TSPOBBB — Barzon et al. 2026, *Brain Behav Immun*
- **Finding:** Peripheral inflammation **reduces TSPO tracer brain influx** via BBB transport limitations
- **Implication:** TSPO PET may underestimate neuroinflammation in conditions with high peripheral inflammation (ME/CFS, Long COVID)
- **Confounds interpretation of:** Raijmakers 2022 (null TSPO), possibly Nakatomi 2014
- **Certainty:** 0.55
- Already in bib (neuroinflammation.bib)

### Mitochondrial dysfunction (competing for motor/cognitive slowing)
- Shungu/Natelson 2017: ventricular lactate elevated in CFS/fibromyalgia — mitochondrial metabolic failure as alternative explanation for slowed motor/cognitive function
- No direct PET evidence linking mitochondrial dysfunction to striatum specifically
- **Certainty:** 0.55

## 8. Cross-Condition / Indirect

### Sauve2023GnRHlongCOVID — Sauve et al. 2023, *EBioMedicine*
- Postmortem: GnRH neuron death in hypothalamus of male long COVID — establishes precedent for viral-induced specific neuronal loss
- **Relevance:** Same Netherlands Brain Bank approach; different cell type but same mechanism (infection → neuron subtype loss)
- **Certainty:** 0.65
- **Bib key:** `Sauve2023GnRHlongCOVID`

### Bantle2019alphavirus — Bantle et al. 2019, *NPJ Parkinsons Dis*
- Alphavirus (WEEV) infection causes selective DA neuron loss, neuroinflammation, protein aggregation in mouse model
- **Relevance:** Mechanistic precedent for postviral selective DA neuron vulnerability
- Only animal model — not directly translatable
- **Certainty:** 0.50
- **Bib key:** `Bantle2019alphavirus`

### Chen2020ACE2brain — Chen et al. 2020, *Front Neurol*
- ACE2 receptor distribution in human brain — high density in SN/VTA (dopaminergic cell body regions)
- **Relevance:** Establishes DA neuron vulnerability to SARS-CoV-2 via ACE2
- **Certainty:** 0.60
- **Bib key:** `Chen2020ACE2brain`

## 9. Indirect Biochemical / Systemic Links Found

| Link | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Gliosis → DA neuron damage | Elevated TSPO (microglia) + MAO-B (astrocytes) in striatum of same Toronto cohort → synaptic pruning / ROS damage to DA terminals |
| ACE2 in SN/VTA | High ACE2 expression in DA neuron cell body regions → direct infection possible → retrograde terminal degeneration |
| Postviral DA neuron vulnerability | Alphavirus (Bantle 2019), SARS-CoV-2 (Yang 2024), influenza (historical postencephalitic parkinsonism) → converging evidence for viral tropism to DA neurons |
| MAO-B hyperactivity | Astrogliosis increases MAO-B → DA degradation accelerates → functional deficiency even without terminal loss |
| Peripheral inflammation → BBB → brain DA | Inflammatory cytokines reduce BH4 (rate-limiting for DA synthesis) → DA depletion without structural loss |
| Mitochondrial metabolic failure | Ventricular lactate elevated in CFS → alternative explanation for motor/cognitive slowing: energy failure rather than DA terminal loss |
| Iron dysregulation | Potential link: gliosis → iron accumulation → DA neuron vulnerability (Fenton chemistry). Not directly tested in long COVID. |
| Kynurenine pathway | IDO activation shunts tryptophan away from serotonin, towards kynurenic acid → suppresses glutamate signaling AND dopamine release (indirect) |

## 10. Key Gaps

1. **No VMAT2 PET study in ME/CFS** — zero direct evidence for DA terminal loss in ME/CFS (only long COVID)
2. **No DAT PET in ME/CFS or long COVID** — DAT is alternative DA terminal marker
3. **No [18F]DOPA PET in ME/CFS** — would measure DA synthesis capacity (distinct from terminal density)
4. **No D2/D3 receptor PET in long COVID** — receptor-level adaptation unknown
5. **No simultaneous TSPO + VMAT2 PET** — would directly test gliosis → DA damage hypothesis
6. **Aregawi 2026 null for DOPAC/HVA** — contradicts DA depletion model; needs replication
7. **Treatment trials absent** — no L-dopa, MAOI, or DA agonist trials in long COVID or ME/CFS (as of July 2026)
8. **Parkinson's disease as positive control missing** — no study comparing VMAT2 loss in long COVID vs PD within same scanner/protocol
9. **Sex differences underexplored** — Liu sample 50% female, effects not stratified

## 11. Cross-Discrepancies

- **Aregawi 2026 vs Liu 2026:** Aregawi found normal DA metabolites (DOPAC, HVA) in CSF of PI-ME/CFS and PASC, but Liu found reduced striatal VMAT2 binding in long COVID. Possible resolutions: (1) peripheral CSF DA metabolites poorly reflect striatal terminal density, (2) Aregawi's PASC group may have different phenotype (PEM+ vs apathy+), (3) VMAT2 reduction reflects vesicle density reduction per terminal, not terminal loss — DA synthesis may be preserved.
- **Raijmakers 2022 (null TSPO) vs Nakatomi 2014 (positive TSPO) in CFS:** first-gen tracer [11C]PK11195 used in both — signal-to-noise issues. Barzon 2026 adds confound of peripheral inflammation → reduced brain tracer delivery. ME/CFS neuroinflammatory status remains ambiguous.
- **Theoharides 2021 MAO-B hyperactivity model** predicts increased DA degradation — consistent with preserved CSF DA synthesis markers but reduced functional signaling.

## 12. Clinical Implications

If Liu 2026 finding generalizes to ME/CFS:
- **DA augmentation** (L-dopa, MAOIs, selegiline, rasagiline) becomes plausible treatment direction
- **Apathy-predominant ME/CFS phenotype** may be most responsive to DA-targeted treatment
- **Dual hit hypothesis:** gliosis-driven DA terminal damage + DA metabolic degradation via MAO-B = two therapeutic targets
- **VMAT2 PET ([18F]AV133) as stratification tool** for clinical trials — identify DA-deficiency phenotypes
- **MAO-B inhibition** specifically relevant if astrogliosis (MAO-B elevation) is driving increased DA turnover
