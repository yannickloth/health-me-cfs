# Literature Summary: Geneviève Fourel Corrections — May 2026

## Paper 1: Cell 2025 — Centromeric DNA amplification triggered by viral proteins activates nuclear cGAS

- **Citation:** Lahaye X, Tran Van P, Chakraborty C, Shmakova A, Cao NTB, Ferran H, Ait-Mohamed O, Maurin M, Waterfall JJ, Kaufer BB, Fischer P, Hennig T, Dölken L, Lomonte P, Fachinetti D, Manel N. Centromeric DNA amplification triggered by viral proteins activates nuclear cGAS. *Cell*. 2025 Jul 24;188(15):4043-4057.e21. DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2025.05.008. Epub 2025 Jun 2.
- **Quality:** Top journal (Cell), comprehensive mechanistic study, multiple viral systems tested. **Certainty: 0.75.**
- **Key findings:**
  - Herpesvirus proteins disrupt centromeres, triggering centromeric DNA amplification
  - This pathway is named VICAR (viral-induced centromeric DNA amplification and recognition)
  - The amplification of centromeric (alpha-satellite) DNA activates nuclear cGAS
  - HSV-1 ICP0 is a key viral protein: its USP7-binding domain targets centromeric proteins
  - HCMV IE1, KSHV K8, and BHV-1 BICP0 also trigger centromere disruption
  - The mechanism is non-mitotic — centromere DNA amplifies without cell division
  - Nuclear cGAS produces cGAMP locally at centromeres, activating innate immunity
- **Relevance to Fourel hypothesis:** This paper provides the mechanistic bridge: herpesvirus infection → centromere rearrangement → pericentromeric (HSAT2) transcription. IE1/IE2 requirement for HSAT2 transcription (Nogalski 2019) is now interpretable as: IE1/IE2 are required for the centromere rearrangement that then triggers HSAT2, rather than directly transactivating HSAT2.

## Paper 2: Nogalski et al. 2019 (Nat Commun) — Tumor-specific endogenous repetitive element induced by herpesviruses

- **Citation:** Nogalski MT, Solovyov A, Kulkarni AS, Desai N, Oberstein A, Levine AJ, Ting DT, Shenk T, Greenbaum BD. A tumor-specific endogenous repetitive element is induced by herpesviruses. *Nat Commun*. 2019;10(1):90. DOI: 10.1038/s41467-018-07944-x. PMID: 30626867.
- **Quality:** Well-cited (already in our bib), multiple lines of evidence, Nat Commun. **Certainty: 0.65.**
- **Key findings (re-confirmed):**
  - Acute induction of HSATII RNA in human cells by HCMV and HSV-1
  - HCMV IE1 and IE2 proteins cooperate to induce HSATII
  - HSATII induction affects viral replication cycle and titers
  - HSATII expression detected in HCMV colitis patient tissue
  - Adenovirus, influenza, Zika, HCV do NOT induce HSATII (herpesvirus specificity)
- **Relevance to Fourel hypothesis:** Already integrated but the mechanism was incorrectly attributed to direct IE1/IE2 action on HSATII. The Cell 2025 paper now reframes this: IE1/IE2 → centromere rearrangement → HSATII transcription.

## Paper 3: Evdokimova et al. — HSAT2 in Ewing sarcoma (exosomes, ETS family)

- **Status:** Already in our bib (@Evdokimova2019HSAT2exosome). The ETS family sponging by HSAT2 GGAA repeats is documented for ERG and FLI1 in Ewing sarcoma. SPI1/PU.1 (also ETS family) binding to HSAT2 is a mechanistic inference from:
  - HSAT2 contains GGAA repeats
  - ETS family TFs bind GGAA motifs
  - ERG and FLI1 (ETS family) are documented to be siphoned by pericentromeric RNAs in Ewing sarcoma
