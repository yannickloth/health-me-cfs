## Key Findings Across Papers

1. **High perceived stigma prevalence:** 62–85% of ME/CFS patients report perceived stigma, with attribution to psychosomatic framing by healthcare providers as the strongest predictor (Froehlich 2022; McManimen 2018).

2. **Stigma → health outcomes pathway:** Perceived stigma mediates the relationship between negative causal attributions and worse functional status, social role satisfaction, and health-related quality of life (Froehlich 2022; Looper 2004).

3. **Stigma scale validated for ME/CFS:** The adapted Chronic Illness Stigma Scale has robust psychometric properties in ME/CFS populations with four factors: alienated, self-blame, perceived discrimination, and disclosure concerns (Terman 2020).

4. **Psychiatric referral as stigmatising:** Mandatory or early psychiatric referral is perceived by patients as delegitimising their illness experience, particularly in the UK model where psychiatry gatekeeps CFS services (Terman 2019).

5. **Healthcare dismissal is pervasive:** Patients report feeling disbelieved, dismissed, or labelled as having a psychiatric disorder — this is the most consistent finding across qualitative studies spanning 1999–2024 (Asbring 2002; Guise 2010; Melby 2024; Deale 2001; Ware 1999).

6. **Healthcare system barriers are structural:** Lack of diagnostic pathways, contested nosology, insufficient clinician education, and absence of specialised services create systemic barriers beyond individual clinician bias (Hussein 2024; Bayliss 2014).

7. **Stigma contributes to suicide risk:** Perceived stigma, unsupportive social interactions, and illness delegitimisation are associated with elevated suicidal ideation in ME/CFS after controlling for depression (McManimen 2018; Johnson 2022; Brown 2020).

8. **Media framing amplifies stigma:** Newspaper coverage disproportionately focuses on psychological/behavioural causes and controversy, with 70% of articles emphasising psychiatric characterisations (Knudsen 2011).

9. **Stigma extends to social relationships:** Patients experience stigma in family, friendship, and workplace contexts, including disbelief, accusations of malingering, and social withdrawal (Brooks 2014; Murray 2023; Ware 1999).

10. **Illness name affects stigmatisation:** The label "chronic fatigue syndrome" generates more negative attributions than "myalgic encephalopathy" or "Florence Nightingale disease," suggesting the name itself is a vector of stigma (Jason 2002).

11. **Child/adolescent stigma understudied:** Children with ME/CFS face stigma from teachers, peers, and healthcare providers, with some evidence it impairs educational outcomes — systematic review of qualitative child studies found stigma as a recurring theme (Parslow 2017).

12. **FSS stigma literature applies:** Stigma in functional somatic syndromes (FSS) systematically associates with worse health outcomes; ME/CFS shows comparable or higher stigma to other FSS but higher than medically explained conditions (Ko 2022; Looper 2004).

13. **PEM-focused care reduces dissatisfaction:** When clinicians focus on post-exertional malaise as the hallmark symptom rather than fatigue, patient satisfaction improves and deterioration decreases (Wormgoor 2023).
