## Key Findings Summary
- **SHY in vivo human evidence:** Elmenhorst 2026 is first direct in vivo human demonstration of synaptic homeostasis mechanism via SV2A PET. SV2A increased +4.6% thalamus, +5.6% hippocampus, +3.2% parietal cortex after sleep deprivation.
- **ME/CFS direct studies:** No direct studies of SV2A or synaptic density in ME/CFS exist. Alpha-delta sleep well-documented in CFS (Van Hoof 2007, Guilleminault 2006) and fibromyalgia (Horne 1991).
- **Null evidence:** Durkin & Aton 2016 show sleep-dependent potentiation in visual cortex — partial challenge to SHY. SV2A as proxy for synaptic density has caveats (Mikkelsen 2023 shows regional SV2A variability).
