# Literature Summary: Maladie de Verneuil (Hidradenitis Suppurativa) and ME/CFS

**Date:** 2026-07-20
**Scope:** HS epidemiology, HS-associated fatigue, NLRP3 inflammasome bridging HS-ME/CFS
**Papers included:** 15

---

## 1. Direct Epidemiological Link: HS and ME/CFS

### Prens et al. 2022 — Population-Based Dutch Cohort (n=56,084)
**Bib key:** Prens2022HSComorbidities | **Certainty:** 0.70

The single strongest piece of evidence for an HS–ME/CFS association. Within the Lifelines Cohort Study (n=56,084 respondents in the Northern Netherlands), the overall HS prevalence was 2.1% (95% CI 2.0–2.2). Among several newly identified comorbidities, chronic fatigue syndrome showed an odds ratio of **1.72 (95% CI 1.06–2.78)**. This remained significant in multivariate analysis after adjusting for age, sex, BMI, smoking status, and socioeconomic status. Fibromyalgia also showed a strong association (OR 2.26, 95% CI 1.64–3.11). Notably, many HS respondents were undiagnosed and untreated, suggesting the true burden may be higher than captured by clinical ascertainment.

**Implications for ME/CFS paper:** HS joins fibromyalgia, IBS, and migraine as a skin-associated comorbidity of ME/CFS. The adjusted OR of 1.72 is moderate but robust. The shared inflammatory substrate — HS as a systemic autoinflammatory condition with elevated TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-17 — provides a plausible mechanistic intersection.

**Limitations:** Self-reported survey (no clinical HS confirmation); cross-sectional; ME/CFS status also self-reported; no PEM assessment.

## 2. Fatigue as a Core HS Symptom

### McGrath et al. 2026 — Fatigue and Sleep in HS: Patient and HCP Perspectives
**Bib key:** McGrath2026HSFatigue | **Certainty:** 0.60

Multi-stakeholder perspective paper including patients and healthcare professionals across Europe and North America. Fatigue described as a "debilitating" symptom affecting all domains of QoL. HCPs noted that patients were often unaware of the full impact of fatigue until treatment improved their HS. Sleep disturbance attributed to pain, pruritus, and lesion drainage, with deficits accumulating over time. The paper advocates for systematic fatigue assessment using validated instruments in clinical care.

**Key insight:** Fatigue in HS is underrecognized by both patients and clinicians, yet is identified as a major driver of reduced work productivity, social withdrawal, and diminished self-esteem. This mirrors patterns seen in ME/CFS where post-exertional symptom exacerbation compounds activity avoidance.

### Meral Ketenci et al. 2026 — Kinesiophobia in HS
**Bib key:** MeralKetenci2026HSKinesiophobia | **Certainty:** 0.60

Cross-sectional study (HS patients + matched controls). First investigation of kinesiophobia (fear of movement) in HS. HS patients had significantly higher kinesiophobia and fatigue, and lower physical activity, compared to controls. In multivariable regression, **pain intensity, fatigue, and depressive symptoms** were independent determinants of kinesiophobia (all p<0.05). The Fatigue Severity Scale (FSS) was used to quantify fatigue.

**Implications:** Kinesiophobia in HS mirrors patterns in ME/CFS where activity avoidance develops secondary to symptom exacerbation. The fatigue–pain–depression–kinesiophobia cluster suggests a shared behavioral phenotype across chronic inflammatory conditions.

### Almahdi et al. 2026 — Palliative and Supportive Care in Advanced HS: Systematic Review
**Bib key:** Almahdi2026HSPalliative | **Certainty:** 0.55

Systematic review (PRISMA 2020) of 7 studies across 27 countries (n=77–1,787). **Fatigue prevalence: 61.0%** (n=793/1,299). Moderate-severe pain in 61.4%. Depression in 30.3–35.8%, anxiety in 36.2–57.5%, suicidal ideation in 7.9%. Only 4.5% achieved complete disease control despite active dermatologist treatment. Emotional burden consistently exceeded physical symptom burden.

**Key insight:** Advanced HS carries a multidimensional palliative care burden comparable to advanced cancer in symptom severity. The high fatigue prevalence (61%) rivals that reported in SLE and RA cohorts.

### Ring et al. 2017 — Prodromal Symptoms in HS
**Bib key:** Ring2017HSProdrome | **Certainty:** 0.65

Questionnaire study of 72 HS patients. 83.3% experienced prodromal symptoms before HS flares. Of these, **32% reported fatigue** as a prodromal symptom, with onset >24h before lesion eruption in 45%. Other systemic prodromes included malaise (23%), headache (11%), and nausea (2%).

**Critical insight for ME/CFS:** The temporal pattern — systemic symptoms emerging 12–24+ hours before a local inflammatory event — mirrors the delayed onset of PEM. This suggests that HS flare-associated immune activation produces systemic cytokine-mediated sickness behavior that may recapitulate features of PEM. If validated, this positions HS as a natural human model for studying the latency between immune activation and fatigue perception.

### Riis et al. 2018 — Pilot Study of Fatigue in HS
**Bib key:** Riis2018HSFatigue | **Certainty:** 0.40

First study specifically quantifying fatigue in HS patients. Small pilot (letter format, Br J Dermatol). Laid groundwork for subsequent HS fatigue research.

### Deckers et al. 2016 — Severe Fatigue Based on Anaemia in HS
**Bib key:** Deckers2016HSAnaemiaFatigue | **Certainty:** 0.30

Two case reports plus literature review. Demonstrated that HS-associated anaemia of chronic disease can produce severe fatigue responsive to treatment of underlying inflammation. Provides mechanistic link: systemic inflammation → anaemia → fatigue, a pathway also implicated in ME/CFS.

## 3. HS and Fibromyalgia / Musculoskeletal Overlap

### D'Onghia et al. 2024 — Fibromyalgia and Skin Disorders: Systematic Review
**Bib key:** DOnghia2024FMskin | **Certainty:** 0.55

Systematic review (PRISMA) of 21 articles. Increased frequency of FM among patients with cutaneous diseases including psoriasis, chronic urticaria, contact allergy, acneiform disorders, **hidradenitis suppurativa**, and vitiligo. Comorbid FM may intensify skin conditions and negatively impact QoL.

**Cross-reference:** This aligns with Prens 2022 finding of FM OR 2.26 in HS, and the known FM–ME/CFS overlap. The HS–FM–ME/CFS triad may share central sensitization and neuroinflammation pathways.

### Romanelli et al. 2025 — Musculoskeletal Symptom Burden in HS
**Bib key:** Romanelli2025HSMSK | **Certainty:** 0.45

Quality improvement initiative using IDEOM MSK-Q (n=115 HS patients). 79.14% reported joint symptoms; 33.91% rated joint pain ≥7/10. **Fatigue rated ≥7/10 in 47.82%**. Higher HS severity correlated with greater MSK symptom burden.

## 4. NLRP3 Inflammasome: The Mechanistic Bridge

### Moran et al. 2023 — Targeting NLRP3 Inflammasome in HS Skin
**Bib key:** Moran2023NLRP3HS | **Certainty:** 0.75

Single-cell RNA-seq + flow cytometry + skin explant culture. Key findings: enrichment of Th17 cells, IL-17, IL-1β, and NLRP3 inflammasome pathways in HS skin. NLRP3 inhibitor (MCC950) significantly reduced IL-1β and IL-17A secretion from HS skin explants. Inflammasome constituent genes mapped to Langerhans cells and dendritic cell subpopulations. **Br J Dermatol**, rigorous methodology.

### Krajewski et al. 2024 — NLRP3 Overexpression in HS Lesions
**Bib key:** Krajewski2024NLRP3HS | **Certainty:** 0.50

RNA sequencing + real-time PCR of skin biopsies. Significantly increased NLRP3 expression in both lesional and perilesional HS skin vs. healthy controls. NLRP3 mRNA higher in lesional vs. non-lesional skin, indicating inflammatory spread. P2X7R expression not significantly different. Supports NLRP3 as a therapeutic target in HS.

### Petrasca et al. 2023 — Metformin and NLRP3 in HS
**Bib key:** Petrasca2023MetforminHS | **Certainty:** 0.65

Demonstrated metabolic dysregulation in PBMCs of HS patients. Metformin restored metabolic pathways, reduced inflammatory markers, suppressed NLRP3 inflammasome activity, and activated AMPK-mTOR pathway. Links immunometabolism to NLRP3 in HS. **Br J Dermatol**.

### McCann et al. 2026 — SGLT2 Inhibitors for HS
**Bib key:** McCann2026SGLT2iHS | **Certainty:** 0.40

Narrative review of SGLT2 inhibitors as potential therapy for HS and other inflammatory skin diseases. Mechanistic rationale: SGLT2i reduce NLRP3 inflammasome activity, lower pro-inflammatory cytokines, and improve insulin resistance. No clinical trial data yet. Narrative review quality.

### Kilgour et al. 2026 — P2X7-NLRP3 Inhibitor AZD9056 Phase 2 Trial
**Bib key:** Kilgour2026AZD9056HS | **Certainty:** 0.55

Phase 2 clinical trial of P2X7-NLRP3 inhibitor AZD9056 in HS. **No significant effect on clinical disease activity** (HiSCR), but did restore cytokine production in PBMCs (ex vivo). Published in JAAD. Important negative/partial result: NLRP3 inhibition alone insufficient for clinical response in HS, suggesting multiple redundant inflammatory pathways.

## 5. NLRP3 Inflammasome and ME/CFS

### Jin et al. 2026 — Pathophysiological Mechanisms of PEM
**Bib key:** Jin2026PEMinflammasome | **Certainty:** 0.65

Systematic review proposing integrated metabolism-immune-neuro model of PEM. Central mechanism: mitochondrial dysfunction → ROS/mtDNA release → NLRP3 inflammasome activation → IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α release → systemic inflammation → neuroinflammation (glia activation, insular cortex) → altered fatigue/pain perception. Explicitly links NLRP3 to PEM in CFS and Long COVID. **Frontiers in Immunology**.

### Zhang et al. 2016 — NLRP3 Inflammasome in LPS-Induced Fatigue
**Bib key:** Zhang2016NLRP3CFS | **Certainty:** 0.70

Mouse model: LPS challenge + swim stress produced fatigue-like behaviors with NLRP3 inflammasome activation in diencephalon. NLRP3 KO mice showed significantly less fatigue (increased locomotor activity, longer fall-off times on rota-rod), decreased serum and brain IL-1β. IL-6 not affected by NLRP3 KO. Concluded LPS-induced fatigue is IL-1β-dependent via NLRP3/caspase-1 pathway. **J Neuroinflammation**, well-controlled.

### Zhang et al. 2017 — NLRP3 Mediates Fatigue via Neuroinflammation
**Bib key:** Zhang2017NLRP3Fatigue | **Certainty:** 0.65

Repeated forced swim model of CFS in mice. NLRP3 KO mice showed moderated fatigue behaviors with decreased prefrontal cortex and serum IL-1β. Elevated NLRP3 protein, inflammasome formation, and mature IL-1β in PFC of wild-type mice. **Neuroscience**.

### Wang et al. 2025 — Jingfang Granule, TCA Cycle, NLRP3 in CFS
**Bib key:** Wang2025JFGCFS | **Certainty:** 0.35

Traditional Chinese Medicine study: Jingfang Granule improved CFS in mouse model by promoting TCA cycle (Idh1, Idh2) and inhibiting NLRP3 inflammasome/TLR4/NF-κB pathways. Reduced TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6. Low certainty due to TCM framework, small n, but provides converging evidence for NLRP3 involvement in fatigue.

## 6. Synthesis and Implications for ME/CFS

### Key themes crossing the HS–ME/CFS boundary:

1. **Epidemiological signal:** One large population study (Prens 2022, n=56,084) establishes HS as a comorbidity of ME/CFS (adjusted OR 1.72). This is the only direct study and is moderate-certainty.

2. **Fatigue prevalence in HS:** Convergent evidence from multiple studies (Ring 2017: 32% prodromal; Almahdi 2026: 61% chronic; Romanelli 2025: 47.82% ≥7/10) establishes fatigue as a major, underrecognized HS symptom. The prodromal pattern (systemic symptoms preceding visible lesions by 12–24h) is mechanistically important.

3. **NLRP3 inflammasome as shared pathway:** HS is an NLRP3-driven disease (Moran 2023: scRNA-seq + NLRP3 inhibition; Krajewski 2024: overexpression in lesions; Petrasca 2023: metformin targets NLRP3). ME/CFS fatigue is mediated by NLRP3 activation (Zhang 2016, 2017: mouse models; Jin 2026: PEM model). This creates a plausible mechanistic bridge: HS-associated systemic inflammation → NLRP3/IL-1β → neuroinflammation → fatigue/PEM.

4. **Kinesiophobia parallels:** Meral Ketenci 2026 demonstrates fatigue, pain, and depression independently predict fear of movement in HS. This behavioral adaptation to symptom exacerbation mirrors the activity avoidance seen in ME/CFS.

5. **Negative/partial findings:**
   - Query 4 (post-exertional malaise × HS): 0 results. **No published literature on PEM specifically in HS patients.** This is a significant gap.
   - Queries 7–9 (HS × cytokines × ME/CFS): 0 results each. **No literature directly examining HS cytokine pathways and ME/CFS.**
   - Query 10 (autoinflammatory diseases × ME/CFS): 0 results. **Shockingly absent — a major gap in the literature.**
   - Kilgour 2026: NLRP3 inhibition alone insufficient for clinical HS response, suggesting redundant pathways.
   - SAPHO syndrome (Demirci 2024): HS is a recognized feature of SAPHO, an autoinflammatory syndrome with prominent fatigue. Added to bibliography as contextual evidence.

### Certainty-weighted summary:

| Finding | Papers | Aggregate certainty |
|---------|--------|-------------------|
| HS is an epidemiological comorbidity of ME/CFS | 1 | 0.70 (single large study, needs replication) |
| Fatigue is a major HS symptom (30–61% prevalence) | 5 | 0.60 (consistent, multiple designs) |
| NLRP3 inflammasome drives HS inflammation | 5 | 0.65 (replicated, scRNA-seq, in vitro) |
| NLRP3 inflammasome mediates ME/CFS fatigue | 4 | 0.60 (mouse models + review, limited human data) |
| HS–ME/CFS share NLRP3/IL-1β pathway | 0 direct | 0.40 (inferred, no direct human studies) |

### Research gaps identified:
1. No study has directly examined PEM in HS patients
2. No study has measured ME/CFS diagnostic criteria in HS cohorts
3. No study has examined whether HS treatment (anti-TNF, anti-IL-17, anti-IL-1) improves ME/CFS-like symptoms
4. No direct measurement of NLRP3 inflammasome activity in ME/CFS patients
5. The "autoinflammatory disease × ME/CFS" intersection is essentially unstudied
