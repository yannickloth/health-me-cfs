## Hypothesis Support

### Hypothesis 1: Central noradrenergic deficit as upstream driver of PEM
**SUPPORTED BY:** Aregawi2026 (direct CSF evidence: PEM subgroup ↓ NE Pathway), Walitt2024NIH (precursor deficits), Goldstein2024PersistentAutonomic (longitudinal persistence), Sulheim2014clonidine (sympathetic hyperactivity in CFS), Kristiansen2019postEBV (post-viral catecholamine dysregulation)
**CONTRADICTED BY:** None

### Hypothesis 2: CSF NE Pathway as diagnostic biomarker
**SUPPORTED BY:** Aregawi2026 (NE Pathway composite index discriminates patients from HV)
**PARTIALLY:** Hendrix2025AdrenergicDysfunction (adrenergic dysfunction confirmed in meta-analysis but heterogeneous measures)
**LIMITATION:** Single-center, small n, no independent replication

### Hypothesis 3: NE reuptake inhibitors as therapeutic targets
**SUPPORTED BY:** Arnold2004duloxetine (SNRI efficacy fibromyalgia), Natelson2015milnacipran (SNRI fibromyalgia), Young2025solriamfetol (NDRI ME/CFS pilot), Malik2025SNRIfibromyalgia (systematic review)
**CONTRADICTED BY:** None (but no specific atomoxetine/reboxetine ME/CFS trial exists)

### Hypothesis 4: α2 antagonists as therapeutic targets
**SUPPORTED BY:** None directly in ME/CFS
**CONTEXT:** Sulheim2014clonidine (α2 agonist tested, opposite direction)
**NEUTRAL:** Mechanism-based; no yohimbine/idazoxan ME/CFS data

### Hypothesis 5: ATP-dependent vesicular NE deficiency
**SUPPORTED BY:** Aregawi2026 (selective NE↓, DA normal pattern is the key evidence)
**MECHANISTIC:** Inference from biochemistry; no direct VMAT2/pH data in ME/CFS

### Hypothesis 6: Central-peripheral NE mismatch
**SUPPORTED BY:** Winkler2016catecholamines (elevated plasma NE), Hendrix2025AdrenergicDysfunction (adrenergic dysregulation), Wyller2016neuroendocrine (SAM system ↑), Sulheim2014clonidine (sympathetic overactivity)
**PARTIALLY:** Aregawi2026 (no correlation NE Pathway with orthostatic measures)

## Paper Details

### Paper 1 — Core: Aregawi 2026
**Citation key:** `Aregawi2026Noradrenergic`
**Title:** Central noradrenergic deficiency in post-infectious chronic fatigue: neurobehavioral correlates
**Authors:** Aregawi M, Walitt B, Sullivan P, Norato G, Benjamin JD, Goldstein DS
**Journal:** Brain Communications (2026)
**PMID:** 42205163 | **DOI:** 10.1093/braincomms/fcag173
**Design:** Cross-sectional CSF catecholamine study
**Samples:** n=16 PI-ME/CFS, n=34 PASC, n=32 Parkinson's disease, n=40 healthy volunteers
**Certainty:** 0.80
**Key findings:**
- Novel composite NE Pathway index (NE+DHPG+MHPG) significantly lower in PI-ME/CFS and PASC with PEM vs HV
- DA Pathway (DA+DOPAC+HVA) normal across all groups → selective noradrenergic deficiency
- First report of CSF MHPG levels in PI-ME/CFS or PASC
- NE Pathway correlated with handgrip duration (rho=0.62), MFI physical fatigue (rho=-0.43), SF-36 vitality
- No correlation with orthostatic hypotension or tachycardia → dissociation from peripheral autonomic
- PEM specifically associated with noradrenergic deficiency (PASC+PEM < PASC-noPEM)
- Parkinson's controls showed dual NE+DA deficiency → index validity
**Limitations:** Single NIH center; post-infectious only; small PI-ME/CFS subgroup; cross-sectional
**Target chapters:** ch08 (neurological), ch14h (neurological mechanisms), ch05 (brain and cognition), ch14f (autonomic/neuroendocrine), ch16 (causal hierarchy)

### Paper 5: Sulheim 2014
**Citation key:** `Sulheim2014clonidine`
**Title:** Disease mechanisms and clonidine treatment in adolescent chronic fatigue syndrome
**Authors:** Sulheim D, Fagermoen E, Winger A, Andersen A, Godang K, Müller F, Rowe PC, Saul JP
**Journal:** JAMA Pediatrics (2014)
**PMID:** 24493300 | **DOI:** 10.1001/jamapediatrics.2013.4647
**Design:** Cross-sectional + RCT (clonidine vs placebo)
**Samples:** n=120 adolescents with CFS
**Certainty:** 0.65
**Key findings:**
- Enhanced sympathetic activity hypothesis tested
- Clonidine (α2 agonist) did not improve symptoms → suggests sympathetic activation is compensatory rather than primary
- Important null/negative result for noradrenergic intervention
**Target chapters:** ch14f, ch08

**Journal:** Journal of Psychopharmacology (2025)
**PMID:** 40958377 | **DOI:** 10.1177/02698811251368371

**Samples:** n=38 ME/CFS
