#### 1. Maccallini P (2026) — Primary Paper
**"Biological Insights from Genome-Wide Association Studies and Whole Genome Sequencing of Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome"**
- **DOI:** 10.21203/rs.3.rs-9702020/v1
- **Type:** Research Square preprint (Jun 2026)
- **Design:** Meta-GWAS + post-GWAS analysis
- **Sample:** 19,470 ME/CFS cases
- **Key Findings:**
  - Meta-GWAS of 19,470 cases identifies brain tissue enrichment, synaptic pathways, specific neuronal cell types
  - Glutamatergic synapse genetics implicated
  - Brain tissue enrichment in ME/CFS GWAS
  - Integrates DecodeME, MVP, UK Biobank summary statistics
  - Post-GWAS analyses (LDSC, MAGMA, FUMA) point to CNS-specific mechanisms
- **Quality:** Preprint; sample size largest single ME/CFS meta-analysis to date
- **Certainty:** 0.45 (preprint, methodology sound but unvalidated by peer review)
- **Integration targets:** ch12 (genetics), ch08 (neuroinflammation), ch14h (glutamatergic signaling)

#### 2. DecodeME Consortium (2025) — Initial GWAS Results
**"Initial findings from the DecodeME genome-wide association study of myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome"**
- **DOI:** 10.1101/2025.08.06.25333109
- **Type:** medRxiv preprint
- **Design:** GWAS (up to 15,579 cases, 259,909 controls, European ancestry)
- **Key Findings:**
  - 8 genome-wide significant loci: BTN2A2, OLFM4, RABGAP1L, FBXL4, CA10, ARFGEF2 (plus 2 additional)
  - Three loci near genes acting in viral/bacterial infection response (BTN2A2, OLFM4, RABGAP1L)
  - Four of eight loci replicated at p<0.05 in UK Biobank/Lifelines
  - CA10 colocalises with multisite chronic pain association
  - No evidence of shared causal variants with depression or anxiety
  - No sex-bias among discovered associations
  - Both immunological and neurological processes involved
- **Quality:** Largest ME/CFS GWAS to date; co-produced with patients; multi-cohort replication
- **Certainty:** 0.60 (preprint, but large sample, replication attempted, robust methods)
- **Integration targets:** ch12 (genetics), ch05 (subgroups)

### Key Themes and Cross-Cutting Findings

1. **Genome-wide significant loci finally emerging:** DecodeME (2025) identified 8 loci — the first robust genome-wide significant associations in ME/CFS. Prior GWAS (Schlauch 2016, Hajdarevic 2022) were underpowered.

2. **Immune and neurological pathways dominate:** DecodeME loci implicate infection response genes (BTN2A2, OLFM4, RABGAP1L) and neurological/ion channel genes (CA10, ARFGEF2). Maccallini 2026 meta-analysis confirms brain tissue enrichment and glutamatergic synapse involvement.

3. **No shared genetics with depression/anxiety:** DecodeME explicitly tested and found no evidence of shared causal variants with psychiatric conditions — important for destigmatization.

4. **Rare variation contributes:** Birch et al. 2025 shows rare monogenic variation contributes beyond common variant architecture.

5. **Mendelian randomization supports immune causality:** Multiple MR studies (Duan 2025, Wang 2025) support causal role for specific immune cells and cytokines.

6. **Negative/null results critical:** Ueland 2022 TRA locus non-replication, Xu 2023 COVID-ME/CFS MR null — field is maturing to include and publish negative findings.

7. **Genetic overlap with FM/IBS:** Steen 2026 twin-sibling study, Kendler 2023 family scores, Hirsch 2025 comparative GWAS — shared genetic architecture across functional somatic syndromes.

8. **Metabolic genetics emerging:** Huang 2026 mGWAS and Zhang 2026 mitochondria MR point to genetic basis for metabolic phenotype.
