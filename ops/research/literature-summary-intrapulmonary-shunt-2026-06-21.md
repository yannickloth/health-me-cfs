## Key Mechanistic Findings

### Direct Findings (Long COVID / Post-COVID)
1. **Persistent shunt post-COVID:** Farrow 2023 demonstrated Qs/Qt ~8% (normal ~3-5%) in recovered COVID patients at 6 months. Worsened with exercise. Normal spirometry, normal DLCO in most patients, yet measurable shunt.
2. **Impaired gas exchange on 129Xe MRI:** Grist 2022 showed reduced RBC:TP ratio (indicating impaired gas transfer) in breathless Long COVID patients despite normal CT, normal PFTs — parallels the "normal PFTs + desaturation paradox."
3. **Mechanism: intrapulmonary anastomoses:** Lyne 2024 reviews that pulmonary artery-vein anastomoses persist in ~30% adults at rest and open in nearly all during exertion. COVID infection may recruit or enlarge these.
4. **Microvascular shunts:** Bush 2022 identified intrapulmonary bronchopulmonary anastomoses as anatomical substrate for shunting in COVID.
5. **Impaired HPV:** Li 2024 demonstrates that SARS-CoV-2 impairs hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction, an effect that persists after infection resolution, increasing shunt vulnerability.
6. **Positional shunt:** Espejo 2025 — platypnea-orthodeoxia (shunt that worsens upright) can occur after COVID without any cardiac shunt.

### Indirect / Downstream Links to ME/CFS
7. **Tissue hypoxia in ME/CFS:** Hoel 2021 — metabolic phenotypes compatible with elevated energy strain and altered fuel utilization. Compatible with tissue hypoxia from shunting.
8. **Exercise-induced shunt mechanism:** Durand 2020 establishes that intrapulmonary shunt is a recognized cause of exercise-induced hypoxemia. IPAVA flow during exercise is now well-established (Davis 2023).

### Null / Negative Findings
- No papers found that tested for intrapulmonary shunt in ME/CFS patients
- No papers found that ruled out shunt in ME/CFS
- One paper on hypoxemia + PEM returned unrelated (stroke ventilation)

### Competing Mechanisms
- V/Q mismatch: NOT found as alternative in Long COVID literature. The 129Xe MRI studies (Grist 2022) show impaired RBC:TP ratio not ventilation defects, favoring shunt over V/Q mismatch.
- Diffusion limitation: Normal DLCO in most Long COVID patients with exercise desaturation argues against diffusion impairment.
- Conclusion: shunt is the leading candidate for the "normal PFTs + desaturation" paradox.

### Clinical Paradox
The core finding driving clinical interest: **normal spirometry + normal DLCO + normal lung volumes + normal VO2max + yet significant Qs/Qt on 100% O2 testing, with exercise desaturation.** This matches exactly the clinical picture reported in Long COVID and overlaps with ME/CFS exercise intolerance/PEM.

## Clinical Relevance
