# Literature Summary: Shor 2011 — Seronegative Chronic Lyme in CFS

**Topic slug:** shor-seronegative-lyme-cfs
**Date:** 2026-07-17
**Null hypothesis:** Non-integration. Gap-only adjudication of a single controversial claim.

## Verdict: **REJECT** — the Shor claim is unsupported and contradicted by high-quality evidence.

The evidence overwhelmingly contradicts the proposition that a large proportion of internationally-defined CFS patients have "seronegative chronic Lyme" that responds to prolonged antimicrobials.

## Papers (7 new bib entries)

### Topic source (to be cited as an example of a weak source)
1. **Shor2011SeronegativeLyme** — certainty 0.20. Retrospective uncontrolled chart review (n=210). Claims 99% of CFS patients have seronegative Lyme. Non-validated diagnostic criteria. Single-author, advocacy-conflicted (past ILADS president). Highest-vs-lowest outcome comparison. **Contradicts no one; is itself the target of contradiction.**

### High-quality contradicting evidence
2. **Berende2016PLEASE** — certainty 0.90. NEJM RCT (n=281). Prolonged antibiotics (doxycycline, clarithromycin+HCQ) vs placebo after 2-week IV ceftriaxone. **No benefit over placebo** for quality of life (P=0.69). **Directly contradicts the antibiotic-response premise.** [treatments.bib]

3. **Marzec2017LymeAntibioticHarm** — certainty 0.85. CDC MMWR case series: septic shock, C. difficile, osteomyelitis, fatal outcomes from IV antibiotics for "chronic Lyme". **Documents tangible harm from Shor's advocated treatment.** [treatments.bib]

### Diagnostic validity evidence
4. **Feder2007ChronicLyme** — certainty 0.85. NEJM multi-author critique. Rejects "chronic Lyme" as a valid diagnosis. Documents serologic misinterpretation and non-validated testing. The definitive position statement. [immune.bib]

5. **Lantos2015ChronicLyme** — certainty 0.80. ID Clinics review. Concludes: no evidence for treatment-refractory persistent Borrelia; prolonged antibiotics do not prevent/ameliorate symptoms and cause harm. Covers specificity issues with CD57, C4a, C6. [immune.bib]

6. **Patrick2015LymeCFS** — certainty 0.65. CID prospective comparison (n=81). Patients diagnosed with "chronic Lyme" by alternative practitioners vs CFS patients. **Phenotypes indistinguishable. No objective evidence of active Borrelia in either group.** Directly relevant: demonstrates "chronic Lyme" diagnosed by alternative methods IS CFS. [immune.bib]

### Foundational concept reference
7. **Dattwyler1988Seronegative** — certainty 0.55. NEJM original description (n=17). Seronegative Lyme exists in patients with DOCUMENTED PRIOR acute Lyme and early antibiotics. **Not applicable to Shor's CFS population without documented antecedent Lyme.** Important boundary condition: Shor extends this concept beyond its evidenced scope. [immune.bib]

## Key takeaway: High-quality RCT contradicts antibiotic claim.

The **PLEASE trial** (Berende 2016, NEJM, n=281, double-blind, placebo-controlled) is the definitive test of prolonged antibiotics for persistent symptoms attributed to Lyme. **Null result with high precision.** Combined with Klempner 2001 (already in bib) and Fallon 2008 (already in bib), the RCT evidence uniformly contradicts prolonged antibiotic benefit. The CDC MMWR case series documents fatal harm.
