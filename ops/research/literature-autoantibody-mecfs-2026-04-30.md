## Papers Added (5 new entries)

### 1. Mignolet et al. 2026 — PRIMARY PAPER
**BibTeX key:** `Mignolet2026passiveTransferLC`
**Journal:** Acta Neuropathologica (2026, open access)
**DOI:** 10.1007/s00401-026-03019-0
**Certainty:** 0.55

**Study:** n=13 LC patients + n=10 COVID-recovered controls. IgG purified via protein G, injected into mice (n=10/donor) for 4 consecutive days.

**Key findings:**
- LC IgG causes transient mechanical + thermal hypersensitivity (week 1 only); effect abolished by IgG depletion or Fc digestion
- No anxiety, depression, or spatial memory differences
- No neuroinflammation or astrogliosis
- LC IgG accumulates in lumbar DRG colocalizing with nociceptive + proprioceptive neurons
- Binds human post-mortem DRG tissue ex vivo

**Mechanistic significance:** Dissociates peripheral (pain) from central (cognitive) symptom origins. DRG targeting without central neuroinflammation places this in the peripheral sensitization camp. Brain fog in long COVID may not be directly IgG-mediated via the same pathway as pain, or requires longer observation windows.

**Limitations:** Small n=13; transient effect (week 1 only); antigens uncharacterized; cognitive tests may lack sensitivity; IgG subclass data absent.

### 2. Goebel et al. 2021 — Fibromyalgia Passive Transfer (Foundational)
**BibTeX key:** `Goebel2021passiveTransferFM`
**Journal:** Journal of Clinical Investigation (2021; 131(13):e144201)
**PMID:** 34196305
**Certainty:** 0.65

**Study:** n=8 FM patients + 6–12 controls. IgG passive transfer into mice.

**Key findings:**
- FM IgG → mechanical/cold hypersensitivity, reduced locomotion, reduced grip strength, loss of intraepidermal innervation
- IgG-depleted serum: no effect
- FM IgG labels satellite glial cells (SGC) and neurons in DRG; activates GFAP/s100b
- Electrophysiology: reduced Aδ and C-nociceptor activation thresholds

**Mechanistic significance:** SGC activation in DRG as the key effector. Foundational proof-of-concept for the passive transfer paradigm directly extended to long COVID by Mignolet 2026 and Chen 2026.

### 3. Chen et al. 2026 — Long COVID IgG Persistent Allodynia (Cell Reports Medicine)
**BibTeX key:** `Chen2026igGLongCOVID`
**Journal:** Cell Reports Medicine (2026; p.102693)
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.xcrm.2026.102693
**Certainty:** 0.60

**Study:** UMC Utrecht / Amsterdam UMC. Pooled LC IgG; longitudinal (baseline + 2-year follow-up from same patients).

**Key findings:**
- Pooled LC IgG → pronounced, persistent mechanical hypersensitivity
- IgG from same patients 2 years later (still symptomatic) reproduces allodynia
- 3 patient subgroups via GFAP/NFL/IFN-β; subgroup-specific proteomic pathways

**Mechanistic significance:** Persistence of pathogenic IgG over 2 years has direct implications for treatment: single-course apheresis may not suffice; subgroup stratification needed for targeted therapy.

### 4. Santos Guedes de Sa et al. 2024 — Causal Link Autoantibodies Neurological LC (Preprint)
**BibTeX key:** `SantosGuedes2024autoantibodiesLC`
**Journal:** medRxiv preprint (2024; pending peer review)
**PMID:** 38947091
**Certainty:** 0.50 (preprint)

**Study:** n=55 LC + n=42 convalescent + n=39 healthy; MY-LC cohort (Mount Sinai–Yale, Iwasaki group). 21,000-protein array + passive transfer.

**Key findings:**
- Autoantibodies target nervous system proteins; cross-react with sciatic nerve, spinal cord, meninges
- Top autoantigens: MED20, USP5 (mass spec confirmed)
- Passive transfer → pain, balance/coordination loss, muscle weakness
- 85% of pain-phenotype mice received IgG from pain-reporting LC patients
- IgG crosses BBB at 5% (day 5)
- Reduced IENFD consistent with SFN

**Mechanistic significance:** Broader CNS and PNS autoantibody profile than Mignolet 2026. The BBB crossing (5%) is low; may not explain cognitive symptoms directly. Complement study: Mignolet focused on DRG/pain; Santos Guedes adds meninges, sciatic nerve, balance/coordination phenotypes.

**Certainty reduction reason:** Preprint; peer-reviewed Cell Reports Medicine version (March 2026) may supersede.

### 5. McAlpine et al. 2024 — Post-COVID Small Fiber Neuropathy Case-Control
**BibTeX key:** `McAlpine2024sfnCOVID`
**Journal:** Neurology: Neuroimmunology & Neuroinflammation (2024; 11(3):e200244)
**PMID:** 38630952
**Certainty:** 0.52

**Study:** Retrospective case-control (Class III); n=16 new-onset SFN post-COVID; Yale NeuroCOVID Clinic.

**Key findings:**
- 92% of post-COVID SFN patients met PEM criterion for ME/CFS
- iCPET (n=7): neurovascular dysregulation confirmed
- IVIG response: 9/9 treated improved vs 3/7 untreated (p=0.02)
- Non-length-dependent SFN = DRG-level pathology

**Mechanistic significance:** Bridges SFN, dysautonomia, and ME/CFS overlap. IVIG response supports autoimmune mechanism. DRG-level SFN links mechanistically to Mignolet 2026 (DRG IgG binding) and Goebel 2021 (DRG IgG accumulation).

## Cross-Paper Synthesis: What the Passive Transfer Cluster Establishes

**Convergence (3 independent groups, 2021–2026):**
1. Goebel 2021 (Liverpool/Karolinska): FM IgG → DRG SGC activation → pain
2. Mignolet 2026 (Namur/Liège): LC IgG → DRG nociceptors → pain only (no cognition)
3. Chen 2026 (Utrecht/Amsterdam): LC IgG → persistent allodynia, 2-year persistence

**Key mechanistic debate this resolves:**
- Peripheral vs central origin: DRG is the primary IgG target in all 3 studies
- Pain is IgG-transferable; cognitive symptoms may not be (Mignolet 2026: no memory effects)
- Neuroinflammation is NOT required for pain sensitization (Mignolet 2026: no gliosis)

**Tension with null findings:**
- Germain 2025 found NO significant autoantibody differences (ME/CFS vs healthy, n=172) using REAP/Luminex panels for extracellular antigens
- The DRG-binding IgG in passive transfer studies may target conformational or intracellular epitopes not captured by REAP (which displays extracellular domains only)
- This tension is not resolved; both findings belong in the document

**Implication for ME/CFS chapters:**
- Autoantibody hypothesis should be presented as a subgroup finding, not universal
- Therapeutic apheresis (immunoadsorption) evidence remains weak-to-medium pending RCT results (EXTINCT, IA-PACS-CFS)
- SFN (McAlpine 2024, Oaklander 2013, Azcue 2023) converges with DRG pathology to explain peripheral pain and dysautonomia overlap
