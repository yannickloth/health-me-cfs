## New Papers — 11 Entries

### 1. Quadt et al. 2024 — Neurodivergent traits, IL-6 mediation, chronic disabling fatigue
**Citation:** Quadt L, Csecs J, Bond R, et al. BMJ Open. 2024;14(7):e084203.
**PMID:** 39038862 | **DOI:** 10.1136/bmjopen-2024-084203
**BibTeX key:** `Quadt2024neurodivergentFatigue`

**Study design:** Longitudinal case-control, ALSPAC cohort (n=4,563), UK
**Key findings:**
- Autism traits age 7 → OR=1.78 for chronic disabling fatigue at 18 (p=0.008)
- ADHD traits age 9 → OR=2.18 for CDF at 18 (p=0.002)
- IL-6 at age 9 mediates the pathway (OR=1.54, p=0.006)
- Mediation analysis: neuroinflammation is mechanistic bridge, not confounder

**Relevance to lower-baseline model:** Provides the causal mechanism — pre-existing low-grade neuroinflammation (elevated IL-6) reduces energy reserves before any acute trigger. The temporal separation (traits measured age 7/9, fatigue at 18) strengthens the temporal precedence argument.

**Quality:** Medium (0.65). Large population cohort; longitudinal; biomarker mediation. Limitations: proxy neurodivergence measures; CDF ≠ ME/CFS diagnostic criteria.

### 2. Frye et al. 2024 — ASD mitochondrial biomarkers meta-analysis (204 studies)
**Citation:** Frye RE, Rincon N, McCarty PJ, et al. Neurobiology of Disease. 2024;197:106520.
**PMID:** 38703861 | **DOI:** 10.1016/j.nbd.2024.106520
**BibTeX key:** `Frye2024ASDmitochondria`

**Study design:** Systematic review and meta-analysis, 204 studies
**Key findings:**
- Elevated lactate (17%), pyruvate (41%), alanine (15%), creatine kinase (9%) in ASD
- ATP deficit + elevated lactate:pyruvate ratio; moderate-to-large effect sizes (d ≥ 0.6)
- Mitochondrial dysfunction correlated with GI symptoms, oxidative stress, immune dysfunction
- mtDNA variants, heteroplasmy, haplogroups, copy number all associated with ASD severity
- Carnitine and ubiquinol showed benefit in mitochondrial ASD subtypes

**Relevance to lower-baseline model:** This is the core evidence that ASD involves systemic mitochondrial dysfunction — the same ETC pathways implicated in ME/CFS energy failure are chronically impaired in ASD as a baseline feature.

**Quality:** Medium-High (0.70). Largest meta-analysis on this topic; consistent direction across diverse biomarkers. Limitations: high heterogeneity; non-standardized assay methods across 204 studies.

### 3. Kindgren et al. 2021 — ADHD/ASD prevalence in hEDS/HSD children
**Citation:** Kindgren E, Quiñones Perez A, Knez R. Neuropsychiatr Dis Treat. 2021;17:379–388.
**PMID:** 33603376 | **DOI:** 10.2147/NDT.S290494
**BibTeX key:** `Kindgren2021hEDSadhd`

**Study design:** Retrospective study, n=201 children ages 6–18 with hEDS or HSD, Sweden
**Key findings:**
- ADHD verified: 16%; under investigation: +7% (total ~23%)
- ASD: 6% (vs. ~2.6% general population)
- Age 17–18 cohort: ADHD in 46%
- hEDS significantly higher ADHD rate than HSD

**Relevance to lower-baseline model:** Establishes the hEDS–ADHD–ASD triad numerically. Children with connective tissue hypermobility carry a substantially elevated neurodevelopmental burden — the three-way cluster (hypermobility + neurodivergence + autonomic dysfunction) is the highest-risk phenotype for ME/CFS onset.

**Quality:** Medium (0.60). Single-centre retrospective; limited controls; useful prevalence data.

### 4. Csecs et al. 2022 — Joint hypermobility mediates neurodivergence → dysautonomia link
**Citation:** Csecs JLL, Iodice V, Rae CL, et al. Front Psychiatry. 2022;12:786916.
**PMID:** 35185636 | **DOI:** 10.3389/fpsyt.2021.786916
**BibTeX key:** `Csecs2022hypermobility`

**Study design:** Prospective cross-sectional, n=109 neurodivergent adults (autism, ADHD, Tourette syndrome)
**Key findings:**
- Joint hypermobility: 51% neurodivergent vs. 20% general population
- Female neurodivergent: 69% hypermobility prevalence
- JH statistically mediated neurodivergence → dysautonomia AND pain
- Relationship between JH and symptoms stronger in neurodivergent than neurotypical participants

**Relevance to lower-baseline model:** Establishes the mechanism linking neurodivergence to autonomic dysfunction — connective tissue laxity impairs vascular proprioception and venous return, producing chronic cerebral hypoperfusion. This is an additive energy drain on top of neurodivergent baseline neural costs.

**Quality:** Medium (0.65). Novel mechanistic mediation finding; adequate sample for primary outcome.

### 5. Baeza-Velasco et al. 2025 — Autism–hypermobility–EDS systematic review and meta-analysis
**Citation:** Baeza-Velasco C, Vergne J, Poli M, Kalisch L, Calati R. Autism. 2025;29(8):1939–1958.
**PMID:** 40145613 | **DOI:** 10.1177/13623613251328059
**BibTeX key:** `BaezaVelasco2025autismEDS`

**Study design:** Systematic review and prevalence meta-analyses, 20 studies
**Key findings:**
- JH in autistic individuals: 22.3% overall; 31% clinical assessment
- HSD/EDS in autistic samples: 27.9% overall; 39% clinical assessment
- 12/15 studies found significant ASD–JH association
- Autistic individuals ~7.4× more likely to have EDS than comparison groups

**Relevance to lower-baseline model:** Meta-analytically confirms the autism–EDS cluster. Since EDS → POTS → cerebral hypoperfusion → reduced brain ATP delivery, autism + EDS represents a compounded lower baseline via two independent mechanisms (mitochondrial dysfunction + cerebral hypoperfusion).

**Quality:** Medium-High (0.70). Systematic meta-analysis; 12/15 studies consistent.

### 6. Lau et al. 2015 — Migraine confers 1.5× CFS risk, national cohort
**Citation:** Lau C-I, Lin C-C, Chen W-H, Wang H-C, Kao C-H. J Psychosom Res. 2015;79(6):514–518.
**PMID:** 26505533 | **DOI:** 10.1016/j.jpsychores.2015.10.005
**BibTeX key:** `Lau2015migraineCFS`

**Study design:** Retrospective cohort, Taiwan NHIRD, n=6,902 migraineurs vs. n=27,608 controls
**Key findings:**
- CFS incidence: 52.72 vs. 28.85 per 10,000 person-years (IRR ~1.5)
- Age ≥65: IRR=2.11 (strongest effect)
- Dose-response: CFS incidence scaled with migraine frequency
- Authors propose mitochondrial dysfunction and central sensitization as shared mechanisms

**Relevance to lower-baseline model:** Large epidemiological evidence that migraine history predisposes to CFS. Dose-response relationship supports cumulative mitochondrial depletion from repeated cortical spreading depression events. The age-amplification is consistent with progressive reserve reduction over time.

**Quality:** Medium (0.60). Large national cohort; ICD-based CFS diagnosis unreliable; older cohort (2006–2010).

### 7. Wang et al. 2023 — Energy metabolism disturbance in migraine: mitochondrial review
**Citation:** Wang Y, Wang Y, Yue G, Zhao Y. Front Physiol. 2023;14:1133528.
**PMID:** 37123270 | **DOI:** 10.3389/fphys.2023.1133528
**BibTeX key:** `Wang2023migraineMito`

**Study design:** Narrative review
**Key findings:**
- Migraineurs: elevated blood lactate, lower NAA, decreased NADH dehydrogenase, citrate synthase, cytochrome c oxidase
- Cortical spreading depression creates massive transient ATP demand beyond mitochondrially impaired cortex recovery capacity
- Migraine triggers and ME/CFS relapse triggers are identical: stress, sleep deprivation, fasting, exercise
- Calcium dysregulation + ROS create feed-forward mitochondrial damage cycle

**Relevance to lower-baseline model:** Provides the mechanistic explanation for Lau 2015 finding. Migraine is a disease of chronic mitochondrial impairment, not just episodic events — between attacks, migraineurs operate with lower ETC reserve, leaving less buffer for post-viral demands.

**Quality:** Medium (0.60). Mechanistically coherent; narrative review limitations apply.

### 8. Colpani Filho et al. 2025 — BH4 pathway in ASD: systematic review
**Citation:** Colpani Filho C, Melfior L, Ramos SL, et al. Brain Sciences. 2025;15(2):151.
**PMID:** 40002484 | **DOI:** 10.3390/brainsci15020151
**BibTeX key:** `ColpaniFilho2025BH4ASD`

**Study design:** Systematic review
**Key findings:**
- Lower BH4 consistently found in ASD biological samples vs. controls
- Elevated neopterin in plasma/urine; decreased in CSF; elevated NO
- BH4 is cofactor for dopamine, serotonin, NE, NO synthesis — all impaired in ASD and ME/CFS
- BH4 supplementation shows symptom improvement in some ASD trials

**Relevance to lower-baseline model:** Links ASD to the BH4–ME/CFS pathway already documented in the project. Low BH4 in ASD → reduced NO → impaired vascular tone → worse cerebral autoregulation → lower brain energy delivery under orthostatic or metabolic stress. GCH1 variants may be a shared genetic predisposition.

**Quality:** Medium (0.60). Consistent cross-study direction; therapeutic evidence preliminary.

### 9. Williams et al. 2025 — GCH1 rs841, BH4 deficit, and mental health: case series
**Citation:** Williams GE, Hausman-Cohen S, Sotos M, et al. Int J Mol Sci. 2025;26(16):8030.
**PMCID:** PMC12386507 | **DOI:** 10.3390/ijms26168030
**BibTeX key:** `Williams2025GCH1mental`

**Study design:** Case series, n=5 patients
**Key findings:**
- GCH1 rs841 AA homozygous (~4% population): reduced GCH1 expression → lower BH4
- Associated with ADHD, ASD, depression, PMDD, treatment-resistant anxiety, insomnia
- Low-dose BH4 (0.088–0.292 mg/kg/day) improved all five patients
- Precision psychiatry framing: genomic targeting vs. symptom management

**Relevance to lower-baseline model:** GCH1 rs841 as a high-frequency shared genetic vulnerability: at 4% homozygous prevalence (plus heterozygous carriers), this represents a substantial population-level predisposition. The same GCH1 pathway is implicated in ME/CFS BH4 literature already in the project.

**Quality:** Low (0.35). Case series only; n=5; no controls; use as hypothesis-generating evidence only.

### 10. DelRosso et al. 2026 — Iron deficiency across ADHD and ASD: comparative review
**Citation:** DelRosso LM, Estrada Chaverri L, Ceballos Fuentes FA. Children (Basel). 2026;13(2):180.
**PMID:** 41749537 | **DOI:** 10.3390/children13020180
**BibTeX key:** `DelRosso2026ironNeurodevelopmental`

**Study design:** Narrative review
**Key findings:**
- Reduced brain iron in dopaminergic regions consistently found in ADHD neuroimaging
- ASD: ferritin <50 ng/mL linked to sleep-related motor symptoms; iron supplementation helps
- Iron is cofactor for dopamine/serotonin/NE synthesis AND mitochondrial complex I/II
- Recommended ferritin thresholds: <30 ng/mL (ADHD), <50 ng/mL (ASD sleep phenotype)

**Relevance to lower-baseline model:** Iron deficiency creates a double metabolic deficit in neurodivergent patients: impaired neurotransmitter synthesis (reducing cognitive efficiency and requiring more compensatory neural effort) plus reduced mitochondrial capacity (lower ATP yield). This is a modifiable pre-existing baseline reducer, making iron screening/correction in neurodivergent ME/CFS patients clinically actionable.

**Quality:** Medium (0.60). Mechanistically robust; thresholds are expert consensus; no direct ME/CFS data.

### 11. Raw et al. 2025 — Neurodivergence as post-COVID-19 risk factor
**Citation:** Raw RK, Rees J, Pearson A, Chadwick DR. COVID. 2025;6(1):1–7.
**DOI:** 10.3390/covid6010001
**BibTeX key:** `Raw2025neurodivergencePostCOVID`

**Study design:** Cross-sectional, n=267 healthcare workers
**Key findings:**
- Higher autistic trait scores (not formal diagnosis) predicted COVID-19 symptoms lasting >12 weeks
- Sensory reactivity subscale: strongest predictor of prolonged symptoms
- Mechanism proposed: central sensitization → amplified post-viral symptom perpetuation

**Relevance to lower-baseline model:** Dimensional evidence (autistic traits, not just diagnosis) implicates the same biology across a spectrum. Central sensitization — a form of neural hyperexcitability that consumes extra metabolic resources — may be the mechanism by which neurodivergent traits specifically predispose to long COVID/ME/CFS transition.

**Quality:** Medium-Low (0.45). Small sample; self-reported traits; cross-sectional; preliminary signal only.

## Cross-Cutting Mechanistic Map

```
Neurodivergent condition          Mechanism(s) reducing metabolic reserve
─────────────────────────────     ──────────────────────────────────────────────
ASD                               Mitochondrial ETC dysfunction (lactate, pyruvate, ↓ATP)
                                  + low BH4 (↓dopamine, serotonin, NO)
                                  + neuroinflammation (IL-6 chronically elevated)

ADHD                              ↓ dopaminergic efficiency → more neural effort per task
                                  + iron deficiency (↓ complex I/II, ↓ dopamine synthesis)
                                  + neuroinflammation (IL-6)

hEDS / hypermobility              Connective tissue laxity → venous pooling → ↓ cerebral perfusion
                                  + POTS (orthostatic hypoperfusion)
                                  + autonomic dysfunction → chronic sympathetic activation → ATP drain

ASD + hEDS (compounded)           Mitochondrial dysfunction + cerebral hypoperfusion
                                  = dual mechanism; highest-risk subgroup

Migraine                          Recurrent CSD → cumulative mitochondrial depletion
                                  + shared energy failure triggers with ME/CFS

GCH1 rs841 (transdiagnostic)      ↓ BH4 → ↓ NO → ↓ vascular tone → ↓ cerebral autoregulation
                                  (affects ASD, ADHD, depression, ME/CFS)

Iron deficiency (transdiagnostic) ↓ Complex I/II → ↓ ATP yield
                                  + ↓ dopamine/serotonin synthesis → ↑ compensatory neural effort
```
