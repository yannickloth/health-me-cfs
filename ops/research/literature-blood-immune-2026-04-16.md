### Paper 1: Extracellular Vesicle Signatures (NEW)
- **Full citation**: Seifert M, Schäfers J, Douglas FF, Schwarzburg C, Boristowski D, Birke A, Klein O, Sotzny F, Rubarth K, Windzio L, Beez CM, Peters CK, Wittke K, Scheibenbogen C, Greco A (2026). Extracellular Vesicle Protein and MiRNA Signatures as Biomarkers for Post-Infectious ME/CFS Patients. *International Journal of Molecular Sciences*, 27(5). DOI: 10.3390/ijms27052314
- **PMID**: 41828537
- **PMCID**: PMC12984851
- **Study design**: Case-control; plasma EV isolation by size-exclusion chromatography; proteomics + small RNA sequencing + qPCR validation
- **Sample size**: n≤10/cohort (proteomics); n=12 post-COVID ME/CFS vs n=15 HC (miRNA); all female
- **Key findings**:
  - EVs strongly expressed tetraspanins with minor surface marker differences between groups
  - Altered EV cargo proteins: hemoglobin subunit alpha and IGFBP acid labile subunit vs HC
  - Significant downregulation of hsa-let-7b-5p in EVs from post-COVID-19 ME/CFS vs HC
  - Reduced hsa-let-7b-5p correlated with impaired physical functioning, increased fatigue, pain, immune activation
- **Certainty**: 0.30
- **Certainty rationale**: Very small sample (n≤10 proteomics, n=12 miRNA); single-center; all female limits generalizability; no independent replication yet; International Journal of Molecular Sciences is mid-tier open-access
- **Replication status**: No replication; builds on prior EV work by Bonilla et al. 2022
- **Limitations**: Very small n; female-only; post-COVID subgroup analysis underpowered; discovery phase only

### Paper 2: CSF Proteomics (NEW)
- **Full citation**: Bragee B, Li P, Meadows D, Widgren A, Sjögren P, Ghatan PH, Bertilson BC, Xiao W, Bergquist J (2026). Proteomic signatures in cerebrospinal fluid and their clinical associations in patients with ME/CFS. *Scientific Reports*. DOI: 10.1038/s41598-026-46965-1
- **PMID**: 41932997
- **Study design**: Cross-sectional CSF proteomics with clinical factor analysis (POTS status, severity)
- **Sample size**: n=31 ME/CFS patients (no control group for differential expression; ratio-based severity analysis within-group)
- **Key findings**:
  - 902 proteins quantified (expressed in ≥11 samples)
  - Neutrophil degranulation and platelet activation enriched in POTS+ patients
  - Complement cascade, coagulation pathways, IGFBP-mediated IGF transport enriched in severe cases
  - Four severity-associated protein ratios related to cellular stress, extracellular remodelling, immune-neuronal interaction
- **Certainty**: 0.35
- **Certainty rationale**: n=31 single cohort, no healthy control group; ratio-based within-group analysis only; ahead-of-print; Scientific Reports is mid-tier; requires validation in independent cohort
- **Replication status**: No replication; authors note need for larger independent cohort validation
- **Limitations**: No control group; small n; single-center; severity stratification within single cohort limits statistical power; cross-sectional

### Paper 3: Diffusion MRI White Matter Neuroinflammation (NEW)
- **Full citation**: Yu Q, Kothe K, Kwiatek RA, Del Fante P, Bonner A, Calhoun VD, Shan ZY (2026). Evidence of White Matter Neuroinflammation in Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome: A Diffusion-Based Neuroinflammation Imaging Study. *Human Brain Mapping*, 47(4):e70505. DOI: 10.1002/hbm.70505
- **PMID**: 41834684
- **Study design**: Case-control advanced diffusion MRI with neuroinflammation imaging (NII) model; voxel-wise TBSS + clinical correlations
- **Sample size**: n=67 ME/CFS vs n=67 matched HC (median age 38; 54F/13M per group)
- **Key findings**:
  - Widespread white matter abnormalities: lower NII-HR (cerebral edema), lower NII-RF (cellular infiltration), higher NII-FF (axonal reorganisation)
  - Conventional DTI showed minimal group differences — NII metrics far more sensitive
  - Lower NII-RF, NII-AD, NII-MD associated with worse mental health
  - Lower NII-RF associated with higher disability
  - Higher NII-FF associated with lower disease severity within ME/CFS
- **Certainty**: 0.60
- **Certainty rationale**: n=67 per group is good for neuroimaging; rigorous matching; advanced NII model vs conventional DTI; Human Brain Mapping is reputable specialty journal; no independent replication yet; cross-sectional limits causal inference
- **Replication status**: No direct replication; provides first NII-based evidence in ME/CFS; addresses prior inconsistent DTI findings
- **Limitations**: Cross-sectional; single-center; NII model requires validation in other cohorts; cannot distinguish neuroinflammation from other white matter pathology

### Paper 4: Single-Cell Immune Analysis — LC-ME/CFS (NEW)
- **Full citation**: Shahbaz S, Bozorgmehr N, Rahmati A, Abouda A, Syed H, Osman M, Elahi S (2026). Single-cell analysis reveals immune remodeling of monocytes, NK cells, T cell exhaustion, and Galectin-9-associated depletion of gamma delta and mucosal-associated invariant T cells in Long COVID with ME/CFS. *Frontiers in Immunology*, 17:1745933. DOI: 10.3389/fimmu.2026.1745933
- **PMID**: 41822518
- **PMCID**: PMC12975919
- **Study design**: Single-cell RNA-seq of PBMCs from LC-ME/CFS patients; comparative analysis with idiopathic ME/CFS datasets
- **Sample size**: Female LC-ME/CFS vs recovered individuals (12 months post-COVID); compared with public idiopathic ME/CFS scRNA-seq datasets
- **Key findings**:
  - Marked reduction in naïve CD4+/CD8+ T cells, Tregs, MAIT cells, γδ T cells in LC-ME/CFS
  - Expansion of effector T cells; NK cells reduced with altered activation
  - Galectin-9-TIM-3 interaction identified as potential driver of γδ and MAIT cell depletion
  - Idiopathic ME/CFS showed less pronounced changes: T cell activation but no MAIT/NK cell reduction, no T cell exhaustion
  - Platelets and low-density neutrophils expanded with activated transcripts
  - Monocytes showed reduced phagocytosis genes, increased pro-inflammatory cytokine genes
- **Certainty**: 0.50
- **Certainty rationale**: scRNA-seq is powerful but sample sizes typically small; novel mechanistic insight (Galectin-9 pathway); comparison with public datasets is methodologically limited by batch effects; Frontiers in Immunology is reputable open-access; no replication
- **Replication status**: No replication; idiopathic ME/CFS comparison uses external datasets with different protocols
- **Limitations**: Female-only LC-ME/CFS cohort; idiopathic ME/CFS comparison across datasets introduces batch effects; sample size not reported explicitly for primary cohort; single timepoint (12 months post-infection)

### Paper 5: Circulating Proteome — Hoel/Bergen (ALREADY CITED)
- **Full citation**: Hoel A, Hoel F, Dyrstad SE, Chapola H, Rekeland IG, Risa K, Alme K, Sørland K, Brokstad KA, Marti HP, Mella O, Fluge Ø, Tronstad KJ (2026). Charting the circulating proteome in ME/CFS using cross-system profiling to uncover mechanistic insights. *Cell Reports Medicine*, 7(3):102647. DOI: 10.1016/j.xcrm.2026.102647
- **PMID**: 41785863
- **PMCID**: PMC13006441
- **Existing citation key**: Hoel2026proteome
- **Study design**: Case-control aptamer-based serum proteomics (SomaScan 7K); antibody-based validation
- **Sample size**: n=50 ME/CFS vs n=29 HC
- **Key findings**:
  - 1823 aptamers significantly different (845 after FDR); 7326 protein targets analyzed
  - Broad increase in secreted proteins; intracellular/skeletal muscle proteins reduced
  - Immune reprogramming: distinct reduction in activated-neutrophil secreted proteins
  - Secretome analysis: intensified regulatory interactions for immune activity, inflammation, vasculature, metabolism
  - Antibody-based validation confirmed selected findings
- **Certainty**: 0.55
- **Certainty rationale**: n=79 total, Cell Reports Medicine is high-quality journal, SomaScan platform well-validated, independent validation with antibodies; single study from Bergen group; no external replication
- **Replication status**: No independent replication; internal validation with orthogonal antibody methods
- **Limitations**: Single-center; n=50/29 modest; cross-sectional; Bergen cohort may not be representative

### Paper 6: Complement Genetics — CDC (NEW)
- **Full citation**: Maya J, Unger ER, Lin JS, Rajeevan MS (2026). Genetic Insights into Circulating Complement Proteins in Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome: A Potential Inflammatory Subgroup. *International Journal of Molecular Sciences*, 27(3). DOI: 10.3390/ijms27031574
- **PMID**: 41683992
- **PMCID**: PMC12898610
- **Study design**: pQTL analysis of complement proteins; linear/logistic regression; UK Biobank validation
- **Sample size**: 50 ME/CFS vs 121 non-fatigued (general population); UK Biobank replication for fatigue phenotypes
- **Key findings**:
  - ME/CFS patients with certain pQTLs show dysregulated alternative complement pathway
  - Identified inflammatory subgroup with high C3/low Bb profile
  - 6 significant pQTLs also associated with fatigue phenotypes in UK Biobank (4 complement-associated)
  - Establishes genetic link to alternative complement pathway dysregulation in a patient subset
- **Certainty**: 0.50
- **Certainty rationale**: n=171 total is modest; novel genetic approach; UK Biobank partial validation is strength; IJMS is mid-tier; CDC authorship adds credibility; single discovery cohort
- **Replication status**: Partial — UK Biobank fatigue phenotype association provides population-level validation; complement protein pQTL findings not independently replicated in another ME/CFS cohort
- **Limitations**: Small discovery cohort; general population controls (not healthy controls); pQTL approach identifies genetic associations, not direct complement activation; cross-sectional

### Paper 7: 7T fMRI Cognitive Fatigue (ALREADY CITED)
- **Full citation**: Inderyas M, Thapaliya K, Marshall-Gradisnik S, Barnden L (2026). Distinct functional connectivity patterns in myalgic encephalomyelitis and long COVID patients during cognitive fatigue: a 7 Tesla task-fMRI study. *Journal of Translational Medicine*, 24(1):236. DOI: 10.1186/s12967-026-07708-y
- **PMID**: 41559785
- **PMCID**: PMC12903671
- **Existing citation key**: Faro2024connectivity
- **Study design**: 7T task-fMRI with Stroop paradigm; Pre vs Post fatigue; ROI-to-ROI connectivity analysis
- **Sample size**: n=32 ME/CFS, n=19 Long COVID, n=27 HC
- **Key findings**:
  - HC showed increased FC in subcortical regions Pre→Post (engagement); patients did not
  - pwLC: reduced nucleus accumbens–vermis FC (Pre), increased prefrontal-hippocampal FC (Post)
  - pwME/CFS: increased left cuneiform nucleus–right medulla FC
  - Reduced dopaminergic hippocampal–nucleus accumbens connectivity in patients
  - Core network FC to cerebellum, amygdala, caudate, red nucleus correlated with cognition scores
- **Certainty**: 0.50
- **Certainty rationale**: n=78 total across 3 groups; 7T provides high spatial resolution; novel task-based paradigm; single-center; J Transl Med is reputable; no replication; cross-sectional
- **Replication status**: No replication; builds on prior resting-state fMRI literature
- **Limitations**: Cross-sectional; LC group not stratified by ME/CFS status per se; single-center; 7T accessibility limits reproducibility

### Paper 8: Lipids Analysis in Cell Lines (ALREADY CITED)
- **Full citation**: Missailidis D, Armstrong CW, Anderson D, Allan CY, Sanislav O, Smith PK, Esmaili T, Creek DJ, Annesley SJ, Fisher PR (2026). Multi-omics identifies lipid accumulation in Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome cell lines: a case-control study. *Journal of Translational Medicine*, 24(1):145. DOI: 10.1186/s12967-025-07620-x
- **PMID**: 41508032
- **PMCID**: PMC12874856
- **Existing citation key**: Lipidaccumulation2025
- **Study design**: Multi-omics (lipidomics + metabolomics + proteomics + transcriptomics) on B-cell-derived lymphoblastoid cell lines
- **Sample size**: n=15 ME/CFS LCLs vs n=17 HC LCLs (all female)
- **Key findings**:
  - PC(O-38:4) significantly reduced, almost entirely discriminative of ME/CFS status
  - Triglycerides, diradylglycerolipids, fatty acids elevated in ME/CFS LCLs
  - PTDSS1 (phosphatidylserine synthase 1) elevated — concordant across proteomics and transcriptomics
  - ME/CFS LCLs showed tendency toward more saturated lipid content
  - Integrated multi-omics identified 15 dysregulated pathways
- **Certainty**: 0.40
- **Certainty rationale**: n=32 total small; cell line model not primary cells; all female; multi-omics integration is methodological strength; J Transl Med is reputable; no replication
- **Replication status**: No replication; builds on group's prior LCL mitochondrial findings
- **Limitations**: Cell line model may not reflect in vivo physiology; all female; small n; B-cell LCLs are transformed cells

### Paper 9: Multi-omics — Heng/Guillemin (ALREADY CITED)

Mapping the complexity of ME/CFS: Evidence for abnormal energy metabolism, altered immune profile, and vascular dysfunction. *Cell Reports Medicine*, 6(12):102514. DOI: 10.1016/j.xcrm.2025.102514
- **PMID**: 41406947
- **PMCID**: PMC12765951
- **Existing citation key**: heng2025mecfs
- **Study design**: Multimodal case-control: energy metabolism (ATP/ADP/AMP), immune profiling, plasma proteomics; CART modeling
- **Sample size**: Age- and sex-matched ME/CFS vs HC (exact n not stated in abstract; reported in full text)
- **Key findings**:
  - Immune cells: elevated AMP/ADP, reduced ATP/ADP ratio → cellular energy stress
  - Immune skewing: less mature CD4+, CD8+, γδ T cell subsets; reduced CD56lowCD16+ terminal NK cells; reduced cDC2
  - Plasma proteins associated with thrombus formation and vascular reactivity elevated → endothelial dysfunction
  - CART modeling identified strong predictive variables for ME/CFS
- **Certainty**: 0.50
- **Certainty rationale**: Multimodal approach is strength; Cell Reports Medicine is high-quality; exact sample sizes needed from full text; no replication; single-center
- **Replication status**: No independent replication
- **Limitations**: Exact sample size not in abstract; single-center; cross-sectional

### Paper 10: Plasma Proteome Post-Exertion — Hanson (NEW)
- **Full citation**: Germain A, Glass KA, Eckert MA, Giloteaux L, Hanson MR (2025). Temporal Dynamics of the Plasma Proteomic Landscape Reveals Maladaptation in ME/CFS Following Exertion. *Molecular & Cellular Proteomics*, 24(12):101467. DOI: 10.1016/j.mcpro.2025.101467
- **PMID**: 41237904
- **PMCID**: PMC12757482
- **Study design**: Longitudinal plasma proteomics (SomaScan 7K) before/after maximal exercise; 2 CPET tests separated by 24h
- **Sample size**: n=79 ME/CFS vs n=53 sedentary controls (96F, 36M total; 132 individuals)
- **Key findings**:
  - Most pronounced proteomic alterations during recovery phase (PEM onset) in ME/CFS
  - Persistent dysregulation of immune, metabolic, neuromuscular pathways in ME/CFS post-exercise
  - Suppression of T and B cell signaling; downregulation of IL-17 and cell-cell communication
  - Upregulation of glycolysis/gluconeogenesis → mitochondrial stress and impaired immune recovery
  - Sex-stratified analyses revealed distinct molecular responses (females vs males)
  - Proteomic-physiology correlations (VO2max, anaerobic threshold) disrupted in ME/CFS
  - Symptom severity correlated with immune protein changes (muscle pain, sore throat, lymph node tenderness)
- **Certainty**: 0.65
- **Certainty rationale**: n=132 is largest proteomics ME/CFS study; longitudinal design captures PEM dynamics; sedentary controls distinguish disease from deconditioning; MCP is top-tier proteomics journal; sex-stratified analysis; no replication yet
- **Replication status**: No replication; builds on group's prior 2022 JCI Insight metabolomics exercise study
- **Limitations**: Single-center; sedentary controls may still differ from ME/CFS in important ways; 24h recovery window may miss later PEM dynamics

### Paper 11: Lipkin Multi-Omics 2025 (ALREADY CITED)
- **Full citation**: Che X, Ranjan A, Guo C, Zhang K, Goldsmith R, Levine S, Moneghetti KJ, Zhai Y, Ge L, Mishra N, Hornig M, Bateman L, Klimas NG, Montoya JG, Peterson DL, Klein SL, Fiehn O, Komaroff AL, Lipkin WI (2025). Heightened innate immunity may trigger chronic inflammation, fatigue and post-exertional malaise in ME/CFS. *npj Metabolic Health and Disease*, 3(1):34. DOI: 10.1038/s44324-025-00079-w
- **PMID**: 40903540
- **PMCID**: PMC12408823
- **Existing citation key**: Che2025
- **Study design**: Multi-omics (plasma metabolomics, proteomics) + immune stimulation assays; pre/post exercise
- **Sample size**: ME/CFS patients and controls (multi-site cohort including multiple US ME/CFS clinics; exact n from full text)
- **Key findings**:
  - Exaggerated innate immune response to microbial antigens
  - Impaired energy production: citric acid cycle, β-oxidation, urea cycle
  - Systemic inflammation linked to lipid abnormalities
  - Disrupted extracellular matrix homeostasis → endogenous inflammatory ligands
  - Reduced cell-cell adhesion + gut dysbiosis
  - Complement activation; redox imbalance (copper-dependent antioxidant pathways)
  - Tryptophan-serotonin-kynurenine pathway dysregulation
  - Many abnormalities worsened post-exercise and correlated with symptom intensity
- **Certainty**: 0.60
- **Certainty rationale**: Multi-site with major ME/CFS research groups (Lipkin, Hornig, Klimas, Montoya, Bateman, Komaroff); npj Metabolic Health and Disease (Nature Portfolio); multi-omics + functional immune assays; Bayesian statistics; some findings replicate prior work; no independent replication of full dataset
- **Replication status**: Partial — many findings confirm prior studies (Naviaux 2016 metabolomics, Montoya 2017 cytokines, Germain 2022 exercise metabolomics); full multi-omics panel not independently replicated
- **Limitations**: Cross-sectional exercise challenge; multi-site introduces site variability; heterogeneous cohort

### Paper 12: TRPM3 NK Cell Dysfunction (ALREADY CITED)
- **Full citation**: Sasso EM, Er TS, Eaton-Fitch N, Hool L, Muraki K, Marshall-Gradisnik S (2025). Large-scale investigation confirms TRPM3 ion channel dysfunction in Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome. *Frontiers in Medicine*, 12:1703924. DOI: 10.3389/fmed.2025.1703924
- **PMID**: 41585253
- **PMCID**: PMC12823910
- **Existing citation key**: Sasso2026trpm3
- **Study design**: Multi-site case-control; whole-cell patch-clamp on isolated NK cells; TRPM3 activity assessment
- **Sample size**: n=36 ME/CFS vs n=42 HC (Canadian Consensus Criteria); 2 independent laboratory sites
- **Key findings**:
  - Significant reduction in TRPM3 function in NK cells from ME/CFS across all parameters
  - No significant effect of laboratory site → confirms consistency
  - Confirms prior TRPM3 dysfunction findings from same research group
- **Certainty**: 0.50
- **Certainty rationale**: Multi-site design is strength; n=78 total reasonable; Canadian Consensus Criteria well-defined; replication of own prior work (not independent); Frontiers in Medicine is mid-tier; patch-clamp is gold standard for ion channel assessment
- **Replication status**: Internal replication across 2 sites; prior work from same group (Marshall-Gradisnik lab); no independent external replication
- **Limitations**: From same research group that originally described TRPM3 findings; no independent lab replication; NK cells only (not other cell types)

## Cross-Cutting Themes

1. **Immune remodeling (not just activation)**: Papers 4 (Shahbaz), 5 (Hoel), 10 (Hanson), 11 (Lipkin) all describe immune reprogramming — reduced naïve T cells, T cell exhaustion, altered monocyte function, neutrophil signature changes
2. **Blood/vascular compartment dysfunction**: Papers 2 (CSF), 3 (NII white matter), 5 (Hoel proteome — vasculature), 9 (Heng — thrombus/vascular proteins), 10 (Hanson — coagulation pathways) converge on blood-brain barrier, coagulation, endothelial dysfunction
3. **Energy metabolism**: Papers 8 (lipid accumulation), 9 (ATP/ADP ratio), 10 (glycolysis upregulation), 11 (TCA cycle, β-oxidation) consistently show metabolic dysfunction
4. **Post-exertional malaise mechanisms**: Papers 10 (Hanson) and 11 (Lipkin) provide the strongest direct evidence for PEM molecular mechanisms
5. **Complement activation**: Papers 2 (CSF complement cascade), 6 (CDC complement genetics), 11 (Lipkin complement activation) form a convergent signal
6. **Biomarker candidates**: Paper 1 (EV signatures), Paper 3 (NII metrics), Paper 8 (PC(O-38:4)), Paper 12 (TRPM3) offer diagnostic biomarker leads
