# Literature Summary: Fitness-Maintenance Framework in ME/CFS

**Date:** 2026-05-26  
**Research stream:** `fitness-maintenance`

## Key Papers (Existing in References.bib)

### 1. Keller et al. 2024 — Two-Day CPET in ME/CFS
- **Cite:** @keller2024cpet
- **Certainty:** 0.75
- **Type:** Largest 2-day CPET study (n=84 ME/CFS, matched controls)
- **Key finding:** 5–8% declines in VO₂peak Day 1→2, recovery 13+ days, doubling of severe impairment. Demonstrates that exercise produces metabolic failure in ME/CFS, not training adaptation.
- **Relevance:** Provides objective foundation for fitness-maintenance model — exercise cannot produce adaptation, only maintenance of current function is possible.

### 2. Jason et al. 2008 — Energy Envelope Theory
- **Cite:** @Jason2008EnergyEnvelope
- **Certainty:** 0.65
- **Type:** Longitudinal cohort study
- **Key finding:** Patients who maintain energy quotient ≤100% show improved physical functioning and reduced fatigue; energy envelope maintenance is active treatment, not just symptom avoidance.
- **Relevance:** Theoretical foundation for fitness-maintenance concept — defines the safe operating range (envelope) within which activity avoids PEM.

### 3. Jason et al. 2013 — Energy Conservation/Envelope Interventions
- **Cite:** @jason2012energy
- **Certainty:** 0.55
- **Type:** Intervention study
- **Key finding:** Energy envelope interventions help patients pace and manage symptoms.
- **Relevance:** Operationalizes the energy envelope as a clinical intervention.

### 4. Charlton et al. 2026 — PEM and Deconditioning in BJSM
- **Cite:** @Charlton2026pem
- **Certainty:** 0.60
- **Type:** Editorial/review in British Journal of Sports Medicine
- **Key finding:** PEM in long COVID cannot be explained by cardiac deconditioning; exercise intolerance reflects pathology, not detraining.
- **Relevance:** Counters the argument that "fitness improvement is needed to reverse deconditioning" — the pathology is not deconditioning.

### 5. Kindlon 2011 — GET Harm Rates
- **Cite:** @Kindlon2011GET
- **Certainty:** 0.60
- **Type:** Patient survey (n=large)
- **Key finding:** 51% of ME/CFS patients report harm from graded exercise therapy.
- **Relevance:** Evidence that fitness-improvement models cause harm in ME/CFS.

### 6. Sommerfelt & Scheibenbogen 2024 — FUNCAP-27
- **Cite:** @Sommerfelt2024FUNCAP
- **Certainty:** 0.60
- **Type:** Validation study
- **Key finding:** FUNCAP-27 provides 8-domain functional capacity assessment validated in ME/CFS.
- **Relevance:** Provides the measurement tool for ADL-capacity-as-primary-outcome reframing.

### 7. Goudsmit et al. 2008 — Pacing Consensus
- **Certainty:** 0.50
- **Type:** Consensus document
- **Key finding:** Pacing can stabilize condition and avoid PEM.
- **Notes:** Already cited in ch17; consensus rather than empirical data.

- **General relevance:** ACT frameworks for accepting limitation while living meaningfully.
- **Relevance to fitness-maintenance:** Psychological dimension of accepting the fitness-maintenance ceiling.

## Gaps Identified

1. **No prospective RCT of fitness-maintenance vs. fitness-improvement framing** — testable prediction exists in ch17 but no trial yet conducted.
2. **No ME/CFS-specific ACT trial** for acceptance of activity limitations.
3. **Cross-disease fitness-maintenance literature** (COPD, heart failure) not yet integrated — these fields have well-developed "functional maintenance" frameworks.
4. **FUNCAP-27 sensitivity to 3-month change** not established.
