# Phase 1 — Literature Summary: Petrov2026 Monocyte/DC Immunophenotyping

## Primary Paper

**Petrov S, Bozhkova M, Ivanovska M, et al.** Comprehensive Immunophenotyping of Monocytes and Dendritic Cells Suggests Distinct Pathophysiology in Chronic Fatigue Syndrome and Long COVID. *Int J Mol Sci*. 2026;27(10):4488. DOI: 10.3390/ijms27104488

**Key finding:** ME/CFS (n=103) and long COVID (n=63) show divergent immune architecture despite clinical overlap. Long COVID → persistent immune activation (M2 polarization, CD80↑, DC expansion) with exhaustion. ME/CFS → immune suppression (CD80↓, CCR7↓, DC contraction).

**Certainty:** 0.70 — Largest comparative immunophenotyping study to date, n=207, FDR correction, post-hoc power ≥0.80. Cross-sectional, not replicated, no functional assays.

## Contradicting/Nuanced Evidence

- Heng 2025 found innate immune hyperactivation in ME/CFS (different direction from Petrov's "immune suppression") — may reflect disease stage/severity heterogeneity
- Che 2025 also found exaggerated innate response — suggests immune suppression may coexist with or follow hyperactivation phase
- Long COVID showed both activation AND exhaustion — suggests immune dysregulation is complex, not simply one state
