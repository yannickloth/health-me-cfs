## Key Mechanistic Findings

### Direct Clinical Evidence (PCC)
1. **Efficacy unclear at best:** Balan 2026 sys rev — all 3 RCTs show no superiority of active taVNS over sham for fatigue in PCC. GRADE: "very low" for efficacy
2. **Paradoxical sham > active:** Percin 2025 — best-powered adequately blinded RCT showed sham outperforming active taVNS on fatigue, despite HRV increases in active group
3. **HRV modulation confirmed:** Active taVNS consistently produces measurable changes in HRV indices (Percin; Balan sys rev), confirming cardiac autonomic engagement. But HRV changes do not translate to clinical improvement
4. **Safety consistently favorable:** All studies report mild AEs only (skin irritation, vertigo, headache). No serious AEs in any PCC trial. COVIVA: mild AE in 6.7% each for skin irritation and vertigo

### Null / Negative / Contradicting Evidence
| Finding | Source | Severity |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Sham > active taVNS for fatigue | Percin 2025 — best-powered RCT | Critical — challenges assumption that taVNS works via specific neural pathway rather than non-specific sham effects |
| No superiority over sham | Gierthmuehlen 2026 (COVIVA) — RCT pilot n=45 | Strong — adequately designed but underpowered |
| All groups improved over time | Multiple RCTs | Suggests large placebo/natural history component |
| GRADE "very low" efficacy | Balan 2026 | Systematic-level null finding |
| taVNS crashes in severe ME/CFS | Lugg 2024 — patient survey n=116 | Critical safety signal for ME/CFS cross-application |

### Competing Mechanisms
- **Alternative pathways for post-COVID fatigue:** Neuroinflammation (TSPO PET: Varying levels of inflammatory activity, PMID 40935606), mitochondrial dysfunction, viral persistence, endothelial dysfunction — all may be more fundamental than vagal tone deficits
- **Non-specific effects dominate:** Consistent improvement across sham groups in all RCTs suggests conditioning, natural recovery, or non-specific VNS effects (auditory/somatosensory) account for apparent benefits in uncontrolled studies
- **Autonomic engagement ≠ clinical benefit:** HRV changes consistently dissociate from symptom improvement, questioning whether vagal modulation is the correct therapeutic target

### Safety Signals for ME/CFS Population
- **Lugg 2024 (n=116 ME/CFS survey):** 56% reported favorable effects; CRITICAL: "normal" tVNS settings can cause PEM/crashes in severe ME/CFS
- Farmer 2022 systematic review: taVNS generally well-tolerated; most common AE skin irritation (~5-10%); no cardiac safety concerns with auricular (vs cervical) VNS
- **Implication for ME/CFS cross-application:** If taVNS is to be tested in ME/CFS, starting parameters must be significantly lower than PCC trials, given Lugg 2024 safety signal

## Research Gaps
1. **No adequately powered trial:** Largest RCT to date is ~n=50 (Percin). All "underpowered for efficacy"
2. **No double-blind sham-controlled trial with objective biomarkers of target engagement**
3. **No trial in ME/CFS** (only patient survey + open-label pilot)
4. **Optimal parameters unknown:** Frequency (25 Hz most common), intensity, duration, laterality all vary
5. **Mechanism uncertain:** HRV changes dissociate from clinical improvement — what is the therapeutic mechanism?
6. **No validated marker of central vagal engagement** in any trial
