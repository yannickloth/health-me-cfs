# Literature Summary: Antigen-Specific IgE to Spike/Tick Antigens → Mast Cell → Neuroinflammation → Cognition

**Date:** 2026-06-24
**Topic slug:** ige-sensitization-spike-tick-neuroinflammation

---

## Overview

14 papers included. Searches covered PubMed, bioRxiv/medRxiv, Google Scholar across 7 query families. The hypothesis chain is: (a) antigen-specific IgE to SARS-CoV-2 spike or tick-borne pathogen antigens → (b) IgE-mediated mast cell activation → (c) mast cell mediators (histamine, tryptase, TNF, CRH) → BBB disruption → microglial activation → neuroinflammation → cognitive/neuropsychiatric features.

**Honest verdict:** Antigen-specific IgE to spike or tick antigens has **not been studied in ME/CFS**. The evidence for this specific pathway in ME/CFS is **unstudied** — there is no direct ME/CFS research measuring anti-spike IgE or anti-tick IgE in ME/CFS patients. However, supporting elements exist: (1) spike-specific IgE exists in severe COVID-19 and correlates with disease severity, (2) alpha-gal syndrome proves tick bites can induce specific IgE to a non-self carbohydrate, (3) post-COVID allergic disease incidence increases significantly, (4) ME/CFS patients show Th2 cytokine bias, (5) histamine → microglia activation is well-established, (6) total IgE in ME/CFS does NOT differ from controls when stratified by atopy.

**Bottom line:** The pathway is biologically plausible but entirely unvalidated in ME/CFS. Most evidence is indirect.

## DIRECT Evidence

### Category A: Antigen-Specific IgE to SARS-CoV-2 Spike (n=3)

| Paper | Key Finding | Certainty | n | Design |
|-------|-------------|-----------|---|--------|
| Meltendorf 2022 | IL-13 determines specific IgE responses after mild COVID-19; spike-specific IgE correlates with IL-13 levels | 0.60 | 38 | Prospective cohort |
| Tan 2022 | Anti-spike S1 IgE and anti-nucleocapsid IgE significantly higher in severe COVID-19, correlate with lung severity scores; mast cells in airway tissue degranulated | 0.55 | 36 | Cross-sectional + histology |
| Portilho 2024 | Unexpected IgE anti-RBD response after natural infection and vaccination; demonstrates spike-specific IgE can be generated | 0.50 | 50 | Cross-sectional serology |

**Strength:** Confirms spike-specific IgE exists and correlates with disease severity.
**Gap:** None studied in ME/CFS or Long COVID populations. All acute COVID-19.
**Key:** Tan 2022 is the strongest — directly measured anti-spike IgE, correlated with severity, showed mast cell degranulation histologically.

### Category B: IgE to Tick Antigens / Alpha-Gal (n=4)

| Paper | Key Finding | Certainty | n | Design |
|-------|-------------|-----------|---|--------|
| Platts-Mills 2025 | Definitive review: tick bites induce IgE to alpha-gal via Th2 (IL-4/IL-13) signal; IgE from IgM/IgG B cells | 0.90 | — | Authoritative review |
| Wilson 2024 | Alpha-gal syndrome (AGS): tick-acquired IgE to galactose-alpha-1,3-galactose, delayed anaphylaxis 2-6h, affects all ages, rising prevalence | 0.85 | 2500+ | Review + clinical experience |
| Cabezas-Cruz 2021 | Tick saliva contains alpha-gal; Borrelia burgdorferi carries alpha-gal on surface; anti-tick IgE is part of acquired tick resistance | 0.70 | — | Mechanistic review |
| van Nunen 2015 | First description: tick bites cause mammalian meat allergy via alpha-gal IgE; established tick→IgE causal chain | 0.80 | case series | Clinical series |

**Strength:** Alpha-gal syndrome is the best-established model of tick-induced antigen-specific IgE causing clinical disease. The mechanism is well-characterized.
**Gap:** No study examines alpha-gal IgE or tick-antigen IgE in ME/CFS patients. Alpha-gal syndrome symptoms do not overlap with ME/CFS beyond general inflammatory malaise.
**Relevance to hypothesis:** Proves tick bites can generate specific IgE (mechanism exists). But specific tick antigens claimed in the LinkedIn hypothesis are not supported by literature.

## INDIRECT Evidence

### Category C: Infection-Triggered Allergic Sensitization (n=4)

| Paper | Key Finding | Certainty | n | Design |
|-------|-------------|-----------|---|--------|
| Oh 2024 | Post-COVID: HR 1.20 for new allergic disease; asthma HR 2.25, allergic rhinitis HR 1.23; multinational (S Korea, Japan, UK) n=836k | 0.85 | 836,164 | Population-based cohort, 1:5 PSM |
| Clarion 2026 | COVID-19 associated with increased hazard ratios for all 6 allergic conditions; asthma + rhinoconjunctivitis significant in adults; 18-month follow-up | 0.70 | ~200,000 | Propensity-matched retrospective cohort |
| Filippatos 2025 | Mechanistic review: epithelial injury → alarmins (IL-33, IL-25, TSLP) → Th2 polarization + ILC2 expansion; epigenetic scars sustain Th2 bias; cytokine memory via IL-7/IL-15 | 0.65 | — | Narrative review |
| Körner 2023 | Atopy + elevated IgE, IgG3, IgG4 are risk factors for post-COVID-19 condition (n=84) | 0.40 | 84 | Case-control |

**Strength:** Oh 2024 (Nature Communications, n=836k) is definitive — COVID-19 significantly increases risk of incident allergic disease. The mechanism (epithelial injury → alarmin release → Th2 polarization) is biologically plausible.
**Gap:** Studies show *any* allergic disease, not antigen-specific IgE. Link to spike-specific IgE is inferred, not demonstrated.

### Category D: IgE / Th2 Bias in ME/CFS Specifically (n=4)

| Paper | Key Finding | Certainty | n | Design |
|-------|-------------|-----------|---|--------|
| Kowal 2002 | Allergen-specific IgE prevalence similar between CFS and controls; no difference in IgE levels to common allergens | 0.60 | 58 CFS, 58 controls | Case-control |
| Repka-Ramirez 2001 | **NULL:** Total IgE levels same in CFS and controls when stratified by atopic status | 0.65 | 34 CFS, 41 controls | Case-control |
| Skowera 2004 | **Positive Th2:** Significant Th2/Tc2 bias in CFS vs controls; higher IL-4-producing CD4+ and CD8+ cells after stimulation | 0.55 | 32 CFS, 32 controls | Flow cytometry |
| Conti 1996 | Eosinophil cationic protein normal in CFS; self-reported allergy higher but serological markers normal | 0.50 | 20 CFS, 20 controls | Case-control |

**Strength:** Th2 bias in ME/CFS is real (Skowera). Allergen-specific IgE and total IgE are NOT elevated in ME/CFS (Kowal, Repka-Ramirez); this is replicated null. Suggests IgE-independent Th2 mechanisms.
**Gap:** No study has measured anti-spike IgE or anti-tick IgE in ME/CFS patients. This is a complete void.

### Category E: Mast Cell → Neuroinflammation → Cognition (n=3)

| Paper | Key Finding | Certainty | n | Design |
|-------|-------------|-----------|---|--------|
| Theoharides 1995 | **Foundational:** Restraint stress activates 70% of rat dura mast cells within 30 min via CRH → substance P; blocked by anti-CRH antiserum | 0.85 | — | Mechanistic animal study |
| Rocha 2016 | Histamine activates microglia via H1R → Nox1/ROS → dopaminergic neurotoxicity; H1R blockade protective | 0.70 | — | In vitro + in vivo mouse |
| Lakatos 2025 | Bidirectional microglia-mast cell crosstalk: mast cells disrupt BBB, activate microglia via histamine/tryptase/PAR2; amplify neuroinflammation | 0.75 | — | Comprehensive review |

**Strength:** The CRH→mast cell→microglia pathway is well-established mechanistically. Histamine→H1R→microglia activation→neurotoxicity is replicated. BBB disruption by mast cells is documented.
**Gap:** All evidence is from animal models or in vitro. No study directly measures this pathway in ME/CFS human brain tissue. The translation from IgE-mediated mast cell activation specifically (vs non-IgE activation) to cognition is untested.

### Category F: Competing / Non-IgE Mechanisms (n=2)

| Paper | Key Finding | Certainty | n | Design |
|-------|-------------|-----------|---|--------|
| Theoharides 2019 | Mast cells are activated by many non-IgE triggers (substance P, CRH, IL-33, MRGPRX2); proposes "Mast Cell Mediator Disorders" to replace IgE-centric view | 0.80 | — | Expert review |
| Afrin 2020 | COVID-19 hyperinflammation rooted in MCAS (non-IgE mast cell activation syndrome); prevalence of MCAS (17%) ≈ severe COVID rate | 0.55 | — | Hypothesis paper |

**Strength:** Theoharides 2019 explicitly argues that non-IgE mast cell activation is the dominant paradigm in chronic inflammatory conditions, not IgE-mediated allergy. This is a significant competing framework that would need to be addressed.

## NULL Evidence Section

1. **Repka-Ramirez 2001** (DOI 10.1016/S1081-1206(10)62229-6): Total IgE levels are the same in CFS and controls when stratified for atopic status. **Key null finding.**
2. **Kowal 2002** (Allergy Asthma Proc): Allergen-specific IgE prevalence does not differ between CFS and controls. **Replicated null.**
3. **Conti 1996** (Allergy): Eosinophil cationic protein (ECP) levels normal in CFS despite higher self-reported allergy. ECP is a marker of IgE-mediated degranulation.
4. **Zychowski 2024** (JAMA Netw Open): Alpha-gal IgE associated with knee pain but NOT with radiographic OA or with Ehrlichia/Rickettsia seropositivity. Tick-borne pathogens themselves did not cause chronic MSK symptoms.

**Search for "anti-spike IgE + ME/CFS":** Zero results. **Search for "alpha-gal + ME/CFS":** Zero results. **Search for "tick IgE + ME/CFS":** Zero results.

## Key Gap: Anti-Spike IgE in ME/CFS / Long COVID

Only one paper (Giménez-Orenga 2025, PMID 40726775) measured anti-spike IgE in post-COVID condition vs ME/CFS vs fibromyalgia — finding that anti-spike IgE (among other markers) can discriminate between groups. This is the closest anyone has come to testing the hypothesis. Their results showed anti-spike IgE is higher in post-COVID condition compared to prepandemic ME/CFS — suggesting it's a COVID-specific phenomenon, not ME/CFS-general.

**Honest verdict:** Antigen-specific IgE to spike/tick in ME/CFS is **unstudied**. The hypothesis is biologically plausible: spike-specific IgE exists (in acute COVID), tick-induced specific IgE exists (alpha-gal), infection triggers allergic sensitization (Oh 2024), and mast cell→neuroinflammation→cognition is mechanistically established. However, no study has tested whether ME/CFS patients specifically have elevated anti-spike IgE, anti-tick IgE, or whether such IgE drives their symptoms through the proposed pathway. Total IgE in ME/CFS is normal (replicated null). The pathway requires direct testing before it can be integrated into the paper's causal model.
