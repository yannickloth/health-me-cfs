## Key Findings by Topic

### 1. DNA Methylation / Epigenetics in ME/CFS (Prior EWAS)

**Established landscape:**
- deVega 2021 (already in bib): meta-analysis of blood EWAS reveals cellular alterations; immune and metabolic pathway enrichment.
- deVega 2014 (already in bib): first genome-wide methylation study; glucocorticoid receptor pathway hypomethylation.
- Helliwell 2020 (new): RRBS n=10 — 76 differentially methylated fragments; neurological pathway enrichment; 59% gene overlap with prior studies.
- Przybylowicz 2023 (new): re-analysis of three prior datasets (n=127 CFS, n=22 FM); site-level reproducibility low but gene-enrichment patterns convergent; changes predominantly in regulatory regions.

**Convergent signal across studies:** Immune, metabolic, and neurological pathway dysregulation consistently emerges across independent EWAS studies despite low site-level overlap. This is expected given differences in tissue (blood vs saliva), array platform, and diagnostic criteria.

**Gap:** No prior EWAS study used saliva DNA or the Illumina EPIC (~850k CpG) platform in ME/CFS before Chalder 2026. No prior study identified PTPRN2 specifically as a replicated ME/CFS locus.

### 2. PTPRN2 Gene Biology

**Stojilkovic 2025 review (new):**
- PTPRN2 (IA-2beta/phogrin) is a neuroendocrine pseudophosphatase expressed in hypothalamus, pituitary, hippocampus, pancreatic islets.
- Required for normal accumulation of norepinephrine, dopamine, and serotonin in the brain.
- Required for secretory vesicle maintenance in hippocampus and pituitary.
- Female-specific: deletion impairs kisspeptin neuron development → impaired ovulation.
- Suppresses POMC/melanotroph proliferation in intermediate pituitary lobe.
- Lipid phosphatase activity (PI(3)P, PI(4,5)P2): possible role in insulin secretion regulation.

**Choza 2024 preprint (new):**
- PTPRN2 shows disease-associated epigenetic shifts (5mC ↔ 5hmC) in Parkinson's disease postmortem brain.
- Independent evidence PTPRN2 is epigenetically dysregulated in neurological disease.

**Interpretation for ME/CFS:** PTPRN2 hypomethylation (Chalder 2026) would potentially alter expression in neurons relevant to monoamine signalling, hippocampal vesicle trafficking, and autonomic/neuroendocrine regulation — all pathways implicated in ME/CFS pathophysiology. This is biologically plausible but causality is not established.

### 3. miR-153-3p and Neurological/Cognitive Function

**Three independent lines of evidence for miR-153-3p in cognitive function:**

- **Stabile 2024** (new, mice): elevated miR-153-3p in hippocampus of cognitively impaired middle-aged mice; intra-hippocampal mimic injection sufficient to cause memory deficit. Causal in mice.
- **Lahiri 2025** (new, human AD): elevation of miR-153-3p associated with *reduced* AD probability; reduces APP, SNCA, REST expression → neuroprotective at normal/high levels.
- **Li 2022** (new, stroke model): miR-153-3p downregulated after ischemia/reperfusion; restoration improves cognitive deficits via SRC/MAPK/NLRP3 axis.

**Reconciliation of apparently contradictory findings:**
- Stabile 2024 shows elevated miR-153-3p causes memory deficit (in mice, possibly by disrupting synaptic plasticity via specific targets).
- Lahiri 2025 and Li 2022 show that reduced/absent miR-153-3p is also harmful (loss of neuroprotection against amyloid/alpha-synuclein/NLRP3).
- These are not contradictory: miR-153-3p likely has a non-linear relationship with cognitive function, and different target genes may dominate in different contexts (aging mice vs AD vs ischemic stroke vs ME/CFS).
- In ME/CFS (Chalder 2026): reduced circulating miR-153-3p correlates with worse delayed memory recognition — consistent with the loss-of-neuroprotection interpretation.

**Moreau group miRNA work (prior art):**
- Nepotchatykh 2020: 11 circulating miRNAs cluster ME/CFS patients by symptom severity (Moreau group).
- Nepotchatykh 2023: miRNA signatures discriminate ME/CFS from fibromyalgia (Moreau group).
- Chalder 2026 extends this work to add PTPRN2 methylation and explicitly links miR-153-3p to cognitive symptoms.

### 4. PHB2 (Prohibitin 2) and miRNA Maturation

**Qi 2023 review (new):**
- PHB2 is pleiotropic: inner mitochondrial membrane mitophagy receptor, nuclear HDAC recruiter, plasma membrane signalling partner.
- **miRNA-relevant finding:** Lnc34a recruits PHB2/DNMT3A/HDAC1 to epigenetically silence the miR-34a promoter (methylation + deacetylation). This is documented in colorectal and hepatocellular cancers.
- Cytoplasmic localization established in multiple cancer cell types.

**Mechanistic plausibility assessment:**
- Known: PHB2 can participate in epigenetic silencing of miRNA promoters (miR-34a precedent).
- Proposed (Chalder 2026): PHB2 impairs miR-153-3p *maturation* post-transcriptionally in the cytoplasm.
- Gap: miR-34a silencing is a nuclear/promoter mechanism (DNA methylation + HDAC). A cytoplasmic post-transcriptional mechanism for PHB2 on miRNA maturation (DROSHA/DICER processing) is not established in the literature. No published study directly links PHB2 to miRNA processing machinery.
- Assessment: The PHB2-miR-153-3p axis is the weakest mechanistic link in the Chalder 2026 paper. The evidence is consistent with PHB2 having roles near miRNA regulation, but the specific cytoplasmic maturation mechanism is a hypothesis without direct experimental demonstration.

### 5. Epigenetic Subtyping / Patient Stratification in ME/CFS

- Chalder 2026: PTPRN2 hypomethylation enables patient subgrouping by epigenetic profile (specific subgroups not yet fully characterised).
- Pshezhetskiy 2025 (new): EpiSwitch 3D chromatin conformation identifies 200 genomic biomarkers; 92%/98% sensitivity/specificity; convergent evidence for epigenetic-based stratification from a completely different epigenetic modality.
- deVega 2021 (in bib): glucocorticoid sensitivity subgroups correlated with methylation differences.
- Nepotchatykh 2020 (new): four miRNA-based symptom-severity clusters in ME/CFS (same Moreau group).

**Convergence:** Multiple independent teams using different epigenetic approaches (CpG methylation in blood, 3D chromatin conformation, circulating miRNA expression) all support the existence of biologically distinct patient subgroups. This is a growing area of consensus.

### 6. Sex Differences in ME/CFS Epigenetics/miRNA

- Cheema 2020 (new): Males show significantly more pronounced miRNA dysregulation than females during exercise challenge in ME/CFS (36 DEGs in males vs 0 in females at peak exercise, FDR<0.1).
- Chalder 2026: respiratory symptom association with PTPRN2 hypomethylation found in males only (exploratory).
- Stojilkovic 2025: PTPRN2 deletion causes female-specific reproductive dysfunction (kisspeptin/ovulation), suggesting sex-differential PTPRN2 biology.

**Gap:** Sex-stratified EWAS in ME/CFS is severely underpowered across all existing studies. No study has sufficient n to draw firm conclusions about sex-differential CpG methylation patterns specifically in ME/CFS.

## Literature Gaps

1. **No replication of PTPRN2 finding**: Chalder 2026 is the only study linking PTPRN2 hypomethylation to ME/CFS. Single study, small n.
2. **No brain or neural tissue methylation data in ME/CFS**: All EWAS studies use blood or saliva. Whether PTPRN2 hypomethylation occurs in neurons is unknown.
3. **PHB2-miR-153-3p mechanism undemonstrated**: No published study directly shows PHB2 impairs miR-153-3p maturation. The Lnc34a-PHB2-miR-34a precedent is in cancer cells, not neurons.
4. **miR-153-3p in ME/CFS not replicated**: Only Chalder 2026 links reduced circulating miR-153-3p to ME/CFS cognitive symptoms.
5. **Sex-stratified EWAS underpowered**: No adequately powered sex-stratified methylation study in ME/CFS exists.
6. **Saliva-brain correlation for methylation**: Saliva methylation is a proxy; correlation with brain-tissue methylation for specific neuroendocrine loci (including PTPRN2) is not validated.
7. **Functional consequence of PTPRN2 hypomethylation**: Does it change PTPRN2 mRNA or protein expression in ME/CFS patients? Not yet measured.
