# Literature: Autoantibodies in Schizophrenia — Parallels to ME/CFS Autoantibody Research

## Session: 2026-06-06 | research_stream: schizophrenia-autoantibodies

---

## Executive Summary

This search targeted literature on autoantibodies in schizophrenia/psychosis as a
comparative model for ME/CFS autoantibody research. Parallels span: (1) diagnostic
heterogeneity obscuring treatable subgroups, (2) transition from targeted to unbiased
autoantibody screening, (3) immunotherapy responses in biomarker-defined subgroups,
(4) GPCR autoantibodies as shared mechanism, and (5) limited sensitivity of
conventional assays missing low-titer pathologically relevant autoantibodies.

## Key Mechanistic Insights

### 1. Diagnostic Heterogeneity Masks Autoantibody Subgroups

Both schizophrenia and ME/CFS suffer from syndromic classification that pools
patients with diverse pathophysiology. In schizophrenia, the Kraepelin/Bleuler
"group of schizophrenias" concept (Bartley & Ross 2020) parallels the ongoing
debate about ME/CFS subgroups. The autoantibody-positive subgroup in schizophrenia
was invisible until Dalmau's discovery of anti-NMDAR encephalitis (2007/2008)
demonstrated that a subset of "schizophrenia" was actually autoimmune encephalitis.

**Key data:** Luykx 2024 showed 3.7% of 1114 schizophrenia patients are NMDAR-Ab
positive. These patients had LESS severe negative symptoms — they look clinically
different but not necessarily "worse". This directly parallels ME/CFS where GPCR-AAB
positive subgroups may have distinct clinical profiles (Freitag 2021, Azcue 2026).

### 2. The IgG Subclass Problem — Specificity vs Sensitivity

A critical methodological parallel: In schizophrenia, the vast majority of NMDAR
antibodies detected are IgA/IgM; only a small fraction (0.5-1.5%) are IgG (Pollak
2014, Schou 2016). IgG subclass is the established pathogenic driver. ME/CFS GPCR
autoantibody research faces the same challenge — distinguishing pathogenic IgG from
natural/non-pathogenic autoantibodies using functional assays (Hartwig 2020).

### 3. Unbiased Screening Transforms Discovery

The Nemani 2026 preprint using REAP (Rapid Extracellular Antigen Profiling) —
a proteome-scale phage display platform — found that schizophrenia patients have
nearly 2× the extracellular autoantibody burden of controls. This mirrors the
Pluvinage 2024 REAP discovery of anti-CD320 in neurologic disease. The transition
from targeted (assay-based) to unbiased (proteome-wide) screening is revolutionizing
both fields.

**ME/CFS implication:** GPCR autoantibodies in ME/CFS have been identified via
targeted ELISA (Freitag 2021, Azcue 2026). Unbiased phage display screening in
ME/CFS could reveal novel autoantibody targets beyond the GPCR panel.

### 4. Immunotherapy Responses in Subgroup-Selected Patients

In schizophrenia, immunotherapy (glucocorticoids, plasma exchange, rituximab) has
shown benefit primarily in case series of autoantibody-positive patients (Endres
2020, Hansen 2023). The only RCT (methotrexate, Chaudhry 2020) showed modest
positive symptom improvement but no biomarker-based patient selection.

In ME/CFS, immunoadsorption shows benefit in β2-AdR autoantibody-positive patients
(Stein 2023). Both fields now recognize that RCTs in unselected patients are likely
to fail — subgroup selection is essential.

**Parallel:** Plasmapheresis trials in schizophrenia in the 1970s-80s (Cox 2020)
were negative — done without autoantibody-based patient selection. Same pattern as
early ME/CFS IVIG/apheresis trials.

### 5. GPCR Autoantibodies as Shared Disease Mechanism

ME/CFS GPCR autoantibodies target: β2-AdR, M1/M3/M4 muscarinic receptors, AT1-R,
ETA/B-R (Freitag 2021, Azcue 2026). These are G-protein-coupled receptors
regulating autonomic, vascular, and immune function.

Schizophrenia autoantibodies target: NMDAR (glutamate ion channel), VGKC, GAD65,
dopamine receptors (indirect targets), and SFT2D2 (Liu 2025). These are primarily
neuronal targets.

**Overlap mechanism:** Both conditions feature functional autoantibodies that
modulate receptor signaling rather than causing frank destruction. Hartwig 2020
showed ME/CFS IgG fails to activate β2-AdR — a functional deficit rather than
cytotoxic effect. Dalmau 2008 showed NMDAR antibodies reduce receptor cluster
density — a modulatory rather than lytic mechanism.

### 6. Cross-Disease Autoantibody Convergence

The Cullen 2019 meta-analysis found elevated risk of psychosis across numerous
autoimmune disorders (OR 1.26), with strongest associations for pernicious anemia
(OR 1.91) and pemphigoid (OR 1.90). This suggests convergent autoantibody
mechanisms across diagnostically distinct conditions.

The rare-to-common paradigm (Casanova & Abel 2022): Rare Mendelian autoimmune
encephalitis → common autoantibody subgroups in idiopathic psychosis. Same pattern
expected in ME/CFS: rare autoimmune conditions → ME/CFS autoantibody subgroups.

## Null / Negative / Contradicting Results

1. **Plasmapheresis/dialysis ineffective in unselected schizophrenia** (Cox 2020):
   Historical trials negative. BUT they did not select for autoantibody-positive
   patients — exactly the same limitation as early ME/CFS apheresis trials.

2. **Luykx 2024 — "Better" functioning paradox**: NMDAR-seropositive schizophrenia
   patients had LESS severe negative symptoms and BETTER psychosocial functioning.
   This contradicts the assumption that autoantibodies cause more severe disease.
   Possible explanations: (a) different illness subtype, (b) IgA/IgM may have
   protective effects, (c) selection bias toward less severe cases.

3. **Schou 2016 — No diagnostic specificity**: Anti-neuronal autoantibodies were
   equally prevalent in psychosis and non-psychosis acute psychiatric admissions.
   This suggests autoantibodies may be general risk factors rather than specific
   to psychosis.

4. **Grain 2017 — High heterogeneity**: GAD65 meta-analysis showed I²=91%.
   Few studies controlled for type 1 diabetes, potentially inflating estimates.

5. **Ezeoke 2013 — Inconsistent clinical correlations**: Despite 20 autoantibodies
   elevated, correlations with clinical features were inconsistent across studies.

6. **Azcue 2026 — ME/CFS had lower M2 and M4 AABs vs controls**: Not all
   autoantibodies are elevated; some are decreased, suggesting dysregulation
   rather than simple overproduction.

## Gap Analysis for ME/CFS

| Gap | Schizophrenia Evidence | ME/CFS Status |
|-----|----------------------|---------------|
| Proteome-wide unbiased screening | Nemani 2026 (REAP) — 352 SCZ, 971 controls — novel extracellular autoantibodies discovered | Not done in ME/CFS. GPCR AABs found via targeted ELISA only. |
| Subgroup-selected immunotherapy RCT | Methotrexate in unselected patients (Chaudhry 2020) — modest effect. No RCT in AAB-selected patients completed. | β2-AdR-selected immunoadsorption pilot (Stein 2023). No RCT completed. Rituximab trials in ME/CFS were negative (unselected). |
| Passive transfer proof of pathogenicity | Dalmau 2008: NMDAR-Ab → reduced cluster density in vitro. Not systematically tested for novel AABs. | Goebel 2021 (fibromyalgia), Mignolet 2026, Chen 2026 (long COVID) — passive transfer models exist. Not done for ME/CFS GPCR AABs. |
| Longitudinal autoantibody trajectories | Pearlman 2014: NR2A/NR2B titers declined 58% over 8 weeks in first-episode; 13% over 4 days in acute mania. | No longitudinal GPCR autoantibody studies in ME/CFS. |
| Standardized assay methodology | Major source of heterogeneity (Pollak 2014, Pearlman 2014). Cell-based vs ELISA vs tissue-based. | GPCR ELISA used (CellTrend) — no standardized comparison with functional bioassays. |
| Functional characterization of AABs | Dalmau 2008: mechanism of NMDAR internalization. Hartwig 2020 analogous for β2-AdR. | Functional effects of GPCR AABs partially characterized (cAMP, β-arrestin). Not linked to specific symptoms in passive transfer models. |
| Diabetes comorbidity adjustment | Grain 2017: GAD65 inflated without T1D adjustment. | Not systematically addressed for ME/CFS AAB studies. |
| Clinical diagnostic criteria for autoimmune subtype | Tebartz van Elst 2025: Neuropsychiatric Checklist for Autoimmune Psychosis. | No equivalent for autoimmune ME/CFS. |

## Clinical Implications for ME/CFS

1. **Subgroup enrichment is essential** — Schizophrenia autoantibody research
   demonstrates that unselected trials fail. ME/CFS immunotherapy trials (rituximab,
   immunoadsorption) must use autoantibody-based patient selection.

2. **Unbiased screening likely to find novel targets** — The REAP platform (Nemani
   2026, Pluvinage 2024) should be applied to ME/CFS. GPCR-focused assays may miss
   the full autoantibody repertoire.

3. **Functional assays > binding assays** — IgG subclass specificity and functional
   effects (receptor activation vs blockade vs internalization) must be prioritized
   over simple ELISA titer measurement.

4. **Temporal dynamics matter** — Autoantibody titers fluctuate (Pearlman 2014).
   Cross-sectional measurement may misclassify patients. Longitudinal sampling is
   needed.

5. **BBB disruption may be upstream** — Nemani 2026 found autoantibodies against
   BBB antigens in schizophrenia. Same mechanism may operate in ME/CFS (BBB
   integrity known to be impaired in subsets).
