## Inclusion criteria

1. English-language peer-reviewed journal article or preprint with DOI
2. Addresses host-cell glycolytic/metabolic reprogramming by Borrelia, Bartonella, or Babesia
3. OR addresses immunometabolism of tick-borne infection
4. OR addresses downstream mechanism (HIF-1α, LDHA, lactate, PDK/PDH, trained immunity) in context of tick-borne pathogens
5. OR provides metabolomic evidence of metabolic dysregulation in PTLDS
6. Indirect-link searches: connections from tick-borne mechanisms to ME/CFS glycolytic/immunometabolic literature

## Exclusion criteria

1. Tick taxonomy, ecology, or epidemiology only (no host-pathogen metabolism)
2. Tick-borne pathogens other than Borrelia, Bartonella, Babesia (e.g., Rickettsia, Ehrlichia, Anaplasma, TBEV — out of scope per task)
3. Purely clinical treatment trials without mechanistic data
4. Case reports without metabolic data
5. Antibiotic pharmacology only (no host metabolism)
6. Review/preprints without original data (except Kempf2005 — landmark study)
7. PTLDS "PTLD" (post-transplant lymphoproliferative disorder) — PTLDS retrieval error filter

## Indirect-link search logic

### Chain: Borrelia → trained immunity → glycolysis → ME/CFS

1. Barriales2021: Bb induces trained immunity with augmented glycolysis in macrophages [in vitro + mouse]
2. Bernard2020: Bb induces cell-type-specific trained immunity in synoviocytes [mouse]
3. Kerstholt2022: Bb alters monocyte glucose metabolism & lactate [human in vitro]
4. Humer2025 (already in bib): advocates trained immunity in ME/CFS

**⇒ Searched** "trained immunity AND (ME/CFS OR CFS)" → 1 result (Humer2025, already in bib)
**⇒ Logic link:** Borrelia → trained immunity (glycolysis-dependent) → ME/CFS (Humer hypothesis). Evidence chain EXISTS but is speculative — no direct demonstration that Borrelia-induced trained immunity causes ME/CFS.

### Chain: Bartonella → HIF-1α → cellular hypoxia → decreased ATP → ME/CFS

1. Kempf2005: B. henselae stabilizes HIF-1α in host cells → VEGF → cellular hypoxia → ↓ATP
2. HIF-1α stabilization is established mechanism driving glycolytic shift

**⇒ Searched** "HIF-1alpha AND chronic fatigue syndrome" → 0 results
**⇒ Searched** "HIF-1alpha AND post-infectious AND fatigue" → 0 results
**⇒ Logic link:** No direct evidence linking Bartonella-driven HIF-1α to ME/CFS. Plausible but uninvestigated.

### Chain: Babesia → erythrocyte glucose uptake → hemolysis → metabolic stress → ME/CFS

1. Ohmori2004: Babesia-infected RBCs increase glucose uptake (new uptake system)
2. Moezzi2025 (already in bib, p=59): haptoglobin depletion in ME/CFS links to hemolysis

**⇒ Searched** "Babesia" AND "hemolysis" AND (oxidative stress OR heme)" → 6 results, none in ME/CFS
**⇒ Logic link:** Babesia → infected RBC glucose consumption ↑ → hemolysis → free heme → oxidative stress. Haptoglobin link to ME/CFS already in bib. Direct glycolytic connection not studied.
