# ADHD-ME/CFS Shared Causation Integration Plan

**Purpose:** Integrate evidence that ADHD and ME/CFS share the same causal mechanisms — mitochondrial energy failure, neuroinflammation, catecholamine dysregulation, and metabolic reserve deficit — as different phenotypic expressions of a common root pathology.

**Target chapters:**
- ch14d-cross-disease (shared causal mechanism framing)
- ch14a-core-mechanistic (strengthen existing Architecture C with same-cause language)
- ch17-lifestyle-interventions (ADHD-specific pacing content)
- hypothesis-registry (new entries for same-cause hypotheses)

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- Shared mitochondrial etiology: ADHD and ME/CFS both show impaired OXPHOS, reduced spare respiratory capacity, and mtDNA haplogroup effects (existing evidence)
- Shared neuroinflammatory signature: microglial activation + dopaminergic deficit co-localized in ADHD (Yokokura 2021); same pattern in ME/CFS (Walitt 2024)
- Shared BH4 bottleneck: GCH1 variants → reduced BH4 → impaired catecholamine synthesis → both ADHD and ME/CFS phenotypes
- Shared cerebral hypoperfusion: Both conditions show prefrontal-striatal CBF deficit (Berthier 2025 for ADHD; van Campen 2020 for ME/CFS)
- Shared dopaminergic effort-invigoration deficit: tonic dopamine depletion produces effort aversion in both conditions (Salamone 2012 framework)
- Same-cause as different phenotypic expression: ADHD = compromised prefrontal energy allocation; ME/CFS = systemic energy allocation failure; both from the same mitochondrial-neuroinflammatory root
