## Pre-identified Hypotheses

- Metabolic adaptation hypothesis: ME/CFS involves a hypometabolic state where standard caloric targets overestimate need → weight gain is partly iatrogenic (Certainty: 0.35)
- Fat oxidation bypass hypothesis: impaired CPT/ACAD forces reliance on glucose; high-carb comfort eating is partly a physiological drive, not just behavioral (Certainty: 0.50)
- Anti-catabolic priority hypothesis: in bedbound ME/CFS, preventing muscle loss is more important than reducing fat mass — body composition > scale weight (Certainty: 0.60)
- Cross-condition caloric recalibration: SCI/ICU evidence on reduced REE applies to bedbound ME/CFS (Certainty: 0.45)
- GLP-1 pathway as weight management tool: low-dose GLP-1 RAs may address both appetite dysregulation and neuroinflammation in ME/CFS with obesity (Certainty: 0.40)
- Time-restricted feeding adaptation: 12:12 or 14:10 windows may improve metabolic markers without caloric restriction risk (Certainty: 0.30)
