# Integration Plan: Mast Cell IgE Pathway → Connective Tissue Degradation in hEDS/POTS

**Purpose:** Evaluate whether IgE-mediated mast cell dysfunction chronically degrades connective tissue in hEDS/POTS, and whether stabilization of the IgE pathway can improve connective tissue integrity and hypermobility severity — with implications for identifying and treating progressive hypermobility downstream of infections/allergies.

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- Mast cell mediators (tryptase, MMPs) degrade collagen/elastin in connective tissue → progressive hypermobility (certainty TBD)
- IgE pathway stabilization reduces mast cell degranulation → slows/arrests connective tissue degradation (certainty TBD)
- Infectious triggers (tickborne, COVID, mold) → newly acquired allergies → mast cell dysfunction → chronic mediator release → connective tissue breakdown cascade (certainty TBD)
- IgE pathway stabilization may partially reverse hEDS/POTS severity in subsets (certainty TBD, likely low)
