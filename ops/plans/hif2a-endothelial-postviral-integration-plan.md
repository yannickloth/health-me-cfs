# Integration Plan: HIF-2α-Mediated Endothelial Dysfunction as Post-Viral ME/CFS Mechanism

**Purpose:** Integrate the finding that SARS-CoV-2 spike protein drives sustained HIF-2α activation in endothelial cells (Ribeiro 2026), impairing barrier function with rescue by belzutifan, and explore whether this mechanism extends to ME/CFS beyond post-COVID — including EBV/HHV-6 triggers and overlap with the β2AdR autoantibody-negative ~70% of ME/CFS patients with documented endothelial dysfunction but no known autoantibody target.

**Parent topic:** hypoxia-altitude-hif1a (H1.2 — HIF-2 post-viral subtype, auto-escalated)

**Target chapters:** ch06 (metabolism — HIF cross-talk), ch10 (cardiovascular/endothelial — primary), ch12 (neurovascular — BBB), ch13 (integrative models), ch14d (autonomic), ch14k (vascular brainstorm), ch18 (emerging therapies — belzutifan expanded), hypothesis registry

## Pre-identified hypotheses
- HIF-2α sustained activation by SARS-CoV-2 spike provides a molecular mechanism for post-COVID endothelial dysfunction distinct from HIF-1α (transient vs sustained)
- Mechanism may extend to EBV/HHV-6 post-viral ME/CFS — viral dUTPases or other conserved viral proteins may similarly stabilize HIF-2α
- HIF-2α axis explains the β2AdR autoantibody-negative ~70% — non-autoantibody endothelial dysfunction mechanism
- Belzutifan rescue of endothelial barrier: testable intervention in post-viral ME/CFS
- HIF-2α ↔ complement cross-talk: HIF-2α-driven VEGF may amplify complement activation via endothelial activation
- HIF-2α ↔ endothelial senescence: sustained HIF-2α may drive or interact with the Nunes 2026 senescence model
