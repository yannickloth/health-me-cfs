# Integration Plan: Emotional Processing, Interoceptive Processing, and Illness Cognitions in ME/CFS

**Purpose:** Determine whether altered emotional recognition, alexithymia, interoceptive accuracy/sensibility dissociation, fatigue catastrophizing, and sickness behavior reflect downstream consequences of neuroimmune and autonomic dysfunction rather than upstream psychological causes.

**Target chapters:**
- ch15 (symptom-producing mechanisms) — interoceptive dysregulation section expansion, sickness behavior
- ch14d (cross-disease neuropsychiatric) — emotional/interoceptive phenotypes
- ch07 (immune dysfunction) — cytokine-induced sickness behavior pathway
- ch33 (controversies) — catastrophizing critique, GET/CBT policy tension
- ch08 (neurological) — autonomic-emotion interface, HRV

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- Interoceptive accuracy/sensibility dissociation in ME/CFS reflects corrupted afferent signals, not psychological misinterpretation
- Alexithymia in ME/CFS is acquired (neuroinflammation-driven), not trait-level
- Fatigue catastrophizing scales measure symptom burden, not maladaptive cognition
- Dantzer cytokine-induced sickness behavior model accounts for the full emotional/interoceptive phenotype
- HRV reductions mediate the autonomic-emotion processing interface
- Predictive coding frameworks (Seth, Barrett, Wager, Quadt/Critchley/Garfinkel, Greenhouse-Tucknott) explain interoceptive dysfunction as computational consequence of peripheral pathology

**Key tension:** NICE NG206 retired CBT/GET and fear-avoidance as ME/CFS treatment rationale; emotional/interoceptive symptoms are real but traverse biology, not psychology.
