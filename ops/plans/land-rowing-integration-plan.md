# Integration Plan: Land-Based Rowing (WaterRower)

**Date:** 2026-05-26
**Purpose:** Integrate land-based rowing (water-resistance rowing machine / WaterRower) as a distinct modality from water-immersed rowing. The paper's existing `@spec:water-rowing` (cert 0.30) covers in-water rowing with hydrostatic pressure benefit. Land rowing loses the hydrostatic component but keeps seated constant-position + compound movement distribution — making it a plausible Tier 2 modality for POTS+ME/CFS patients.

**Target chapters:**
- Part 3: Treatment — ch17-lifestyle-interventions (new section `subsec:land-rowing`, after water-rowing section)
- Part 4: Research — hypothesis-registry.typ (new entry)
- Appendix H: annotated bibliography (new entry for any papers found)

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- Land rowing (seated, constant position, compound movement) is a Tier 2 safe modality for POTS+ME/CFS (0.45 — Leeds POTS protocol precedent, no ME/CFS rowing trial)
- Land rowing differs from water rowing in losing hydrostatic benefit but gaining home accessibility
- WaterRower's water-resistance mechanism may produce smoother, more PEM-compatible loading vs. air flywheel ergometers
