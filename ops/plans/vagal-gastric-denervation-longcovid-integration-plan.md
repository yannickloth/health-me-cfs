# Integration Plan: Vagal Cholinergic Gastric Denervation in Long COVID

**Topic:** Structural (in vivo) evidence of selective cholinergic denervation of the gastric mucosa in Long-COVID-19, correlating with HRV parameters — an anatomical substrate for dysautonomia.

**Source paper:** Acanfora D, Nolano M, et al. "Vagal cholinergic denervation of the gastric mucosa in Long-COVID-19: in vivo evidence of structural autonomic dysfunction." Int J Infect Dis 2026. doi:10.1016/j.ijid.2026.108973. (Case–control, n=12 LC / 8 controls, gastric mucosal biopsy IHC PGP9.5 + VIP.)

**Purpose:** Evaluate whether structural vagal/cholinergic denervation evidence from Long COVID strengthens the ME/CFS autonomic-dysfunction and cholinergic-anti-inflammatory-pathway models, and whether it warrants integration.

## Pre-identified hypotheses (preliminary)

- Structural (not merely functional) vagal denervation may underlie ME/CFS dysautonomia (preliminary certainty ~0.35 — cross-disease/indirect for ME/CFS)
- Disrupted cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway sustains proinflammatory state (preliminary certainty ~0.40)
- Gastric mucosal nerve density as a candidate structural autonomic biomarker (preliminary certainty ~0.30)
