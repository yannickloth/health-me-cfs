# CPET Null Replication Integration Plan (Mancini/Natelson 2026)

**Date:** 2026-05-26
**Purpose:** Integrate Mancini/Natelson et al. 2026 null replication of 2-day CPET in ME/CFS, which found no significant Day1→Day2 decline in peak VO₂ or VO₂VT in 58 ME/CFS vs 25 sedentary controls. Directly challenges the evidence base for `@hyp:fitness-maintenance-distinction` (cert 0.60) in Ch17 and prior CPET literature in Ch14f.

**Hypothesis tree:** `ops/plans/hypotheses-trees/subtrees/cpet-null-replication.md` (to be created Phase 3a)

**Target chapters:**
- Part 2: Pathophysiology — ch14f-cpet-derived (add null-replication caveat, reconcile with Keller2024)
- Part 3: Treatment — ch17-lifestyle-interventions (revise `@hyp:fitness-maintenance-distinction`'s CPET evidence statement; add limitation)
- Part 1: Clinical — ch05-disease-course (if PEM measurement section references 2-day CPET as objective marker)
- Part 4: Research — ch20-biomarker-research or ch25b-proposed-studies (if 2-day CPET discussed as diagnostic tool)
- Appendix H: annotated bibliography (new entry for Mancini2026)

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- 2-day CPET may not reliably distinguish ME/CFS from sedentary controls (Mancini2026 null)
- 2-day CPET results may depend on patient selection criteria (Fukuda vs CCC), effort criteria enforcement, and/or control group matching
- Subjective symptom monitoring post-exercise may be more sensitive than CPET metrics for PEM detection
- Chronotropic incompetence (lower max HR) may be the more robust CPET finding in ME/CFS
