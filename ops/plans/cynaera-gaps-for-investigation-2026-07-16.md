## Priority 1 — T-cell Mitochondrial Exhaustion (medium-high, N=1 paper found)

**CYNAERA claim:** CD8+/CD4+ T cells in post-infectious chronic illness show mitochondrial fragmentation → impaired immune memory → failure to clear viral triggers → prevents sustained recovery. Cites: Selin 2023, Zong 2024. Measurement tools: Seahorse XF, MitoTracker, scRNA-seq.

**Our coverage:** PARTIAL — we have CD8+ dysfunction (exhaustion-like features, ch14d) and NK/PBMC mitochondrial impairment (ch6, ch7), but NOT T-cell–specific mitochondrial fragmentation as an immune-memory failure mechanism.

**Testable hypothesis:** ME/CFS patients with post-infectious onset show elevated CD8+ mitochondrial fragmentation (MitoTracker + confocal) vs gradual-onset ME/CFS. Fragmentation correlates with impaired viral-specific T-cell responses.

**Primary-start points:** Selin 2023 (UMass Chan), Zong 2024, Seahorse-based T-cell metabolic profiling literature in chronic viral infection.

**Target chapters:** ch7 (immune dysfunction), ch6 (energy metabolism — T-cell metabolic failure as extension of multi-tissue mitochondrial pathology)

## Priority 2 — Iron Redox Polarity: ME/CFS vs Long COVID Divergence (medium-high)

**CYNAERA claim:** Iron handling differences between ME/CFS and Long COVID create opposite treatment responses — treatments that work in one may harm the other. ME/CFS = functional iron deficiency (high ferritin, low transferrin saturation). Long COVID = hepcidin-independent dysregulation.

**Our coverage:** SIMILAR BUT DIFFERENT — we have the hepcidin-inflammation-iron axis (ch9: IL-6→STAT3→hepcidin→ferroportin→iron sequestration affecting thyroid, adrenal, dopamine). We do NOT frame iron handling as a fundamental diagnostic or therapeutic differentiation between ME/CFS and Long COVID.

**Testable hypothesis:** ME/CFS patients show elevated ferritin with low transferrin saturation (functional iron deficiency) vs Long COVID patients who show elevated serum iron with normal hepcidin. The two groups show opposite responses to iron supplementation and iron chelation.

**Primary-start points:** Iron metabolism in post-infectious chronic illness, hepcidin-independent iron dysregulation, comparative iron profiles in ME/CFS vs Long COVID cohorts.

**Target chapters:** ch9 (endocrine), ch10 (cardiovascular/oxygenation), ch14d (cross-disease — LC-vs-ME/CFS comparison)

## Priority 3 — Sequenced Protocol Staging: Which System First? (medium)

**CYNAERA claim:** 5-phase sequenced protocol: stabilize immune → reinforce mitochondrial → rewire autonomic → repair neuroplasticity → sustain remission. The staging matters because treating a destabilized system in the wrong order can cause harm (e.g., mitochondrial boosters before immune stabilization → flares).

**Our coverage:** SIMILAR BUT DIFFERENT — we have all the interventions (immune modulation, mitochondrial support, autonomic management, sleep/cognition, pacing), organized by severity, mechanism, and intervention type. We do NOT have a temporal staging framework — which system to address first given multi-system interdependence.

**Testable hypothesis:** A staged protocol (immune stabilization first, then mitochondrial support, then autonomic retraining) produces better 12-month outcomes than simultaneous intervention across all domains in severe ME/CFS.

**Primary-start points:** De novo synthesis from our own treatment chapters — the staging logic should be derivable from the causal hierarchy (ch16: amplifiers must stabilize before trigger-capable interventions become tolerable).

**Target chapters:** ch20 (urgent action severe), ch21 (mild-moderate), ch26 (integrative approaches) — or a new staging section

## Priority 5 — Primary Chronic Trigger (PCT) Framework (medium)

**Our coverage:** NOT FOUND — we discuss triggers qualitatively (ch5 onset patterns, ch7 viral persistence) but have no formal trigger-identification methodology.

**Testable:** Apply the PCT framework to known ME/CFS cohorts; compare trigger assignments with clinical history.

**Target chapters:** ch5 (disease course / onset patterns), ch7 (viral reactivation and persistence)
