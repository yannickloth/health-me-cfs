- ME/CFS is highly polygenic and heterogeneous — different combinations of risk variants drive disease in different patients (Certainty: 0.65)

- GLP-1 RA efficacy in ME/CFS will be subgroup-specific, mapping to autoimmune/inflammatory, cardiovascular, and energy metabolism strata (Certainty: 0.40)
- AI-driven combinatorial analytics can predict strong vs. weak GLP-1 RA responders (Certainty: 0.35)
- Low-dose initiation of GLP-1 RA is essential in ME/CFS due to heightened medication sensitivity (Certainty: 0.50)

### Mechanism Pathway Traces (Chain-by-Chain)

**Chain 1 — T Cell Exhaustion Druggable (length 4)**
```
CD80 suppression (EH, 0.50) → impaired T cell costimulation via surface CD80↓
  ── reinforced by ── DPP-4/CD26 protease (NH4, 0.25) → impaired T cell priming via CXCL12/CXCL10 cleavage
  ── reinforced by ── GLP-1 RA immunomodulation (NH3, 0.25) → NF-kB/MAPK → T cell cytokine modulation
  ── reinforced by ── Corticosteroid T cell exhaustion (EH, 0.45) → steroid-driven exhaustion exacerbation
```
All four nodes converge on the same claim: T cell functional impairment in ME/CFS is mediated by costimulatory defects (CD80, DPP-4) and worsened by steroids, potentially modifiable via GLP-1 RA.

**Chain 2 — Endothelial Dysfunction as Therapeutic Target (length 4)**
```
NH1 GLP-1 genetics (0.40) → GWAS enrichment for endothelial pathways → upstream genetic vulnerability
NH6 glycocalyx (0.25) → exercise-induced glycocalyx shedding → downstream structural failure
b2AR-Negative subtype (EH, 0.55) → HIF-2α-driven ED without autoantibodies
Belzutifan (EH, 0.45) → HIF-2α inhibition preserves barrier
  ── all anchored on ── State-Dependent ED (EH, 0.55) — endothelial cells fail under stress
```
Genetic (NH1), structural (NH6), and pharmacological (belzutifan) evidence streams independently converge on endothelial dysfunction as a core, druggable pathology.

**Chain 3 — NK Enhancement Strategy (length 4)**
```
Corticosteroid NK Suppression (EH, 0.55) → steroids reduce NK function
SLE-NK Mitochondrial Dysfunction (EH, 0.35) → mito defect impairs NK
IL-11→mTORC1→PIP2→TRPM3 (EH, 0.30) → channelopathy impairs NK calcium signalling
  ── all three identify upstream NK impairment mechanisms ──
NH7 GLP-1 RA NK enhance (0.35) + NH3 immunomodulation (0.30) → first proposed NK-enhancing intervention
```
Three upstream impairment mechanisms lack interventions; GLP-1 RA fills the gap as first NK-enhancing candidate.

**Chain 4 — PEM Generation Cascade (length 4+)**
```
Exercise → Glycocalyx shedding (NH6, 0.25) → microvascular leakage → tissue hypoxia
  → HIF-1α persistence (PEM Hibernation-Mimetic, EH, 0.55) → PDK1 → PDH suppression
  → Sustained lactate → Prolonged recovery

  ── additionally: State-Dependent ED (EH, 0.55) → endothelium fails under exertion → permissive for cascade
```
NH6 glycocalyx fills the endothelial gap between exertion trigger and HIF-1α metabolic consequences. Full 5-node causal cascade now mechanistically coherent.
