# Integration Plan: Narcolepsy Type 2 → ME/CFS Cross-Disease Metabolic Link

**Purpose:** Integrate narcolepsy Type 2 (NT2, without cataplexy) as a cross-disease parallel for ME/CFS — focusing on the shared orexin/hypocretin hypofunction, post-infectious trigger precedent (H1N1/Pandemrix), sleep-wake energy failure, and the "same metabolic root, different compartment" framework established for ADHD.

**Target chapters:**
- ch14d-cross-disease (primary — narcolepsy as cross-disease speculation/hypothesis)
- ch08-neurological (orexin neurobiology, sleep-wake architecture, unrefreshing sleep mechanisms)
- ch06-energy-metabolism (orexin neurons as energy-expensive; metabolic sensing)
- ch14e-2025-research or hypothesis registry (research directions)
- appendix-h-annotated-bibliography (annotated entries)

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- NT2 as orexin-compartmentalized energy failure sharing metabolic root with ME/CFS (speculation, cert ~0.25)
- Post-infectious orexin damage as shared trigger pathway: H1N1/Pandemrix → narcolepsy ∥ EBV/COVID → ME/CFS (speculation, cert ~0.30)
- Unrefreshing sleep in ME/CFS as subclinical orexinergic dysfunction (speculation, cert ~0.20)
- Orexin neurons as "canary" for mitochondrial stress due to high metabolic demand (speculation, cert ~0.25)
- ME/CFS hypersomnia phases as partial orexin failure episodes (speculation, cert ~0.20)
