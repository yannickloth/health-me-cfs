## Cross-Cutting Themes

These themes span multiple studies and should inform child cycle integration:

1. **IgG-mediated pathology is now experimentally validated.** Chen et al. (2026, Cell Reports Medicine) transfer passif reproduces symptoms in mice — a gold-standard demonstration. This changes the certainty floor for all autoantibody-related hypotheses.

2. **Fatigue dissociates from peripheral inflammation.** Omdal et al. (2026) shows severe fatigue (fVAS 63 vs 5) with zero peripheral biomarker differences. The mechanism must be CNS-confined or non-inflammatory.

3. **Mast cell-neuroimmune axis gaining systematic review evidence.** Theoharides 2026 review + Savigamin POTS-MCAS overlap + existing mcas-domain6 work converge on mast cells as a neuroimmune amplifier.

4. **Coagulation/thromboinflammation persists as mechanism.** Jones 2026 + existing complement (Science 2024) and Pretorius microclot work form a coherent vascular pillar.

5. **pTau-181 and neurodegenerative parallels emerging.** Yang 2026 pTau-181 in LC, Coleon/Pasteur brainstem persistence and dopaminergic dysregulation suggest neurodegenerative-like processes.

6. **ME/CFS under-diagnosis documented in general practice.** Tattan 2026 quantifies the diagnostic gap — data for the paper's clinical awareness sections.

7. **3-year PASC outcomes confirm chronicity.** Jones 3-year follow-up provides natural history data relevant to ME/CFS chronicity modeling.
