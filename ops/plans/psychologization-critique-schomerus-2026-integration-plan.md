**Purpose:** Integrate Schomerus et al. (2026) critical essay on harms of psychologizing Long COVID and ME/CFS — arguing for evidence-based, patient-oriented psychiatry that resists psychological attribution of post-infectious physical illness.

**Target chapters:**

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- Psychologization of ME/CFS causes measurable harm (activation therapy, invalidation, care neglect, medico-legal disadvantage, family harm) — certainty: 0.70
- Current standards for monitoring adverse effects in psychosocial intervention studies for ME/CFS are inadequate — certainty: 0.65
- Valid psychiatric differential diagnosis requires distinguishing somatic ME/CFS symptoms from psychiatric conditions — certainty: 0.60
