# Integration Plan: Fatigue Without Peripheral Inflammation (Omdal et al. 2026)

**Purpose:** Integrate Omdal et al. (2026, Brain Behav Immun Health) finding that severe Long COVID fatigue dissociates from peripheral inflammatory biomarkers into ME/CFS pathophysiology framework — forces fatigue mechanism rethink toward CNS-confined or non-inflammatory models.

**Source:** `/integrate-topic fatigue-no-inflammation-omdal-2026` (child of myboussole-fr-veille-2026)
**Paper:** Omdal R, et al. Persistent fatigue in long-COVID is not associated with peripheral inflammatory or cellular stress biomarkers: A cross-sectional controlled study. *Brain Behav Immun Health*. 2026;54:101226. PMID 42004496.
**Date:** 2026-06-01
**Topic slug:** fatigue-no-inflammation-omdal-2026

**Key findings:**
- n=96, 48 Long COVID (NICE criteria) vs 48 recovered controls, matched
- Fatigue: fVAS median 63 vs 5 (p<0.001), FACIT-F 21.5 vs 49 (p<0.001)
- Zero peripheral biomarker differences: CRP, TNF-α, IL-6, HSP90α, Serpin F1, hémopexine, APOA4 all normal
- ANA positive 10.4%, no correlation with fatigue severity
- Multivariate analysis: no significant biomarker-fatigue association
- Conclusion: Long COVID fatigue not sustained by systemic peripheral inflammation
