# Integration Plan: Extracellular Vesicles — Rydland 2026

**Purpose:** Integrate Rydland et al. (2026) proteomic study of extracellular vesicle (EV) protein cargo in ME/CFS plasma vs healthy controls — the largest EV study in ME/CFS to date (n=99 total), replicating previous EV count elevations and identifying 11 differentially abundant proteins (liver-derived upregulated; erythrocyte/B-cell-derived downregulated), though none survived multiple-comparison correction.

**Target chapters:**
- ch07 (immune dysregulation) — B-cell immunoglobulin light chain EV cargo; immune cell communication
- ch06 (mitochondria/energy metabolism) — hemoglobin proteins in EVs; potential RBC involvement
- ch20 (biomarkers) — EV count as replicated biomarker; EV protein cargo panel
- ch13 (integrative models) — EV as cellular communication mediator in ME/CFS pathophysiology

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- EV count elevation is a replicated finding in ME/CFS (Oltra, Castro-Marrero, Hanson, now Rydland)
- EV protein cargo may reveal cell-of-origin and pathological process
- Liver-derived EV proteins (ITIH3, AMBP, FGB) are upregulated — possible hepatic involvement
- B-cell/erythrocyte-derived EV proteins are downregulated — possible immune/bone marrow involvement
- None of the 11 differentially abundant proteins survived multiple-comparison correction
