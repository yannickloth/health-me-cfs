# Neuroimmune Blitshteyn 2026 Integration Plan

**Purpose:** Integrate Blitshteyn, Doherty, and Steinman (2026) review "Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome, Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome and Long COVID as Neuroimmune Disorders" — a review paper framing POTS, ME/CFS, and Long COVID as shared neuroimmune disorders, with emphasis on autoantibodies, immune dysregulation, autonomic nervous system involvement, and therapeutic implications.

**Paper DOI:** 10.2147/ITT.S581262
**Journal:** ImmunoTargets and Therapy, Volume 15 (2026)

**Target chapters:**
- ch08-neurological — autonomic dysfunction as neuroimmune pathophysiology
- ch10-cardiovascular — POTS neuroimmune mechanisms
- ch13-immunology — immune dysregulation, autoantibody mechanisms
- ch14d-cross-disease — shared neuroimmune features across POTS/ME/CFS/Long COVID
- ch25-translational-findings — neuroimmune biomarkers and clinical translation
- ch29-modeling — neuroimmune model integration
- ch30-modeling — neuroimmune mathematical model extensions
- Part 4 hypothesis registry — new neuroimmune hypotheses

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- Neuroimmune framing: POTS, ME/CFS, and Long COVID share autoimmune/neuroinflammatory mechanisms (certainty ~0.50)
- Autonomic ganglia as autoimmune targets in all three conditions
- GPCR autoantibodies as common mechanism across triad
- Neuroimmune amplification of autonomic dysfunction via nervous system signaling
- Therapeutic crossover: immunomodulatory treatments effective across conditions
