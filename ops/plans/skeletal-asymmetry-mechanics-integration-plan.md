# Integration Plan: Skeletal Asymmetry as Mechanical Driver of ME/CFS

**Purpose:** Evaluate and integrate the hypothesis that skeletal asymmetries (rotoscoliosis, DAMI — Des Axes Morphostatiques Insuffisants) act as a primary mechanical driver triggering cascading metabolic/autonomic/neurological dysfunction in a subset of ME/CFS patients. Hypothesis origin: Colette Marie Gerlier (independent researcher, BTS Biochimie, clinical experience in neurology/EEG).

**Target chapters:**
- `ch08-neurological` — nerve fiber compression/irritation at spinal level → dysautonomia
- `ch13-integrative-models` or `ch14-speculative-hypotheses` — skeletal asymmetry as distinct CTD-adjacent mechanism
- `ch05-subgroups` — mechanical/postural ME/CFS subgroup definition
- `ch06-energy-metabolism` — compensatory muscle overuse → metabolic cascade
- `ch15-symptom-producing-mechanisms` — allodynia, hypersensitivity from mechanical nerve irritation
- `ch10-cardiovascular` — structural autonomic dysfunction from thoracolumbar sympathetic chain irritation

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
1. Skeletal asymmetry (rotoscoliosis, DAMI) → chronic asymmetrical muscle loading → metabolic cascade (preliminary cert: 0.15)
2. Thoracolumbar sympathetic chain compression/irritation → multi-organ dysautonomia (preliminary cert: 0.10)
3. Compensatory muscle overuse as primary (not secondary) cause of mitochondrial failure (preliminary cert: 0.10)
4. Mechanical nerve root irritation → sensitization, allodynia, SFN-like symptoms (preliminary cert: 0.10)
5. Arthrose cervicale progressive → worsening CCI with age → symptom spiral (preliminary cert: 0.15)
