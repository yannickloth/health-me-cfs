# Integration Plan: VMAT2 Loss in Striatum in Long COVID

**Purpose:** Integrate findings from Liu et al. (2026, eBioMedicine) showing reduced VMAT2 binding (index of dopamine-releasing neuron density) in striatum of long COVID patients, with correlations to apathy, motor slowing, and memory decline. Assess relevance to ME/CFS pathophysiology — particularly neuropsychiatric symptoms, dopaminergic dysfunction, and therapeutic directions.

**Target chapters:** ch08 (neurological), ch14a–ch19 (treatments — dopaminergic augmentation), ch24 (differential diagnosis/treatment response), hypothesis-registry

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- Dopaminergic terminal loss in striatum may be a shared pathology between long COVID and ME/CFS (preliminary certainty: 0.25)
- VMAT2 reduction correlates with apathy, motor slowing, memory decline — symptoms also common in ME/CFS
- Dopamine augmentation (L-dopa, MAOIs, rasagiline) as potential treatment for neuropsychiatric ME/CFS symptoms

**Notes:**
- Paper: Liu YK et al. "Loss of vesicular monoamine transporter 2 in striatum of long COVID and relationship to neuropsychiatric symptoms." eBioMedicine 2026. DOI: 10.1016/j.ebiom.2026.106339
- PET imaging study: (+)[11C]DTBZ BPND, n=24 long COVID vs 24 age-matched controls
- Key finding: 16–20% reduction in VMAT2 binding across ventral striatum, dorsal putamen, dorsal caudate
- Correlated with apathy (r=-0.54), motor slowing (r=0.51), memory decline (r=0.58)
- Study limitation: sample is young (mean 32.2), apathy-predominant phenotype — may not generalize to all long COVID phenotypes
