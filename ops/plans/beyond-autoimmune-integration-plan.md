# Beyond Autoimmune — ME/CFS Integration Plan

**Companion to:** `autoimmune-diseases-integration-plan.md`  
**Purpose:** Systematically identify and integrate topics outside the autoimmune disease list that are likely to yield novel ME/CFS mechanistic insights, new hypotheses, or diagnostic/treatment implications.  
**Same approach:** each topic → `/integrate-topic` pipeline; research-first, literature-grounded, both directions of causality where applicable.

---

## Central Question (per topic)

> "What does this system, disease, or mechanism contribute to ME/CFS pathophysiology — as a causal upstream context, a shared mechanism, a differential diagnosis, or a novel treatment target — and what does the literature actually say vs. what is merely plausible?"

Same scientific review constraint as autoimmune plan: **find what the literature says, do not confirm priors.**

## Category 1 — Other Post-Infectious / Post-Acute Syndromes

These are the closest phenotypic relatives of ME/CFS. Direction: shared mechanisms → cross-illuminate.

| # | Topic | ME/CFS Link | Priority |
|---|-------|------------|----------|
| 1 | **Long COVID / PASC** | Largest overlap; shared biomarkers (microclots, GPCR-Ab, NK dysfunction, TRPM3); Long COVID as ME/CFS induction model | 🔴 Critical |
| 2 | **Post-Lyme Disease Syndrome** | Borrelia persistence; immune exhaustion; small fibre neuropathy; CXCL13; overlapping symptoms | 🔴 Critical |
| 3 | **Post-COVID vaccination syndrome** | Spike protein persistence; GPCR-Ab; molecular mimicry; ME/CFS phenotype induction | 🟠 High |
| 4 | **Post-dengue / post-chikungunya fatigue** | Tropical post-viral ME/CFS equivalents; arthralgia; different immune backdrop | 🟡 Medium |
| 5 | **Post-sepsis syndrome** | Immune exhaustion; mitochondrial damage; cognitive impairment; ME/CFS as post-sepsis sequela | 🟠 High |
| 6 | **Post-intensive care syndrome (PICS)** | Deconditioning vs. ME/CFS pathology distinction; muscle wasting; cognitive sequelae | 🟡 Medium |
| 7 | **Post-Ebola / post-West Nile fatigue** | Neurotropic virus sequelae; brain fog; fatigue; IgG persistence | 🟡 Medium |

## Category 2 — Neurological and Psychiatric Conditions

Direction: shared mechanisms, differential diagnosis, bidirectional risk.

| # | Topic | ME/CFS Link | Priority |
|---|-------|------------|----------|
| 8 | **Functional Neurological Disorder (FND)** | Most important differential/comorbidity after autoimmune; central sensitization; brain-body disconnect; NOT the same as ME/CFS | 🔴 Critical |
| 9 | **Central Sensitization Syndrome** | Pain amplification; shared with fibromyalgia; glial sensitization; substance P / CGRP | 🔴 Critical |
| 10 | **Alzheimer's / neurodegeneration** | Neuroinflammation; glymphatic failure; microglial priming; tau/amyloid — shared upstream drivers? | 🟠 High |
| 11 | **Parkinson's Disease** | Alpha-synuclein; gut-brain axis (Braak staging); vagal nerve; mitochondrial Complex I defect overlaps | 🟠 High |
| 12 | **Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) / concussion** | Post-concussion syndrome ≈ ME/CFS phenotype; BBB disruption; neuroinflammation; CTE overlap | 🟠 High |
| 13 | **ADHD** | Dopamine dysregulation; HPA axis; shared EF deficits; ME/CFS brain fog vs. ADHD inattention | 🟠 High |
| 14 | **Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)** | Shared: immune activation, mitochondrial dysfunction, gut dysbiosis, sensory hypersensitivity | 🟠 High |
| 15 | **Burnout / chronic stress** | HPA axis overlap; distinction from ME/CFS; cortisol trajectory diverges | 🟡 Medium |
| 16 | **Glymphatic system dysfunction** | Brain waste clearance; sleep-dependent; AQP4; already mentioned in paper but no dedicated section | 🟠 High |
| 17 | **Dysautonomia spectrum (beyond POTS)** | Autoimmune autonomic ganglionopathy (AAG); pure autonomic failure; MSA; ganglionic AChR-Ab | 🔴 Critical |

Direction: rare diseases with well-characterised mechanisms that illuminate ME/CFS pathways.

## Pre-Identified Novel Hypotheses

| # | Hypothesis | Relevant Categories |
|---|-----------|-------------------|
| H1 | Gulf War Illness and ME/CFS share a mitochondrial + neuroinflammatory pathology despite different triggers (chemical vs. viral) — same downstream mechanism | Cat 7 |
| H2 | Glymphatic failure during non-restorative sleep in ME/CFS → accumulation of metabolic waste → morning symptoms and cognitive impairment | Cat 2, Cat 9 |
| H3 | Gut virome dysregulation amplifies bacterial dysbiosis effects through bacteriophage-mediated bacterial lysis → endotoxin release → immune activation | Cat 5 |
| H4 | hEDS connective tissue laxity → chronic low-grade mechanoreceptor activation → sympathetic overdrive → POTS → ME/CFS; all one causal chain | Cat 6 |
| H5 | Cancer-related fatigue and ME/CFS share the same cytokine-sickness behaviour pathway; CRF treatments (IL-6 blockade, exercise oncology) as ME/CFS treatment models | Cat 3 |
| H6 | McArdle disease as a "perfect model" for ME/CFS energy failure: same second-wind phenomenon absent in ME/CFS suggests glycogenolysis failure is not the primary block | Cat 10 |
| H7 | ACE-programmed HPA hyporesponsiveness creates the immune tolerance deficit that, upon viral trigger, fails to resolve → ME/CFS onset | Cat 11 |
| H8 | Post-sepsis mitochondrial danger signalling (mtDNA → cGAS-STING) is identical to ME/CFS immunosenescence pathway — post-sepsis as a compressed ME/CFS model | Cat 1 |
