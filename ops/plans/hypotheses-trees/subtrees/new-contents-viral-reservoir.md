## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠  
**Tier:** 2  
**ME/CFS links:**
- Multiple trigger viruses (EBV, HHV-6, enteroviruses, SARS-CoV-2) establish tissue-resident reservoirs that persist indefinitely
- Standard serology misses tissue-level persistence; tissue biopsy or imaging needed to detect reservoir burden
- Persistent tissue reservoirs maintain chronic immune activation without active replication, explaining why antivirals targeting active replication often fail
- EBV latency in memory B cells with constitutive LMP1/2A expression sustains B cell activation; HHV-6 chromosomal integration creates persistent genome integration
- SARS-CoV-2 spike protein detected in plasma >12 months post-infection; enteroviruses persist in gastric/muscle tissue
- Immune evasion mechanisms (MHC-I downregulation, viral miRNAs) prevent clearance of reservoir cells
- Common downstream signature across viruses: IFN dysregulation, T cell exhaustion, B cell activation independent of triggering virus

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- "Trigger-Agnostic Viral Reservoir as Common ME/CFS Pathogenic Mechanism" (cert 0.55)
  - Core: multiple viruses capable of triggering ME/CFS share the ability to establish tissue reservoirs; shared reservoir-mediated immune activation drives chronic pathophysiology, not the specific virus
  - Testable: ME/CFS patients share common IFN signature regardless of triggering virus; tissue biopsy detects viral nucleic acids at higher rates than blood PCR; tissue viral load correlates with symptom severity independent of serology

**Research questions:**
1. Do ME/CFS patients share a common IFN signature tissue expression pattern regardless of triggering virus?
2. Does tissue biopsy (gut, tonsil, lymph node) in ME/CFS patients detect viral nucleic acids at higher rates than peripheral blood PCR in the same patients?
3. Does tissue viral load magnitude correlate with symptom severity independently of serology titre?
4. Would latency-reversing agents (e.g., vorinostat for EBV) combined with immune reconstitution produce greater benefit than antivirals alone?
5. How does HHV-6 chromosomal integration (ciHHV-6) affect serology interpretation and treatment response in ME/CFS?
