## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟡  
**Tier:** 3  
**ME/CFS links:**
- GI tract as immune-privileged site (high Treg density, IgA-dominant immunity, tolerogenic DCs) permits long-term viral persistence after systemic clearance
- SARS-CoV-2 RNA persists in gut at 7+ months (Natarajan 2022) despite respiratory clearance; temporal mismatch crucial for understanding persistent infection
- Enterovirus VP1 documented in gastric antrum (Chia 2008); enteroviruses use GI as primary replication site; fecal-oral route perpetuates persistence
- Bidirectional viral-dysbiosis maintenance: viral persistence → dysbiosis; dysbiosis → impaired antiviral defence (reduced IgA)
- Viral epithelial damage creates barrier disruption distinct from dysbiosis-driven permeability; additive effect on LPS translocation

**Target chapters:** ch11 (gut microbiome), ch07 (immune dysfunction / viral persistence)  
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** The GI Tract as an Immune-Privileged Viral Sanctuary in ME/CFS (cert 0.50)  
**Research questions:**
1. Do ME/CFS patients with SARS-CoV-2-triggered onset show detectable viral RNA in stool at 6+ months at higher rates than COVID-recovered patients?
2. Do ME/CFS patients with detectable gut viral RNA show higher plasma LPS, higher I-FABP, and worse gut symptoms than those without?
3. Are secretory IgA levels in ME/CFS patient stool lower than controls, correlating with greater gut viral persistence?
4. Does antiviral treatment reducing gut viral persistence lead to measurable reduction in intestinal permeability markers?
