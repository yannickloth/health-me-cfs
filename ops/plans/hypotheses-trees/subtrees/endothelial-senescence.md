## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠 medium — high-impact journal (Cell Death & Disease, Nature Publishing Group); theoretical framework not yet confirmed in ME/CFS tissue
**ME/CFS links:**
- Virus-induced endothelial senescence → SASP (proinflammatory IL-6/IL-1β/TNF-α, procoagulant, pro-oxidative, vasoconstriction-primed) as perpetuating mechanism in ME/CFS and long COVID (Nunes2026)
- NK cell dysfunction + T cell exhaustion (documented in ME/CFS) → impaired clearance of senescent endothelial cells → bidirectional self-sustaining loop: SASP sustains immune dysfunction; immune dysfunction sustains senescence
- Tissue-specific SASP effects: BBB (neuroinflammation, permeability), cerebral arteries (reduced CBF), GI endothelium (dysbiosis, leaky gut), skeletal muscle microvasculature (impaired exertional perfusion, PEM) — explains multisystem symptom heterogeneity
- Mechanistically distinct from transient endothelial dysfunction: senescent cells actively secrete SASP rather than being passively damaged
- Senolytics (dasatinib + quercetin) trialled in long COVID; senescence markers (p16, p21, β-galactosidase, SASP cytokines) as candidate biomarkers
- Does not account for non-post-viral ME/CFS cases (limitation noted)
**Target chapters:** ch10 (cardiovascular/endothelial) — primary, new hypothesis after Hoel2026 proteomics paragraph; ch07 (immune dysfunction) — NK cell dysfunction consequence; ch13 (integrative models) — table update for endothelial dysfunction row; ch18 (emerging therapies) — senolytics rationale
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Endothelial senescence as the perpetuating mechanism explaining chronicity of vascular dysfunction; SASP → immune dysfunction → senescence loop as self-sustaining disease maintenance; senolytics as mechanistically grounded therapeutic target
**Research questions:**
1. Can endothelial senescence markers (p16, p21, β-galactosidase, SASP cytokines) be measured in ME/CFS patient tissue or biopsies?
2. Does senolytic treatment (dasatinib + quercetin) improve vascular function and symptom severity in ME/CFS?
3. Is NK cell cytotoxicity restoration sufficient to clear senescent endothelial cells and interrupt the SASP loop?
4. Does the model explain non-post-viral ME/CFS, or does it apply only to post-viral cases?
