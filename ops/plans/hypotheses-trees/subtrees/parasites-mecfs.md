## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠 medium — Bergen Giardia outbreak series is the strongest natural experiment in post-infectious ME/CFS; NK cell immunophenotyping adds mechanistic data
**ME/CFS links:**
- Bergen 2004 Giardia waterborne outbreak: ≥5% of lab-confirmed giardiasis cases developed full CFS (Naess2012); 41.5% of persistently fatigued patients at 5 years met Fukuda CFS criteria (Morch2013)
- Controlled prospective cohort: 3× excess chronic fatigue risk in Giardia-exposed vs. matched controls at 6 years (Hanevik2014, n=748+878); prevalence declining slowly — partial natural recovery
- Immunophenotyping: post-infectious CFS showed significantly lower NK-cell levels at 5 years; NK levels correlated negatively with fatigue AND abdominal symptom severity (Hanevik2012immunophenotype, n=19)
- sCD40L elevated in post-Giardia CFS — platelet/immune activation marker correlating with fatigue severity (Hanevik2017, n=15)
- Negative result: BAFF, anti-CdtB, anti-flagellin not elevated in post-Giardia CFS — proposed IBS biomarkers are not universal for post-infectious GI/fatigue syndromes (Hanevik2022)
- Cryptosporidium: fatigue 22%, joint pain 33%, multi-system symptoms at 12 months post-infection (Stiff2017) — extends trigger spectrum beyond Giardia
- Trigger spectrum: 60.3% of 1773 ME/CFS patients reported infectious onset; mononucleosis most common (30%) but >100 distinct agents identified; parasitic triggers implicitly included (Jason2022)
- Chronic fatigue + overactive bladder comorbidity strongly associated 6 years post-Giardia (OR 2.73) — autonomic multi-system dimension (Persson2015)
**Target chapters:** Post-infectious chapter (Giardia as natural experiment); immunology chapter (NK cell reduction); trigger spectrum/etiology chapter; disease course chapter (natural history)
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Parasitic infection as sufficient trigger for full-criteria ME/CFS via the same post-infectious mechanism as EBV/COVID; NK cell depletion as a shared immunological consequence; slow partial recovery trajectory (3-year peak, partial improvement by 6 years); Giardia as a model system for studying post-infectious immune dysfunction
**Research questions:**
1. Does the NK cell depletion in post-Giardia CFS share mechanistic features with NK dysfunction in other ME/CFS etiologies?
2. Is SIBO a common secondary complication in post-Giardia CFS due to MMC disruption?
3. Does the natural history of post-Giardia CFS (partial recovery at 6 years) differ from post-EBV or post-COVID ME/CFS?
4. What is the prevalence of parasitic triggers in ME/CFS vs. the general population beyond Bergen cohort data?
