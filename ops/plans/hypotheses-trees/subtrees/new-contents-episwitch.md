## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠  
**Tier:** 2  
**ME/CFS links:**
- EpiSwitch uses 3D genomic architecture (chromatin conformation capture) to detect epigenetic dysregulation patterns beyond standard DNA methylation assays
- Validation study reports 92% sensitivity and 98% specificity — the highest analytical specificity of any ME/CFS diagnostic biomarker published to date
- Blood-based liquid biopsy approach makes it accessible compared to tissue-based assays
- Chromosomal conformation changes reflect disease-specific epigenetic dysregulation that simple methylation arrays miss
- Currently offers CE marking in UK and commercial availability; FDA pathway status unclear
- However: single validation study from test developer; independent academic replication needed before clinical adoption

**Target chapters:**
- `contents/part4-research/ch20-biomarkers.tex` (primary: new §EpiSwitch subsection documenting 3D genomic architecture and diagnostic validation)
- `contents/part2-pathophysiology/ch12-genetics-epigenetics.tex` (cross-ref: 3D genome dysregulation as a new dimension beyond DNA methylation patterns)
- `contents/shared/changelog.tex` (motivated-by entry)

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- EpiSwitch represents a leap in diagnostic specificity (cert 0.65 on sensitivity/specificity replication, 0.55 on distinguishing from other fatigue conditions)
  - Core claim: 3D chromosomal conformation dysregulation is specific to ME/CFS and can be detected in blood
  - Testable: independent academic cohorts replicate 92% sensitivity and 98% specificity; EpiSwitch distinguishes ME/CFS from fibromyalgia, depression, and other chronic fatiguing illnesses

**Research questions:**
1. Do independent academic replication studies confirm the 92% sensitivity and 98% specificity reported in the validation study?
2. Can EpiSwitch distinguish ME/CFS from fibromyalgia, chronic depression, other fatiguing autoimmune conditions, or other chronic fatiguing illnesses?
3. Do EpiSwitch positive and negative results correlate with disease severity or symptom subgroups?
4. Can EpiSwitch monitoring detect disease progression or improvement and thereby support longitudinal patient management?
5. What is the cost and accessibility of EpiSwitch testing in different healthcare systems, and what is the regulatory pathway to clinical adoption?
