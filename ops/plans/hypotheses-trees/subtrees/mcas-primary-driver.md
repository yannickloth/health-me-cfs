## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🔴 high — clinically actionable Domain 6 framework; treatment protocols included; 5 new bib entries added
**ME/CFS links:**
- MCAS present in 16–25% of ME/CFS patients (Rohrhofer2025, n=687+383); associated with significantly higher orthostatic intolerance (89% vs 72%, p<0.0001)
- Mast cells perivascular near nerve endings in carotid bodies, heart, and hypothalamus; CRH activates hypothalamic mast cells → VEGF + tryptase + neurotensin + CXCL8 → BBB disruption → neuroinflammation (Theoharides2024ANS)
- Mast cell–ANS axis: positions MCAS as upstream initiator of dysautonomia, not merely comorbidity; histamine-mediated vasodilation + plasma exudation exacerbate preload failure in orthostatic stress
- POTS + HSD + MCAS triad: therapy response 75–91% in young POTS regardless of diagnostic criteria set used (Yao2025, n=100) — clinical response spans all groups
- Large fraction of dysautonomia/hEDS/POTS patients fail strict MCAS criteria but respond to mast-cell-directed therapy (Conway2024)
- Null result: Mayo Clinic retrospective (JACI 2026): 110 adults with hEDS/POTS — none met established MCAS diagnostic criteria; 95% had unconfirmed labels; important counterevidence against overclaiming
- MCAS as "frequent, clinically significant comorbidity with mechanistic plausibility as upstream dysautonomia driver in subset" — not "rate-limiting upstream cause of all ME/CFS"
**Target chapters:** ch13 (integrative models) — Domain 6 addition to biological phenotyping framework; ch18 (emerging therapies) — MCAS treatment ladder (H1/H2 → cromolyn/ketotifen → omalizumab); diagnostic workup chapter — urinary NMH + LTE4 + PGD2 metabolite panel
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** MCAS as upstream dysautonomia driver in a biological phenotype subset; Domain 6 inclusion criteria (≥2 of: episodic multi-system symptoms, elevated urinary NMH/LTE4/PGD2 metabolite, response to mast-cell-directed therapy); tiered treatment protocol for Domain 6–positive patients
**Research questions:**
1. Does MCAS-targeted treatment improve orthostatic intolerance metrics (NASA lean test, COMPASS-31) in ME/CFS patients meeting Domain 6 criteria?
2. Are urinary NMH + LTE4 + PGD2 metabolite panels more sensitive than serum tryptase for identifying MCAS in ME/CFS?
3. Can the MCAS primary driver hypothesis be distinguished from the comorbidity model by intervention timing (do MCAS treatments improve ME/CFS or only MCAS symptoms)?
4. What is the prevalence of the full POTS + HSD + MCAS triad in a well-characterised ME/CFS cohort?
