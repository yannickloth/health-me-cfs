## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟡  
**Tier:** 3  
**ME/CFS links:**
- Spike protein persists in blood and tissues months to years after SARS-CoV-2 infection
- Gut RNA persistence documented at 7+ months (Natarajan 2022), providing a local source of continued immune activation
- Monocyte viral reservoirs carry spike protein and viral RNA, acting as trafficking vehicles for chronic antigen presentation
- Spike protein biological activity independent of viral replication drives endothelial dysfunction and platelet activation
- Persistence provides mechanistic distinction between COVID-recovered and Long COVID/ME/CFS transitions

**Research questions:**

2. Does antiviral treatment reduce circulating spike protein and improve symptom burden in Long COVID/ME/CFS?
3. Do spike protein levels in monocytes correlate with inflammatory biomarker profiles and symptom severity?
4. Do patients with immune-privilege site persistence have longer recovery times and higher ME/CFS incidence?
