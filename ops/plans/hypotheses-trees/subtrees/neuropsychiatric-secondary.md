## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🔴 high — rich brainstorm with treatment implications, novel hypotheses, and mathematical models; directly extends ch14d content
**ME/CFS links:**
- Three-mechanism cascade (Architecture B): neuroinflammation (IDO activation) → dopaminergic depletion → interoceptive hierarchy failure → secondary ADHD-like, ASD-like, and depressive phenotypes
- BH4 as cross-chapter bridge (ch06↔ch14d): mitochondrial dysfunction depletes GTP (→ less BH4 synthesised) and NADH (→ less BH4 recycled from BH2); BH4 is rate-limiting for tyrosine hydroxylase (dopamine), tryptophan hydroxylase (serotonin), and all three NOS isoforms
- Effort-cost model augmented with PEM_risk: A_i(t) = π_i·R_i − k(D(t))·C_i − λ·PEM_risk(C_i, E(t)); distinguishes dopaminergic depletion (elevated k) from rational PEM avoidance (elevated λ)
- Mast cell mediators as precision-weighting disruptors: H3R activation → acute dopamine suppression; peripheral serotonin from mast cells → vagal 5-HT3 flooding → brainstem interoceptive prediction errors; PGD2 → sleep drive dysregulation
- Sleep architecture: precision-weighting rigidity → inability to reduce interoceptive monitoring → reduced N3 (slow-wave) → reduced glymphatic clearance → neuroinflammatory waste accumulation → vicious cycle
- Estrogen-microglial phenotype hypothesis: estradiol shifts microglia M1→M2 via ERβ; predicts menstrual cycle modulation of secondary neuropsychiatric phenotypes and menopausal inflection point
- NMDA receptor autoantibody hypothesis: subclinical anti-NMDA antibodies → impaired dopaminergic and thalamocortical NMDA function → convergence with Architecture B cascade
- Architecture discrimination: Architecture A (pre-existing ADHD/ASD predisposes to ME/CFS) vs B (ME/CFS neuroinflammation generates secondary phenotypes) vs C (bidirectional amplification); Mendelian randomisation with inflammatory gene variants as instruments
- Patient stratification subtypes: N (neuroinflammation-dominant), D (dopamine-dominant), I (interoceptive-dominant), C (combined) — predicts different treatment response profiles
**Target chapters:** ch14d (cross-disease neuropsychiatric) — BH4 bridge, mast cell mediators, augmented effort-cost equation; ch06 (energy metabolism) — BH4 → neurotransmitter bridge; ch15 (interoceptive section) — cross-reference body-wandering; hypothesis registry — new hypotheses

**Research questions:**
1. Does BH4 supplementation (sapropterin or methylfolate proxy) improve both mitochondrial function and neuropsychiatric symptoms in ME/CFS?

3. Is there a detectable subset of ME/CFS patients with low-titer NMDA receptor antibodies in CSF correlating with cognitive and interoceptive dysfunction?
4. Does interoceptive precision-weighting flexibility (Powers 2021 paradigm) correlate with depression severity in ME/CFS, and does successful POTS treatment improve it?
