**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- ER-Stress-Driven WASF3 Upregulation Suppressing Mitochondrial Supercomplex Assembly in ME/CFS (cert ~0.55): chronic ER stress driven by viral protein expression, oxidative stress, or metabolic dysfunction upregulates WASF3, which drives actin polymerization that disrupts mitochondrial supercomplex assembly and ATP synthase efficiency

**Research questions:**
1. Do WASF3 protein levels in ME/CFS muscle biopsies correlate inversely with VO2max on CPET?
2. Do ME/CFS patients with higher WASF3 expression show greater mitochondrial supercomplex fragmentation on Blue Native PAGE analysis?

4. Is WASF3 protein level higher in SARS-CoV-2-triggered ME/CFS compared to EBV-triggered, consistent with ORF9b-driven ER stress?
5. Can WASF3 inhibitors (from oncology context) restore mitochondrial supercomplex assembly and exercise capacity in ME/CFS cell and animal models?
