## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🔴 Critical
**Tier:** 1

**ME/CFS links:**
- Anti-mitochondrial antibodies (AMA-M2) → mitochondrial Complex II disruption
- Profound fatigue phenotype near-identical to ME/CFS (most studied fatigue in hepatology)
- Autonomic dysfunction in PBC (orthostasis, nocturnal hypotension)
- Central sensitization in PBC fatigue — identical phenomenology to ME/CFS
- PBC fatigue does not correlate with liver disease severity — supports non-hepatic mechanism

**Target chapters:** ch04-08-differential-diagnosis, ch05-subgroups, ch06-energy-metabolism, ch07-immune-dysfunction, ch14d-cross-disease, ch20-biomarker-research

**Pre-identified hypotheses:** H4 (PBC fatigue phenotype = ME/CFS fatigue phenotype via anti-mitochondrial → mitochondrial dysfunction)

**Research questions:**
1. Are AMA-M2 antibodies found in ME/CFS cohorts without liver disease?
2. Does mitochondrial Complex II disruption by AMA-M2 produce the same metabolic signature as ME/CFS?
3. Is PBC fatigue severity correlated with ME/CFS severity scores in comorbid patients?
4. Does obeticholic acid (PBC treatment) affect fatigue through mitochondrial restoration?
5. Is there a "post-PBC ME/CFS" subtype where fatigue persists after liver normalisation?
6. Does PBC autonomic dysfunction phenotype overlap with ME/CFS POTS/dysautonomia?
