## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠
**Tier:** (if applicable)
**ME/CFS links:**
- Severe/bedbound patients may have chronically elevated gut permeability and baseline endotoxemia via the same barrier-failure mechanism as active patients, but triggered by minimal activities (standing, showering, cognitive tasks)
- Zonulin, I-FABP, LPS/endotoxemia, and claudin-2 are candidate biomarkers for predicting dietary intervention response
- Mast cell activation cascade (wheat → barrier failure → LPS translocation → mast cell degranulation → histamine/tryptase) may explain exercise-exacerbated histamine symptoms
- Supplement protocols (barrier repair: L-glutamine, butyrate; mast cell stabilizers: ketotifen, quercetin; splanchnic blood flow: L-citrulline, compression) may synergize with wheat elimination
- Pediatric ME/CFS may share the same gut-permeability mechanism with different activity thresholds and safety considerations

**Target chapters:** `ch11-gi-microbiome.typ`, `ch17-lifestyle-interventions.typ`, `ch14a-urgent-action-severe.typ`, `ch14c-pediatric-severe.typ`
**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- "Exercise intolerance in severe ME/CFS is mediated by the same barrier failure mechanism, but with dramatically lower activity threshold: minimal activities trigger sufficient splanchnic hypoperfusion to activate wheat-primed barrier failure."
- "Wheat-primed gut barrier failure + exercise-induced splanchnic hypoperfusion creates chronic LPS exposure → mast cell activation cascade → histamine/tryptase release → exercise exacerbates histamine symptoms."
**Research questions:**
1. What activities beyond traditional exercise trigger splanchnic hypoperfusion in severe ME/CFS?
2. Is baseline gut permeability chronically elevated in severe patients?
3. Do severe patients show elevated baseline LPS/I-FABP markers?
4. Which biomarkers (zonulin, I-FABP, LPS, dysbiosis index, tryptase) correlate with wheat sensitivity and predict response to elimination trials?
5. Can functional capacity be improved by wheat elimination even without exercise testing?
6. Do ME/CFS patients with exercise intolerance show elevated mast cell activation markers?
7. Do antihistamines or mast cell stabilizers improve exercise tolerance?
