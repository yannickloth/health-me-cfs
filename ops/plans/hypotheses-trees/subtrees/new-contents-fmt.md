## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🔴  
**Tier:** 1  
**ME/CFS links:**
- FMT is the most direct intervention targeting documented gut dysbiosis (reduced Faecalibacterium, Roseburia, Bifidobacterium in ch11)
- Super-donor phenomenon: not all donors produce equivalent outcomes; "super-donors" with high diversity and specific taxa drive most response signal
- Patient selection: GI-symptom-dominant ME/CFS patients and those with severe dysbiosis most likely to respond; personalized/stratified approach
- Delivery routes (colonoscopic, capsule, enema) show differential engraftment; capsule reduces invasiveness but may have lower engraftment
- Durability unclear: single vs multiple infusions; microbiome shifts persistence variable; booster protocols emerging
- Trial landscape (2024–2025): Norwegian open-label trial results available; multiple ME/CFS trials in progress; Long COVID evidence increasingly relevant

**Target chapters:** ch18-experimental-therapies.tex (new subsection "Fecal Microbiota Transplantation"), ch11-gut-microbiome.tex (cross-reference from dysbiosis subsection)  
**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- FMT can restore microbiome diversity and composition in dysbiotic ME/CFS patients, with treatment response dependent on patient selection and donor microbiome characteristics (cert ~0.35): plausible from mechanism and C. difficile/IBD evidence but direct ME/CFS RCT data lacking

**Research questions:**
1. Which ME/CFS subgroups (GI-dominant, dysbiosis severity, symptom profile) show best response to FMT?
2. What microbiome characteristics define a "super-donor" and can these be identified pre-transplant?
3. Does FMT improve ME/CFS fatigue, PEM, cognitive function, or GI symptoms compared to placebo in controlled trials?
4. How long do microbiome shifts from FMT persist in ME/CFS patients? Do booster infusions maintain benefits?
5. What is the safety profile and serious adverse event rate (bacteraemia, resistant organism transmission) in ME/CFS patients vs other populations?
