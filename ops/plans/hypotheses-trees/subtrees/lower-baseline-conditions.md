## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🔴 high — strongest framing for why neurodivergent/hypermobile patients are highest-risk ME/CFS subgroup
**ME/CFS links:**
- ASD: systemic mitochondrial ETC dysfunction (elevated lactate 17%, pyruvate 41%, ↓ATP; Frye2024 meta-analysis of 204 studies) — same ETC pathways as ME/CFS energy failure, chronically impaired as a baseline
- ASD: low BH4 consistently found (ColpaniFilho2025 systematic review) → impaired NO, dopamine, serotonin synthesis → vascular tone and cerebral autoregulation deficits
- ADHD + ASD: IL-6 elevation at age 9 mediates neurodivergent traits → chronic disabling fatigue at 18 (Quadt2024 ALSPAC, n=4,563); temporal precedence established
- hEDS/hypermobility: joint laxity → venous pooling → cerebral hypoperfusion (Csecs2022 mediation analysis: JH mediates neurodivergence → dysautonomia + pain); 51% of neurodivergent adults have JH vs. 20% general population
- ASD + hEDS compound: autism 7.4× more likely to have EDS (BaezaVelasco2025 meta-analysis); dual mechanism = mitochondrial dysfunction + cerebral hypoperfusion
- Migraine: 1.5× CFS incidence (Lau2015 national cohort); dose-response with migraine frequency; recurrent CSD → cumulative mitochondrial depletion (Wang2023 review)
- GCH1 rs841 (transdiagnostic): ~4% homozygous; ↓BH4 → ↓NO → impaired cerebral autoregulation; affects ADHD, ASD, depression, and ME/CFS
- Iron deficiency transdiagnostic: ↓Complex I/II + ↓dopamine synthesis in ADHD and ASD (DelRosso2026); modifiable baseline reducer
**Target chapters:** ch12 (genetics/predisposition) — GCH1 rs841, mtDNA; ch13 (onset/triggers) — lower-baseline hypothesis as predisposition framework; ch14b (immune) — IL-6 mediation; ch14d (hEDS/POTS/MCAS) — ASD–EDS cluster; new hypothesis environment: "Metabolic reserve depletion hypothesis"
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Metabolic reserve depletion as the predisposition mechanism shared across ASD, ADHD, hEDS, migraine; ASD+hEDS compound-mechanism highest-risk subgroup; GCH1 rs841 as high-frequency shared genetic vulnerability; neurodivergent traits dimensional (not categorical) risk factor for long COVID/ME/CFS transition
**Research questions:**
1. Does pre-illness neurodivergent status predict ME/CFS severity and recovery trajectory?
2. Does the ASD+hEDS subgroup show earlier onset and more severe mitochondrial + autonomic phenotype?
3. Is GCH1 rs841 overrepresented in ME/CFS patients vs. general population?
4. Does iron correction in neurodivergent ME/CFS patients improve cognitive and mitochondrial outcomes?
