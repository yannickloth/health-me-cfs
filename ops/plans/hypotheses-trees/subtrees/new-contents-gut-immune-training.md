## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟡  
**Tier:** 3  
**ME/CFS links:**
- Neonatal microbiome disruption (caesarean delivery, formula feeding, antibiotics) impairs immune tolerance training, leaving regulatory deficit
- Childhood antibiotic exposure creates selective pressure against immune-educating commensals; epidemiological evidence links antibiotics to allergy and autoimmune risk
- Breastfeeding provides HMOs (prebiotics) and IgA (antibodies); duration effects on lifelong immune tone; protective against post-infectious immune persistence
- Hygiene hypothesis explains why reduced pathogen exposure → impaired regulation → disproportionate responses to infections → persistent ME/CFS
- Epigenetic imprinting: gut bacteria produce butyrate (HDAC inhibitor); early microbiome shapes epigenome via DNA methylation patterns

4. Does breastfeeding duration in ME/CFS patients inversely correlate with adult disease severity?
