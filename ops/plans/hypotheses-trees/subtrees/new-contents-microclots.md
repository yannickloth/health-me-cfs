## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🔴  
**Tier:** 1  
**ME/CFS links:**
- Fibrinaloid microclots >10-fold elevated in ME/CFS vs controls (Nunes 2022); 80% platelet hyperactivation documented
- Amyloid fibrin conformation with impaired fibrinolysis provides direct mechanism for microvascular obstruction
- Endothelial-microclot feedback loop (microclots damage endothelium → promotes further coagulation) explains sustained vascular dysfunction
- Connection to exercise intolerance and PEM: microclots obstructing capillaries during exertion impair oxygen delivery
- Triple anticoagulant therapy (aspirin + clopidogrel + apixaban) emerging as preliminary intervention in Long COVID with mechanistic rationale

**Target chapters:** ch10-cardiovascular.tex (§Fibrin Deposition ~line 635), ch10-cardiovascular.tex (§Endothelial Dysfunction ~line 214/348), ch18-experimental-therapies.tex  
**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- Microclot-Mediated Microvascular Obstruction as a Mechanism of Exercise Intolerance and PEM (cert ~0.50): amyloid fibrin microclots obstruct microcapillaries, impair oxygen delivery during exertion, amplify post-exertional symptoms through tissue hypoxia and inflammatory molecule release

**Research questions:**
1. Does microclot burden correlate quantitatively with CPET performance (VO2max, anaerobic threshold) and PEM severity?
2. Do microclot levels fluctuate with symptom severity (higher during crashes, lower during remissions)?
3. Can fibrinolytic enhancement (nattokinase, lumbrokinase) reduce microclot burden and improve capillary perfusion on NIRS or similar measures?
4. What circulating factors in ME/CFS plasma trigger fibrin misfolding to amyloid conformation (SAA, LPS, other)?
5. Can triple anticoagulant therapy reduce microclot burden in ME/CFS with acceptable bleeding risk?
