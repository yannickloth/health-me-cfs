## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🔴 Critical
**Category:** 3 — Metabolic and endocrine diseases

**ME/CFS links:**
- Most studied fatigue syndrome in medicine — largest evidence base for cytokine-driven fatigue
- Cytokine-driven; central sensitization; sickness behaviour model shared with ME/CFS
- CRF and ME/CFS share the same cytokine-sickness behaviour pathway (H5)
- CRF treatments (IL-6 blockade, exercise oncology protocols) as ME/CFS treatment models
- Post-cancer fatigue persisting after remission = phenotypically equivalent to ME/CFS

**Target chapters:** ch13-integrative-models, ch14d-cross-disease, ch18-emerging-therapies

**Pre-identified hypotheses:** H5 (CRF and ME/CFS share cytokine-sickness behaviour pathway; CRF treatments as ME/CFS models)

**Research questions:**
1. Are the cytokine profiles in CRF and ME/CFS quantitatively similar (IL-6, TNF-α, IL-1β)?
2. Does tocilizumab (IL-6 blockade, used in cancer) reduce ME/CFS-like fatigue in CRF patients?
3. What does the CRF exercise oncology literature say about exertion tolerance — does it support or contradict ME/CFS pacing?
4. Is post-cancer ME/CFS (fatigue persisting after remission) a documented subtype in oncology literature?
5. Do the sickness behaviour animal models (LPS-induced fatigue) reproduce ME/CFS or CRF more accurately?
