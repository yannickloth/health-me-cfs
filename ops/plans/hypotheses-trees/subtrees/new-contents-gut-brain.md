## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠  
**Tier:** 2  
**ME/CFS links:**
- Reduced vagal tone (measurable by heart rate variability decline) impairs parasympathetic gut-brain signalling
- Enteric nervous system (500 million neurons) produces 90% of the body's serotonin; ENS dysfunction reduces CNS serotonin precursor availability
- SCFA-producing bacteria (Faecalibacterium, Roseburia) are reduced in ME/CFS; butyrate and propionate cross blood-brain barrier and modulate microglial activation
- Gut dysbiosis allows tryptophan diversion from serotonin synthesis toward kynurenine, reducing central serotonin and worsening mood, sleep, fatigue
- LPS translocation from damaged gut epithelium crosses blood-brain barrier and activates microglia → neuroinflammation perpetuates brain fog and cognitive impairment
- Cholinergic anti-inflammatory reflex (vagal efferent) is impaired when vagal tone is reduced, allowing unchecked gut-derived inflammation
- Bidirectionality: brain stress → HPA activation → gut motility failure → dysbiosis → worsened gut signals to brain (feedback loop)

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- "Gut-Brain Axis Dysfunction as a Mediator of Neurological Symptoms in ME/CFS" (cert 0.50)
  - Core: reduced vagal tone, SCFA deficiency, and LPS-driven neuroinflammation collectively impair gut-to-brain signalling, contributing to brain fog, fatigue, and autonomic dysregulation
  - Testable: lower HRV correlates with higher plasma LPS and more severe cognitive impairment; stool SCFA negatively correlates with neurological symptom severity; vagal nerve stimulation reduces plasma LPS and improves brain fog

**Research questions:**
1. Do ME/CFS patients with lower HRV (proxy for reduced vagal tone) show higher plasma LPS and more severe cognitive impairment scores?
2. Do stool SCFA concentrations in ME/CFS patients negatively correlate with neurological symptom severity (brain fog, sleep disruption)?
3. Does non-invasive vagal nerve stimulation (auricular) reduce plasma LPS and improve brain fog scores in a crossover study?
4. Do ME/CFS patients show lower ENS serotonin precursor availability (reflected by plasma tryptophan ratios and tryptophan/kynurenine balance) than healthy controls?
5. Is the bidirectional stress-dysbiosis-inflammation feedback loop present, such that stress reduction improves dysbiosis and neuroinflammation markers?
