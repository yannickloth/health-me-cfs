## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟡  
**Tier:** 3  
**ME/CFS links:**
- Gut mycobiome (fungal community) entirely absent from current ME/CFS documentation despite clinical observations of Candida overgrowth
- Candida overgrowth correlations with brain fog, fatigue, GI symptoms lack controlled data but represent recurring clinical concern
- Candida hyphae facilitate bacterial translocation and physically disrupt tight junctions; fungal-bacterial inter-kingdom competition
- Th17/IL-17/IL-22 axis dysregulation in ME/CFS context may permit fungal overgrowth; anti-fungal immune response insufficiency
- β-D-glucan (fungal cell wall biomarker) elevated in some ME/CFS patients; mycotoxin neurotoxic properties speculative but mechanistically plausible

**Target chapters:** ch11 (gut microbiome)  
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Gut Mycobiome Dysbiosis as a Contributing Factor in ME/CFS (cert 0.30, speculation env)  
**Research questions:**
1. Does ITS2 sequencing reveal altered fungal community composition (elevated Candida) in ME/CFS vs controls?
2. Do serum β-D-glucan levels in ME/CFS correlate with intestinal permeability markers (LPS, I-FABP) and symptom severity?
3. Do ME/CFS patients with elevated Candida show distinct Th17/Treg balance compared to ME/CFS with normal mycobiome?
4. Can antifungal treatment targeting Candida overgrowth improve neurological symptoms in a controlled trial?
