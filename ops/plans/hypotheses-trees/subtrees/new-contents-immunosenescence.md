## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠  
**Tier:** 2  
**ME/CFS links:**
- Accelerated immune aging via persistent viral antigen exposure (EBV/CMV) drives senescent T cell and NK cell populations
- Senescent cells maintain pro-inflammatory SASP phenotype while failing to clear viral reservoirs
- Epigenetic aging biomarkers detect biological aging acceleration beyond chronological age
- Senolytics (dasatinib, quercetin) and NK cell infusions remain experimental but represent novel treatment angle
- NIH roadmap explicitly designates immunosenescence as a priority research area

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- "Premature Immunosenescence Driven by Chronic Antigen Stimulation in ME/CFS" (cert 0.50)
  - Core: persistent viral antigen → accelerated T/NK aging → irreversible senescence → chronic SASP inflammation
  - Testable: higher CD57+CD28− senescent T cells in ME/CFS independent of CMV serostatus; CMV-seropositive worse outcomes; T cell telomere shortening

**Research questions:**
1. Do ME/CFS patients show higher proportions of CD57+CD28− senescent T cells than age-matched controls, independent of CMV status?
2. Does the SASP cytokine signature (IL-6, IL-8, TNF) in ME/CFS correlate more tightly with senescent T cell frequency than with classical activation markers?
3. Are senescent immune cells (vs merely exhausted) partly irreversible and thus resistant to conventional immunosuppression?
4. What is the role of premature thymic involution in narrowing the T cell receptor repertoire and impairing response to new pathogens?
5. How does CMV-driven memory inflation contribute specifically to the prognosis worsening in CMV-seropositive ME/CFS patients?
