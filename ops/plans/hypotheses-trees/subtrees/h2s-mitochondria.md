## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠 medium — mechanistically compelling; evidentially thin for ME/CFS specifically
**ME/CFS links:**
- Gut-derived H2S from sulfidogenic bacteria (Desulfovibrio, Bilophila wadsworthia) competitively inhibits mitochondrial Complex IV at cytochrome a3 — same site as cyanide; IC50 ~1–3 µM
- SQOR (mitochondrial H2S clearance enzyme) is CoQ10-dependent; CoQ10 deficiency documented in some ME/CFS studies would impair H2S detox, creating accumulation even at normal gut production
- Hepatic H2S clearance is oxygen-dependent; orthostatic hypoperfusion (ME/CFS) → impaired portal clearance → systemic H2S spillover
- Desulfovibrio H2S directly inhibits GLP-1-secreting intestinal L-cell mitochondria (Qi2024, Nature Metabolism) — proves gut-to-mitochondria H2S toxicity axis is real
- Low-sulfur diet reduces sulfidogenic bacteria, improves microbiome diversity, reduces LPS-binding protein (Ye2025, pilot RCT in UC)
- H2S is biphasic: low concentrations are cytoprotective; excess concentrations (gut lumen range) overwhelm clearance and inhibit ETC
**Target chapters:** ch06 (mitochondrial dysfunction) — hypothesis/speculation; ch11 (gut microbiome) — observation; ch18 (treatments) — dietary/bismuth rationale
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Gut H2S as gut-derived Complex IV inhibitor amplifying ME/CFS energy failure; SQOR/CoQ10 insufficiency as clearance bottleneck; low-sulfur diet + bismuth as ME/CFS subgroup intervention
**Research questions:**
1. Are sulfidogenic bacteria (Desulfovibrio, Bilophila wadsworthia) elevated in ME/CFS microbiome studies?
2. Does H2S breath testing (Trio-Smart three-gas device) correlate with ME/CFS severity or PEM?
3. Does CoQ10 deficiency in ME/CFS impair SQOR function, measurable by H2S clearance assays?
4. Does low-sulfur diet or bismuth subsalicylate improve ME/CFS symptoms in SIBO/H2S-positive subgroup?
