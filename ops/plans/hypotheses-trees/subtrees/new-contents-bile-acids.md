## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟡  
**Tier:** 3  
**ME/CFS links:**
- Bile acid-metabolising bacteria (Bifidobacterium, Clostridiales) depleted in ME/CFS dysbiosis; conversion of primary to secondary bile acids disrupted
- FXR signalling loss leads to impaired gut barrier protection and increased intestinal permeability
- TGR5 activation on mitochondria drives PGC-1α → mitochondrial biogenesis; dysbiosis-induced TGR5 signalling loss directly links gut dysbiosis to energy deficit
- Primary/secondary bile acid imbalance documented in IBS/functional GI disease; ME/CFS-specific metabolomics study needed
- UDCA (ursodeoxycholic acid) available OTC; activates TGR5; mechanistic rationale for supplementation but no ME/CFS RCT

**Target chapters:** ch11 (gut microbiome), ch06 (energy metabolism)  
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Bile Acid Dysbiosis as a Mechanistic Link Between Gut Dysbiosis and Systemic Metabolic Dysfunction (cert 0.38, speculation env)  
**Research questions:**
1. Do LC-MS metabolomics reveal elevated primary-to-secondary bile acid ratios in ME/CFS stool/plasma vs controls?
2. Are TGR5-activating bile acids (UDCA, lithocholic acid) specifically reduced in ME/CFS metabolomics profiles?
3. Does UDCA supplementation in ME/CFS improve objective mitochondrial function measures (VO2max, NIRS oxygenation)?
4. Does bile acid pool composition correlate with PGC-1α expression in peripheral blood mononuclear cells?
