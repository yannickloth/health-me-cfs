### 7. gwas-meta-maccallini-2026

**DOI:** 10.21203/rs.3.rs-9702020/v1 | **Preprint:** Research Square v1 (Jun 2026) | **Certainty:** 0.80

**Paper:** Maccallini P. Biological Insights from GWAS and Whole Genome Sequencing of ME/CFS. Preprint (Jun 2026).

**Key findings:**
- Meta-analysis of DecodeME + Million Veteran Program GWAS (19,470 cases, 699,111 controls)
- Tissue enrichment: multiple brain regions + pituitary; no peripheral tissue significant; three survived Bonferroni-corrected replication
- Gene-set: neuronal/synaptic gene sets replicated; glutamatergic synapses as most specific replicated signal
- Cell-type: independent replicated signals in distinct neuronal populations of subcortical and cerebellar regions
- Convergent support from both common (GWAS) and rare (WGS) variant data
- Brain/synaptic role in ME/CFS pathogenesis, not peripheral/immune from this genetic lens

**Target chapters:** ch06, ch05, ch14h, ch21
**Relevance score:** mech:5 tx:1 expl:5 math:4 dx:3
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Glutamatergic synaptic dysfunction as genetically-driven core mechanism — dovetails with E/I imbalance (Wirth/Scheibenbogen); subcortical + cerebellar neuron populations as ME/CFS vulnerability loci; no peripheral tissue enrichment → brain-first genetic model; convergence of common + rare variant data strengthens causal inference
**Status:** ⬜
