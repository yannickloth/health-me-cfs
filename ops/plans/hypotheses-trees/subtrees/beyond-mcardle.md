## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠 High
**Category:** 10 — Rare diseases as mechanistic models

**ME/CFS links:**
- Muscle glycogen phosphorylase (PYGM) deficiency → exercise-induced fatigue, cramps, rhabdomyolysis
- Lactate non-rise on ischaemic exercise test — definitive diagnostic marker
- Second-wind phenomenon: absent in ME/CFS → suggests glycogenolysis failure is not the primary block (H6)
- Mechanistic comparator for ME/CFS energy failure at the muscle level
- Metabolic distinction from ME/CFS: McArdle has normal mitochondrial function; ME/CFS has impaired

**Target chapters:** ch04-08-differential-diagnosis, ch06-energy-metabolism, ch14d-cross-disease

**Pre-identified hypotheses:** H6 (McArdle as "perfect model" for ME/CFS energy failure — second-wind absent in ME/CFS distinguishes glycogenolysis failure from ME/CFS primary block)

**Research questions:**
1. Does the ischaemic forearm exercise test (lactate non-rise) distinguish ME/CFS from McArdle?
2. Is the second-wind phenomenon present in any ME/CFS subtype?
3. Does McArdle metabolic profile (normal mitochondria, glycolysis block) help localise ME/CFS energy failure?
4. What is the evidence for glycogen metabolism abnormalities in ME/CFS muscle?
5. Does vitamin B6 supplementation (used in McArdle, cofactor for PYGM) affect ME/CFS energy?
