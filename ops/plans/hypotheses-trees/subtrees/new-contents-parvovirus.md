## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟡

**ME/CFS links:**
- Parvovirus B19 confirmed as ME/CFS trigger via post-infection cohort epidemiological data yet systematically understudied relative to EBV
- B19 bone marrow tropism produces transient aplastic crisis; residual erythroid progenitor effects may sustain fatigue mechanistically
- Persistent B19 DNA in bone marrow and synovium documented; autoimmune phenomena via VP2 molecular mimicry against collagen and phospholipid
- Anti-phospholipid antibodies triggered by B19 provide a mechanistically distinct autoimmune subtype
- B19 often not tested in ME/CFS workup; serological interpretation complex (IgM wanes, IgG persists; avidity testing needed)

**Research questions:**

3. Do B19-triggered ME/CFS patients have lower reticulocyte counts and hemoglobin, consistent with residual erythroid compromise?
4. Do post-B19 ME/CFS patients show distinct immune profiles (elevated anti-VP2 T cell responses) vs post-EBV ME/CFS?
