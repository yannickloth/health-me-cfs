## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🔴  
**Tier:** 1  
**ME/CFS links:**
- Direct viral-mitochondrial protein interactions distinct from secondary metabolic effects of immune activation
- SARS-CoV-2 ORF9b targets TOM20 (mitochondrial translocase); inhibits mitochondrial protein import and suppresses interferon signaling
- EBV BHRF1 (Bcl-2 homologue) localizes to outer mitochondrial membrane; disrupts apoptosis and alters membrane potential
- CMV UL37 (vMIA) disrupts mitochondrial cristae structure and calcium signaling
- HHV-6 proteins localize to mitochondria; exploit host ATP for viral replication; leave residual mitochondrial dysfunction post-infection
- Provides direct mechanistic link between viral persistence (ch07) and metabolic dysfunction (ch06), filling integrated model gap

**Target chapters:** ch06-energy-metabolism.tex (new subsection "Viral Disruption of Mitochondrial Function"), ch07-immune-dysfunction.tex (viral persistence sections), ch13-integrative-models.tex  
**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- Persistent Viral Protein Expression Directly Impairing Mitochondrial Respiratory Chain Function (cert ~0.55): viral proteins from EBV, SARS-CoV-2, HHV-6, or CMV expressed during persistent or reactivating infection directly disrupt mitochondrial membrane potential and respiratory chain activity

**Research questions:**
1. Do ME/CFS patients with evidence of active EBV reactivation (elevated EA-D IgG) show lower mitochondrial membrane potential in PBMCs compared to non-reactivating patients?
2. Does ME/CFS PBMC transcriptomic data show upregulation of viral stress-response genes (CHOP, ATF4) in mitochondrial protein-encoding pathways?
3. Is ORF9b protein detectable in PBMCs of ME/CFS patients and does it correlate with reduced mitochondrial Complex I activity on Seahorse assay?
4. Do cells transfected with SARS-CoV-2 ORF9b show oxygen consumption deficit equivalent to ME/CFS patient PBMC measurements?
5. What is the temporal relationship between viral reactivation markers and decline in mitochondrial function in longitudinal ME/CFS cohorts?
