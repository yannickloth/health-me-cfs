## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🔴 Critical
**Category:** 2 — Neurological and psychiatric conditions

**ME/CFS links:**
- Autoimmune autonomic ganglionopathy (AAG): ganglionic AChR antibodies → autonomic failure
- Pure autonomic failure; multiple system atrophy (MSA) as structural end-stage
- GPCR autoantibodies shared with ME/CFS (ganglionic AChR same class as β-AR antibodies)
- AAG as a "pure" autonomic autoimmune disease — useful mechanistic model for ME/CFS autonomic dysfunction

**Target chapters:** ch04-08-differential-diagnosis, ch07-immune-dysfunction, ch14b-autoantibodies, ch14d-cross-disease

**Pre-identified hypotheses:** H2 (GPCR autoantibodies — ganglionic AChR antibodies in AAG as same class as ME/CFS GPCR antibodies)

**Research questions:**
1. Are ganglionic AChR antibodies found in ME/CFS cohorts?
2. Is AAG a subtype of ME/CFS or a distinct disease that produces ME/CFS phenotype?
3. Does IVIG or plasma exchange in AAG remit ME/CFS-like symptoms?
4. What distinguishes MSA from ME/CFS neurologically (alpha-synuclein, motor involvement)?
5. Does the GPCR autoantibody panel in ME/CFS include ganglionic AChR (not just β1/β2-AR and M2/M3)?

**Note:** Distinct from POTS subtypes (see `subtrees/beyond-pots-subtypes.md`). AAG → near-complete autonomic failure; POTS → partial/segmental autonomic dysfunction.
