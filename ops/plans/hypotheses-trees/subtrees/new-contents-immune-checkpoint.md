## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠  
**Tier:** 2  
**ME/CFS links:**
- T cell exhaustion (distinct from activation) results from chronic antigen exposure; characterized by progressive loss of effector functions and stable phenotype maintained by checkpoint receptors
- PD-1/PD-L1, CTLA-4, LAG-3, TIM-3 upregulation prevents T cell proliferation and cytokine production even when antigen persists
- Exhausted T cells cannot effectively clear latent EBV and HHV-6, perpetuating viral reactivation and immune activation feedback
- LAG-3 + PD-1 co-expression marks the most deeply dysfunctional T cell subset, indicating terminal exhaustion
- Functional consequence: inability to suppress viral reactivation → more immune activation → perpetuation of ME/CFS immune dysfunction cycle
- Note: cancer immunotherapy checkpoint blockade (anti-PD-1 drugs) is INAPPROPRIATE for ME/CFS; blockade would cause immune activation-induced PEM exacerbation, not improvement

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- "Chronic Antigen-Driven T Cell Exhaustion Impairing Viral Latency Control in ME/CFS" (cert 0.50)
  - Core: persistent viral antigens chronically stimulate T cells, driving upregulation of PD-1, LAG-3, TIM-3 → exhausted T cells unable to suppress viral reactivation → perpetuates viral reactivation-immune activation cycle
  - Testable: higher PD-1+ CD8+ T cells (especially EBV-specific tetramers) in ME/CFS vs healthy controls; PD-1+ exhaustion correlates with viral reactivation markers; PD-1+ LAG-3+ co-expressing T cells enriched in ME/CFS

**Research questions:**
1. Do ME/CFS patients show a higher proportion of PD-1+ CD8+ T cells (especially EBV-specific tetramers) compared to healthy EBV-seropositive controls?
2. Does the degree of PD-1+ T cell exhaustion correlate with viral reactivation markers (EA-D IgG for EBV; HHV-6 IgG avidity) in the same ME/CFS patients?
3. Are PD-1+ LAG-3+ co-expressing T cells enriched in ME/CFS compared to healthy controls, indicating deep exhaustion specifically?
4. Does in vitro anti-PD-L1 stimulation of ME/CFS patient T cells partially restore their ability to lyse EBV-transformed B cells compared to untreated ME/CFS T cells?
5. How do the exhaustion phenotypes in ME/CFS compare to those in chronic viral infections (HIV, HCV, CMV) in terms of reversibility and therapeutic targets?
