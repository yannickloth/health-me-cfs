## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟡  
**Tier:** 3  
**ME/CFS links:**
- Gut bacteria directly regulate the indole pathway and IDO1 upregulation, controlling tryptophan fate (serotonin vs kynurenine shunt)
- Dysbiosis reduces bacterial indole pathway products (IPA), removing protection against IDO1 activation and neuroinflammation
- Gut inflammation upregulates IDO1 locally, creating a tryptophan-depleted, kynurenine-rich local environment
- Serotonin-melatonin cascade disruption from tryptophan depletion explains sleep pathology origin at the gut level
- Distinguishes gut IDO1 activation from systemic IDO1, providing mechanistic specificity for targeted interventions

**Target chapters:** ch11 (gut microbiome), ch15 (symptom-producing mechanisms / kynurenine pathway)  
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Gut Bacterial Dysbiosis Driving Tryptophan Diversion to the Kynurenine Pathway (cert 0.50)  
**Research questions:**
1. Are IPA levels reduced in ME/CFS patient plasma/urine and do they correlate with dysbiosis severity?
2. Is gut biopsy IDO1 protein expression elevated in ME/CFS patients with active gut inflammation?
3. Does probiotic supplementation restoring Lactobacillus reduce plasma kynurenine/tryptophan ratio and improve sleep?
4. Do ME/CFS patients with low IPA show worse neurological symptoms than those with normal IPA?
