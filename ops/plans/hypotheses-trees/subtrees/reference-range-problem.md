## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠 medium — clinically important framing for diagnostic gaps; not pathophysiology per se
**ME/CFS links:**
- Iron deficiency without anemia (IDWA): fatigue reduction at ferritin <50 µg/L (3× WHO threshold); most labs flag only <12–15 µg/L; RCT evidence (Vaucher2012, Krayenbuehl2011, Yokoi2017 meta-analysis)
- Magnesium: 99% intracellular; serum has "no reliable correlation with total body magnesium" (Workinger2018); RBC magnesium is a better proxy but not standardised across labs
- B12 functional deficiency in CFS: elevated methylmalonic acid with normal/elevated serum B12 ("paradoxical B12 deficiency" — RussellJones2022); note low-tier journal, use cautiously
- Vitamin D: bone-health threshold ≥20 ng/mL vs. immune-optimisation threshold 40–60 ng/mL; gene expression differences begin above 30 ng/mL (Charoenngam2020, Holick2011 guideline)
- DIO2 Thr92Ala variant: intracellular T4→T3 conversion impaired; measurably worse psychological wellbeing on T4-only despite normal TSH (Panicker2009, n=552)
- Low T3 syndrome in ME/CFS: FT3 below reference in 16/98 patients vs. 7/99 controls; TSH normal in all (RuizNunez2018); pattern of non-thyroidal illness syndrome
- Hashimoto's euthyroid fatigue: autoimmune activity (anti-TPO >121 IU/mL) drives fatigue independent of thyroid function; TSH identical between groups (Ott2011, n=426)
- Counterweight: Joustra2017 systematic review (45 studies, 27 in meta-analyses) found no consistent serum-level differences between CFS/FMS patients and controls — but those studies used the same inadequate serum assays
**Target chapters:** Diagnostic workup chapter; treatment chapter (nutritional correction protocols); comorbidities chapter (Hashimoto, thyroid, IDWA)
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Standard serum assays encode population distributions, not functional thresholds; intracellular deficiencies (Mg, B12, iron) are systematically invisible to standard panels; DIO2 variants create tissue-level T3 deficiency invisible to TSH screening; immune-function vitamin D threshold is 2–3× the bone-health threshold used in clinical labs
**Research questions:**
1. What ferritin threshold should trigger iron supplementation trials in ME/CFS patients?
2. Does RBC magnesium testing identify a subgroup of ME/CFS patients who respond to magnesium supplementation?
3. Is DIO2 Thr92Ala genotyping warranted in ME/CFS patients with normal TSH and persistent fatigue?
4. Should ME/CFS patients be screened for IDWA at ferritin <50 µg/L rather than WHO <15 µg/L?
