## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠 medium — 48% SIBO prevalence in referred ME/CFS cohort (Karhu2023); significant gap in document's existing gut chapter
**ME/CFS links:**
- SIBO prevalence: 48% in referred ME/CFS patients (n=479, Karhu2023 ACG abstract; retrospective referral bias applies)
- H2S–Complex IV link: H2S (biphasic) inhibits cytochrome c oxidase at cytochrome a3 prosthetic group — same mechanism as cyanide; colonocytes normally clear H2S via SQOR, but barrier failure exposes systemic circulation (Szabo2014H2Smito, Nicholls2013sulfideCOX)
- MMC dysfunction: vagal autonomic dysfunction + enteric neuropathy impairs migrating motor complex → bacterial accumulation; prokinetics (low-dose nocturnal erythromycin, prucalopride) extend symptom-free interval after eradication (Pimentel2009)
- Rifaximin evidence base: phase 3 RCTs (TARGET 1+2, n~1200, NEJM 2011) in IBS-D — proxy evidence for SIBO eradication; no ME/CFS-specific RCT
- Nutrient malabsorption: SIBO causes B12 deficiency (bacterial consumption), fat-soluble vitamin depletion (bile salt deconjugation), iron malabsorption (mucosal inflammation) — ACG guideline (Pimentel2020)
- Critical negative: Kashyap2024 (ESNM/ANMS) — lactulose breath test measures oro-cecal transit, not bacterial overgrowth; "after two decades the [SIBO-IBS] hypothesis remains unproven"; raises antimicrobial stewardship concerns
**Target chapters:** ch11 (gut microbiome) — SIBO prevalence, H2S mechanism, MMC pathophysiology; ch06 (mitochondrial dysfunction) — H2S-Complex IV link; ch18 (treatments) — rifaximin, prokinetics, dietary management
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** ME/CFS autonomic dysfunction (vagal) impairs MMC → SIBO in a significant subgroup; SIBO-derived H2S adds to mitochondrial Complex IV inhibition; rifaximin + prokinetic protocol for SIBO-positive ME/CFS subgroup; nutrient malabsorption from SIBO explains some ME/CFS nutritional deficiencies
**Research questions:**
1. What is the true SIBO prevalence in ME/CFS using validated diagnostic criteria (glucose H2 breath test rather than lactulose)?
2. Does SIBO eradication improve ME/CFS symptoms and objective measures (PEM threshold, cognitive function)?
3. Does MMC dysfunction severity correlate with SIBO recurrence in ME/CFS?
4. Does SIBO treatment reduce H2S breath content and improve mitochondrial function markers in ME/CFS?
