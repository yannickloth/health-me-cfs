## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠 medium — strong evidence for shared immune profiles; meningitis susceptibility extrapolation is speculative
**ME/CFS links:**
- Long COVID and ME/CFS share comparable immune profiles: lower lymphocytes, CD8+ T cells, NK cells; higher IL-6, TNF, IL-4, IL-10; no meaningful differences between patient groups (Petrov2025, n=190: 65 ME/CFS, 54 Long COVID, 71 controls)
- CD8 T-cell dysfunction shared: reduced IFN-γ and TNF-α capacity in both ME/CFS and Long COVID (Gil2024); nebulized antioxidant improved function and reduced symptoms ~54%
- NK cell cytotoxicity in ME/CFS: ~50% of healthy control levels (Baraniuk2024 meta-analysis of 28 studies); multi-site study (Querec2023) contradicts — assay standardisation issue
- T-cell exhaustion in COVID-19 and Long COVID: terminal dysfunction with PD-1/TIM-3/CTLA-4 expression; impaired proliferation and cytokine production (Chen-Camano2025 review)
- Persistent lymphopenia in 7–12% of moderate-severe COVID-19 survivors affecting B cells, CD4+ T, and Tregs (An2025, n=279 longitudinal)
- COVID does NOT cause measles-like immune amnesia: pre-existing toxoid vaccine antibodies stable post-COVID (Jokiranta2023, n=59); but immune imprinting constrains future SARS-CoV-2 variant responses
- Complement dysregulation: terminal complement activation in Long COVID (Cervia-Hasler2024, Science); post-exercise complement protein changes in ME/CFS (Glass2025); 23% of chronic meningococcal cases have complement deficiencies (Schulz2025)
- Endothelial senescence link: Nunes2026 cited in this report (cross-reference to endothelial-senescence subtree)
- ME/CFS patients show stronger herpesvirus reactivation after even mild COVID-19 vs. healthy controls (Apostolou2022)
**Target chapters:** ch07 (immune dysfunction) — shared profiles, NK dysfunction; ch10 (cardiovascular) — endothelial senescence; ch13 (integrative models) — Long COVID/ME/CFS convergence; context for vaccine-efficacy section
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Long COVID and ME/CFS as immunologically equivalent post-infectious states; COVID immune imprinting as distinct from immune amnesia; complement dysregulation (not deficiency) as plausible contributor to increased pathogen susceptibility; NK dysfunction + T cell exhaustion as shared perpetuating mechanism; pre-existing ME/CFS may amplify COVID-induced immune dysfunction (Apostolou2022 viral reactivation finding)
**Research questions:**
1. Do pre-existing ME/CFS patients show worse immune dysfunction after COVID-19 than de novo long COVID?
2. Does complement dysregulation in ME/CFS translate to measurable deficits in meningococcal or other pathogen defense?
3. Can vaccine immunogenicity be measured in ME/CFS patients — do NK and T cell dysfunction impair response?
4. Does the TRPM3 ion channel dysfunction in Long COVID NK cells (Sasso2025) match the previously described ME/CFS NK cell TRPM3 dysfunction?
