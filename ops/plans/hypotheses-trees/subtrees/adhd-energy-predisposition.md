## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠 medium — epidemiological overlap + mechanistic plausibility; no direct causal evidence
**ME/CFS links:**
- ADHD traits predict 2× risk of chronic disabling fatigue (ALSPAC longitudinal; OR=2.18 for ADHD, OR=1.78 for ASD)
- IL-6 mediates ADHD traits → adolescent fatigue trajectory (Quadt 2024)
- Shared prefrontal hypometabolism: ADHD baseline CBF deficit (hypoperfusion in orbitofrontal, basal ganglia) converges with ME/CFS cerebral hypoperfusion
- Shared BH4 bottleneck: GCH1 variants → reduced BH4 → impaired catecholamine synthesis; BH4 already implicated in ME/CFS orthostatic intolerance
- 29.7% of adult CFS patients had childhood ADHD (SaezFrancas2012); 20.9% persisted to adulthood
- Shared mitochondrial dysfunction: ADHD shows decreased mitochondrial membrane potential, impaired OXPHOS (Almutairi2024); dopaminergic depletion + neuroinflammation co-imaged (Yokokura2021)
**Target chapters:** Neurodivergence comorbidities chapter; catecholamine/prefrontal hypometabolism chapter; stimulant treatment chapter; lower-baseline predisposition framework
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** ADHD metabolic reserve deficit lowers threshold for post-infectious ME/CFS; shared BH4 bottleneck; adult-onset ADHD as acquired catecholaminergic depletion (post-infectious trajectory)
**Research questions:**
1. Does childhood-onset ADHD (with documented metabolic reserve deficit) specifically predispose to ME/CFS, distinct from adult-onset ADHD?
2. Is the GCH1 rs841 variant a shared genetic predisposition for ADHD, ME/CFS, and orthostatic intolerance via BH4 depletion?
3. Do stimulant responders in ME/CFS show pre-existing ADHD neurobiology vs. acquired dopaminergic depletion?
4. Does prefrontal hypoperfusion precede ME/CFS onset in ADHD patients?
