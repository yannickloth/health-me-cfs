**ME/CFS links:**
- Oxaloacetate replenishes depleted Krebs cycle intermediates, directly targeting the PDH block and Krebs cycle depletion in ME/CFS
- NAD+/NADH ratio regeneration via malate dehydrogenase reaction bypasses blocked Complex I without requiring mitochondrial electron transport
- Anti-inflammatory properties via α-ketoglutarate reduction (substrate for HIF-1α and inflammatory epigenetic enzymes)
- Glutamate excitotoxicity reduction via transamination bridges metabolic and neurological treatment
- Limited clinical data (single small trial); OAA bioavailability after oral administration uncertain; mechanism is biochemically plausible but clinical benefit not established
