## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🔴  
**Tier:** 1  
**ME/CFS links:**
- 67% ME/CFS-specific SFN prevalence (Levine et al. 2024) vs fibromyalgia extrapolation previously used
- Autonomic vs sensory SFN distinction explains why many ME/CFS patients lack classic neuropathic pain despite SFN on biopsy
- Specific autoimmune SFN subtype with anti-TS-HDS and anti-FGFR3 antibodies represents a potentially treatable subset
- Mechanism connecting autonomic SFN to POTS and temperature dysregulation (postganglionic fiber damage)
- IVIG evidence for autoimmune SFN provides targeted treatment pathway for defined subgroup

**Target chapters:** ch08-neurological.tex (Skin Biopsy Findings ~line 862), ch08 (new Autonomic vs Sensory subsubsection ~line 873), ch08 (Potential Causes of SFN ~line 894), ch08 (Autoimmune SFN ~line 990), ch10-cardiovascular.tex (POTS Subtypes ~line 465)  
**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- Autoimmune Small Fiber Neuropathy as a Distinct ME/CFS Subtype (cert ~0.65): core claim that subgroup of ME/CFS patients have SFN driven by anti-TS-HDS or anti-FGFR3 antibodies with specific diagnostic markers and potentially treatable cause

**Research questions:**
1. What is the true prevalence of autoimmune-antibody-positive SFN in ME/CFS cohorts, and can anti-TS-HDS/FGFR3 testing be standardized for routine diagnostic use?
2. Does IVIG demonstrate efficacy in ME/CFS-specific autoimmune SFN populations, and can response be predicted by antibody serology?
3. How frequently does autonomic SFN (without prominent pain) account for dysautonomia and POTS in ME/CFS, and can this subtype be distinguished from other POTS mechanisms?
4. What is the relationship between IENFD on skin biopsy and ME/CFS symptom severity, particularly for sensory vs autonomic manifestations?
5. Can molecular markers of autoimmune SFN (anti-TS-HDS, anti-FGFR3) predict treatment response to IVIG or other immunotherapies?
