## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟡 low-medium — speculation-level ME/CFS application; base pharmacology is established; OTC availability makes it practically relevant
**ME/CFS links:**
- Ambroxol (OTC mucolytic, active metabolite of bromhexine) has dual pain mechanism: (1) Nav1.8 preferential block (TTX-resistant sodium channel in small-diameter nociceptors, Gaida2005); (2) direct TRPV1 antagonism demonstrated by whole-cell patch-clamp on human TRPV1 channels (Hefner2025)
- If TRPV1 sensitization contributes to ME/CFS pain and chemical hypersensitivity (expanded TRP channelopathy hypothesis in ch14h), ambroxol's TRPV1 antagonism could reduce sensitization-driven nociceptor activation
- Also modulates TRPA1 at high concentrations (Hefner2025) — relevant to oxidative-stress-driven TRPA1 activation proposed in ME/CFS
- Preclinical: efficacy in rat neuropathic and inflammatory pain exceeds gabapentin at clinically achievable doses; Nav1.8 species gap (rat IC50 18 µM vs human IC50 279 µM) — rodent data may overestimate human Nav1.8 effect; hTRPV1 data more directly translatable
- Clinical case series: pain reduction in trigeminal neuralgia, fibromyalgia, CRPS at 75–300 mg/day; no serious adverse effects (Russo2023); no ME/CFS-specific trials
- Available OTC in Belgium (Mucosolvan 30 mg); above 75 mg/day is off-label
**Target chapters:** ch14h (TRPM3 channelopathy) — speculation environment after expanded TRP hypothesis block, as direct instantiation of the testable prediction for TRPV1 antagonist in ME/CFS
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Ambroxol as repurposed TRP channel modulator in ME/CFS pain/MCS subgroup; TRPV1 antagonism as targeted intervention for chemical hypersensitivity; Nav1.8 block as adjunct for small-fibre neuropathic pain in ME/CFS
**Research questions:**
1. Do ME/CFS patients with MCS features and elevated pain scores show measurable pain reduction with oral ambroxol 75–150 mg/day?
2. Does pain response correlate with capsaicin flare-threshold improvement (TRPV1-specific endpoint)?
3. What systemic ambroxol concentration is needed for meaningful hTRPV1 antagonism — achievable at clinical doses?
4. Does TRPA1 modulation at higher ambroxol doses reduce oxidative-stress-triggered symptom exacerbation?
