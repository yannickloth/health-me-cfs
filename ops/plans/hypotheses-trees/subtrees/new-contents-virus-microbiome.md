## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟡  
**Tier:** 3  
**ME/CFS links:**
- Acute viral infection alters mucosal immune tone (IFN-γ, TNF-α) creating selective pressure against beneficial bacteria
- SARS-CoV-2 acute dysbiosis well-characterized; some taxa fail to recover at 6 months despite clinical resolution
- Persistent viral infection maintains dysbiotic state through ongoing low-grade mucosal inflammation and immune activation
- Bidirectional viral-dysbiosis maintenance: viral persistence → dysbiosis; dysbiosis → impaired antiviral defence
- Antibiotic confounding substantial; must distinguish viral effects from antibiotic effects; respiratory viruses produce indirect effects via systemic inflammation

**Target chapters:** ch11 (gut microbiome), ch07 (immune dysfunction / viral persistence)  
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Persistent Viral Gut Infection as the Primary Mechanism Sustaining ME/CFS Dysbiosis (cert 0.45)  
**Research questions:**
1. Do ME/CFS patients with detectable gut viral RNA at 6+ months show significantly greater dysbiosis than those without viral persistence?
2. Does antiviral treatment eliminating gut viral persistence lead to microbiome diversity recovery better than probiotic supplementation alone?
3. Do gut biopsy mucosal cytokine levels (IFN-γ, TNF-α) inversely correlate with Faecalibacterium abundance in ME/CFS?
4. Do ME/CFS onset after antibiotic-treated bacterial infections show different dysbiosis patterns than post-viral ME/CFS?
