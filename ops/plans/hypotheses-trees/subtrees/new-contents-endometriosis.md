## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟠  
**Tier:** 2  
**ME/CFS links:**
- Endometriosis prevalence in reproductive-age women (~10% population) is substantially elevated in ME/CFS patients (20–40% estimated from patient registries)
- Both conditions feature macrophage hyperactivation and pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-1β, TNF-α, IL-6); NK cell dysfunction documented in both
- Estrogen-immune axis: estrogen activates immune cells and promotes peritoneal inflammation in endometriosis, paralleling estrogen's role in ME/CFS symptom severity (premenstrual worsening)
- Central sensitisation (heightened pain processing in CNS) shared between endometriosis and ME/CFS; both co-occur with fibromyalgia in triad
- Diagnostic delay parallels are striking: endometriosis average 7–8 years to diagnosis, ME/CFS 5–7 years; both dismissed as psychosomatic; both predominantly affect women
- Clinical implications: undiagnosed endometriosis in ME/CFS women worsens fatigue/pain burden; hormone treatment for endometriosis may affect ME/CFS symptoms; pelvic pain evaluation should be standard in women with ME/CFS

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- "Shared Immune Susceptibility Underlying ME/CFS and Endometriosis Comorbidity" (cert 0.35 — speculation level)
  - Core: shared immune regulatory deficit (NK cell dysfunction, macrophage hyperactivation, Treg impairment) allows both conditions to establish/persist; estrogen-immune axis may be common driver
  - Testable: women with both conditions show lower NK cytotoxicity than single-condition or control groups; endometriosis women with ME/CFS show broader autoantibody profiles; hormonal endometriosis suppression improves ME/CFS fatigue; ME/CFS severity in endometriosis women follows menstrual cycle more tightly

**Research questions:**
1. Do women with both ME/CFS and endometriosis show lower NK cell cytotoxicity compared to women with single condition or healthy controls?
2. Do ME/CFS patients with endometriosis show higher anti-endometrial antibody titres and broader autoantibody profiles than ME/CFS patients without endometriosis?
3. Does hormonal suppression of endometriosis (GnRH analogues) lead to measurable improvement in ME/CFS fatigue scores in women with confirmed co-occurrence?
4. Does ME/CFS severity in women with endometriosis co-occurrence follow the menstrual cycle more tightly than in ME/CFS women without endometriosis?
5. Is the shared immune defect (NK dysfunction, macrophage activation, estrogen sensitivity) a heritable trait predisposing to both conditions?
