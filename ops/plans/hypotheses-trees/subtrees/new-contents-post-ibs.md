## Integration Context

**Priority:** 🟡  
**Tier:** 3  
**ME/CFS links:**
- PI-IBS occurs in ~10–15% of GI infection survivors; shares risk factors with ME/CFS (female sex, infection severity, psychological distress)
- Mucosal immune changes in PI-IBS (elevated mast cells, enterochromaffin cells, CD3+ T cells) match ME/CFS gut pathology
- Gut-brain signalling alterations in PI-IBS (ENS remodelling, visceral hypersensitivity, serotonin dysregulation) produce systemic neuroimmune dysfunction
- Enteroendocrine cell damage from acute infection incompletely recovers; GLP-1, PYY, serotonin depletion sustains abnormal signalling
- Walkerton cohort showed CFS-like features in PI-IBS subgroup; PI-IBS is identifiable intermediate state before ME/CFS diagnosis

**Target chapters:** ch11 (gut microbiome), ch05 (disease course), ch04 (comorbidities / diagnostic criteria)  
**Pre-identified hypotheses:** Post-Infectious IBS as an Intermediate State in GI-Infection-Triggered ME/CFS (cert 0.40)  
**Research questions:**
1. Do PI-IBS patients followed prospectively show ME/CFS incidence significantly higher than GI-infection survivors without PI-IBS?
2. Do PI-IBS patients progressing to ME/CFS show more severe mucosal mast cell and enterochromaffin cell changes than PI-IBS patients who recover?
3. Do PI-IBS patients show reduced heart rate variability compared to healthy controls, indicating gut-derived autonomic dysregulation?
4. Does treatment targeting mucosal immune activation in PI-IBS reduce subsequent ME/CFS development?
