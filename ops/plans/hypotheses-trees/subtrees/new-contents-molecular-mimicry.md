**Priority:** 🔴

**ME/CFS links:**
- EBV EBNA-1 cross-reactivity with human proteins (GlialCAM documented in MS literature; implications for ME/CFS CNS autoimmunity)
- Potential GPCR receptor cross-reactivity explains why GPCR autoantibodies already documented in ME/CFS could originate from viral triggers
- Coxsackievirus B capsid (VP2) homology with cardiac myosin provides link to myocarditis and cardiomyopathy risk
- Different viral triggers (EBV, CV-B, enterovirus) can generate same autoimmune outcome via shared epitope homologies—explains "trigger agnosticism" in autoimmune ME/CFS
- Latent EBV continuously expressing EBNA-1 maintains molecular mimicry stimulus indefinitely

**Target chapters:** ch07-immune-dysfunction.tex (§Molecular Mimicry ~line 1273), ch07 (§GPCR Autoantibodies ~line 1262)  
**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- EBV EBNA-1 Molecular Mimicry as a Trigger for Autoimmune ME/CFS (cert ~0.45): cross-reactive antibodies generated against EBV EBNA-1 during primary infection target structurally similar human proteins including neuronal antigens and potentially GPCR epitopes, establishing persistent autoimmunity

**Research questions:**
1. Do ME/CFS patients with EBV-triggered illness have higher rates of anti-GPCR antibodies compared to ME/CFS patients with non-EBV triggers?
2. Does serum from EBV-triggered ME/CFS patients show cross-reactivity between anti-EBV antibodies and human GPCR or neuronal antigen peptides in competitive inhibition assays?
3. Can specific EBNA-1 epitopes be identified that cross-react with human GPCR extracellular domains?
4. Do ME/CFS patients with high anti-EBNA-1 titre have more severe autonomic symptoms (POTS, HRV abnormalities) than those with low titre?
5. Can removing EBNA-1-reactive antibodies via immunoadsorption reduce GPCR autoantibody titre in EBV-triggered ME/CFS patients?
