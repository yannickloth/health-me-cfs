# Lithium as Pro-Resolving Mediator (SPM Enhancement) — Integration Plan

**Purpose:** Integrate the hypothesis that ultra-low-dose lithium enhances specialized pro-resolving mediator (SPM) biosynthesis — resolvins, protectins, maresins — shifting the cytokine profile from pro-inflammatory to pro-resolving, providing a mechanism for Toricelli 2021's observed IL-10↑ / IL-6↓ / IL-1α↓ profile at microdose concentrations.

> **Safety notice:** Lithium has a narrow therapeutic index. This plan explores an ultra-low-dose hypothesis (nM-µM range), NOT standard therapeutic dosing (0.6-1.2 mM). Any clinical application requires serum level monitoring, renal/thyroid function assessment, and medical supervision. This document does not constitute clinical advice.

## Notes

- Parent cert 0.15 — grounded in: (a) zero direct studies measuring Li⁺ effect on SPMs, (b) single inferential cytokine study (Toricelli 2021, did not measure SPMs), (c) plausible but untested GPCR conjecture
- Toricelli 2021 cytokine profile is the ONLY direct evidence (IL-10↑, IL-6↓, IL-1α↓ at 2-20 µM Li)
- No study has tested lithium's effect on SPM biosynthesis
- SPMs act through high-affinity GPCRs (ChemR23, GPR32, ALX/FPR2) at nM-pM concentrations — consistent with Li⁺ at nM-µM, but Li⁺ has NOT been shown to activate these receptors directly
- Resolution biology (SPMs) is an active, programmed process, not passive inflammation decay
- Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA/DHA) are resolvin precursors — existing supplements in ME/CFS
- Existing content covers aspirin-triggered resolvins (AT-SPMs) in `ch14a-core-mechanistic` — potential cross-ref to section on resolution pathways

## Formalized Predictions (Falsifiable Form)

### Hypothesis 1 — SPM biosynthesis enhancement
**Claim:** Li⁺ at 1-20 µM increases resolvin D1, protectin D1, or maresin 1 concentration in LPS-stimulated microglial culture supernatant at 24-72h.

**Falsified by:** No significant increase in ≥1 measured SPM (LC-MS/MS) across triplicate experiments at ≥2 Li⁺ concentrations.

### Hypothesis 2 — Microglial phenotype shift
**Claim:** 1-20 µM Li⁺ shifts LPS-stimulated microglial gene expression toward pro-resolving markers (IL-10↑, TGF-β↑, CD206↑, Arg1↑) with no change or decrease in pro-inflammatory markers (IL-6, IL-1β, TNF, iNOS).

**Falsified by:** No significant shift in ≥2 pro-resolving markers, or increase in ≥1 pro-inflammatory marker.

### Hypothesis 3 — Delayed-onset kinetics
**Claim:** In lithium-responder ME/CFS cases, serum IL-10 rises above baseline by day 5-7 of microdose lithium initiation, preceding subjective symptom improvement by 3-7 days.

**Falsified by:** No IL-10 rise by day 14 in ≥2 cases with available serum samples.
