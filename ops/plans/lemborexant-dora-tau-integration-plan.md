(2025, *Nature Neuroscience*) finding that dual orexin receptor antagonists (DORAs), specifically lemborexant, prevent tau-mediated neurodegeneration via PKA-mediated tau phosphorylation — establishing a production-prevention mechanism distinct from glymphatic clearance.

## Notes

- Citation key needed: `@Parhizkar2025LemborexantTau` (Nature Neuroscience, May 2025)
- Key mechanism: orexin receptor antagonism → reduced PKA activity → reduced tau phosphorylation at Ser202/409, Thr205
- Key distinction: zolpidem increased sleep but did NOT protect — orexin signaling specificity, not sleep duration
- Active-phase dosing more effective than inactive-phase dosing → hyperarousal reduction > sleep enhancement
- Gender dimorphism: effects seen only in male mice (females had milder baseline neurodegeneration)
- Existing ME/CFS paper already discusses orexin deficiency (ch15:58-108), zolpidem glymphatic warning (ch15:549-560), and DORAs for sleep (ch14:286)
- No ME/CFS-specific data — all extrapolation from tauopathy mouse model
