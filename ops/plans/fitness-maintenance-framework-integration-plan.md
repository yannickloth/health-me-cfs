# Integration Plan: Fitness-Maintenance Conceptual Framework

**Purpose:** Add a dedicated conceptual section distinguishing "fitness maintenance" from "fitness improvement" in ME/CFS — what it means to remain "truly fit" when exercise capacity cannot be increased. Anchored in the energy envelope theory and 2-day CPET evidence.

**Target chapters:** ch17-lifestyle-interventions, ch02-core-symptoms, possibly ch19-integrative-approaches
**Parent topic:** exercise-modality-expansion

## Pre-identified Hypotheses

| Hypothesis | Preliminary certainty |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Fitness maintenance (preventing further loss) is a distinct goal from fitness improvement (building capacity) | 0.60 — strongly grounded in energy envelope theory, CPET evidence |
| The "energy envelope expansion" observed with strict pacing represents natural recovery, not training adaptation | 0.35 — anecdotal patient reports; no controlled study |
| ME/CFS patients who strictly pace show slower functional decline than those who don't | 0.30 — correlational surveys; no prospective RCT |
| "Fitness" in ME/CFS context should be redefined as: functional capacity within the envelope, not absolute VO₂max | 0.40 — semantical/philosophical; no empirical test |
