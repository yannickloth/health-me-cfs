# ME/CFS Open Data Sources — Annotated Reference

Compiled: 2026-03-27

Comprehensive inventory of open data services, registries, biobanks, and research databases
containing ME/CFS data worldwide. Each entry annotated with data type, access level, and
ME/CFS specificity.

**Access level legend:**
- 🟢 Fully open (no registration or approval needed)
- 🔵 Registration required (free account)
- 🟡 Application required (ethical approval, institutional affiliation, or committee review)
- 🔴 Collaborative only (direct contact with investigators)

## 1. Nordic / Scandinavian

### 1.1 Sweden

**Swedish National Patient Register (Socialstyrelsen)**
- URL: https://www.socialstyrelsen.se/en/statistics-and-data/registers/national-patient-register/
- Access: 🟡 Ethical approval + application (SEK 1,200/hr processing)
- Data: Inpatient stays since 1964 (nationwide from 1987), outpatient specialist since 2001. ICD-10-SE coding; ME/CFS under G93.3. Excludes primary care.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — ME/CFS identifiable via G93.3 within population-level registry
- Notes: ~40,000 estimated ME/CFS cases in Sweden (RME). Order via bestalladata.socialstyrelsen.se

**Swedish Twin Registry (Karolinska Institutet)**
- URL: https://ki.se/en
- Access: 🟡 Application to Karolinska + ethical approval
- Data: 31,406 twins screened for fatiguing illness (ages 42–64). CFS-like illness prevalence 2.36%. Heritability and environmental contribution data.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — CFS-like illness subset within twin cohort

**Biobank Sweden**
- URL: https://biobanksverige.se/en/
- Access: 🟡 Application via Research Guide + ethical approval
- Data: ~150 million samples across Swedish biobanks. Linkable to National Patient Register via G93.3.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — samples identifiable through register linkage

**Open Medicine Foundation — Uppsala Collaborative Center**
- URL: https://www.omf.ngo/collaborative-research-center-uppsala/
- Access: 🔴 Collaborative (contact OMF)
- Data: CSF neurochemical analysis, metabolomics, proteomics signatures. Led by Jonas Bergquist (Uppsala University).
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes — focused on ME/CFS biomarker discovery

**Bragee ME Center Stockholm (historical)**
- Access: 🔴 Collaborative (via Karolinska Institutet)
- Data: ~1,600 ME/CFS patients (Canadian Consensus Criteria). MED Study data on cervical nerve findings. Clinic closed late 2023; research data via Karolinska.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

### 1.2 Norway

**Norwegian Patient Registry (NPR) via Helsedata**
- URL: https://helsedata.no/en/
- Access: 🟡 Two pathways: (1) identified data requires ethical approval via NIPH, (2) aggregated/anonymous data more readily available
- Data: Nationwide specialist diagnoses. Published: 5,556 new ME/CFS cases 2016–2018, incidence 36.1/100k person-years. Socioeconomic and demographic analyses available.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — ME/CFS via ICD-10 G93.3; strongest Nordic ME/CFS publication record
- Notes: Norway drafted new 2026 ME/CFS guidelines

**Biobank Norway (BBMRI.no)**
- URL: https://bbmri.no/health-data
- Access: 🟡 Application through member biobanks
- Data: Network of Norwegian biobanks with linkage to health registries.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — linkable to NPR diagnoses

**Fluge/Mella Research Group (Haukeland University Hospital, Bergen)**
- Access: 🔴 Published in open-access journals; raw trial data via direct collaboration
- Data: RituxME (n=152, rituximab phase III), CycloME (n=40, cyclophosphamide), daratumumab pilot (n=10). Proteomics profiling of circulating proteome (2026).
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes — major ME/CFS treatment trial datasets

### 1.3 Denmark

**Danish National Patient Registry (Landspatientregisteret)**
- URL: https://english.sundhedsdatastyrelsen.dk/health-data-and-registers/national-health-registers
- Access: 🟡 Requires Danish institutional affiliation (foreign researchers need Danish partner). Secure Research Platform.
- Data: 40+ years hospital data. ICD-10 + Danish extensions. Personal ID enables cross-registry linkage. ME/CFS prevalence ~0.2–0.4% (~15,000 individuals). QoL data: EQ-5D-3L mean 0.47 vs population 0.85.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — ME/CFS via ICD codes

**Danish Research Health Data Gateway**
- URL: https://www.danishhealthdata.com/
- Access: 🟢 Metadata browsing open; 🟡 actual data requires application
- Data: Nationwide catalogue of Danish health register data with Metadata Map for searching/comparing sources.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — discovery tool for Danish health data

### 1.4 Finland

**Findata (Finnish Social and Health Data Permit Authority)**
- URL: https://findata.fi/en/data/
- Access: 🟡 Application to Findata; must analyze in secure Kapseli environment
- Data: Centralized access to Finnish social and health register data: patient care events, lab data, medications, causes of death, vaccinations. Data from Kanta Services.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — query by ICD-10 codes

**THL Biobank (Finnish Institute for Health and Welfare)**
- URL: https://thl.fi/en/research-and-development/thl-biobank/
- Access: 🟡 Application to THL + separate Findata permission for register linkage
- Data: Biological samples and survey data from FINRISK, Health 2000/2011, FinHealth cohorts. Pre-processed disease-status data from national registers.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — register linkage can identify relevant samples

**FINBB (Finnish Biobank Cooperative)**
- URL: https://finbb.fi/en/
- Access: 🟡 Single application point for 8 Finnish biobanks
- Data: Centralized access to 6 regional hospital biobanks, Arctic Biobank, THL Biobank.
- ME/CFS-specific: No

**FinnGen GWAS Summary Statistics (Postviral Fatigue Syndrome)**
- URL: https://www.finngen.fi/en/access_results
- Access: 🟢 Freely downloadable
- Data: GWAS summary statistics for G93.3/8E49 phenotype. Pan-UKBB + FinnGen meta-analysis results for 867 phenotypes.
- ME/CFS-specific: Contains CFS/postviral fatigue phenotype

### 1.5 Iceland

- Access: 🟢 Published data only
- Data: Prevalence study (Lindal et al.): 4,000 randomly selected, CFS prevalence 2.1% (CDC-1994) / 7.6% (Australian criteria). Now included in ME/CFS Research Register (mrr.mecfs-research.org).
- ME/CFS-specific: Limited — no dedicated registry or biobank
- Notes: Patient advocacy group engaged with Medical Director of Health

### 1.6 Nordic Cross-Border

**NordForsk / Nordic Biobank Network (NBN)**
- URL: https://www.nordforsk.org/projects/nordic-biobank-network-nbn
- Access: 🟡 Country-by-country ethical approval required (can take months to years)
- Data: Collaborative infrastructure across Denmark, Faroe Islands, Iceland, Finland, Norway, Sweden. Expanding Fingenious platform to Nordic-wide service.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — infrastructure for cross-Nordic registry studies

## 2. United Kingdom

**DecodeME (University of Edinburgh)**
- URL: https://institute-genetics-cancer.ed.ac.uk/decodeme
- Access: 🟡 Managed access via Data Access Committee; summary statistics will be 🟢 openly published
- Data: World's largest ME/CFS GWAS: 21,620 recruited, 15,579 cases + 259,909 controls (European ancestry). 8 genetic signals identified (immune + nervous systems). Phenotype data, DNA, saliva samples. 86% consented to data sharing. At least 4 external studies already using data.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**UK ME/CFS Biobank (UKMEB)**
- URL: https://cureme.lshtm.ac.uk/
- Access: 🟡 Ethics + peer review required (Human Tissue Act 2004 compliant)
- Data: 30,000+ blood aliquots from 600+ donors (ME/CFS, MS controls, healthy controls). Longitudinal samples at up to 6 timepoints. Stored at Royal Free Hospital; data at LSHTM.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes — first ME/CFS-specific biobank in Europe

**UK Biobank (ME/CFS subcohort)**
- URL: https://www.ukbiobank.ac.uk/
- Access: 🟡 Application-based (global approved researchers)
- Data: 5,354 ME/CFS-evidence individuals out of 502,364 participants. Four cohorts: C1 (self-reported), C2 (pain questionnaire), C3 (ICD-10 G93.3 hospital), C4 (primary care codes). Linked to hospital inpatient (446,974) and primary care (230,055) records. Rich phenotyping + genomics.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — well-defined ME/CFS subcohorts within population study

**PrecisionLife Combinatorial Genetics**
- URL: https://precisionlife.com/news-and-events/me-genetics-study
- Access: 🟢 Published findings; underlying data via DecodeME/UKBB access
- Data: Analyzed DecodeME + UK Biobank genomics. 250+ core genes associated with ME/CFS, 76 overlapping with long COVID. Drug repurposing opportunities.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**ME Research UK Database**
- URL: https://www.meresearch.org.uk/research/other-resources/research-database/
- Access: 🟢 Fully open
- Data: Searchable catalogue of published ME/CFS research.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**NICE ME/CFS Guideline Evidence Base (NG206, 2021)**
- URL: https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng206
- Access: 🟢 Fully open
- Data: Comprehensive systematic evidence review on diagnosis and management. Key conclusion: GET not recommended; CBT only for symptom management. Evidence rated low/very low for most interventions.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

## 3. Germany / Austria / Switzerland (DACH)

**German Multicenter ME/CFS Registry (MECFS-R)**
- URL: https://www.mdpi.com/2077-0383/13/11/3168
- Access: 🔴 Data available from corresponding authors upon reasonable request
- Data: Longitudinal clinical, epidemiological, and lab data from adults, adolescents, children. Web-based multilayer-secured database. Paired with multicenter biobank (MECFS-Bio). ~500–600 adult patients/year at CFC Berlin alone.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes
- Clinical trial registration: NCT05778006

**Charité Fatigue Center (CFC) Registry/Biobank**
- URL: https://cfc.charite.de/en/clinical_research/mecfs_registrybiobank
- Access: 🔴 Collaborative research through CFC
- Data: Epidemiological, clinical, and care-related data. Blood samples from clinically characterized patients.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**Munich Chronic Fatigue Center for Young People (MCFC), TU Munich**
- URL: https://mcfc.mri.tum.de/
- Access: 🔴 Through MECFS-R registry; collaborative arrangements
- Data: Pediatric/young adult ME/CFS cohort (up to age 25). Munich ME/CFS Cohort Study (MUC-CFS). Biobank with housebound patient samples. Part of PEDNET-LC network (20 centers, EUR 41M funding).
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**ME/CFS Research Register (MRR)**
- URL: https://mrr.mecfs-research.org/
- Access: 🟢 Fully open
- Data: Systematic overview of ME/CFS research landscape. 39 working groups catalogued (35 Germany, 4 Austria). Since late 2024 also covers Netherlands and Switzerland.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**ME/CFS Biobank Austria (MedUni Vienna / WE&ME Foundation)**
- URL: https://www.meduniwien.ac.at/ (search ME/CFS Biobank)
- Access: 🔴 Contact mecfs-biobank@meduniwien.ac.at
- Data: First Austrian ME/CFS biobank (under construction since Sept 2023). Harmonized with UK, Netherlands, and German ME/CFS biobanks. Biomarker research published 2024.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**D-A-CH Consensus Statement (2024)**
- URL: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00508-024-02372-y
- Access: 🟢 Open access
- Data: Interdisciplinary consensus on ME/CFS diagnosis and treatment across DACH region. Harmonized clinical standards.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

## 4. Netherlands

**Netherlands ME/CFS Cohort and Biobank (NMCB)**
- URL: https://nmcb.eu/en/
- Access: 🟡 FAIR principles; collaborative with international partners
- Data: 750 ME/CFS patients, 8-year follow-up (~25% severely ill, home/bed-bound). FAIR and open-access data infrastructure. Biobank with patient material. Funded by ZonMw (€7.2M). Data harmonization with UK, Germany, US, Canada, Australia.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes — one of the most ambitious dedicated ME/CFS data projects (started May 2023)

- Access: 🔴 Collaborative research

## 6. United States

### 6.1 NIH / Federal

**mapMECFS (RTI International / NIH)**
- URL: https://mapmecfs.org/
- Access: 🔵 Free registration
- Data: Interactive portal with experimental results across multiple omics: proteomics, metabolomics, methylation, gene expression, microbiome, demographic, clinical surveys. 8+ datasets from Cornell CRC. Integrates NIH Intramural Study data.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes — central NIH data-sharing platform

**searchMECFS (RTI International / NIH)**
- URL: https://searchmecfs.org/
- Access: 🔵 Registration; biospecimen requests via ME/CFS BRAC committee
- Data: Biospecimen search tool: DNA, RNA, urine, plasma, PBMCs from CFI epidemiology study (401 participants: 201 ME/CFS, 200 controls) and MCAM study data. Query by demographic and clinical characteristics.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**NIH ME/CFS BRAC (Biospecimen Resource Access Committee)**
- URL: https://ninds.nih.gov/ (search BRAC)
- Access: 🟡 Application through BRAC
- Data: Coordinates access to ME/CFS biospecimens across all NIH-funded studies.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**NIH NINDS Common Data Elements (CDEs) for ME/CFS**
- URL: https://cde.nlm.nih.gov/home
- Access: 🟢 Publicly available
- Data: 119 standardized measures for ME/CFS research covering symptoms, diagnosis, clinical assessment. Not a dataset — a standardization framework.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**NIH Intramural Study (Deep Phenotyping)**
- URL: https://www.nih.gov/mecfs/nih-intramural-mecfs-study
- Access: 🔵 Through mapMECFS portal; additional releases planned
- Data: fMRI brain imaging of 17 PI-ME/CFS patients + 21 healthy controls. Published Nature Communications (Feb 2024). Multi-system deep phenotyping.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**CDC National Health Interview Survey (NHIS)**
- URL: https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/nhis/
- Access: 🟢 Public-use microdata files
- Data: 2021–2022: 1.3% of US adults have ME/CFS (3.3 million people). Demographic breakdowns by age, sex, race/ethnicity, income.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes (prevalence data)

**NINDS ME/CFS Research Roadmap (May 2024)**
- URL: https://www.ninds.nih.gov/ (search Research Roadmap)
- Access: 🟢 Freely downloadable PDF
- Data: Research priorities across 8 areas (nervous system, immune system, less-studied pathologies).
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

### 6.2 Research Centers & Biobanks

**Stanford End ME/CFS Data Center (OMF)**
- URL: https://omf.ngo/mecfs-severely-ill-big-data-study
- Access: 🔵 Registration at Stanford End ME/CFS Data Center
- Data: 1,000+ tests per patient in severely ill cohort. Genome, gene expression, metabolomics, microbiome, cell-free DNA, cytokines. Claimed largest ME/CFS dataset per patient.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes
- PIs: Ron Davis, Wenzhong Xiao

**Bateman Horne Center Biorepository (ChronicalBio)**
- URL: https://batemanhornecenter.org/
- Access: 🔴 Collaborative research
- Data: Saliva, blood, urine linked to clinical data for POTS, ME/CFS, long COVID. MCAM study data (7 specialist clinics, 2012–2017). Cognitive impairment/blood flow research (with U of Utah and Jackson Lab).
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**Open Medicine Foundation BioQuest**
- URL: https://openmedicinefoundation.ngo/ (search BioQuest)
- Access: 🔴 Results to be published; data sharing via OMF
- Data: Large-scale biomarker project: metabolomics, proteomics, cytokines in ~1,000 plasma samples (including CFI samples). Testing expected to have begun by end of 2025.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**Workwell Foundation CPET Database**
- URL: https://workwellfoundation.org/
- Access: 🔴 Collaborative research (not in public repository)
- Data: Hundreds of ME/CFS patients tested over 20+ years using 2-day CPET protocol. Demonstrates inability to reproduce CPET measures on day 2.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**Cornell CRC 2-Day CPET Study**
- URL: doi:10.1186/s12967-024-05410-5
- Access: 🔴 Through Cornell CRC/mapMECFS
- Data: 84 ME/CFS participants + 71 sedentary controls. Two CPETs separated by 24h. Metabolic and cardiopulmonary response data.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

### 6.3 Multi-Omics / Key Datasets

**BioMapAI (Jackson Laboratory / Bateman Horne Center)**
- URL: doi:10.1038/s41591-025-03609-1 (Nature Medicine 2025)
- Access: 🟢 Published; dataset access details in publication
- Data: Integrated gut metagenomics, plasma metabolomics, immune cell profiles, blood tests, clinical symptoms. 153 ME/CFS + 96 healthy controls over 4 years. AI model achieves 90% accuracy.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes
- Key findings: Reduced butyrate, elevated tryptophan/benzoate

**HEAL2 Whole-Genome Sequencing**
- URL: doi:10.1101/2025.04.15.25325899 (medRxiv)
- Access: 🟢 Published; multi-cohort
- Data: WGS on 1,075 individuals (464 ME/CFS, 611 controls) from 3 cohorts (Stanford, CureME/UK, Cornell). 115 ME/CFS-risk genes. Deep learning framework.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**Naviaux Metabolomics ("Dauer" Signature)**
- URL: Metabolomics Workbench accession ST000450
- Access: 🟢 Fully open
- Data: Targeted broad-spectrum metabolomics showing hypometabolic "dauer" signature in ME/CFS.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**Cornell ME/CFS scRNA-seq**
- URL: doi:10.1016/j.xcrm.2023.100991 (Cell Reports Medicine, 2024)
- Access: 🟢 Data deposited (GEO/SRA)
- Data: Single-cell RNA sequencing of PBMCs from ME/CFS patients and matched controls, at baseline and 24h post-exercise. Monocyte transcriptome alterations and platelet activation. Disease severity correlation.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**CD8+ T Cell Exhaustion Transcriptomics**
- URL: doi:10.1073/pnas.2415119121 (PNAS 2025)
- Access: 🟢 Dataset in publication
- Data: Transcriptomic, epigenomic, and flow cytometric profiles of CD8+ T cell subsets showing exhaustion in ME/CFS.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**Lipkin/Hornig Microbiome (Columbia University)**
- URL: NCBI SRA, BioProject PRJNA751448
- Access: 🟢 Fully open
- Data: Shotgun metagenomic sequences from fecal samples. 106 ME/CFS cases + 91 healthy controls from 5 US clinics. Host-subtracted.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

## 7. Canada

**ICanCME (Interdisciplinary Canadian Collaborative ME Research Network)**
- URL: https://icancme.ca/
- Access: 🔴 Through CIHR-funded network; data harmonized with mapMECFS/searchMECFS
- Data: Canadian ME National Research Cohort (screened patients), biobank (patients + healthy controls), harmonized data standards. Wearable vital signs database (Hexoskin — claimed world's largest for ME). Renewed for 5 years (Oct 2024), $2M CIHR catalyst grant.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes
- PI: Dr. Alain Moreau (Sainte-Justine University Hospital, Montreal)

**Canadian Longitudinal Study on Aging (CLSA)**
- URL: https://www.clsa-elcv.ca/
- Access: 🟡 Application-based
- Data: 51,338 participants aged 45–85. Blood/urine, physical assessments. CFS prevalence 1.4% via self-report. Followed every 3 years until 2033.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — CFS self-report subset

**Statistics Canada Biobank**
- URL: https://www.statcan.gc.ca/en/microdata/biobank
- Access: 🟡 Application through Statistics Canada
- Data: Population-level biobank linked to health surveys. CFS captured in Canadian Community Health Survey.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — population-level with CFS subset

**ANZMES / New Zealand Research**

## 10. South Korea / Taiwan

- URL: https://translational-medicine.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s12967-021-03170-0
- Access: 🟡 Application to HIRA
- Data: Complete national medical records (mandatory enrollment). 10-year CFS dataset 2010–2020: annual mean incidence 44.71/100k; prevalence 57.70/100k.
- ME/CFS-specific: CFS subset via ICD codes

**Taiwan NHIRD (National Health Insurance Research Database)**
- Access: 🟡 Application to NHI Administration
- Data: Claims data covering ~99% of Taiwan's 23M population. Multiple CFS cohorts extracted (e.g., 6,306 CFS patients 2000–2012; 309,692 influenza patients tracked for CFS development).
- ME/CFS-specific: CFS subset via ICD codes
- Key DOI: 10.1186/s12967-025-06600-5

## 11. Other Regions

**Nigeria — Community-based CFS Prevalence**
- DOI: 10.1177/1359105307076233
- Access: 🟢 Published data only
- Data: CFS prevalence (adults + children) comparable to or higher than US community studies.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes

**Brazil — CFS Epidemiology**
- DOI: 10.1192/bjp.bp.108.056291
- Access: 🟢 Published data only
- Data: CFS prevalence in primary care 1.6% (CDC 1994 criteria). Key finding: CFS unrecognized by Brazilian physicians.
- ME/CFS-specific: Yes
- Notes: No formal registry or biobank in Brazil

**China — CFS Epidemiological Studies**
- Access: 🟢 Published data only
- Data: University students (Wuhan, n=1,826, prevalence 6.25%). Middle-school students (Suzhou, n=18,139, prevalence 0.9%). TCM treatment research data.
- ME/CFS-specific: CFS studies (may use different diagnostic criteria)
- Notes: No centralized CFS registry identified

### 12.2 Proteomics

**PRIDE / ProteomeXchange (EMBL-EBI)**
- URL: https://www.ebi.ac.uk/pride/archive

## 13. Long COVID Overlapping Datasets

**NIH RECOVER Initiative**
- URL: https://recovercovid.org/data
- Access: 🟡 Application through dbGaP; ROA for biosamples
- Data: 1.4M+ biosamples. De-identified participant-level datasets. ME/CFS phenotyping subcohort: 4,515 participants assessed using IOM criteria at 6 months post-COVID. Data snapshots released ~every 6 months.

- URL: https://covid.cd2h.org/
- Access: 🟡 Enclave access application (4,300+ users)
- Data: EHR data on 22.9M+ patients from 84 sites. ME/CFS phenotype via ICD codes. PASC vs ME/CFS comparison study (Communications Medicine 2025). ML-driven computable phenotypes.
- ME/CFS-specific: Contains ME/CFS-coded subset

**ISARIC COVID-19 Clinical Dataset**
- URL: https://isaric.org/
- Access: 🟡 Application through IDDO
- Data: 705,000+ hospitalized COVID-19 records from 60+ countries, 1,500+ centres. Long-term follow-up data.
- ME/CFS-specific: No — long COVID follow-up may contain ME/CFS-like phenotypes

## Coverage Gaps

Regions/categories with minimal or no formal ME/CFS data infrastructure:

- **Sub-Saharan Africa**: Only one Nigerian prevalence study (2007); no registries or biobanks
- **South America**: Only one Brazilian prevalence study; no registries or biobanks
- **India**: Minimal published prevalence data; no ME/CFS-specific infrastructure
- **Southeast Asia**: No ME/CFS-specific data infrastructure found
- **Neuroimaging repositories**: No ME/CFS-specific datasets deposited on OpenNeuro or NITRC (data remains with investigators)
- **Flow cytometry repositories (FlowRepository)**: No ME/CFS-specific datasets confirmed as deposited
