# Integration Plan: Inadequate Treatment Duration in Research

**Purpose:** Evaluate the methodological critique that negative/null treatment trial results in chronic illness (Lyme, ME/CFS, post-infectious syndromes) may reflect inadequate treatment intensity/duration rather than true inefficacy. Example: 3-month antibiotic monotherapy for Lyme failing because it ignores co-infections (Babesia, Bartonella) and requires longer courses. Extend this reasoning to ME/CFS trials — were prior negative trials genuinely negative, or were they underpowered in duration, dosing, or pathogen coverage?

**Target chapters (provisional):**
- Part 2: ch06 (pathophysiology — chronic infection hypotheses)
- Part 3: ch18 (investigational treatments), ch29 (medication response reference)
- Part 4: research methodology / open questions

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- Inadequate treatment duration/dosing as a confound in null-result clinical trials for post-infectious chronic illness
- Co-infection undertreatment as a source of false-negative trial outcomes
- Lyme → ME/CFS translational parallels: if Lyme trials failed due to undertreatment, do ME/CFS antiviral/immunomodulatory trials share the same design flaw?
