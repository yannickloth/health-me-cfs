# Integration Plan: Hypoxia / Altitude / HIF1α in ME/CFS

**Purpose:** Integrate the paradoxical observation that high altitude (lower O₂ partial pressure) appears to *improve* ME/CFS symptoms, suggesting that hypoxia-mediated signalling (HIF1α, EPO, cerebral perfusion shift) may be therapeutically relevant — not via O₂ *deficiency* but via O₂ *sensing* adaptation pathways.

**Target chapters:** ch06 (metabolism), ch12 (neurovascular/cerebral blood flow), ch13 (cross-disease), ch14d (autonomic), hypothesis registry

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- HIF1α activation at altitude compensates for a baseline deficit in O₂-sensing pathway → hypoxia preconditioning/hormesis
- Altitude-induced shift in cerebral autoregulation (CO₂ reactivity) repairs a broken vasoregulation mechanism — predicts normalisation of cerebral blood flow during orthostasis
- EPO elevation at altitude has neuroprotective effects beyond RBC mass (BDNF, VEGF, neurogenesis)
- If true, altitude-mimetic drugs (Roxadustat, other HIF-PH inhibitors) are predicted to be beneficial
- Low altitude (sea level) may represent a *relative hyperoxia* stress for ME/CFS patients with impaired O₂ utilisation → explains paradox: lowering PO₂ *improves* function
