## Inflammation Sources — Preliminary Catalog

### Core Signaling Systems
1. Cytokine network — TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6, IL-17, IFN-γ, IL-10 family
2. Chemokine network — CXCL8/IL-8, CCL2/MCP-1, CXCL10/IP-10
3. Inflammasomes — NLRP3, NLRC4, AIM2, pyrin
4. NF-κB pathway — canonical, non-canonical, atypical
5. JAK-STAT pathway
6. MAPK pathway (p38, ERK, JNK)
7. Eicosanoid cascade — prostaglandins (COX-1/2), leukotrienes (5-LOX), lipoxins
8. Complement system — classical, alternative, lectin pathways
9. Coagulation system — thrombin, PARs, fibrin, tissue factor
10. Kinin system — bradykinin, kallikrein
11. Histamine — H1–H4 receptors, mast cell derived
12. Serotonin — 5-HT receptors, platelet derived, gut derived
13. Sphingolipid signaling — ceramide, S1P

### Innate Immune Effectors
14. Mast cells — tryptase, chymase, histamine, PGD2, LTB4, cytokines, VEGF
15. Macrophage polarization — M1 (pro-inflammatory), M2 (anti-inflammatory)
16. Microglia — CNS resident macrophages, TSPO, fractalkine
17. Neutrophils — NETosis, MPO, ROS, elastase, defensins
18. Eosinophils — ECP, EDN, MBP, eosinophil peroxidase
19. Basophils — histamine, LTC4
20. NK cells — IFN-γ, perforin, granzyme (dysfunctional = viral persistence)
21. Dendritic cells — pDC (IFN-α), cDC1, cDC2
22. Monocytes — classical, non-classical, intermediate
23. γδ T cells — IL-17, IFN-γ
24. ILCs — ILC1 (IFN-γ), ILC2 (IL-5, IL-13), ILC3 (IL-22)

### Adaptive Immune Effectors
25. Th1 cells — IFN-γ, TNF-α
26. Th2 cells — IL-4, IL-5, IL-13
27. Th17 cells — IL-17A, IL-17F, IL-22
28. Tfh cells — IL-21
29. Th9 cells — IL-9
30. Treg cells — IL-10, TGF-β (anti-inflammatory, dysfunction = inflammation)
31. CD8+ T cells — perforin, granzyme, IFN-γ
32. B cells — autoantibodies, cytokines (IL-6, TNF-α), antigen presentation
33. Plasma cells — autoantibody production

### Damage Signals (DAMPs)
34. HMGB1 — TLR2/4/RAGE ligand
35. S100 proteins — S100A8/A9 (calprotectin), S100B
36. ATP — P2X7 receptor, NLRP3 activator
37. Uric acid / monosodium urate — NLRP3
38. DNA (mitochondrial, nuclear) — cGAS-STING, TLR9
39. RNA — TLR3, TLR7, TLR8, RIG-I, MDA5
40. Heat shock proteins — HSP60, HSP70
41. Hyaluronan fragments — TLR2/4, CD44
42. Heparan sulfate fragments
43. Biglycan, versican — TLR2/4
44. Fibrinogen, fibronectin fragments — TLR4
45. Oxidized LDL — LOX-1, CD36, TLR4
46. Advanced glycation end products (AGEs) — RAGE
47. β-amyloid — NLRP3, TLR2
48. α-synuclein — TLR2, NLRP3

### Pathogen Signals (PAMPs)
49. LPS (Gram-negative) — TLR4/MD2/CD14
50. Lipoteichoic acid (Gram-positive) — TLR2
51. Peptidoglycan — NOD1, NOD2
52. Flagellin — TLR5, NLRC4
53. CpG DNA — TLR9
54. dsRNA — TLR3, RIG-I, MDA5
55. ssRNA — TLR7, TLR8
56. Zymosan (fungal) — Dectin-1, TLR2
57. β-glucan — Dectin-1
58. Viral glycoproteins — TLR2, TLR4
59. Bacterial toxins — superantigens, pore-forming toxins

### Redox / Metabolic Sources
60. Reactive oxygen species (ROS) — NADPH oxidase, mitochondrial ETC leak, xanthine oxidase
61. Reactive nitrogen species — NO, peroxynitrite, iNOS
62. Lipid peroxidation products — 4-HNE, MDA, F2-isoprostanes
63. Oxidized phospholipids
64. Mitochondrial dysfunction — mtDNA release, cardiolipin oxidation, mito-ROS, mito-DAMPs
65. ER stress — unfolded protein response (UPR), PERK, IRE1, ATF6
66. Hypoxia — HIF-1α, HIF-2α
67. Reperfusion injury
68. Iron dysregulation — ferroptosis, free iron, Fenton chemistry
69. Metabolic intermediates — succinate (HIF-1α), itaconate (NLRP3), fumarate
70. Lactate — GPR81 signaling
71. Ketone bodies — β-hydroxybutyrate (generally anti-inflammatory, but context-dependent)
72. Bile acids — TGR5, FXR (generally anti-inflammatory, dysregulation = pro-inflammatory)

### Neurogenic Inflammation
73. Substance P — NK1R, mast cell degranulation
74. CGRP — vasodilation, mast cell activation
75. Neurokinin A — NK2R
76. VIP — generally anti-inflammatory, but pro-inflammatory in some contexts
77. NMDA receptor activation — excitotoxicity, Ca2+ influx
78. TRP channels — TRPV1 (capsaicin, heat, protons), TRPA1 (ROS, irritants), TRPM3 (heat, cholesterol)
79. P2X receptors — ATP-gated ion channels
80. Cannabinoid system — CB1, CB2, endocannabinoids
81. Nociceptor activation — dorsal root reflex, neurogenic flare

### Endothelial / Vascular Inflammation
82. Adhesion molecules — ICAM-1, VCAM-1, E-selectin, P-selectin
83. BBB disruption — CLDN5, OCLN, ZO-1, transcellular transport
84. Glycocalyx degradation — syndecan-1, hyaluronan
85. VEGF — vascular permeability
86. Angiopoietin/Tie2 axis — Ang-2 destabilization
87. Endothelin-1 — vasoconstriction, inflammation
88. Nitric oxide — eNOS uncoupling → ROS, iNOS → peroxynitrite
89. vWF — endothelial activation marker, platelet adhesion
90. ADAMTS13 — VWF-cleaving protease (deficiency = microthrombi)

### Coagulation / Thrombotic Inflammation
91. Thrombin — PAR1/3/4 activation → cytokine release
92. Tissue factor — extrinsic coagulation initiator
93. Fibrinogen → fibrin — immune cell activation
94. Platelets — P-selectin, CD40L, TXA2, PF4, serotonin
95. Microclots — fibrin(ogen), α2-antiplasmin, serum amyloid A
96. NETs — neutrophil extracellular traps
97. D-dimer — fibrin degradation product

### Autoantibody Sources
98. GPCR autoantibodies — β2AR, M3R, M4R, AT1R, ETAR
99. Nuclear autoantibodies — ANA, anti-dsDNA
100. Phospholipid autoantibodies — aPL, anti-cardiolipin, anti-β2GPI
101. Thyroid autoantibodies — anti-TPO, anti-Tg, TRAb
102. Mitochondrial autoantibodies — anti-ANT, anti-BCKDK, anti-KGDHC
103. Neurotransmitter receptor — anti-NMDA, anti-GABA, anti-D2R
104. Ion channel — anti-VGKC, anti-AQP4, anti-TRPM3?
105. Tissue-specific — anti-muscle, anti-endothelial, anti-enteric

### Inflammation Resolution Pathways (Deficiency = Chronic Inflammation)
106. Specialized pro-resolving mediators (SPMs) — resolvins, protectins, maresins, lipoxins
107. Efferocytosis — apoptotic cell clearance
108. Treg function
109. IL-10 signaling
110. TGF-β signaling
111. Regulatory B cells (Breg)
112. Regulatory macrophages (M2c)
113. PD-1/PD-L1 immune checkpoint
114. CTLA-4
115. FoxP3 stability
