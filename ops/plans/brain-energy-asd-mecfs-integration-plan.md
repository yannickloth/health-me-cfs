# Integration Plan: Brain Energy Metabolism / ASD → ME/CFS

**Purpose:** Evaluate whether the brain energy metabolism framework for ASD (astrocytic glucose dysregulation → ATP shortage → cognitive sacrifice) has sufficient evidence to inform ME/CFS pathophysiology. ASD and ME/CFS share overlapping features (cognitive dysfunction, executive function deficits, sensory processing issues, PEM/crash parallels) — the "energy-saving adaptation" model may offer mechanistic cross-disease insight.

**Topic slug:** `brain-energy-asd-mecfs`

**Source paper:** Blagojevic-Stokic et al. (2026), "Autism as a brain energy disorder," Brain Network Disorders, DOI: 10.1016/j.bnd.2026.04.002

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**
- ME/CFS cognitive symptoms (brain fog, EF deficits) reflect an astrocyte-mediated cerebral energy conservation program analogous to ASD's "energy-saving adaptation" — certainty pending
- Glucose hypometabolism in ME/CFS prefrontal cortex mirrors ASD FDG-PET findings — certainty pending
- ME/CFS post-exertional malaise = metabolic "crash" when brain energy budget is exceeded — certainty pending

## Notes
- Source paper is a review article (no new primary data) — evidence is synthetic, not novel
- ASD is a neurodevelopmental condition; ME/CFS is acquired — developmental timing differs
- Paper authors include Paul Whiteley (co-author on ME/CFS paper ch14d autoimmune encephalitis section) and Ben Marlow — potential author overlap with our paper
- Key downstream mechanisms to search: brain glucose hypometabolism, astrocyte dysfunction, PFC energy deficits, GLUT1, glycogen depletion, OXPHOS-to-glycolysis shift, lactate shuttle
