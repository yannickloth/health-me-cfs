# Post-Exercise Recovery Optimization in ME/CFS — Integration Plan

**Date:** 2026-06-18
**Purpose:** Integrate the full spectrum of post-exercise recovery strategies — contrasting healthy athlete recovery physiology with ME/CFS recovery failure — covering active recovery ("décrassage"), passive recovery (sauna, massage), nutrition, sleep, and pharmacological/supplement support for muscle energy production and repair.

## Scope and Novelty vs. Existing Plans

This plan is **distinct from but builds on** several existing plans:

| Existing plan | Overlap | This plan's delta |
|---------------|---------|-------------------|
| `exercise-recovery-pem-integration-plan.md` | PEM duration, CPET recovery | Recovery *optimization strategies* (what to do to improve recovery, not just how long it takes) |
| `heat-cold-thermoregulation-integration-plan.md` | Sauna/Waon therapy, HSP70 | Sauna specifically as post-exercise recovery tool; integration with other recovery modalities |
| `exercise-modality-expansion-integration-plan.md` | Exercise modality safety | Post-exercise window (what happens *after* the exercise, not during) |
| `muscle-preservation-bedbound-integration-plan.md` | Anti-catabolic nutrition | Recovery nutrition *after exertion* vs. anti-catabolic during immobility |

**Key novel contributions:**
1. Healthy-vs-ME/CFS recovery physiology contrast (educational value — explains *why* GET fails)
2. Active recovery / "décrassage" — mechanism + danger in ME/CFS
3. Post-exercise nutrition timing and composition for ME/CFS
4. Massage and manual therapy as recovery tool (risk/benefit in ME/CFS)
5. Combined recovery protocols (sequencing multiple modalities)
6. Muscle energy production optimization (mitochondrial support post-exercise)
7. "Slow jogging de décrassage" — what it is, why it works in healthy people, why it's contraindicated in ME/CFS

## Pre-identified Hypotheses

| Hypothesis | Preliminary certainty | Source |
|------------|----------------------|--------|
| Active recovery ("décrassage") accelerates lactate clearance via maintained perfusion in healthy athletes | 0.70 | Sports science consensus |
| Active recovery is contraindicated in ME/CFS because the recovery machinery itself is broken (mitophagy, NAD+, PDK) | 0.55 | Mechanistic inference from existing ch06 content |
| Passive heat exposure (infrared sauna) post-exercise may provide HSP70/perfusion benefits without exertional cost | 0.40 | Cross-reference heat-cold plan; limited ME/CFS evidence |
| Massage can trigger PEM in moderate-severe ME/CFS via mechanical stress → immune activation | 0.35 | Clinical observation, no controlled trials |
| Post-exercise protein+carb timing matters less in ME/CFS because the rate-limiting step is mitochondrial, not substrate | 0.30 | Mechanistic speculation |
| Sleep is the single most important recovery modality for ME/CFS and healthy athletes alike, but ME/CFS sleep is non-restorative | 0.65 | Well-established |
| ME/CFS recovery failure is a *systems-level* property, not a single-pathway failure | 0.60 | Integrative hypothesis from ch06/ch15 |
| Contrast therapy (hot/cold) is higher-risk in ME/CFS due to autonomic dysfunction / POTS | 0.45 | Mechanistic inference from ch10 |
| Creatine supplementation may support phosphocreatine recovery post-exercise | 0.40 | Sports science + ME/CFS PCr depletion evidence |
| Tart cherry / anti-inflammatory foods may reduce PEM severity if PEM has an inflammatory component | 0.25 | Speculative cross-application from sports |
| Exercise-induced mast cell degranulation (mechanical stress) amplifies PEM via histamine/prostaglandin cascade | 0.45 | Mechanistic: exercise-induced urticaria literature + MCAS prevalence in ME/CFS |
| Pre-emptive H1+H2 antihistamine blockade before exercise reduces PEM severity in MCAS+ME/CFS subgroup | 0.35 | No direct trial; inferred from MCAS management + exercise-induced anaphylaxis protocols |
| Post-exercise supplement timing window differs in ME/CFS because mitochondrial recovery is rate-limiting, not substrate delivery | 0.30 | Mechanistic inference from ch06 |
| Larger muscle mass provides a larger mitochondrial/PCr buffer, potentially raising the PEM threshold — but also raises basal metabolic cost | 0.45 | Dual-edged: sports physiology + ME/CFS energy envelope |
| Training through PEM causes cumulative mitochondrial damage because adaptation pathways (PGC-1α/AMPK/SIRT1/mitophagy) are dysfunctional in ME/CFS | 0.60 | Core anti-GET argument; two-day CPET + Workwell data |
| Deconditioning genuinely co-exists with ME/CFS but is not causal — must treat both without conflating | 0.55 | Clinical consensus post-PACE reanalysis |
| PEM is multi-component (cognitive, muscular, autonomic) with different pathways — cognitive PEM (brain fog + headache) may be the most interceptable via anti-neuroinflammation | 0.50 | Decomposition enables targeted treatment; partial relief = major QoL gain |
