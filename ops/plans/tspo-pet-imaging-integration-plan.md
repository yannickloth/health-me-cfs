# TSPO PET Imaging Integration Plan

**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Purpose:** Integrate findings on whole-body [11C]DPA-713-PET/MR imaging of ME/CFS vs healthy controls, particularly TSPO signal patterns in peripheral tissues vs brain and correlation with fatigue severity.
**Hypothesis tree:** ops/plans/hypotheses-trees/subtrees/tspo-pet-imaging.md (to be created in Phase 3a)

**Target chapters:**
- Part 1: Clinical (ch02-core-symptoms - fatigue measurement, ch05-severity-levels - objective biomarkers)
- Part 2: Pathophysiology (ch07-immune-dysfunction - neuroinflammation, ch08-neurological - microglial activation, ch13-integrative-models - multi-system patterns)
- Part 4: Research (ch20-biomarker-research - TSPO as biomarker, ch25b-proposed-studies - imaging protocols)

**Pre-identified hypotheses:**

- TSPO signal in peripheral tissues (shoulders, postural muscles, neck, glutes, bone marrow) is elevated in ME/CFS
- TSPO signal in brain is decreased in ME/CFS, contrary to microglial activation predictions
- Peripheral inflammation inhibits TSPO tracer from crossing blood-brain barrier
- TSPO signal in peripheral tissues correlates with fatigue severity
- TSPO signal reflects mitochondrial dysfunction and bioenergetic impairment, not just microglial activation
