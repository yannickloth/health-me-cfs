# LSR Sub-Research Integration Guide

## Papers Found: 4 (new, not already in bib)

### Paper 1: de Sanjosé (2007) — Epstein-Barr virus infection and risk of lymphoma: immunoblot analysis of antibody responses against EBV-related proteins
- DOI: 10.1002/ijc.22857
- PMID: 17557295
- Certainty: 0.65
- Type: primary (case-control)
- Key findings: n=1085 lymphoma + 1153 controls. Immunoblot measuring IgG to BZLF1 (Zebra, lytic switch), VCA-p18 (BFRF3, structural), VCA-p40 (BdRF1), EA, EBNA1 simultaneously. Defined "abnormal reactive EBV pattern" (ab_EBV) as broad reactivity across multiple antigen classes. ab_EBV in 23.9% cases vs 18.0% controls (OR 1.42). First large-scale multi-antigen EBV serological profiling in disease.
- Relevance to LSR: Closest precedent to computing an LSR-like metric. Measured BZLF1 (lytic) AND VCA-p18 (structural) in same individuals. Did NOT compute their ratio, but did define a combined "abnormal pattern" based on simultaneous reactivity. Demonstrates principle that multi-antigen EBV serological profiles carry more disease information than single-antigen titers.
- Limitations: Lymphoma not ME/CFS. "ab_EBV" is a qualitative categorization (broad reactivity), not a quantitative ratio of lytic to structural. Cases had malignancy-associated immunosuppression potentially confounding antibody profiles.

### Paper 2: Xuan (2020) — Serological Evidence for the Association Between Epstein-Barr Virus Infection and Sjögren's Syndrome
- DOI: 10.3389/fimmu.2020.590444
- PMID: 33193425
- Certainty: 0.60
- Type: primary (case-control)
- Key findings: n=119 SjS + 65 HC. Measured IgG-anti-VCA, IgG-anti-EA, IgG-anti-EBNA1, IgM-anti-VCA. EA IgG seropositivity: 31.9% SjS vs 3.1% HC (p<0.001). VCA IgG titers also elevated. EA IgG associated with low C3/C4 complement. Both EA and VCA measured in same individuals.
- Relevance to LSR: Another disease where both EA (lytic) and VCA (structural) IgG were measured in same patients and both found elevated. Authors did NOT compute EA/VCA ratio, but data is available for post-hoc calculation. Demonstrates that EA IgG can show stronger disease association than VCA IgG in autoimmune context. Suggests LSR concept may have broader applicability beyond ME/CFS.
- Limitations: Sjögren's not ME/CFS. No ratio computation. Small HC group (n=65). Single timepoint.

### Paper 3: Lerner (2012) — Antibody to Epstein-Barr virus deoxyuridine triphosphate nucleotidohydrolase and deoxyribonucleotide polymerase in a chronic fatigue syndrome subset
- DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0047891
- PMID: 23155374
- Certainty: 0.45
- Type: primary (case-control, serial sampling)
- Key findings: n=6 EBV-subset CFS + 20 controls. Measured VCA IgM (structural lytic?), EA(D) (lytic), neutralizing antibodies to dUTPase and DNA polymerase (both lytic-cycle enzymes). EA(D) positivity: 93.9% CFS vs 31.6% controls. dUTPase neutralizing antibodies elevated. Serial sampling over 13-16 months showed persistent elevation.
- Relevance to LSR: Earliest paper from Ariza/Williams group. Measured both lytic (EA[D], dUTPase, DNA pol) and structural (VCA) antibodies. Did NOT compute LSR explicitly but had data to do so. Established that lytic-cycle enzyme antibodies (dUTPase polymerase) are persistently elevated in CFS subset. Conceptual precursor to Palomo 2026.
- Limitations: Tiny sample (n=6 CFS). EBV-subset by selection. CFS (Fukuda), not ME/CFS (CCC). Authors were Ariza/Williams group — same group as Palomo 2026. No independent replication.

### Paper 4: Maes (2025) — Chronic fatigue syndrome, depression, and anxiety symptoms due to relapsing-remitting multiple sclerosis are associated with reactivation of Epstein-Barr virus and Human Herpesvirus 6
- PMID: 40319455
- Journal: Neuro Endocrinol Lett, 2025; 46(1):15-26
- Certainty: 0.50
- Type: primary (cross-sectional)
- Key findings: n=58 RRMS + 63 controls. Measured IgG/IgA/IgM to EBNA and dUTPases of EBV and HHV-6. Combined IgG+IgM-HHV-6-dUTPase accounted for 63.7% of variance in CFS-depression-anxiety factor. Total fatigue scores correlated with dUTPase responses across isotypes.
- Relevance to LSR: **First independent partial replication of dUTPase antibody findings outside Ariza/Williams group.** Maes/Almulla/Vojdani group (not Ariza/Williams) also found elevated dUTPase antibodies associated with CFS symptoms. Supports the robustness of dUTPase IgG as a biomarker. Did not simultaneously measure structural antigens — no LSR computable. However confirms that lytic-cycle enzyme antibodies are detectable and disease-associated.
- Limitations: RRMS population with comorbid CFS symptoms, not primary ME/CFS. Only dUTPase (not other lytic antigens like EA or BZLF1). No structural antigen counterpart, so LSR cannot be derived. Cross-sectional.

## Summary
- Direct LSR measurement studies: 0 (concept is entirely novel — no study has computed a lytic-to-structural IgG ratio)
- Studies measuring both lytic AND structural antibodies in same individuals (LSR-derivable): 2 (de Sanjosé 2007, Xuan 2020 — neither ME/CFS)
- Studies measuring both lytic and structural in ME/CFS: 1 (Lerner 2012, n=6 — data exists but ratio not computed)
- Lytic-only antibody studies in ME/CFS: 4 (Lerner 2012, Palomo 2026, Maes 2025, Liu 2023 preprint)
- Studies on antibody ratio as herpesvirus diagnostic: 0 (no precedent in any disease)
- dUTPase replication outside Ariza/Williams: 1 (Maes 2025 — partial, different assay, different population)
- IgG avidity studies in ME/CFS: 0
- Antibody fine specificity studies in ME/CFS: 0 (Loebel 2017 used peptide microarray — closest, but addressed epitope binding breadth, not qualitative antibody differences)
- Null/negative findings: Blomberg 2019 (overall IgG no difference), Domingues 2021 (subgroup differences lost on multiple testing correction), Cameron 2010 (post-infective fatigue no persistent herpesvirus serology difference)

## Key Gaps
1. **No study has ever computed an LSR** (lytic IgG / structural IgG ratio) in any disease — the concept is entirely novel.
2. **No study simultaneously measures BZLF1/Zebra IgG AND VCA-p18 IgG in ME/CFS** — these have been measured together only in lymphoma (de Sanjosé 2007) and nasopharyngeal carcinoma, never in ME/CFS.
3. **No independent replication of Palomo 2026 dUTPase findings in ME/CFS** — Maes 2025 uses dUTPase in RRMS, not primary ME/CFS. The dUTPase-ME/CFS link remains single-group evidence.
4. **No known normal range for LSR in healthy LLPC biology** — the hypothesis that LSR should be stable in health rests on LLPC biology principles that have not been directly tested for herpesvirus antibodies.
5. **Simultaneous lytic + structural IgG measurement in the same ME/CFS cohort has never been done.**
