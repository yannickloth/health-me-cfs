### PRIMARY PAPERS

#### 1. Chinnappan et al. 2026 — Elevated IL-11 and MMP-9 in ME/CFS Serum
**BibTeX key:** `Chinnappan2026IL11MMP9`
**Journal:** Frontiers in Immunology (2026; 17:1827700)
**DOI:** 10.3389/fimmu.2026.1827700
**Certainty:** 0.35

**Design:** Case-control study — n=40 ME/CFS vs 38 controls for IL-11; n=18 vs 18 for MMP-9. Also in vitro: EBV BRLF1 protein activates cultured human mast cells (LAD2) to release MMP-9.

**Key findings:**
- Serum IL-11 significantly elevated in ME/CFS vs controls (p<0.0001)
- Serum MMP-9 significantly elevated in ME/CFS vs controls
- EBV BRLF1 protein directly activates human mast cells to release MMP-9 (cell culture)
- IL-11 correlated with symptom severity measures
- Provides mechanistic link: EBV → mast cell activation → MMP-9 release → neuroinflammation

**Limitations (critical methodological concerns):**
- Used serum, NOT plasma — MMP-9 is 3-4× higher in serum due to platelet/leukocyte release during clotting (Jung 2008; Olson 2008)
- Samples stored >10 years before analysis
- Age mismatch: patients mean 51 vs controls 43 (p<0.05)
- Different collection sites: patients from Miami, controls from external biobank
- Small sample for MMP-9 (n=18 per group)
- No blinding reported for lab assays
- No adjustment for multiple comparisons
- Single timepoint, no serial measurements
- No assessment of menstrual cycle phase despite known MMP-9 variation

**Significance for our paper:** First report of IL-11 in ME/CFS. Potential mast cell–MMP-9–neuroinflammation axis. But methodological concerns substantially limit interpretability.

#### 2. Kempuraj et al. 2024 — Long COVID MMP-9 and Microglial Release
**BibTeX key:** `Kempuraj2024LongCOVIDMMP9`
**Journal:** Translational Neuroscience (2024; 15(1):20220352)
**DOI:** 10.1515/tnsci-2022-0352
**Certainty:** 0.40

**Design:** Case-control + in vitro. Long COVID serum MMP-9 measured; SARS-CoV-2 spike protein applied to HMC3 human microglia cell line to measure MMP-9 release.

**Key findings:**
- Elevated MMP-9 in Long COVID serum vs controls
- SARS-CoV-2 spike protein directly stimulates microglia to release MMP-9
- MMP-9 can disrupt neuronal connectivity (synaptic remodeling)
- Provides mechanistic pathway for neurological symptoms in Long COVID

**Limitations:**
- Serum not plasma (same preanalytical concern as Chinnappan)
- Cell line only (HMC3) — not primary microglia
- Sample size for human data not clearly reported
- Translational Neuroscience — lower-tier journal
- No replication cohort
- Single timepoint

**Significance:** Directly supports MMP-9 elevation in post-viral conditions; mechanism via spike protein → microglia → MMP-9 → neuroinflammation. Analogous pathway may apply to ME/CFS (EBV → mast cells → MMP-9).

#### 3. Garvin et al. 2015 — Plasma MMP-9 Predicts CHD (Methods Authority)
**BibTeX key:** `Garvin2015MMP9CHD`
**Journal:** PLOS ONE (2015; 10(9):e0138290)
**DOI:** 10.1371/journal.pone.0138290
**Certainty:** 0.65 (general population, not ME/CFS)

**Design:** Prospective cohort — n=866, 8-year follow-up. Plasma MMP-9 measured at baseline, first-time CHD events tracked.

**Key findings:**
- Plasma MMP-9 independently predicts first-time CHD (HR ~1.5 per SD)
- Used citrate plasma — avoids clotting-induced MMP-9 release
- Demonstrates plasma MMP-9 is clinically informative when properly measured
- Well-controlled for traditional risk factors

**Limitations:**
- General population, not ME/CFS
- CHD endpoint not directly relevant to ME/CFS
- Single baseline measurement
- Moderate effect size

**Significance:** Critical as methods authority. Demonstrates plasma (not serum) is the correct matrix for MMP-9 measurement. Provides reference for criticizing serum-based MMP-9 studies (Chinnappan, Kempuraj).

#### 4. Jung 2008 — Serum MMP-9 Pre-Analytical Interference (Methods Authority)
**BibTeX key:** `Jung2008MMP9Preanalytical`
**Journal:** British Journal of Cancer (2008; 99:553-554)
**DOI:** 10.1038/sj.bjc.6604491
**Certainty:** N/A (methods authority, not clinical finding)

**Design:** Letter/re-analysis. Compared serum vs plasma MMP-9 in colorectal cancer patients.

**Key findings:**
- Serum MMP-9 is 3-4× higher than plasma MMP-9 in paired samples
- Platelet/leukocyte degranulation during clotting causes artifactual MMP-9 elevation
- Citrate plasma is the correct matrix for MMP-9 measurement
- Prior studies using serum MMP-9 as a cancer biomarker may reflect preanalytical artifact rather than disease biology

**Limitations:**
- Letter format, limited data presented
- Cancer population only
- Single laboratory

**Significance:** Foundational methods paper. Directly undermines the validity of any study using serum MMP-9, including Chinnappan 2026 and Kempuraj 2024. Must be cited alongside any MMP-9 claim.

### ADDITIONAL PAPERS

#### 5. Widjaja et al. 2024 — IL-11 Drives Inflammaging (Landmark)
**BibTeX key:** `Widjaja2024IL11Aging`
**Journal:** Nature (2024; 632(8023):157-165)
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41586-024-07701-9
**Certainty:** 0.90

**Design:** Comprehensive mouse study with genetic and pharmacological IL-11 blockade. 26 authors.

**Key findings:**
- IL-11 is a master regulator of inflammaging
- IL-11 knockout mice live ~25% longer, with reduced aging pathology
- Pharmacological IL-11 blockade (anti-IL-11 antibody) improves healthspan and lifespan
- IL-11 drives ERK/AMPK/mTORC1 pathway dysregulation, metabolic dysfunction, and chronic inflammation
- IL-11 levels increase with age in multiple tissues
- Anti-IL-11 therapy reduces age-related muscle wasting, frailty, and cardiac fibrosis

**Limitations:**
- Mouse model; human translation unproven
- Anti-IL-11 antibody not yet in human clinical trials for aging
- Some effects may be species-specific

**Significance:** Landmark paper establishing IL-11 as a central driver of aging/inflammaging. Provides the mechanistic framework for why IL-11 elevation in ME/CFS (Chinnappan 2026) could be pathophysiologically meaningful — it links ME/CFS to accelerated aging/senescence pathways. Widely discussed in Immunity and Cell Metabolism commentaries (Khan 2024; Kim 2024).

#### 6. Lerum et al. 2021 — MMP-9 in Post-COVID Pulmonary Pathology
**BibTeX key:** `Lerum2021MMP9PostCOVID`
**Journal:** Scientific Reports (2021; 11:23205)
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41598-021-02547-x
**Certainty:** 0.70

**Design:** Prospective cohort — n=108 patients, 3-month follow-up after COVID-19 hospitalization. Measured MMP-9 and lung function.

**Key findings:**
- High MMP-9 levels associated with persistent pulmonary pathology post-COVID
- Association with high viral load and weak antibody response
- MMP-9 as profibrotic mediator in post-COVID pulmonary sequelae
- Suggests persistent MMP-9 elevation contributes to tissue remodeling after viral infection

**Limitations:**
- Single timepoint (3 months)
- Hospitalized COVID only (not ME/CFS or Long COVID)
- Pulmonary focus

**Significance:** Demonstrates MMP-9 elevation persists after acute viral infection and correlates with end-organ pathology. Supports the concept of MMP-9 as a mediator of post-viral tissue damage, potentially relevant to ME/CFS.

#### 7. Bonetto et al. 2022 — MMP-9 and BBB Disruption in COVID
**BibTeX key:** `Bonetto2022MMP9BBBCOVID`
**Journal:** Frontiers in Immunology (2022; 13:1070379)
**DOI:** 10.3389/fimmu.2022.1070379
**Certainty:** 0.65

**Design:** Prospective cohort — COVID-19 patients with neurological manifestations. Measured MMP-9, S100B, and other BBB markers.

**Key findings:**
- MMP-9 elevated early and persistently in COVID patients with neurological symptoms
- MMP-9 correlated with BBB disruption markers (S100B, NSE)
- Suggests MMP-9-mediated BBB breakdown contributes to neuro-COVID
- Neurofilament light chain also elevated, indicating neuronal injury

**Limitations:**
- Moderate sample size
- No ME/CFS/Long COVID comparison
- Observational

**Significance:** Direct evidence that MMP-9 elevation in post-viral states is mechanistically linked to BBB disruption and neurological symptoms. Supports the MMP-9 → neuroinflammation → ME/CFS symptoms pathway.

#### 8. Tsilioni, Theoharides 2023 — Spike Protein Activates Microglia MMP-9
**BibTeX key:** `Tsilioni2023SpikeMicrogliaMMP9`
**Journal:** Molecular Neurobiology (2023; 60(11):6704-6714)
**DOI:** 10.1007/s12035-023-03493-7
**Certainty:** 0.55

**Design:** In vitro — HMC3 human microglia cell line treated with SARS-CoV-2 spike protein and RBD.

**Key findings:**
- Spike protein and RBD stimulate microglia to release different pro-inflammatory mediators
- MMP-9 release specifically triggered by spike protein (not RBD)
- Differential receptor activation: spike → TLR4, RBD → ACE2
- Distinct mediator profiles suggest multiple activation pathways

**Limitations:**
- Cell line only
- In vitro, supraphysiological concentrations
- No ME/CFS patient samples

**Significance:** From the same Theoharides group. Mechanistically complements Kempuraj 2024 by showing spike protein → microglia → MMP-9 via TLR4. This is a second independent demonstration of viral protein → glial MMP-9 release.

#### 9. Tsilioni et al. 2025 — Flavonoids Suppress Microglia MMP-9 Release
**BibTeX key:** `Tsilioni2025FlavonoidsMMP9`
**Journal:** International Journal of Molecular Sciences (2025; 26(2):636)
**DOI:** 10.3390/ijms26020636
**Certainty:** 0.50

**Design:** In vitro — HMC3 microglia treated with LPS, SARS-CoV-2 spike, ochratoxin A + nobiletin or eriodictyol.

**Key findings:**
- Nobiletin and eriodictyol suppress MMP-9 release from activated microglia (all 3 stimuli)
- Also suppress IL-1β, CXCL8, IL-6
- Natural flavonoids as potential therapeutic strategy for neuroinflammation
- Same cellular model as Kempuraj 2024 and Tsilioni 2023

**Limitations:**
- Cell line only, in vitro
- No pharmacokinetic data
- No human studies

**Significance:** Demonstrates MMP-9 release from microglia is pharmacologically suppressible. Relevant if the MMP-9 → neuroinflammation axis is targeted therapeutically in ME/CFS.

#### 10. Olson et al. 2008 — MMP-9 Sampling Methods and Atherosclerosis
**BibTeX key:** `Olson2008MMP9Sampling`
**Journal:** Journal of Internal Medicine (2008; 263(6):626-635)
**DOI:** 10.1111/j.1365-2796.2008.01927.x
**Certainty:** 0.70 (methods authority)

**Design:** Cross-sectional — n=~500. Compared MMP-9 by sampling method; correlated with carotid/femoral atherosclerosis.

**Key findings:**
- Significant preanalytical variability in MMP-9 depending on sampling method
- Serum MMP-9 substantially higher than plasma (confirming Jung 1998, 2008)
- MMP-9 levels affected by tube type, processing time, centrifugation
- Association between plasma MMP-9 and atherosclerosis only detectable with standardized protocols
- Citrate plasma recommended; heparin affects MMP-9 measurement

**Limitations:**
- General population (CHD risk)
- Cross-sectional design

**Significance:** Independent replication of Jung's preanalytical findings in a larger sample. Demonstrates that MMP-9 associations with disease are only detectable when sampling protocols are standardized. This is a critical methodological point for evaluating all MMP-9 biomarker studies.

#### 11. Jung et al. 1998 — Preanalytical Determinant of MMP-2/9 (Foundational Methods)
**BibTeX key:** `Jung1998MMP9Preanalytical`
**Journal:** Clinical Chemistry (1998; 44(5):1060-1062)
**DOI:** (no DOI available)
**Certainty:** 0.75 (methods authority, foundational)

**Design:** Methods comparison — MMP-2, MMP-9, TIMP-2 in serum vs various plasma types.

**Key findings:**
- Serum MMP-9 significantly higher than all plasma types
- EDTA plasma, citrate plasma, heparin plasma all give lower, more reproducible MMP-9 values
- Effect driven by platelet degranulation during clotting
- TIMP-2 less affected by sample type than MMP-9
- Recommends plasma for all MMP-9 clinical studies

**Limitations:**
- Small sample
- 1998 — newer assays may differ

**Significance:** Foundational methods paper, earliest demonstration of serum vs plasma MMP-9 differences. Cited by all subsequent preanalytical MMP-9 literature. Essential citation for the methodological critique of serum-based MMP-9 studies.

#### 12. Theoharides 2020 — COVID-19 as Mast Cell Activation Syndrome?
**BibTeX key:** `Theoharides2020COVIDMCAS`
**Journal:** Journal of Biological Regulators and Homeostatic Agents (2020; 34(5):1595-1598)
**DOI:** 10.23812/20-edit3
**Certainty:** 0.40 (hypothesis)

**Design:** Editorial/commentary.

**Key findings:**
- Proposes COVID-19 severity may reflect underlying mast cell activation syndrome
- SARS-CoV-2 activates mast cells via ACE2, TLR4, and other receptors
- Mast cell mediators (histamine, tryptase, MMP-9) contribute to cytokine storm

- Hypothesis-level

- Low-tier journal

**Significance:** Positions mast cell activation as a mechanistic link between viral infection and inflammatory pathology. Directly relevant to the Chinnappan 2026 hypothesis that EBV → mast cells → MMP-9 in ME/CFS. Theoharides group has built a framework connecting MCAS to post-viral syndromes.

## Key Methodological Finding

**The single most important methodological issue** is that Chinnappan 2026 and Kempuraj 2024 both used serum, not plasma, for MMP-9 measurement. The preanalytical literature (Jung 1998, 2008; Olson 2008) consistently shows serum MMP-9 is 3-4× higher than plasma due to platelet/leukocyte degranulation during clotting. This means the reported MMP-9 elevations may partially or wholly reflect differential clotting/platelet activation rather than disease biology.

**Recommended language for paper:** "A critical methodological limitation applies to all serum-based MMP-9 measurements in ME/CFS research. Serum MMP-9 is 3–4-fold higher than plasma MMP-9 due to platelet and leukocyte degranulation during coagulation (Jung 1998, 2008; Olson 2008). Future studies must adopt citrate plasma protocols."

## Gaps in Literature
- No study has measured plasma MMP-9 in ME/CFS
- No study has measured IL-11 in ME/CFS beyond Chinnappan 2026 (single unreplicated finding)
- No study has directly demonstrated mast cell degranulation → MMP-9 release in ME/CFS patients
- No study has simultaneously measured MMP-9 and IL-11 in plasma from ME/CFS patients
- No study has examined MMP-9 in ME/CFS CSF
- No longitudinal MMP-9 data in ME/CFS (pre/post PEM, or post-treatment)
