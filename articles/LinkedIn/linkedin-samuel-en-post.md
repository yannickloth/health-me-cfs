
Samuel was 22 years old. He chose assisted suicide after months of an ordeal that no one should have to endure.

The RTBF (Julien Beauvois) headlined about "long Covid management." Not once does the article name the disease that killed him: Myalgic Encephalomyelitis (ME/CFS).

This is not a detail. It is the heart of the problem.

ME/CFS is a neuroimmune disease recognized by the WHO since 1969, biologically proven by the NIH in 2024, with a documented quality of life lower than that of terminal cancers.

Not naming it means:
— preventing the diagnosis of future patients
— maintaining dangerous treatments (graded exercise) that NICE withdrew in 2021
— abandoning families facing a disease that destroys couples, traumatizes children, and isolates caregivers
— reducing Samuel's act to a "Covid" news item

Samuel knew what he suffered from. He wrote it on Reddit. His last wish was that ME/CFS "no longer be forced into invisibility."

Let us honor that wish. Let us name the disease.
