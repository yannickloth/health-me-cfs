I published an article about how energy production fails in ME/CFS. When I say energy I mean ATP, the molecule your cells run on — not "feeling tired."

I went through the biomedical literature and found over 30 distinct mechanisms where things go wrong, from mitochondrial damage to oxygen delivery to immune cells that can't switch metabolic gears. Most of them come from a single chapter of a larger paper I wrote on ME/CFS pathophysiology. The article includes a full reference list for anyone who wants to dig into the details.

What surprised me most is how few of these can be tested with standard blood work. Patients end up trying supplements and medications more or less blind, spending hundreds of euros a month, waiting months to see a specialist — if they can find one at all. Most GPs have never been trained on any of this.
