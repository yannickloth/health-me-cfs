Each article follows the same structure as [muscle-pain-without-exertion](posts/muscle-pain-without-exertion/index.qmd):
1. Patient experience vignette (2nd person, sensory, immediate)
2. "In healthy people, X happens when Y. In ME/CFS, X happens without Y."

4. Falsifiable predictions
5. Why the conventional explanation gets it wrong

## - #1: flu-like sickness behavior without infection

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Patient hook** | "You feel poisoned. Aching, feverish, malaise so deep it feels like the beginning of the flu — the one where you know you're about to be wrecked for a week. But the fever never comes. You're not contagious. You just feel sick, without being sick." |
| **Normal trigger** | Active infection → peripheral cytokines → sickness behavior |
| **ME/CFS mechanism** | Chronic microglial activation sustains hypothalamic PGE2 signaling independently of peripheral immune activation. "Locked sickness behavior" — CNS-confined neuroinflammation maintains the program after viral clearance |
| **Key doc lines** | ch15-symptom-producing-mechanisms.typ:175, 316–337; ch16-causal-hierarchy.typ:394 |
| **Falsifiable prediction** | If COX-2 inhibitors reduce flu-like symptoms without affecting infection markers, the CNS PGE2 source is confirmed |
| **Can pair with** | #3 brain fog, #10 cachexia-without-cancer |

## - #2: unrefreshing sleep despite adequate hours

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Patient hook** | "You slept nine hours. Dark room, no interruptions, early bedtime. And you wake up like you haven't slept at all — heavy, poisoned, eyes burning. The sleep happened. The recovery didn't." |
| **Normal trigger** | Sleep deprivation → accumulated waste → fatigue on waking |
| **ME/CFS mechanism** | Constitutive mTORC1 hyperactivation blunts nocturnal autophagy surge → damaged proteins/mitochondria accumulate overnight despite objectively normal sleep architecture. CNS-confined inflammation also blocks synaptic downscaling (SV2A PET evidence) |
| **Key doc lines** | hypothesis-registry.typ:1870–1872; ch08-neurological.typ:555–580 |
| **Falsifiable prediction** | If autophagy inducer (e.g. spermidine, fasting-mimetic) reduces morning symptoms independently of sleep duration, mTORC1/autophagy mechanism confirmed |
| **Can pair with** | #3 brain fog, ch08 sleep alpha-delta discussion |

## - #3: brain fog independent of mood and sleep deprivation

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Patient hook** | "You're not depressed. You're not sleep deprived. You want to think — and the words dissolve before they finish forming. The screen blurs. Someone speaks the third sentence while you're still decoding the first. You're not lazy or distracted. You're fogged." |
| **Normal trigger** | Sleep deprivation, mood disorder, or acute metabolic challenge |
| **ME/CFS mechanism** | Neuroinflammation → microglial activation → impaired synaptic function. Cerebral hypoperfusion reduces oxygen/glucose delivery. Central catecholamine deficiency (low CSF dopamine/norepinephrine). All three operate independently of mood and sleep duration |
| **Key doc lines** | ch08-neurological.typ:218, 1978; ch15-symptom-producing-mechanisms.typ:839 |
| **Falsifiable prediction** | If cognitive testing shows dissociation between subjective fog and objective performance, and fMRI shows prefrontal hypoperfusion correlating with fog severity, the perfusional mechanism is supported |
| **Can pair with** | #1 flu-like, #2 unrefreshing sleep, #4 cognitive PEM |

## - #4: cognitive PEM — crash without physical exertion

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Patient hook** | "You didn't move. You just thought too hard — a difficult conversation, a complex email, a decision you couldn't avoid. And now you're crashed. Muscle pain, poisoned feeling, hours in bed. Your body is paying for something you did entirely in your head." |
| **Normal trigger** | Physical exertion exceeding energy envelope |
| **ME/CFS mechanism** | PEM is a threshold phenomenon — it occurs whenever local metabolic demand exceeds local reserve. Prefrontal cortex is one of the most ATP-demanding tissues. Sufficiently intense cognitive exertion produces focal R_crit breach → same PEM cascade, anatomically restricted |
| **Key doc lines** | ch14d-cross-disease.typ:2125; ch39-energy-metabolism-models.typ:435 |
| **Falsifiable prediction** | If 2-day cognitive CPET (standardised mental exertion, retest 24h later) shows worsened performance + PEM symptoms on day 2, cognitive PEM is objectively measurable |
| **Can pair with** | #3 brain fog, the muscle pain article |

## - #5: spontaneous allergic symptoms — MCAS without allergen

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Patient hook** | "4 p.m. again. Your face flushes, your nose runs, your throat tightens — same time as yesterday, same as last Tuesday. There's no pollen. You ate nothing new. No detergent change. No cat. Your mast cells just... decided it was time." |
| **Normal trigger** | Allergen → IgE crosslinking → FcεRI → mast cell degranulation |
| **ME/CFS mechanism** | Non-IgE activation pathways dominate: substance P, CRH, IL-33, MRGPRX2. Mast cell priming can follow circadian rhythms → spontaneous degranulation at predictable times without trigger exposure |
| **Key doc lines** | ch14d-cross-disease.typ:1034; ch14b-autoimmune-immune.typ:704 |
| **Falsifiable prediction** | If mast cell stabilisers (cromolyn, ketotifen) reduce symptoms independently of allergen avoidance, non-IgE activation is confirmed |
| **Can pair with** | #1 flu-like, #7 immune flare |

## - #6: orthostatic physiology at rest — tachycardia without standing

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Patient hook** | "You're lying down. Horizontal, motionless, calm. And your heart is at 95. You check your pulse because it feels wrong — a quiet room, a rested body, and your heart is running like you just climbed stairs. You haven't moved." |
| **Normal trigger** | Orthostatic challenge → baroreflex → compensatory tachycardia |
| **ME/CFS mechanism** | Central-peripheral autonomic dissociation: peripheral sympathetic tone elevated independent of central control. Reduced blood volume + endothelial dysfunction → compensatory tachycardia even supine |
| **Key doc lines** | ch08-neurological.typ:383–385; ch14d-cross-disease.typ:901 |
| **Falsifiable prediction** | If supine plasma norepinephrine is elevated and HR normalises with alpha-blockade, peripheral sympathetic excess confirmed |
| **Can pair with** | The whole autonomic cluster: POTS, blood volume, α-adrenergic autoantibodies |

## - #7: immune flare without active infection

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Patient hook** | "Swollen glands, sore throat, that heavy immune feeling — but no virus. No fever. No positive test. Your body is mounting an immune response to nothing. Or rather, to itself." |
| **Normal trigger** | Active pathogen → innate/adaptive immune activation |
| **ME/CFS mechanism** | Three self-sustaining loops: (1) exosomal HSAT2 satellite RNA propagation → TLR7 → IFN (2) endothelial senescence SASP → chronic cytokine release (3) long-lived plasma cell sanctuary → autoantibody production without B cell restimulation |
| **Key doc lines** | ch07-immune-dysfunction.typ:1710, 2924; ch43-temporal-evolution.typ:61 |
| **Falsifiable prediction** | If anti-IFNAR or JAK inhibitors reduce immune symptoms, the interferon loop is operative. If plasmapheresis reduces symptoms, the autoantibody loop is operative |
| **Can pair with** | #1 flu-like, #5 MCAS |

## - #8: endogenous relapse-remission cycling — without identifiable trigger

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Patient hook** | "You're having a good week. You didn't do anything different — same rest, same diet, same pacing. And then Thursday comes and you're back in bed. There was no trigger. No exertion. No infection. The crash just... arrived." |
| **Normal trigger** | Exogenous perturbation (stress, infection, overexertion) |
| **ME/CFS mechanism** | Coupled multi-system positive feedback (energy-immune, neuroimmune) + delayed negative feedback (cortisol, anti-inflammatory cytokines) → Hopf bifurcation → limit cycle oscillations. Relapse-remission is an endogenous dynamical property of the system |
| **Key doc lines** | ch42-integrated-systems.typ:222; ch43-temporal-evolution.typ:97, 576 |
| **Falsifiable prediction** | If time-series data (daily symptom scores + biomarkers) shows periodicity matching model predictions even when external triggers are controlled, endogenous oscillation confirmed |
| **Can pair with** | #1 flu-like (oscillations in sickness behavior), ch39-energy-metabolism-models |

## - #10: cachexia-like metabolic shutdown without cancer

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Patient hook** | "Your body is conserving energy like you're starving or dying — but you're not. You eat. You rest. You're not fighting a tumor. And yet your metabolism has switched into a survival mode designed for severe illness, and it won't switch back." |
| **Normal trigger** | Cancer, severe infection, trauma → metabolic shutdown for survival |
| **ME/CFS mechanism** | The hypometabolic (dauer-like) state documented by Naviaux: "safe mode" metabolic program designed for survival during life-threatening conditions, locked on inappropriately. "Cachexia without cancer" |
| **Key doc lines** | ch14d-cross-disease.typ:1742; ch06-energy-metabolism.typ:1749 |
| **Falsifiable prediction** | If metabolomics shows the same conserved hypometabolic signature across ME/CFS, long COVID, and cancer cachexia, the shared program hypothesis is supported |
| **Can pair with** | #1 flu-like (both are "the body thinks it's dying" programs) |

## # patient symptoms at rest in severe disease — negative energy budget

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Patient hook** | "You're so severe that you can't meet even basic metabolic needs at rest. Lying flat, in silence, and your body is still in deficit. You're draining power faster than you can generate it, doing nothing." |
| **Normal trigger** | Exertion → exceeding energy envelope |
| **ME/CFS mechanism** | Negative energy budget: ATP demand exceeds production even for basal metabolic functions. Body reduces basal metabolism and defers repair to achieve a precarious balance |
| **Key doc lines** | ch39-energy-metabolism-models.typ:435; ch06-energy-metabolism.typ:2415 |
| **Falsifiable prediction** | If indirect calorimetry shows resting metabolic rate below predicted for body mass, and symptoms worsen with any activity above basal, negative budget confirmed |
| **Can pair with** | #4 cognitive PEM, #9 muscle pain |

- **"The loop closed — and the trigger left"** (immune activation, neuroinflammation, COX-2/PGE2)
- **"The cleanup never finished"** (glymphatic, autophagy, oxidative stress overnight)
