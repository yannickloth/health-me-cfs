## Pattern Analysis

### What Got Written vs. What Didn't

**Written content tends to be:**
- Pathophysiology mechanisms (Part 2 chapters are 50-99 words per section)
- Speculative hypotheses (ch14 series reasonably complete)
- Edge cases and detailed observations (sleep apnea, wheat sensitivity, Lyme)
- Introductory frameworks (comorbidity importance, pacing theory)

**Missing content tends to be:**
- Practical treatment protocols
- Medication dosing and monitoring
- Stepwise clinical decision algorithms
- Patient self-management instructions
- Integration and synthesis sections

### Hypothesis

The document reflects **research-first, clinical-application-second** priorities. Mechanisms, hypotheses, and interesting observations were developed extensively, while practical "how to actually treat patients" content was deferred. This is common in academic writing but problematic for a document intended for clinical use.

### Recommended Priority Shift

To make this document clinically useful, the next phase should **invert priorities**:

1. Fill all empty treatment sections first (Phase 1-2)
2. Expand brief treatment sections to minimum viable (Phase 3)
3. Only then return to expanding pathophysiology stubs (Phase 4-5)

The pathophysiology chapters are already functional - they have structure and key points. The treatment chapters are not - they're skeletons.
