\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Changelog}

This changelog records which chapters and sections were substantially revised
in each version of the document. Minor corrections (typos, formatting) are not
listed. Version markers in the right margin of the text (e.g.\ \textsf{\small v.\,2})
indicate the specific paragraphs or sections that changed.

Each entry follows the format: \textbf{What} changed, \textit{why} it changed
(new evidence, error correction, reviewer feedback, etc.), and which
\textbf{key sources} motivated the change where applicable.

\section*{Version 6 (2026-03-04)}

\begin{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Epistemic stratification --- \texttt{limitation} environment}: New
    \texttt{limitation} and \texttt{limitation*} environments created and deployed
    across 10 locations in Chapters~5, 8, 10, 11, 12, and~13 to explicitly mark
    what the data do \emph{not} allow concluding.
    \textit{Motivated by:} Reviewer feedback requesting a fourth epistemic layer
    (``what not to conclude'') beyond \texttt{hypothesis}/\texttt{speculation}/\texttt{warning}.

  \item \textbf{Appendix K --- Assertion--Source Audit Trail}: New appendix
    auditing 10 key assertions against their cited sources. Verdicts:
    5~SUPPORTS, 4~PARTIALLY~SUPPORTS, 1~DOES~NOT~SUPPORT.
    \textit{Motivated by:} Reviewer concern about assertion--citation traceability.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 18 --- Suramin citation correction}: Corrected suramin
    text to explicitly state the cited pilot study~\cite{Naviaux2018suraminpilot}
    was conducted in autism spectrum disorder, not ME/CFS. Previously implied
    ME/CFS patient data.
    \textit{Motivated by:} Appendix~K audit finding (Row~9, DOES NOT SUPPORT).

  \item \textbf{Chapter 5 --- Endotype table enhanced}: Added reproducibility
    status (R/P), evidence levels (A/B/C), and biomarker accessibility tiers
    to the comprehensive endotype--biomarker--treatment mapping table.
    Added \texttt{limitation} environment for provisional classification status.
    \textit{Motivated by:} Reviewer request for integrated endotype table with
    evidence grades.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 21 --- Negative Trials section}: New section documenting
    9 key negative or null-result trials in ME/CFS (rituximab phase~III,
    PACE trial, IVIG, acyclovir, valganciclovir, rintatolimod, galantamine,
    fluoxetine, hydrocortisone) with summary table and cross-cutting lessons.
    \textit{Motivated by:} Reviewer request for explicit coverage of negative
    evidence to combat confirmation bias.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 25b --- Cross-Document Hypothesis Registry}: New section
    consolidating 20 key hypotheses, speculations, predictions, and open questions
    from a systematic harvest of 305 environments across the document. Organized
    by mechanism domain with testable predictions and available tests.
    \textit{Motivated by:} Reviewer request for hypothesis--prediction--test mapping.

  \item \textbf{Patient FAQ}: New chapter in front matter with 12 questions
    addressing common patient concerns (What is ME/CFS?, PEM, exercise, diagnosis,
    treatments, supplements, Long COVID overlap, promising research).
    \textit{Motivated by:} Reviewer request for accessible patient-facing content.

  \item \textbf{Changelog enriched}: V5 entries now include ``why'' (motivation)
    and key sources for each change, per reviewer recommendation.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 15 --- Environment type correction}: Converted
    \texttt{observation} to \texttt{hypothesis} for mechanistic treatment
    sequencing rationale (certainty: 0.25), fixing epistemic mismatch where
    content self-described as speculative was in an \texttt{observation} environment.
    \textit{Motivated by:} A8 audit finding (Issue~13, HIGH PRIORITY).

  \item \textbf{Chapter 14c --- Speculative model language}: Revised text
    presenting the time-dependent reversibility model from asserting it
    ``explains'' outcomes to stating it ``hypothesizes'' outcomes, with
    ``speculative'' qualifier added.
    \textit{Motivated by:} A8 audit findings (Issues~28--29, HIGH PRIORITY).

  \item \textbf{A8 audit completed}: Full Part~III hypothesis vs.\ recommendation
    audit identified 31 findings (3 critical fixed, 14 medium, 14 low priority).
    Report archived for future revision cycles.

\end{itemize}

\section*{Version 5 (2026-03-04)}

\begin{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Chapter 1 — Introduction to ME/CFS}: Expanded coverage of
    ME/CFS terminology, disease classification, and epidemiological context.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 5 — Disease Course and Subgroups}: Added new material
    on patient subgroups and phenotypic variability.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 6 — Energy Metabolism}: Major expansion covering new
    research on mitochondrial dysfunction, metabolic trap hypothesis, and the
    relationship between immune activation and energy production. Three-pass
    scientific rigor review of heterogeneity and energy sections.
    \textit{Motivated by:} Hwang et al.~2023 (WASF3--Complex~IV mechanism)~\cite{hwang2023wasf3};
    Keller et al.~2024 (two-day CPET)~\cite{keller2024cpet};
    Cheema et al.~2023 (miRNA convergence)~\cite{Cheema2023mirna}.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 7 — Immune System Dysfunction}: Integrated new findings
    on the gut-immune-brain axis, including the role of vagal signalling in
    immune dysregulation and the cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway. Resolved
    citation violations.
    \textit{Motivated by:} Heng et al.~2025 (T cell effector memory profiling)~\cite{heng2025mecfs};
    scientific rigor audit identifying uncited claims.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 8 — Neurological Dysfunction}: Added the
    enterochromaffin-vagal pathway hypothesis (Wirth--Scheibenbogen 2025),
    explaining how gut dysbiosis drives autonomic dysfunction via serotonin and
    vagal mechanisms.
    \textit{Motivated by:} Barton et al.~2023, 2025 (enterochromaffin serotonin--vagal
    signaling)~\cite{Barton2023,Barton2025}.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 10 — Cardiovascular Dysfunction}: Expanded
    gut-mediated vagal impairment section with Wirth--Scheibenbogen 2025
    mechanistic support and new cross-references.
    \textit{Motivated by:} van~Campen et al.~2024 (cardiac output--cerebral blood flow
    study, $n=534$)~\cite{VanCampenEtAl2024}.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 11 — Gut Microbiome}: Expanded clinical implications of
    microbiome alterations and their connection to systemic symptoms.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 14a — Urgent Action Plan for Severe Cases}: Added
    phenotyping cross-references and energy-category treatment sequencing
    guidance.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 14b — Action Plans for Mild to Moderate Cases}: Integrated
    a new acute-onset protocol for patients in early disease stages; expanded
    severity-stratified care pathways.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 14j — Selective Energy Dysfunction Hypothesis}: Thorough
    scientific rigor review; strengthened internal consistency, corrected
    mathematical formulations, and expanded related-hypotheses discussion.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 15 — Symptom-Producing Mechanisms}: Updated MCAS
    prevalence citations and mast cell--microglia reference keys.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 15 — Medications Targeting Underlying Mechanisms}: Significant
    expansion covering immune-modulating agents, antivirals, mitochondrial
    support, and phenotype-targeted treatment pathways.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 16 — Supplements and Nutraceuticals}: Substantially updated
    based on recent evidence; expanded foundational and mitochondrial support
    protocols; added new supplement categories.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 17 — Lifestyle Interventions}: Minor corrections and
    clarifications.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 18 — Experimental and Emerging Therapies}: Added
    energy profile for immunoadsorption; corrected nicotinamide riboside
    trial data and citation.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 19 — Integrative and Personalised Approaches}: Major
    expansion covering cross-domain medical parallels, novel mechanistic
    hypotheses, combination therapy strategies, and research opportunities.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 20 — Biomarker Research}: Minor updates reflecting recent
    findings.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 25 — Translational Findings}: Substantially revised
    shared-mechanism tables with added citations; corrected treatment dosing
    and trial data; improved specificity of cross-condition evidence.

  \item \textbf{Chapter 25b — Proposed Research Studies}: New chapter added,
    describing fourteen original research protocols targeting key open questions
    in ME/CFS pathophysiology and treatment.

  \item \textbf{Appendix H — Annotated Bibliography}: New appendix added,
    providing a comprehensive annotated bibliography of ME/CFS literature
    organized by research domain.

\end{itemize}
