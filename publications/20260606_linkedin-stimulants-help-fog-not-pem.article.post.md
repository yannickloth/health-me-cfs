Stimulants improve brain fog in 77% of ME/CFS patients. Their net effect on PEM: -1.5% — essentially zero.

These numbers aren't contradictory. They're exactly what the metabolic reserve model predicts — and they reveal an untested treatment combination.

New article on why stimulants help cognition but not the crash:

- Stimulants reduce energy DEMAND per cognitive unit — they don't increase energy SUPPLY
- The behavioural trap: feeling better → doing more → exceeding the envelope
- The -1.5% is the average of two opposing effects (patients who pace vs. patients who don't)
- The untested prediction: stimulants + strict pacing should REDUCE PEM frequency
- Why methylphenidate may also protect mitochondria (Parkin, ROS reduction)
- Practical rules for using stimulants without paying the crash tax
