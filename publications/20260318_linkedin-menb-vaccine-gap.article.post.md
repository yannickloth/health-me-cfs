If you were born before 2015, you have never been vaccinated against the type of meningitis behind this week's UK outbreak — now declared a national incident, with two deaths and twenty cases. Neither have your parents, your colleagues, or your doctor. The standard meningitis vaccines cover serogroups A, C, W, and Y. Not B.

The vaccine has existed since 2013. The real-world data from millions of UK doses shows 83% effectiveness. But across Belgium, France, Germany, Luxembourg, and most of Europe, no routine programme exists for anyone beyond infants. The policy hasn't caught up with the science.
