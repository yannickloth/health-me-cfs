Migraine patients have a 1.5× elevated risk of developing ME/CFS. By age 65+, that risk doubles to 2.11×. The risk scales with migraine frequency — a dose-response relationship.

This is what decades of cumulative energy depletion look like.

New article on migraine as progressive metabolic reserve erosion:

- Cortical spreading depression: one of the most energy-demanding events in neurobiology
- Migraineurs show chronic mitochondrial impairment BETWEEN attacks (elevated lactate, reduced complex IV)
- Each CSD event depletes and damages mitochondria; each recovery is slightly incomplete
- The same triggers (stress, sleep deprivation, fasting, exercise) provoke both migraine and ME/CFS relapse
- Why aggressive migraine prevention is metabolic reserve preservation
- The age amplification explained: 50 years of CSD > 10 years of CSD

Based on Lau et al. 2015 (Taiwan national cohort, n=34,510) and Wang et al. 2023 (mitochondrial review).
