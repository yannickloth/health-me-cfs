### What the document covers

The document synthesizes hundreds of peer-reviewed sources into five parts and nine appendices:

**Part I — Clinical Overview** covers the evolution of diagnostic criteria, detailed symptom characterization, severity phenotyping from mild to very severe, and disease course variations including subgroup analysis. A patient FAQ provides 12 accessible entry-point questions.

**Part II — Pathophysiology** examines mechanisms across eleven chapters (expanded in v6 with a new causal hierarchy chapter): mitochondrial dysfunction and energy metabolism, immune exhaustion and autoimmunity, neuroinflammation and central sensitization, endocrine dysregulation, cardiovascular and autonomic failure, gut-microbiome disruption, genetic and epigenetic factors, integrative multi-system models, speculative hypotheses with falsifiable predictions, symptom-producing mechanisms, and a new causal hierarchy classifying which mechanisms are root causes versus amplifiers versus downstream consequences.

**Part III — Treatment** covers urgent action protocols for severe and very severe cases, pediatric-specific management, symptom-based management, medications, supplement regimens, lifestyle interventions, and emerging therapies including immunoadsorption, BC007 (rovunaptabin), daratumumab, LDN, and TMS. All treatment content explicitly distinguishes evidence-based recommendations from mechanistic hypotheses.

**Part IV — Research** synthesizes biomarker research, clinical trial outcomes, the PACE trial controversy, translational findings, and proposed research protocols. New in v6: substantially expanded coverage of the psychosomatic history parallel across ten diseases, FITNET replication failure, and systematic blinding failure analysis.

**Part V — Computational Modeling** develops formal ODE-based mathematical models of energy metabolism, immune cell dynamics, neuroendocrine regulation, multi-system coupling, temporal disease evolution, and predictive clinical applications. New in v6: a healthy exercise baseline model for face validation, a four-compartment nociceptive pain model, and a formal causal hierarchy chapter with attractor analysis and minimum intervention set enumeration.

**Nine appendices** provide a glossary, abbreviations, diagnostic tools, patient resources, mathematical derivations, supplement protocols, research synthesis, an annotated bibliography, and a registry of ongoing ME/CFS studies.

### What's new in Version 6

**Causal hierarchy (Chapters 16 and 33).** A new sister chapter pair synthesizes all pathophysiology mechanisms and formal models into a three-tier causal classification: trigger-capable root causes, amplifiers, and downstream consequences. Four trigger-capable mechanisms are identified (CNS energy crisis, metabolic safe mode lock, GPCR autoantibody cascade, TRPM3 channelopathy). Chapter 33 formalizes the hierarchy with the ODE model, performs trigger sufficiency analysis, derives a Minimum Lock Release Theorem predicting that recovery from the severe/locked attractor requires simultaneous restoration of 3–4 parameters, and introduces a separatrix nudging framework for treatment sequencing.

**Genetics: DecodeME GWAS integration.** The largest ME/CFS genetic study to date integrated in depth: 8 genome-wide significant loci, 9.5% SNP heritability, brain-focused gene expression (CA10, SHISA6, SOX6, LRRC7, DCC, UNC13C), genetic correlations with IBS (r_g=0.75) and depression (r_g=0.60) but not autoimmune conditions, and autophagy genes (FBXL4, CCPG1) motivating a new Genetic Mitophagy Vulnerability hypothesis. Four novel cross-system hypotheses generated from DecodeME genetic correlations: Glutamatergic–Autonomic Bridge, Amitriptyline Signal, Diagnostic Mirage, and a Peptic Ulcer Parallel historical analogy.

**Exercise physiology critique.** NASA bed rest comparison refuting deconditioning as explanation; Keller 2024 CPET biomarker limitations (Bell scale ρ=−0.009, group overlap); two-day CPET discrepancies documented; boom-and-bust myth debunked by accelerometry. Supercompensation Failure hypothesis formalized as mechanistic basis of exercise intolerance with healthy exercise baseline model for validation.

**Immune null findings and novel hypotheses.** Systematic integration of well-powered null results: Germain 2025 autoantibody screen (7,542 interactions, complete null), MCAM NK cytotoxicity study (n=174, p=0.79), Baraniuk 2024 NK meta-analysis. Balanced against: IGHV3-30 B cell receptor skewing with three-group replication, TGF-β Rosetta Stone hypothesis linking NK dysfunction to TGF-β signaling, frustrated NETosis hypothesis. Six new hypotheses and two major open questions formalized.

**Pain mechanisms.** Three new mechanistic subsections: nerve sheath pain (Schwann cell TRPA1 activation, endoneurial hypoxia, autoimmune targeting, perineurial mast cell degranulation), periarticular and muscular pain (ASIC3 combinatorial gating, P2X3, TRPV1, fascial innervation, microvascular ischemia), and a stimulation intolerance unified model. Formal four-compartment nociceptive model in Chapter 29.

**Mast cell activation.** New comprehensive coverage of non-IgE food-triggered mast cell degranulation (MRGPRX2, complement C3a/C5a, lectin–IgE glycan cross-linking), mediator-to-symptom mapping, and a practical elimination-rechallenge protocol adapted for energy-limited patients. MCAS antihistamine up-dosing section with drug-specific dosing tables and a four-step protocol reflecting EAACI urticaria guidelines.

**IgG immune complex–endothelial injury.** Liu 2026 demonstrates IgG immune complexes from ME/CFS patient sera enter endothelial cells, induce mitochondrial fragmentation, and trigger IL-1β secretion, with subtype-specific proteomic signatures distinguishing classic from post-COVID ME/CFS. Integrated across energy metabolism, immune, and cardiovascular chapters with a new speculation on immune complex–endothelial injury as central vascular mechanism.

**TMS evidence review.** Comprehensive section on transcranial magnetic stimulation covering neurophysiological rationale (motor cortex hypoexcitability), four ME/CFS therapeutic studies (total n≈72), and indirect sham-controlled evidence from fibromyalgia meta-analyses (11–18 RCTs).

**BC007 expanded.** Full subsubsection covering mechanism of action, Phase IIa (n=29) and Phase II RCT (n=114) data, seven-dimension comparison with immunoadsorption, and cross-reference to GPCR autoantibody cascade.

**Evolutionary PEM hypothesis.** New speculation proposing the 12–72 hour PEM delay reflects an evolutionarily selected deferred-cost architecture: the emergency override enabling exertion through acute threat is intact; the restoration phase is broken. Connects to sickness behaviour biology and adrenergic physiology literature.

**Critical evidence and methodology.** FITNET-NHS replication failure (n=314, 25% deterioration), systematic blinding failure pattern in ME/CFS trials, Cochrane exercise review critique, NICE guideline shift with GRADE ceiling analysis, catastrophizing critique (PCS measures pain not distortion), and a psychosomatic history parallel documenting ten diseases that followed the same trajectory before biological mechanisms were confirmed (MS, asthma, epilepsy, diabetes, autism, schizophrenia, cancer, heart disease, RA, peptic ulcer).

**Oral microbiome.** New section identifying the oral compartment as an unstudied inflammatory reservoir in ME/CFS, with three candidate pathways (periodontal bacteremia, _P. gingivalis_ neuroinflammation and BBB disruption, citrullination-driven neoantigen generation) and an oral dysbiosis–disability vicious cycle speculation.

**Post-viral pituitary injury.** New subsubsection on structural HPA axis damage as a fourth mechanistic pathway distinct from functional dysregulation, covering Leow 2005 SARS-1 data (39.3% central hypocortisolism), COVID-19 pituitary literature, and Ruiz-Pablos autoimmune hypophysitis model.

**LaTeX to Typst migration.** The entire document source was migrated from LaTeX to Typst, reducing compilation time and maintenance friction. All 40 TikZ figures converted to CeTZ equivalents.

**100+ new references** spanning genetics, exercise physiology, immunology, pain mechanisms, pharmacology, and epidemiology.
