Version 6 of my open-access ME/CFS comprehensive medical documentation is now published on Zenodo.

The document synthesizes hundreds of peer-reviewed sources into a single reference covering clinical presentation, pathophysiology across ten mechanism domains plus a new causal hierarchy chapter, treatment protocols (including severe/very severe and pediatric care), research evidence, and formal mathematical modeling of disease dynamics.

New in v6:
- New causal hierarchy chapter classifying which mechanisms are root causes vs. amplifiers vs. downstream consequences — with a formal Minimum Lock Release Theorem predicting recovery requires simultaneous restoration of 3–4 parameters
- DecodeME GWAS integration: 8 genome-wide significant loci, 9.5% SNP heritability, brain-focused gene expression, genetic correlations with IBS (0.75) and depression (0.60) but not autoimmune conditions
- Exercise physiology critique: NASA bed rest comparison, CPET biomarker limitations, boom-and-bust myth debunked by accelerometry
- Immune null findings integrated alongside positive results: balanced picture of what is and isn't replicated
- Pain mechanisms: nerve sheath, periarticular/muscular, and stimulation intolerance — now with formal four-compartment nociceptive model
- Non-IgE food-triggered mast cell activation and MCAS antihistamine up-dosing protocol
- IgG immune complexes as direct endothelial and mitochondrial injurers (Liu 2026)
- BC007 (rovunaptabin) comprehensive coverage; TMS evidence review
- Evolutionary PEM timing hypothesis: the 12–72 hour delay as a deferred-cost survival architecture
- Oral microbiome as unstudied inflammatory reservoir
- Post-viral pituitary injury as a fourth HPA axis mechanism
- Critical methodology section: FITNET replication failure, blinding failures, psychosomatic history parallel across 10 diseases
- Complete migration from LaTeX to Typst
- 100+ new references

For researchers, clinicians, patients, caregivers, and advocates.
