TSH 2.8. Reference range 0.4-4.0. "Normal thyroid."

TSH is the pituitary gland's opinion about circulating thyroid hormones. It says nothing about whether T3 — the active hormone — is reaching your mitochondria and driving the metabolic processes it's supposed to drive.

Three situations produce cellular hypothyroidism with a normal TSH:

Central hypothyroidism — the pituitary itself is underperforming, common when the HPA axis is suppressed (documented in ME/CFS). It sends a "normal" signal because its definition of normal has been recalibrated downward.

Impaired T4-to-T3 conversion — chronic inflammation suppresses the enzyme (DIO2) that activates thyroid hormone and upregulates the enzyme (DIO3) that inactivates it. The result is low T3, high reverse T3, normal TSH. Euthyroid sick syndrome. Occurs in any chronic inflammatory state.

Hashimoto's thyroiditis — autoimmune destruction of the thyroid, affecting 10% of the population. Takes years before TSH rises above range. Fatigue correlates with antibody levels, not TSH. A €20 antibody test catches it. Most GPs don't order it.

The complete panel (TSH + free T3 + free T4 + reverse T3 + antibodies) costs €50-80. The standard panel — TSH alone — costs €15 and misses every scenario above.
