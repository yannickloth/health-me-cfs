EBV. Q fever. Ross River virus. Giardia. SARS-CoV-2. Chikungunya. Dengue.

Seven taxonomically unrelated organisms. Different kingdoms of life. Different tissues infected, different immune responses triggered, different replication strategies.

Same aftermath. 10-12% of severe acute infections lead to a chronic fatigue syndrome clinically indistinguishable from ME/CFS, regardless of which pathogen caused the acute illness. The Dubbo Infection Outcomes Study showed this prospectively in 2006: the severity of acute illness predicted who developed chronic fatigue. The identity of the pathogen did not.

COVID proved it at scale. Tens of millions of Long COVID cases. The same pattern that was always there for every pathogen, finally visible because the numbers were too large to ignore.

The convergence forces a conclusion: ME/CFS is not a chronic infection disease. It is a host-response disease. The pathogen triggers the immune cascade. The illness is what the immune system does after the trigger is gone — a resolution failure where the combat phase never transitions to recovery.

This reframes treatment. Stop hunting for the one pathogen. Treat the stuck immune state: the autoantibodies, the trained immunity, the exhausted T cells, the persistent danger signals. The entry point differs between patients. The current disease does not.
