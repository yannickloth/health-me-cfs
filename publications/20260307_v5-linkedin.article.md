### What the document covers

The document synthesizes hundreds of peer-reviewed sources into five parts and nine appendices:

**Part I — Clinical Overview** covers the evolution of diagnostic criteria from Fukuda through the Canadian Consensus and International Consensus Criteria, detailed symptom characterization, severity phenotyping from mild to very severe presentations, and disease course variations including subgroup analysis.

**Part II — Pathophysiology** examines mechanisms across ten chapters: mitochondrial dysfunction and energy metabolism, immune exhaustion and autoimmunity, neuroinflammation and central sensitization, endocrine dysregulation, cardiovascular and autonomic failure, gut-microbiome disruption, genetic and epigenetic factors, integrative multi-system models, speculative hypotheses with falsifiable predictions, and symptom-producing mechanisms. Key studies integrated include the February 2024 NIH deep phenotyping study demonstrating measurable immune, neurological, and metabolic dysfunction, and the WASF3-Complex IV mitochondrial findings.

**Part III — Treatment** opens with urgent action protocols for severe and very severe cases, followed by pediatric-specific management. It then covers symptom-based management (pacing, sleep, pain, cognitive dysfunction, autonomic symptoms), medications targeting underlying mechanisms, supplement regimens, lifestyle interventions, emerging therapies (immunoadsorption, suramin, LDN), and integrative personalized approaches. All treatment content explicitly distinguishes evidence-based recommendations from mechanistic hypotheses.

**Part IV — Research** synthesizes biomarker research, clinical trial outcomes — including a dedicated section on nine key negative or null-result trials (rituximab phase III, PACE, IVIG, and others) — mechanistic studies, epidemiological patterns, the PACE trial controversy, translational findings, and fourteen fully specified proposed research protocols with explicit hypotheses and trial designs.

**Part V — Computational Modeling** develops formal ODE-based mathematical models of energy metabolism, immune cell dynamics, neuroendocrine regulation, multi-system coupling with feedback loops, temporal disease evolution with tipping-point dynamics, and predictive clinical applications for pacing optimization and treatment selection. Each model includes practical application guides specifying required measurements, worked examples, and falsification criteria.

**Nine appendices** provide a glossary, abbreviations, diagnostic tools, patient resources, full mathematical derivations, supplement protocols, a research synthesis, an annotated bibliography, and a registry of ongoing ME/CFS studies.

### What's new in Version 5

**Epistemic infrastructure.** A structured classification system now distinguishes achievements, hypotheses (certainty >= 0.45), speculations (certainty < 0.45), predictions, warnings, and recommendations throughout the document. A new `limitation` environment deployed across 40+ locations explicitly marks what the data do not support concluding. A cross-document hypothesis registry tracks 40+ speculative claims with falsifiability assessments.

**Comprehensive biomedical review.** A 20-category scientific review system covering pathway accuracy, immunology, biochemistry, neuroscience, microbiome, biomarkers, epidemiology, comorbidities, pharmacology, patient safety, falsifiability, and bibliography was applied throughout.

**Negative evidence.** Nine key negative or null-result trials are now documented with a summary table and cross-cutting lessons — directly addressing confirmation bias, which is rarely done in patient-oriented medical writing.

**Part V expansion.** Mathematical models received practical Model Application Guides with required measurements, worked examples with realistic parameter values, inter-model dependencies, falsification criteria, and clinical implications. A coverage justification maps which pathophysiological domains are and aren't modeled. Ten new TikZ illustrations were added.

**Part III audit.** A systematic audit of all treatment chapters identified and corrected 31 instances where mechanistic hypotheses were presented as clinical recommendations (3 critical).

**Patient FAQ.** A new front-matter chapter with 12 questions addressing common patient concerns — diagnosis, PEM, exercise, treatments, Long COVID overlap, and promising research directions.

**200+ new references** spanning recent ME/CFS research across all domains.
