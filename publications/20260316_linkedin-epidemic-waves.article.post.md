ME/CFS did not start with COVID. The first documented outbreak was in 1934.

Los Angeles, 1934. Iceland, 1948. London, 1955. Lake Tahoe, 1984. Each time, a viral epidemic left behind a wave of patients with the same chronic post-infectious illness. Each time, it was investigated briefly, then forgotten — and patients were told it was psychological.

COVID-19 broke the cycle through sheer scale. The RECOVER study found that 4.5% of infected people developed ME/CFS, pushing US cases from 1.5 million to an estimated 5–9 million. Too many to dismiss.
