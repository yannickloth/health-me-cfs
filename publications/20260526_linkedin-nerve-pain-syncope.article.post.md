Hit a nerve — wrist on a desk corner, elbow on a doorframe, shin on a coffee table — and for about one second you think "oh, I hit myself there." Then the pain starts materializing and rising instead of fading. Another one to two seconds pass. Before words can form, your brain's verdict lands: *not survivable standing up.* Everything goes dark. You wake up on the floor.

Here's exactly what happened in those ~3 seconds:

1. **Aδ fibers** fired first — the express-lane pain wires that say "you got hit, right there." That gave the 1-second window of thinking it was just a bruise.

2. **C fibers** joined in — the slow, unmyelinated wires that release substance P at the injury site, triggering local inflammation that amplifies the pain *even after the hit is over*. That's why the pain escalates instead of fading.

3. The **nucleus tractus solitarius (NTS)** in the brainstem received this massive nociceptive volley and triggered an emergency override.

4. Two things happened simultaneously: **vagal output to the heart surged** (bradycardia — heart rate drops) AND **sympathetic output to blood vessels shut off** (vasodilation — blood pressure plummets).

5. **Cerebral perfusion collapsed.** No blood reaching the brain. Consciousness failed.

6. The body collapsed horizontal — which is exactly the most effective countermeasure. On the floor, gravity no longer pulls blood away from the brain. Perfusion restored.

7. Consciousness returned a few seconds later, with nausea, pallor, and confusion about what just happened.

Why do some people pass out from a nerve hit and others just curse at the furniture?

- **High baseline vagal tone** → bigger heart-rate drop from the same pain input
- **POTS** → impaired vasoconstriction, less cardiovascular reserve, often low blood volume
- **Pre-existing nerve inflammation** (neuritis, neuropathy, EDS) → sensitized nociceptors → larger pain volley hitting the NTS
- **ME/CFS / Long COVID** → autonomic dysfunction, reduced baroreflex sensitivity, neuroinflammation lowering the NTS threshold
- **Iron deficiency** → impaired norepinephrine synthesis → lower baseline sympathetic tone → less blood pressure to lose before hitting the floor
- **Anxiety / pain hypervigilance** → top-down cortical amplification of the NTS response

This is not a psychological overreaction. It's a hardwired brainstem reflex — same circuit in every human. The 1-second lag between the sharp jab and the intolerable wave is the exact signature of Aδ-first, C-fiber-amplified nociception triggering the autonomic override.
