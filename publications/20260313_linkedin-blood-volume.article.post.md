The average adult has 5 liters of blood. Many ME/CFS patients are missing 500-750 ml of that. Permanently.

That's not a metaphor. Gold-standard radioisotope measurements show total blood volume deficits of 10-15% — comparable to having just donated blood, except the body never replenishes the loss. Plasma volume alone can be 21% lower.

New article on why this single finding explains so much:

- Why 70-90% of patients can't tolerate standing — there literally isn't enough blood to reach the brain upright
- What a 15% volume deficit actually does downstream: reduced cardiac output, up to 40% less cerebral blood flow, and tissue-level oxygen starvation
- The hormones that should compensate — aldosterone and ADH — are themselves suppressed, both running 33% below normal
- Why standard blood tests miss it entirely (red blood cell concentration looks normal even when total volume is 15% low)

All the numbers come from peer-reviewed literature, including studies in Circulation. And this isn't a fringe finding — it's one of the most replicated results in ME/CFS research.
