# LinkedIn Announcement Post

Version 5 of my open-access ME/CFS comprehensive medical documentation is now published on Zenodo.

The document synthesizes hundreds of peer-reviewed sources into a single reference covering clinical presentation, pathophysiology across ten mechanism domains, treatment protocols (including severe/very severe and pediatric care), research evidence, and formal mathematical modeling of disease dynamics.

New in v5:
- Structured epistemic classification: every claim graded by certainty, with explicit limitation markers and a falsifiability-tracked hypothesis registry
- 20-category biomedical review applied throughout
- Dedicated negative evidence section covering 9 null-result trials (rituximab phase III, PACE, IVIG, and more)
- Expanded mathematical models with practical application guides, worked examples, and falsification criteria
- Part III audit correcting 31 cases where hypotheses were presented as recommendations
- Patient FAQ with 12 accessible entry-point questions
- 200+ new references

For researchers, clinicians, patients, caregivers, and advocates.
