\section*{Protocol Overview}
%------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This simplified protocol focuses on the core interventions for:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Restoring fat-burning capability (addressing ``running on empty'' sensation)
    \item Maximizing mitochondrial energy production
    \item Correcting vitamin D deficiency (common with fat malabsorption)
    \item Supporting cellular ATP availability
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=yellow!10,colframe=orange!75!black,title=\textbf{Core Principle}]
Most chronic fatigue involves \textbf{impaired fat metabolism}. The body cannot access stored fat for energy despite having adequate reserves. This protocol:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Provides immediate energy bypass (MCT oil)
    \item Repairs the fat-burning system (acetyl-L-carnitine)
    \item Supports the energy production machinery (CoQ10, B vitamins, magnesium)
    \item Ensures fat-soluble vitamin absorption (critical for vitamin D)
\end{enumerate}
\end{tcolorbox}

\section*{Morning Protocol (with breakfast)}
%------------------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection*{Core Energy Supplements}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Acetyl-L-Carnitine 1000\,mg}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Opens the ``carnitine shuttle'' to transport fats into mitochondria for energy
        \item \textbf{This is the root cause fix for fat metabolism}
        \item Timeline: 4--6 weeks initial effect; 3--6 months maximum benefit
        \item Can take on empty stomach or with food
    \end{itemize}

    \item \textbf{CoQ10 Ubiquinol 100--200\,mg} \textcolor{red}{$\star$ needs fat}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Essential cofactor in mitochondrial energy production (electron transport chain)
        \item Antioxidant protecting mitochondrial membranes
        \item \textbf{MUST take with dietary fat} or absorption is <10\%
        \item Ubiquinol is the active, reduced form (better absorption than ubiquinone)
    \end{itemize}

    \item \textbf{B-Complex (containing at least B1, B2, B3, B5, B6, B12, Folate)}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item B vitamins are cofactors in energy metabolism at every step
        \item B2 (riboflavin) essential for fat-burning
        \item B12 and folate support methylation and cellular energy
        \item Any reputable B-complex works
    \end{itemize}

    \item \textbf{Magnesium Glycinate 200--400\,mg}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Cofactor for ATP synthesis (your body makes ATP using magnesium)
        \item ``Off switch'' for muscle contraction --- prevents cramps
        \item Glycinate form: best absorbed, gentlest on stomach
        \item Can split dose: half morning, half bedtime
    \end{itemize}

    \item \textbf{MCT Oil 1 tablespoon (15\,mL)}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Medium-chain fats bypass the broken carnitine shuttle
        \item Provide immediate energy while acetyl-L-carnitine repairs the system
        \item Go straight to liver; preferentially burned for energy (not stored as fat)
        \item Also helps fat-soluble vitamin absorption
        \item Add to coffee, tea, smoothie, or drizzle on food
        \item \textcolor{red}{Start with 1 tsp; increase slowly over 1--2 weeks to avoid GI upset}
    \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Breakfast Requirements}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=yellow!10,colframe=orange!75!black]
\textbf{Breakfast MUST contain dietary fat for CoQ10 absorption:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Best:} MCT oil (already in protocol), olive oil, eggs, fatty fish
    \item \textbf{Good:} Avocado, nuts, nut butter, full-fat yogurt, cheese
    \item \textbf{Minimum:} 1--2 tablespoons of fat source
\end{itemize}

Without fat, CoQ10 absorption fails and vitamin D will remain deficient.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section*{Important Notes}
%------------------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection*{Why This Protocol Works}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=green!5,colframe=green!75!black,title=The Three-Part Strategy]

\textbf{1. Immediate Energy Bypass}
\begin{itemize}
    \item MCT oil provides fuel that mitochondria can use \textit{right now}
    \item Bypasses blocked carnitine shuttle
    \item Supports you while repair happens
\end{itemize}

\textbf{2. Root Cause Repair}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Acetyl-L-carnitine opens carnitine shuttle over weeks
    \item Restores ability to burn stored body fat
    \item Addresses the fundamental energy deficit
\end{itemize}

\textbf{3. System Optimization}
\begin{itemize}
    \item CoQ10, B vitamins, magnesium support the energy production machinery
    \item Once fuel can get into mitochondria, these ensure it's used efficiently
    \item Maximizes ATP output from available fuel
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Fat-Soluble Vitamin Fix}
\begin{itemize}
    \item MCT oil + dietary fat ensure vitamin D actually absorbs
    \item Fixes chronic deficiency common in fatigue conditions
\end{itemize}

\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{What NOT to Do}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textcolor{red}{Never skip dietary fat} when taking CoQ10 or vitamin D --- absorption will fail
    \item \textcolor{red}{Don't increase MCT oil too quickly} --- causes diarrhea (GI system needs to adapt)
    \item \textcolor{red}{Don't expect instant results} --- acetyl-L-carnitine takes 4--6 weeks minimum
    \item \textcolor{red}{Don't stop after 2 weeks} if you don't see dramatic changes --- repair takes time
    \item \textcolor{red}{Don't use coconut oil instead of MCT oil} --- coconut oil is only $\sim$15\% MCTs; rest requires broken carnitine shuttle
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{When to Add More}

This is a \textbf{light protocol}. Consider adding if:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Vitamin D remains low after 2--3 months $\rightarrow$ add digestive enzymes with lipase
    \item Significant migraines $\rightarrow$ add riboflavin B2 400\,mg at dinner
    \item Poor sleep or severe nocturnal cramps persist $\rightarrow$ add D-Ribose at bedtime
    \item Iron deficiency confirmed $\rightarrow$ add iron supplement with vitamin C (morning, separate from magnesium)
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{When to Consult Healthcare Provider}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Before starting if you have liver disease, kidney disease, or take medications
    \item If no improvement after 2--3 months
    \item If symptoms worsen
    \item To verify vitamin D levels after 2--3 months
    \item If considering adding other supplements or medications
\end{itemize}
