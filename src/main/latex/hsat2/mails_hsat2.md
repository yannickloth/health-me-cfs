> > > > > > Compatibility: No, the paper (le mien, avant intégration de ton hypothèse) is not fully compatible (avec ton hypothèse). The integration added a speculation layer without reconciling deeper contradictions. The ODE model (ch33) still defines cal(M) as driven upward by DNMT3A/B with demethylation as reversal — if consolidation is loss, the variable should move down. The treatment-order hypothesis (hyp:treatment-order) still recommends epigenetic modifiers like 5-azacitidine and HDAC inhibitors as the final step, all of which worsen hypomethylation. The reverse-cascade prediction (hyp:reverse-cascade) says epigenetic normalization comes last during recovery — but if loss is the problem, remethylation might need to come first. The integration flagged these tensions (oq:consolidation-directionality, the ch14 treatment gap update) but did not resolve them. The paper currently speaks with two voices on this.

The "intervention window" would be redefined not as time before methylation accumulates, but as time before methylation at ProB repeats erodes below a recoverable threshold.

> > > > > > The gap: Under the current gain-model, the paper (le mien) says demethylating agents (HDAC inhibitors, 5-azacitidine) should work. The only medications with any mechanistic safety regardless of direction are methyl donors — and their efficacy for ME/CFS symptoms is unproven.
