## Verdicts

### 1. Clarity of the central hypothesis — PASS
The abstract states the cascade explicitly (viral centromere disruption → HSAT2 transcription → methylation loss → EV propagation → immune exhaustion) and explicitly disclaims definitive proof ("Rather than presenting definitive proof, this Perspective assembles the available evidence...").

### 2. Accessibility to target audience — PASS
All terms glossed on first use (pericentromere, CpG, heterochromatin, EVs, MDSCs, NK cells, SAMe, HDAC, TRAP-ddPCR, RT-qPCR, RNA-seq). Abstract is verbatim per author and contains some unglossed terms (H3K9me3), but the body defines them immediately in §1.

### 3. Narrative arc — PASS
§1 (what HSAT2 is) → §2 (what it does in cancer) → §3 (why it may apply in PAIS) → §4 (how to test). Earlier drafts included tangential material (HSF1, CTCF, SPI1, endothelial-HSC transition); removed to match the abstract's scope.

### 4. Evidence calibration — PASS
Language is hedged throughout: "may contribute," "we propose," "we hypothesize," "By analogy with cancer," "would support the hypothesis." The one strong claim ("DNA methylation patterns are known to be altered in ME/CFS and Long COVID") is directly supported by cited literature.

### 5. Falsifiability — PASS
Four numbered predictions; §4 states both what would support the hypothesis and what would argue against it; names specific assays (TRAP-ddPCR, hybridisation capture, RT-qPCR) and the stratification variable (viral trigger).

### 6. Mechanistic coherence — PASS (one soft link)
Trigger (viral centromere amplification) → derepression (methylation loss) → transcription → EV packaging → MDSC expansion → immune exhaustion are each independently documented. The soft link: transfer from brain-localized centromere disruption to circulating EV HSAT2 in PAIS is inferred, not yet shown — flagged in the Limitations paragraph.

### 7. Reference discipline — PASS
15 references, all cited; two preprints flagged in Acknowledgments and `note` field; BonnetFourel2026ProAB (senior author's framework paper) cited for the epigenetic-erosion claim.

### 8. Clinical relevance — PASS
Subgroup (EV HSAT2-positive) and biomarker panel predictions are directly clinically relevant. Prediction 4 names methyl donors as a potential intervention and demethylating drugs as predicted to worsen erosion — explicitly framed as a testable prediction with the italic disclaimer "These are testable predictions, not treatment recommendations."

### 9. Honesty about limitations — PASS
A dedicated Limitations paragraph at the end of §4 names the two weakest points explicitly: the single-study dependency of the cancer EV evidence and the inferred (not observed) transfer from virus-disrupted cells to circulating HSAT2. It closes with "This Perspective is a framework for testing, not established fact."

### 10. Actionability — PASS
Seifert et al. 2026 EV RNA-seq data identified for re-analysis; two cancer-validated HSAT2 assays named; RT-qPCR primer sources implied via Nogalski/Ting citations.

### 11. Fidelity to source material — PASS
All claims traceable to cited literature or to personal communications documented in email threads. No fabricated evidence. The overarching hypothesis origin is attributed to Fourel per email correspondence.

### 12. Appropriate collaboration disclosure — PASS
AI assistance (Opus, Anthropic) transparently disclosed in Acknowledgments with scope. All preprints flagged.

### 13. Author integrity — PASS
Five authors listed in agreed order; corresponding author with full institutional affiliation.

### 14. Scope discipline (abstract–body alignment) — PASS
Body only contains claims the abstract commits to. Material from earlier drafts (HSF1, CTCF, SPI1, endothelial-HSC transition) removed because the abstract does not mention them. Every body section maps to an abstract sentence.
