## Slide 1: Our research mission

**Mission**

- Build the largest linked biological and participant-reported database to study Long COVID and ME/CFS at scale.
- Work toward validated tools that can support diagnostics, subgrouping and treatment response predictions.

**Conceptual flow shown:**
- Inputs:
  - RNA biology / blood RNA
  - Participant-reported data / questionnaire
- Secure research database
- Outputs:
  - Diagnostics tools
  - Subgroup clustering

---

## Slide 2: Our research approach

Tests completed by participants include questionnaire data such as symptoms, severity, diagnoses, treatments, treatment response and health history, which can be linked with RNA for consented research.

**Workflow:**
1. Order / test kit
2. Questionnaire data
   - Symptoms
   - Severity
   - Diagnoses
   - Treatments
   - Treatment response
   - Health history
3. RNA data
4. Linked research database

---

## Slide 3: Our research goals

1. Stratify complex neuroimmune patients into distinct biological subgroups.
2. Scale our dataset to study small subgroups.
3. Identify signatures that can become stratification diagnostics.
4. Match biological groups to treatments.
5. Identify novel drug targets and treatment approaches.

## Slide 17: Building and testing a classifier

**How we approach this work:**

- Train models on 80% of the data and then test on a held-out 20%.
- Rotate and repeat 10 times.
- AUC is how well it classifies the held-out people.

**Diagram:** Training (80%) → Held-out test (20%)

## Slide 20: Gene floor cutoff increased AUC

**per-gene mean count; genes below the floor are dropped**

**held-out AUC across a wide range of floor cutoffs — nearly flat (0.86 to 0.89)**

- First tried raising the expression floor.
- AUC shifted 0.860 → 0.886.
- Stability still failed.

---

## Slide 21: What impacted stability

**Tuning the elastic-net dial**

- Elastic net: how the model balances picking a few strong genes against spreading weight across many correlated ones.
- Tuned it toward the dense end (α = 0.1), sharing weight across correlated genes rather than picking a sparse set.
- That gave the first configuration passing every criterion.

**Chart legend:** Held-out AUC, Panel stability

---

## Slide 22: Combining tuning and floor increase

**held-out ROC AUC progression:**
- First pass: 0.877
- Tune α → 0.1: 0.882
- Raise floor (floor 50, α 0.5) to 100: 0.894

**Alpha tuning together with increasing the floor maximised our AUC at 0.894.**

## Slide 23: Is it real? The permutation test

**Real result beats all 97 label shuffles (p = 0.010, 7.5 SD above the null)**

- Shuffled the diagnosis labels at random and re-ran the entire pipeline 97 times.
- Shuffled runs averaged 0.52 (chance).
- The real result beat all 97 of them, 7.5 standard deviations above the shuffled mean.
- Empirical p = 0.010.

**real: 0.894**

## Slide 29: Cohort-level findings — B-cell / antibody arm DOWN

**B-cell / antibody arm — DOWN.**
- The B-cell receptor, immunoglobulin, master B-cell transcription factors are all reduced, alongside IL7 (which supports lymphocyte development).
- Apparent reduction in the adaptive/antibody compartment at a cohort level.

**Neutrophil / emergency granulopoiesis — UP.**
- Defensins and neutrophil activation and pathogen response genes are elevated.
- A signature of expanded, activated, immature neutrophils (“emergency granulopoiesis”).
- Neutrophil cell compartment found to be reduced in our cohort via cell type deconvolution.
