# Key Points — Fricke et al. 2026 ACHTSAM Protocol

Note: This is a study protocol — results are not yet available. Key points relate to study design and rationale.

## Critical Gap Addressed

- >60% of severe ME/CFS patients unable to complete standard inpatient diagnostic protocols due to PEM (citing Cox & Findley)
- ~25% of ME/CFS patients are severely affected and largely housebound
- Severe patients systematically excluded from clinical studies due to immobility, lack of caregiver support, and PEM risk

## Methodological Innovations

1. **Home-based outreach design** — all assessments conducted at patient's home
2. **Staged assessment protocol** with rest periods and option to pause/terminate
3. **Tolerability-first approach** — assessments ordered from low to high burden
4. **Supine-position prioritization** to minimize orthostatic stress and PEM risk
5. **Subjective perceived exertion assessed after each procedure** to systematically characterize individual tolerability limits
6. **Bell score stratification** (≤20 very severe vs 21-30 severe) for subgroup analysis

## Assessment Battery Highlights

- **EndoPAT** for non-invasive endothelial function (peripheral arterial tonometry)
- **Pupillography** for autonomic nervous system activation and daytime sleepiness
- **Near-infrared spectroscopy (NIRS)** for muscle oxygenation and blood flow
- **HRV** with paced breathing for autonomic function
- **Schellong test** for orthostatic response
- **Salivary cortisol** before and after assessments (stress response)
- **Blood panel** including thyroid, renal function, CRP, ferritin, vitamin D, SARS-CoV-2/EBV antibodies, CMV

## Biobank Component

Blood and saliva samples stored for future investigations into biomarkers, viral reactivation, autoantibodies, and therapeutic targets.

## Implications

- Results will inform which diagnostic procedures can be safely used in severe ME/CFS
- Could accelerate enrolment in clinical trials by identifying tolerable diagnostic tools
- Home-based assessments may provide more ecologically valid data (reduced clinic-induced stress)
- Establishes framework for inclusive research that does not exclude the most severely affected
