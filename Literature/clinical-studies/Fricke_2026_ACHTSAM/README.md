# Fricke et al. 2026 — ACHTSAM Study Protocol

**Title:** ACHTSAM study protocol: outreach diagnostics and assessment of tolerability in severe ME/CFS — a pilot study

**Authors:** Carmen Fricke, Peter Deibert, Philipp Maier, Winfried Kern, Oliver Krumnau, Friedrich Barsch

**Journal:** BMJ Open 2026;16:e113095

**DOI:** 10.1136/bmjopen-2025-113095

**Trial Registration:** DRKS00035231; FRKS005506

**Type:** Study protocol (prospective, non-interventional observational study)

## Key Details

- **Population:** Severe to very severe ME/CFS (Bell score ≤30), n=25
- **Setting:** Home-based outreach assessments, University Medical Centre Freiburg, Germany
- **Design:** Two-phase pilot study
  - Phase 1: Validated questionnaires (remote)
  - Phase 2a: Home visits for physiological, cognitive, and biological assessments
  - Phase 2b: Additional non-invasive assessments via home visits
- **Primary outcome:** Feasibility and tolerability of home-based diagnostics
- **Ethics:** University of Freiburg Ethics Committee (No. 25-1031-S1)
- **Recruitment:** September 2025 – April 2026

## Assessment Battery (Phase 2)

**Low burden:** Resting ECG (5 min), pupillography (11 min), light activity sensor (2 min), osteosonography (5 min), body composition (5 min), NIRS muscle oxygenation (3 min)

**Moderate burden:** HRV with paced breathing (15 min), blood sampling (5 min), saliva cortisol (2 min), Schellong test (15 min), handgrip strength (3 min)

**High burden:** Cognitive assessments — MoCA, TMT-B, SDMT (30 min); EndoPAT endothelial function (25 min); neurocognitive/psychological assessment (30 min)

## Study Hypotheses

- **H1:** <50% of standard clinical assessments will be fully completable in severe ME/CFS (Bell ≤30) due to PEM or exhaustion
- **H2:** Non-invasive supine-position assessments will have ≥80% completion; high-burden tests <30%
- **H3:** Very severe patients (Bell ≤20) will tolerate ≥20% fewer assessments than severe (Bell 21-30)
- **H4:** Autonomic, endothelial, and pupillary parameters will show deviations in substantial proportion of participants

## Significance for ME/CFS Research

- First systematic study of diagnostic tolerability in severe/very severe ME/CFS
- Addresses critical gap: severe patients systematically excluded from research
- Home-based approach reduces clinic-induced stress and PEM risk
- Establishes baseline biomarker data for severe cohort (HRV, cortisol, endothelial function)
- Pilot biobank for future investigations (blood, saliva)
- Informs design of future clinical trials for severe patients
