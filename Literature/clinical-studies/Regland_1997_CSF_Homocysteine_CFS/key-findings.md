# Key Findings: Regland et al. 1997 - CSF Homocysteine in CFS/FM

## Main Findings

1. **Universal CSF Elevation:** 100% of 12 FM/CFS patients showed elevated CSF homocysteine.
2. **Fatigue Correlation:** Significant positive correlation between CSF-HCY and fatiguability on CPRS.
3. **B12-HCY Correlation:** CSF B12 levels correlated with CSF homocysteine and mental fatigue severity.
4. **CNS Compartment Effect:** Serum homocysteine was normal while CSF was elevated, indicating a CNS-specific metabolic failure distinct from systemic hyperhomocysteinemia.
5. **Mechanistic Interpretation:** Authors interpret finding as CNS B12 deficiency impairing the methionine synthase reaction (B12-dependent remethylation of homocysteine).

## Clinical Implications

- Routine serum homocysteine testing may miss CNS-localized methylation failure in ME/CFS patients
- CSF homocysteine could be a more relevant biomarker for the subset of ME/CFS patients with neurological fatigue symptoms
- Supports B12 supplementation strategies that can cross the blood-brain barrier (methylcobalamin)

## Limitations

- Very small sample (n=12)
- Combined FM+CFS cohort; not pure ME/CFS
- No matched control group
- All female participants; generalizability unknown
- 1997: pre-ICC and pre-Canadian Consensus Criteria diagnostic standards
- Same research group as Regland 2015 (not independent replication)
