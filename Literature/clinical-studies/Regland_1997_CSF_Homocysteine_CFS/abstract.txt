Title: Increased concentrations of homocysteine in the cerebrospinal fluid in patients with fibromyalgia and chronic fatigue syndrome
Authors: B Regland, M Andersson, L Abrahamsson, J Bagby, LE Dyrehag, CG Gottfries
Journal: Scandinavian Journal of Rheumatology
Year: 1997
Volume: 26(4):301-7
DOI: 10.3109/03009749709105320
PMID: 9310111

ABSTRACT:
12 female outpatients meeting diagnostic criteria for both fibromyalgia (FM) and chronic fatigue
syndrome (CFS) were investigated. CSF samples and psychopathological assessments using the
Comprehensive Psychopathological Rating Scale (CPRS) were performed.

Key findings:
- ALL 12 patients showed elevated homocysteine in CSF
- Significant positive correlation between CSF-HCY levels and fatiguability (CPRS score)
- CSF vitamin B12 levels generally low
- CSF-homocysteine and CSF-B12 levels correlated significantly with ratings of mental fatigue
- Blood homocysteine levels were usually NOT increased (CSF elevation without serum elevation)

Conclusion: Increased homocysteine in CNS characterizes patients meeting criteria for FM and CFS,
suggesting vitamin B12 deficiency impairs remethylation of homocysteine in the CNS.
