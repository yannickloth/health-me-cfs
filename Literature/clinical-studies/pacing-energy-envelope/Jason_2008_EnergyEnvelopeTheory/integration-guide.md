## Key Points to Convey

1. **Dose-Response Validated:** Exceeding energy capacity correlates with worse outcomes
2. **Quantifiable Metric:** Energy quotient = (expended/perceived) × 100
3. **Multiple Outcomes:** Affects depression, anxiety, fatigue, pain, quality of life, disability
4. **Long-term Evidence:** 15-year development and validation period

## Certainty Assessment for Integration

- **Quality:** High (large sample, peer-reviewed, long validation period)
- **Sample:** n=114 ME/CFS patients
- **Replication:** 15-year development with multiple validation studies
- **Limitations:** Self-reported energy (subjective), cross-sectional design limits causal claims

## Phrasing Recommendations

**Strong phrasing (justified by evidence):**
- "Energy quotient correlates with symptom severity"
- "Exceeding energy envelope is associated with worse outcomes"
- "Provides a quantifiable framework for pacing"

**Avoid overstating:**
- ✗ "Energy quotient causes symptoms" (correlation, not proven causation)
- ✗ "Works for all patients" (some may struggle with energy perception)
- ✓ "Patients staying within energy envelope show better functioning"

## Connection to Crash Severity Classification

This paper directly supports the concept that crash severity has a dose-response relationship with exertion magnitude. The energy quotient provides one possible quantification method for tracking this relationship.

**Clinical Guidance Implication:** Advise patients to maintain energy quotient ≤100% to minimize crash severity and frequency.
