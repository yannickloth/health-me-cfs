# Wang et al. 2025 — LS Epigenetic Immune Dysregulation

**Authors:** Wang J, Fan H, Bao Z, et al.
**Journal:** Journal of Inflammation Research, 2025, Vol. 18, pp. 283-299
**DOI:** 10.2147/JIR.S481324
**PMID:** 39802516
**PMC:** PMC11724625

## Key Points
- Novel: epigenetic approach (DNA methylation) rather than just protein/cytokine measurement
- Confirms Th1 pathway at epigenomic level: IFNG, CD28 hypomethylated (transcriptionally active)
- T cells markedly increased; fibroblasts decreased — tissue remodeling with immune infiltration
- Both CD8+ T cells and Tregs elevated — simultaneous effector and regulatory expansion
- Very small sample: n=8 male patients only — low generalizability
- Male LS (penile) may differ from female genital LS

## Methodology
DNA methylation array (850K EPIC), scRNA deconvolution (EpiSCORE/MethylCIBERSORT), IHC validation. Case-control design.

## Acronym Definitions
- IFNG: Interferon gamma gene
- CD28: T cell co-stimulatory receptor gene
- TLA: T lymphocyte activation (gene pathway)
- EpiSCORE/MethylCIBERSORT: cell type deconvolution algorithms for methylation data

## Certainty Assessment
- **Study Quality:** Medium (JIR peer-reviewed; novel methodology; but n=8 is critically small)
- **Sample Size:** n=8 — LOW confidence for generalization
- **Replication:** Not replicated; first epigenomic study
- **Conflicts of Interest:** Not disclosed
- **Note:** Mechanistic value high despite small n; epigenetic confirmation of Th1 activation adds orthogonal evidence
