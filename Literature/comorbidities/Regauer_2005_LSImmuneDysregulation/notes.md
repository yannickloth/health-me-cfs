# Regauer 2005 — Immune Dysregulation in Lichen Sclerosus

**Authors:** Regauer S
**Journal:** European Journal of Cell Biology, 2005, Vol. 84(2-3), pp. 273-7
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.ejcb.2004.12.003
**PMID:** 15819407

## Key Points
- Foundational review establishing LS as immune-mediated (lymphocyte-driven) dermatosis
- Monoclonal TCR gamma-chain rearrangements in 1.4-21% of LS infiltrates: antigen-driven clonal selection
- CD4+ T cells, B cells, dendritic cells predominate (not purely Th1-CD8+ as later studies show)
- Evidence for restricted TCR usage: sustained antigen-driven immune response
- Subset shows T-cell immune deficiency markers (serological evidence)
- 2005 vintage: predates modern cytokine profiling; newer studies have more nuanced phenotyping

## Methodology
Review article; no original data.

## Certainty Assessment
- **Study Quality:** Medium (EJCB peer-reviewed; 2005 — older methodology)
- **Sample Size:** Review; cited studies vary
- **Replication:** Antigen-driven clonal T-cell selection replicated in later studies
- **Conflicts of Interest:** Not reported
- **Note:** Historically important as early immune dysregulation framing; superseded in mechanistic detail by De Luca 2023 and Paganelli 2025
