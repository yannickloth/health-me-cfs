Regauer S. Immune dysregulation in lichen sclerosus. European Journal of Cell Biology. 2005;84(2-3):273-7. doi: 10.1016/j.ejcb.2004.12.003. PMID: 15819407.

ABSTRACT:
Review article. LS characterized as chronic localized lymphocyte-mediated dermatosis of genital skin with presumed autoimmune etiology. T-cell receptor gamma-chain gene monoclonal rearrangement present in 1.4-21% of lymphocytic infiltrates. Dominant immunophenotype: B-cells, CD4-positive T-cells, antigen-presenting dendritic cells. Evidence of antigen-driven selection of T-cells and restricted T-cell receptor usage suggesting prolonged exposure to disease-associated antigens. Dense lymphocytic infiltrates with vasculitic processes and significant tissue destruction. Subset of patients shows serological evidence of T-cell immune deficiencies. Timing of T-cell clone emergence unclear; no long-term follow-up data on monoclonal rearrangement significance.
