# Hu et al. 2021 — LS Comorbidities: University of Michigan Single-Center Study

**Authors:** Hu J, Hesson A, Haefner HK, Rominski S
**Journal:** International Journal of Gynecology and Obstetrics, 2021, Vol. 153(2), pp. 340-343
**DOI:** 10.1002/ijgo.13480
**PMID:** 33184843

## Key Points
- 1,983 women (865 VLS, 1,118 non-VLS); University of Michigan specialized vulvar disease center
- Fibromyalgia: univariate association with VLS but NOT significant in adjusted regression
- Age, thyroid disease, anorectal fissures: strongest confirmed associations
- Interstitial cystitis also elevated (pain syndrome)
- ME/CFS not examined directly — partial null result for fibromyalgia (confounded by age/thyroid)
- Self-report methodology; single specialized center (selection bias)

## IMPORTANT NOTE for ME/CFS hypothesis
- The fibromyalgia signal appearing in univariate but disappearing in multivariate suggests either:
  (a) Confounding by age/thyroid disease; OR
  (b) True association diluted by collinearity
- This does NOT definitively rule out a fibromyalgia/CFS-LS connection; it signals the need for better-powered studies with ME/CFS-specific inclusion

## Methodology
Retrospective chart review; patient intake questionnaires; 1996-2019; single center.

## Certainty Assessment
- **Study Quality:** Medium (self-report; single center; specialized referral population)
- **Sample Size:** n=865 VLS
- **Replication:** Not replicated
- **Conflicts of Interest:** Not reported
