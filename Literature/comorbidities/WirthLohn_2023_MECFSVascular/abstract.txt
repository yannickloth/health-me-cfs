Wirth KJ, Löhn M. Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (ME/CFS) and
Comorbidities: Linked by Vascular Pathomechanisms and Vasoactive Mediators? Medicina (Kaunas).
2023;59(5):978.
DOI: 10.3390/medicina59050978 | PMID: 37241210 | PMCID: PMC10224216

ABSTRACT:
This hypothesis review proposes that ME/CFS and its common comorbidities (including MCAS,
POTS, and fibromyalgia) are linked through shared vascular pathomechanisms and vasoactive
mediator release.

Key proposals:
- Mast cell activation (MCA) and ME/CFS may mutually trigger each other through bidirectional
  pathomechanisms involving excessive inflammatory mediators
- Dysfunctional beta2-adrenergic receptors destabilize mast cells, causing inappropriate
  degranulation that worsens ME/CFS symptomatology
- Histamine excess from mast cells disrupts vascular regulation and increases microvascular
  leakage, potentially initiating or exacerbating ME/CFS symptoms
- Chronic ME/CFS stress can itself trigger mast cell degranulation, creating a reinforcing cycle

Important for Septad diagnostic map: Wirth and Löhn explicitly acknowledge bidirectional
causality rather than establishing MCAS as unidirectional driver of ME/CFS. The causal
arrow runs both ways, complicating sequencing logic.

Limitations: No original data; hypothesis synthesis only; causal claims unconfirmed; no
clinical cohort data on prevalence or temporal sequence.
