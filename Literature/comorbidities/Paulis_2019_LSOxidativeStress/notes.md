# Paulis & Berardesca 2019 — Oxidative Stress in Lichen Sclerosus

**Authors:** Paulis G, Berardesca E
**Journal:** Research and Reports in Urology, 2019, Vol. 11, pp. 223-232
**DOI:** 10.2147/RRU.S205184
**PMID:** 31687365
**PMC:** PMC6709801

## Key Points
- Oxidative stress central to LS pathogenesis AND malignant transformation
- Specific biomarkers: malondialdehyde (lipid peroxidation), 8-OHdG (oxidative DNA damage), reduced SOD
- OS creates new autoantigenic epitopes via DNA damage — potential mechanism for initiating ECM1 autoantibody production
- 5-6% lifetime malignant transformation risk via tumor suppressor downregulation
- Very few antioxidant treatment trials in LS — major evidence gap
- Review only; cited studies: Nasca 1999 (n=86), Barbagli 2006 (n=130)

## Relevance to ME/CFS Mechanistic Overlap
- ME/CFS: extensive evidence for systemic oxidative stress, reduced antioxidant capacity, mitochondrial ROS
- Shared OS pathway could drive tissue damage in different compartments (skin in LS; muscle/brain in ME/CFS)
- OS-driven autoepitope generation: relevant to ME/CFS autoantibody hypothesis (Batham 2024)

## Methodology
Review article; no original data.

## Certainty Assessment
- **Study Quality:** Medium (RRU peer-reviewed; 2019; review not systematic)

- **Replication:** Oxidative stress in LS replicated across multiple biomarker studies
