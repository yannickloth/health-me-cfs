Vazquez M, Chovanec J, Kim J, DiMaggio T, Milner JD, Francomano CA, Gurnett CA, Ritelli M,
Colombi M, Lyons JJ. Hereditary alpha-tryptasemia modifies clinical phenotypes among
individuals with congenital hypermobility disorders. Human Genetics and Genomics Advances.
2022;3(2):100094.
DOI: 10.1016/j.xhgg.2022.100094 | PMID: 35287299 | PMCID: PMC8917312

ABSTRACT:
Background: Hereditary alpha-tryptasemia (HaT), caused by increased germline TPSAB1 copy
number encoding alpha-tryptase, is present in ~5% of Western populations and associated with
mast cell activation symptoms and autonomic dysfunction. We examined whether HaT prevalence
is elevated in hypermobility disorders and whether HaT modifies clinical phenotype.

Methods: Cross-sectional analysis of three hypermobility cohorts: hypermobility spectrum
disorder (HSD, n=132), hypermobile Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (hEDS, n=78), axial skeletal
abnormalities with hypermobility (n=56). TPSAB1/TPSB2 genotyped by droplet digital PCR.
Clinical manifestations compared between HaT+ and HaT- individuals.

Results: HaT prevalence: 4.9% (combined cohorts) vs 4.4-7.5% general population — no
significant enrichment. However, HaT significantly associated with dysphagia (OR 5.9-7.3,
p=0.007) and retained primary dentition (p=0.0003). Anaphylaxis prevalence increased in
HaT+ individuals with connective tissue disorders.

Conclusion: HaT does not occur at elevated frequency in hypermobility disorders, but when
present, it modifies clinical phenotype toward more severe mediator-release and GI symptoms.
Null result for HaT-hEDS association: genetics predisposition layer cannot assume automatic
HaT enrichment in hEDS; testing should be reserved for symptomatic individuals.
