# Halonen et al. 2024 — LS Risk Factors: Finnish Case-Control Study (n=43,000)

**Authors:** Halonen P, Heikinheimo O, Hadkhale K, Gissler M, Pukkala E, Jakobsson M
**Journal:** Journal of Lower Genital Tract Disease, 2024, Vol. 28(2), pp. 164-168
**DOI:** 10.1097/LGT.0000000000000796
**PMID:** 38518214
**PMC:** PMC11520338

## Key Points
- Largest case-control study: 10,692 LS women vs. 32,076 controls
- FIBROMYALGIA: OR 0.85 (95% CI 0.61-1.18) — null result; no association
- ME/CFS not examined as a separate category
- Interstitial cystitis elevated temporally near LS diagnosis (<5 years: OR 2.38)
- Surprising: T1DM, RA, CAD are PROTECTIVE (OR <1) — challenges simple autoimmune burden model
- Captures specialized healthcare diagnoses only; may miss primary-care conditions

## CRITICAL NULL RESULT
- Fibromyalgia is NOT associated with LS in this large Finnish cohort (OR 0.85, CI crosses 1.0)
- This is the most statistically powerful null result for fibromyalgia-LS association to date (n=43,768)
- Limits extrapolation from ME/CFS to LS via fibromyalgia overlap pathway

## Methodology
Case-control; Finnish HILMO hospital registry; 1998-2016; ICD-10 codes; nationwide.

## Certainty Assessment
- **Study Quality:** High
- **Sample Size:** n=10,692 LS cases (largest case-control for LS risk factors)
- **Replication:** First large Finnish registry study; consistent autoimmune findings with other registries
- **Conflicts of Interest:** Not reported
- **Note on fibromyalgia null:** High confidence null — large sample, registry-based, no confounding
