Steinsvik EK, Hausken T, Fluge Ø, Mella O, Gilja OH. Gastric dysmotility and gastrointestinal
symptoms in myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome. Scandinavian Journal of
Gastroenterology. 2023;58(7):769-776.
DOI: 10.1080/00365521.2023.2173533 | PMID: 36728717

ABSTRACT:
Background: Gastrointestinal (GI) symptoms are common in ME/CFS, but detailed data on gastric
motility features are sparse. We investigated GI symptom profiles and gastric motility
characteristics in ME/CFS patients presenting with abdominal complaints.

Methods: Double-blind, randomised, placebo-controlled crossover trial. n=20 ME/CFS patients
with abdominal symptoms vs matched healthy controls. Gastric motility assessed by ultrasound
drink test. Symptoms by Rome III questionnaire and IBS-SSS scale.

Results: GI symptom prevalence: fullness/bloating 75%, abdominal pain 45%, nausea 35%.
Ultrasound revealed lower proximal stomach measurements after meals and larger fasting antral
area vs healthy controls. Patients showed stronger symptomatic responses to liquid meals
(epigastric pain, discomfort, nausea). 90% had normal bowel movement frequencies despite
high bowel habit dissatisfaction.

Conclusion: ME/CFS patients with abdominal symptoms show patterns of impaired gastric
accommodation resembling functional dyspepsia rather than irritable bowel syndrome.
