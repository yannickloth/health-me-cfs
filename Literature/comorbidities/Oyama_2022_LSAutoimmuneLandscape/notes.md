# Oyama & Hasegawa 2022 — LS Autoimmune and Genetic Landscape

**Authors:** Oyama N, Hasegawa M
**Journal:** Diagnostics (Basel), 2022, Vol. 12(12), Article 3070
**DOI:** 10.3390/diagnostics12123070
**PMID:** 36553077
**PMC:** PMC9777366

## Key Points
- ECM1 autoantibodies: ~70-80% prevalence in female genital LS; 94% specificity
- ECM1 is a multi-functional immune regulator: controls Th2, Tregs, macrophage polarization, B-cell activation
- ECM1 dysfunction is not just a marker — it actively disrupts immune balance across multiple cell types
- HLA class II associations (DQ7, DR12) — same HLA region associated with many autoimmune diseases
- ECM1 as a convergence point for LS immune dysregulation

## Methodology
Review synthesizing histopathological, genetic, and experimental data. Uses passive-transfer mouse model and human fibroblast knockdown experiments.

## Acronym Definitions
- ECM1: Extracellular Matrix Protein 1
- HLA: Human Leukocyte Antigen
- Treg: Regulatory T cells
- UC: Ulcerative Colitis
- M1: Pro-inflammatory macrophage phenotype
