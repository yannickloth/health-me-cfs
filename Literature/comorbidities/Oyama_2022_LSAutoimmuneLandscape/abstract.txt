Oyama N, Hasegawa M. Lichen Sclerosus: A Current Landscape of Autoimmune and Genetic Interplay. Diagnostics (Basel). 2022;12(12):3070. doi: 10.3390/diagnostics12123070. PMID: 36553077. PMC: PMC9777366.

ABSTRACT:
Review of autoimmune and genetic mechanisms in LS. IgG autoantibodies against ECM1 in approximately 70-80% of female genital LS patients; 94% specificity distinguishing LS from other autoimmune diseases. HLA class II associations: DQ7, DR12, DRB1*12, DRB1*13. Loss-of-function ECM1 gene mutations cause lipoid proteinosis (histopathological counterpart). ECM1 modulates: Th2 trafficking via IL-2 receptor interactions, CD4+CD25+ Treg function (elevated ECM1 transcripts), M1 macrophage polarization in IBD, B-cell activation and follicular helper T cell differentiation. Mouse passive-transfer anti-ECM1 experiment reproduced early LS features but not dermal hyalinosis (species-specific ECM1 differences). Synthesizes data from 1052-case LS cohort, 411-case penile dermatosis analysis, 905-case UC cohort. ECM1 knockdown in human fibroblasts impairs growth, migration, collagen gel contraction.
