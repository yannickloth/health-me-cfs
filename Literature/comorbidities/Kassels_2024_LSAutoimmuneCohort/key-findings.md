# Key Findings: Kassels 2024 — LS Autoimmune Population Study

## Main Findings
1. **Broad autoimmune comorbidity burden:** 17 autoimmune conditions significantly elevated in LS vs. matched controls; no single-organ specificity — both organ-specific (thyroid, vitiligo, alopecia) and systemic (SLE, sclerosis) conditions elevated
2. **Highest ORs:** Morphea (OR 395.988), systemic sclerosis (OR 10.371), lichen planus (OR 4.112), vitiligo (OR 3.391) — cutaneous autoimmune conditions cluster strongly
3. **Systemic autoimmunity confirmed:** PBC (OR 1.891), SLE (OR 1.628), Sjögren's syndrome, celiac disease, Crohn's disease, RA all elevated
4. **Null result for MS and myasthenia gravis:** OR <1 — LS does not associate with all autoimmune phenotypes; neurological autoimmunity patterns differ
5. **ME/CFS/fibromyalgia not examined:** These conditions were not in the condition set tested; absence from results is a methodological gap, not a negative finding

## Clinical Implications
- LS patients warrant systematic autoimmune screening beyond thyroid function
- The pattern (cutaneous > neurological autoimmune) may reflect shared mucosal/dermal immune dysregulation pathways
- LS should be considered a potential marker of systemic autoimmune predisposition

## Limitations
- Retrospective ICD-10 coding (misclassification risk)
- Mixed anogenital and extragenital LS
- Female patients only
- ME/CFS not in comorbidity set — cannot infer negative association
- TriNetX captures US healthcare system users; may miss mild/undiagnosed cases
