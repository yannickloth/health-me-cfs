# Kassels et al. 2024 — Population-Level LS Autoimmune Comorbidities

**Authors:** Kassels A, Min MS, Kraus CN
**Journal:** Journal of the American Academy of Dermatology, 2024, Vol. 91(3), pp. 588-589
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.jaad.2024.05.073
**PMID:** 38848901
**PMC:** PMC11952824

## Key Points
- Largest population-level study to date: 83,103 LS patients vs. 83,103 matched controls
- 17 autoimmune conditions significantly elevated in LS
- Strongest associations: morphea, systemic sclerosis, lichen planus, vitiligo, PBC, SLE
- ME/CFS, fibromyalgia, chronic fatigue NOT in the condition list examined
- Female patients only (controls: postmenopausal atrophic vaginitis)

## Methodology
Retrospective cohort study. TriNetX database (60 US organizations, >104M patients). ICD-10 L90.0 for LS. Matched 1:1 on age and race/ethnicity. Cross-sectional comorbidity odds analysis.

## Acronym Definitions
- LS: Lichen Sclerosus
- PBC: Primary Biliary Cirrhosis
- SLE: Systemic Lupus Erythematosus
- OR: Odds Ratio
- TriNetX: health research network

## Certainty Assessment
- **Study Quality:** High
- **Sample Size:** n=83,103 LS patients
- **Replication:** Partially replicated (consistent with smaller prior studies)
- **Conflicts of Interest:** Not reported
- **Note:** Does not examine ME/CFS or fibromyalgia directly — this is a null result for direct LS-ME/CFS association in ICD-10 database terms
