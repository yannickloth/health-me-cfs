Afrin LB, Weinstock LB, Molderings GJ. Covid-19 hyperinflammation and post-Covid-19 illness
may be rooted in mast cell activation syndrome. International Journal of Infectious Diseases.
2020;100:327-332.
DOI: 10.1016/j.ijid.2020.09.016 | PMID: 32920235 | PMCID: PMC7529115

ABSTRACT:
This perspective article proposes that severe COVID-19 hyperinflammation and post-COVID-19
chronic illness are driven by uncontrolled mast cell activation syndrome (MCAS) in susceptible
individuals, rather than by normal immune responses to SARS-CoV-2.

Proposed mechanism: Mast cells express ACE2 receptors and recognize SARS-CoV-2 through
multiple pathways. In individuals with underlying MCAS, viral exposure triggers dysregulated
mast cell degranulation releasing >1000 mediators (histamine, proteases, cytokines,
eicosanoids), driving cytokine storms, hyperinflammation, and post-infectious symptom
persistence. Major stressors can escalate MCAS through epigenetic mechanisms.

Key claims: (1) MCAS estimated prevalence ~17% may explain why only a subset develop severe
or chronic COVID-19. (2) Same mechanism proposed for post-viral ME/CFS phenotype. (3)
MCAS treatment (antihistamines, mast cell stabilizers) may modify post-COVID trajectory.

Limitations: Hypothesis only; no original data; no controlled observations; authors
acknowledge "solid data are not yet available." Estimated MCAS prevalence figure uncertain.
