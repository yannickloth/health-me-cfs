Paganelli A, Didona D, Scala E. Cytokine Networks in Lichen Sclerosus: A Roadmap for Diagnosis and Treatment? International Journal of Molecular Sciences. 2025;26(9):4315. doi: 10.3390/ijms26094315. PMID: 40362551. PMC: PMC12072692.

ABSTRACT:
Narrative review identifying LS as characterized by predominant Th1 immune response with overproduction of pro-inflammatory cytokines IL-1, IL-7, IL-15, IFN-gamma, and TNF-alpha. Reduced Treg activity and lower IL-10 levels documented; TGF-beta elevated. Anti-ECM1 autoantibodies detected in approximately 75% of vulvar LS patients with 94% specificity. Collagen V interaction impairment and latent TGF-beta dysregulation described. IL-4 and IFN-gamma show inverse expression patterns distinguishing LS from lichen planus. Elevated IL-17 in epidermis and dermal infiltrates of 20 LS patients vs. 10 controls. microRNA-155 overexpression linked to reduced Foxp3+ Treg levels. Definitive disease marker remains elusive; conflicting findings on IL-17 role. Review only — no original quantitative data.
