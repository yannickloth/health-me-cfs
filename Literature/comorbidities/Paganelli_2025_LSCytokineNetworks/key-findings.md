# Key Findings: Paganelli 2025 — LS Cytokine Networks

## Main Findings
1. **Th1 cytokine signature confirmed:** IL-1, IL-7, IL-15, IFN-gamma, TNF-alpha constitute the core inflammatory signature of LS; this profile overlaps directly with published ME/CFS cytokine studies
2. **ECM1 autoantibody specificity:** Anti-ECM1 IgG in ~75% vulvar LS; 94% specificity vs. other autoimmune diseases — this makes ECM1 a candidate stratification biomarker; ECM1 also modulates Th2 trafficking and Treg function
3. **Treg failure mechanism:** miR-155 overexpression reduces Foxp3+ Tregs — the same Treg exhaustion pathway seen in ME/CFS autoimmune subgroup studies
4. **IL-17 controversy:** Conflicting data on IL-17 role (some studies show elevation, some do not) — suggests LS may have subgroups with different cytokine-dominant profiles, paralleling ME/CFS immunological heterogeneity
5. **No definitive biomarker:** Despite multiple cytokine candidates, no single marker distinguishes all LS subtypes — mirrors ME/CFS biomarker landscape

## Relevance to ME/CFS Mechanistic Overlap
- IL-7 and IL-15 are key survival signals for CD8+ memory T cells: their elevation in both LS and ME/CFS suggests chronic T cell activation state
- IFN-gamma: central to both LS Th1 phenotype and ME/CFS interferon-signature studies
- Treg suppression via miR-155/Foxp3 axis: parallel mechanism to loss of immune tolerance in ME/CFS autoimmune hypothesis

## Limitations
- Narrative review; no original data
- Conflicting IL-17 findings unresolved
- Small sample sizes in referenced comparative studies (as low as n=10 controls)
- No longitudinal data on cytokine evolution
