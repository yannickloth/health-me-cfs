Eccles JA, Thompson B, Themelis K, et al. Beyond bones: The relevance of variants of connective
tissue (hypermobility) to fibromyalgia, ME/CFS and controversies surrounding diagnostic
classification: an observational study. Clinical Medicine (London). 2021;21(1):e83-e88.
DOI: 10.7861/clinmed.2020-0743 | PMID: 33479068

ABSTRACT (from PubMed):
Hypermobility-related variants of connective tissue disorders are common in clinical practice
and may underlie and/or co-occur with chronic pain and fatigue conditions. We assessed the
prevalence of symptomatic hypermobility in a patient cohort with fibromyalgia and/or ME/CFS
using revised Brighton criteria and 2017 hEDS classification criteria. 81% of patients met
Brighton criteria for hypermobility syndrome (OR 7.08; p≤0.001), compared with 37% of healthy
controls. 18% met 2017 hEDS classification criteria. Only 23.5% had received a prior diagnosis
of hypermobility despite meeting criteria. 85% of patients met all three diagnostic criteria
for both fibromyalgia and ME/CFS simultaneously, demonstrating extensive diagnostic overlap.
Historical joint laxity significantly predicted pain and fatigue severity across multiple measures.
