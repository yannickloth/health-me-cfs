Batham J, Dwyer J, Eaton-Fitch N, Marshall-Gradisnik S. Autoimmunity's enigmatic role: exploring the connection with myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome. BMC Immunology. 2024;25:62. doi: 10.1186/s12865-024-00657-5. PMID: 39354352. PMC: PMC11443722.

ABSTRACT:
Systematic review of 10 publications examining autoimmune mechanisms in ME/CFS. Concluded "inconsistent and insufficient evidence" exists to classify ME/CFS as a true autoimmune disease. Specific autoantibodies identified: beta2-glycoprotein-I, neural antigens, voltage-gated calcium channels, anti-pituitary, anti-hypothalamic, GABA receptors (particularly in comorbid fibromyalgia), IgM against lipid molecules. HLA allele associations (HLA-C, HLA-DBQ1). Shared genetic predisposition genes (PTPN22, CTLA4) with other autoimmune diseases. B-cell abnormalities (reduced CD19+ in infection-triggered cases). ME/CFS lacks validated diagnostic autoantibodies and characteristic tissue damage of established autoimmune diseases. ME/CFS shares overlapping features with Hashimoto's thyroiditis, SLE, Sjögren's syndrome. Key limitations: small samples, heterogeneous populations, inconsistent diagnostic criteria.
