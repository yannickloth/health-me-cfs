# Key Findings: Batham 2024 — ME/CFS Autoimmunity Systematic Review

## Main Findings
1. **Insufficient evidence for ME/CFS as autoimmune disease:** Systematic review of 10 studies finds inconsistent and contradictory evidence; no validated diagnostic autoantibody; no characteristic tissue damage — ME/CFS does not currently meet criteria for established autoimmune disease classification
2. **Autoantibodies present but heterogeneous:** Multiple autoantibodies detected in subgroups: beta2-glycoprotein-I, neural antigens, voltage-gated calcium channels, anti-pituitary/hypothalamic, GABA receptor; IgM against lipid molecules correlates with symptom severity — suggesting immune-mediated component without fulfilling autoimmune disease criteria
3. **Shared genetic risk architecture with autoimmune diseases:** PTPN22 and CTLA4 variants (T cell checkpoint genes) found in ME/CFS, same genes as Hashimoto's, RA, T1DM; HLA-C and HLA-DBQ1 associations — immune system architecture predisposes to both ME/CFS and autoimmune disease
4. **Phenotypic overlap with Hashimoto's, SLE, Sjögren's:** ME/CFS shares dysregulated cytokine signaling, HLA associations, B-cell abnormalities with these autoimmune diseases — the same diseases that also co-occur with lichen sclerosus
5. **Treg and B-cell dysfunction:** Reduced CD19+ B cells in infection-triggered cases; Treg dysfunction — parallel to LS pathophysiology (miR-155/Foxp3 axis)

## Clinical Implications for LS-ME/CFS Hypothesis
- The shared genetic predisposition loci (PTPN22, CTLA4) could explain why some individuals develop both LS and ME/CFS
- Both conditions involve loss of immune tolerance via Treg dysfunction without fulfilling classical autoimmune disease criteria
- The Hashimoto's/SLE/Sjögren's phenotypic overlap creates a "shared autoimmune terrain" linking LS comorbidities to ME/CFS immune features

## Limitations
- Only 10 publications reviewed — very small systematic review
- Heterogeneous patient populations; different diagnostic criteria across studies
- No meta-analysis or quantitative synthesis
- Cannot establish causality
