# Batham et al. 2024 — Autoimmunity's Enigmatic Role in ME/CFS

**Authors:** Batham J, Dwyer J, Eaton-Fitch N, Marshall-Gradisnik S
**Journal:** BMC Immunology, 2024, Vol. 25, p. 62
**DOI:** 10.1186/s12865-024-00657-5
**PMID:** 39354352
**PMC:** PMC11443722

## Key Points
- Systematic review: 10 publications
- Core finding: "inconsistent and insufficient evidence" to classify ME/CFS as autoimmune disease
- Multiple autoantibodies identified but none validated as diagnostic
- Shared genetic risk loci (PTPN22, CTLA4) with established autoimmune diseases
- Overlap features with Hashimoto's, SLE, Sjögren's — same cluster as LS comorbidities
- Tregs, B cell abnormalities, HLA associations documented

## Methodology
Systematic review (10 publications). Searches of ME/CFS and autoimmunity literature. No meta-analysis; qualitative synthesis.

## Acronym Definitions
- PTPN22: Protein Tyrosine Phosphatase Non-Receptor Type 22 (T cell regulation gene)
- CTLA4: Cytotoxic T-Lymphocyte Associated Protein 4 (immune checkpoint gene)
- HLA: Human Leukocyte Antigen
- GABA: Gamma-Aminobutyric Acid
- SLE: Systemic Lupus Erythematosus

## Certainty Assessment
- **Study Quality:** Medium (BMC Immunology peer-reviewed; systematic review of small heterogeneous studies)
- **Sample Size:** 10 publications reviewed; individual study sizes not reported in abstract
- **Replication:** Findings inconsistent across reviewed studies
