# De Luca et al. 2023 — Lichen Sclerosus: The 2023 Update

**Authors:** De Luca DA, Papara C, Vorobyev A, Staiger H, Bieber K, Thaci D, Ludwig RJ
**Journal:** Frontiers in Medicine, 2023, Vol. 10, p. 1106318
**DOI:** 10.3389/fmed.2023.1106318
**PMID:** 36873861
**PMC:** PMC9978401

## Key Points
- Definitive 2023 review of LS pathophysiology
- Th1-dominant immune phenotype: IFN-gamma, IL-1alpha, IL-7, IL-15, TNF-alpha elevated
- IL-10 (anti-inflammatory) downregulated; IL-4 elevated in phimosis subgroup
- miR-155 drives Th1 polarization and Treg suppression (loss of immune tolerance)
- ECM1 autoantibodies present but causality uncertain
- Oxidative stress: lipid peroxidation, oxidative DNA damage, low antioxidant enzymes

## Methodology
Narrative review synthesizing epidemiological, pathogenic, clinical, diagnostic, and therapeutic data from dermatology literature.

## Acronym Definitions
- LS: Lichen Sclerosus
- ECM1: Extracellular Matrix Protein 1
- miR-155: microRNA-155
- MMP9: Matrix Metalloproteinase 9
- TGF-beta: Transforming Growth Factor beta
- IFN-gamma: Interferon gamma
- Treg: Regulatory T cells
