# Key Findings: Petrov 2026 — Monocyte/DC Immunophenotyping

## Main Findings

1. **Divergent immune architecture:** Long COVID → persistent immune activation + exhaustion; ME/CFS → immune suppression with impaired trafficking
2. **M2-like monocyte polarization:** Elevated in long COVID, not ME/CFS — suggests ongoing antigenic drive in long COVID
3. **CD80 costimulatory deficit in ME/CFS:** Reduced CD80 on M1-like monocytes points to impaired antigen-presenting capacity
4. **CCR7 impairment in ME/CFS:** Reduced CCR7 on both monocytes and DCs — defective immune cell trafficking to lymph nodes
5. **DC expansion in long COVID vs. contraction in ME/CFS:** Opposite dendritic cell dynamics
6. **Inverse HLA-DR/psychiatric symptom correlation in ME/CFS:** Higher monocyte HLA-DR → lower depression/anxiety — possible protective mechanism

## Clinical Implications
- Composite monocyte/DC phenotyping may distinguish ME/CFS from long COVID where standard lab tests fail
- CCR7 pathway may be a therapeutic target in ME/CFS
- Different treatment approaches warranted: immune restoration for ME/CFS vs. immune resolution for long COVID

## Limitations
- Cross-sectional design
- No functional assays (phenotyping only)
- Vaccination status not included
- Single-center study
- No replication cohort
- Excluded many medications that ME/CFS patients commonly use (LDN, antihistamines, beta-blockers)
