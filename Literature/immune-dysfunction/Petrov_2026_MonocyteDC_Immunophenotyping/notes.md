# Petrov et al. 2026 — Monocyte/DC Immunophenotyping in ME/CFS and Long COVID

**Authors:** Steliyan Petrov, Martina Bozhkova, Mariya Ivanovska, Teodora Kalfova, Dobrina Dudova, Yana Todorova, Radostina Dimitrova, Marianna Murdjeva, Hristo Taskov, Maria Nikolova, Michael Maes
**Journal:** Int. J. Mol. Sci. 2026, 27, 4488
**DOI:** 10.3390/ijms27104488

## Key Points

- **Study design:** Cross-sectional, n=207 (103 ME/CFS, 63 long COVID, 41 HC)
- **Method:** 12-parameter flow cytometry on PBMCs — monocytes (CD14, CD80, CD163, CD206, HLA-DR), DCs (CD3-CD14-HLA-DR+), T cells (CD4, CD8, CD45RA, CD62L, CD95, CCR7)
- **ME/CFS diagnosis:** IOM 2015 criteria
- **Long COVID definition:** Symptoms >4 months post-SARS-CoV-2, positive PCR
- **Exclusions:** Psychiatric disorders, autoimmune diseases, immunosuppressive drugs, antidepressants, LDN, antihistamines, corticosteroids, beta-blockers, antivirals (within 2 months)

## Results Summary

### Monocyte subsets
- **Long COVID:** ↑ M2-like monocyte polarization (CD206-high), ↑ CD80 expression across all monocyte subsets
- **ME/CFS:** ↓ CD80 on M1-like monocytes, ↓ CCR7 expression on monocytes
- **M1/M2 ratio:** Not significantly different between groups

### Dendritic cells
- **Long COVID:** ↑ DC frequency, ↓ HLA-DR expression on DCs (immune exhaustion)
- **ME/CFS:** ↓ DC frequency, ↓ CCR7 expression on DCs

### T cell subsets
- **Long COVID:** ↑ CD8+ central memory T cells, ↑ CD4+CD95+ cells
- **ME/CFS:** ↑ CD8+CD45RA+CD62L- effector memory/terminal differentiation, ↑ CD4+CD95+ cells

### Correlation networks
- **Long COVID:** More extensive and integrated immune interactions
- **ME/CFS:** Less coordinated activation patterns, more fragmented network

### PCA/PLS-DA
- Moderate discrimination between ME/CFS and long COVID achievable with composite immune markers
- VIP scores: CD80 on monocytes, CCR7 on DCs, M2-like polarization strongest discriminators

### Clinical correlations
- Inverse relationship between monocyte HLA-DR expression and psychiatric symptom severity in ME/CFS
- Protective association: higher HLA-DR on monocytes → lower depression/anxiety scores

## Certainty Assessment

- **Study Quality:** High — adequate sample size, well-defined cohorts, multiparameter flow cytometry, FDR correction, post-hoc power ≥ 0.80
- **Sample Size:** n=207 (103 ME/CFS, 63 LC, 41 HC) — largest comparative immunophenotyping study to date
- **Replication:** Novel — first comprehensive monocyte/DC comparison between ME/CFS and long COVID; some findings consistent with Eaton-Fitch 2024 (immune exhaustion), Shahbaz 2026 (monocyte remodeling)
- **Limitations:** Cross-sectional (no temporal dynamics), no functional assays, vaccination status not analyzed, no replication cohort, single time point
- **Conflicts of Interest:** None declared

## Quotes for Citation
> "Long COVID was characterized by increased M2-like monocyte polarization, elevated CD80 expression across monocyte subsets, expansion of dendritic cells, and reduced expression of activation markers, indicating persistent immune activation with features of immune exhaustion."

> "ME/CFS exhibited reduced costimulatory molecule expression, impaired CCR7-mediated immune cell trafficking, and less coordinated activation patterns, consistent with a state of immune suppression."
