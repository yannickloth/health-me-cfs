# Comprehensive Immunophenotyping of Monocytes and Dendritic Cells Suggests Distinct Pathophysiology in Chronic Fatigue Syndrome and Long COVID

**Authors:** Petrov et al.
**Year:** 2026
**Journal:** International Journal of Molecular Sciences
**DOI:** 10.3390/ijms27104488

## Summary
Multiparameter flow cytometry study (n=207) comparing monocyte, dendritic cell, and T cell profiles in ME/CFS (n=103), long COVID (n=63), and healthy controls (n=41). Finds divergent immune architecture: long COVID shows persistent immune activation with M2-like monocyte polarization and exhaustion features, while ME/CFS exhibits immune suppression with impaired CCR7-mediated trafficking and reduced costimulatory capacity.

## Why This Matters
Provides direct comparative immunophenotyping evidence that ME/CFS and long COVID have distinct immune profiles despite clinical overlap. Supports divergent pathophysiology rather than shared mechanism. Identifies candidate biomarkers (CCR7, CD80, monocyte polarization) for diagnostic differentiation.
