# Key Findings: Kennedy et al. 2004

## Primary Finding

**CFS patients show increased neutrophil apoptosis consistent with ongoing inflammatory activation**

Quantitative differences:
- Annexin V binding: 37.4% (CFS) vs 22.8% (controls), p=0.001
- TNFRI expression: significantly elevated (p=0.004)
- Active TGFβ1: elevated (p<0.005)
- Viable neutrophils: reduced in CFS

## Mechanistic Insight

**Death receptor pathway activation:**
- Increased TNFRI (tumor necrosis factor receptor I) on neutrophils
- Suggests TNF-α-mediated apoptosis induction
- Links to elevated TNF-α found in cytokine studies (e.g., Brenu 2011)

**TGFβ1 role:**
- Elevated active TGFβ1 may drive apoptosis
- Could contribute to immunosuppression
- May explain paradoxical immune dysfunction despite activation

## Significance for ME/CFS Pathophysiology

1. **Systemic immune activation:** Not limited to lymphocytes
2. **Innate immunity affected:** Neutrophils show dysfunction
3. **Accelerated turnover:** Higher apoptosis → shorter neutrophil lifespan
4. **Functional consequence:** Reduced viable neutrophils may impair antimicrobial defense

**MODERATE-HIGH CERTAINTY**

- Published in reputable pathology journal
