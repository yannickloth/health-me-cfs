# Neural and humoral pathways of communication from the immune system to the brain: parallel or convergent?

**Authors:** Dantzer R, Konsman JP, Bluth√© RM, Kelley KW
**Journal:** Autonomic Neuroscience, 2000, 85(1-3):60-5
**DOI:** 10.1016/S1566-0702(00)00220-4
**PMID:** 11189027

## Key Points
- Two complementary routes: (1) fast neural pathway via vagus nerve; (2) slower humoral pathway via circumventricular organs and choroid plexus
- Cytokine activation of brain neurons occurs directly or indirectly via prostaglandins
- Locally produced brain cytokines reach targets through neuronal projection or volume transmission into brain tissue
- Both pathways converge on the same hypothalamic and limbic circuits

## Methodology
- Review/conceptual paper synthesizing multiple animal experiments
- Integration of neuroanatomical tracing, pharmacological blockade, and cytokine injection studies
