# Sander et al. 2025 — Detailed Notes

## Study Design
- Prospective validation study
- Handheld monocular pupillometer
- Patients with autonomic dysfunction + healthy controls

## Key Findings
- PLR metrics show diagnostic utility for autonomic failure
- Establishes normative reference ranges
- Noninvasive, quick assessment suitable for clinical settings

## Relevance to ME/CFS
- Provides validated methodology applicable to ME/CFS autonomic assessment
- No direct ME/CFS data but establishes proof-of-concept for pupil-based autonomic screening
- Could be used to quantify autonomic dysfunction in ME/CFS subgroups

## Limitations
- Moderate sample size
- Single study, independent replication needed
- No ME/CFS-specific data
