# Key Findings: Sander et al. 2025

## Main Findings
1. Handheld monocular pupillometer validated against established autonomic testing
2. PLR parameters (constriction amplitude, latency, dilation velocity) distinguish autonomic failure patients from healthy controls
3. Normative values established for clinical interpretation
4. Supports pupillometry as accessible, noninvasive autonomic screening tool

## Clinical Implications
- Provides validated, portable alternative to traditional autonomic testing
- Potential for point-of-care autonomic assessment in ME/CFS and related conditions
- Quantitative PLR parameters offer objective autonomic readout

## Certainty: 0.70
- High quality (prospective validation, top journal)
- Medium sample
- Single study
