# Pupillary Reflexes in CRPS: LC Deficit (Drummond & Finch 2022)

**Citation:** Drummond PD, Finch PM. Pupillary reflexes in complex regional pain syndrome: asymmetry to arousal stimuli suggests an ipsilateral locus coeruleus deficit. *Journal of Pain*. 2022;23(1):131-140.

**DOI:** 10.1016/j.jpain.2021.07.003
**PMID:** 34375745
**BibTeX key:** `drummond2022crps`

## Key Contribution
Uses pupillary dilation to arousal stimuli as probe of locus coeruleus function. Demonstrates ipsilateral LC deficit in CRPS. Establishes pupillometry as noninvasive LC function probe relevant to pain/autonomic conditions.

## Evidence Grade: B-
- Innovative methodology (arousal-evoked pupillometry)
- Moderate sample (n=33)
- Single study
- Directly relevant to LC noradrenergic dysfunction hypothesis in ME/CFS
