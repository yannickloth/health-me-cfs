# Key Findings: van Campen et al. 2019 - B12 Nasal Drops in ME/CFS

## Main Findings

1. **Intranasal Delivery Efficacy:** Intranasal hydroxocobalamin tripled median serum B12 (328 to 973 pmol/L), confirming the route achieves systemic absorption.
2. **Responder Rate:** 67% of ME/CFS participants showed improvement on physical activity measures; 33% did not respond, consistent with ME/CFS heterogeneity.
3. **Objective Outcomes:** Step count (actigraphy) improved in responders, providing an objective (not purely self-reported) outcome.
4. **CNS Relevance:** Intranasal route bypasses gut absorption issues and offers more direct CNS exposure, potentially relevant if CNS B12 deficiency (Regland 1997) is the therapeutic target.

## Clinical Implications for ME/CFS

- Supports intranasal or parenteral B12 delivery over oral supplementation for CNS-targeted effect
- The 33% non-responder rate suggests patient stratification is needed (MTHFR genotype, baseline B12, homocysteine levels)
- Objective improvement (step count) provides signal for further RCT design

## Limitations

- Open-label; no placebo control
- Cannot rule out Hawthorne/placebo effect for self-reported outcomes
- Step count may be influenced by seasonal variation over 3 months
- Single center; Dutch population; may not generalize
