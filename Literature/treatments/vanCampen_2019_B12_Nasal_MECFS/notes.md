# B12 Nasal Drops in ME/CFS - Open Trial

**Authors:** van Campen CMC, Riepma K, Visser FC
**Journal:** Frontiers in Pharmacology, 2019, 10:1102
**DOI:** 10.3389/fphar.2019.01102
**PMID:** 31616305

## Key Points

- Intranasal hydroxocobalamin substantially raised serum B12 levels
- 67% of participants (34/51) reported functional improvement (step count, physical activity)
- 33% were non-responders, suggesting heterogeneity in ME/CFS population
- Intranasal route achieves CNS delivery without gut absorption limitations
- No placebo arm: response rate interpretation is limited

## Methodology

Open-label pilot trial. 51 ME/CFS adults. 3-month duration. Outcomes: serum B12, daily step count (actigraphy), RAND-36 physical activity scale. No blinding.

## Acronym Definitions

- ME/CFS: Myalgic Encephalomyelitis / Chronic Fatigue Syndrome
- RAND-36: Health Survey questionnaire (36-item)

## Certainty Assessment

- **Study Quality:** Low-Medium (open-label, no control, pilot)
- **Sample Size:** n=51 (small but larger than Regland 1997)
- **Replication:** Not yet replicated; authors explicitly call for RCT
- **Conflicts of Interest:** Not disclosed; Dutch academic institution
- **Limitations:** No placebo control; self-reported disease duration; potential selection bias;
  functional improvement measured objectively (step count) which partially mitigates bias;
  single center
