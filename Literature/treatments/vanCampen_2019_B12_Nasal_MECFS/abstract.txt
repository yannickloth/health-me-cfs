Title: Open Trial of Vitamin B12 Nasal Drops in Adults With Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome: Comparison of Responders and Non-Responders
Authors: C (Linda) MC van Campen, Klaas Riepma, Frans C Visser
Journal: Frontiers in Pharmacology
Year: 2019
Volume: 10:1102
DOI: 10.3389/fphar.2019.01102
PMID: 31616305

ABSTRACT:
Open-label pilot trial of intranasal hydroxocobalamin (B12) in 51 ME/CFS adults (44 female,
mean age 42 years, mean disease duration 16 years) over 3 months.

Key findings:
- Intranasal route produced substantial B12 serum increases: median 328 pmol/L pre-treatment
  to 973 pmol/L post-treatment
- Among 34 responders (67%): step count, RAND-36 physical activity scale, and serum B12
  all significantly increased
- Among 17 non-responders (33%): no meaningful changes
- Open design without placebo control; cannot exclude placebo effect
- Supports intranasal as a CNS-accessible delivery route (avoids first-pass metabolism)
