# Key Findings: Mei et al. (2026) - Ubiquinol vs Ubiquinone Bioavailability

## Main Findings
1. **2-3.4x higher bioavailability:** Novel cocrystal ubiquinol shows substantially higher systemic bioavailability vs standard ubiquinone
2. **Robust separation of confidence intervals:** 90% CIs for Cmax (1.59-3.04) and AUC0-t (1.51-2.70) do not cross 1.0
3. **No safety concerns:** Both formulations well-tolerated with zero adverse events
4. **Novel cocrystal technology:** Cocrystal formulation significantly improves absorption
5. **Complete crossover design:** Within-subject comparison eliminates inter-individual variability

## Clinical Implications
- **Formulation choice:** Novel cocrystal ubiquinol superior to standard ubiquinone
- **Dose optimization:** Higher bioavailability may allow lower doses with same effect
- **Cost-effectiveness:** Reduced dose may lower treatment cost while maintaining efficacy
- **Patient compliance:** Lower pill burden possible with enhanced bioavailability

## Limitations
- **Small sample size:** n=12 limits statistical power and generalizability
- **Healthy subjects only:** ME/CFS patients may have different absorption profiles
- **Fasting conditions:** Fed state (typical dosing scenario) not tested
- **Single dose study:** Chronic dosing pharmacokinetics not assessed
- **No clinical outcomes:** Bioavailability advantage not linked to symptom improvement
