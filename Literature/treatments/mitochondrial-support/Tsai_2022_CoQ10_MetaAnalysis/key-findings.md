# Key Findings: Tsai et al. (2022) - CoQ10 Fatigue Meta-Analysis

## Main Findings
1. **Significant fatigue reduction:** CoQ10 supplementation significantly reduces fatigue symptoms across multiple conditions (Hedges' g = -0.398, p = 0.001)
2. **Dose-response relationship:** Higher daily doses correlate with greater fatigue reduction (coefficient = -0.0017 per mg, p < 0.001)
3. **Duration-response relationship:** Longer treatment durations correlate with greater fatigue reduction (coefficient = -0.0042 per day, p = 0.007)
4. **Formulation superiority:** CoQ10-only formulations more effective than CoQ10 compounds
5. **Excellent safety:** Only 1 adverse event in 602 participants (0.17%)

## Clinical Implications
- **Dosing strategy:** Higher doses and longer treatment durations yield better outcomes
- **Formulation choice:** Prefer CoQ10-only over compounded formulations
- **Safety profile:** CoQ10 is safe for long-term use in most patients
- **Broad applicability:** Effective across both healthy individuals and those with disease-related fatigue

## Limitations
- **Study heterogeneity:** Different protocols, doses, and formulations across included studies
- **Industry funding:** First author has industry affiliation (InnovaRad Inc.)
- **Publication bias:** Not formally assessed
- **Disease-specific data:** Limited ME/CFS-specific subgroup analysis
- **Formulation standardization:** Lack of standardized bioavailability data
