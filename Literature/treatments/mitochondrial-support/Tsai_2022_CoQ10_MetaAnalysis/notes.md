# Tsai et al. (2022) - CoQ10 Fatigue Meta-Analysis

**Authors:** Tsai, I.C. and Hsu, C.W. and Chang, C.H. and Tseng, P.T. and Chang, K.V.
**Journal:** Frontiers in Pharmacology
**Year:** 2022
**Volume:** 13
**Pages:** 883251
**DOI:** 10.3389/fphar.2022.883251
**PMID:** 36091835
**PMCID:** PMC9449413

## Key Points
- Meta-analysis of 13 RCTs with 1,126 participants
- CoQ10 significantly reduces fatigue (Hedges' g = -0.398, 95% CI = -0.641 to -0.155, p = 0.001)
- Dose-response relationship: higher daily doses correlate with greater fatigue reduction (coefficient = -0.0017 per mg, p < 0.001)
- Duration-response relationship: longer treatment durations correlate with greater fatigue reduction (coefficient = -0.0042 per day, p = 0.007)
- Effects consistent across healthy and diseased participants
- CoQ10-only formulation more effective than CoQ10 compounds
- Only 1 adverse event (gastrointestinal) in 602 participants receiving CoQ10

## Methodology
- Search: database inception to January 2022
- Study design: randomized controlled trials
- Analysis: random effects model
- Population: 1,126 participants across 13 RCTs
- Meta-regression: dose and duration as predictors

## Acronym Definitions
- RCT: Randomized Controlled Trial
- CoQ10: Coenzyme Q10
- Hedges' g: Effect size measure for meta-analysis
- CI: Confidence Interval

## Certainty Assessment
- **Study Quality:** High (systematic review and meta-analysis)
- **Sample Size:** n=1,126 (large)
- **Replication:** Meta-analysis aggregates multiple studies
- **Conflicts of Interest:** I-CT is founder of InnovaRad Inc.; others declare none
- **Certainty:** High (0.85)

## Critical Findings
- **Dose-dependent effect:** Each additional mg of daily CoQ10 correlates with 0.0017 greater reduction in fatigue scores
- **Duration-dependent effect:** Each additional day of treatment correlates with 0.0042 greater reduction in fatigue scores
- **Formulation matters:** CoQ10-only outperformed CoQ10 compounds
- **Safety:** Excellent safety profile with minimal adverse events
- **Generalizability:** Effects consistent across healthy and diseased populations

## Limitations
- Heterogeneity across included studies
- Different formulations not standardized
- Industry funding for first author (InnovaRad Inc.)
- Publication bias not assessed
- No subgroup analysis by disease severity

## Integration Points
- **Primary evidence base:** Supports CoQ10 supplementation for fatigue reduction in ME/CFS
- **Dose guidance:** Provides quantitative dose-response relationship
- **Duration guidance:** Provides quantitative duration-response relationship
- **Safety data:** Supports excellent safety profile
- **Clinical translation:** Can inform dosing strategies for ME/CFS patients

## Relevance to ME/CFS
- **Direct relevance:** Meta-analysis includes studies with ME/CFS and fatigue-related conditions
- **Mechanistic plausibility:** CoQ10 addresses mitochondrial dysfunction documented in ME/CFS
- **Clinical applicability:** Dose-response data directly translatable to ME/CFS treatment
- **Evidence strength:** Provides highest level of evidence (meta-analysis of RCTs)
