# Key Findings: Regland et al. 2015 - B12/Folate Response in ME/FM

## Main Findings

1. **MTHFR-Stratified Folate Dosing:** Patients whose folic acid dose was matched to their MTHFR genotype showed better outcomes, suggesting genetic-guided dosing is clinically relevant.
2. **Methylcobalamin Superiority:** Among B12 forms, methylcobalamin injections appeared more effective than hydroxycobalamin for ME/FM symptoms.
3. **Dose and Frequency Matter:** Higher doses and more frequent injections both independently predicted better outcomes; simple low-dose supplementation is likely insufficient.
4. **Analgesic Confound:** Daily analgesic use was the strongest negative predictor of good response. Authors speculate analgesics may interfere with B12/folate mechanisms.
5. **Thyroid Hormone Interaction:** More frequent thyroid hormone use in good responders may indicate a linked metabolic deficit or that optimal thyroid function is required for B12/folate efficacy.

## Clinical Implications for ME/CFS Integration

- In ch16 (Supplements), the B12 section should note: (a) methylcobalamin may be preferred over cyanocobalamin/hydroxycobalamin, (b) MTHFR genotyping can guide folate dosing, (c) high-frequency parenteral administration may be necessary for CNS effect
- This study supports the biological plausibility of the Regland 1997 CSF-HCY finding by showing clinical response to the mechanistically expected treatment
- The dose-response observation is a meaningful scientific signal despite the absence of an RCT

## Limitations

- No control/placebo group
- Combined ME+FM cohort; not pure ME/CFS
- Small sample (n=38)
- Same research group as 1997 study (not independent)
- Retrospective cross-sectional design
- Analgesic confound unresolved
