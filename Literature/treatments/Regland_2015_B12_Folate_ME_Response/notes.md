# Response to Vitamin B12 and Folic Acid in ME and Fibromyalgia

**Authors:** Regland B, Forsmark S, Halaouate L, Matousek M, Peilot B, Zachrisson O, Gottfries CG
**Journal:** PLoS One, 2015, 10(4):e0124648
**DOI:** 10.1371/journal.pone.0124648
**PMID:** 25902009

## Key Points

- High-frequency B12 injections + folate (dose matched to MTHFR genotype) produced good response in ~40% of ME/FM patients
- Methylcobalamin more effective than hydroxycobalamin form
- MTHFR genotyping guided folate dosing, suggesting genetic stratification matters
- Concomitant analgesic use was a strong negative predictor of response
- Thyroid hormone co-treatment associated with better response (metabolic intersection)
- Dose-response relationship observed supports a true pharmacological effect

## Methodology

Cross-sectional observational survey. Validated Fibro Fatigue (FF) scale for outcome. MTHFR genotyping. Ethics committee approved. Open-label; no placebo control.

## Acronym Definitions

- ME: Myalgic Encephalomyelitis
- FM: Fibromyalgia
- FF: Fibro Fatigue (scale)
- MTHFR: Methylenetetrahydrofolate reductase

## Certainty Assessment

- **Study Quality:** Low-Medium (observational, no control group, open-label, small n)
- **Sample Size:** n=38
- **Replication:** Not independently replicated by separate group in an RCT
- **Conflicts of Interest:** Not reported; Swedish academic institutions
- **Limitations for ME/CFS use:** Combined ME+FM cohort. No placebo control means response bias
  cannot be excluded. Retrospective design. Authors are the same group as Regland 1997 (not
  independent replication). Analgesic confound could reflect severity bias.
- **Strength:** Dose-response relationship; MTHFR genotyping integration; long observation period
