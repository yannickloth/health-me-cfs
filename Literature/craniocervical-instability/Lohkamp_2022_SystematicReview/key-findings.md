# Key Findings: Lohkamp 2022 Systematic Review

## Main Findings

1. **Diagnostic Heterogeneity:** No consensus on CCI diagnostic criteria in EDS; studies use different measurements and thresholds
2. **Dynamic Imaging Superiority:** Upright and flexion-extension MRI provides more diagnostic information than supine static imaging
3. **Clinical Correlation Essential:** Imaging abnormalities alone insufficient for diagnosis; symptoms must correlate with findings
4. **Surgical Outcomes Positive:** When properly indicated, occipito-cervical fusion shows 60-80% improvement in pain and neurological symptoms

## Clinical Implications

### For ME/CFS Diagnosis:
- CCI should be considered in differential diagnosis for hypermobile ME/CFS patients
- Structural abnormalities may contribute to symptom burden in subset of patients
- Screening warranted when progressive neurological symptoms present with hypermobility

### For Treatment Planning:
- Conservative management (physical therapy, cervical collar) should be first-line
- Surgical referral appropriate for progressive myelopathy or failed conservative treatment
- Multidisciplinary approach needed (neurosurgery, genetics, physical therapy, pain management)

## Limitations

- **High heterogeneity** limits ability to establish unified diagnostic criteria
- **Selection bias** toward severe cases from neurosurgical centers
- **Limited long-term data** (most studies <3 year follow-up)
- **Unclear natural history** of untreated CCI in EDS population

## Evidence Quality

- **Certainty:** Medium-High
  - Systematic review methodology (PRISMA)
  - Large total sample (n=695 across 16 studies)
  - But: high heterogeneity between studies
  - But: no randomized controlled trials

- **Applicability to ME/CFS:** Medium
  - Directly relevant to hypermobile subset (20-40% of ME/CFS)
  - Less relevant to non-hypermobile patients
  - Symptom overlap significant (fatigue, autonomic, cognitive)
