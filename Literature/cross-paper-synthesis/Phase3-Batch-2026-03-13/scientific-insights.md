**Key insight**: This is not a linear pathway but a **self-amplifying multi-organ loop** with at least three feedforward circuits:
1. NETosis --> NLRP3 --> IL-18 --> CD8 suppression --> viral persistence --> more NETosis
2. Viral persistence --> S1 release --> brain priming --> lower threshold --> more symptoms from less
3. IL-18 --> insulin resistance --> metabolic dysfunction --> impaired immune metabolism --> worse immune function

**Omdal's null result** constrains the neurological arm: the brain effects are functional (microglial priming) not structural (no axonal damage), explaining why standard neuroinflammation markers fail to detect them.

**Wu's vaccination finding** establishes that this entire cascade can be prevented by avoiding the initial infection, but cannot be interrupted by post-infection immune modification -- consistent with a committed trajectory once the loop initiates.

## Five Highest-Priority Cross-Paper Insights

### 1. IL-18 as the Convergent Hub Cytokine

IL-18 appears in four of six papers' mechanistic implications:
- **Source**: NETosis-NLRP3 loop (Monsalve/existing ch07)
- **Effect 1**: CD8 suppression --> viral persistence (Autaa)
- **Effect 2**: Insulin resistance --> metabolic syndrome pattern (Beentjes)
- **Effect 3**: Neuroinflammatory contribution via inflammasome priming (Frank)
- **Blockade potential**: Could simultaneously improve immune function, metabolic status, and neuroinflammation

**Priority**: IL-18 measurement should be added to every ME/CFS research study as a stratification biomarker.

### 2. Functional Neuroinflammation as a Distinct Pathological Category

Frank + Omdal together establish that ME/CFS neurological symptoms likely involve:
- Microglial priming without neuronal death
- Corticosteroid depletion without structural damage
- Circuit-level dysfunction without biomarker-detectable injury

This requires **new biomarker modalities** (TSPO-PET, MR spectroscopy, CSF kynurenine pathway) rather than continued reliance on NfL/GFAP.

### 3. The Deconditioning Refutation is Now Four-Pronged

Beentjes adds a fourth independent refutation line to the existing three documented in Chapter 13:
1. Exercise physiology (Charlton 2026)
2. Clinical guidelines (NICE 2021)
3. Comparative virology (Kol 2026)
4. **Population-scale biomarker mediation analysis (Beentjes 2025)** -- 290 traits with direct effects, only 1 mediated by activity

### 4. Age as a Critical Effect Modifier Across All Mechanisms

Autaa's age-dependent CD8 vulnerability interacts with every other paper:
- Older patients clear reservoirs less effectively (Proal amplified)
- More persistent reservoirs produce more brain priming (Frank amplified)
- More brain priming lowers neuroinflammatory threshold further
- Worse metabolic dysfunction through prolonged IL-18 exposure (Beentjes amplified)
- Less vaccine protection (Wu -- implied by Autaa mechanism)

**ME/CFS clinical implication**: Age at onset and current age should be primary stratification variables in clinical trials.

### 5. Acute-Phase Commitment and the Prevention Imperative

Wu's mediation analysis, combined with the self-amplifying nature of the cascade, suggests that prevention (vaccination, infection avoidance) is far more tractable than post-infection treatment. Once the cascade initiates:
- Reservoirs establish within days (Proal)
- Brain priming begins with first antigen exposure (Frank)
- NETosis loop amplifies within the first week of infection
- CD8 suppression prevents self-resolution (Autaa)

**Public health implication**: Long COVID / post-viral ME/CFS prevention should receive at least equal funding priority to treatment research.

## Caveats and Limitations

- This cross-paper synthesis is creative extrapolation; the mechanistic cascade has not been tested as a whole
- Individual papers address different conditions (ME/CFS, long COVID, acute COVID-19, rat models) and should not be uncritically unified
- The IL-18 hub hypothesis, while supported by convergent evidence, may oversimplify a multi-cytokine system
- Treatment combinations have not been tested for safety or efficacy in ME/CFS
- The self-amplifying loop model predicts that the disease should always worsen; the existence of spontaneous recoveries suggests additional regulatory mechanisms not captured here
- All treatment suggestions are mechanistic hypotheses requiring clinical validation
- Publication bias may inflate the apparent mechanistic coherence -- null results like Omdal's are underrepresented
