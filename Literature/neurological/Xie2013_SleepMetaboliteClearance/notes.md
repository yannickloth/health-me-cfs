# Sleep drives metabolite clearance from the adult brain

**Authors:** Xie L, Kang H, Xu Q, Chen MJ, Liao Y, Thiyagarajan M, O'Donnell J, Christensen DJ, Nicholson C, Iliff JJ, Takano T, Deane R, Nedergaard M
**Journal:** Science. 2013 Oct 18;342(6156):373-7
**DOI:** 10.1126/science.1241224
**PMID:** 24136970

## Key Points
- Sleep has critical function in ensuring metabolic homeostasis
- Natural sleep or anesthesia associated with 60% increase in interstitial space
- Increased interstitial space enhances convective exchange of CSF with interstitial fluid
- Convective fluxes increase rate of β-amyloid clearance during sleep
- Restorative function of sleep may be consequence of enhanced removal of neurotoxic waste products

## Methodology
- Real-time assessments of tetramethylammonium diffusion in live mice
- Two-photon imaging to measure interstitial space volume
- Measurement of CSF-interstitial fluid exchange during sleep vs. wake
- Quantification of β-amyloid clearance rates

## Acronym Definitions
- **CSF**: Cerebrospinal fluid (fluid surrounding brain and spinal cord)
- **ISF**: Interstitial fluid (fluid between cells in brain tissue)
- **β-amyloid**: Amyloid-beta peptide (protein fragment accumulates in Alzheimer's)
- **SWS**: Slow-wave sleep (deep sleep stage)
- **Glymphatic system**: Waste clearance system in brain, most active during sleep
