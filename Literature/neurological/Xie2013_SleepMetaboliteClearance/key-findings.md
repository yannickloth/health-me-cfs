# Key Findings: Sleep Drives Metabolite Clearance

## Main Findings
1. **Sleep increases interstitial space:** 60% increase during sleep vs. wake
2. **Enhanced fluid exchange:** Increased CSF-ISF convective exchange during sleep
3. **Accelerated waste clearance:** β-amyloid clearance rate significantly increased during sleep
4. **Metabolic homeostasis:** Sleep's restorative function linked to neurotoxic waste removal
5. **Glymphatic system:** Identification of novel brain waste clearance pathway

## Clinical Implications
- Sleep disruption may impair brain's ability to clear neurotoxic metabolites
- Glymphatic dysfunction could contribute to neurodegenerative diseases
- Sleep quality directly impacts brain metabolic health

## Limitations
- Animal study (mice), translation to humans needs confirmation
- Focused on β-amyloid, other metabolites not studied
- Sleep deprivation mechanisms not fully explored
