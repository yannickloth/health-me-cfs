## Key Points

- Proposes endothelial senescence (not merely endothelial dysfunction) as a unifying mechanistic cause of ME/CFS and long COVID
- Acute viral infection triggers endothelial dysfunction and senescence directly (viral infection of endothelial cells) and indirectly (immune-mediated damage)
- Senescent endothelial cells adopt the senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP): proinflammatory, pro-oxidative, procoagulant, primed for vasoconstriction, impaired tissue repair
- Dysfunctional immune system prevents clearance of senescent endothelial cells, perpetuating the disease chronically
- Bidirectional loop: SASP promotes immune dysfunction; immune dysfunction sustains endothelial senescence
- Tissue-specific SASP effects at four key sites explain specific symptom clusters:
  1. Blood-brain barrier (BBB): brain fog, neuroinflammation, reduced cerebral blood flow
  2. Cerebral arteries: perfusion deficits, impaired autoregulation
  3. Gastrointestinal tract: gut symptoms, dysbiosis, leaky gut
  4. Skeletal muscle: exercise intolerance, PEM, metabolic dysfunction

## Mechanisms Proposed

### Trigger: Viral Endothelial Infection / Immune-Mediated Damage
- Direct: virus infects endothelial cells, inducing senescence
- Indirect: immune response (cytokines, autoantibodies) damages endothelium

### SASP Properties (senescent endothelial cell secretome)
- Proinflammatory: IL-6, IL-1beta, TNF-alpha, other cytokines
- Pro-oxidative: reactive oxygen species production
- Procoagulant: platelet activation, fibrin deposition, microclot formation
- Vasoconstriction-primed: reduced NO bioavailability, impaired vasodilation
- Impaired tissue repair: reduced angiogenesis, impaired wound healing

### Chronicity Mechanism
- Normal clearance of senescent cells requires functional NK cells and cytotoxic T cells
- ME/CFS and long COVID feature NK cell dysfunction, T cell exhaustion, and immune dysregulation
- This prevents senescent endothelial cell clearance
- Persistent SASP sustains the inflammatory and vascular state indefinitely

### Bidirectional Amplification Loop
SASP cytokines --> immune dysfunction --> impaired clearance of senescent cells --> more SASP --> further immune dysfunction

### Tissue-Specific Effects
1. BBB endothelium: increased permeability, neuroinflammation, brain fog
2. Cerebral arteries: impaired blood flow autoregulation, reduced cerebral perfusion
3. GI endothelium: increased gut permeability, microbiome alterations, GI symptoms
4. Skeletal muscle microvasculature: impaired perfusion during exercise, metabolic crisis, PEM

## Biomarkers Implied / Suggested

- Markers of endothelial senescence (p16, p21, beta-galactosidase in endothelial cells)
- SASP cytokine panel (IL-6, IL-1beta, TNF-alpha, etc.)
- Endothelial activation markers (E-selectin, VCAM-1, ICAM-1)
- Coagulation/microclot markers (already studied in long COVID: fibrinogen, VWF, microclots)
- Cerebral blood flow (MRI perfusion imaging)

## Treatment Targets Implied

- Senolytics: eliminate senescent endothelial cells (dasatinib + quercetin studied in long COVID)
- Senomorphics: suppress SASP without killing cells
- NK cell/immune restoration: enable natural clearance of senescent cells
- Anticoagulants / fibrinolytics: address procoagulant SASP effects
- Anti-inflammatory: reduce SASP-driven inflammation
- Vasodilators / NO donors: compensate for impaired endothelial vasodilation
