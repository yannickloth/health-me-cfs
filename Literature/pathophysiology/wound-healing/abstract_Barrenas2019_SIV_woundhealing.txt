PMID: 31704931
Title: Macrophage-associated wound healing contributes to African green monkey SIV pathogenesis control
Authors: Barrenas F, Raehtz K, Xu C, Law L, Green RR, Silvestri G, Bosinger SE, Nishida A,
         Li Q, Lu W, Zhang J, Thomas MJ, Chang J, Smith E, Weiss JM, Dawoud RA, Richter GH,
         Trichel A, Ma D, Peng X, Komorowski J, Apetrei C, Pandrea I, Gale M Jr.
Journal: Nature Communications
Year: 2019
Volume: 10
Pages: 5101
DOI: 10.1038/s41467-019-12987-9

Abstract:
African green monkeys naturally control SIV infection without developing AIDS through distinct
mechanisms. Using Conserved Gene Signature Analysis (CGSA), researchers found that AGMs rapidly
activate and then maintain evolutionarily conserved regenerative wound healing mechanisms in
mucosal tissue. These animals display enhanced tissue repair signatures with reduced inflammatory
gene expression compared to AIDS-susceptible rhesus macaques. Notable differences include
distinct fibronectin distribution patterns and monocytes exhibiting an embryonic development
and repair/regeneration signature featuring TGF-β. This regenerative response appears to
maintain mucosal integrity and prevent inflammatory damage associated with immune exhaustion.
Critically, this paper establishes the inverse relationship: when tissue repair/wound healing
mechanisms are impaired (as in immune exhaustion states), systemic pathology worsens.
