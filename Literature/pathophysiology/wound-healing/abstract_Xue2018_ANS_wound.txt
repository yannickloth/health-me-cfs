PMID: 29988115
Title: The mouse autonomic nervous system modulates inflammation and epithelial renewal after
      corneal abrasion through the activation of distinct local macrophages
Authors: Xue Y, He J, Xiao C, Guo Y, Fu T, Liu J, Lin C, Wu M, Yang Y, Dong D, Pan H,
         Xia C, Ren L, Li Z
Journal: Mucosal Immunology
Year: 2018
Volume: 11
Number: 5
Pages: 1496-1511
DOI: 10.1038/s41385-018-0031-6

Abstract:
This study demonstrates that the autonomic nervous system regulates corneal wound healing
and inflammatory responses through distinct macrophage populations with opposite effects.
Sympathetic activation inhibited reepithelialization after corneal abrasion and increased
neutrophil influx, while parasympathetic activation had the opposite effects. Two macrophage
subsets were identified: CD64+CCR2+ cells expressing β2-adrenergic receptors (pro-inflammatory,
SNS-responsive) and CD64+CCR2- cells expressing α7 nicotinic acetylcholine receptors
(anti-inflammatory, PNS-responsive). The findings suggest that manipulation of autonomic
nervous system inputs to wounds may represent an alternative approach to treating impaired
wound healing. Importantly, sympathetic overactivation — well-documented in ME/CFS through
POTS and dysautonomia — is shown here to directly impair epithelial repair.
