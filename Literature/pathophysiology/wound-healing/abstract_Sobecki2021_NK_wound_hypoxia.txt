PMID: 34349124
Title: NK cells in hypoxic skin mediate a trade-off between wound healing and antibacterial defence
Authors: Sobecki M, Krzywinska E, Nagarajan S, Audigé A, Huỳnh K, Zacharjasz J, Debbache J,
        Kerdiles Y, Gotthardt D, Takeda N, Fandrey J, Sommer L, Sexl V, Stockmann C
Journal: Nature Communications
Year: 2021
Volume: 12
Pages: 4700
DOI: 10.1038/s41467-021-25065-w

Abstract:
NK cells in the wound microenvironment must balance two competing biological functions under
hypoxic conditions. HIF-1α in NK cells acts as a regulatory switch: when absent in NK cells,
mice show impaired release of IFN-γ and GM-CSF, resulting in faster wound repair but weakened
infection control. Enhanced HIF pathway activation strengthens NK cell-mediated antibacterial
defence while slowing healing. The researchers conclude that HIF-1α in NK cells serves as a
regulatory mechanism determining whether the body prioritizes tissue regeneration or immune
protection during skin injury recovery. The study establishes that NK cells are active,
mechanistically required participants in wound healing (not passive bystanders), with
their cytokine output directly modulating the rate of tissue closure.
