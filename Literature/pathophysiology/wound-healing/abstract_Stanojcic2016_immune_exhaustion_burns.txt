PMID: 26649579
Title: Impaired Immune Response in Elderly Burn Patients: New Insights Into the Immune-senescence Phenotype
Authors: Stanojcic M, Chen P, Xiu F, Jeschke MG
Journal: Annals of Surgery
Year: 2016
Volume: 264
Number: 1
Pages: 195-202
DOI: 10.1097/SLA.0000000000001408

Abstract:
This research compared inflammatory and immune responses between burned adults (n=94, under 65)
and elderly burn patients (n=36, age 65+) plus 10 healthy controls. Elderly patients, despite
smaller burn sizes, had worse clinical outcomes. The researchers observed that the elderly are
immune compromised and unable to respond with the expected inflammatory response during the early
phase after injury. This pattern reversed later, becoming hyperinflammatory. Septic elderly
patients and non-survivors displayed an immune exhaustion phenotype which may represent one of
the main mediators responsible for striking mortality.

Relevance to ME/CFS wound healing claim: ME/CFS exhibits an immune exhaustion phenotype
(exhausted NK cells, skewed cytokine profiles, persistent immune activation) analogous to
what this study documents in elderly burn patients — where that same phenotype predicts
impaired wound healing responses and elevated mortality from infection.
