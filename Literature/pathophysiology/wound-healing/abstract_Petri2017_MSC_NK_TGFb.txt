PMID: 28781075
Title: Activated Tissue-Resident Mesenchymal Stromal Cells Regulate the Immune Response in the Tumor Microenvironment
Authors: Petri RM, Hackel A, Hahnel K, Dumitru CA, Bruderek K, Flohe SB, Paschen A, Lang S, Brandau S
Journal: Stem Cell Reports
Year: 2017
Volume: 9
Number: 3
Pages: 985-998
DOI: 10.1016/j.stemcr.2017.06.020

Abstract:
This study challenges the static inhibitory model of MSC-NK cell interactions. Using poly(I:C)
stimulation to model infection/tissue injury, it reveals a time-dependent system: early on,
activated MSCs secrete type I interferon to enhance NK cell effector function; at later time
points, TGF-β and IL-6 limit NK cell effector function and terminate inflammatory responses
by inducing a regulatory senescent-like NK cell phenotype. Feedback from these regulatory NK
cells to MSCs promotes survival, proliferation, and pro-angiogenic properties critical to
wound healing.

Key implication: In ME/CFS, where TGF-β is chronically elevated and NK cells are in a
functionally exhausted state, this regulatory circuit may be constitutively activated —
driving premature inflammatory resolution and impaired angiogenic wound support without
completing the repair process.
