PMID: 34163486
Title: Reversing Post-Infectious Epigenetic-Mediated Immune Suppression
Authors: Abhimanyu, Ontiveros CO, Guerra-Resendez RS, Nishiguchi T, Ladki M, Hilton IB,
         Schlesinger LS, DiNardo AR
Journal: Frontiers in Immunology
Year: 2021
Volume: 12
Pages: 688132
DOI: 10.3389/fimmu.2021.688132

Abstract:
Following severe infections like sepsis and tuberculosis, detrimental epigenetic scars persist
and result in long-lasting immune suppression. The authors describe how pathway modulation
(AMPK-mTOR, NFAT, NR4A) and epigenetic enzyme inhibitors can reverse these harmful marks.
The immune response must balance pro-inflammatory, cell-mediated cytotoxicity with the
anti-inflammatory and wound repair response. Evidence from laboratory and animal studies
demonstrates that approaches like HDAC inhibitors, DNA hypomethylating agents, and
bioengineering techniques can restore host immunity in the setting of cancer-, LCMV- or
murine sepsis-induced epigenetic-mediated immune suppression, though clinical translation
studies remain necessary.

Relevance: ME/CFS has documented post-infectious epigenetic immune changes (trained
immunity gone wrong). This paper establishes that post-infectious immune suppression
specifically impairs the wound repair arm of immunity, supporting the claim that
ME/CFS immune dysfunction would impair wound healing.
