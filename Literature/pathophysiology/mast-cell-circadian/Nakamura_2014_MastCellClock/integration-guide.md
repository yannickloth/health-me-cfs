```latex
\begin{hypothesis}[Circadian Dysregulation of Mast Cells]
\label{hyp:mast-cell-circadian}
Nakamura et al.~\cite{Nakamura2014} demonstrated that mast cells possess intrinsic
circadian clocks entrained by adrenal hormones, with Clock gene mutation abolishing
time-of-day variation in degranulation (mouse model, High certainty for mechanism).
Given HPA axis dysfunction in ME/CFS, disrupted circadian entrainment could cause
inappropriate mast cell activation timing, potentially explaining symptom patterns
and sleep-symptom interactions (extrapolation to ME/CFS: speculative).
\end{hypothesis}
```

## Key Points to Convey
1. Mast cells have functional circadian clocks (Clock gene-dependent)
2. Adrenal hormones entrain these clocks (adrenalectomy disrupts rhythms)
3. Clock controls FcÎµRI expression and signaling (mechanistic link)
4. HPA dysfunction in ME/CFS could disrupt this system (extrapolation)

## Certainty Assessment
- **Mechanism:** High (elegant genetics, multiple assays, replicated)
- **ME/CFS relevance:** Low-Medium (extrapolation from mouse allergy model)
- **Overall:** Medium certainty for hypothesis
