# Key Findings: Schubert et al. 2021 - Tinnitus and CFS Association

## Main Findings

1. **CFS-Tinnitus Association:** ME/CFS patients have 1.568 times higher odds of experiencing constant tinnitus compared to general population (first large-scale epidemiological evidence)
2. **High Prevalence:** 6.4% of general population experiences constant tinnitus, 31.8% any tinnitus
3. **Novel Category:** Functional somatic syndromes (including CFS) represent newly identified risk category beyond traditional audiological causes

## Clinical Implications

- Tinnitus should be considered a potential neurological manifestation in ME/CFS
- Clinicians should screen ME/CFS patients for tinnitus symptoms
- Shared pathophysiology may exist between ME/CFS and tinnitus (neuroinflammation, vascular dysfunction, autonomic dysfunction)
- Tinnitus severity may correlate with ME/CFS symptom burden

## Limitations

- Cross-sectional design cannot establish causality (does CFS cause tinnitus, or vice versa, or common underlying mechanism?)
- Self-reported CFS diagnosis not verified with standardized diagnostic criteria
- No data on tinnitus severity or ME/CFS symptom severity correlation
- Single cohort (Dutch population); generalizability unknown
