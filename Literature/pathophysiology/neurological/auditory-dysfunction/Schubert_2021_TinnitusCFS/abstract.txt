A retrospective cross-sectional study on tinnitus prevalence and disease associations in the Dutch population-based cohort Lifelines

This investigation examined tinnitus occurrence and connected health conditions using data from over 167,000 participants in the Lifelines cohort study. Among 124,609 individuals analyzed, approximately 6.4% reported experiencing constant tinnitus while 31.8% reported any tinnitus. The research confirmed established associations with hearing difficulties, cardiac arrhythmias, and major depression.
