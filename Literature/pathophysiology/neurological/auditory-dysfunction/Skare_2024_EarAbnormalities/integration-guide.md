## Key Points to Convey

1. **Comprehensive synthesis:** 172 articles reviewed (1990-2024) - emphasize breadth and recency
2. **Cochlear complaints predominant:** Tinnitus, hearing loss, hyperacusis most frequent
3. **Cross-syndrome consistency:** Similar patterns in ME/CFS, fibromyalgia, long-COVID, PoTS
4. **Four mechanisms:** Viral, vascular, autoimmune, oxidative - NOT mutually exclusive
5. **Clinical implication:** Systematic auditory assessment warranted

## Certainty Assessment for Integration

- **Quality:** High (systematic review in *Autoimmunity Reviews*, comprehensive search)
- **Sample:** 172 articles reviewed (extensive literature base)
- **Replication:** Synthesis across multiple conditions and studies
- **Limitations:** Heterogeneous study quality; primarily descriptive (no meta-analysis); mechanisms largely hypothetical

## Integration Strategy

**Narrative Arc:**
1. **Establish phenomenon:** Johnson1996 demonstrated auditory processing deficits (cognitive)
2. **Population scale:** Schubert2021 showed tinnitus prevalence (epidemiology)
3. **Comprehensive synthesis:** Skare2024 systematic review establishes breadth and mechanisms
4. **Anatomical basis:** Nelson2021 brainstem abnormalities provide structural explanation

**Mechanistic Integration:**
- Use Skare2024 to introduce the FOUR mechanisms (viral, vascular, autoimmune, oxidative)
- Connect each mechanism to other chapters:
  - Viral → Chapter on infectious triggers
  - Vascular → Chapter 10 (cardiovascular dysfunction)
  - Autoimmune → Chapter 7 (immune dysfunction)
  - Oxidative → Chapter on mitochondrial/metabolic dysfunction
- Emphasize that these mechanisms are NOT mutually exclusive and may operate together

**Clinical Integration:**
- Use systematic review authority to recommend auditory screening
- Note cross-syndrome patterns (ME/CFS, fibromyalgia, long-COVID, PoTS)
- Suggest this supports post-infectious syndrome commonalities
