# Key Findings: eMSN in Primate Striatum

## Main Findings
1. **D1/D2 hybrid neurons (eMSN) exist in primate striatum:** Confirmed presence of hybrid MSNs expressing both DRD1 and DRD2
2. **eMSN marker genes:** RXFP1 is highly specific marker for D1/D2 hybrid eMSN
3. **eMSN prevalence:** ~10% of MSNs in dorsal striatum
4. **eMSN distribution:** Uniformly distributed throughout dorsal striatum
5. **eMSN receptor expression:** High DRD1, low DRD2 compared to D2-MSNs
6. **MSN diversity:** Nine distinct MSN subtypes identified in primate striatum

## Clinical Implications
- eMSN represent a hybrid cell type integrating both direct (D1) and indirect (D2) basal ganglia pathways
- May have unique functional properties relevant to motor control, reward, and motivation
- Could be involved in neuropsychiatric disorders involving striatal dysfunction

## Limitations
- Primate model, not human
- Small sample size for tissue validation (n=2 for FISH)
- Does not address disease associations or ME/CFS
