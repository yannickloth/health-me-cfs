```latex
\begin{achievement}[Eccentric Medium Spiny Neurons in Primate Striatum]
\label{ach:he2021-emsn-primate}
He et al.~\cite{He2021} identified nine distinct MSN subtypes in rhesus macaque striatum, including D1/D2 hybrid neurons (eMSN). eMSN are marked by RXFP1 expression, constitute ~10\% of MSNs, and are uniformly distributed in dorsal striatum. eMSN express high DRD1 and low DRD2 compared to D2-MSNs, integrating both direct and indirect basal ganglia pathways.
Study: (n=4 macaques, snRNA-seq + FISH validation, certainty: High).
\end{achievement}
```

## Key Points to Convey
1. eMSN are a distinct MSN subtype with hybrid D1/D2 receptor expression
2. RXFP1 is a highly specific marker for eMSN identification
3. eMSN may have unique integrative functions in basal ganglia circuitry
4. This finding is well-established but not linked to ME/CFS in this paper

## Certainty Assessment for Integration
- **Quality:** High (peer-reviewed, rigorous methodology, FISH validation)
- **Sample:** n=4 macaques
- **Replication:** Yes (supported by mouse studies, human data emerging)
- **Limitations:** Primate model, not human; small N for tissue validation

## Notes
- This paper is critical background for eMSN biology but does not address ME/CFS
- Should be cited as foundational for eMSN hypothesis but not as evidence for ME/CFS involvement
- Future studies needed to examine eMSN in ME/CFS patients or disease models
