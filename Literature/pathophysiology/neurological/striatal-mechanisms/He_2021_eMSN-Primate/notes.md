# Transcriptional and Anatomical Diversity of Medium Spiny Neurons in the Primate Striatum

**Authors:** Jing He, Michael Kleyman, Jianjiao Chen, et al.
**Journal:** Current Biology, 2021, 31(24):5473-5486
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.cub.2021.10.015
**PMID:** 34727523

## Key Points
- Identified nine distinct MSN subtypes in rhesus macaque striatum
- Confirmed presence of D1/D2 hybrid neurons (eMSN) in primate striatum
- eMSN marker gene: RXFP1 (highly specific)
- eMSN express both DRD1 and DRD2 dopamine receptors
- eMSN constitute ~10% of MSNs in dorsal striatum
- Uniformly distributed throughout dorsal striatum
- Express high DRD1, low DRD2 compared to D2-MSNs
- Hybrid cell type with features of both D1 and D2 pathways

## Methodology
- Single-nucleus RNA-seq (snRNA-seq) from caudate, putamen, ventral striatum
- Fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) validation
- Probes for DRD1, DRD2, RXFP1, and other marker genes
- Archetypal analysis for gene expression gradients
- Comparison with mouse striatum data
- Sample: 4 rhesus macaques (2 for snRNA-seq, 2 for FISH)

## Acronym Definitions
- **MSN:** Medium Spiny Neurons
- **eMSN:** Eccentric Medium Spiny Neurons (D1/D2 hybrid)
- **DRD1:** Dopamine D1 Receptor
- **DRD2:** Dopamine D2 Receptor
- **RXFP1:** Relaxin Family Peptide Receptor 1
- **snRNA-seq:** Single-nucleus RNA sequencing
- **FISH:** Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization
- **NAc:** Nucleus Accumbens
- **OPRM1:** μ-Opioid Receptor 1

- Provides marker genes (RXFP1) for eMSN identification
- Critical for eMSN-ME/CFS hypothesis but does not mention ME/CFS
