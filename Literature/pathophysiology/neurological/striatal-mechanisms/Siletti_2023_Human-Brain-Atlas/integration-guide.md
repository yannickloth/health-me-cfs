## Key Points to Convey
1. Human brain cell atlas confirms eMSN as distinct cell type
2. Human eMSN marker genes identified (GAD1, GAD2, CXCL14, DRD1, ADARB2)
3. Hypothalamic neuronal diversity is high and complex
4. Dataset provides reference for future ME/CFS brain tissue studies

## Certainty Assessment for Integration
- **Quality:** Very High (landmark study, Science journal, rigorous methodology)
- **Sample:** n=3 donors, 3.3 million cells
- **Replication:** Dataset available for independent validation
- **Limitations:** Postmortem tissue quality, limited donors

## Notes
- This atlas is a foundational resource but does not address ME/CFS
- Should be cited as reference for human brain cell types
- Future studies needed to compare ME/CFS brain tissue against this atlas
- eMSN-specific disease associations remain unknown
