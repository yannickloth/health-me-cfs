# Transcriptomic diversity of cell types across the adult human brain

**Authors:** Kimberly Siletti, Rebecca D. Hodge, Alejandro Mossi Albiach, et al.
**Journal:** Science, 2023, 382(6667)
**DOI:** 10.1126/science.add7046
**PMCID:** PMC12725688

## Key Points
- Comprehensive single-nucleus RNA-seq atlas of entire human brain
- 3.3 million single-cell transcriptomes from 105 dissections
- 31 superclusters, 461 clusters, 3,313 subclusters identified
- High neuronal diversity outside cortex (hypothalamus, midbrain, hindbrain)
- **eMSN cluster confirmed in human brain:**
  - Markers: GAD1, GAD2, CXCL14, DRD1, ADARB2
  - Listed in Human Protein Atlas as "eccentric medium spiny neurons"
- Dataset available for independent validation

## Methodology
- Single-nucleus RNA-seq from ~100 locations across forebrain, midbrain, hindbrain
- 10xv3 chemistry
- 3 postmortem human donors
- Iterative clustering with SCCAF validation
- Comparison with mouse brain atlases

## Acronym Definitions
- **snRNA-seq:** Single-nucleus RNA sequencing
- **GAD1:** Glutamate Decarboxylase 1 (GABA synthesis enzyme)
- **GAD2:** Glutamate Decarboxylase 2 (GABA synthesis enzyme)
- **CXCL14:** C-X-C Motif Chemokine Ligand 14
- **DRD1:** Dopamine D1 Receptor
- **ADARB2:** Adenosine Deaminase RNA Specific B2 (RNA editing enzyme)
- **SCCAF:** Single-cell Clustering Assessment Framework
