# Key Findings: Human Brain Cell Atlas

## Main Findings
1. **Comprehensive human brain cell atlas:** 3.3 million cells from 105 dissections across entire brain
2. **eMSN confirmed in human brain:** Human Protein Atlas lists eMSN cluster with specific markers
3. **Human eMSN markers:** GAD1, GAD2, CXCL14, DRD1, ADARB2
4. **Unprecedented neuronal diversity:** High diversity outside cortex (hypothalamus, midbrain, hindbrain)
5. **Resource availability:** Dataset publicly available for validation and analysis

## Clinical Implications
- Establishes human eMSN as validated cell type
- Enables future cell-type specific analyses in disease contexts
- Provides reference atlas for post-mortem brain studies
- Facilitates comparison across brain regions and species

## Limitations
- Postmortem tissue quality limitations
- Limited number of donors (n=3)
- Does not address disease associations or ME/CFS
- Dataset requires bioinformatics expertise for analysis
