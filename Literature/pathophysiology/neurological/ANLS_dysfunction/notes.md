# ANLS Dysfunction in ME/CFS: Literature Synthesis

**Topic:** Astrocyte-Neuron Lactate Shuttle (ANLS) dysfunction as a mechanism for CNS-specific energy failure in ME/CFS

## Key Papers

### 1. Kim, Dube & Park (2025) - ANLS Evolution Review
- **Journal:** Korean J Physiol Pharmacol, 29(1):1-8
- **DOI:** 10.4196/kjpp.24.388
- **PMID:** 39725609
- Key: Comprehensive review of ANLS evolution, MCT roles, disease associations
- Alzheimer's, ALS, epilepsy, depression all show ANLS dysfunction
- MCT1/MCT4 downregulation reduces neuronal lactate supply by 60%
- Neurons have more metabolic flexibility than originally thought (LDHA expression)

### 2. Godlewska et al. (2025) - Brain Lactate in ME/CFS at 7T
- **Journal:** Molecular Psychiatry, 30:5215-5226
- **DOI:** 10.1038/s41380-025-03108-8
- Key: Elevated brain lactate in ME/CFS (pgACC: 1.52 vs 1.22 mM, p=0.003; dACC: 1.45 vs 1.40 mM, p=0.005)
- n=24 ME/CFS, n=25 Long COVID, n=24 healthy controls
- ME/CFS and Long COVID show different neurochemical profiles
- Lactate elevation consistent with energetic stress and mitochondrial dysfunction

### 3. Mueller et al. (2020) - Whole-Brain MRS in ME/CFS
- **Journal:** Brain Imaging Behav, 14(2):562-572
- **DOI:** 10.1007/s11682-018-0029-4
- **PMID:** 30617782
- Key: Elevated lactate/creatine in right insula, thalamus, cerebellum
- Also elevated choline and myo-inositol (neuroinflammation markers)
- Brain temperature increases correlated with lactate elevations
- n=15 ME/CFS, n=15 healthy controls

### 4. Syed et al. (2025) - Mitochondrial Dysfunction in ME/CFS
- **Journal:** Physiology (Bethesda)
- **DOI:** 10.1152/physiol.00056.2024
- Key: Comprehensive review of mitochondrial dysfunction in ME/CFS
- Brain-specific lactate elevation linked to neuroinflammation
- Shift toward anaerobic metabolism documented

### 5. Jang et al. (2024) - Ketone Bodies and Neuroprotection
- **Journal:** Int J Mol Sci, 25(1):124
- **DOI:** 10.3390/ijms25010124
- **PMID:** 38203294
- Key: Ketone bodies bypass ANLS via MCT1/MCT2, directly enter neuronal TCA
- Beta-hydroxybutyrate enters neurons via MCT2 (same transporter as lactate)
- Ketogenic diet can substitute for impaired lactate shuttle

## Acronym Definitions
- ANLS: Astrocyte-Neuron Lactate Shuttle
- MCT1: Monocarboxylate Transporter 1 (astrocytes, endothelial cells)
- MCT2: Monocarboxylate Transporter 2 (neurons, high affinity)
- MCT4: Monocarboxylate Transporter 4 (astrocytes, low affinity, lactate export)
- GLUT1: Glucose Transporter 1 (brain endothelial cells, astrocytes)
- GLUT4: Glucose Transporter 4 (peripheral tissues, insulin-responsive)
- LDH: Lactate Dehydrogenase
- LDHA: LDH-A isoform (astrocytes, favors pyruvate -> lactate)
- LDHB: LDH-B isoform (neurons, favors lactate -> pyruvate)
- GFAP: Glial Fibrillary Acidic Protein
- pgACC: Pregenual Anterior Cingulate Cortex
- dACC: Dorsal Anterior Cingulate Cortex
- BHB: Beta-Hydroxybutyrate
- MRS: Magnetic Resonance Spectroscopy
- HCAR1: Hydroxycarboxylic Acid Receptor 1 (lactate receptor, GPR81)

## Certainty Assessment
- **ANLS physiology:** HIGH certainty - well-established since 1994, extensively replicated
- **Brain lactate elevation in ME/CFS:** MODERATE certainty - replicated across multiple MRS studies, small samples
- **ANLS dysfunction as ME/CFS mechanism:** LOW certainty - plausible but no direct evidence
- **MCT dysfunction in disease:** MODERATE certainty - documented in AD, ALS, epilepsy
- **Ketone bypass:** MODERATE certainty - well-established physiology, not tested in ME/CFS context
