Title: COVID-19: A methyl-group assault?
Authors: Andrew McCaddon, Bjorn Regland
Journal: Medical Hypotheses
Year: 2021
Volume: 149:110543
DOI: 10.1016/j.mehy.2021.110543
PMID: 33657459
PMC: PMC7890339

ABSTRACT:
Hypothesis paper. Authors propose SARS-CoV-2 infection creates competing demands on methylation
capacity that may explain long-COVID symptoms. Two-pronged mechanism:
1. Virus increases methyl-group consumption (viral RNA modification via m6A; viral RNA capping)
2. Virus impairs methyl supply via oxidative stress that inactivates methionine synthase

This is predicted to elevate homocysteine and deplete SAM.

Key connections:
- Long-COVID symptoms (fatigue 87%, memory complaints 78%) overlap remarkably with pernicious
  anemia symptoms (both mediated by B12/methylation deficiency)
- ME/CFS patients show "very markedly raised homocysteine in cerebrospinal fluid" (citing Regland 1997)
  with significant correlation to fatigue severity
- Mechanism proposes a common methylation-depletion pathway linking viral illness to ME/CFS-like states

Note: This is a HYPOTHESIS paper. Predicts testable outcomes (elevated homocysteine in long-COVID)
but mechanism requires in vivo confirmation.
