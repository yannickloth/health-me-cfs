# Key Findings: McCaddon & Regland 2021 - COVID Methyl Assault Hypothesis

## Main Claims (Unconfirmed Hypothesis)

1. **Dual Methylation Depletion:** SARS-CoV-2 simultaneously increases methyl-group demand (viral RNA m6A modification and capping) and reduces supply (oxidative inactivation of methionine synthase).
2. **Homocysteine Elevation Predicted:** This mechanism would elevate homocysteine (methionine synthase cannot clear it) and deplete SAM (less methionine -> less SAM).
3. **ME/CFS as Precedent:** Authors cite Regland 1997 as evidence that ME/CFS (which can follow viral triggers) already shows the predicted CSF homocysteine elevation.
4. **Symptom Overlap as Diagnostic Clue:** Long-COVID and pernicious anemia share fatigue, cognitive impairment, and other symptoms at strikingly similar rates, suggesting a shared methylation-deficiency phenomenology.
5. **Testable Predictions:** Elevated homocysteine in long-COVID patients; therapeutic response to B12/folate supplementation.

## Status

HYPOTHESIS ONLY. As of knowledge cutoff (2025), this has not been confirmed by randomized trial evidence in long-COVID or ME/CFS.
