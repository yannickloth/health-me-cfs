# COVID-19: A Methyl-Group Assault?

**Authors:** McCaddon A, Regland B
**Journal:** Medical Hypotheses, 2021, 149:110543
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.mehy.2021.110543
**PMID:** 33657459
**PMC:** PMC7890339

## Key Points

- Proposes SARS-CoV-2 simultaneously depletes methyl groups (via viral RNA modification) and impairs
  their resupply (via methionine synthase inactivation by oxidative stress)
- Predicted outcome: elevated homocysteine and depleted SAM in long-COVID
- Explicitly connects to ME/CFS via Regland 1997 CSF data
- Long-COVID fatigue symptom overlap with pernicious anemia is the clinical observation motivating
  the hypothesis

## Methodology

Hypothesis/speculation paper. Medical Hypotheses is a journal for speculative ideas requiring
validation. No primary data collected.

## Acronym Definitions

- SAM: S-Adenosylmethionine
- m6A: N6-methyladenosine (RNA modification)
- ME/CFS: Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome
