# ME/CFS Autopsy Study Finds a Wrecked HPA Axis

**Authors:** University of Amsterdam researchers (names not provided)
**Source:** Health Rising blog report of IACFS 2025 conference presentation
**Date:** 2025 (conference presentation)
**URL:** https://www.healthrising.org/blog/2025/12/04/chronic-fatigue-hpa-axis-autopsy/

## Key Points
- **University of Amsterdam autopsy study** of 7-10 ME/CFS patients
- **Very low levels of CRH-producing neurons in hypothalamus**
- Indicates dramatic CRH depletion and HPA axis dysfunction
- Presented at IACFS 2025 conference
- **No mention of striatum, eMSN, or basal ganglia findings**
- Preliminary evidence for hypothalamic CRH depletion in ME/CFS

## Methodology
- Brain autopsy of deceased ME/CFS patients
- CRH neuron quantification in hypothalamus
- Sample size: 7-10 autopsies
- Presented at conference (not peer-reviewed publication)

## Acronym Definitions
- **CRH:** Corticotropin-Releasing Hormone
- **HPA:** Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal (axis)
- **IACFS:** International Association for Chronic Fatigue Syndrome
- **ME/CFS:** Myalgic Encephalomyelitis / Chronic Fatigue Syndrome
