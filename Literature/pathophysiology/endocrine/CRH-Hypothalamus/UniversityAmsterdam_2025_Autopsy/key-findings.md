# Key Findings: ME/CFS Autopsy - CRH Depletion

## Main Findings
1. **CRH-producing neurons depleted:** Very low levels in ME/CFS hypothalamus
2. **HPA axis dysfunction:** Dramatic reduction suggests axis impairment
3. **Sample:** 7-10 ME/CFS brain autopsies
4. **Hypothalamic focus:** Study examined hypothalamus, not striatum
5. **Conference presentation:** Not yet peer-reviewed publication

## Clinical Implications
- Supports HPA axis dysfunction hypothesis in ME/CFS
- Suggests hypothalamic pathology may contribute to ME/CFS
- May explain abnormal cortisol patterns in ME/CFS
- Does not address striatal or eMSN involvement

## Limitations
- **Secondary source:** Health Rising blog report
- **Conference presentation:** Not peer-reviewed, limited details
- **Small sample:** n=7-10 autopsies
- **No primary publication:** Full methods not available
- **No striatal analysis:** Focused on hypothalamus only
