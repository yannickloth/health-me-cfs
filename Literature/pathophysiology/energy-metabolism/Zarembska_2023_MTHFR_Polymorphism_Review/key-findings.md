# Key Findings: Zarembska et al. 2023 - MTHFR Polymorphism Review

## Main Findings

1. **Quantified Enzyme Deficit:** C677T heterozygotes have 67% of normal MTHFR activity; TT homozygotes have only 25%. This is a substantial biochemical handicap, not a subtle polymorphism.
2. **Direct Homocysteine Link:** Reduced MTHFR activity directly impairs the remethylation of homocysteine to methionine via the 5-MTHF pathway.
3. **Mitochondrial DNA Methylation Affected:** One-carbon metabolism disruptions affect both nuclear and mitochondrial DNA methylation (mtDNA methylation has functional consequences on OXPHOS gene expression).
4. **SAM/SAH Ratio as Key Metric:** The SAM/SAH ratio is the functional index of methylation capacity; MTHFR dysfunction reduces this ratio by reducing methionine synthesis.
5. **Population-Level Prevalence:** C677T is one of the most common functional SNPs; estimated carrier prevalence is high in many populations, making it a potentially important contributor in any complex disease.

## Clinical Implications for ME/CFS

- Provides biochemical mechanistic foundation for why ~40-50% of ME/CFS patients may have elevated homocysteine (via MTHFR variants) even with adequate dietary folate
- mtDNA methylation effects provide a direct link from MTHFR deficiency to mitochondrial function impairment
- Supports MTHFR genotyping as a potentially useful clinical tool in ME/CFS (especially before B-vitamin supplementation)

## Limitations

- Not ME/CFS-specific; MTHFR prevalence in ME/CFS vs general population is not established
- Most disease associations reviewed are cardiovascular, not fatigue-related
- Narrative review (not systematic/meta-analytic); selection bias possible
