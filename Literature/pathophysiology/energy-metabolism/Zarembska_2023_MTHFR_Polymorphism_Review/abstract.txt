Title: The Implication of a Polymorphism in the Methylenetetrahydrofolate Reductase Gene in Homocysteine Metabolism and Related Civilisation Diseases
Authors: Emilia Zarembska, Klaudia Slusarczyk, Malgorzata Wrzosek
Journal: International Journal of Molecular Sciences
Year: 2023
Volume: 25(1):193
DOI: 10.3390/ijms25010193
PMID: 38203363

ABSTRACT:
Comprehensive narrative review of MTHFR C677T and A1298C polymorphisms and their implications for
homocysteine metabolism, with disease associations.

Key findings:
- MTHFR C677T (rs1801133): enzyme activity reduced to ~67% in heterozygotes, ~25% in homozygotes
- Impaired MTHFR reduces 5-methyltetrahydrofolate production, impairing homocysteine remethylation
- Elevated homocysteine increases ROS production and oxidative stress
- Associated with: cardiovascular disease, stroke, T2DM, insulin resistance, obesity, metabolic syndrome
- C677T is one of the most common functional polymorphisms in the human genome
- Population prevalence varies by ethnicity (higher in Hispanic/Mediterranean populations)
- 146+ references reviewed
