# MTHFR Polymorphism and Homocysteine Metabolism

**Authors:** Zarembska E, Slusarczyk K, Wrzosek M
**Journal:** International Journal of Molecular Sciences, 2023, 25(1):193
**DOI:** 10.3390/ijms25010193
**PMID:** 38203363

## Key Points

- C677T MTHFR: reduces enzyme activity to 67% (heterozygous) or 25% (homozygous TT)
- Reduced MTHFR -> less 5-methyltetrahydrofolate -> impaired homocysteine remethylation -> HHcy
- HHcy is an independent cardiovascular risk factor and increases oxidative stress
- One-carbon metabolism: SAM/SAH ratio as a methylation index; MTHFR influences methyl group flux
- One-carbon metabolism disruptions affect both nuclear and mitochondrial DNA methylation
- MTHFR C677T is one of the most common functional SNPs in the human genome

## Methodology

Narrative review; 146+ citations; covers multiple ethnic populations; discusses conflicting findings.
No primary data.

## Acronym Definitions

- MTHFR: Methylenetetrahydrofolate Reductase
- C677T: Cytosine-to-Thymine substitution at position 677
- 5-MTHF: 5-Methyltetrahydrofolate (active folate form)
- SAM: S-Adenosylmethionine (methyl donor)
- SAH: S-Adenosylhomocysteine (methylation product; precursor to homocysteine)
- SNP: Single Nucleotide Polymorphism
- HHcy: Hyperhomocysteinemia

## Certainty Assessment

- **Study Quality:** Medium-High (comprehensive review, peer-reviewed IJMS, broad evidence base)
- **Sample Size:** N/A (review; synthesizes population-level data)
- **Replication:** MTHFR C677T associations with HHcy are among the most replicated findings in
  human genetics; well-established in the literature
- **ME/CFS Specificity:** This paper does NOT study ME/CFS patients. The connection to ME/CFS is
  inferential via the homocysteine-elevation mechanism.
- **Conflicts of Interest:** Not reported; Polish academic institutions
- **Limitations for ME/CFS use:** No ME/CFS data; mechanistic bridge to ME/CFS requires explicit
  caveat that MTHFR prevalence in ME/CFS vs general population is not directly established
