# Homocysteine and Mitochondria in Cardiovascular and Cerebrovascular Systems

**Authors:** Kaplan P, Tatarkova Z, Kmetova Sivonova M, Racay P, Lehotsky J
**Journal:** International Journal of Molecular Sciences, 2020, 21(20):7698
**DOI:** 10.3390/ijms21207698
**PMID:** 33080955

## Key Points

- Hyperhomocysteinemia (HHcy) reduces activities of mitochondrial ETC complexes I, II, III, IV, V in both cardiac and neural tissue
- ROS generation occurs through two routes: direct auto-oxidation of homocysteine (in presence of transition metals) and indirect impairment of ETC function
- Homocysteine thiolactone causes N-homocysteinylation of proteins including cytochrome c, altering electron transport
- Brain-specific: complex I inhibition is persistent and selective in cortex; complexes IV and V decrease in hippocampus and cortex
- Mitochondrial dynamics disrupted: DRP1/Mfn2/Opa1 imbalance leads to fragmentation and increased mitophagy
- ER stress (PERK/CHOP axis) amplifies mitochondrial apoptosis pathway
- Antioxidant defense is depleted: mitochondrial SOD and catalase activity decreased

## Methodology

Comprehensive narrative review. No primary data collected. Synthesizes animal model (rat, mouse) and in vitro studies. No human clinical trial data. Focused on cardiovascular and cerebrovascular systems.

## Acronym Definitions

- HHcy: Hyperhomocysteinemia (elevated homocysteine in blood)
- ETC: Electron Transport Chain
- ROS: Reactive Oxygen Species
- SOD: Superoxide Dismutase
- GPx: Glutathione Peroxidase
- DRP1: Dynamin-Related Protein 1 (fission)
- Mfn2: Mitofusin 2 (fusion)
- Opa1: Optic Atrophy Protein 1 (fusion)
- PERK: Protein Kinase RNA-like ER kinase (ER stress sensor)
- CHOP: C/EBP Homologous Protein (pro-apoptotic)
- H2S: Hydrogen Sulfide
- Nrf2: Nuclear factor erythroid 2-related factor 2 (antioxidant transcription factor)

## Certainty Assessment

- **Study Quality:** Medium (review of animal/in vitro data; no human ME/CFS specific data)
