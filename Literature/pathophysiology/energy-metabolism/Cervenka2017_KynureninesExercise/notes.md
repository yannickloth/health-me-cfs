# Kynurenines: Tryptophan's metabolites in exercise, inflammation, and mental health

**Authors:** Cervenka I, Agudelo LZ, Ruas JL
**Journal:** Science. 2017 Jul 28;357(6349):eaaf9794
**DOI:** 10.1126/science.aaf9794
**PMID:** 28751584

## Key Points
- Kynurenine metabolites regulate host-microbiome signaling, immune cell response, and neuronal excitability
- Kynurenine pathway enzymes are expressed in different tissues and cell types, regulated by nutritional and inflammatory signals
- Peripheral inflammation contributes to kynurenine accumulation in the brain, associated with depression and schizophrenia
- Exercise activates kynurenine clearance in skeletal muscle, reducing brain accumulation
- Interorgan cross-talk mediated by kynurenine metabolites connects exercise to mental health benefits

## Methodology
- Review article synthesizing current understanding of kynurenine pathway
- Focus on peripheral mechanisms of tryptophan-kynurenine metabolism
- Integration of findings across inflammatory, metabolic, oncologic, and psychiatric disorders

## Acronym Definitions
- **IDO**: Indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenase (enzyme that converts tryptophan to kynurenine)
- **KYN**: Kynurenine (central metabolite in tryptophan catabolism)
- **KYNA**: Kynurenic acid (neuroprotective kynurenine metabolite)
- **QA**: Quinolinic acid (neurotoxic kynurenine metabolite, NMDA receptor agonist)
- **KAT**: Kynurenine aminotransferases (enzymes converting kynurenine to kynurenic acid)
- **KMO**: Kynurenine 3-monooxygenase (enzyme in neurotoxic branch of pathway)
- **NAD+**: Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide (cofactor derived from quinolinic acid)
