# Effects of Dendrobium officinale on chronic fatigue in rats: Modulation of tryptophan metabolism

**Authors:** Yan M, Shi M, Li C, Yu B, Zhou H, Su J, Yu J, Lv G, Chen S
**Journal:** J Ethnopharmacol. 2026 Jun 28;365:121527
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.jep.2026.121527
**PMID:** 41861923

## Key Points
- Chronic fatigue induced by complex poor lifestyles (stress, inadequate sleep, irregular diet, overwork)
- Dendrobium officinale water extract (DOW) improved exercise endurance and depression-like behavior
- DOW attenuated dysregulation of neuroendocrine-immune (NEI) system
- DOW modulated gut microbiota and increased tryptophan-derived indole metabolites
- DOW restored kynurenine pathway and enhanced production of downstream metabolites (3-HAA, QA, PA, XA)
- Correlation analysis linked TRP metabolic regulation to symptom relief

## Methodology
- Rat model of chronic fatigue via complex lifestyle stressors
- 4-week intervention with DOW at 0.1, 0.2, 0.4 g/kg doses
- Swimming test, elevated plus maze, open field tests for behavioral assessment
- 16S rRNA gene sequencing for gut microbiota
- LC-MS analysis for tryptophan metabolites
- Western blotting and qRT-PCR for enzyme expression

## Acronym Definitions
- **DOW**: Dendrobium officinale water extract
- **TRP**: Tryptophan (essential amino acid)
- **IDN**: Indole (gut microbiota metabolite)
- **IAA**: Indole-3-acetic acid (gut microbiota metabolite)
- **IPA**: Indole-3-propionic acid (gut microbiota metabolite)
- **IAld**: Indole-3-aldehyde (gut microbiota metabolite)
- **KYN**: Kynurenine (central TRP metabolite)
- **3-HAA**: 3-Hydroxyanthranilic acid (kynurenine metabolite)
- **QA**: Quinolinic acid (kynurenine metabolite, neurotoxic)
- **PA**: Picolinic acid (kynurenine metabolite)
- **XA**: Xanthurenic acid (kynurenine metabolite)
- **TDO2**: Tryptophan 2,3-dioxygenase 2 (kynurenine pathway enzyme)
- **KMO**: Kynurenine 3-monooxygenase (kynurenine pathway enzyme)
- **KAT1**: Kynurenine aminotransferase 1 (kynurenine pathway enzyme)
- **NEI**: Neuroendocrine-immune (system)
