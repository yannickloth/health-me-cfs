# Key Findings: CADM1-Mediated Mast Cell-Neuron Adhesion

## Main Findings

1. **CADM1 mediates physical adhesion between mast cells and sensory neurons:** Expression confirmed in both cell types, localized to contact sites in co-cultures

2. **Neuronal contact amplifies mast cell inflammatory responses:**
   - Degranulation enhanced ~2-fold
   - IL-6 secretion increased ~3-fold
   - TNFÎ± unchanged (selective pathway modulation)

3. **Adhesion is necessary but not sufficient:** Blocking CADM1 abolished enhancement, but CADM1+ non-neuronal cells didn't replicate the effect (neurons provide additional signals)

4. **Functional specificity:** Enhancement only occurred when mast cells were physically adhered to neurons, not in conditioned medium or transwell co-cultures

## Clinical Implications

- **Pain amplification mechanism:** Explains how mast cells at nerve endings can create localized inflammatory microenvironments
- **Small fiber neuropathy connection:** Chronic mast cell activation at nerve sites may contribute to nerve fiber loss
- **Therapeutic target:** CADM1 blocking could potentially interrupt pain amplification loops
- **Biomarker potential:** CADM1 expression levels in patient tissues could indicate susceptibility to neurogenic inflammation

## Limitations

- **In vitro only:** Mouse cells in culture - requires validation in human tissues and in vivo models
- **Acute stimulation:** Single antigen challenge - chronic repeated activation not examined
- **Mechanism incomplete:** Additional neuronal factors required for full enhancement not identified
- **Clinical translation unclear:** No direct evidence this mechanism operates in ME/CFS patients yet
