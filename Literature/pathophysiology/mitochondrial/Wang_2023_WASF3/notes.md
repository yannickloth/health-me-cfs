# WASF3 Disrupts Mitochondrial Respiration in ME/CFS

**Authors:** Wang P-Y, Ma J, Kim Y-C, and colleagues
**Journal:** Proceedings of the National Academy Sciences, 120(34):e2302738120, 2023
**DOI:** 10.1073/pnas.2302738120
**PMID:** 37579159

## Key Points

- **WASF3 protein disrupts mitochondrial respiratory supercomplex assembly**
- **ER stress induces WASF3 expression, creating ER stress → mitochondrial dysfunction pathway**
- **Elevated WASF3 observed in muscle biopsies from ME/CFS patients (n=14 vs n=10 controls)**
- **Knockdown of WASF3 restores respiratory capacity in patient cells**
- **Transgenic mice with elevated WASF3 show exercise intolerance and elevated lactate**

## Methodology

**Study Design:** Combined human muscle biopsy analysis, cell culture experiments, and transgenic mouse models

**Sample Size:**
- ME/CFS patients: n=14 muscle biopsies
- Healthy controls: n=10 muscle biopsies
- Transgenic mouse models for functional validation

**Methods:**
- Immunoblot analysis of muscle biopsies
- shRNA knockdown experiments in patient-derived cells
- Transgenic mouse treadmill exercise testing
- Blood lactate measurements
- Respiratory supercomplex assembly assays

## Results Summary

### Human Findings
1. **Elevated WASF3 in ME/CFS muscle:** Patient biopsies showed increased WASF3 protein levels
2. **ER stress activation:** Elevated PERK (ER stress marker) correlated with WASF3
3. **Reciprocal CIV relationship:** Complex IV subunits inversely related to WASF3/PERK
4. **shRNA rescue:** Knocking down WASF3 restored basal and spare respiratory capacity to control levels

### Mouse Model Validation
1. **Exercise intolerance:** WASF3 transgenic mice had markedly reduced treadmill running capacity
2. **Impaired supercomplexes:** Respiratory supercomplex assembly disrupted in transgenic mice
3. **Elevated lactate:** Higher blood lactate at rest, mirroring ME/CFS patient observations
4. **Lactate-PEM correlation:** Elevated lactate correlates with post-exertional malaise severity in patients

## Certainty Assessment

- **Study Quality:** HIGH (PNAS publication, rigorous methodology, multi-level validation)
- **Sample Size:** MODERATE (n=14 patients - small but adequate for mechanistic study)
- **Replication:** PENDING (published 2023, too recent for independent replication)
- **Validation:** STRONG (shRNA rescue experiments + transgenic mouse model validation)
- **Conflicts of Interest:** None apparent (NIH intramural research)

### Caveats
1. **Sample size:** 14 ME/CFS patients is small; subset-specificity unknown
2. **Replication needed:** Too recent for independent validation studies
3. **Therapeutic translation:** WASF3 inhibitors not yet developed for human use
4. **Subset question:** Unknown whether WASF3 elevation applies to all ME/CFS patients or specific subgroup
