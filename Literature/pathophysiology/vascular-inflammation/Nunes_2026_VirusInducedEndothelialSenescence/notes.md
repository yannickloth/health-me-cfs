# Virus-induced endothelial senescence as a cause and driving factor for ME/CFS and long COVID: mediated by a dysfunctional immune system

**Authors:** Nunes M, Kell L, Slaghekke A, Wüst RC, Fielding BC, Kell DB, Pretorius E
**Journal:** Cell Death & Disease, 2026 Jan 9;17(1):16
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41419-025-08162-2
**PMID:** 41513611
**PMCID:** PMC12789617

## Key Points
- **Core hypothesis:** Acute viral infection causes direct or indirect endothelial dysfunction and senescence
- **Key sites affected:** Blood-brain barrier, cerebral arteries, gastrointestinal tract, skeletal muscle
- **SASP characteristics:** Proinflammatory, pro-oxidative, procoagulant, vasoconstriction-prone, impaired tissue repair
- **Immune-endothelial bidirectional loop:** Immune abnormalities prevent clearance of senescent endothelial cells, perpetuating endothelial senescence
- **Explains multisystem involvement:** Through systemic and tissue-specific effects of endothelial senescence
- **Explains key symptoms:** Reduced cerebral blood flow/perfusion, PEM, GI disturbances, fatigue

## Methodology
- Type: Hypothesis paper / theoretical framework
- No experimental data presented
- Based on integration of existing literature on:
  - Endothelial dysfunction in ME/CFS
  - Endothelial dysfunction in Long COVID
  - Endothelial senescence mechanisms
  - Immune dysregulation in both conditions

## Acronym Definitions
- **ME/CFS:** Myalgic Encephalomyelitis / Chronic Fatigue Syndrome
- **SASP:** Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype
- **PEM:** Post-Exertional Malaise
- **BBB:** Blood-Brain Barrier

## Strengths
- Integrates multiple observations into coherent framework
- Explains multisystem involvement through common mechanism
- Accounts for chronicity (failure to clear senescent cells)
- Provides testable predictions
- Bridges ME/CFS and Long COVID research

## Limitations
- No primary experimental data
- Hypothetical - requires validation
- Does not specify biomarkers for clinical testing
- Does not propose specific interventions beyond recognition of the mechanism
- Immune abnormalities are described but not detailed

## Key Hypotheses for Testing
1. **Senescent endothelial cells accumulate** in ME/CFS and Long COVID patients
2. **SASP factors** are elevated in plasma of affected patients
3. **Immune dysfunction** (e.g., reduced clearance, altered signaling) prevents senescent cell removal
4. **Endothelial senescence precedes symptom onset** or correlates with severity
5. **Clearing senescent cells** (senolytics) should improve symptoms

## Clinical Implications
- **Diagnostic:** Potential biomarkers in SASP profile
- **Therapeutic:** Senolytic agents (drugs that clear senescent cells) may be effective
- **Monitoring:** Endothelial function tests could track disease progression/treatment response
- **Subtyping:** Different patterns of endothelial senescence may explain ME/CFS vs Long COVID vs comorbid presentations

## Cross-Disease Relevance
- Mechanism may apply to other post-viral syndromes
- Parallels to aging (inflammaging) where senescent cells accumulate
- Potential overlap with fibrotic conditions (endothelial-to-mesenchymal transition)
- May explain cardiovascular comorbidities in ME/CFS/Long COVID
