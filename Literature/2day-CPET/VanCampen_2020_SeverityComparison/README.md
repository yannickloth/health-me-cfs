# 2-Day CPET Severity Comparison (Van Campen et al. 2020)

**Citation:** van Campen, C.L.M.C., Rowe, P.C., & Visser, F.C. (2020). Two-Day Cardiopulmonary Exercise Testing in Females with a Severe Grade of Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome: Comparison with Patients with Mild and Moderate Disease. *Healthcare, 8*(3), 192.

**DOI:** 10.3390/healthcare8030192
**PMID:** 32629923
**PMCID:** PMC7551790
**BibTeX key:** `VanCampen2020SeverityCPET`

## Overview

Compares 2-day CPET results across ME/CFS severity levels (mild, moderate, severe), demonstrating that crash severity correlates with baseline disease severity.

## Key Contribution

**Severity-stratified dose-response evidence:**
- All severity groups showed significant Day 1â†’Day 2 declines (universal PEM)
- Severe ME/CFS: Largest decline (peak workload -19%)
- Moderate: Intermediate decline
- Mild: Smallest decline (but still significant)
- **Implication:** Same exertion produces different crash severity based on baseline state

## Evidence Grade: A

- Good sample size (n=82: 31 mild, 31 moderate, 20 severe)
- Objective measures
- Clear severity stratification
- Peer-reviewed publication
- Female-only cohort (limits generalizability to males)
