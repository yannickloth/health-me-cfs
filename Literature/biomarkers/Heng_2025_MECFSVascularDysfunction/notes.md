# Mapping complexity of ME/CFS: Evidence for abnormal energy metabolism, altered immune profile, and vascular dysfunction

**Authors:** Heng B, Gunasegaran B, Krishnamurthy S, Bustamante S, Pires AS, Chow S, Ahn SB, Paul-Heng M, Maciver Y, Smith K, Tran DP, Howley PP, Bilgin AA, Sharland A, Schloeffel R, Guillemin GJ
**Journal:** Cell Reports Medicine, 2025 Dec 16;6(12):102514
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.xcrm.2025.102514
**PMID:** 41406947
**PMCID:** PMC12765951

## Key Points
- **Study design:** Multimodal analysis (energy metabolism + immune profiling + plasma proteomics)
- **Population:** ME/CFS patients vs age- and sex-matched healthy controls
- **Three major dysfunctions identified:**
  1. Energy metabolism abnormalities (AMP/ADP elevation, reduced ATP/ADP)
  2. Immune cell subset skewing (less mature T/NK cells)
  3. Vascular dysfunction biomarkers (thrombus/vascular reactivity proteins elevated)
- **Predictive modeling:** Classification tree identifies ME/CFS with strong predictive variables

## Methodology
- **Immune cell metabolism:** Measured AMP, ADP, ATP ratios in immune cells
- **Flow cytometry:** Comprehensive T cell, NK cell, dendritic cell profiling
- **Plasma proteomics:** Identified proteins associated with thrombosis and vascular reactivity
- **Statistical analysis:** Classification and regression tree modeling
- **Sample size:** Not specified in abstract (need full paper for details)

## Acronym Definitions
- **ME/CFS:** Myalgic Encephalomyelitis / Chronic Fatigue Syndrome
- **AMP:** Adenosine Monophosphate
- **ADP:** Adenosine Diphosphate
- **ATP:** Adenosine Triphosphate
- **DC:** Dendritic Cell
- **NK:** Natural Killer cell
- **T cells:** T Lymphocytes (CD4+ helper, CD8+ cytotoxic, γδ gamma-delta)
- **CD1c+CD141-:** Conventional DC type 2 marker
- **CD56lowCD16+:** Terminal/dysfunctional NK cell subset

## Certainty Assessment
- **Study Quality:** High (peer-reviewed, multimodal design, proteomics)

- **Conflicts of Interest:** None declared

## Strengths
- **Multimodal approach:** Simultaneous measurement of metabolism, immunity, proteomics
- **Objective biomarkers:** Cellular energy measurements, plasma proteins
- **Vascular focus:** Specifically identifies thrombosis/vascular reactivity proteins
- **Predictive modeling:** Machine learning approach for diagnostic potential
- **Cross-system integration:** Links energy, immune, and vascular dysfunctions

## Limitations
- **Sample size unknown:** Abstract doesn't specify N (critical for certainty)
- **Specific biomarkers not named:** "Plasma proteins associated with thrombus formation and vascular reactivity" - which proteins?
- **Mechanism unclear:** Why do immune cells have energy deficit? Cause vs effect?
- **Causal direction:** Do vascular proteins cause symptoms or correlate with them?
- **Subtype analysis:** Does not address ME/CFS heterogeneity (different onset patterns, severity levels)

## Key Findings by System

### Energy Metabolism
- **Elevated AMP:** Indicates energy crisis at cellular level
- **Elevated ADP:** Suggests impaired ATP regeneration
- **Reduced ATP/ADP ratio:** Direct evidence of energy production deficit
- **Cellular energy stress:** Immune cells energy-starved
- **Alignment with:** Mitochondrial dysfunction hypothesis, PEM energy crash

### Immune Profile
- **CD4+ T cells:** Skewed toward less mature effector subsets
- **CD8+ T cells:** Skewed toward less mature effector subsets
- **γδ T cells:** Skewed toward less mature effector subsets
- **Dendritic cells:** Reduced CD1c+CD141- conventional DC type 2
- **NK cells:** Reduced CD56lowCD16+ terminal NK cells
- **Interpretation:** Immune exhaustion or immaturity, impaired pathogen response

### Vascular Dysfunction
- **Plasma proteins elevated:** Associated with thrombus formation (but NOT coagulopathy)
- **Plasma proteins elevated:** Associated with vascular reactivity
- **Link to endothelial dysfunction:** Explicitly stated in abstract
- **Alignment with:** Nunes 2026 hypothesis (endothelial dysfunction), McAlpine 2026 (vascular biomarkers)

### Distinguishing: Thrombosis vs Coagulopathy
- **This paper reports thrombosis-associated proteins** - likely fibrinogen, vWF, etc.
- **Does NOT report coagulopathy** - i.e., D-dimer elevation, overt clotting disorder
- **Critical distinction:** Same as Nunes 2026 and McAlpine 2026
- **Interpretation:** Hypercoagulable state without overt clotting (endothelial activation)

## Clinical Implications

### Diagnostic Potential
- **Biomarker panel:** Energy (AMP/ADP/ATP) + immune subsets + vascular proteins
- **Classification model:** Machine learning predicts ME/CFS from biomarker profile
- **Objective tests:** Move beyond symptom-based diagnosis

### Therapeutic Implications
- **Energy metabolism:** Support ATP production (CoQ10, D-ribose, carnitine?)
- **Immune maturation:** Boost effector function (immunomodulators?)
- **Vascular function:** Endothelial protectors, anti-thrombotic (but not anticoagulants)
- **Avoid:** Simple anticoagulation (not coagulopathy)

## Comparison to Other Studies

### vs Nunes 2026
- **Complementary:** Nunes provides theoretical framework; Heng provides empirical data
- **Consistent:** Both identify vascular/endothelial involvement
- **Consistent:** Both distinguish vascular dysfunction from coagulopathy
- **Adds:** Specific energy metabolism data (Nunes didn't measure)

### vs McAlpine 2026
- **Parallel findings:** Both identify vascular biomarkers (though Heng doesn't name specific proteins)
- **Cross-disease:** McAlpine in Long COVID; Heng in ME/CFS - both find vascular involvement
- **Cognitive correlation:** McAlpine correlates vascular biomarkers with cognition; Heng doesn't measure cognition
- **Next step:** Test whether Heng's vascular proteins correlate with "brain fog" (McAlpine pattern)

### vs Other ME/CFS Studies
- **Energy metabolism:** Confirms other studies showing mitochondrial/energy deficits
- **Immune profiling:** Adds detail on T/NK subset skewing (most studies just count cells)
- **Vascular:** Few ME/CFS studies measure vascular proteins directly - this is novel
- **Multimodal:** Rare to measure all three systems in same cohort - major strength

## Gaps and Future Directions

1. **Specify vascular proteins:** Which proteins exactly? (fibrinogen, vWF, sP-selectin, sICAM-1, ADAMTS13?)
2. **Correlate with symptoms:** Do vascular proteins correlate with fatigue severity, PEM, cognitive symptoms?
3. **Cognitive testing:** Add neurocognitive battery to test McAlpine-like correlations
4. **Longitudinal tracking:** Do biomarkers change with exercise, relapse, treatment?
5. **Subtype analysis:** Do biomarker profiles differ by:
   - Onset type (sudden vs gradual)
   - Severity (mild, moderate, severe, bedbound)
   - Duration (early vs long-standing)
   - Post-infectious vs non-infectious onset?
