# Key Findings: DecodeME ME/CFS GWAS

## Main Findings
1. **Largest ME/CFS GWAS:** 15,579 cases vs 259,909 controls
2. **8 genome-wide significant loci:** Identified for ME/CFS
3. **Brain tissue enrichment:** All 13 brain tissues examined showed significant gene enrichment
4. **Immune and neurological genes:** BTN2A2, OLFM4, RABGAP1L (immune); CA10, ARFGEF2 (neurological)
5. **No eMSN-specific findings:** Despite brain enrichment, eMSN not identified
6. **Limited replication:** Only 4 loci replicated in external biobanks

## Clinical Implications
- Establishes ME/CFS as having genetic component
- Suggests both immunological and neurological involvement
- Brain tissue enrichment warrants investigation of basal ganglia and striatum
- Does not support eMSN hypothesis (no eMSN genes identified)

## Limitations
- Preprint (not peer-reviewed)
- Limited to European ancestry
- Case definition challenges
- No cell-type specific analysis (eMSN not examined)
- Limited replication success

- This does not refute eMSN hypothesis (analysis was tissue-level, not cell-type specific)
- **Cell-type specific analysis required** to test eMSN hypothesis properly
