# Initial findings from the DecodeME genome-wide association study of ME/CFS

**Authors:** DecodeME Genetics Consortium, Andrew D. Bretherick, Thibaud Boutin, Joshua J. Dibble, et al.
**Journal:** medRxiv preprint, 2025
**DOI:** 10.1101/2025.08.06.25333109

## Key Points
- Largest ME/CFS GWAS to date: 15,579 cases vs 259,909 controls
- Identified 8 genome-wide significant loci associated with ME/CFS
- **MAGMA analysis showed significant enrichment of ME/CFS-related genes in all 13 brain tissues** examined
- Genes implicated in:
  - Immune function: BTN2A2, OLFM4, RABGAP1L
  - Neurological processes: CA10, ARFGEF2
- **No eMSN-specific findings reported** in initial results
- Four loci replicated in external biobank analyses

## Methodology
- GWAS using REGENIE with European genetic ancestry
- Stringent case criteria (Canadian Consensus + IOM criteria, requires post-exertional malaise)
- MAGMA gene-tissue analysis
- Colocalization analysis with depression, anxiety, and chronic pain
- Replication attempts in external biobanks

## Acronym Definitions
- **GWAS:** Genome-Wide Association Study
- **MAGMA:** Multi-marker Analysis of GenoMic Annotation
- **REGENIE:** REgression-based GENetic association tool
- **IOM:** Institute of Medicine (now National Academy of Medicine)
- **BTNA2A2:** Butyrophilin Subfamily 2 Member A2 (immune checkpoint)
- **OLFMA4:** Olfactomedin 4 (immune regulator)
- **RABGAP1L:** RAB GTPase Activating Protein 1 Like
- **CA10:** Carbonic Anhydrase 10 (neurological)
- **ARFGEF2:** ADP Ribosylation Factor Guanine Nucleotide Exchange Factor 2

## Certainty Assessment
- **Study Quality:** High (large sample, rigorous methods, preprint)
- **Sample Size:** n=15,579 cases, n=259,909 controls
- **Replication:** Partial (some loci replicated in males, external replication limited)
- **Conflicts of Interest:** Not specified
- **Peer Review:** Preprint (not peer-reviewed)

## Relevance to ME/CFS
- **Primary relevance:** First large-scale genetic evidence for ME/CFS
- **Secondary relevance:** Brain tissue enrichment suggests neurological involvement
- **No eMSN link:** Does not identify eMSN-specific genetic associations
- **Future direction:** Cell-type specific analyses needed (eMSN not examined)

## Critical Note
- **No direct eMSN findings:** Despite brain tissue enrichment, eMSN-specific genes were not identified
- **MAGMA analysis:** Examined broad tissue categories, not specific cell types like eMSN
- **Cell-type specific analysis:** Required to test eMSN hypothesis (not performed)
