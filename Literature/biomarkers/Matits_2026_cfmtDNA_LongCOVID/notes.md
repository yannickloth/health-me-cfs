# Matits et al. 2026 — Circulating Mitochondrial and Cellular Damage Markers in Long COVID

**Authors:** Lynn Matits, [full author list with EPILOC Phase 2 Study Group]
**Journal:** Molecular Psychiatry (Nature portfolio)
**Year:** 2026 (online ahead of print: 7 February 2026)
**Volume/Pages:** Online first
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41380-026-03471-0
**PMID:** 41654652
**URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41380-026-03471-0

## Key Points

- Long COVID patients (PASC, n=128) had LOWER circulating cell-free mitochondrial DNA than recovered controls (n=100)
- cf-mtDNA reduction lost significance after covariate adjustment (p=0.089) but remained significant when excluding high-CRP outliers (p=0.038, eta²=0.02; very small effect size)
- General cognition correlated positively with cf-mtDNA: less cf-mtDNA associated with worse cognition
- CRP correlated negatively with cf-mtDNA: higher inflammation associated with less cf-mtDNA
- No increase in total cell-free DNA: the reduction is mitochondria-specific, not a general cell death signal
- 25% of PASC patients had low-grade inflammation (CRP elevation)
- Authors interpret reduced cf-mtDNA as altered mitochondrial quality control / impaired mitophagy, NOT as reduced cellular damage

## Methodology

- **Design:** Nested case-control study (part of EPILOC population-based cohort)
- **Location:** Baden-Württemberg, Germany
- **Sample:** 228 adults total (PASC n=128; recovered controls n=100)
- **PASC definition:** General health/work capacity ≤80% of pre-COVID levels plus ≥1 moderate-to-severe symptom from 30-item list
- **Key measurements:**
  - Relative ccf-mtDNA: quantitative real-time PCR (-ΔCT method)
  - Total ccf-DNA: UV-Vis spectroscopy
  - CRP and systemic immune-inflammation index (SII)
  - Cognitive assessment: PCA of neuropsychological tests yielding 3 components (general cognition, verbal memory, psychological distress)
- **Covariates adjusted:** Age, sex, education, smoking, BMI, psychiatric medication

## Acronym Definitions

WARNING: Critical acronym disambiguation required.
- PASC: Post-Acute Sequelae of SARS-CoV-2 infection (= Long COVID)
- ccf-mtDNA: Circulating cell-free mitochondrial DNA
- EPILOC: Epidemiology of Long COVID (population-based cohort, Baden-Württemberg)
- SII: Systemic Immune-Inflammation Index
- CRP: C-reactive protein
- PCA: Principal Component Analysis

## Statistical Details

- After full covariate adjustment: F(1,192)=2.92; p=0.089; partial η²=0.01 (marginal/non-significant)
- Excluding high-CRP participants (>10 mg/L): F(1,181)=4.36; p=0.038; partial η²=0.02 (just significant; very small effect)
- Note: Partial η²=0.01-0.02 represents a very small effect size regardless of p-value

## Certainty Assessment

- **Study Quality:** Medium-High (population-based cohort, large sample for this literature, peer-reviewed high-impact journal)
- **Sample Size:** n=228 (PASC n=128, controls n=100)
- **Effect Size:** Very small (partial η²=0.01-0.02)
- **Replication:** This is only the second study of cf-mtDNA in PASC (first: Szögi et al. 2024, n=5 — much smaller)
- **Conflicts of Interest:** None declared
- **Key Methodological Concerns (from S4ME peer review):**
  1. Possible overcorrection (too many covariates for sample size)
  2. No p-value correction for multiple comparisons (Figure 2)
  3. PASC definition heterogeneity (diverse symptom profiles lumped together)
  4. Physical function assessments excluded without explanation (important given activity levels differ)
  5. Reduced cf-mtDNA may reflect reduced physical activity vs controls, not primary pathology

## Context: cf-mtDNA in Other Conditions

**Acute SARS-CoV-2:** Elevated cf-mtDNA predicts mortality and ICU admission — marker of massive cell death/damage
**Long COVID (this paper + Szögi 2024):** REDUCED cf-mtDNA — opposite pattern, different mechanism
**Anxiety disorders:** Reduced cf-mtDNA previously reported (similar to Long COVID finding)
**Exercise response:** cf-mtDNA increases physiologically with exercise; reduced cf-mtDNA could reflect deconditioning/reduced activity
**ME/CFS (Tsilioni 2022):** Exosome-associated mtDNA ELEVATED after exercise (at rest: no difference vs controls) — may be a different compartment/mechanism
**ME/CFS (Myhill 2012):** Total cell-free DNA elevated up to 3.5x normal reference range — but this is total cfDNA, not specifically mitochondrial
