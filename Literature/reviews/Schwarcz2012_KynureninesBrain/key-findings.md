# Key Findings: Kynurenines in Brain Physiology and Pathology

## Main Findings
1. **Dual Metabolite Effects:** Kynurenine pathway produces both neuroprotective (KYNA) and neurotoxic (QA, 3-HK) metabolites
2. **Immune Regulation:** Pathway tightly controlled by immune system via IDO activation
3. **Neurodegeneration Link:** Dysregulation associated with neurodegenerative disorders
4. **Psychiatric Link:** Pathway dysregulation contributes to depression and schizophrenia
5. **Therapeutic Potential:** Pharmacological agents can restore metabolic equilibrium

## Clinical Implications
- Kynurenine pathway represents targetable mechanism in neuropsychiatric disorders
- Immune activation can shift balance toward neurotoxic metabolites (QA, 3-HK)
- Restoring KYNA/QA ratio may protect neurons from excitotoxicity

## Limitations
- Review article - no new primary data
- Clinical translation of kynurenine modulators still experimental
- Complexity of pathway makes precise therapeutic targeting challenging
