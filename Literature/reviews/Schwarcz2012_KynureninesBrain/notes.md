# Kynurenines in the mammalian brain: when physiology meets pathology

**Authors:** Schwarcz R, Bruno JP, Muchowski PJ, Wu HQ
**Journal:** Nat Rev Neurosci. 2012 Jul;13(7):465-77
**DOI:** 10.1038/nrn3257
**PMID:** 22678511

## Key Points
- Tryptophan is degraded to neuroactive compounds via kynurenine pathway (KYNA, 3-HK, QA)
- Kynurenine pathway is tightly controlled by immune system
- Dysregulation causes hyper- or hypofunction of active metabolites
- Associated with neurodegenerative disorders, psychiatric diseases (depression, schizophrenia)
- Pharmacological agents can restore metabolic equilibrium for therapeutic intervention

## Methodology
- Comprehensive review of kynurenine pathway in brain physiology and pathology
- Integration of findings across neurodegeneration and psychiatric disorders
- Discussion of therapeutic targeting strategies

## Acronym Definitions
- **KYNA**: Kynurenic acid (neuroprotective, NMDA receptor antagonist)
- **3-HK**: 3-hydroxykynurenine (pro-oxidant, generates reactive oxygen species)
- **QA**: Quinolinic acid (neurotoxic, NMDA receptor agonist)
- **NMDA**: N-methyl-D-aspartate (glutamate receptor subtype)
- **KAT**: Kynurenine aminotransferases (convert kynurenine to KYNA)
- **KMO**: Kynurenine 3-monooxygenase (converts kynurenine to 3-HK)
- **IDOs**: Indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenases (rate-limiting enzymes activated by inflammation)
- **TDO**: Tryptophan 2,3-dioxygenase (hepatic enzyme, constitutive)
