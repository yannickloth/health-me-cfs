# ANS Dysregulation and Vagal Stimulation in ME/CFS

date: 2026-04-17

## Pattern

Chronic sympathetic dominance + vagal withdrawal — the typical autonomic imbalance in ME/CFS.

## What vagal stimulation addresses

### Direct autonomic effects
- Increases parasympathetic tone → counteracts sympathetic dominance
- Improves HRV (direct readout of vagal tone)
- Reduces resting HR instability and blood pressure fluctuations
- Dampens baroreceptor hypersensitivity driving orthostatic symptoms

### Immune-ANS axis (ME/CFS-specific)
- Vagus is the main anti-inflammatory pathway (cholinergic anti-inflammatory reflex)
- Vagal stimulation → spleen releases acetylcholine → suppresses TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6
- Chronic neuroinflammation in ME/CFS partly sustained by vagal withdrawal
- **Relevance to current protocol:** pyridostigmine (already taken) inhibits acetylcholinesterase → prolongs acetylcholine at vagal terminals — same pathway, upstream entry point
- Breathing-based vagal stimulation + pyridostigmine = synergistic on this axis

### HPA axis
- Vagal afferents modulate cortisol rhythm via NTS → hypothalamus
- Disrupted vagal tone → dysregulated cortisol → worsens the HPA flattening seen in ME/CFS
- Consistent stimulation helps re-entrain cortisol rhythm over weeks

### Cerebral blood flow
- Vagal tone influences cerebrovascular autoregulation
- Better vagal tone → improved autoregulation → less orthostatic cerebral hypoperfusion
- Downstream: less brain fog, less tinnitus (see tinnitus-fatigue-correlation.yaml)

## Limitations

- Vagal stimulation is modulatory, not curative — does not address upstream causes (mitochondrial dysfunction, TRPM3 channelopathy, immune activation)
- Effects require consistency and accumulate over weeks
- Severe ANS dysregulation may involve structural vagal impairment (small fiber neuropathy) — limits response

## Connection to tinnitus pattern

Tinnitus worsening with fatigue/poor sleep is partly mediated by ANS dysregulation:
- Poor SWS → elevated sympathetic tone → vasoconstriction → reduced auditory pathway perfusion
- Vagal stimulation addresses this via cerebrovascular autoregulation improvement
- Tinnitus intensity may serve as a proxy readout of current vagal tone

## Practical protocol

Best options for ME/CFS (usable lying down, no exertion):
1. Slow diaphragmatic breathing — 4–6 breaths/min, exhale longer than inhale
2. Humming / sustained "mmm" — laryngeal vagal branch stimulation
3. Cold water on face — strong diving reflex vagal activation
4. Gargling — pharyngeal vagal branch, 30–60s
