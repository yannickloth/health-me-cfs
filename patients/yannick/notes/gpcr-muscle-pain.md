# GPCR Pathways and Muscle Pain in ME/CFS

date: 2026-05-01

## What GPCRs are

G-protein-coupled receptors — largest family of cell surface receptors (~800 in humans).
7 transmembrane helices; ligand binding → G-protein activation → second messengers (cAMP, IP3, DAG, Ca²⁺) → intracellular cascade.

## Mestinon → GPCR relationship

Mestinon does **not** bind GPCRs directly.

```
Mestinon inhibits acetylcholinesterase
        ↓
↑ synaptic ACh
        ↓
ACh binds muscarinic receptors (M1–M5) — these ARE GPCRs
ACh binds nicotinic receptors (nAChR)  — these are NOT GPCRs (ligand-gated ion channels)
```

Autonomic benefit in dysautonomia/POTS mediated partly via:
- M2 mAChR (GPCR) → vagal slowing of heart
- M3 mAChR (GPCR) → vasomotor tone

## Key GPCRs implicated in muscle pain

| Receptor | Ligand | Role |
|----------|--------|------|
| P2Y (purinergic) | ATP from damaged cells | sensitizes muscle nociceptors |
| EP1/EP2/EP4 (prostaglandin) | PGE2 | inflammatory muscle pain |
| B2 bradykinin | bradykinin | direct nociceptor activation |
| A1/A2 adenosine | adenosine (ATP breakdown) | modulates pain sensitivity |
| β2-adrenergic | epinephrine/NE | vasomotor tone in muscle |
| M2/M3 muscarinic | ACh | autonomic-mediated perfusion |
| mGluR5 (metabotropic glutamate) | glutamate | central sensitization / wind-up |
| μ/δ/κ opioid | endorphins | pain inhibition |

## Most relevant to this case

### 1. Purinergic / adenosine axis
- Exertion → ATP release from muscle cells → P2Y/P2X activation → nociceptor sensitization
- ATP degrades → adenosine → A1/A2 GPCRs → modulates pain threshold
- ME/CFS: possibly prolonged receptor sensitization after exertion (Naviaux metabolomics)
- Relevant to PEM-associated muscle pain and spontaneous cramps

### 2. β2-adrenergic autoantibodies (Scheibenbogen hypothesis)
- IgG autoantibodies dysregulate β2-adrenergic and M3/M4 muscarinic GPCRs
- Disturbed sympathetic tone in muscle vasculature → inadequate perfusion → ischemia-like pain
- Mestinon partially compensates: ↑ACh → M3 → vasodilation

### 3. Central sensitization via mGluR5
- mGluR5 (GPCR) in dorsal horn → wind-up → widespread muscle hyperalgesia
- Mechanism behind fibromyalgia overlap; relevant to diffuse periarticular pain pattern

## Connection to spontaneous cramps

Spontaneous cramps (not effort-triggered) point toward:
- Neuronal hyperexcitability at motor neuron level
- Possibly ion channel dysregulation (TRPM3 channelopathy hypothesis)
- GPCR-mediated amplification: purinergic and adenosine receptors could lower cramp threshold
  even at rest if baseline sensitization is elevated
- Magnesium deficiency lowers cramp threshold via NMDA receptor (not GPCR) but intersects
  with same nociceptor pathways
