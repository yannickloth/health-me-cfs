# Phase 3: Elimination-Rechallenge — Detailed Daily Protocol

## General Rules (apply to ALL cycles)

1. **One category at a time.** Never overlap eliminations.
2. **2 rest days between cycles.** Eat normally. This clears carryover effects.
3. **Replace, don't restrict.** Every food removed must be replaced with something
   filling. Under-eating causes fatigue that looks like a food reaction.
4. **Continue logging food + symptoms** every day (same as Phase 1).
5. **Snacks count.** A single handful of cashews during a nut-free cycle invalidates
   the data for that day.
6. **If you crash from a non-food cause** (overexertion, terrible sleep, acute stress),
   note it. That day may be excluded from analysis.
7. **If you feel dramatically better during elimination,** don't extend it beyond 7
   days — proceed to rechallenge. The rechallenge is what confirms the finding.
8. **If you feel dramatically worse during rechallenge,** you may stop after day 8 if
   symptoms are clear. No need to push through 3 days of misery.

---

## Symptom Scoring (use during Phase 3)

Rate each symptom 0-3 daily. This standardized scoring makes comparison easier.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0 | Absent |
| 1 | Mild — noticeable but doesn't limit activity |
| 2 | Moderate — limits some activity |
| 3 | Severe — significantly disabling |

### Symptoms to score daily during Phase 3

```yaml
phase3_scoring:
  fatigue: 0-3
  brain_fog: 0-3
  joint_pain: 0-3
  muscle_pain: 0-3
  headache: 0-3
  heart_rate_issues: 0-3  # racing, palpitations, orthostatic
  gut_symptoms: 0-3       # bloating, pain, diarrhea, nausea
  skin_issues: 0-3        # flushing, itching, rashes
  sleep_quality: 0-3      # 0=good, 3=terrible
  overall_functioning: 0-3
  # Total: sum of all scores (0-30)
```

### Interpretation guide
- **Elimination success:** Average total score days 4-7 is notably lower than your Phase 1 baseline average
- **Rechallenge positive:** Total score days 8-10 rises back toward or above baseline
- **No effect:** Scores stay roughly the same throughout

## Cycle A: High-Histamine Foods (default: Apr 21 - Apr 30)

This is tested first because it's the most common MCAS food trigger.

### Day 0 (Apr 20): Preparation
- Clear the kitchen of easy-to-grab high-histamine snacks
- Cook fresh meat/chicken for the next day
- Buy fresh cheese (mozzarella, ricotta, chèvre frais)
- Make sure you have rice, potatoes, fresh vegetables
- Tell your wife: "For the next 7 days, no aged cheese, cured meat, tomato, soy sauce, or vinegar in cooking"

### Days 1-7 (Apr 21-27): Elimination

#### What to remove
- Aged/hard cheese: comté, parmesan, gruyère, cheddar, gouda, emmental
- Cured/smoked meat: saucisson, jambon cru, chorizo, lardons, smoked salmon, bacon
- Canned fish: tuna, sardines, anchovies, mackerel
- Fermented foods: sauerkraut, kimchi, soy sauce, miso, vinegar, pickles, tempeh, kombucha
- Wine (red and white), beer
- Tomato: raw, sauce, concentrate, ketchup, sun-dried
- Avocado
- Spinach (cooked), eggplant
- Black tea
- Leftover cooked food older than 12 hours

#### Sample meal plans

**Day 1 — Monday**
- Breakfast: fresh bread + butter + chèvre frais, coffee with milk
- Lunch: rice + fresh grilled chicken breast + steamed broccoli + olive oil
- Snack: apple, herbal tea
- Dinner: fresh pan-fried fish fillet + mashed potatoes (butter, milk) + green beans

**Day 2 — Tuesday**
- Breakfast: oatmeal with milk + blueberries + honey
- Lunch: fresh mozzarella + basil + olive oil (no vinegar/tomato!) on bread
- Snack: pear, rice cake with cream cheese
- Dinner: lamb chops + roasted carrots + zucchini + rice

**Day 3 — Wednesday**
- Breakfast: scrambled eggs + fresh bread + butter, coffee
- Lunch: potato leek soup (fresh cream, no stock cube — many contain yeast extract)
- Snack: melon slices
- Dinner: fresh beef steak + sweet potato + steamed cauliflower

**Day 4 — Thursday**
- Breakfast: fresh bread + ricotta + honey, coffee with milk
- Lunch: buckwheat noodles + fresh chicken stir-fry (no soy sauce! use salt only) + bok choy
- Snack: blueberries, handful of pumpkin seeds
- Dinner: roasted whole chicken (fresh) + roasted fennel + potatoes

**Day 5 — Friday**
- Breakfast: omelette (2-3 eggs) + fresh bread, coffee
- Lunch: fresh white fish (cod, sole) + rice + steamed green beans + lemon
- Snack: rice cake + butter, herbal tea
- Dinner: fresh turkey escalope + pasta (no tomato — try olive oil + garlic + cooked zucchini)

**Day 6 — Saturday**
- Breakfast: pancakes (flour, eggs, milk) + maple syrup or honey, coffee
- Lunch: roasted vegetables (carrot, zucchini, fennel, sweet potato) + fresh goat cheese + olive oil
- Snack: mango slices
- Dinner: fresh ground beef + mashed potatoes + steamed broccoli

**Day 7 — Sunday**
- Breakfast: bread + butter + honey, coffee with milk
- Lunch: roast chicken (fresh) + rice + cooked leeks + cream sauce
- Snack: apple + rice cake
- Dinner: soup (fresh vegetables, potato, leek, carrot — no stock cube)

#### Key reminders
- **Cook fresh and eat the same day.** This is the most important rule for this cycle.
- If you batch cook, freeze portions immediately and thaw/reheat the same day you eat.
- No stock cubes (most contain yeast extract, which is high-histamine). Use homemade stock or just salt.
- Lemon juice is OK as a substitute for vinegar in dressings.
- Read labels: many sauces contain tomato, vinegar, or soy sauce.

### Days 8-10 (Apr 28-30): Rechallenge

**Day 8 — Monday**
- Breakfast: bread + aged comté + coffee
- Lunch: pasta with tomato sauce + parmesan, glass of red wine (optional)
- Snack: slice of saucisson
- Dinner: charcuterie plate: jambon cru, saucisson, cornichons, comté

**Day 9 — Tuesday**
- Breakfast: bread + smoked salmon + cream cheese
- Lunch: canned tuna salad with tomato, avocado, vinaigrette
- Snack: aged cheese + crackers
- Dinner: eggplant parmesan (eggplant, tomato sauce, parmesan)

**Day 10 — Wednesday**
- Breakfast: normal breakfast
- Lunch: leftover from yesterday (intentionally aged >12h)
- Rest of day: observe and log symptoms
- **Important:** Log symptoms until bedtime. Some reactions appear 6-12 hours after.

### What to look for
- **During elimination (days 4-7):** Do you feel any different? Less foggy? Less joint pain? Better energy? Even subtle improvements count.
- **During rechallenge (days 8-10):** Do symptoms return? Brain fog, fatigue, pain, GI discomfort, flushing?
- **Delayed reaction:** Check the morning after rechallenge day 10 — some reactions peak at 12-24 hours.

## Cycle B: Dairy (default: May 1 - May 10)

### Day 0 (Apr 30): Preparation
- Buy oat milk or rice milk
- Check bread labels (most baguettes are dairy-free)
- Get olive oil for cooking (replaces butter)
- Identify which of your usual meals contain dairy and plan alternatives

### Days 1-7 (May 1-7): Elimination

#### What to remove
- All milk: cow, goat, sheep
- All cheese (including fresh)
- Yogurt, kefir, fromage blanc
- Cream, crème fraîche, butter
- Ice cream
- Milk powder, whey, casein (check processed food labels)

#### Sample meal plans

**Day 1**
- Breakfast: bread + olive oil drizzle + honey, coffee with oat milk
- Lunch: rice + grilled chicken + roasted vegetables + olive oil
- Snack: apple, dark chocolate (check: no milk powder)
- Dinner: pasta with pesto (dairy-free: basil, olive oil, pine nuts, garlic, no parmesan)

**Day 2**
- Breakfast: oatmeal with oat milk + banana + cinnamon
- Lunch: potato + fresh fish + green beans + olive oil + lemon
- Snack: rice cake + almond butter
- Dinner: stir-fry: chicken + vegetables + rice + soy sauce

**Day 3**
- Breakfast: eggs scrambled in olive oil + toast (dairy-free bread)
- Lunch: lentil soup (onion, carrot, celery, lentils, olive oil)
- Snack: fruit salad
- Dinner: lamb chops + couscous + roasted zucchini

**Days 4-7:** Continue similar patterns. The main adjustment is cooking with olive oil instead of butter and using oat milk in coffee.

### Days 8-10 (May 8-10): Rechallenge

**Day 8**
- Breakfast: café au lait with whole cow's milk, bread with butter and cheese
- Lunch: croque-monsieur (bread, ham, béchamel, gruyère)

**Day 9**
- Breakfast: bread + butter, glass of milk

**Day 10**
- Breakfast: normal

## After All Cycles: Summary Assessment

Create a summary document with:

1. **Baseline average symptom score** (from Phase 1 data)
2. **Each cycle's elimination average** (days 4-7)
3. **Each cycle's rechallenge average** (days 8-10)
4. **Confirmed triggers** (clear improvement + clear worsening on rechallenge)
5. **Cleared categories** (no change)
6. **Ambiguous results** (consider retesting)

This becomes the evidence for Phase 4 (medical testing) and for long-term dietary management.
