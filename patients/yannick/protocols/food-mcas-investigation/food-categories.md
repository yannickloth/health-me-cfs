# Food Categories — Elimination & Rechallenge Reference

Quick reference for each elimination cycle: what to remove and what to eat instead.

---

## Category A: High-Histamine Foods

### ELIMINATE (days 1-7)
- Aged/hard cheese: comté, parmesan, gruyère, cheddar, gouda, emmental
- Cured/smoked meat: saucisson, jambon cru, chorizo, lardons, smoked salmon, bacon
- Canned fish: tuna, sardines, anchovies, mackerel
- Fermented foods: sauerkraut, kimchi, soy sauce, miso, vinegar, pickles, tempeh
- Wine (red and white), beer
- Tomato: raw, sauce, concentrate, ketchup, sun-dried
- Avocado
- Spinach, eggplant
- Black tea
- Leftover cooked food older than 12 hours

### EAT INSTEAD
- Fresh meat/poultry, cooked and eaten same day
- Fresh fish, cooked and eaten same day
- Fresh/young cheese: mozzarella, ricotta, chèvre frais, cream cheese, cottage cheese
- Butter, cream, milk
- Rice, potatoes, sweet potatoes, pasta (without tomato sauce)
- Cooked vegetables: carrots, zucchini, green beans, broccoli, cauliflower, leeks, fennel, celery
- Fresh bread (plain)
- Fruits: apples, pears, blueberries, melon, mango, peach
- Eggs (fully cooked)
- Coffee, herbal tea (mint, chamomile, rooibos)
- Olive oil, sunflower oil for cooking
- Pesto (basil + olive oil, no vinegar)

### RECHALLENGE (days 8-10)
Eat aged cheese + cured meat + tomato sauce at meals. These are the strongest histamine sources.

**Meal ideas for rechallenge:**
- Breakfast: bread with aged cheese
- Lunch: pasta with tomato sauce and parmesan
- Dinner: charcuterie plate with comté

## Category B: Dairy

### ELIMINATE (days 1-7)
- All milk: cow, goat, sheep
- All cheese (including fresh cheese)
- Yogurt, kefir, fromage blanc
- Cream, crème fraîche
- Butter (or use clarified butter/ghee if tolerated)
- Ice cream
- Foods containing milk powder, whey, casein, lactose

### EAT INSTEAD
- Olive oil or vegetable oil for cooking (replaces butter)
- Oat milk or rice milk for coffee (avoid soy — fermented)
- Bread without dairy (most baguettes, many sourdoughs)
- Normal meals, just skip the cheese/cream component
- Coconut cream for sauces if needed
- Check packaged food labels for hidden dairy

### RECHALLENGE (days 8-10)
- Breakfast: café au lait with cow's milk, bread with butter
- Lunch: gratin or béchamel, cheese
- Snack: yogurt
- Dinner: cheese course

## Category C: Gluten/Wheat

### ELIMINATE (days 1-7)
- Bread, baguette, toast, sandwich bread
- Pasta (wheat-based)
- Pastries, cakes, biscuits, croissants, pain au chocolat
- Pizza dough
- Flour-based sauces: béchamel, roux-thickened sauces
- Beer
- Couscous, bulgur, semolina
- Breadcrumbs (in breaded foods)
- Check labels: wheat flour is in many sauces, processed foods

### EAT INSTEAD
- Rice, potatoes, sweet potatoes (main starch replacement)
- Rice pasta, buckwheat noodles (soba)
- Rice cakes, corn crackers
- Gluten-free bread (optional — or just skip bread)
- Corn, quinoa, buckwheat, polenta
- Oats (gluten-free certified if being strict)
- Meat, fish, eggs, vegetables, cheese — all naturally gluten-free
- Corn tortillas instead of bread wraps

### RECHALLENGE (days 8-10)
- Breakfast: toast or croissants
- Lunch: pasta or sandwich
- Snack: biscuits or pastry
- Dinner: bread with meal, couscous

## Category D: Nightshades

### ELIMINATE (days 1-7)
- Tomato: raw, sauce, concentrate, ketchup, sun-dried
- Bell peppers: all colors
- Chili peppers, hot sauce
- Potatoes (sweet potato is NOT a nightshade — it's fine)
- Eggplant
- Spices: paprika, cayenne, chili powder, chili flakes
- Goji berries
- Check labels: "spices" in ingredient lists may include paprika

### EAT INSTEAD
- Sweet potatoes, rice, pasta for starch (replaces potatoes)
- Carrots, zucchini, green beans, broccoli, cauliflower for vegetables
- Pesto, olive oil + lemon, cream sauce instead of tomato sauce
- Black pepper is fine (not a nightshade)
- All fruits, all other vegetables
- All meat, fish, dairy as normal

### RECHALLENGE (days 8-10)
- Lunch: pasta with tomato sauce, side of bell peppers
- Dinner: ratatouille (tomato, eggplant, bell pepper) or potatoes with a tomato-based dish

---

## Category E: Histamine Liberators

### ELIMINATE (days 1-7)
- Chocolate (all forms: dark, milk, cocoa powder)
- Citrus: orange, lemon, grapefruit, mandarin, lime, clementine
- Strawberries
- Shellfish, crustaceans: shrimp, crab, lobster, mussels, oysters
- Pork (all forms: chops, tenderloin, ground — but other meats are fine)
- Nuts: especially walnuts, cashews, peanuts; also almonds, hazelnuts
- Pineapple, papaya, kiwi
- Egg whites (yolks only, or skip eggs entirely if too impractical)
- Licorice

### EAT INSTEAD
- Carob or vanilla for sweet flavor (replaces chocolate)
- Apples, pears, blueberries, melon, mango for fruit
- Chicken, beef, lamb, turkey for protein (replaces pork)
- Seeds: pumpkin seeds, sunflower seeds (replaces nuts)
- Whole eggs are ok if removing whites is impractical — note it in log
- Lemon can be replaced with vinegar-free dressings or herb-based flavoring
  (but during Cat A testing, avoid vinegar too)

### RECHALLENGE (days 8-10)
- Breakfast: hot chocolate, orange juice
- Lunch: pork chop, side salad with lemon dressing
- Snack: chocolate, strawberries, nuts
- Dinner: shrimp/shellfish dish

## Quick Summary Table

| Category | Key items removed | Main replacements |
|----------|------------------|-------------------|
| A. High histamine | Aged cheese, cured meat, canned fish, tomato, fermented foods, wine | Fresh cheese, fresh meat/fish, carrots/zucchini, pesto/cream sauce |
| B. Dairy | All milk, cheese, yogurt, butter, cream | Oat milk, olive oil, dairy-free bread |
| C. Gluten/wheat | Bread, pasta, pastries, beer | Rice, potatoes, rice pasta, corn |
| D. Nightshades | Tomato, peppers, potato, eggplant, paprika | Sweet potato, rice, pesto/cream sauce |
| E. Histamine liberators | Chocolate, citrus, strawberry, shellfish, pork, nuts | Carob, apple/pear, chicken/beef, seeds |
