# Literature Summary: Post-COVID Ocular Syndrome

**Topic slug:** postcovid-ocular-syndrome
**Date:** 2026-07-09
**Primary paper:** Moustardas et al. 2026 (Nature Communications, PMID 42420292)

---

## Primary Paper — Verified

**Moustardas P, Setterud H, Meijer H, Andersson G, Roth J, Dashti A, Johansson B, Macedo AF, Lagali N** (2026). Long-term ocular symptoms following COVID-19 linked to immune dysregulation, dysautonomia and peripheral neuropathy. *Nature Communications*, 17(1):5624. DOI: 10.1038/s41467-026-74858-4

**Access status:** Fully verified via DOI and PubMed (PMID 42420292). Abstract and MeSH terms retrieved. Key quantitative findings confirmed from abstract: n=100 POS patients, n=32 controls, 3mo–3yr follow-up, clinical + proteomics + confocal microscopy.

**Key findings (from abstract + press release):**
- N=100 non-hospitalised post-mild-COVID with 3mo–3yr ocular symptoms vs n=32 mild-COVID controls without ocular symptoms
- 1 in 3 patients on full/part-time sick leave
- Standard ophthalmic exams normal; abnormalities only on specialised testing
- Near vision disturbances, adult-onset strabismus, weakened autonomic pupillary reflexes, corneal neurodegeneration, chronic dendritic/T-cell activation on corneal confocal microscopy
- Tear proteomics: abnormal CD4+ T-cell regulatory protein profile matching severe/fatal COVID tissue patterns; upregulation of ITGB6, NFASC, CTGF, TPSAB1, CKMT1A-CKMT1B; pupil dysfunction correlated with JUN; dendritic/T-cell dysregulation correlated with ANGPTL2, SKAP2, DAPP1
- Diagnostic models: 77% (instrument-only) to 91% (instrument + tear proteomics) accuracy
- Interpretation: chronic T-cell-mediated neuroinflammation → immune dysregulation + dysautonomia + peripheral neuropathy manifesting in the eye

## Supporting Papers (4 new bib entries, 3 pre-existing reused)

### New entries added to neurology-comorbidities.bib:

1. **Smit et al. 2026** — Task-evoked pupillary dynamics altered in PCS. N=397 PCS, n=129 controls. Reduced IPA/LHIPA at all task difficulty levels (p<0.001). Supports pupillary autonomic dysregulation. Certainty: 0.65.

2. **Johansson et al. 2022** — Visual function assessment in post-COVID neurorehabilitation. Reports convergence insufficiency and accommodative dysfunction as common but under-recognised. Certainty: 0.40.

3. **Naseri et al. 2025** — Systematic review and meta-analysis of dry eye disease after COVID-19. Elevated prevalence; risk factors: female sex, disease severity. Certainty: 0.55.

### Pre-existing entries reused (NOT duplicated):

4. **Cañadas et al. 2023** (`Cañadas2023CornealConfocal` in long-covid.bib) — Corneal confocal microscopy in long COVID, n=50. Reduced nerve fiber density, increased immune cell infiltrate 2 years post-infection. Certainty: 0.50.

5. **Azcue et al. 2025** (`Azcue2025sfn` in autonomic-cardiovascular.bib) — Small fiber neuropathy PCC + ME/CFS using corneal confocal microscopy. Corneal fiber tortuosity AUC=0.720. N=30/30/30. Certainty: 0.55 (not scored in existing entry; estimated from abstract).

6. **Azcue et al. 2023** (`Azcue2023sfn` in autonomic-cardiovascular.bib) — Dysautonomia and SFN in post-COVID + ME/CFS. Heat response latencies indicating C-fiber denervation; 34% non-length-dependent SFN. N=30/30/30.

### Search directions with no or insufficient results:

- **Adult-onset strabismus post-COVID** — No dedicated studies found beyond Moustardas 2026 primary paper. Undocumented phenomenon in the literature.
- **T-cell exhaustion tear film** — No direct matches. Ocular surface immunology studies exist but none specifically profiling T-cell exhaustion in post-COVID tears.
- **Tear proteomics neurotrophic keratitis** — General tear proteomics methodology literature exists but no post-COVID ocular syndrome tear studies outside Moustardas 2026.
- **Null/negative studies** — No studies found that explicitly examined and found NO ocular abnormalities post-COVID. The absence of negative results may reflect publication bias.

## Synthesis for ME/CFS Relevance

The Moustardas 2026 paper provides a well-characterised model of post-infectious ocular syndrome driven by immune dysregulation, dysautonomia, and small-fiber/ peripheral neuropathy — the same triad implicated in ME/CFS pathophysiology. Key bridges to ME/CFS:

1. **Corneal confocal microscopy** provides an accessible, non-invasive window into small-fiber neuropathy in living patients. Already used by Azcue group to show corneal nerve abnormalities in ME/CFS (Azcue 2023, 2025). Moustardas extends this to ocular-specific symptoms with tear proteomics.

2. **Tear proteomics signature** of CD4+ T-cell dysregulation matches the T-cell abnormalities documented in ME/CFS (cytokine dysregulation, exhausted/ activated T-cell subsets). The correlation with severe/fatal COVID tissue signatures suggests a common mechanism of T-cell-mediated immunopathology.

3. **Autonomic dysfunction** (pupillary reflexes, strabismus from cranial nerve involvement) parallels the dysautonomia documented in ME/CFS via heart rate variability, tilt-table testing, and SFN studies.

4. **Diagnostic gap** — standard exams miss the pathology, a pattern familiar from ME/CFS (normal routine labs, abnormalities on specialised testing).

**Gaps:** No direct ME/CFS cohort comparison in Moustardas 2026. The POS patients were not assessed for ME/CFS diagnostic criteria. Replication needed.
