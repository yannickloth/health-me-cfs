## Papers Found

### 1. Götz et al. 2025 (bioRxiv) — ISR Suppression Extends Fly Lifespan

**BibTeX key:** `Gotz2025ISRLifespan`

**Authors:** Miriam S Götz, Dan J Hayman, Gracie Adams, Fumiaki Obata, Mirre J P Simons
**Affiliation:** University of Sheffield (School of Biosciences)
**Server:** bioRxiv
**Posted:** 2025-07-14
**Preprint DOI:** 10.1101/2025.07.14.664701
**Claimed PNAS DOI (unverified):** 10.1073/pnas.2518812123
**Study design:** Genetic manipulation of GCN2 and ATF4 in *Drosophila melanogaster*; lifespan assays with overexpression and RNAi knockdown; proteostasis and DNA repair pathway analysis

**Key findings:**
- Prevailing hypothesis (from yeast/C. elegans): ISR/GCN2–ATF4 activation is pro-longevity
- Fly data: overexpression of dGCN2 and dATF4 **significantly reduces lifespan**
- RNAi knockdown of dATF4 **extends lifespan** (first Drosophila evidence)
- dATF4 overexpression drives metabolic stress; knockdown upregulates proteostasis and DNA repair
- Conclusion: ATF4 role in aging is dose- and context-dependent; chronic activation detrimental in flies

**ME/CFS relevance:** Contradicts the "activate ISR = beneficial" framing sometimes applied to ME/CFS. Suggests chronic ISR activation (as hypothesised in ME/CFS) may be harmful, not adaptive. Supports therapeutic ISR suppression angle.

**Certainty:** Low–Medium (0.30) — preprint, not peer-reviewed; *Drosophila* model, requires vertebrate replication; directly contradicts yeast/C. elegans data
**Sample size:** N/A (model organism); multiple genetic manipulations

### 2. Hennig, Prusty et al. 2022 — HHV-6 miR-aU14 Drives Mitochondrial Fragmentation via miR-30/p53/DRP1

**BibTeX key:** `Hennig2022HHV6miRNA`

**Authors:** Thomas Hennig, Archana B Prusty, Benedikt B Kaufer, Adam W Whisnant, Manivel Lodha, Antje Enders, Julius Thomas, Francesca Kasimir, Arnhild Grothey, Teresa Klein, Stefanie Herb, Christopher Jürges, Markus Sauer, Utz Fischer, Thomas Rudel, Gunter Meister, Florian Erhard, Lars Dölken, Bhupesh K Prusty
**Journal:** Nature
**Year:** 2022
**Volume/Pages:** 605(7910):539–544
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41586-022-04667-4
**PMID:** 35508655
**Study design:** Molecular virology; HHV-6A infection models; miRNA processing analysis; latency/reactivation assays; mitochondrial morphology assessment

**Key findings:**
- HHV-6A encodes miR-aU14, which selectively inhibits processing of host miR-30 family by binding pri-miRNA hairpin loops
- Loss of miR-30 activates miR-30–p53–DRP1 axis → profound mitochondrial architectural disruption
- Impairs type I interferon response, enabling productive HHV-6A infection and reactivation
- miR-aU14 identified as "readily druggable master regulator of the herpesvirus lytic–latent switch"
- Does not explicitly measure eIF2α or ISR, but DRP1-mediated fission is the downstream effector of ISR kinase activation (link to ISR via downstream pathway)

**ME/CFS relevance:** Core Prusty group mechanistic paper. HHV-6 reactivation → miR-aU14 → mitochondrial fragmentation. The DRP1/mitochondrial fission mechanism overlaps with ISR-mediated fragmentation described in Baron 2025. Direct viral trigger for mitochondrial dysfunction in ME/CFS.

**Certainty:** Medium–High (0.68) — Nature publication, mechanistic dissection, independent replication expected; ISR link is indirect
**Sample size:** Cell line and viral models; multiple independent experiments

### 4. Wang, Hwang et al. 2023 — WASF3 Disrupts Mitochondrial Respiration via ER Stress / PERK in ME/CFS

**BibTeX key:** `Wang2023WASF3MECFS`

**Authors:** Ping-Yuan Wang, Jin Ma, Young-Chae Kim, Annie Y Son, Abu Mohammad Syed, Chengyu Liu, Mateus P Mori, Rebecca D Huffstutler, JoEllyn L Stolinski, S Lalith Talagala, Ju-Gyeong Kang, Brian T Walitt, Avindra Nath, Paul M Hwang
**Journal:** Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences USA
**Year:** 2023
**Volume/Issue/Pages:** 120(34):e2302738120
**DOI:** 10.1073/pnas.2302738120
**PMID:** 37579159
**PMCID:** PMC10450651
**Study design:** Translational; skeletal muscle biopsies from ME/CFS patients (n=14) vs healthy controls (n=10–14); fibroblast experiments; transgenic mouse treadmill assay; proteomic analysis

**Key findings:**
- WASF3 protein overexpressed in ME/CFS skeletal muscle; regulated by BiP/GRP78 (ER quality control)
- PERK (ISR kinase) markers elevated in ME/CFS muscle → PERK is one of the four eIF2α kinases → this is **direct ISR activation evidence in ME/CFS**
- WASF3 disrupts Complex IV in mitochondrial respiratory supercomplexes → exercise intolerance
- WASF3 transgenic mice: markedly decreased treadmill running capacity
- Pharmacologic ER stress reduction → improved mitochondrial function in ME/CFS patient cells

**ME/CFS relevance:** PERK is the ER-stress arm of the ISR. Elevated PERK in ME/CFS muscle = ISR activation via the ER stress branch. Connects ISR to mitochondrial respiratory failure and exercise intolerance. Best direct ISR evidence in ME/CFS to date.

**Certainty:** Medium (0.58) — PNAS; small n (14 patients); single study; NIH cohort; PERK/ISR connection present but not the primary paper focus
**Sample size:** n=14 ME/CFS, n=10–14 controls

### 5. Aloise, van Kuppeveld et al. 2023 — SARS-CoV-2 Nucleocapsid Inhibits PKR-ISR via N2b Domain

**BibTeX key:** `Aloise2023CoVPKR`

**Authors:** Chiara Aloise, Jelle G Schipper, Arno van Vliet, Judith Oymans, Tim Donselaar, Daniel L Hurdiss, Raoul J de Groot, Frank J M van Kuppeveld
**Journal:** PLoS Pathogens
**Year:** 2023
**Volume/Issue/Pages:** 19(8):e1011582
**DOI:** 10.1371/journal.ppat.1011582
**PMID:** 37607209
**PMCID:** PMC10473545
**Study design:** Molecular virology; cell-based ISR reporter assays; SARS-CoV-2 N protein domain dissection; dsRNA binding mutations

**Key findings:**
- SARS-CoV-2 nucleocapsid protein domain N2b blocks PKR-mediated ISR activation
- Mechanism: N2b sequesters dsRNA, preventing PKR and RIG-I receptor activation
- Abolishes stress granule formation and ISR-induced translational arrest
- Conserved across coronavirus species: fundamental viral immune evasion strategy

**ME/CFS/Long COVID relevance:** Provides mechanism by which SARS-CoV-2 chronically suppresses PKR-ISR; viral persistence could maintain this suppression. If virus persists in long COVID, ongoing ISR antagonism may impair cellular stress responses.

**Certainty:** Medium (0.62) — PLoS Pathogens, peer-reviewed, mechanistic; indirect long COVID relevance (active infection, not persistence specifically)
**Sample size:** Cell-based; multiple independent experiments

### 6. Christ, Klingström, Tynell 2023 — SARS-CoV-2 Variant-Specific PKR-ISR Differences

**BibTeX key:** `Christ2023CoVISRVariants`

**Authors:** Wanda Christ, Jonas Klingström, Janne Tynell
**Journal:** Virus Research
**Year:** 2023
**Volume/Pages:** 339:199271
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.virusres.2023.199271
**PMID:** 37979658
**PMCID:** PMC10716588
**Study design:** Comparative infection; ancestral vs Delta vs Omicron SARS-CoV-2; ISR reporter assays; stress granule quantification; eIF2α phosphorylation analysis

**Key findings:**
- All variants activate PKR-mediated eIF2α phosphorylation
- Delta: weaker PKR activation and eIF2α phosphorylation than ancestral
- Omicron: ~60% of infected cells form stress granules vs ~5% for ancestral/Delta
- Omicron nucleocapsid less efficient at stress granule suppression
- Variant-specific ISR manipulation may contribute to different clinical/long COVID profiles

**Long COVID relevance:** Variant-specific ISR differences could partly explain different long COVID symptom rates across waves. Omicron-induced higher ISR activation may have different persistence consequences.

**Certainty:** Medium (0.55) — peer-reviewed; cell-based; no direct clinical long COVID connection
**Sample size:** Cell-based; multiple experiments

### 7. Baron, Wiseman et al. 2025 — Pharmacologic ISR Kinase Activation Prevents Mitochondrial Fragmentation

**BibTeX key:** `Baron2025ISRMitochondria`

**Authors:** Kelsey R Baron, Samantha Oviedo, Sophia Krasny, Mashiat Zaman, Rama Aldakhlallah, Prerona Bora, Prakhyat Mathur, Gerald Pfeffer, Michael J Bollong, Timothy E Shutt, Danielle A Grotjahn, R Luke Wiseman
**Journal:** eLife
**Year:** 2025
**Volume/Pages:** 13:RP100541
**DOI:** 10.7554/eLife.100541
**PMID:** 39937095
**PMCID:** PMC11820110
**Study design:** Small-molecule screen; cell-based ISR kinase activation assays (HRI, GCN2); mitochondrial morphology quantification; patient fibroblasts (MFN2-D414V CMT2A)

**Key findings:**
- Identified compounds activating HRI selectively (0357, 3610) → adaptive mitochondrial elongation
- ISR kinase activation (HRI or GCN2) promotes mitochondrial elongation, prevents DRP1-mediated fragmentation
- Effects blocked by ISRIB, confirming ISR pathway specificity
- Rescues mitochondrial morphology in MFN2-D414V patient fibroblasts (ataxia, CMT2A)
- Contradicts simple "suppress ISR = beneficial" framing: in context of mitochondrial disease, ISR activation is protective

**ME/CFS relevance:** Not directly studied. However: DRP1-mediated mitochondrial fragmentation is central to Prusty/HHV-6 ME/CFS model. ISR kinase activation (particularly HRI/GCN2) prevents this fragmentation. Therapeutic angle: ISR modulators could counteract ME/CFS mitochondrial pathology. ISRIB as ISR suppressor would counteract this protective ISR-mitochondrial axis.

**Certainty:** Medium–High (0.65) — peer-reviewed eLife; mechanistic; no ME/CFS-specific evidence; extrapolation required
**Sample size:** Cell-based; patient fibroblasts

### 8. Cuoco, Wiseman et al. 2025 — Pharmacological RIG-I Targeting Selectively Activates ISR via HRI

**BibTeX key:** `Cuoco2025RIGISR`

**Authors:** Caroline A Cuoco, Wen Ren, Kelsey R Baron, and colleagues (Scripps Research / UC San Francisco)
**Journal:** Science Advances
**Year:** 2025
**Volume/Pages:** 11(44):eadt3014
**DOI:** 10.1126/sciadv.adt3014
**PMID:** 41160678
**PMCID:** PMC12571054
**Study design:** Chemical biology; compound A8 characterization; RIG-I binding (biolayer interferometry); ISR reporter assays; HRI-depleted cell controls

**Key findings:**
- Compound A8 binds RIG-I cytosolic receptor → activates ISR through HRI kinase, independent of interferon response
- Active metabolite CC81 achieves ISR activation without lipid metabolism off-target effects
- RIG-I–HRI axis: new ISR activation route linking innate immune sensing to translational control
- Selective ISR activation (no interferon): potential therapeutic tool to induce adaptive response without inflammation

**ME/CFS relevance:** Not directly studied. Mechanistically relevant: RIG-I is an innate immune dsRNA sensor implicated in viral-triggered ME/CFS models. The RIG-I → HRI → eIF2α pathway connects innate immune activation to ISR in a way that could be chronically active in post-viral conditions.

**Certainty:** Medium (0.60) — Science Advances, peer-reviewed; mechanistic; no ME/CFS data
**Sample size:** Cell-based

### 9. Omdal et al. 2026 — Long COVID Fatigue NOT Associated with Peripheral Cellular Stress Biomarkers [NULL RESULT]

**BibTeX key:** `Omdal2026LCFatigueStress`

**Authors:** Roald Omdal and colleagues (full author list not available from forum source; Stavanger University Hospital)
**Journal:** Brain, Behavior, & Immunity - Health
**Year:** 2026
**Volume/Pages:** not confirmed; article DOI from ScienceDirect: 10.1016/j.bbih.2026.100xxx (exact article number not retrieved — 403 error)
**Note:** DOI partially confirmed via S2666354626000591 ScienceDirect identifier; full DOI not verified
**Study design:** Single-center cross-sectional controlled study; n=48 long COVID, n=48 age/sex-matched recovered controls (no persistent symptoms); blood biomarker panel

**Key findings:**
- **Null result:** No significant differences in measured biomarkers between long COVID and controls
- Biomarkers tested: HSP90α, Serpin F1/PEDF, Hemopexin, APOA4 (cellular stress); CRP, TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β (inflammation)
- Multivariable regression: no significant associations between any biomarker and fatigue severity

**ME/CFS relevance:** Important negative result. Peripheral blood HSP/inflammatory markers do not track fatigue severity in long COVID. Suggests ISR or cellular stress signatures, if present, may be tissue-specific (CNS, muscle) rather than circulating. Supports need for tissue-level ISR measurement.

**Certainty:** Medium (0.55) — apparent peer-reviewed publication; n=48; but ISR markers specifically not measured; null result on general stress markers
**Sample size:** n=48 long COVID, n=48 controls

### 10. Le, Back et al. 2025 — eIF2α Phosphorylation-ATF4 Axis Mitigates Mitochondrial Impairment During ER Stress

**BibTeX key:** `Le2025ATF4Mitochondria`

**Authors:** Hien Thi Le, Jiyoung Yu, Hee Sung Ahn, Mi-Jeong Kim, In Gyeong Chae, Hyun-Nam Cho, Juhee Kim, Hye-Kyung Park, Hyuk Nam Kwon, Han-Jung Chae, Byoung Heon Kang, Jeong Kon Seo, Kyunggon Kim, Sung Hoon Back
**Journal:** Molecules and Cells
**Year:** 2025
**Volume/Pages:** article 100176
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.mocell.2024.100176
**PMID:** 39756584
**Study design:** Cell-based (cell lines); eIF2α phosphorylation mutants; ATF4 overexpression rescue experiments; mitochondrial dynamics and bioenergetics assays

**Key findings:**
- eIF2α phosphorylation–ATF4 axis required for expression of transcription factors maintaining mitochondrial health during ER stress
- Cells lacking eIF2α phosphorylation: dysregulated mitochondrial dynamics, reduced energy production, impaired mtDNA replication
- ATF4 overexpression rescued these defects
- Establishes: ISR activation (eIF2α–ATF4) is **protective** during acute ER stress for mitochondria

**ME/CFS relevance:** Not studied. Mechanistically: if ME/CFS involves persistent ER/mitochondrial stress (WASF3/PERK data from Wang 2023), then the eIF2α–ATF4 axis may be engaged as a protective response. Chronic activation, however, may become maladaptive (per Götz 2025 lifespan data).

**Certainty:** Medium (0.53) — peer-reviewed; cell-based only; no clinical data; no ME/CFS connection
**Sample size:** Cell-based

### 11. Costa-Mattioli & Walter 2020 — The Integrated Stress Response: From Mechanism to Disease [KEY REVIEW]

**BibTeX key:** `CostaMattioli2020ISRReview`

**Authors:** Mauro Costa-Mattioli, Peter Walter
**Journal:** Science
**Year:** 2020
**Volume/Pages:** 368(6489):eaat5314
**DOI:** 10.1126/science.aat5314
**PMID:** 32327570
**Study design:** Comprehensive review

**Key findings:**
- Authoritative review of ISR biology: four kinases (PERK/GCN2/PKR/HRI), eIF2α phosphorylation, ATF4 induction, eIF2B as GEF target
- ISRIB mechanism: stabilizes eIF2B complex, enhances GEF activity, suppresses ISR
- Therapeutic approaches: ISR inhibition (ISRIB, eIF2B activators) vs ISR activation (salubrinal, sephin1) depending on disease
- Covers cognitive function, neurodegeneration, diabetes, cancer; no ME/CFS
- Establishes conceptual framework for ISR as target in chronic disease

**ME/CFS relevance:** Essential background reference for ISR pathway in any ME/CFS integration. Provides mechanistic grounding for all other ISR papers in this stream.

**Certainty:** High (0.90) — Science, peer-reviewed authoritative review by field leaders; widely cited
**Sample size:** Review
