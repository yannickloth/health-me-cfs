# Cross-Hypothesis Compatibility Audit: Stigmatisation in ME/CFS

**Date:** 2026-06-16
**8 new hypotheses audited against ~90 existing registry entries**
**Topic domain:** Psychosocial / social determinants of health

## Summary

**Zero mechanistic domain overlaps found.** All 8 new stigmatisation hypotheses operate in the psychosocial domain (healthcare interactions, stigma perception, social determinants, structural barriers). All ~90 existing registry entries operate in biological/mechanistic domains (immune, metabolic, neurological, vascular, endocrine, genetic). No pair engages the same mechanism — all relationships are classified as **independent**.

## Detailed Analysis

### Entry 1: Stigma → Functional Outcomes (H, cert 0.50)
**Mechanism:** stigma perception → reduced social role satisfaction + functional status  
**Domain:** Psychosocial → clinical outcomes  
**Overlap search:** No existing hypothesis addresses stigma as a mediator of clinical outcomes. Closest entries address depression secondary to illness (allostatic self-inefficacy in ch14d) but as a biological consequence of interoceptive hierarchy failure, not as a social determinant.  
**Classification:** Independent

### Entry 2: Illness Name as Stigma Vector (H, cert 0.45)
**Mechanism:** diagnostic label → negative attributions → stigma  
**Domain:** Nomenclature → social perception  
**Overlap search:** No existing hypothesis. The registry has no nomenclature/semantics hypotheses.  
**Classification:** Independent

### Entry 3: Healthcare Dismissal as Chronic Stressor (S, cert 0.30)
**Mechanism:** dismissal → chronic psychosocial stress → HPA/SNS dysregulation → disease amplification  
**Domain:** Psychosocial → biological (HPA stress pathway)  
**Potential overlap:** Existing entry "Corticosteroid-induced HPA Trust-Breaking" (cert 0.55, H) addresses HPA feedback via pharmacological iatrogenesis — different mechanism (pharmacological vs psychosocial), different trigger (corticosteroids vs healthcare dismissal), different pathway.  
**Overlap search detail:** The ch14d interoceptive hierarchy speculation (spec:interoceptive-asd-secondary, cert 0.30) addresses allostatic load models but through the lens of predictive processing/corrupted interoceptive precision — not through social-stress-driven HPA dysregulation. Different mechanistic locus.  
**Classification:** Independent  
**Note:** If future evidence links healthcare dismissal to measurable HPA/immune changes, this speculation would **feed into** the existing HPA models (ch09, ch40). Currently too speculative to establish a feed-into relationship (relationship certainty < 0.30).

### Entry 4: Psychiatric Embedding as Structural Stigma (S, cert 0.25)
**Mechanism:** system design (psychiatry-gatekept services) → structural stigma  
**Domain:** Health systems → healthcare interactions  
**Overlap search:** No existing hypothesis addresses health system design. The ch33 psychogenic-vs-biomedical controversy is documented as historical context, not as a formal hypothesis with testable predictions.  
**Classification:** Independent

### Entry 5: Media Framing Amplifies Stigma (S, cert 0.30)
**Mechanism:** media content → public perception → healthcare stigma  
**Domain:** Media → public perception → healthcare  
**Overlap search:** No existing hypothesis. Stigma as a downstream consequence of media framing has no biological counterpart in the registry.  
**Classification:** Independent

### Entry 6: ME/CFS Stigma Comparable to Other FSS (H, cert 0.45)
**Mechanism:** invisible illness → delegitimisation → elevated stigma vs medically explained conditions  
**Domain:** Comparative illness perception / social determinants  
**Overlap search:** No existing hypothesis. FSS comparison is descriptive, not mechanistic.  
**Classification:** Independent

### Entry 7: Biomedical Validation as Anti-Stigma Intervention (P, cert 0.35)
**Mechanism:** clinician validation → reduced perceived stigma → improved outcomes  
**Domain:** Therapeutic intervention (psychosocial, not biological)  
**Overlap search:** No existing hypothesis addresses clinician behaviour as therapeutic. The closest is ch21's moderate pathway mention of clinician validation for disability assessments — not formalised as a testable hypothesis.  
**Classification:** Independent

### Entry 8: Child Stigma Understudied (O, n/a)
**Mechanism:** paediatric research gap — no formal mechanism proposed  
**Domain:** Research gap identification  
**Overlap search:** No existing paediatric stigma hypotheses. Parslow 2017 not in registry.  
**Classification:** Independent
