## Paper Details

### 1. tavakoli2015normaliveccm — High
- **Author:** Tavakoli M et al.
- **Year:** 2015
- **Title:** Normative values for corneal nerve morphology assessed using corneal confocal microscopy: a multinational normative data set
- **Journal:** Diabetes Care 38(5)
- **DOI:** 10.2337/dc14-2311
- **Key Finding:** Multicenter collaboration providing worldwide age-adjusted normative values of CNFD, CNBD, CNFL, and tortuosity. Essential reference for interpreting CCM across all diseases.
- **Certainty:** 0.80 (multinational, large sample, established reference standard)

### 2. cao2022cornealreference — Medium
- **Author:** Cao J et al.
- **Year:** 2022
- **Title:** Corneal Nerve Parameter Reference Values for Chinese Adults Assessed by Corneal Confocal Microscopy
- **Journal:** J Diabetes Res 2022
- **DOI:** 10.1155/2022/4913031
- **Key Finding:** Reference values for healthy Chinese adults. Age and sex associations with corneal nerve parameters. Confirms normative values generalize across populations.
- **Certainty:** 0.60 (single ethnic group, moderate sample)

### 3. kubat2026fibromyalgiaccm — Low
- **Author:** Kubat B et al.
- **Year:** 2026
- **Title:** Corneal confocal microscopy in fibromyalgia syndrome: small fiber neuropathy, and treatment outcomes
- **Journal:** Eye (Lond) 40(2)
- **DOI:** 10.1038/s41433-025-04132-2
- **Key Finding:** FMS patients showed reduced CNFD, CNFL, and increased corneal DC density vs controls. SNRI treatment partially reversed corneal nerve changes — suggests CCM can track treatment response.

### 4. sommer2025fibromyalgiasfn — High
- **Author:** Sommer C, Üçeyler N
- **Year:** 2025
- **Title:** Small fiber pathology in fibromyalgia syndrome
- **Journal:** Pain Rep 10
- **DOI:** N/A
- **Key Finding:** ~50% of women with FMS have reduced skin innervation; consistent across global cohorts. CCM detects SFN in ~59% of FMS. Microneurography shows spontaneous C-fiber activity. Strong evidence for SFN subset in FMS.
- **Certainty:** 0.75 (authoritative review, consistent converging evidence)

### 5. akowuah2025msccm — High

## Null/Negative Findings
None identified in the included literature. All 15 papers report positive findings for CCM utility. This reflects publication bias in the field — negative CCM studies are likely under-published.

## Contradictions / Competing Evidence
- **Ghadban2025mixed** found only moderate correlation between CCM and IENFD (skin biopsy), suggesting they measure partially distinct aspects of small fiber pathology (morphology vs density)
- **Jänsch2024fibromyalgiasfn** (not included but noted) highlights that FMS and SFN are distinct conditions requiring careful differential diagnosis — CCM alone may not distinguish them
- **Raasing2023** (PMID 38047722, not included) shows analysis method (manual vs automated) significantly affects CCM results — methodological heterogeneity must be accounted for

## Cross-Disease Narrative Summary
The evidence supports CCM as a **transdiagnostic small fiber neuropathy biomarker**:
- **Normative data** (Tavakoli 2015, Cao 2022) provides reference framework
- **Diabetic neuropathy** (largest literature, 85+ papers) validates CCM against gold-standard measures
- **Fibromyalgia** (Sommer 2025, Kubat 2026, Ranathunga 2026): ~50-59% prevalence of SFN
- **Multiple sclerosis** (Akowuah 2025): consistent corneal nerve loss, correlates with disability
- **Parkinson's disease** (Yin 2025, Niu 2024, Yang 2025): SFN involvement, pain prediction, differential diagnosis
- **Chemotherapy-induced** (Baghdasaryan 2026): treatment monitoring utility
- **SLE / autoimmune** (Gharib 2025): connective tissue disease SFN
- **POTS** (Cantrell 2025): autonomic SFN
- **Migraine** (van Welie 2026): trigeminal small fiber involvement
- **CCM vs skin biopsy** (Ghadban 2025): moderate correlation, CCM less invasive
