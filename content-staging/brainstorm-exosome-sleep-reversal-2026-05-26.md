# Brainstorm: Exosome-Mediated Sleep Deprivation Reversal → ME/CFS Integration
**Date:** 2026-05-26
**Source:** Kang et al. 2026, Translational Psychiatry
**Integrated in Phase 2:** ch08, ch18, ch25

## Tier 1 — High Confidence, Direct ME/CFS Relevance

### 1.1 RVG-HSP70 exosomes reverse neuroinflammation in ME/CFS
- Mechanistic rationale: RVG-modified exosomes deliver HSP70 mRNA across compromised BBB → restore chaperone capacity → suppress hippocampal TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β → enhance IL-10, BDNF, pCREB signaling → normalize memory/learning circuits disrupted in ME/CFS.
- Evidence link: Kang et al. 2026 demonstrate complete reversal of sleep-deprivation induced neuroinflammation and cognitive deficits in mice using this exact platform; Ramos-Zaldívar 2022 confirm EV BBB crossing mechanisms enhanced by inflammation.
- Certainty: 0.68
- Falsifiable prediction: ME/CFS patients with elevated neuroinflammatory biomarkers (CSF IL-6, TNF-α) receiving RVG-HSP70 exosomes show ≥30% reduction in hippocampal TSPO PET signal after 8 weeks.
- ME/CFS relevance type: drug

### 1.2 Exosome-HSP70 restores glymphatic clearance
- Mechanistic rationale: HSP70-mediated neuroinflammation reduction re-establishes aquaporin-4 polarization on astrocytic endfeet → restores glymphatic convective flow → clears accumulated metabolic waste (β-amyloid, tau, α-synuclein) → resolves morning headache and unrefreshing sleep.
- Evidence link: Kang et al. show HSP70 exosomes reduce inflammatory cytokines that disrupt AQP4 polarization; existing paper links glymphatic dysfunction to metabolite accumulation and sleep impairment.
- Certainty: 0.62
- Falsifiable prediction: ME/CFS patients receiving HSP70 exosomes show ≥25% improvement in glymphatic clearance measured by DTI-ALPS index after 6 weeks.
- ME/CFS relevance type: drug

### 1.3 Biomarker: Exosomal HSP70 predicts sleep phenotype
- Mechanistic rationale: Brain-derived exosomes carry molecular signatures of CNS chaperone capacity; reduced HSP70 in neuron-derived exosomes reflects impaired proteostasis → correlates with unrefreshing sleep severity and cognitive dysfunction.
- Evidence link: Kang et al. establish exosomal mRNA delivery efficacy; Sanadgol 2025 review confirms exosomes as CNS biomarker platforms; existing paper documents microglial activation via TSPO PET.
- Certainty: 0.71
- Falsifiable prediction: Lower exosomal HSP70 mRNA levels in plasma neuron-derived exosomes (L1CAM+) predict higher PSQI scores and worse cognitive test performance (r > 0.5) in ME/CFS cohorts.
- ME/CFS relevance type: diagnostic

### 1.4 Research: Dose-response curve for exosome CNS penetration
- Mechanistic rationale: Establish therapeutic window by quantifying exosome biodistribution, CNS accumulation, and mRNA translation efficiency across escalating doses → optimize risk-benefit ratio for clinical translation.
- Evidence link: Kang et al. used single dose; Ramos-Zaldívar 2022 document variable BBB crossing efficiency; Sanadgol 2025 outline dosing considerations for exosome therapeutics.
- Certainty: 0.88
- Falsifiable prediction: Dose-dependent increase in exosome accumulation in mouse hippocampus quantified by luciferase reporter, plateauing at ≥10^11 particles/kg without toxicity markers.
- ME/CFS relevance type: research

### 1.5 Drug: HSP70-inducing drugs + exosome delivery synergy
- Mechanistic rationale: Small-molecule HSP70 inducers (geranylgeranylacetone, arimoclomol) increase endogenous HSP70 production → exosome-delivered HSP70 mRNA provides immediate chaperone capacity while transcriptional induction ramps up → synergistic neuroprotection.
- Evidence link: Kang et al. show exosomal HSP70 mRNA efficacy; existing paper discusses HSP70/HSF1 pathway in heat therapy; arimoclomol approved for ALS with proven CNS penetration.
- Certainty: 0.65
- Falsifiable prediction: Combined HSP70 exosomes + arimoclomol produce greater reduction in neuroinflammatory cytokines (≥40% vs ≤25% monotherapy) in sleep-deprived mice.
- ME/CFS relevance type: drug

### 1.6 Research: Exosome cargo timing relative to sleep
- Mechanistic rationale: Circadian regulation of BBB permeability and endocytic pathways predicts optimal exosome delivery windows → administer during peak CNS uptake to maximize therapeutic index.
- Evidence link: Ramos-Zaldívar 2022 document enhanced EV uptake during inflammation; circadian variations in BBB permeability established in literature; sleep-wake cycle modulates transporter expression.
- Certainty: 0.75
- Falsifiable prediction: Exosomes administered 2 hours after dark-phase onset achieve 2-fold higher hippocampal accumulation vs morning administration in mice.
- ME/CFS relevance type: research

### 1.7 Non-pharm: Heat therapy enhances exosome HSP70 loading
- Mechanistic rationale: Controlled hyperthermia (sauna, HBOT) upregulates HSP70 expression in donor cells → exosomes harvested from heat-stressed MSCs contain higher HSP70 cargo → amplified therapeutic effect without genetic engineering.
- Evidence link: Kang et al. use engineered HSP70 mRNA; existing paper documents HSAT2 activation via heat therapy; heat shock response known to increase exosomal HSP70.
- Certainty: 0.73
- Falsifiable prediction: Exosomes derived from heat-pretreated MSCs (42°C, 1 hour) contain 3-fold higher HSP70 protein levels and produce superior neuroprotection in sleep-deprivation model.
- ME/CFS relevance type: non-pharm

### 1.8 Hypothesis: Impaired exosome release in ME/CFS
- Mechanistic rationale: ME/CFS cells exhibit defective EV biogenesis pathways (ESCRT complex dysfunction) → reduced endogenous intercellular signaling via exosomes → impaired tissue repair and immunomodulation contributing to chronicity.
- Evidence link: Existing paper mentions EV signaling and HSAT2 exosomal MDSC pathway; Kang et al. rely on intact exosome production; exosome release defects documented in other fatigue conditions.
- Certainty: 0.58
- Falsifiable prediction: ME/CFS patients show reduced circulating exosome concentration and altered ESCRT protein expression in peripheral blood mononuclear cells vs healthy controls.
- ME/CFS relevance type: hypothesis
