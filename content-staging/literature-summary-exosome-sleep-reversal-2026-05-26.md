# Literature Summary: Exosome-Mediated Sleep Deprivation Reversal
**Date:** 2026-05-26  
**Research Stream:** exosome-sleep-reversal

## Primary Research Paper

### Kang et al. 2026 - Engineered Exosomes Reverse Sleep Deprivation Brain Damage in Mice
**Citation:** Kang, Z., Zhu, G., Su, C., et al. (2026). Delivery of HSP70 mRNA via exosomes ameliorates sleep deprivation-induced cognitive impairments in mice. *Translational Psychiatry*. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41398-026-04044-z

**Key Findings:**
- Engineered HEK293T cells expressing HSP70 mRNA + brain-targeting RVG-Lamp2b fusion protein
- HSP70@ExoRVG exosomes efficiently delivered HSP70 mRNA to neural progenitor cells
- In vivo systemic administration restored memory performance in sleep-deprived mice
- Reduced hippocampal TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β; increased IL-10
- Elevated BDNF and phosphorylated CREB
- First demonstration of RVG-modified exosomes delivering mRNA (not siRNA/miRNA) for sleep deprivation treatment
- Effects surpassed non-targeted or empty exosomes

**Sample Size:** Not specified in abstract (mice model)  
**Certainty Assessment:** 0.55 (single preclinical study; no replication; animal model only)

**Methodology:** Engineered exosomes, TEM characterization, NTA, Western blotting, behavioral tests, inflammatory marker analysis  
**Replication Status:** None yet (published April 2026)

## Supporting Literature

### 1. Exosome BBB Delivery - Review Papers

#### Ramos-Zaldívar et al. 2022 - EV BBB Crossing Review
**Citation:** Ramos-Zaldívar, H. M., et al. (2022). Extracellular vesicles through the blood–brain barrier: a review. *Fluids and Barriers of the CNS*, 19(1), 60. https://doi.org/10.1186/s12987-022-00359-3

**Key Findings:**
- Bidirectional EV transport across BBB remains poorly understood
- Most evidence from in vitro models (transwell, organ-on-chip)
- No direct transcytosis visualization in mammals in vivo
- Evidence from zebrafish in vivo experiments
- EVs can cross BBB via multiple mechanisms: macropinocytosis, clathrin-mediated endocytosis, caveolae-mediated endocytosis, adsorptive-mediated endocytosis
- Inflammation (TNF-α, LPS) increases BBB permeability to EVs

**Sample Size:** N/A (review paper)  
**Certainty:** 0.70 (systematic review; 268 citations; established methodology)

**Limitations:** Focuses on EV transport mechanisms; limited on mRNA cargo delivery

#### Sanadgol et al. 2025 - Exosomes for Brain-Targeted Nucleic Acid Delivery
**Citation:** Sanadgol, N., Abedi, M., Hashemzaei, M., et al. (2025). Exosomes as nanocarriers for brain-targeted delivery of therapeutic nucleic acids: advances and challenges. *Journal of Nanobiotechnology*, 23, 453. https://doi.org/10.1186/s12951-025-03528-2

**Key Findings:**
- Comprehensive review of exosomes as NA-BT delivery vehicles to brain
- BBB structure and function overview
- NA-BT types: siRNAs, miRNAs, ASOs, mRNAs, aptamers, PNAs, DNA, DNAzymes, CRISPR/Cas
- Exosome engineering strategies for brain targeting
- Comparison with conventional nanocarriers (LNPs) and cell-based therapies
- Methodological limitations: cargo variability, isolation technique heterogeneity

**Sample Size:** N/A (review paper)  
**Certainty:** 0.70 (comprehensive review; recent 2025 publication)

**Limitations:** Review format; no primary data

## Technical Limitations

### Exosome Engineering Challenges (from Sanadgol 2025)
1. Cargo variability and loading efficiency
2. Isolation technique heterogeneity
3. Standardization issues across studies
4. Off-target effects concerns
5. Scalability for clinical translation

### BBB Delivery Limitations (from Ramos-Zaldívar 2022)
1. Most evidence from in vitro models; limited in vivo mammalian data
2. Transcytosis mechanisms not fully characterized
3. Inflammation enhances permeability but may not be therapeutically desirable
4. EV heterogeneity complicates generalizability
