## 1. Evidence Landscape

**Strong (certainty ≥ 0.60):** 7/8 papers
- Tracey 2002 (0.85) — Nature; cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway (CAP), massively replicated.
- Woo 2023 (0.75) — Acta Neuropathol; SARS-CoV-2 RNA + monocyte infiltration in vagus nerve, n=323 cohort + postmortem histology.
- Oaklander/Joseph 2021 (0.70) — Chest; n=160 ME/CFS, 31% small fiber neuropathy by PGP 9.5 skin biopsy.
- Bonaz 2018 (0.70) — vagus/microbiota-gut-brain axis review; VNS anti-TNF rationale.
- Lladós 2024 (0.65) — CMI pilot; vagus nerve ultrasound thickening + reduced GI peristalsis post-COVID.
- Azcue 2023 (0.65) — J Transl Med; C-fiber denervation + non-length-dependent SFN in post-COVID/ME/CFS.
- Acanfora 2026 (0.60) — SEED; first in vivo gastric mucosal cholinergic (VIP) denervation in Long COVID; correlates with HRV/NT-proBNP/D-dimer; skin IENF spared.

**Weak (< 0.40):** none. VanElzakker 2013 sits at exactly 0.40 (hypothesis paper, but predictively validated by Woo 2023 + Acanfora 2026).

**Missing:** ME/CFS-specific vagal-denervation histology; longitudinal course of vagal denervation; head-to-head structural-vs-functional dysautonomia study; dedicated null/negative studies.

**Null:** none found — flagged as possible publication-bias gap (integrate as a limitation/evidence-quality caveat).

**Indirect biochemical/systemic links (strong):**
- CAP disruption → sustained proinflammation: Tracey 2002, Bonaz 2018 (both ≥0.60) link vagal efferent loss → α7nAChR failure → macrophage TNF disinhibition. This is the mechanistic bridge from structural denervation (Acanfora) to the proinflammatory state documented in ME/CFS.
- Small fiber neuropathy in ME/CFS: Oaklander 2021 (0.70) + Azcue 2023 (0.65) establish PGP-9.5-quantified neuropathy in ME/CFS — same marker Acanfora used.
- Viral vagus infection: Woo 2023 (0.75) provides the proximal mechanism (SARS-CoV-2 → vagus inflammation).
→ ≥5 indirect papers with certainty ≥ 0.40 intersecting documented ME/CFS pathophysiology.

## 2. Internal Contradiction Detection

No fundamental contradictions. One **cross-disease difference** (not a contradiction):
- Acanfora 2026: Long COVID shows selective *visceral* cholinergic denervation with SPARED skin IENF.
- Oaklander 2021 / Azcue 2023: ME/CFS shows ~31% *somatic* SFN (skin) plus non-length-dependent pattern.
- Certainty difference ≤ 0.15 band? Acanfora 0.60 vs Oaklander 0.70 = 0.10 gap (within ±0.10 uncertainty). → Per band rule, present the visceral-vs-somatic distinction as an **#open-question**, not weighting toward either. It may reflect (a) two different diseases, (b) different disease stages, or (c) methodological differences (mucosal vs cutaneous sampling).

## 3. Evidence Summary

This evidence establishes, at moderate-to-high research quality, that Long COVID involves **structural** (not merely functional) autonomic pathology, specifically selective loss of cholinergic (VIP+) nerve fibers in the gastric mucosa, correlating with reduced vagal HRV indices. Convergent lines (vagus histopathology, ultrasound, CAP biology) support a coherent model: post-viral vagal injury → cholinergic denervation → CAP disruption → sustained proinflammation → persistent symptoms.

**Strongest supportable mechanistic claim:** Post-viral illness can produce *structural* vagal/cholinergic denervation that disrupts the cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway — a mechanism directly relevant to ME/CFS given shared SFN, HRV, and autonomic findings. (Support: Tracey 0.85 + Woo 0.75 + Acanfora 0.60.)

**Must remain speculative:** Direct extrapolation to ME/CFS (no ME/CFS-specific vagal-mucosal histology exists); whether the denervation is reversible; whether VNS is therapeutic in ME/CFS; the visceral-vs-somatic SFN distinction between LC and ME/CFS.

**Evidence does NOT support:** any claim that vagal denervation is *established* in ME/CFS specifically (only cross-disease/indirect); any clinical VNS recommendation for ME/CFS.
