### Significance

First structured, transdisciplinary consensus-based nursing care guide specifically for severe/very severe ME/CFS. Developed by the Austrian Society for ME/CFS in collaboration with MedUni Vienna. Practice-oriented consensus document drawing on experiential knowledge from patients and caregivers, validated by professional experts.

### Key Concepts

- **PEM-aware nursing:** Goal is stabilization, not activation — fundamentally different from standard activating nursing paradigms
- **Rolling PEM:** Overlapping crash cycles without full stabilization between episodes — formalizes clinically observed phenomenon
- **Care principles:** Harm avoidance, stimulus reduction, minimal intervention
- **Palliative care mapping:** Applies total pain concept and radical patient orientation to severe ME/CFS
- **Caregiver burden:** 24/7 care, secondary traumatization, financial strain, social isolation treated as integral to care plan — not ancillary

### Protocol Domains

1. Body care (bedbound patients)
2. Oral care
3. Nutrition and hydration
4. Mobility and transfers
5. Communication (non-verbal, minimal energy)
6. Sensory intolerance management
7. Emotional and existential support
8. Home visit planning (pre-visit tolerance assessment, minimal-stay, stop-signal establishment)

### Limitations

- Practice-oriented consensus, not systematic evidence review
- Primarily experiential knowledge basis
- No formal grading of recommendations
- Austrian healthcare context may limit transferability
- German language — English translation needed for international use
