## Category 1: Novel Hypotheses

### 1.1 IgE Sensitisation to Self-Antigens Drives MC-Mediated CT Degradation

**Mechanistic Rationale:** In a subset of hEDS/MCAS patients, IgE may be directed against self-antigens exposed during mechanical micro-trauma to CT (collagen fragments, tenascin-C, fibronectin neoepitopes). Mechanical joint stress → cryptic epitope exposure → IgE sensitisation → FcεRI crosslinking on perivascular mast cells → mediator release → local CT degradation → more epitope exposure (self-amplifying loop). This creates a positive-feedback mechanism where mechanical load itself perpetuates MC activation via auto-IgE.

**Evidence Link:** Wilson2026 (0.45) showed IgE elevation in high-MC-score hEDS; auto-IgE against self-antigens documented in chronic urticaria and atopic dermatitis (auto-IgE against thyroperoxidase, dsDNA, IL-24); tenascin-C fragments are DAMPs with known MC-activating properties; mechanical injury exposes neoepitopes in skin and joint models.

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.35 — auto-IgE against CT antigens is a novel synthesis; no direct data; supported by analogies in auto-IgE literature.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** hEDS/MCAS patients with elevated IgE will show IgE binding to autologous collagen types I/III/V, tenascin-C, or fibronectin by ELISA or ImmunoCAP. IgE-depleted serum will show reduced MC degranulation when exposed to CT-derived peptides (mast cell line or skin biopsy mast cells). `origin: brainstorm`

### 1.2 IgE → MC → TGF-β Dysregulation → Fibroblast Collagenopathy

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Mast cells are a major source of TGF-β1, which regulates fibroblast collagen synthesis. Chronic MC activation via IgE shifts the TGF-β signalling balance: early degranulation releases TGF-β (pro-fibrotic), but chronic/sustained MC activation drives TGF-β receptor desensitisation in fibroblasts → impaired collagen repair response → net degradation outweighs synthesis. The IgE-FcεRI-mast cell-fibroblast axis represents a paracrine loop where MC mediator profile determines whether CT remodelling is degradative or reparative.

**Evidence Link:** TGF-β1 is stored in MC granules and released upon FcεRI crosslinking (certainty 0.60, general MC biology); TGF-β signalling dysregulation documented in ME/CFS (certainty 0.45-0.55); chronic TGF-β exposure downregulates its own receptors in fibroblasts (certainty 0.65, general cell biology); hEDS fibroblasts show altered collagen synthesis profiles (Mohamed2024, certainty 0.40).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.30 — indirect chain with good individual evidence but no study connecting all nodes.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Fibroblasts from hEDS/MCAS patients co-cultured with autologous IgE-activated mast cells will show reduced collagen synthesis and altered TGF-β receptor expression (phospho-Smad2/3 ratio) compared to fibroblasts from non-MCAS hEDS or healthy controls. `origin: brainstorm`

### 1.3 IgE-Mediated MC Activation as Threshold Modifier for TNXB Haploinsufficiency

**Mechanistic Rationale:** TNXB haploinsufficiency (Imanaka2026, 0.65) reduces tenascin-X in CT, lowering baseline ECM integrity. In individuals with TNXB mutations, IgE-driven MC activation may push CT degradation past a clinical threshold — the genetic susceptibility alone is subclinical, but MC inflammatory load converts subclinical TNXB deficiency into symptomatic hypermobility. This makes the IgE-MC pathway a *modifier* rather than a primary cause, bridging the Imanaka2026 genetic mechanism with the acquired MC hypothesis.

**Evidence Link:** TNXB haploinsufficiency (0.65); MCAS-hEDS comorbidity rate (Wilson2026, 0.45); modifier gene + environmental trigger model in other collagen disorders (e.g. vascular EDS with modifier genes, certainty 0.50); TGF-β pathway links TNXB and MC biology via latent TGF-β binding protein.

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.40 — compelling synthesis of genetic and acquired mechanisms; testable via existing cohorts.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** TNXB mutation carriers with high MC symptom burden will have higher Beighton scores than TNXB carriers without MC symptoms. MC stabiliser treatment will reduce symptom progression in TNXB carriers more than non-carriers. The interaction term (TNXB × MC score) will predict hypermobility severity beyond either factor alone. `origin: brainstorm`

### 1.4 IgE-Positive Perivascular Mast Cells Create a Degradative Niche in Ligament Insertion Zones

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Ligament insertion zones (entheses) are highly vascularised and mechanically stressed — ideal for perivascular mast cell accumulation. IgE-sensitised mast cells at entheses degranulate upon antigen exposure → local MMP activation → peri-entheseal CT degradation → progressive ligament laxity → measurable hypermobility progression. This predicts that hypermobility is *focal* before *generalised* — specific joints (wrist, MCP, ankle) should show degradation before the full Beighton score rises.

**Evidence Link:** Perivascular mast cells are enriched in entheseal tissues (certainty 0.55); entheseal CT is collagen-rich and MMP-sensitive (certainty 0.70); tryptase/chymase activate MMP-1/-3/-13 (Magarinos2013, 0.70; Saarinen1994, 0.75); Wilson2026 IgE elevation in high-MC hEDS (0.45).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.35 — anatomically plausible; enthesis-specific MC-CT studies are sparse; the enthesis as MC effect site in hypermobility is unexplored.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Ultrasound or MRI of wrist/MCP entheses in MCAS-hEDS patients will show increased Doppler signal (MC-driven hyperaemia) and reduced echogenicity (CT degradation) compared to non-MCAS hEDS. Histology of entheseal biopsies will show mast cell hyperplasia with IgE-positive staining. `origin: brainstorm`

## Category 2: Research Directions

### 2.1 Provoked Tryptase Protocol with Beighton Progression Tracking

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Wilson2026 (0.45) found random tryptase normal in high-MC-score hEDS — consistent with known MCAS diagnostic difficulty where provoked/baseline measurements are needed. A standardised provocation protocol (exercise challenge, heat provocation, or allergen challenge) could unmask subtle MC activation that correlates with CT degradation. Longitudinal provoked tryptase + PGD2/LTE4 paired with 6-monthly Beighton scores and US-based CT thickness would directly test the causal chain.

**Evidence Link:** Provoked tryptase is diagnostic standard in MCAS (consensus criteria, certainty 0.65); exercise provocation increases tryptase in some MCAS patients (certainty 0.40); Wilson2026 random tryptase normal (0.45); Magarinos2013 tryptase → MMP-3/-13 (0.70).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.50 — high feasibility; leverages existing MCAS diagnostic protocols; addresses the key gap (random tryptase normal).

**Falsifiable Prediction:** In MCAS-hEDS patients, provoked tryptase (but not baseline) will correlate with 12-month change in Beighton score and with US-measured patellar tendon thickness reduction. Correlation ≥0.4 is considered positive. `origin: brainstorm`

### 2.2 Omalizumab CT Biomarker Trial (Proof-of-Mechanism)

**Mechanistic Rationale:** The strongest test of the IgE → MC → CT hypothesis is an open-label mechanistic trial of omalizumab in hEDS/MCAS patients with elevated IgE and progressive hypermobility. Primary endpoint: change in serum MMP-3, MMP-13 (tryptase-activated) and histaminylation markers (TG2 activity, collagen-bound histamine). Secondary: Beighton score trajectory (12 months), US patellar tendon thickness, POTS symptoms, MC mediator panels. This is the highest-priority experiment because it tests the IgE-specific pathway using an approved drug with established MCAS safety (Matheny2025, 0.55).

**Evidence Link:** Matheny2025 omalizumab safety in MCAS (0.55); Berry2019 low-dose case report (0.40); Magarinos2013 (0.70); Saarinen1994 (0.75); Zhu2026 histaminylation (0.70).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.55 — strongest research direction; safety established; unclear effect size; no CT-specific data.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** 12 weeks of omalizumab will reduce serum MMP-3 by ≥30% and normalise histaminylation markers in ≥50% of IgE-elevated hEDS/MCAS patients vs pre-treatment baseline. Beighton score stability (no progression) at 12 months would be confirmatory. `origin: brainstorm`

## Category 3: Drug/Medication Ideas

### 3.1 Omalizumab as CT-Preserving Therapy in IgE-Elevated hEDS/MCAS

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Omalizumab binds free IgE, blocks FcεRI crosslinking, and downregulates FcεRI on mast cells and basophils. In IgE-elevated hEDS/MCAS patients, this should reduce IgE-driven MC degranulation, decreasing downstream tryptase/chymase/MMP-mediated CT degradation and histaminylation. The established safety profile in MCAS (Matheny2025, 0.55) and 18% complete/61% partial response rate makes this the highest-priority drug candidate for CT outcomes.

**Evidence Link:** Matheny2025 (0.55); Berry2019 (0.40); Zhu2026 (0.70); Magarinos2013 (0.70); Saarinen1994 (0.75); Wilson2026 IgE elevation in hEDS (0.45).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.55 — strongest drug candidate; limiting factor is cost and specialist-only prescribing.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Omalizumab 300-600mg Q2-4W (standard dosing) for 12 months in IgE-elevated hEDS/MCAS patients will stabilise or reduce Beighton score and improve US tendon thickness vs matched untreated controls. `origin: brainstorm`

### 3.2 Ligelizumab (High-Affinity Anti-IgE) for Deeper IgE Sequestration

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Ligelizumab (anti-IgE, Novartis) binds IgE with higher affinity than omalizumab and produces greater suppression of free IgE and FcεRI. If IgE-mediated mast cell activation drives CT degradation, ligelizumab's deeper IgE suppression could produce stronger CT-preserving effects. Currently in Phase 3 for chronic urticaria — an existing safety database supports repurposing.

**Evidence Link:** Ligelizumab superior IgE suppression vs omalizumab in CSU (Phase 2/3 data, certainty 0.65); Matheny2025 omalizumab MCAS (0.55); shared IgE-FcεRI mechanism; ligelizumab safety established in >2000 CSU patients.

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.40 — reasonable extension of omalizumab mechanism; no MCAS-specific data; higher cost barrier.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Ligelizumab 240mg Q4W will suppress free IgE to below the omalizumab-equivalent level and produce greater reduction in serum MMP-3 and histaminylation markers in hEDS/MCAS patients. `origin: brainstorm`

### 3.3 Ketotifen as First-Line MC Stabiliser for CT Preservation

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Ketotifen, a mast cell stabiliser with H1 antihistamine activity, is affordable, well-tolerated, and proposed for CT preservation (Hart2026, 0.55). It reduces MC degranulation regardless of trigger pathway (IgE, MRGPRX2, TLR), making it a broader agent than anti-IgE. If MC → CT degradation is multivalent (not solely IgE-driven), ketotifen may outperform omalizumab for CT preservation.

**Evidence Link:** Hart2026 ketotifen proposal (0.55); MC stabiliser safety in ME/CFS (clinical experience, certainty 0.45); broad MC degranulation inhibition (certainty 0.60, general pharmacology); low cost, oral administration.

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.50 — practical first-line candidate; accessible; broad mechanism; limited CT-specific data.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Ketotifen 1-2mg BID for 6 months in newly-diagnosed MCAS-hEDS patients will reduce rate of Beighton score increase by ≥50% compared to 6-month pre-treatment baseline and reduce serum MMP-9 and tryptase levels. `origin: brainstorm`

### 3.4 Low-Dose Doxycycline + MC Stabiliser Dual Therapy

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Combines two independent mechanisms: MC stabiliser (ketotifen/cromolyn) reduces degranulation and reduces upstream mediator production; subantimicrobial doxycycline (20mg BID) inhibits MMP-2/9/13 directly. This dual blockade should be synergistic — the MC stabiliser reduces the *rate* of new MMP activation while doxycycline inhibits *already-activated* MMP activity already in the CT. The combination could break the established degradation cycle more effectively than either alone.

**Evidence Link:** Doxycycline MMP inhibition in periodontitis/rosacea (certainty 0.75); Magarinos2013 tryptase→MMP (0.70); Saarinen1994 chymase→MMP (0.75); Hart2026 ketotifen proposal (0.55); Hart2026 doxycycline-MMP-CADM1 review (0.50).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.45 — strong mechanistic rationale; both drugs well-tolerated; limited CT-specific combination data.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Ketotifen 2mg BID + doxycycline 20mg BID for 12 weeks in MCAS-hEDS patients will reduce serum MMP-9 and MMP-13 significantly more than either drug alone, with synergy confirmed by interaction term in 2×2 factorial design. `origin: brainstorm`

### 3.5 Vitamin C (High-Dose) as Collagen Synthesis Support in MCAS-Hypermobility

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Ascorbic acid is an essential cofactor for prolyl and lysyl hydroxylases — enzymes critical for collagen crosslinking and tensile strength. MCAS-mediated CT degradation increases collagen turnover, creating increased demand for ascorbic acid. High-dose vitamin C (2-10g/day IV or oral liposomal) could support compensatory collagen synthesis and improve CT quality. Additionally, vitamin C inhibits histamine release from mast cells and degrades histamine directly.

**Evidence Link:** Ascorbate essential for collagen crosslinking (certainty 0.90, foundational biochemistry); vitamin C inhibits MC histamine release in vitro and in vivo (certainty 0.55); anecdotal MCAS improvement on high-dose vitamin C (clinical experience, certainty 0.30); low risk, high accessibility.

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.30 — low risk, plausible synergy with MC-targeted therapy; primary value is as adjunct, not monotherapy.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** High-dose liposomal vitamin C (2g/day) adjunctive to ketotifen will increase serum hydroxyproline (collagen synthesis marker) more than ketotifen alone, and reduce skin extensibility measured by SkinFibrometer at 12 weeks. `origin: brainstorm`

## Category 4: Supplement/Nutraceutical Ideas

### 4.1 Quercetin as Natural MC Stabiliser with Collagen-Protective Effects

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Quercetin, a flavonoid, inhibits mast cell degranulation via multiple pathways (inhibits PLCγ/IP3/calcium mobilisation, stabilises MC membranes, inhibits FcεRI signalling) and inhibits MMP-1/-9 expression. It is a dual-action agent — reducing both MC mediator release and the downstream MMP activity that degrades CT. Well-tolerated, oral bioavailability enhanced by liposomal or phytosomal formulations. Already used empirically in MCAS.

**Evidence Link:** Quercetin inhibits MC degranulation via FcεRI pathway (certainty 0.65, in vitro evidence); quercetin inhibits MMP-1/-9 in fibroblast models (certainty 0.60); MCAS patient community reports (certainty 0.25-0.30); quercetin safety in chronic use (certainty 0.70).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.35 — plausible dual-action supplement; affordable; limited controlled data in MCAS.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Phytosomal quercetin 500mg BID for 8 weeks in MCAS-hEDS patients will reduce serum tryptase and MMP-9 levels and improve subjective joint stability (VAS stability score) vs placebo in a crossover trial. `origin: brainstorm`

### 4.2 Luteolin as MC-Stabilising Collagen Crosslinking Support

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Luteolin, like quercetin, inhibits MC degranulation and additionally inhibits hyaluronidase (which degrades synovial HA, a key joint lubricant and shock absorber). In the context of IgE-driven MC activation, luteolin's dual MC-stabilising and HA-preserving effects could protect both CT integrity and joint function. Liposomal formulations improve bioavailability.

**Evidence Link:** Luteolin inhibits IgE-mediated MC degranulation (certainty 0.60); luteolin inhibits hyaluronidase (certainty 0.55); Guo2021 MC proteases degrade synovial HA (0.50); luteolin used in MCAS protocol (Theoharides, certainty 0.45).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.30 — synergises with quercetin; theoretical HA-preserving benefit is novel.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Luteolin (liposomal 200mg/day) adjunctive to MC stabiliser treatment will reduce synovial HA degradation markers (urinary HA fragments) and improve joint comfort (WOMAC pain subscore) compared to MC stabiliser alone. `origin: brainstorm`

PEA also reduces neurogenic inflammation and has direct analgesic properties, addressing both the MC activation and the joint pain from hypermobility/instability.

**Evidence Link:** PEA inhibits mast cell degranulation in multiple models (certainty 0.65); PEA effective in chronic pain conditions (certainty 0.60, meta-analyses); PEA reduces joint inflammation in OA models (certainty 0.55); excellent safety profile (certainty 0.80).

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Micronised PEA 600mg BID for 12 weeks in MCAS-hEDS patients will reduce serum tryptase and joint pain scores while also reducing MMP-9 levels, with CT integrity (skin echogenicity on US) stable or improved. `origin: brainstorm`

## Category 5: Non-Pharmacological Interventions

### 5.1 MC-Trigger Avoidance Protocol for Hyper-Mobility Preservation

**Mechanistic Rationale:** If IgE-mediated MC activation drives CT degradation, then systematic identification and avoidance of IgE triggers (allergens, foods, environmental) should reduce MC activation burden and slow CT degradation. A structured elimination/reintroduction protocol focusing on common IgE triggers (dust mite, pollens, molds, foods) and non-IgE triggers (exercise, heat, stress, infections) could be tested as a behavioural intervention with measurable CT outcomes.

**Evidence Link:** Trigger avoidance is cornerstone of MCAS management (consensus criteria, certainty 0.60); Wilson2026 IgE elevation in MC-hEDS (0.45); histaminylation from non-IgE histamine release also contributes (Zhu2026, 0.70).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.35 — low risk, clinically pragmatic; feasibility high; evidence is indirect (general MCAS trigger management extrapolated to CT outcomes).

**Falsifiable Prediction:** MCAS-hEDS patients completing a 6-week structured trigger elimination protocol (low histamine diet + environmental allergen reduction + stress management) will show reduced provoked tryptase levels and stable Beighton scores, while a matched control group without trigger management will show Beighton progression. `origin: brainstorm`

### 5.2 Graded Joint Stabilisation Physical Therapy in MCAS-Driven Hypermobility

**Mechanistic Rationale:** In MCAS-driven CT degradation, joints become progressively lax, leading to recurrent microtrauma → DAMPs released → further MC activation (extending the positive-feedback loop). A graded joint stabilisation programme using isometric strengthening (not high-range eccentric loading, which aggravates MC degranulation via mechanoactivation) could break the trauma → inflammation → degradation cycle. Exercise type and intensity may need to be calibrated below the MC degranulation threshold for each patient.

**Evidence Link:** Isometric exercise reduces systemic inflammation markers in some populations (certainty 0.50); eccentric exercise activates MC degranulation in tendinopathy models (certainty 0.45); MC degranulation occurs in response to mechanical stress of tendon (certainty 0.55).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.30 — plausible but speculative; no data on exercise type-specific MC effects in hypermobility; important theoretical framework for clinical practice.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** MCAS-hEDS patients assigned to isometric joint stabilisation PT will show reduced serum tryptase after exercise sessions compared to eccentric-loading PT, and slower Beighton score progression over 12 months. `origin: brainstorm`

### 5.3 Thermal MC Modulation (Contrast Hydrotherapy)

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Mast cells are temperature-sensitive: heat (≥40°C) can degranulate MCs via TRPV2/TRPV4, while cold suppresses degranulation. Contrast hydrotherapy (alternating warm/cold) could suppress MC degranulation through cold-mediated inhibition while improving blood flow through reactive hyperaemia during warm phases. This may modulate perivascular MC activation in CT and joint tissues. Cold exposure specifically reduces histamine release from MCs.

**Evidence Link:** Heat activates MC degranulation via TRPV2/TRPV4 (certainty 0.60); cold suppresses MC activation and histamine release (certainty 0.55); contrast hydrotherapy used in MCAS management empirically (clinical experience, certainty 0.30).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.20 — very low; mechanistic plausibility but no CT-outcome data; highly accessible and low risk.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** MCAS-hEDS patients performing daily cold-water (15-20°C, 3 min) limb immersion for 8 weeks will show reduced baseline serum tryptase levels and improved subjective joint stability vs warm-water immersion or no treatment. `origin: brainstorm`

## Category 7: Mathematical Model Extensions

### 7.1 Two-Compartment ODE for MC-Mediated Acquired Hypermobility

**Mechanistic Rationale:** An ODE model with two compartments — MC mediator concentration in tissue and CT structural integrity — can model the acquired hypermobility trajectory. Key variables: MC degranulation rate (driven by IgE, modified by triggers→_k_a_), MMP activation rate (_k_m_), CT degradation rate (_k_d_), CT repair rate (_k_r_ — includes TGF-β signalling, collagen synthesis, mechanical load). The model predicts distinct progression regimes: slow-degradation (repair keeps pace), threshold-crossing (trigger accumulation overwhelms repair), and accelerated-degradation (degradation releases DAMPs → further MC activation).

**Evidence Link:** Tryptase→MMP kinetics (Magarinos2013, 0.70); Saarinen1994 chymase (0.75); Janicki2006 cardiac MC-MMP (0.70); histaminylation as additional CT degradation rate (Zhu2026, 0.70); DAMP-MC positive feedback (conceptual, certainty 0.40).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.35 — mathematically tractable; parameters estimable from existing literature; could generate testable predictions about progression rates and treatment thresholds.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** The ODE model predicts that MC degranulation rate must exceed a threshold of ~1.5× baseline for sustained CT degradation (Beighton increase ≥1 point/year). Intervention efficacy in the model is strongly threshold-dependent: a 50% reduction in degranulation rate slows progression only if it crosses below this threshold, explaining partial vs full responses to treatment. `origin: brainstorm`

### 7.2 IgE-MC-CT Causal DAG Extension

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Extend the ME/CFS causal DAG with IgE → MC activation → CT degradation nodes, adding the IgE → MC edge (with FcεRI crosslinking as mediator), MC → CT edge (mediated by tryptase, chymase, histaminylation), and CT → hypermobility edge. Include bidirectional edges between CT degradation and mechanical load (more load → more degradation via DAMP→MC feedback), and CT degradation → POTS worsening (via reduced vessel CT integrity → reduced vasoconstriction). The DAG formalises what must be conditioned on to estimate the IgE→CT causal effect.

**Evidence Link:** Causal DAG modelling methodology (certainty 0.85); IgE→MC established (0.90); MC→MMP activation (0.70); MMP→CT degradation (0.70); CT→POTS via vascular compliance (0.50); DAMP→MC feedback (0.40).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.45 — formalises the hypothesis space; identifies minimal adjustment sets for causal inference in observational studies.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** In the DAG, the minimal sufficient adjustment set for estimating IgE→CT degradation includes: MRGPRX2 activation, total MC burden, tryptase levels, and prior CT injury. Studies that do not adjust for MRGPRX2 activation cannot estimate the IgE-specific effect — this predicts why Wilson2026 found only modest IgE associations. `origin: brainstorm`

### 7.3 Agent-Based Model of Entheseal Mast Cell Niche Dynamics

**Mechanistic Rationale:** An agent-based model where individual mast cells in an enthesis region respond to local IgE concentration, mechanical stress, and DAMP signals. Each agent (MC) has a degranulation threshold determined by local IgE density (FcεRI occupancy), and degranulation releases mediators that degrade local ECM in a radius-dependent manner. The model predicts spatial patterns of CT degradation — focal hotspots around perivascular MC clusters rather than diffuse degradation.

**Evidence Link:** Perivascular MC distribution at entheses (certainty 0.55); focal degradation pattern in tendinopathy (certainty 0.60); IgE density determines MC activation probability (certainty 0.75); spatial modelling methodology (certainty 0.80).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.30 — methodologically plausible; parameterisation requires enthesis-specific data that is sparse; useful for hypothesis generation about spatial progression patterns.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** The ABM predicts that CT degradation in MCAS-hEDS occurs in discrete foci around perivascular MC clusters, not homogeneously. This predicts patchy CT thinning on high-resolution US, with focal areas of reduced echogenicity corresponding to regions with highest MC density. `origin: brainstorm`

## Category 8: Cross-Disease Bridges

### 8.1 Chronic Urticaria as Model for IgE-Mediated CT Pathology

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Chronic spontaneous urticaria (CSU) is driven by IgE-mediated MC degranulation in skin — the same IgE-FcεRI-MC axis posited for CT degradation. CSU patients show elevated serum MMP-9 and reduced skin collagen density — analogous to proposed CT degradation in hEDS. If CSU patients have measurable CT changes (skin extensibility, joint hypermobility, serum MMP/ECM markers) that correlate with disease activity, this would provide cross-disease evidence for IgE → CT pathology without waiting for hEDS-specific studies.

**Evidence Link:** CSU driven by IgE/FcεRI (certainty 0.85); CSU patients have elevated MMP-9 (certainty 0.60); reduced skin collagen density in CSU (certainty 0.50); omalizumab is first-line for CSU (certainty 0.80); Wilson2026 IgE-CT link (0.45).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.45 — strong cross-disease bridge; existing CSU cohorts could be analysed retrospectively for CT outcomes at low cost.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** A cross-sectional study of CSU patients (n=100) will show higher Beighton scores and reduced skin echogenicity on US compared to age/sex-matched controls. Beighton score and skin measures will correlate with CSU disease activity (UCT score) and serum MMP-9 levels. `origin: brainstorm`

### 8.2 Eosinophilic Esophagitis as Model for Allergic CT Remodelling

**Mechanistic Rationale:** EoE is driven by IgE-mediated allergic inflammation (type 2 immunity) leading to oesophageal CT remodelling — fibrosis and stricture from chronic allergic inflammation. This demonstrates that IgE-mediated allergic responses _can_ drive CT pathology, establishing precedent. While the outcome in EoE is fibrosis rather than degradation, the mechanism (allergen→IgE→MC activation→cytokines→fibroblast/ECM changes) shares the initial steps. The _direction_ of CT remodelling (fibrotic vs degradative) may depend on MC mediator balance and tissue context.

**Evidence Link:** EoE ECM remodelling is IgE-associated (certainty 0.65); EoE involves MC activation (certainty 0.60); MC mediator profiles differ by tissue (certainty 0.55); different ECM outcome from same initial pathway supports context-dependent effects (certainty 0.40).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.35 — establishes precedent for IgE→CT remodelling; highlights context-dependence of outcome.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** MC mediator profiles differ between EoE (predominantly TGF-β, IL-13 → fibrosis) and MCAS-hEDS (predominantly tryptase/chymase/histamine → degradation), demonstrating that MC phenotype rather than IgE presence determines CT outcome. `origin: brainstorm`

### 8.3 Periodontitis as Model for IgE-Mediated Connective Tissue Degradation

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Periodontitis is the best-established model of IgE-mediated CT degradation in humans. IgE against oral bacteria (e.g. _P. gingivalis_) drives local MC activation → MMP-mediated degradation of periodontal ligament and alveolar bone → tooth loss. The parallel is direct: IgE → MC → MMP → CT degradation, with measurable CT outcomes (clinical attachment loss, bone height). Periodontitis treatments targeting the IgE-MC-MMP axis (including subantimicrobial doxycycline) are already in use. This establishes precedent for therapeutic repurposing.

**Evidence Link:** IgE against oral bacteria in periodontitis (certainty 0.65); MC activation degrades periodontal collagen via MMPs (certainty 0.70); subantimicrobial doxycycline preserves periodontal attachment (certainty 0.80); elevated periodontal IgE predicts tooth loss (certainty 0.60).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.55 — strongest cross-disease bridge; parallel mechanisms are well-established; existing treatments validate the approach.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** MCAS-hEDS patients will have elevated IgE to common periodontal pathogens compared to non-MCAS hEDS patients. If confirmed, this model predicts that periodontal treatment (scaling + subantimicrobial doxycycline) will be associated with reduced serum MMP-9 and slower Beighton score progression. `origin: brainstorm`

### 8.4 Cardiac ECM Remodelling in Mast Cell-Driven Heart Failure

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Janicki2006 (0.70) established that cardiac mast cells regulate MMP-mediated collagen degradation in ventricular remodelling. This is the most directly analogous established pathway — cardiac ECM degradation via MC → MMP in heart failure is a well-replicated model. The same mediators (tryptase, chymase, TNF-α) that degrade cardiac ECM likely degrade ligamentous ECM. Extending this to joint CT is a tissue-specific translation of a conserved pathological mechanism.

**Evidence Link:** Janicki2006 (0.70); additional replication in cardiac MC-MMP (certainty 0.65); conserved ECM proteins across cardiac and ligamentous tissues (collagen I/III, fibronectin, proteoglycans); MC protease targets are conserved (certainty 0.75).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.60 — highest-certainty cross-disease bridge; direct mechanistic analogy; conserved biology.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Serum MMP-3, MMP-9, and TIMP levels in MCAS-hEDS patients will fall within the same range as heart failure patients with elevated cardiac MC activation, after adjusting for age and sex. Ligament biopsy MMP expression will mirror cardiac MMP profiles from heart failure studies. `origin: brainstorm`

## Category 9: Diagnostic/Biomarker Ideas

### 9.1 IgE-to-Tryptase Ratio as CT Degradation Risk Biomarker

**Mechanistic Rationale:** If IgE-mediated activation drives the CT-degradative mast cell phenotype, then the ratio of IgE (indicating pathway engagement) to tryptase (constitutive MC burden) may predict CT degradation risk better than either alone. A high IgE/low tryptase ratio would indicate IgE-driven MC activation without constitutive HaT confound — distinguishing the acquired mechanism from the HaT-elevated but non-hypermobile group (Vazquez2022, 0.70). This addresses the key null finding: HaT does not cause hypermobility.

**Evidence Link:** Wilson2026 IgE elevated in MC-hEDS (0.45); Vazquez2022 HaT null finding (0.70); IgE-to-tryptase ratio distinguishes different MC activation patterns in asthma and urticaria (certainty 0.55); ratio biomarkers reduce confound variance (methodology, certainty 0.70).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.40 — simple, testable ratio; could be validated retrospectively in Wilson2026 or similar cohorts.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** The IgE-to-tryptase ratio will predict Beighton score ≥5/9 (OR ≥2.0) in MCAS patients, independent of age, sex, and BMI. The ratio will outperform IgE alone and tryptase alone in logistic regression. C-statistic improvement ≥0.10 over individual markers. `origin: brainstorm`

### 9.2 Serum Histaminylation-Adduct Panel

**Mechanistic Rationale:** If histaminylation directly alters collagen mechanics (Zhu2026, 0.70) in MCAS-hEDS, then serum or urine levels of histaminylated collagen fragments may serve as a biomarker of active CT degradation via this MMP-independent pathway. A proteomics panel measuring histaminyl-glutamine on collagen fragments could distinguish MC-mediated CT degradation from mechanical-only CT injury.

**Evidence Link:** Zhu2026 histaminylation mechanism (0.70); histaminylated proteins detected in plasma in inflammatory conditions (certainty 0.50); collagen fragments as CT degradation biomarkers in arthritis (certainty 0.65).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.35 — novel biomarker concept; requires mass spectrometry method development; high specificity but uncertain clinical utility.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Serum histaminylated collagen peptides will be elevated in MCAS-hEDS patients vs non-MCAS hEDS and healthy controls, correlate with provoked tryptase (rho≥0.4), and decrease with MC stabiliser therapy. Receiver-operator AUC ≥0.75 for distinguishing MCAS-hEDS from non-MCAS hEDS. `origin: brainstorm`

### 9.3 Ligament US Elastography as CT Integrity Biomarker

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Ultrasonic elastography measures tissue stiffness non-invasively. If MC-driven CT degradation reduces ligament stiffness, shear-wave elastography of the patellar tendon (accessible, standardised) could serve as a quantitative biomarker of CT integrity. Serial measurements over time could track degradation progression and response to MC-targeted therapy — addressing the lack of objective CT degradation metrics.

**Evidence Link:** Elastography validated for tendinopathy assessment (certainty 0.75); shear-wave velocity correlates with collagen content (certainty 0.65); patellar tendon is accessible and standardisable (certainty 0.80); US is low-burden, suitable for ME/CFS patients.

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.40 — practical, non-invasive, repeatable; requires validation studies in MCAS-hEDS; high patient acceptability.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Patellar tendon shear-wave velocity will be lower in MCAS-hEDS patients vs age/sex-matched healthy controls, correlate inversely with Beighton score (rho ≤ -0.4), and increase after 6 months of MC stabiliser therapy (by ≥0.5 m/s). `origin: brainstorm`

### 9.4 Provoked-Tryptase Predictive Nomogram for CT Progression

**Mechanistic Rationale:** A clinical nomogram combining provoked tryptase (peak-post-provocation), IgE, Beighton score, age, and MC trigger count could predict individualised 12-month CT progression risk. The nomogram would operationalise the causal chain: if MC activation drives CT degradation, then provoked tryptase should be the strongest predictor of progression, and its predictive value should exceed baseline unprovoked markers.

**Evidence Link:** Provoked tryptase is MCAS diagnostic standard (certainty 0.65); Wilson2026 found random tryptase normal (0.45); Vazquez2022 HaT null (0.70); nomogram methodology in clinical prediction (certainty 0.80).

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.35 — requires prospective development cohort; could be derived retrospectively from existing MCAS registries.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** Nomogram including provoked tryptase will predict 12-month Beighton score increase (AUC ≥0.80 in derivation, ≥0.70 in validation). Provoked tryptase will account for ≥30% of model R², confirming MC activation as the primary CT progression driver. `origin: brainstorm`

### 9.5 Skin Collagen Histaminylation Immunofluorescence in MCAS-hEDS

**Mechanistic Rationale:** If histaminylation is a key MC-mediated CT degradation mechanism (Zhu2026, 0.70), skin punch biopsy with immunofluorescence staining for histaminyl-glutamine adducts could serve as a tissue-based diagnostic. Positive staining in dermal collagen of MCAS-hEDS patients but not in non-MCAS hEDS or healthy controls would provide direct tissue evidence linking MC histamine release to CT modification.

## Category 10: Reasons This Mechanism May NOT Be Relevant to ME/CFS

### 10.1 ME/CFS Pathophysiology May Be Independent of Connective Tissue Degradation

**Mechanistic Rationale:** ME/CFS core features — PEM, unrefreshing sleep, orthostatic intolerance, cognitive dysfunction — can be explained by immunological, neurological, and metabolic mechanisms _without invoking any CT contribution_. The MCAS-hEDS-POTS triad is a comorbid cluster that may represent a _separate condition_ (hypermobility spectrum disorder with autonomic dysfunction) that happens to overlap with ME/CFS epidemiologically. The CT degradation mechanism would explain hypermobility and POTS worsening via reduced vascular CT compliance, but would not explain PEM, cognitive dysfunction, or pain — the defining ME/CFS symptoms.

**Evidence Link:** Wilson2026 (0.45): 25% of hEDS/HSD patients have high MC score — meaning 75% do NOT. Even in the high-MC group, the relationship to ME/CFS-specific symptoms (PEM, cognitive dysfunction) was not established. The MCAS-hEDS-POTS triad may be a distinct clinical entity that overlaps with ME/CFS but is not causal for it. No study demonstrates that CT integrity correlates with PEM severity, the defining ME/CFS feature.

**Alternative Explanation:** The MCAS-hEDS-POTS triad is an independent comorbidity that amplifies ME/CFS symptom burden (via pain, POTS, sleep disruption) but is not part of ME/CFS pathophysiology. Treating it may improve quality of life without addressing ME/CFS core mechanisms.

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.45 — substantial possibility that this is a comorbidity, not a mechanism.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** If CT degradation is relevant to ME/CFS core features, then CT integrity measures (Beighton, skin US, tendon stiffness) should correlate with PEM severity and cognitive dysfunction, not just with POTS or pain scores. If CT measures correlate only with POTS/pain and not with PEM, this supports the comorbidity model over the mechanism model. `origin: brainstorm`

---

### 10.2 The Dominant MC Activation Pathway in ME/CFS May Be Non-IgE

**Mechanistic Rationale:** The IgE-FcεRI pathway is one of many MC activation triggers. In ME/CFS, the dominant MC activation may be via MRGPRX2 (substance P, VIP, neuropeptides), TLRs (LPS, viral RNA), complement (C3a, C5a), or cytokine receptors (IL-33, TSLP) — which would render IgE elevation an _epiphenomenon_ of a broader MC activation state rather than a specific pathway. Wilson2026's IgE finding (p=0.0004) is statistically modest in a cohort of 2141 — the effect size may be small.

**Evidence Link:** Wilson2026 (0.45): IgE elevation significant but modest effect; MRGPRX2 is the dominant non-IgE MC activator in MCAS literature (certainty 0.60); neuropeptide levels elevated in ME/CFS (certainty 0.50); LPS/TLR4 activates MCs directly (certainty 0.70); IgE may be elevated as bystander type 2 inflammation rather than causal trigger.

**Alternative Explanation:** IgE elevation in MCAS-hEDS is a general marker of type 2 immune skewing (atopy, allergy, parasite defence) that correlates with MC burden but is not causally specific to CT degradation. The actual CT-degrading mediators (tryptase, chymase, histamine) can be released by any MC trigger, not just IgE.

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.50 — significant challenge to the IgE-specific hypothesis. The field may need to focus on MC _mediators_ rather than MC _triggers_.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** In hEDS/MCAS patients, provoked tryptase levels (total MC degranulation) will predict CT progression better than IgE levels (specific pathway engagement). If IgE pathway engagement explains ≤30% of provoked tryptase variance, the IgE-specific hypothesis is weakened. `origin: brainstorm`

---

### 10.3 CT Degradation in ME/CFS May Be Non-MC Mediated

**Mechanistic Rationale:** Acquired CT degradation could be caused by entirely non-MC mechanisms: (1) reduced collagen synthesis from metabolic insufficiency (mitochondrial dysfunction → reduced ATP for collagen production); (2) systemic inflammation activating MMPs independently of mast cells (neutrophil-derived MMP-8/9, macrophage-derived MMP-12); (3) corticosteroid-induced CT degradation (HPA axis dysfunction → elevated cortisol → skin thinning, CT weakening); (4) mechanical unloading in severely deconditioned patients (bedrest → CT atrophy). All of these are present in ME/CFS and could explain CT changes without requiring a mast cell intermediary.

**Evidence Link:** Metabolic insufficiency in ME/CFS (certainty 0.70); systemic inflammation elevates non-MC MMPs (certainty 0.75); HPA axis dysfunction in ME/CFS (certainty 0.55); mechanical unloading → CT remodelling (certainty 0.70); no study has attributed CT changes specifically to MC mediators in ME/CFS.

**Alternative Explanation:** The CT degradation observed in ME/CFS patients (increased skin extensibility, joint laxity progression) is a multifactorial consequence of metabolic, inflammatory, endocrine, and mechanical factors — mast cells are one contributor among many, possibly minor.

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.55 — substantial possibility; non-MC mechanisms are well-documented in other contexts.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** If MC mediators are the dominant CT degradation driver, then MC stabilisation should reduce CT degradation markers (MMPs, collagen fragments) by ≥50% and slow hypermobility progression. If non-MC mechanisms dominate, MC stabilisation will show <30% reduction. `origin: brainstorm`

---

### 10.4 Reverse Causality: CT Weakness Causes MC Activation, Not Vice Versa

**Mechanistic Rationale:** The causal direction may be opposite to the proposed mechanism. Inherent CT weakness (genetic, e.g. TNXB haploinsufficiency or as-yet-unidentified hEDS genes) → joint instability → recurrent microtrauma → DAMP release → local MC activation. The MC activation observed in hEDS is a _consequence_ of CT weakness, not its cause. This would explain the MCAS-hEDS association but reverse the therapeutic implication: MC stabilisers would treat symptoms but not CT progression.

**Evidence Link:** TNXB haploinsufficiency → hypermobility independent of MC (Imanaka2026, 0.65); mechanical trauma activates local MCs in tendinopathy (certainty 0.60); DAMP receptors (TLR2, TLR4) on MCs respond to CT breakdown products (certainty 0.55); Wilson2026 75% of hEDS did NOT have high MC score.

**Alternative Explanation:** The MCAS-hEDS relationship is bidirectional: early CT weakness → microtrauma → MC activation → more CT degradation → a self-amplifying loop regardless of initial cause. The initial trigger could be genetic (TNXB), infectious (MC activation), or mechanical (injury), with the loop propagating once established.

**Preliminary Certainty:** 0.40 — reverse causality is a legitimate alternative that would change treatment strategy fundamentally.

**Falsifiable Prediction:** If reverse causality dominates, then MC stabiliser therapy will reduce MC mediator levels and MC symptoms but will NOT slow CT degradation progression (Beighton, skin US) — joint symptoms improve but CT structure continues to decline. This would be a critical trial result. `origin: brainstorm`

## Category 11: Null Hypothesis Assessment

### 11.1 What If IgE-Mediated MC Activation Does Not Contribute to CT Degradation?

**Consequence for the paper:** A substantial section of ch07, ch14d, and their sub-sections would need revision or removal. The `spec:histaminylation-collagen` (cert 0.30), `spec:acquired-progressive-hypermobility-mc` (cert 0.35), and `oq:ige-pathway-ct-mcas` environments would be invalidated. The MC mediator → CT degradation mechanism would be restricted to its well-established contexts (cardiac ECM, cartilage, periodontium) without extension to systemic CT in ME/CFS.

**Claims requiring revision:**
- Histaminylation mechanism exists (Zhu2026, cert 0.70) — this is robust and would stand, but its relevance to ME/CFS clinical CT would be unsupported.
- Acquired progressive hypermobility via MC (spec cert 0.35) — would default to `#limitation` or removal.
- IgE elevation as CT risk marker (oq cert n/a) — would revert to `#open-question` or `#limitation`.
- Any treatment recommendation for MC stabilisers/anti-IgE for CT preservation — would become unsupported.

**Alternative explanation that fits the evidence better if null is true:**
1. **TNXB haploinsufficiency** for the minority (5-10%): genetic, non-MC (Imanaka2026, 0.65).
2. **Multifactorial CT degradation from metabolic + mechanical + endocrine causes** (Section 10.3). No single driver dominates — ME/CFS' diverse pathophysiology produces CT changes through multiple convergent pathways.
3. **MCAS and hEDS are independent comorbid conditions** that share no causal relationship but co-occur via a shared upstream mechanism (e.g. type 2 immune skewing, autoantibody predisposition, neuropeptide dysregulation) affecting both MC behaviour and CT integrity independently.

**What the paper would lose if null is accepted:**
- A mechanistic explanation for the MCAS-hEDS-POTS triad that connects them _causally_ rather than just epidemiologically
- A treatment target (IgE-MC) for CT preservation in ME/CFS
- A unifying mechanism for acquired hypermobility in post-infectious ME/CFS
- The histaminylation-ME/CFS clinical link (novelty value)

**What the paper would gain if null is accepted:**
- Accurate representation of the evidence boundary (strong mechanistic components, weak integration)
- Prevents premature MC treatment claims for CT outcomes
- Redirects research effort to non-MC CT degradation mechanisms

**Preliminary Certainty of Null:** 0.35 — the component mechanisms are too well-established to dismiss entirely, but the clinical chain is genuinely unproven. `origin: brainstorm`

---

### 11.2 What If Omalizumab Trials Fail for CT Outcomes?

**Consequence for the paper:** Omalizumab is the most direct therapeutic probe for the IgE→MC→CT hypothesis. A negative trial — effective IgE suppression without CT preservation (stable Beighton, unchanged US CT thickness) — would strongly disconfirm the IgE-specific pathway. However, it would NOT disconfirm the broader MC→CT hypothesis (which could operate via MRGPRX2, TLR, or complement-mediated MC activation unaffected by omalizumab).

**Claims requiring revision:**
- Any implication that anti-IgE is a CT-preserving therapy for ME/CFS → `#limitation` and restricted to symptom-only use.
- The `oq:ige-pathway-ct-mcas` would close with a negative result — the open question would be resolved.
- `spec:acquired-progressive-hypermobility-mc` would remain viable but via non-IgE pathways.
- The broader MC→CT framework would survive this specific disconfirmation.

**Relevant papers that would need re-evaluation:**
- Matheny2025 (0.55) omalizumab MCAS review — still valid for symptom outcomes, not CT.
- Wilson2026 IgE elevation (0.45) — would become an epiphenomenon, not causal.
- The periodontitis model (Section 8.3) and cardiac ECM model (Section 8.4) would remain as evidence that MC→CT degradation is valid, just not IgE-driven in ME/CFS.

**Preliminary Certainty of Null:** 0.40 — omalizumab is the most direct test; failure would be informative regardless of outcome. `origin: brainstorm`

---

### 11.3 What If Histaminylation Is Not Clinically Relevant in CT?

**Consequence for the paper:** Zhu2026 (0.70) demonstrated histaminylation alters collagen mechanics in vitro. If in vivo histaminylation levels are too low to affect CT integrity — or if TG2 activity is limiting in ligamentous CT — then this elegant mechanism would be mechanistically valid but clinically irrelevant. The `spec:histaminylation-collagen` environment would need downgrading.

**Alternative explanation:** Histaminylation may be a clearance/pathway (histamine sink) rather than a damage mechanism. TG2-mediated histaminylation could be a protective adaptation — sequestering excess histamine onto inert collagen rather than allowing it to bind MC receptors. The altered mechanics (Zhu2026) may be an incidental consequence of this protective sequestration.

**Preliminary Certainty of Null:** 0.30 — Zhu2026 data is strong in vitro; clinical relevance is the open question. `origin: brainstorm`

## Category 12: Evidence Quality Concerns

### 12.1 Key Evidence Is Retrospective Cohort with Preprint-Level Certainty

Wilson2026 (n=2141 hEDS/HSD) is the largest dataset linking MC symptoms to IgE in hEDS but remains a preprint (medRxiv, 2026). Prospective replication with pre-registered Beighton scoring and standardised MC mediator measurement is needed. The IgE p-value (0.0004) in a cohort of 2141 suggests modest effect size — statistical significance at this sample size is expected even for very small effects. The absolute difference in IgE between high and low MC groups should be examined before overinterpreting the association.

**Impact:** The single strongest clinical link for IgE→MC→CT rests on a preprint with possible selection bias (online survey sample, volunteer population). `origin: brainstorm`

---

### 12.2 No Direct Ligament/Enthesis MC Data in Any Included Paper

None of the 14 papers examine mast cell density, mediator levels, or MC activation state specifically in ligamentous tissue or entheses from hEDS/MCAS patients. The entire mechanistic chain extrapolates from cardiac ECM (Janicki2006), cartilage (Magarinos2013), bone (Lind2022), and skin (Zhu2026, CSU models) to joint CT — four distinct tissue types with different ECM composition, MC density, and mechanical environment. Tissue specificity is a genuine concern.

**Impact:** The causal chain is assembled from different tissues, not demonstrated in a single tissue type. Ligament-specific replication is the most important gap. `origin: brainstorm`

---

### 12.3 Effect Size of MC Contribution to CT Degradation Is Unknown

Even if the mechanism operates, its _relative contribution_ to CT degradation in ME/CFS is entirely unknown. The MC pathway could account for 5% of CT variation (trivial) or 50% (major). Without knowing effect size, therapeutic targeting may be inefficient. The clinical significance hinges on effect size, not just mechanism existence. No included study provides a metric (R², Cohen's d, OR) for MC contribution to CT outcomes in ME/CFS.

**Impact:** The paper should not imply that MC→CT is a major mechanism without effect size estimates. Framing should reflect magnitude uncertainty. `origin: brainstorm`

---

### 12.4 Haemodynamic Confound in IgE-EDS Association

Wilson2026 IgE elevation in hEDS may partially reflect haemoconcentration from POTS-related plasma volume depletion, not true increased IgE production. POTS patients have reduced plasma volume, concentrating all serum proteins including IgE. This confound could produce an artifactual IgE-hEDS association. The HaT null finding (Vazquez2022, cert 0.70) adds weight: if tryptase (stable, primarily MC-derived) is not elevated but IgE (heritable, environmentally reactive) is, the association may reflect environmental exposure patterns or haemoconcentration rather than MC biology.

**Impact:** Before IgE elevation is interpreted as MC-specific, plasma volume correction should be applied. This methodological detail could substantially change the Wilson2026 interpretation. `origin: brainstorm`

---

### 12.5 Publication Bias Favours Positive MC-CT Findings

The MC→CT literature is dominated by mechanistic studies designed to demonstrate MC effects (positive result expected). Negative studies — where MC mediator modulation did NOT change CT outcomes — may exist in the file drawer. For example, MC stabilisation trials in tendinopathy or wound healing that showed no CT benefit may not have been published. The absence of negative results in the literature inflates apparent certainty.

**Impact:** The field lacks a registered negative study. The apparent consistency of MC→CT evidence may partly reflect publication bias. `origin: brainstorm`
