## 1. Evidence Landscape

- **Strong (≥0.70):** oh2024incident (n=836k, infection→incident allergic disease), plattsmills2025immunology / wilson2024ticksalpha / vanrunen2015tickinduced (alpha-gal: tick→antigen-specific IgE proven), theoharides1995stressinduced / lakatos2025bidirectional (mast-cell→BBB→microglia→neuroinflammation), clarion2026postcovid.
- **Medium (0.40–0.69):** rocha2016histamine, meltendorf2022iligespike, tan2022hypersensitivity, portilho2024igeanti, kowal2002prevalence, reparramirez2001igelevels, skowera2004type2, caberascruz2021tickhuman, filippatos2025immunological, gimenezorenga2025blood.
- **Weak (<0.40):** korner2023atopy (0.40 boundary).
- **NULL (replicated):** reparramirez2001igelevels + kowal2002prevalence — total IgE and allergen-specific IgE NORMAL in ME/CFS.
- **Indirect biochemical links:** Strong but animal/in-vitro. histamine→H1R→microglia (rocha2016histamine); CRH→mast cell degranulation (theoharides1995stressinduced); mast-cell→BBB/tryptase/PAR2→microglia (lakatos2025bidirectional). Logic: IgE-MC activation releases histamine/tryptase/TNF/CRH-amplified mediators → searched those in neuroinflammation → established pathway, but not IgE-specific in ME/CFS.

## 2. Internal Contradictions

**One tension (resolvable, not fundamental):** LinkedIn hypothesis = IgE-mediated activation. Existing paper coverage + skowera2004type2 (Th2 bias WITHOUT IgE elevation) + 2 nulls → non-IgE mast-cell activation is the dominant ME/CFS paradigm (theoharides2019, pre-existing). Resolution: both can hold — a minority IgE-sensitized post-infectious subset within a predominantly non-IgE picture. Certainty gap is large (nulls 0.60–0.65 vs untested IgE-subset ~0.20) → weight toward null/non-IgE; present IgE-subset as speculation/open-question. Framing decided HERE for Phase 3.

## 3. Evidence Summary

Infection CAN induce antigen-specific IgE (spike in acute COVID; alpha-gal from ticks) and raises incident allergic disease at population scale. Mast-cell mediators CAN drive BBB-adjacent neuroinflammation affecting cognition. BUT in ME/CFS specifically, total + allergen-specific IgE are normal (replicated null), and anti-spike/anti-tick IgE has never been measured in ME/CFS. Strongest supportable claim: infection-triggered allergic sensitization is real and *could* identify an IgE-sensitized subset — unvalidated in ME/CFS. Must remain speculative. Evidence does NOT support general IgE elevation in ME/CFS.
**Primary targets:** ch13/ch07 (immune), ch08 (neuro), ch14d (cross-disease).

## 4. Clinical Relevance

**LOW** — research-only. No actionable patient-care implication. Proposed antigen-specific IgE testing in ME/CFS is a research direction, not clinical advice. **Subset: unknown** (hypothesized post-infectious subset only).
