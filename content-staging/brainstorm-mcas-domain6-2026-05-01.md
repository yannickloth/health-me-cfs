## TIER 1 — WELL-SUPPORTED (integration priority: HIGH)

### 1.1 Hypothesis: Carotid Body Mast Cell Sensitization as the Hidden Driver of Orthostatic Intolerance in ME/CFS

- **Category:** Novel hypothesis
- **Certainty:** 0.55
- **Novelty:** NOVEL extension of Theoharides 2024
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Mast cells cluster at the carotid body glomus and release histamine/tryptase/VEGF in response to CRH (stress) and hypoxia. Carotid body chemoreceptors are exquisitely sensitive to local mediators; degranulation lowers chemoreceptor activation threshold, producing inappropriate sympathetic outflow on orthostatic challenge. The 89% OI rate in MCAS+ME/CFS (vs 72% in MCAS-) is explained not by autonomic neuropathy but by *peripheral chemoreceptor sensitization*.
- **Evidence link:** Rohrhofer2025; Theoharides2024ANS; known carotid body mast cell density (Heath 1987 anatomical literature).
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Patients with MCAS+OI will show exaggerated ventilatory and sympathetic responses to mild hypercapnia/hypoxia versus MCAS-only or OI-only controls; cromolyn pre-treatment will normalize this response within 2 weeks.

### 1.2 Hypothesis: Hypothalamic Mast Cells as the "Master Switch" for Sickness-Behavior Persistence

- **Category:** Novel hypothesis
- **Certainty:** 0.50
- **Novelty:** NOVEL — distinct from existing CADM1 neural gain hypothesis (which is generic CNS); this is region-specific
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Median eminence and paraventricular nucleus contain dense mast cell populations outside the BBB. CRH-driven mast cell activation here produces local IL-6/TNF/histamine that disrupts HPA rhythm, leptin signaling, and TRH release — generating fatigue, anhedonia, thermal dysregulation, and weight changes characteristic of ME/CFS. This explains why peripheral immune findings are inconsistent: the critical activation is *hypothalamic*, invisible to blood draws.
- **Evidence link:** Theoharides2024ANS (hypothalamic mast cells); known CRH-mast cell autocrine loop.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** CSF tryptase and histamine metabolites (tele-methylhistamine) will be elevated in ME/CFS vs healthy controls even when serum is normal; intranasal cromolyn (which reaches median eminence via nose-to-brain transport) will reduce sickness-behavior scores within 4 weeks.

### 1.3 Drug Repurposing: Low-Dose Ketotifen + Famotidine Stack as First-Line Domain 6 Therapy

- **Category:** Drug/medication
- **Certainty:** 0.55
- **Novelty:** Combination is novel as ME/CFS-Domain-6-specific; components are individually known
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Ketotifen (mast cell stabilizer + H1 antagonist, crosses BBB) addresses CNS mast cells; famotidine (H2, peripheral, also has anti-inflammatory CNS effects per Long COVID data) addresses GI/cardiovascular mast cells. The combination covers H1+H2+stabilization at low cost with excellent safety. Distinct from cromolyn (poor absorption, GI-only).
- **Evidence link:** Yao2025 (75–91% triad therapy response); Conway2024 (empirical therapy benefit); Long COVID famotidine RCT signals.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** A 12-week RCT of ketotifen 1mg BID + famotidine 40mg BID in MCAS-positive ME/CFS (Valent 2021 criteria) will produce >30% improvement in DSQ-PEM score versus placebo, with effect size correlating with baseline urinary N-methylhistamine.

### 1.4 Biomarker: Urinary Leukotriene E4 / Prostaglandin D2-Methylimidazoleacetic Acid Ratio as Domain 6 Phenotype Discriminator

- **Category:** Biomarker
- **Certainty:** 0.50
- **Novelty:** NOVEL ratio
- **Mechanistic rationale:** LTE4 reflects activated mast cell *and* basophil/eosinophil cysteinyl leukotriene production; PGD2 metabolites are mast-cell-specific. The ratio distinguishes "pure" mast cell activation (low ratio) from broader Th2/eosinophilic patterns (high ratio). ME/CFS patients with low ratios are predicted to be cromolyn/ketotifen responders; high ratios suggest montelukast-first.
- **Evidence link:** Lee2025CanadaMCAS (urinary mediator panels); Valent2021 criteria.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In a prospective cohort, the LTE4/PGD2-MIAA ratio measured at baseline will predict differential response to ketotifen vs montelukast monotherapy with AUC > 0.70.

### 1.5 Research Direction: Cromolyn N-of-1 Trial Network for Causality Test

- **Category:** Research direction
- **Certainty:** 0.60 (feasibility)
- **Novelty:** Specific design tailored to the "rate-limiting cause vs comorbidity" question
- **Mechanistic rationale:** If MCAS is *upstream* of ANS/neuroinflammation in a subset, then mast cell stabilization (cromolyn — non-systemic, non-confounding by CNS effects) should improve OI/PEM/cognition in *that order* and with *that latency* (peripheral first, central later). If MCAS is downstream/comorbid, OI/PEM should be unchanged. N-of-1 design with structured washout disambiguates from placebo.
- **Evidence link:** Conway2024 (empirical therapy framework); Valent2021.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In ≥50 N-of-1 cromolyn trials in MCAS+ME/CFS, the proportion showing OI improvement preceding cognitive improvement (within-patient temporal ordering) will exceed 60%, supporting upstream causality. If symptoms improve simultaneously or randomly, the comorbidity model is favored.

### 1.6 Supplement: Luteolin–Quercetin–PEA Triple Stack with Sublingual/Liposomal Delivery

- **Category:** Supplement
- **Certainty:** 0.45
- **Novelty:** Delivery-form innovation; combination is partially in literature
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Quercetin (mast cell stabilizer, but bioavailability ~2%) + luteolin (microglial + mast cell stabilizer, BBB penetrant) + palmitoylethanolamide (PEA — endocannabinoid-like, mast cell down-regulator via PPAR-α). Liposomal/sublingual forms bypass first-pass metabolism, addressing the bioavailability ceiling that has historically limited quercetin trials.
- **Evidence link:** Theoharides luteolin literature; PEA fibromyalgia RCTs.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Liposomal quercetin 250mg + luteolin 100mg + PEA 600mg BID for 8 weeks will reduce serum tryptase by ≥15% in baseline-elevated patients and produce measurable functional improvement on 6MWT or 10-min stand test.

### 1.7 Non-Pharm: Histamine-Smart Nutritional Timing (not just low-histamine)

- **Category:** Non-pharmacological intervention
- **Certainty:** 0.40
- **Novelty:** NOVEL framing — chronobiological rather than purely eliminationist
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Mast cell histamine release peaks circadianly (early morning per murine data; mid-afternoon in humans per limited data). DAO enzyme activity also varies. Rather than chronic low-histamine restriction (which causes nutrient deficiency and food fear), patients time histamine-rich foods (aged cheese, fermented foods) to *trough* histamine periods and front-load fresh proteins/low-histamine foods at peak periods.
- **Evidence link:** Circadian mast cell biology; DAO chronobiology literature.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Patients on time-restricted histamine eating (high-histamine foods only 10am–2pm window) will show fewer flare days per month than continuous low-histamine restriction at equivalent total histamine load.

### 1.8 Cross-Disease Bridge: hEDS-POTS-MCAS Triad as a Single "Connective-Tissue-Mast-Cell Permissivity" Spectrum

- **Category:** Cross-disease bridge
- **Certainty:** 0.50
- **Novelty:** NOVEL unifying mechanism distinct from Yao2025's symptomatic triad description
- **Mechanistic rationale:** The triad shares an underlying defect: extracellular matrix abnormalities (tenascin-X, collagen) reduce the physical barriers that normally constrain mast cell migration into perivascular and perineural niches. The same matrix laxity that causes joint hypermobility permits mast cell tissue infiltration in the vasa nervorum, brainstem, and gut submucosa. ME/CFS with hEDS features represents the "permissive matrix" subtype.
- **Evidence link:** Yao2025; tenascin-X mast cell literature; Ehlers-Danlos Society MCAS prevalence data.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS patients with Beighton ≥5 will show higher skin biopsy mast cell density (>20/HPF) than Beighton ≤2 patients, even when matched for serum tryptase.

## TIER 2 — SPECULATIVE (integration priority: MEDIUM)

### 2.1 Hypothesis: Mast Cell-Derived Heparin as a Cause of Hypocoagulable Bleeding in ME/CFS

- **Category:** Novel hypothesis
- **Certainty:** 0.30
- **Novelty:** NOVEL clinical observation explanation
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Many ME/CFS patients report easy bruising, prolonged menses, and post-procedure bleeding. Mast cell granules contain heparin (the biological prototype of the drug). Sustained low-grade degranulation could produce a chronic, hard-to-detect heparin effect — explaining why standard coagulation panels are normal but anti-Xa or thrombin time may be subtly prolonged.
- **Evidence link:** Mast cell granule composition (textbook); ME/CFS patient reports.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Anti-Xa activity will be detectable in MCAS+ME/CFS patients reporting bleeding symptoms, and will correlate with serum tryptase. Cromolyn treatment will normalize anti-Xa within 8 weeks.

### 2.2 Drug Repurposing: Low-Dose Imatinib for KIT-Driven Mast Cell Hyperplasia Subset

- **Category:** Drug/medication
- **Certainty:** 0.25
- **Novelty:** NOVEL for ME/CFS (used in mastocytosis)
- **Mechanistic rationale:** A subset of MCAS patients have somatic KIT mutations or KIT pathway upregulation without meeting mastocytosis criteria. Low-dose imatinib (100mg) has shown benefit in non-clonal MCAS in case series. ME/CFS-MCAS patients with elevated baseline tryptase (>11 ng/mL) or KIT D816V positivity may represent a tractable, well-defined subgroup.
- **Evidence link:** ECNM-AIM protocol (Lee2025); mastocytosis literature.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS patients with persistent tryptase >11 ng/mL and KIT-pathway expression in skin/marrow biopsy will show >40% symptom reduction on imatinib 100mg/day at 6 months.

### 2.3 Hypothesis: Mast Cell Mitochondrial Transfer as a Bidirectional Bridge Between Domain 2 and Domain 6

- **Category:** Novel hypothesis
- **Certainty:** 0.25
- **Novelty:** NOVEL link between two domains
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Recent literature shows mast cells can transfer mitochondria to neighboring cells via tunneling nanotubes, and conversely accept dysfunctional mitochondria from stressed cells. In ME/CFS, dysfunctional mitochondria from skeletal muscle/neurons may be "phagocytosed" by tissue-resident mast cells, triggering activation. Conversely, mast cells may export healthy mitochondria as a rescue mechanism that becomes exhausted.
- **Evidence link:** Mast cell mitochondrial biology emerging literature (2022–2024).
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Co-culture of ME/CFS patient PBMC-derived mast cells with damaged mitochondria will produce greater degranulation than healthy mast cells; serum cell-free mitochondrial DNA will correlate with tryptase in ME/CFS.

### 2.4 Biomarker: Skin Prick "Mechanical Threshold" Test as Bedside Domain 6 Screen

- **Category:** Biomarker
- **Certainty:** 0.35
- **Novelty:** NOVEL clinical tool
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Dermatographism is a classic MCAS sign but poorly quantified. A standardized stylus delivering graded mechanical force (5g, 10g, 20g, 40g) measures the *threshold* for wheal formation. Lower thresholds correlate with mast cell hyperreactivity. Cheap, rapid, reproducible bedside Tier 2 screening tool.
- **Evidence link:** Dermatographism in MCAS criteria.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** A 5g-threshold dermatographism response will have >70% sensitivity and >80% specificity for biochemically confirmed MCAS in ME/CFS, with AUC > 0.80.

### 2.5 Mathematical Model Extension: Domain 6 as a "Bistable Switch" Layer in the Causal Graph

- **Category:** Mathematical model extension
- **Certainty:** 0.40
- **Novelty:** NOVEL formalization
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Existing 5-domain causal model uses linear or weakly-coupled feedback. Mast cells exhibit characteristic *bistability*: below activation threshold, quiescent; above, self-sustaining via autocrine CRH/SCF/IL-6 loops. Domain 6 should be modeled as a Hill-function switch (n≥4) with hysteresis, not a linear node. This predicts the observed clinical pattern of sudden flares and prolonged remissions.
- **Evidence link:** Mast cell autocrine loops; bistability in immune cell literature.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Time-series of urinary mediators in ME/CFS-MCAS patients will show bimodal distribution (quiescent vs activated states) with sharp transitions, not unimodal/Gaussian; transition probabilities will be predictable from prior-state and stressor input.

### 2.6 Supplement: Diamine Oxidase (DAO) Replacement Timed to Meals + Vitamin B6/Copper Cofactor Optimization

- **Category:** Supplement
- **Certainty:** 0.35
- **Novelty:** Cofactor-stack framing is novel
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Exogenous DAO (porcine kidney) degrades dietary histamine in the gut lumen. Endogenous DAO requires pyridoxal-5-phosphate (active B6), copper, and vitamin C. Many ME/CFS patients have low active B6 (despite normal serum B6) or copper-zinc imbalance. Combined supplementation may be more effective than DAO alone.
- **Evidence link:** DAO enzymology; ME/CFS micronutrient surveys.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Patients with low pyridoxal-5-phosphate at baseline will show greater response to DAO + P5P + copper combination than DAO alone, measured by post-meal symptom scores.

### 2.7 Non-Pharm: Vagal Nerve Stimulation (Auricular tVNS) as Mast Cell Brake

- **Category:** Non-pharmacological intervention
- **Certainty:** 0.30
- **Novelty:** NOVEL framing for MCAS-ME/CFS
- **Mechanistic rationale:** The cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway (α7 nAChR) suppresses mast cell degranulation. Auricular tVNS activates this pathway non-invasively. In ME/CFS-MCAS, repeated tVNS may raise the mast cell activation threshold.
- **Evidence link:** Vagal-mast cell axis literature; tVNS RCTs in inflammatory conditions.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** 30 minutes daily auricular tVNS for 8 weeks in MCAS+ME/CFS will reduce urinary N-methylhistamine by ≥20% versus sham stimulation.

### 2.8 Cross-Disease Bridge: Long COVID-MCAS as "Spike-Protein-Imprinted" Mast Cell Memory

- **Category:** Cross-disease bridge
- **Certainty:** 0.35
- **Novelty:** NOVEL specific mechanism (distinct from generic "post-viral MCAS")
- **Mechanistic rationale:** SARS-CoV-2 spike protein binds ACE2 and may directly activate mast cells via TLR4 / Mas-related G-protein receptor X2 (MRGPRX2). Repeated antigenic exposure may produce *epigenetically imprinted* mast cells (training memory) that respond to non-spike triggers more aggressively. This connects to the "epigenetic archivist" hypothesis but specifies the molecular target.
- **Evidence link:** Spike-MRGPRX2 in vitro work; Long COVID MCAS prevalence.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Long COVID-MCAS mast cells will show MRGPRX2 hypersensitivity to substance P versus pre-pandemic ME/CFS-MCAS controls; bromelain (MRGPRX2 antagonist) trials will preferentially benefit Long COVID subset.

### 2.9 Research Direction: Tissue-Resident Mast Cell Mapping via 18F-PBR28 PET-MRI

- **Category:** Research direction
- **Certainty:** 0.30 (technical feasibility)
- **Novelty:** NOVEL imaging application
- **Mechanistic rationale:** TSPO (translocator protein) is upregulated on activated mast cells as well as microglia. Combined with regional anatomy (brainstem vs cortex) and pre/post cromolyn imaging, PBR28 PET could distinguish mast cell from microglial contributions to neuroinflammation.
- **Evidence link:** TSPO mast cell expression; PBR28 ME/CFS pilot studies.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Cromolyn pre-treatment for 4 weeks will reduce hypothalamic and brainstem PBR28 binding in ME/CFS-MCAS but not ME/CFS-MCAS- patients.

## TIER 3 — LONG-SHOT (integration priority: LOW; document for completeness)

### 3.1 Hypothesis: Mast Cell-Driven Lymphatic Stasis as the "Missing Link" for Brain Fog

- **Category:** Novel hypothesis
- **Certainty:** 0.20
- **Novelty:** NOVEL
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Mast cell histamine modulates lymphatic vessel contractility. Sustained release may produce paradoxical lymphatic spasm or atony, impairing glymphatic clearance overnight. This connects MCAS directly to the unrefreshing sleep / cognitive sluggishness symptom cluster.
- **Evidence link:** Lymphatic mast cell literature; glymphatic ME/CFS speculation.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** DTI-ALPS index (glymphatic proxy) will be lower in MCAS+ME/CFS than MCAS- ME/CFS and will improve after 12 weeks of mast cell stabilization.

### 3.2 Drug: Topical Cromolyn for Skin Mast Cells as Systemic-Sparing Trial

- **Category:** Drug/medication
- **Certainty:** 0.20
- **Novelty:** NOVEL trial design
- **Mechanistic rationale:** If skin mast cell activation drives systemic effects via dermal afferents, hemibody topical cromolyn (vs vehicle on contralateral side) provides within-subject control. Improvement only on cromolyn side argues for skin-centered phenotype; bilateral improvement argues for systemic absorption (unlikely with cromolyn) or placebo.
- **Evidence link:** Topical cromolyn pharmacology.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In hemibody design, treated-side wheal/flare to mechanical stimulus will reduce by >50% within 2 weeks.

### 3.3 Hypothesis: Mast Cell-Plasmacytoid Dendritic Cell "Type-I IFN Echo" Loop

- **Category:** Novel hypothesis
- **Certainty:** 0.15
- **Novelty:** NOVEL cell-cell axis
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Activated mast cells release CXCL8 and recruit pDCs; pDCs produce IFN-α; IFN-α primes mast cells for further activation. This creates a self-reinforcing low-grade interferonopathy that mimics chronic viral illness in absence of detectable virus.
- **Evidence link:** Mast cell-pDC interaction literature; ME/CFS interferon signature studies.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Serum CXCL8 + IFN-α2 will co-elevate in MCAS+ME/CFS subset; baricitinib (JAK1/2 inhibitor) trial would reduce both with proportional symptom benefit.

### 3.4 Supplement: Sodium Cromoglicate-Mimetic Polyphenol Combinations (Procyanidins + Resveratrol)

- **Category:** Supplement
- **Certainty:** 0.15
- **Novelty:** NOVEL framing
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Cromolyn binds the S100A4 protein on mast cells. Several plant polyphenols share this binding pocket conformationally. Specific combinations (grape seed procyanidins + resveratrol) may achieve "natural cromolyn" effect at higher tissue penetration.
- **Evidence link:** S100A4-cromolyn structural biology; polyphenol mast cell literature.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In vitro RBL-2H3 mast cell degranulation assay will show synergistic inhibition (Bliss synergy score >0.2) for procyanidin + resveratrol combinations.

### 3.5 Cross-Disease Bridge: Mast Cell Gene Expression Convergence with Chronic Lyme / Bartonellosis

- **Category:** Cross-disease bridge
- **Certainty:** 0.20
- **Novelty:** NOVEL transcriptomic angle
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Persistent stealth pathogens (Bartonella, Borrelia) may chronically prime mast cells via specific TLR2/TLR9 ligands. Transcriptomic comparison of ME/CFS, chronic Lyme, and bartonellosis mast cell signatures may reveal a shared "chronic-pathogen-primed" mast cell state distinct from atopic mast cells.
- **Evidence link:** Bartonella mast cell tropism; Lyme post-treatment syndrome MCAS overlap.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** scRNA-seq of skin biopsies will identify a transcriptomic mast cell subcluster present in ME/CFS, chronic Lyme, and Bartonellosis but absent in atopic dermatitis controls.

### 3.6 Mathematical Model: Multi-Scale Domain 6 Integration via Stochastic Petri Nets

- **Category:** Mathematical model extension
- **Certainty:** 0.20
- **Novelty:** NOVEL methodology
- **Mechanistic rationale:** Domain 6 spans molecular (mediator release), cellular (mast cell state), tissue (perivascular/peri-neural), and systemic (OI, PEM) scales. Stochastic Petri nets handle this multi-scale, discrete-event, partially-observable structure better than ODEs. Tokens represent activation events; transitions encode degranulation, recovery, and inter-domain coupling.
- **Evidence link:** Petri net applications in immune modeling.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** A Petri-net model fitted to one cohort will predict flare frequency distribution in a held-out cohort with KS-test p>0.05 (i.e., not rejected), outperforming linear ODE baseline.
