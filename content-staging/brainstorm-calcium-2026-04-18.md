### H1. TRPM3-TRPM7 Dual Channelopathy as Subtype-Defining Signature

- **Category:** Novel hypothesis + diagnostic/biomarker
- **Mechanism:** TRPM3 dysfunction is well-replicated (Nguyen2017 through Sasso2026). DuPreez2023 now shows TRPM7 dysfunction in the same patient population (n=9). TRPM7 is a chanzyme (channel + kinase) that regulates Mg2+ homeostasis, cell migration, and apoptosis. TRPM7 kinase phosphorylates annexin A1 and myosin IIA -- both required for immune cell motility and phagocytosis. If both TRPM3 (Ca2+ entry for degranulation) and TRPM7 (Mg2+ homeostasis + motility) are dysfunctional, immune cells are simultaneously unable to reach targets efficiently AND unable to kill once they arrive. This creates a more severe functional deficit than either alone.
- **Evidence link:** DuPreez2023trpm7, Nguyen2017trpm3, EatonFitch2021trpm3pip2; existing ch14h systemic channelopathy hypothesis
- **Certainty:** 0.42
- **Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS patients with dual TRPM3+TRPM7 dysfunction (measured by patch-clamp in NK cells) should show more severe NK cytotoxicity impairment than patients with TRPM3 dysfunction alone. Disease severity (Bell scale) should correlate with the number of dysfunctional TRP channels.
- **Integration target:** ch14h-trpm3-channelopathy.typ (expand systemic channelopathy section); ch07-immune-dysfunction.typ (NK cytotoxicity subsection)

### H2. PIP2 Depletion as the Convergence Point for GPCR Autoantibodies and TRP Dysfunction

- **Category:** Novel hypothesis
- **Mechanism:** EatonFitch2021 showed reduced TRPM3/PIP2 co-localization in ME/CFS NK cells. PIP2 is required for TRPM3 gating AND is consumed by phospholipase C (PLC) downstream of GPCR signaling. The existing TRPM3-GPCR convergence hypothesis in ch14h proposes autoantibodies dysregulate GPCRs, but the specific molecular link is underspecified. PIP2 depletion is that link: chronic GPCR autoantibody stimulation (anti-beta2, anti-M3/M4) tonically activates PLC, consuming PIP2 faster than PI4K/PIP5K can regenerate it. The resulting PIP2 deficit simultaneously impairs TRPM3 gating, TRPM7 gating, Piezo channel mechanosensitivity, and KCNQ potassium channel function. A single upstream deficit (PIP2 pool exhaustion) explains dysfunction across multiple ion channel families.
- **Evidence link:** EatonFitch2021trpm3pip2 (direct PIP2/TRPM3 co-localization data), existing autoantibody literature (Wirth/Scheibenbogen), GPCR-PLC-PIP2 canonical pathway
- **Certainty:** 0.45
- **Falsifiable prediction:** (a) PIP2 levels (measured by anti-PIP2 immunofluorescence or mass spectrometry of membrane phosphoinositides) in ME/CFS NK cells should be reduced vs. controls. (b) In vitro PIP2 supplementation (exogenous diC8-PIP2) should partially rescue TRPM3 current in ME/CFS cells even without NTX. (c) Autoantibody titers should inversely correlate with cellular PIP2 levels.
- **Integration target:** ch14h (TRPM3-GPCR convergence hypothesis -- sharpen the mechanism); ch07 (autoantibody section)

### H3. Thalamic T-Type Calcium Channel Dysfunction as Mechanistic Basis for Alpha-Delta Sleep

- **Category:** Novel hypothesis (bridging sleep and calcium)
- **Mechanism:** The document discusses alpha-delta sleep intrusion (ch02, ch13, ch15) and the sleep architecture failure hypothesis (ch08) but attributes it to generic "energy-limited coordination failure." Crunelli2006 establishes that CaV3.1 T-type calcium channels are the most crucial voltage-dependent conductance for thalamic delta-wave generation -- CaV3.1 KO mice have reduced NREM sleep. Vijayan2015 models how altered Ih + GABAB + K+ leak transforms delta into alpha-delta pattern. If ME/CFS involves systemic calcium dysregulation (Wirth NCX reversal model + TRPM3 channelopathy), thalamic T-type channels are plausible collateral damage. Specifically: (a) chronic neuroinflammation alters thalamic membrane properties (Ih conductance changes from cytokine exposure), (b) energy deficit reduces the Na+/K+-ATPase-dependent ionic gradients that set T-channel window current, (c) altered calcium homeostasis shifts CaV3.1 steady-state inactivation. The result: impaired delta oscillation generation, alpha-frequency intrusion during NREM, and non-restorative sleep.
- **Evidence link:** Crunelli2006thalamicTtype, Vijayan2015alphaDelta, existing hyp:sleep-architecture-failure, Moldofsky1975alphaDeltaSleep, existing calcium-PEM hypothesis
- **Certainty:** 0.45
- **Falsifiable prediction:** (a) ME/CFS patients with alpha-delta sleep pattern on PSG should show reduced delta power in thalamic source-localized EEG compared to ME/CFS patients without alpha-delta. (b) T-type calcium channel modulators (ethosuximide, low-dose zonisamide) should increase delta power and reduce alpha intrusion in ME/CFS sleep EEG. (c) Severity of alpha-delta intrusion should correlate with biomarkers of calcium dysregulation (intracellular sodium MRI as proxy).
- **Integration target:** ch08-neurological.typ (sleep section -- add thalamic calcium mechanism); ch15-symptom-producing-mechanisms.typ (alpha-delta discussion)

### H4. LDN + MDC002 as Orthogonal Calcium Correction (Subtype A + B Convergence Therapy)

- **Category:** Combination therapy / drug idea
- **Mechanism:** The document defines two subtypes: (A) energy failure -> NCX reversal -> Ca2+ overload (Wirth model, MDC002 target), and (B) primary TRPM3 channelopathy -> impaired Ca2+ signaling (Griffith group, LDN target). These are presented as distinct, but many patients may have both: chronic TRPM3 dysfunction impairs immune surveillance -> persistent infection/inflammation -> hypoperfusion -> energy failure -> NCX reversal. LDN addresses the TRPM3 arm (restores Ca2+ influx for immune function; EatonFitch2022 shows p<0.0001), MDC002 addresses the NCX arm (restores Na+/K+-ATPase, prevents reverse-mode Ca2+ import). Combined, they correct calcium in opposite directions in different compartments: LDN restores Ca2+ signaling IN immune cells (where it's deficient), MDC002 prevents Ca2+ toxicity IN muscle cells (where it's excessive).
- **Evidence link:** EatonFitch2022trpm3ntx, Cabanas2019trpm3ldnFront, Wirth2022muscleSodium, Scheibenbogen2025muscle, Mitodicure2024pipeline
- **Certainty:** 0.40
- **Falsifiable prediction:** (a) Patients receiving LDN+MDC002 combination should show improvement in both NK cytotoxicity (TRPM3 arm) AND post-exercise sodium accumulation (NCX arm), whereas monotherapy improves only one. (b) Subtype A-dominant patients (high intracellular sodium, normal TRPM3) respond to MDC002 alone; Subtype B-dominant (abnormal TRPM3, normal sodium) respond to LDN alone; mixed patients require both.
- **Integration target:** ch18-emerging-therapies.typ; ch14h (subtyping section)

## Tier 2: Speculative ideas (certainty 0.20-0.39, plausible mechanisms)

### S1. Ethosuximide for Alpha-Delta Sleep in ME/CFS

- **Category:** Drug/medication (repurposed)
- **Mechanism:** Ethosuximide is a selective T-type calcium channel blocker (CaV3.1-3.3), FDA-approved for absence epilepsy. Absence seizures arise from pathological thalamocortical oscillations -- the same circuits that generate sleep spindles and delta waves. In absence epilepsy, ethosuximide reduces pathological thalamic burst firing. In ME/CFS, the problem may be opposite (insufficient T-type current for delta generation), so ethosuximide could paradoxically worsen sleep. However, the Vijayan2015 model suggests the alpha-delta pattern arises from altered Ih and K+ leak, not necessarily from T-channel hyperactivation. If alpha intrusion results from residual thalamic burst firing during NREM (T-channels generating alpha-frequency oscillations instead of delta), then partial T-channel block could suppress the aberrant alpha component. The direction of effect is genuinely uncertain.
- **Evidence link:** Crunelli2006thalamicTtype, Vijayan2015alphaDelta
- **Certainty:** 0.25 (uncertain direction of effect)
- **Falsifiable prediction:** A single-dose crossover study: ethosuximide 250mg vs. placebo, with overnight PSG, measuring delta power and alpha-delta index. If delta power increases and alpha intrusion decreases, the alpha component is T-channel-mediated. If delta power decreases, T-channels are needed for delta generation and the drug worsens sleep.
- **Integration target:** ch08 (sleep section, as research question); ch14h (add thalamic T-type to therapeutic speculation)

### S2. Zonisamide as Dual-Mechanism Sleep + Calcium Agent

- **Category:** Drug/medication (repurposed)
- **Mechanism:** Zonisamide blocks T-type calcium channels (like ethosuximide) but also blocks sodium channels and enhances GABA-A. It is used in epilepsy and Parkinson disease (reduces wearing-off, improves sleep). The GABA enhancement could address the GABAB component of Vijayan2015's alpha-delta model, while T-type block may modulate thalamocortical oscillations. It also has antioxidant properties (free radical scavenging). In ME/CFS context: simultaneous sleep architecture modulation + mild neuroprotection. Low-dose (25-50mg) could be trialed as sleep-targeted intervention.
- **Evidence link:** Vijayan2015alphaDelta, Crunelli2006thalamicTtype, general zonisamide pharmacology
- **Certainty:** 0.25
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Zonisamide 25-50mg at bedtime should improve subjective sleep quality scores and increase delta power on home EEG in ME/CFS patients with documented alpha-delta pattern vs. placebo over 4 weeks.
- **Integration target:** ch08 (sleep pharmacology); ch15-medications (add to sleep medication options as research-stage)

### S3. Magnesium as Physiological Calcium Antagonist and TRPM7 Cofactor

- **Category:** Supplement/nutraceutical
- **Mechanism:** Mg2+ is a physiological Ca2+ antagonist at multiple levels: (a) competes with Ca2+ for entry through NMDA receptors, (b) required for Na+/K+-ATPase function (Mg-ATP is the actual substrate), (c) TRPM7 is a primary Mg2+ entry channel -- if TRPM7 is dysfunctional (DuPreez2023), intracellular Mg2+ depletion follows, (d) Mg2+ deficiency shifts the NCX reversal potential toward Ca2+ import (lower intracellular Mg2+ impairs Na+/K+-ATPase, raising intracellular Na+, promoting NCX reversal). Mg2+ supplementation is already common in ME/CFS clinical practice but the rationale is usually generic ("mitochondrial cofactor"). The calcium-specific rationale is much stronger: Mg2+ directly opposes the calcium overload cascade at multiple steps.
- **Evidence link:** DuPreez2023trpm7 (TRPM7 as Mg2+ channel), Wirth model (Na+/K+-ATPase requires Mg-ATP), general physiology
- **Certainty:** 0.38
- **Falsifiable prediction:** (a) ME/CFS patients with lowest RBC magnesium should show highest post-exercise intracellular sodium accumulation (23Na-MRI). (b) Magnesium glycinate 400mg/day for 12 weeks should reduce post-exercise sodium accumulation in Mg-deficient ME/CFS patients. (c) Effect should be amplified when combined with MDC002 (Mg provides substrate, MDC002 activates pump).
- **Integration target:** ch16-supplements (strengthen magnesium rationale with calcium-specific mechanism); ch06 (Na+/K+-ATPase section -- add Mg-ATP requirement)

### S4. Omega-3 Fatty Acids for TRP Channel Membrane Environment Restoration

- **Category:** Supplement/nutraceutical
- **Mechanism:** TRP channel function depends on membrane lipid composition. TRPM3, TRPM7, TRPV1 gating properties are modulated by the surrounding lipid bilayer (cholesterol content, phospholipid acyl chain length, omega-3/omega-6 ratio). ME/CFS patients show altered membrane fatty acid profiles (documented in multiple studies). Omega-3 supplementation (EPA/DHA 2-3g/day) modifies membrane composition over 8-12 weeks, potentially restoring the lipid environment needed for normal TRP channel function. This is distinct from the anti-inflammatory rationale usually cited for omega-3 in ME/CFS.
- **Evidence link:** Existing ch14h text ("Membrane lipid composition affects TRP channel function / Omega-3 fatty acids might normalize membrane environment"), general TRP channel biophysics
- **Certainty:** 0.30

(b) Omega-3 supplementation (2g EPA+DHA/day x 12 weeks) should improve TRPM3 function measured by Ca2+ flux assay, independent of anti-inflammatory effects (control for CRP/cytokine changes).
- **Integration target:** ch16-supplements; ch14h (membrane environment section -- expand)

### S5. Pregnenolone Sulfate as Endogenous TRPM3 Agonist Supplement

- **Category:** Supplement/nutraceutical
- **Mechanism:** Already mentioned briefly in ch14h and ch07 as TRPM3 agonist. Pregnenolone sulfate is the most potent known endogenous TRPM3 activator (EC50 ~23 microM). Pregnenolone is available as OTC supplement (50-100mg). Sulfation occurs endogenously via SULT2A1. If TRPM3 channels in ME/CFS are functional but hypoactive (reduced Ca2+ influx rather than completely non-functional -- consistent with Cabanas2019 showing reduced rather than absent current), supraphysiological agonist concentrations might partially overcome the deficit. Critically, neurosteroid levels decline with chronic stress and illness. ME/CFS patients may have depleted pregnenolone pools (not yet measured in ME/CFS).
- **Evidence link:** Vriens2011pregnenolone, Wagner2008pregnenolone, Cabanas2019trpm3val (reduced but not absent current)
- **Certainty:** 0.30
- **Falsifiable prediction:** (a) Serum pregnenolone sulfate levels should be measured in ME/CFS cohort vs. controls. (b) If low, supplementation should correlate with improved TRPM3 Ca2+ flux in vitro (add patient serum + pregnenolone to TRPM3 assay). (c) Oral pregnenolone 50mg/day should improve NK cytotoxicity in TRPM3-dysfunctional ME/CFS patients over 8 weeks.
- **Integration target:** ch14h (TRPM3 therapeutics -- expand pregnenolone); ch16-supplements

### S6. Thermal Biofeedback Exploiting TRPM3 Thermosensory Function

- **Category:** Non-pharmacological
- **Mechanism:** TRPM3 is a thermosensor activated in the warm/noxious heat range (>40C). The ch14h temperature dysregulation hypothesis proposes TRPM3 dysfunction causes aberrant thermoregulation. Inverting the logic: could controlled thermal stimulation be used therapeutically? Graded warm exposure (sauna at tolerable temperatures, warm baths 38-39C) activates remaining functional TRPM3 channels, providing calcium entry that is deficient at baseline. This is essentially a non-pharmacological TRPM3 agonist. However, ME/CFS patients report heat intolerance, and excessive thermal load could worsen symptoms. The key would be finding the therapeutic window: warm enough to activate TRPM3 but not so warm as to cause energy expenditure crisis.
- **Evidence link:** Existing ch14h thermosensor hypothesis, TRPM3 thermal activation physiology
- **Certainty:** 0.25
- **Falsifiable prediction:** (a) Brief (5-10min) warm water immersion at 38C should produce measurable increase in NK cell Ca2+ flux (via TRPM3 activation) sampled immediately after vs. before. (b) ME/CFS patients with more severe TRPM3 dysfunction should require higher temperatures to achieve the same Ca2+ response. (c) Regular mild thermal exposure (3x/week, 10 min, 38C) should improve TRPM3 function measurements over 8 weeks.
- **Integration target:** ch17-lifestyle-interventions.typ (add thermal protocol); ch14h (temperature section)

### S8. Cross-Disease Bridge: Hypokalemic Periodic Paralysis Model

- **Category:** Cross-disease bridge
- **Mechanism:** Hypokalemic periodic paralysis (HypoPP) is a channelopathy where attacks of muscle weakness are triggered by exertion, carbohydrate intake, and stress -- triggers strikingly similar to ME/CFS PEM. In HypoPP, mutations in CaV1.1 (L-type calcium channel) or Nav1.4 create an anomalous "gating pore current" that depolarizes muscle fibers, inactivating sodium channels and causing inexcitable paralysis. The parallel to ME/CFS: if ME/CFS involves acquired (not genetic) channel dysfunction across multiple channel families (TRPM3, TRPM7, potentially CaV, Nav), the functional consequence could be similar -- episodes of reduced cellular excitability triggered by metabolic stress. Key difference: HypoPP affects a single channel genetically; ME/CFS may affect multiple channels via autoimmune/post-infectious mechanisms. Treatment insight from HypoPP: acetazolamide (carbonic anhydrase inhibitor) prevents attacks, possibly by acidifying muscle pH and closing the aberrant gating pore. Acetazolamide is already mentioned in ME/CFS for intracranial pressure; its muscle-protective mechanism in HypoPP suggests a separate rationale.
- **Evidence link:** HypoPP literature, existing calcium cascade (ch06), Wirth model
- **Certainty:** 0.25
- **Falsifiable prediction:** (a) ME/CFS patients should show post-exertional changes in compound muscle action potential (CMAP) amplitude similar to (but milder than) HypoPP patients. (b) Acetazolamide 250mg BID should reduce PEM severity in patients with the most pronounced post-exercise sodium shifts.
- **Integration target:** ch14/ch14d-cross-disease.typ (add HypoPP comparison); ch06 (calcium cascade)

### S12. NCX Reversal as Unifying Mechanism for PEM and Cardiac Preload Failure

- **Category:** Novel hypothesis
- **Mechanism:** NCX1 (SLC8A1) is expressed in both skeletal muscle and cardiac muscle. The Wirth model describes NCX reversal in skeletal muscle causing PEM. The same mechanism should operate in cardiomyocytes: hypoperfusion -> anaerobic metabolism -> Na+ overload -> NCX reversal -> Ca2+ overload in cardiac cells. In heart, Ca2+ overload produces diastolic dysfunction (impaired relaxation) and reduced cardiac output. This connects the calcium cascade directly to the documented reduced cardiac output in ME/CFS (Hollingsworth 2012, Miwa 2011). Diastolic dysfunction would reduce preload, worsening orthostatic intolerance. The calcium cascade is thus not limited to PEM in muscle -- it may be operating continuously in heart at lower intensity, contributing to the baseline cardiovascular deficit.
- **Evidence link:** Wirth NCX reversal model, cardiac NCX1 physiology, existing cardiovascular chapter findings
- **Certainty:** 0.35
- **Falsifiable prediction:** (a) Echocardiography should show subtle diastolic dysfunction (elevated E/e' ratio) in ME/CFS patients, worsening post-exercise. (b) Cardiac 23Na-MRI (technically feasible) should show elevated myocardial sodium in ME/CFS. (c) MDC002 should improve diastolic function parameters alongside PEM reduction.
- **Integration target:** ch10-cardiovascular.typ; ch06 (calcium cascade -- extend to cardiac)

## Tier 3: Long-shots (certainty < 0.20, creative bridges)

### L1. Timothy Syndrome as Extreme Model of TRPM3-Like Dysfunction

- **Category:** Cross-disease bridge
- **Mechanism:** Timothy syndrome (CaV1.2 L-type calcium channel gain-of-function mutation) produces multi-organ dysfunction: cardiac arrhythmias, immune deficiency, syndactyly, autism. The immune deficiency in Timothy syndrome (recurrent infections due to impaired T-cell activation) parallels ME/CFS immune dysfunction, but through a different channel. If calcium channel dysfunction of ANY type impairs immune cell function, Timothy syndrome is a proof-of-concept that channelopathies cause immunodeficiency. The insight: the specific channel matters less than the disruption of calcium signaling homeostasis. ME/CFS may be an acquired Timothy-like state affecting different channel families.
- **Evidence link:** Timothy syndrome literature, existing systemic channelopathy hypothesis
- **Certainty:** 0.15
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Timothy syndrome patients (rare) should show TRPM3 dysfunction measurements similar to ME/CFS if calcium homeostasis disruption is the shared mechanism, OR they should show normal TRPM3 with abnormal CaV1.2, which would argue channel-specificity matters.
- **Integration target:** ch14d-cross-disease.typ

### L2. Brugada Syndrome Parallel: Post-Viral Ion Channel Dysfunction

- **Category:** Cross-disease bridge
- **Mechanism:** Brugada syndrome can be acquired after febrile illness -- fever unmasks latent sodium channel dysfunction. Some Brugada patients develop the phenotype after viral infection and it persists. This demonstrates a precedent for post-infectious acquired channelopathy. If viral infection can permanently alter Nav1.5 function in cardiomyocytes, it can potentially alter TRPM3 function in immune cells. The mechanism may be shared: viral protease cleavage of channel proteins, autoimmune targeting, or persistent epigenetic silencing. Brugada researchers have established protocols for studying post-infectious channelopathy that could be adapted for ME/CFS TRPM3 research.
- **Evidence link:** Brugada post-febrile literature, existing post-infectious TRPM3 acquisition hypothesis (ch14h)
- **Certainty:** 0.15
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Viral protease screens (testing whether SARS-CoV-2 3CLpro, EBV proteases, etc. cleave TRPM3 protein in vitro) should identify which viruses can directly damage TRPM3.
- **Integration target:** ch14h (post-infectious acquisition -- add Brugada parallel)

### L3. Cold Thermogenesis Protocol Exploiting TRPM8

- **Category:** Non-pharmacological
- **Mechanism:** TRPM8 is activated by cold (<25C) and menthol. Unlike TRPM3, TRPM8 has not been studied in ME/CFS. Brief cold exposure activates TRPM8 in brown adipose tissue, triggering thermogenesis and norepinephrine release. If ME/CFS patients have intact TRPM8 but dysfunctional TRPM3, cold exposure could provide calcium signaling through an alternative TRP channel, partially compensating for TRPM3 deficit. Cold exposure also activates the sympathetic nervous system, potentially improving orthostatic tolerance short-term. Very speculative -- could worsen symptoms in cold-intolerant patients.
- **Evidence link:** TRPM8 physiology, ME/CFS temperature dysregulation observations
- **Certainty:** 0.10
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Brief cold exposure (cold water hand immersion 10C, 2 min) should produce measurable norepinephrine increase in ME/CFS patients. Whether this improves or worsens symptoms would indicate whether non-TRPM3 TRP channel compensation is beneficial.
- **Integration target:** ch17-lifestyle (speculative thermal protocol)

### L4. GsMTx-4 (Spider Venom Peptide) as Piezo1 Research Tool

- **Category:** Research direction
- **Mechanism:** GsMTx-4 is a mechanosensitive channel inhibitor from tarantula venom that blocks Piezo1. It is the most selective Piezo1 tool compound available. The existing ch14h Piezo1-RBC speculation could be directly tested using GsMTx-4 on patient RBCs in vitro: if Piezo1 overactivation causes RBC stiffness, GsMTx-4 should restore deformability. Not a therapeutic candidate (venom peptide) but essential research tool.
- **Evidence link:** Existing ch14h spec:ch14h-piezo1-rbc, Saha2019, GsMTx-4 literature
- **Certainty:** 0.15
- **Falsifiable prediction:** GsMTx-4 treatment of ME/CFS RBCs in vitro should improve deformability measured by ektacytometry if Piezo1 overactivation is the mechanism. No improvement = competing mechanism (oxidative damage, ATP depletion).
- **Integration target:** ch14h (Piezo1 section -- add research tool)

### L5. Fibromyalgia Calcium Model Convergence

- **Category:** Cross-disease bridge
- **Mechanism:** Fibromyalgia shares alpha-delta sleep, central sensitization, and often co-occurs with ME/CFS. The Vijayan2015 model was developed FOR fibromyalgia. If thalamic T-type calcium channel dysfunction explains alpha-delta sleep in fibromyalgia, and ME/CFS shares this sleep phenotype, then the thalamic calcium mechanism may be a shared pathophysiological node. Treatment insight: gabapentinoids (already used in both conditions) reduce thalamic excitability partly through calcium channel modulation (alpha-2-delta subunit binding reduces surface expression of CaV2.1/2.2). The mechanistic rationale for gabapentinoids in ME/CFS sleep has never been articulated through the calcium lens -- it's usually framed as "reduces pain/excitability."
- **Evidence link:** Vijayan2015alphaDelta (explicitly fibromyalgia), existing fibromyalgia comparison in ch13, gabapentinoid calcium mechanism
- **Certainty:** 0.20
- **Falsifiable prediction:** (a) ME/CFS patients with concurrent fibromyalgia should have worse alpha-delta intrusion than ME/CFS-only patients. (b) Pregabalin response (sleep improvement) should correlate with baseline alpha-delta severity, not with pain severity, if the mechanism is thalamic calcium rather than peripheral pain reduction.
- **Integration target:** ch13-integrative-models.typ (FM overlap); ch08 (sleep)

### L6. Cardiac Glycoside Microdosing (Digitalis Derivatives)

- **Category:** Drug/medication (highly speculative)
- **Mechanism:** Cardiac glycosides (digoxin, ouabain) inhibit Na+/K+-ATPase. This seems counterproductive given the Wirth model of Na+/K+-ATPase failure. However, at ultra-low concentrations, ouabain activates Na+/K+-ATPase through a signaling cascade (Src kinase pathway) distinct from the transport-inhibiting mechanism. Endogenous ouabain is a hormone that regulates Na+/K+-ATPase at nanomolar concentrations. If ME/CFS involves loss of endogenous ouabain signaling, exogenous ultra-low-dose digoxin could potentially restore pump regulation. This is dangerously speculative and the therapeutic window would be extremely narrow.
- **Evidence link:** Endogenous ouabain physiology, Na+/K+-ATPase biphasic response to cardiac glycosides
- **Certainty:** 0.10
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Endogenous ouabain levels should be measured in ME/CFS serum. If depleted, sub-transport-inhibiting concentrations of ouabain applied to patient cells in vitro should improve Na+/K+-ATPase activity.
- **Integration target:** NOT for clinical integration -- research curiosity only

### L7. CRISPR-Based TRPM3 Reporter for High-Throughput Drug Screening

- **Category:** Research direction (long-term)
- **Mechanism:** Create a cell line with TRPM3 promoter driving GFP + TRPM3-GCaMP fusion for simultaneous expression and function monitoring. Use ME/CFS patient iPSC-derived NK cells differentiated in this reporter system. Screen compound libraries for TRPM3 function restoration. This would enable systematic identification of TRPM3 modulators beyond the handful known (pregnenolone sulfate, CIM0216, nifedipine, LDN).
- **Evidence link:** General CRISPR reporter technology, existing TRPM3 pharmacology gap noted in ch14h
- **Certainty:** 0.15
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Screen should identify novel TRPM3 modulators; hits should restore Ca2+ flux in ME/CFS patient cells.
- **Integration target:** part4-research (drug discovery section)
