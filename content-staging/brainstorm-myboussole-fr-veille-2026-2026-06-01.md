## Synthesis: What 19 Studies Collectively Tell Us About ME/CFS

The myBoussole veille spans 5 months (Jan–Mai 2026) and 19 distinct studies. Reading them as a collective body reveals 4 signals not visible in any single paper:

### Signal 1: IgG-mediated pathology is no longer hypothetical — it's experimentally proven

Chen et al. (2026, Cell Reports Medicine) demonstrates that IgG from Long COVID patients, injected into naive mice, reproduces symptoms. This is the gold-standard experimental demonstration of autoantibody pathogenicity — the same protocol that proved myasthenia gravis was autoimmune 50 years ago. This elevated certainty floor means:
- GPCR autoantibody hypotheses (Azcue et al., Wallukat, Bynke) gain a validation anchor
- The IgG allodynia transfer (Mignolet) provides a complementary pain-specific axis
- Immunoadsorption/plasmapheresis trials gain mechanistic rationale (not just clinical signal)

### Signal 2: Fatigue ≠ peripheral inflammation — the mechanism is elsewhere

Omdal et al. (2026, n=96 matched) shows severe fatigue (fVAS 63 vs 5, FACIT-F 21.5 vs 49) with zero peripheral biomarker differences (CRP, TNF-α, IL-6, HSP90α, Serpin F1, hemopexine, APOA4 all normal). This functionally forces the fatigue mechanism debate toward three candidates:
1. CNS-confined neuroinflammation (microglia, complement, glymphatic)
2. Epigenetic/metabolic reprogramming (Mobius methylation signature)
3. Predictive processing derangement (brain receiving noise as threat signal)

### Signal 3: Mast cells are transitioning from clinical anecdote to systematic evidence

Theoharides 2026 causal/systematic review marks a tipping point. Combined with Savigamin POTS-MCAS-urticaria overlap, existing mcas-domain6 work, and the ISTH mast-cell-coagulation connection, mast cells are no longer a fringe MCAS theory — they are a neuroimmune amplifier with systematic review backing.

### Signal 4: The vascular pillar is converging

Jones 2026 micro-coagulation + Cataldo 2026 cerebral microvascular MRI + existing Cervia-Hasler (Science 2024) complement activation + Pretorius microclots form a coherent vascular-endothelial-mechanism pillar. This isn't about visible clots — it's about microcirculatory dysfunction that standard imaging misses.
