## 1. Evidence landscape

- **Strong (≥0.60):** Moustardas 2026 (0.75; Nat Commun; prospective, n=132; clinical + tear proteomics + corneal confocal). Smit 2026 (0.65; n=526 pupillometry; PCS pupillary autonomic dysregulation, p<0.001).
- **Medium (0.40–0.59):** Naseri 2025 (0.55; dry-eye meta-analysis). Azcue 2025 (0.55; corneal confocal SFN in PCC+ME/CFS — already in paper). Cañadas 2023 (0.50; corneal confocal long COVID — already in paper). Azcue 2023 (0.50; SFN+dysautonomia PCC+ME/CFS — already in paper).
- **Weak (<0.40):** Johansson 2022 (0.40; uncontrolled neurorehab visual assessment).
- **Null:** none found. Flagged as possible publication bias, not as confirmatory absence.
- **Indirect biochemical links:** strong. Corneal confocal microscopy is already a validated SFN window in ME/CFS (Azcue). Tear CD4+ T-cell dysregulation signature intersects documented ME/CFS T-cell abnormalities and matches severe/fatal-COVID tissue signatures.

## 2. Internal contradictions

None. All papers point the same direction (post-COVID ocular/autonomic/small-fiber pathology). No fundamental or resolvable contradictions requiring open-question framing.

## 3. Evidence summary

The evidence establishes that mild COVID-19 can produce a persistent ocular syndrome undetectable by standard ophthalmic testing, mechanistically attributable to chronic T-cell-mediated neuroinflammation, autonomic (pupillary/oculomotor) dysfunction, and corneal small-fiber neuropathy. Strongest supportable claim: the same dysautonomia + SFN + immune-dysregulation triad the paper already models as core ME/CFS pathophysiology manifests measurably in the eye after mild COVID, and is quantifiable via corneal confocal microscopy and tear-fluid proteomics. Must remain speculative: direct equivalence of post-COVID ocular syndrome to ME/CFS (Moustardas cohort was NOT screened for ME/CFS criteria). Primary integration targets: ch08-neurological (retinal-corneal CNS window, SFN, dysautonomia), ch07-immune-dysfunction (T-cell signature), ch03 sec-10-ocular (clinical features), ch10-cardiovascular (corneal confocal biomarker), hypothesis-registry, appendix-h.

## 4. Clinical relevance

**MEDIUM** — mechanistic context + biomarker/diagnostic value (corneal confocal microscopy and tear proteomics as accessible non-invasive windows into neuro-immune status). Not a treatment topic; no dosing/harm analysis required. **Subset applicability:** post-viral-onset ME/CFS most directly; ocular-symptom prevalence across severity levels unknown in ME/CFS cohorts (Moustardas studied post-COVID, not ME/CFS-diagnosed patients) → state "severity applicability unknown."

## 5. Integration decision

**PROCEED.**
- Criteria met: two papers ≥0.60 (Moustardas 0.75, Smit 0.65); evidence not uniformly null; only 1/7 papers <0.40 (~14%, ≤50% threshold).
- Framing: primarily **confirmatory** — the paper's existing causal cascade (CNS energy crisis → dysautonomia + immune dyscoordination + SFN) already predicts ocular manifestation. Integration adds (a) direct confirmatory citation, (b) tear-fluid proteomics as a novel biomarker window not previously covered, (c) adult-onset strabismus / oculomotor involvement as a new clinical sign. Must NOT overclaim ME/CFS equivalence.
