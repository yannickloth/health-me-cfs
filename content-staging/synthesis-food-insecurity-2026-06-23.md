## Internal Contradictions
None. Lin and Datta both find positive LC→FI association; consistent in direction.

## Evidence Summary

Food insecurity is well-established as a Long COVID consequence (3 large studies ≥0.70). ME/CFS socioeconomic burden — employment loss, poverty, health costs — is well-documented and predictively consistent with food insecurity. The anchor paper (Dey 2026) directly fills the ME/CFS-specific gap. Integration as **socioeconomic disease burden dimension**, not pathophysiological mechanism.

Strongest supportable mechanistic claim: none — this is epidemiology, not mechanism.

## Clinical Relevance Assessment

**MEDIUM.** Socioeconomic finding; no direct treatment implications. Affects clinical assessment (screening), referral pathways (social work, dietitian), disability advocacy. Applicable to all severity levels (severe/bedbound likely most affected but data absent). Not treatment-related — no safety data needed.
