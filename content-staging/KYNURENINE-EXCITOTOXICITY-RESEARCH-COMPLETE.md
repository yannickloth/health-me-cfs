## Key Findings Summary

### Kynurenine Pathway (established mechanisms)
- **Immune activation** → IDO upregulation → tryptophan → kynurenine conversion
- **Peripheral inflammation** → kynurenine accumulation in brain (Cervenka2017)
- **Dual metabolite effects:** KYNA (neuroprotective, NMDA antagonist) vs QA (neurotoxic, NMDA agonist) (Schwarcz2012)
- **Exercise protection:** Skeletal muscle clears kynurenine, reducing brain accumulation (Cervenka2017)

### ME/CFS-Specific Evidence
- **Che2025:** Multi-omics shows kynurenine-serotonin pathway dysregulation, worsens post-exercise
- **Chojnacki2026:** Clinical study (n=40) shows elevated QA, low KYNA, probiotic improves KYNA/QA ratio 45%
- **Yan2026:** Rat fatigue model shows inhibited kynurenine pathway, treatment restores it

### Sleep/Glymphatic Disruption (established mechanism)
- **Xie2013:** Sleep increases interstitial space 60%, enhances glymphatic clearance of neurotoxins
- **Relevance to ME/CFS:** Unrefreshing sleep may impair glymphatic function, causing metabolite accumulation (kynurenine metabolites, glutamate)

### Mitochondrial Dysfunction (emerging evidence)
- **FanoIllic2026:** Comprehensive review (1990-2025) shows oxidative stress, redox imbalance, impaired energy regulation in ME/CFS muscle
- **Che2025:** Impaired citric acid cycle, β-oxidation, urea cycle in ME/CFS, worsens post-exercise
- **Zhang2026MitochondriaFatigue:** Genetic evidence (MR, n=449,019) links AKAP10, MTHFD1L to chronic fatigue

### EAAT2/Excitotoxicity (extrapolation from other disorders)
- **Elbaga2026, Manisha2026, Zhang2026PirBEAAT2:** EAAT2 downregulation → glutamate accumulation → NMDA overactivation → excitotoxicity (Alzheimer's models)
- **Gap:** No direct EAAT2 studies in ME/CFS
- **Hypothesis:** Kynurenine pathway activation + mitochondrial dysfunction + sleep disruption → QA accumulation + energy depletion → EAAT2 impairment (Na+/K+ gradient collapse) → glutamate excitotoxicity

## Evidence Gaps

### Critical Gaps (High Priority)
1. **Direct ME/CFS EAAT2 studies** - No post-mortem brain, CSF transporter, or in vivo imaging
2. **Sleep architecture in ME/CFS** - Limited polysomnography with glymphatic assessment
3. **Kynurenine metabolite profiling** - Limited comprehensive KYNA/QA/3-HK measurements, pre/post-exercise
4. **Na+/K+ gradient studies** - No direct ATPase function studies in ME/CFS neurons/astrocytes
5. **Glymphatic function in ME/CFS** - No DTI-ALPS or direct flow measurements

## Contradictions

### 1. Kynurenine Pathway Direction
- **Activation:** Che2025 (ME/CFS), Cervenka2017 (inflammation)
- **Inhibition:** Yan2026 (rat fatigue model)
- **Possible resolution:** Acute activation → chronic depletion; different study timepoints

### 2. Mitochondrial Dysfunction Specificity
- **Primary:** Zhang2026 (genetic), FanoIllic2026 (review)
- **Secondary:** Che2025 (worsens post-exercise)
- **Possible resolution:** Genetic predisposition + environmental trigger

### 3. Glutamate Excitotoxicity in ME/CFS
- **Indirect:** Chojnacki2026 (elevated QA = NMDA agonist)
- **Missing:** Direct extracellular glutamate, NMDA activity measurements
- **Status:** Hypothetical, requires direct measurement

## Feedback Loop Hypothesis (Proposed Structure)

Based on collected evidence, the kynurenine-excitotoxicity feedback loop in ME/CFS can be structured as:

1. **Upstream trigger:** Chronic immune activation (infection, dysbiosis) → IDO upregulation
2. **Kynurenine shunt:** Tryptophan → kynurenine pathway → QA accumulation
3. **NAD+ depletion:** QA → NAD+ pathway diverts tryptophan from NAD+ synthesis → mitochondrial dysfunction
4. **Sleep disruption:** ME/CFS unrefreshing sleep → impaired glymphatic clearance → QA + glutamate accumulation
5. **Mitochondrial collapse:** Energy depletion → Na+/K+ ATPase failure → EAAT2 impairment
6. **Excitotoxicity:** Glutamate accumulation → NMDA overactivation → neuronal damage → cognitive symptoms
7. **Amplification:** Neuronal damage → inflammatory signals → further IDO activation (feedback to 1)

**Key connections supported by literature:**
- 1→2: Cervenka2017, Schwarcz2012, Che2025
- 2→3: Cervenka2017, Yan2026 (metabolic shunt)
- 3→5: FanoIllic2026, Zhang2026MitochondriaFatigue (energy defects)
- 4: Xie2013glymphatic (sleep-dependent clearance)
- 5→6: Elbaga2026, Manisha2026, Zhang2026PirBEAAT2 (EAAT2 mechanism, extrapolation)
- 2→6: Schwarcz2012, Chojnacki2026 (QA as NMDA agonist)
- 6→1: Jin2026PEMMechanism (neuroinflammation feedback)

**Evidence gaps requiring future research:**
- Direct EAAT2 measurement in ME/CFS
- Na+/K+ gradient studies in ME/CFS neurons
- Glymphatic function assessment in ME/CFS
- Kynurenine metabolite time-course studies
