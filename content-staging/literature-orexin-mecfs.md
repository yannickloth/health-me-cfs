## Papers Processed

### 1. LopezAmador2025orexin (UPDATED — entry already existed, corrected and expanded)

**Citation:** López-Amador N. An integrative review on the orexin system and hypothalamic dysfunction in myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome: implications for precision medicine. *Exploration of Neuroprotective Therapy*. 2025;5:1004112.
**DOI:** 10.37349/ent.2025.1004112
**Study type:** Integrative review (Whittemore–Knafl five-stage method), 27 studies
**Certainty:** 0.55

Key findings:
- Consistent evidence of reduced orexin-A levels in ME/CFS across 27 studies
- Variable orexin-B responses identified as candidate subtyping biomarker
- Hypothalamic orexinergic dysfunction is a central feature of ME/CFS
- Neuroendocrine findings: hypocortisolism alongside orexin-A reduction (CSF orexin-A ~250 ± 30 pg/mL reported)
- No ME/CFS-specific DORA trials yet; recommends controlled trials as high priority
- Correction: previous bib entry had wrong DOI (10.37349/ent.2025.00112) and wrong title — fixed

### 2. Grossberg2011orexinLethargy — NEW

**Citation:** Grossberg AJ et al. Inflammation-induced lethargy is mediated by suppression of orexin neuron activity. *J Neurosci*. 2011;31(31):11376–11386.
**PMID:** 21813697 | **DOI:** 10.1523/JNEUROSCI.2311-11.2011
**Study type:** Animal experimental (rat/mouse), pharmacological rescue
**Certainty:** 0.65

Key findings:
- LPS inflammation suppresses perifornical hypothalamic orexin neuron activity
- CSF orexin-A levels decrease during inflammation; dark-phase activation blocked
- Central orexin-A replacement reverses inflammation-induced lethargy (causal proof)
- Orexin neurons lack direct cytokine receptors — suppression via lateral hypothalamic neurotensin interneurons
- Prostaglandin and melanocortin pathways NOT required (important negative result)

ME/CFS relevance: Establishes cytokine → neurotensin interneuron → orexin suppression → lethargy circuit. Chronic low-grade ME/CFS neuroinflammation would activate this pathway persistently.

### 3. Gaykema2009orexinSickness — NEW

**Citation:** Gaykema RPA, Goehler LE. Lipopolysaccharide challenge-induced suppression of Fos in hypothalamic orexin neurons: their potential role in sickness behavior. *Brain, Behavior, and Immunity*. 2009;23(7):926–930.
**PMID:** 19328847 | **DOI:** 10.1016/j.bbi.2009.03.005
**Study type:** Rat in vivo, Fos immunohistochemistry
**Certainty:** 0.60

Key findings:
- LPS reduces Fos expression in lateral orexin neurons during exploration (47% → 25%) and dark-phase activity (42% → 9%)
- Histaminergic tuberomammillary neurons show parallel suppression (64% → 11%)
- Co-shutdown of orexin–histamine arousal axis mediates sickness inactivity
- Differential effect: lateral orexin neurons > medial, suggesting motivational/reward drive more affected than homeostatic drive

ME/CFS relevance: Fos evidence for context-dependent suppression. In ME/CFS, chronic immune activation → chronic orexin–histamine suppression → persistent low arousal drive.

### 4. Weymann2014orexinChemoFatigue — NEW

**Citation:** Weymann KB, Wood LJ, Zhu X, Marks DL. A role for orexin in cytotoxic chemotherapy-induced fatigue. *Brain, Behavior, and Immunity*. 2014;37:84–94.
**PMID:** 24216337 | **DOI:** 10.1016/j.bbi.2013.11.003
**Study type:** Rodent experimental, pharmacological rescue
**Certainty:** 0.55

Key findings:
- CAF chemotherapy induces hypothalamic neuroinflammation (IL-1R1, IL-6, TNFα, MCP-1 upregulated)
- Orexin neuron cFos and CSF orexin-A both suppressed in fatigued animals
- Central orexin-A (1 µg) fully restores locomotor activity in treated animals
- Causal direction established: orexin depletion drives fatigue, not merely accompanies it

ME/CFS relevance: Same cytokine → hypothalamic inflammation → orexin suppression → fatigue pathway. Pharmacological rescue result translates to therapeutic hypothesis.

### 5. Ito2023orexinREM — NEW

**Citation:** Ito H et al. Deficiency of orexin signaling during sleep is involved in abnormal REM sleep architecture in narcolepsy. *PNAS*. 2023;120(41):e2301951120.
**PMID:** 37796986 | **DOI:** 10.1073/pnas.2301951120
**Study type:** Mouse optogenetics + calcium imaging (causal)
**Certainty:** 0.65

Key findings:
- Orexin neuron activity: high during wake, synchronised bursting during NREM, silent before REM, subpopulation active during REM
- Subpopulation active during REM suppresses subsequent REM episodes and cataplexy
- Loss of orexin signaling → failure of REM gating → excessive REM intrusions and sleep fragmentation
- Optogenetic inhibition confirms causality (not just correlation)

ME/CFS relevance: Mechanistic basis for unrefreshing sleep: partial orexin deficiency → impaired REM gating → alpha intrusion into NREM → non-restorative sleep architecture. Directly links orexin dysfunction to cardinal ME/CFS symptom.

### 6. Rauf2025narcolepsyOrexin — NEW

**Citation:** Rauf R et al. Orexin deficiency in narcolepsy: molecular mechanisms, clinical phenotypes, and emerging therapeutic frontiers. *Brain and Behavior*. 2025;15(10):e70984.
**PMID:** 41076550 | **DOI:** 10.1002/brb3.70984
**Study type:** Narrative review
**Certainty:** 0.60 (narcolepsy mechanisms); 0.35 (ME/CFS applicability)

Key findings:
- >90% of NT1 patients: CSF orexin-A < 110 pg/mL + HLA-DQB1*06:02
- Up to 95% of hypothalamic orexin neurons destroyed by autoimmune mechanism
- Post-infectious trigger confirmed: H1N1 influenza + Pandemrix vaccination → temporal NT1 clustering
- Danavorexton (OX2R-selective agonist): +11.1 points on MWT, outperforming modafinil
- ALKS 2680 and gene therapy also in development pipeline

ME/CFS relevance: Post-infectious orexin loss mechanism (H1N1 → NT1) directly parallels ME/CFS viral triggers. ME/CFS may occupy intermediate CSF orexin-A zone (110–200 pg/mL). OX2R agonists relevant therapeutic class.

Null/caution: No direct ME/CFS orexin measurement in this paper; analogy not empirically tested.

### 7. Ruhrländer2025orexinPASC — NEW

**Citation:** Ruhrländer J, Syntila S, Schieffer E, Schieffer B. The orexin system and its impact on the autonomic nervous and cardiometabolic system in post-acute sequelae of COVID-19. *Biomedicines*. 2025;13(3):545.
**PMID:** 40149526 | **DOI:** 10.3390/biomedicines13030545
**Study type:** Narrative review
**Certainty:** 0.45

Key findings:
- Orexin regulates baroreflex sensitivity, sympathetic tone, catecholamine release, electrolyte balance, circadian rhythms
- Disruption plausibly explains POTS, orthostatic hypotension, endothelial dysfunction, insulin resistance in PASC
- No human PASC orexin measurement data presented
- Most mechanistic evidence extrapolated from animal studies

ME/CFS relevance: PASC/ME/CFS share orthostatic intolerance and fatigue. Orexin → autonomic → POTS pathway applies to ME/CFS. Extends orexin's role beyond sleep into cardiovascular/autonomic dysregulation.

### 8. Heinicke2025orexinCOVID — NEW

**Citation:** Heinicke U et al. Systemic role of orexin A, substance P, bradykinin, and DABK in severe COVID-19 and 2.5-yr follow-ups: an observational study. *BJA Open*. 2025;14:100415.
**PMID:** 40529720 | **DOI:** 10.1016/j.bjao.2025.100415
**Study type:** Single-centre observational cohort; 78 ICU patients + 14 survivors at 2.5 yr + 14 controls; 423 ELISA measurements
**Certainty:** 0.50

Key findings:
- Plasma orexin-A correlates with ICU survival (Cohen's d=0.4) and inversely with LOS (r=−0.26, p=0.02)
- Bradykinin and substance P reduced acutely during COVID-19
- Substance P remains elevated at 2.5-year follow-up (potential Long COVID neuropsychological marker)
- Bradykinin–substance P–orexin axis dynamics linked to disease severity

ME/CFS relevance: First observational data measuring plasma orexin-A in a post-COVID follow-up cohort. Long-term neuropeptide perturbations consistent with post-infectious orexin hypothesis.

Limitations: ICU population not directly analogous to ME/CFS; sedation confounds orexin-A; single-centre.

### 9. Sakurai1998orexin — NEW

**Citation:** Sakurai T et al. Orexins and orexin receptors: a family of hypothalamic neuropeptides and G protein-coupled receptors that regulate feeding behavior. *Cell*. 1998;92(5):573–585.
**PMID:** 9527442 | **DOI:** 10.1016/S0092-8674(02)09256-5
**Study type:** Foundational discovery (reverse pharmacology, animal)
**Certainty:** 0.90 (for mechanism); N/A for ME/CFS

Key findings:
- Discovery of orexin-A and orexin-B as novel hypothalamic neuropeptides
- Two orphan GPCRs identified: OX1R (orexin-A selective), OX2R (non-selective)
- Lateral/posterior hypothalamic localisation
- Central administration stimulates feeding; fasting up-regulates mRNA
- Established orexin system as energy-status/arousal integrator

ME/CFS relevance: Foundational reference only; all orexin-ME/CFS biology traces to this paper.

## Negative/Null Results Note

No specific null-result paper for orexin in ME/CFS was found in the published literature. The López-Amador 2025 review (27 studies) reports "consistent evidence of reduced orexin-A" — not a null finding. One study cited within that review (Myhill et al., reference [47] in the review) reportedly found non-significant CSF orexin-A differences between ME/CFS and controls, but this is cited within the review and not a standalone published paper accessible via PubMed search. The absence of a dedicated null-result paper should be noted in integration: the evidence base is small and methodologically heterogeneous.

The Heinicke 2025 ICU study is the only paper with direct orexin-A measurements in a post-COVID context; the lack of ME/CFS-specific CSF orexin measurements is a major gap.
