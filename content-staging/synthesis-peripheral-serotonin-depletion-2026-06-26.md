## Contradiction Analysis

**mathe2025noserotonin (0.60) vs wong2023serotonin (0.85):** Certainty diff 0.25 > 0.15 band → not indistinguishable. But compartment explanation: Mathé measured *serum* serotonin (<1% of total — body keeps it at nanomolar levels), while Wong documented *platelet* storage and *enterochromaffin* absorption defects. Non-contradictory when compartments considered. Framing: cite both; note serum measurement alone may miss platelet-pool depletion.

## Internal Contradiction Detection

None fundamental. Compartmental explanation resolves the Mathé/Wong discrepancy. Anderson's methodological concerns are compatible — they don't contradict evidence, they qualify confidence.

## Evidence Summary

Peripheral serotonin depletion is supported by converging evidence across ME/CFS, Long COVID, POTS, and fibromyalgia. The mechanism: immune activation (IFN-γ, IL-6) → IDO upregulation → tryptophan diversion from serotonin toward kynurenine + enterochromaffin cell damage → reduced gut serotonin production + impaired platelet serotonin storage. This depletion impairs multiple functions: mitochondrial energy production, vagal afferent signalling, platelet-dependent vascular tone, gut motility, and immune modulation (via 5-HT receptors on immune cells).

Strongest mechanistic claim: Inflammation-driven tryptophan diversion + enterochromaffin damage drives peripheral serotonin depletion across post-infectious conditions. This is a downstream consequence of the already-documented IDO/kynurenine pathway shift.

Must remain speculative: Whether restoring peripheral serotonin (via tryptophan/5-HTP supplementation) reverses symptoms. No ME/CFS-specific RCTs exist. SSRI data in POTS (worsening) suggests central serotonin reuptake inhibition is not the answer for peripheral deficiency.

Not supported by evidence: Direct causation (cross-sectional/observational data only); CNS serotonin changes (Lee2024 mouse model shows central hyperactivity despite peripheral depletion — distinct pools).

## Clinical Relevance Assessment

**Clinical relevance: MEDIUM** — mechanistic context for treatment decisions. Specifically:
- Explains why SSRIs worsen POTS symptoms (mar2014ssripots)
- Provides cautionary rationale against serotonergic drugs in ME/CFS
- Identifies platelet serotonin measurement as potential biomarker
- No direct dosing data for tryptophan/5-HTP in ME/CFS — all mechanistic inference

**Patient subset applicability:** Likely all severity levels — serotonin depletion affects fundamental functions (energy metabolism, autonomic regulation, vascular tone). Evidence strongest in POTS/orthostatic intolerance subset.
