# Research Report: COVID-19 Immune Reset, ME/CFS Overlap, and Implications for Vaccine Efficacy and Meningitis Susceptibility

Date: 2026-03-18

---

## 1. COVID-19 and Immune System "Reset" / Damage

### 1.1 T Cell Exhaustion in COVID-19 and Long COVID

**DIRECT EVIDENCE:**

- **Chen-Camano R et al. (2025)** "T-cell exhaustion in COVID-19: what do we know?" *Frontiers in Immunology*. DOI: 10.3389/fimmu.2025.1678149. PMID: 41235245.
  - Comprehensive review describing T-cell exhaustion as "a terminal state of immune dysfunction" in COVID-19, marked by impaired proliferation, reduced cytokine production, and sustained expression of inhibitory receptors (PD-1, TIM-3, CTLA-4). Links exhausted T-cell phenotypes to severe disease, delayed viral clearance, and Long COVID persistence.

- **Roe K (2021)** "A role for T-cell exhaustion in Long COVID-19 and severe outcomes for several categories of COVID-19 patients." *J Neurosci Res*. DOI: 10.1002/jnr.24917. PMID: 34288064.
  - Proposes that SARS-CoV-2 combined with latent coinfections (e.g., *Toxoplasma gondii*) induces mutual T-cell exhaustion, explaining variable mortality and Long COVID symptoms.

- **Gupta G et al. (2025)** "Mechanistic Insights Into Long Covid: Viral Persistence, Immune Dysregulation, and Multi-Organ Dysfunction." *Comprehensive Physiology*. DOI: 10.1002/cph4.70019. PMID: 40474772.
  - Synthesizes evidence that viral persistence triggers "cytokine imbalances, T-cell exhaustion" driving persistent Long COVID manifestations paralleling ME/CFS pathophysiology.

### 1.2 Persistent Lymphopenia After COVID-19

**DIRECT EVIDENCE:**

- **An H et al. (2025)** "Persistent lymphocytopenia in convalescent patients with COVID-19: dysregulated B cell, CD4+ T cell, and treg compartments in 7-12% of moderate-severe cases." *Front Cell Infect Microbiol*. DOI: 10.3389/fcimb.2025.1693743. PMID: 41141591.
  - Longitudinal study of 279 unvaccinated COVID-19 patients: **approximately 7-12% of moderate-to-severe survivors displayed persistent lymphopenia** affecting B cells, CD4+ T cells, and Tregs. Demonstrates that COVID-19 causes lasting immune cell depletion in a significant minority.

### 1.3 Immune Imprinting / Original Antigenic Sin

**DIRECT EVIDENCE:**

- **Yisimayi A et al. (2024)** "Repeated Omicron exposures override ancestral SARS-CoV-2 immune imprinting." *Nature*. DOI: 10.1038/s41586-023-06753-7. PMID: 37993710.
  - Demonstrates that vaccine-induced immune imprinting from wildtype-strain vaccines constrains antibody responses to Omicron variants. Repeated natural infections can partially overcome this.

- **Jian F et al. (2025)** "Evolving antibody response to SARS-CoV-2 antigenic shift from XBB to JN.1." *Nature*. DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08315-x. PMID: 39510125.
  - Shows that ancestral immunity-derived antibodies "inhibit activation of Omicron-specific naive B cells," demonstrating mechanistic immune imprinting.

- **Delgado JF et al. (2022)** "SARS-CoV-2 Spike Protein Vaccine-Induced Immune Imprinting Reduces Nucleocapsid Protein Antibody Response." *J Immunol Res*. DOI: 10.1155/2022/8287087. PMID: 35935586.
  - mRNA vaccines targeting spike suppress subsequent anti-nucleocapsid antibody responses after breakthrough infection, illustrating how imprinting narrows immunological focus.

### 1.4 Does COVID-19 Cause "Immune Amnesia" (Like Measles)?

**MIXED / MOSTLY NEGATIVE EVIDENCE:**

- **Jokiranta ST et al. (2023)** "Stable Levels of Antibodies Against Unrelated Toxoid Vaccines After COVID-19." *Pathog Immun*. DOI: 10.20411/pai.v8i2.627. PMID: 38347963.
  - **Key negative finding:** Measured IgG against diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis in 59 COVID-19 patients. Found "no significant differences in the unrelated vaccine responses" and "could not detect significant decline in overall humoral immunity during or after COVID-19 infection." Contradicts measles-like immune amnesia hypothesis for SARS-CoV-2.

- **Di Guardo G (2024)** "Measles Virus-Induced Immune Amnesia and SARS-CoV-2 Evolution." *Pathogens*. DOI: 10.3390/pathogens13030226. PMID: 38535569.
  - Editorial noting measles virus "diminishes preexisting antibodies" but examining whether COVID-19 has similar effects. Does not provide direct evidence of COVID-19 immune amnesia.

- **Al Meslamani AZ (2023)** "Will SARS-CoV-2 Impact the Incidence and Nature of Other Respiratory Infections?" *Expert Rev Clin Immunol*. DOI: 10.1080/1744666X.2023.2263646. PMID: 37740687.
  - Raises "immune amnesia" as a concern for SARS-CoV-2 but presents it as a question, not established finding.

**ASSESSMENT:** Unlike measles, SARS-CoV-2 does NOT appear to cause broad-spectrum immune amnesia (loss of pre-existing antibodies to other pathogens). However, it does cause persistent lymphopenia in a subset (7-12%), T cell exhaustion, and immune imprinting that constrains future SARS-CoV-2 responses. These are different mechanisms from measles-like immune erasure.

### 1.5 COVID-19 Effects on Bone Marrow and Long-Lived Plasma Cells

- **Nguyen DC et al. (2025)** "SARS-CoV-2-specific plasma cells are not durably established in the bone marrow long-lived compartment after mRNA vaccination." *Nat Med*. DOI: 10.1038/s41591-024-03278-y. PMID: 39333316.
  - SARS-CoV-2-specific serum antibodies waned within 3-6 months after vaccination, associated with IgG non-long-lived plasma cells. Contrasts with durable influenza and tetanus responses. *Note: this is about vaccine-induced immunity to SARS-CoV-2, not damage to pre-existing immunity.*

- **Schulz AR et al. (2023)** "SARS-CoV-2 specific plasma cells acquire long-lived phenotypes in human bone marrow." *EBioMedicine*. DOI: 10.1016/j.ebiom.2023.104735. PMID: 37556944.
  - Somewhat contradicting the above, found evidence of durable bone marrow plasma cells from vaccination.

## 2. COVID-19 and ME/CFS Immune Overlap

### 2.1 Comparable Immune Profiles

**DIRECT EVIDENCE:**

- **Petrov S et al. (2025)** "Comparable Immune Alterations and Inflammatory Signatures in ME/CFS and Long COVID." *Biomedicines*. DOI: 10.3390/biomedicines13123001. PMID: 41463013.
  - **Key study:** 190 participants (65 ME/CFS, 54 Long COVID, 71 healthy controls). Both patient groups had **lower lymphocytes, CD8+ T cells, NK cells** and **higher pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, TNF, IL-4, IL-10)**. **No meaningful differences between the two patient groups** across measured biomarkers. Concluded "ME/CFS and Long COVID display comparable immune and inflammatory profiles."

- **Ivanovska M et al. (2025)** "Differential Characteristics and Comparison Between Long-COVID Syndrome and ME/CFS." *Biomedicines*. DOI: 10.3390/biomedicines13112797. PMID: 41301889.
  - Review identifying shared mechanisms: "viral persistence, immune dysregulation, endothelial dysfunction, and autoimmunity."

- **Shahbaz S et al. (2025)** "Integrated immune, hormonal, and transcriptomic profiling reveals sex-specific dysregulation in long COVID patients with ME/CFS." *Cell Rep Med*. DOI: 10.1016/j.xcrm.2025.102449. PMID: 41205594.
  - Females show "shift toward myelopoiesis, reduced lymphocytes, increased neutrophils/monocytes, and depleted regulatory T cells." Sex-specific differences with females showing more severe inflammation.

### 2.2 CD8 T Cell Dysfunction Shared Between Long COVID and ME/CFS

**DIRECT EVIDENCE:**

- **Gil A et al. (2024)** "Identification of CD8 T-cell dysfunction associated with symptoms in ME/CFS and Long COVID." *Brain Behav Immun Health*. DOI: 10.1016/j.bbih.2023.100720. PMID: 38327880.
  - Both Long COVID and ME/CFS patients demonstrated "significantly diminished capacity to produce both cytokines, IFN-gamma or TNF-alpha" compared to healthy controls. Treatment with nebulized antioxidant improved CD8 T-cell function and decreased symptom severity by ~54%.

### 2.3 Shared Mechanisms: Endothelial Senescence

- **Nunes M et al. (2026)** "Virus-induced endothelial senescence as a cause and driving factor for ME/CFS and long COVID." *Cell Death Dis*. DOI: 10.1038/s41419-025-08162-2. PMID: 41513611.
  - Proposes that immune system abnormalities prevent clearance of senescent endothelial cells, creating a "proinflammatory, pro-oxidative, procoagulant" secretory phenotype explaining multisystem symptoms in both conditions.

### 2.4 Viral Reactivation Patterns

- **Apostolou E et al. (2022)** "Saliva antibody-fingerprint of reactivated latent viruses after mild/asymptomatic COVID-19 is unique in ME/CFS patients." *Front Immunol*. DOI: 10.3389/fimmu.2022.949787. PMID: 36341457.
  - ME/CFS patients showed significantly stronger herpesvirus reactivation antibody responses after mild COVID-19 than healthy controls. Particularly elevated EBNA1 IgG levels.

### 2.5 Do Post-COVID ME/CFS Patients Show WORSE Immune Dysfunction?

**ASSESSMENT (extrapolation):** Petrov et al. (2025) found comparable (not worse) immune profiles between ME/CFS and Long COVID. However, this does not address the specific scenario of someone with pre-existing ME/CFS who then gets COVID-19. The Apostolou et al. (2022) finding that ME/CFS patients show stronger viral reactivation after even mild COVID suggests ME/CFS patients may be more immunologically vulnerable to COVID-19's secondary effects. No study directly compares pre-COVID ME/CFS vs post-COVID ME/CFS immune parameters.

## 3. NK Cell Dysfunction: Shared Feature

### 3.1 NK Cells in ME/CFS

**DIRECT EVIDENCE:**

- **Baraniuk JN et al. (2024)** "Meta-analysis of natural killer cell cytotoxicity in ME/CFS." *Front Immunol*. DOI: 10.3389/fimmu.2024.1440643. PMID: 39483457.
  - Meta-analysis of 28 studies: **NK cytotoxicity in ME/CFS was reduced to about half of healthy control levels** (effect size 0.96). Robust, reproducible finding.

- **Querec TD et al. (2023)** "Natural killer cytotoxicity in ME/CFS: a multi-site clinical assessment." *J Transl Med*. DOI: 10.1186/s12967-023-03958-2. PMID: 37013608.
  - Contradictory multi-site study: found no statistically significant differences, suggesting assay standardization issues.

- **TRPM7 calcium signaling** (PMID: 37509075, 2023): Impaired calcium influx in NK cells of ME/CFS patients.

### 3.2 NK Cells in Long COVID

- **Sasso EM et al. (2025)** "Low-Dose naltrexone restored TRPM3 ion channel function in NK cells from long COVID patients." *Front Mol Biosci*. DOI: 10.3389/fmolb.2025.1582967. PMID: 40458265.
  - NK cells from Long COVID patients show TRPM3 ion channel dysfunction with "reduced calcium influx." LDN (3-4.5 mg/day) restored function.

- **Sularea VM et al. (2025)** "Digitally Assessed Long COVID Symptomatology Is Associated With Lymphocyte Mitochondrial Dysfunction." *Open Forum Infect Dis*. DOI: 10.1093/ofid/ofaf447. PMID: 41255761.
  - Long COVID patients show "decreased mitochondrial membrane potential of CD56 bright NK cells."

## 4. Vaccine Efficacy in ME/CFS Context

### 4.1 Do ME/CFS Patients Respond Adequately to Vaccines?

**VERY LIMITED DIRECT EVIDENCE. No studies directly measuring standard vaccine responses in ME/CFS patients were found.**

- **Ruiz-Pablos M et al. (2024)** "Hypocortisolemic ASIA: a vaccine- and chronic infection-induced syndrome behind the origin of long COVID and myalgic encephalomyelitis." *Front Immunol*. DOI: 10.3389/fimmu.2024.1422940. PMID: 39044822.
  - Proposes ME/CFS involves "deficient CD4 T-cell response" and "immune depletion," which would theoretically impair vaccine responses, but this is mechanistic hypothesis, not measured vaccine immunogenicity data.

- **Ruiz-Pablos M et al. (2021)** PMID: 34867935. Proposes altered cellular immunity with "augmented Th2 response" and reduced CD4 T cell function in ME/CFS via EBV-mediated mechanisms.

**ASSESSMENT (extrapolation):** Given that ME/CFS involves:
- Reduced NK cell cytotoxicity (~50% of normal; Baraniuk 2024)
- Lower lymphocyte counts including CD8+ T cells (Petrov 2025)
- CD8 T-cell dysfunction with reduced IFN-gamma/TNF-alpha production (Gil 2024)
- Hypothesized CD4 T-cell deficiency (Ruiz-Pablos 2024)
- Complement system dysregulation (Glass 2025)

...it is biologically plausible that vaccine responses could be suboptimal, but this has not been directly studied. This is a significant research gap.

### 4.2 COVID + ME/CFS Compounding Immune Dysfunction?

**EXTRAPOLATION only.** No study has measured vaccine immunogenicity in patients with both pre-existing ME/CFS and prior COVID-19. The theoretical concern is valid: if both conditions independently reduce immune competence (particularly T cell function and NK cell activity), additive or synergistic effects could further impair vaccine responses. However, this remains undemonstrated.

### 4.3 Meningococcal Vaccine Responses in Post-Infectious Fatigue

**NO DIRECT DATA FOUND.** No studies examined meningococcal vaccine responses specifically in ME/CFS, Long COVID, or post-infectious fatigue populations.

## 5. Implications for Meningitis Susceptibility

### 5.1 Complement Deficiency and Meningococcal Disease

**WELL-ESTABLISHED EVIDENCE (not COVID-specific):**

- **Fijen CA et al. (1994)** "Complement deficiency predisposes for meningitis due to nongroupable meningococci." *Clin Infect Dis*. DOI: 10.1093/clinids/18.5.780. PMID: 8075270.
  - Demonstrates that inherited C7 or C8 complement deficiency carries "rather high frequency" of rare meningococcal infections.

- **van den Broek B et al. (2021)** "Neisseria meningitidis Serogroup Z Meningitis in a Child With Complement C8 Deficiency." *Pediatr Infect Dis J*. DOI: 10.1097/INF.0000000000003259. PMID: 34285166.
  - Case of meningitis in C8-deficient child. MenB-4C vaccine induced cross-reactive antibodies, suggesting vaccination value even in complement-deficient patients.

- **Schulz LP et al. (2025)** "Chronic meningococcal disease: Systematic literature review." *J Infect Public Health*. DOI: 10.1016/j.jiph.2025.102900. PMID: 40768968.
  - Of 126 chronic meningococcal disease cases, **23% had complement deficiencies**. Establishes strong complement-meningococcal connection.

### 5.2 Complement Dysregulation in Long COVID

**DIRECT EVIDENCE:**

- **Cervia-Hasler C et al. (2024)** "Persistent complement dysregulation with signs of thromboinflammation in active Long Covid." *Science*. DOI: 10.1126/science.adg7942. PMID: 38236961.
  - **Key paper:** Found "terminal complement system dysregulation and ongoing activation of the alternative and classical complement pathways" in Long COVID, with markers of hemolysis, tissue injury, and platelet activation.

- **Lee JD, Woodruff TM (2024)** "Complement(ing) long-COVID thromboinflammation and pathogenesis." *Trends Immunol*. DOI: 10.1016/j.it.2024.04.001. PMID: 38637200.

- **Bayarri-Olmos R et al. (2025)** "The role of complement in long COVID pathogenesis." *JCI Insight*. DOI: 10.1172/jci.insight.194314. PMID: 40857408.

### 5.3 Complement Dysregulation in ME/CFS

- **Glass KA et al. (2025)** "Extracellular vesicle proteomics uncovers energy metabolism, complement system, and endoplasmic reticulum stress response dysregulation postexercise in ME/CFS." *Clin Transl Med*. DOI: 10.1002/ctm2.70346. PMID: 40465195.
  - ME/CFS patients show "dysregulation in energy metabolism, the complement system, and the endoplasmic reticulum stress response," with complement proteins upregulated post-exercise.

### 5.4 COVID-19 and Complement Consumption

- **Wu MCL et al. (2025)** "Ischaemic Endothelial Necroptosis Induces Haemolysis and COVID-19 Angiopathy." *Nature*. DOI: 10.1038/s41586-025-09076-x. PMID: 40468079.
  - Identifies "complement-dependent red blood cell haemolysis" in COVID-19 microvasculature, demonstrating active complement consumption during infection.

### 5.5 Combined Risk Assessment for Meningococcal Susceptibility

**ASSESSMENT (extrapolation -- no direct evidence exists):**

The chain of evidence suggests plausible but unproven heightened meningococcal susceptibility in post-COVID ME/CFS patients:

1. **Complement is critical for meningococcal defense:** Complement deficiency is the single strongest risk factor for meningococcal disease (Fijen 1994; Schulz 2025: 23% of chronic cases have complement deficiency).

2. **Long COVID involves persistent complement dysregulation:** Terminal complement activation and consumption documented (Cervia-Hasler 2024, *Science*). This is complement *consumption*, not deficiency per se -- the complement system is overactive but potentially depleted.

3. **ME/CFS shows complement dysregulation:** Post-exercise complement protein changes (Glass 2025).

4. **COVID-19 does NOT cause measles-like immune amnesia:** Pre-existing antibodies to toxoid vaccines remain stable (Jokiranta 2023). So existing meningococcal vaccine antibodies would likely persist.

5. **However, functional immune capacity may be reduced:** NK cell cytotoxicity ~50% of normal in ME/CFS (Baraniuk 2024), CD8 T cell dysfunction (Gil 2024), persistent lymphopenia in 7-12% (An 2025).

**Bottom line:** The primary meningococcal defense pathway (complement) shows dysregulation in both Long COVID and ME/CFS. While this is distinct from complement deficiency (it's dysregulation/consumption rather than absence), chronic complement activation could theoretically reduce available complement for pathogen defense. Combined with reduced NK cell and T cell function, there is a biologically plausible (but undemonstrated) case for modestly increased meningococcal susceptibility in post-COVID ME/CFS patients. However:

- No study has measured meningococcal antibody levels in ME/CFS or Long COVID patients
- No epidemiological data shows increased meningococcal disease rates in these populations
- The Jokiranta 2023 study finding stable pre-existing vaccine antibodies is reassuring
- Complement *consumption* (Long COVID) differs mechanistically from complement *deficiency* (classical meningococcal risk factor)

## 6. Summary of Evidence Quality

| Question | Evidence Level | Key Finding |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| COVID causes T cell exhaustion | **Strong direct evidence** | Well-documented with PD-1/TIM-3/CTLA-4 markers (Chen-Camano 2025) |
| COVID causes persistent lymphopenia | **Direct evidence** | 7-12% of moderate-severe cases (An 2025) |
| COVID causes immune imprinting | **Strong direct evidence** | Constrains variant-specific responses (Yisimayi 2024, Jian 2025) |
| COVID causes measles-like immune amnesia | **Evidence against** | Pre-existing vaccine antibodies stable (Jokiranta 2023) |
| COVID reduces childhood vaccine immunity | **No direct evidence found** | Jokiranta 2023 suggests stability of toxoid vaccines |
| Long COVID and ME/CFS share immune profiles | **Strong direct evidence** | Comparable across all measured biomarkers (Petrov 2025) |

| NK cell dysfunction in ME/CFS | **Strong (meta-analysis)** | ~50% reduced cytotoxicity (Baraniuk 2024) |
| ME/CFS patients respond to vaccines | **No data** | Major research gap |
| Post-COVID ME/CFS vaccine efficacy | **No data** | Major research gap |
| Meningococcal vaccine response in ME/CFS | **No data** | No studies found |
| Complement dysregulation in Long COVID | **Strong direct evidence** | Terminal complement dysregulation (*Science* 2024) |
| Complement dysregulation in ME/CFS | **Emerging evidence** | Post-exercise complement changes (Glass 2025) |
| Increased meningococcal susceptibility | **Biological plausibility only** | No epidemiological or clinical data |
