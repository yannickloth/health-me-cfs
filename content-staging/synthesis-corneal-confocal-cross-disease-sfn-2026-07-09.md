# Synthesis: Corneal Confocal Microscopy as Cross-Disease SFN Biomarker
**Date:** 2026-07-09
**Topic slug:** corneal-confocal-cross-disease-sfn

## Evidence Landscape

**Strong (≥0.60):** 12 papers. Tavakoli 2015 (0.80, multinational normative CNFD/CNBD/CNFL values), Sommer 2025 (0.75, ~50% FMS with SFN, CCM detects ~59%), Ranathunga 2026 (0.75, SFN transdiagnostic in chronic pain — meta-analysis), Akowuah 2025 (0.72, MS corneal nerve loss/dendritic cell increase — meta-analysis), Oreskovic 2026 (0.70, scoping review across PD/MS/AD/ALS).

**Weak (<0.50):** 3 papers. Kubat 2026 (0.45, SNRI partially reversed corneal changes — single-center), Baghdasaryan 2026 (0.40, chemo neuropathy pilot), Cantrell 2025 (0.35, POTS — lower-tier journal).

**Missing:** Zero CCM studies in ME/CFS beyond Azcue 2025. No head-to-head CCM vs skin biopsy in ME/CFS. Limited longitudinal CCM data.

**Null:** None found (publication bias likely).

**Indirect biochemical links:** N/A — CCM is measurement tool, not pathway.

## Internal Contradictions
- Ghadban 2025: moderate CCM-vs-IENFD correlation → complementary, not replacement
- Jänsch 2024 (noted): FMS and SFN are distinct — CCM alone insufficient
- No direct inter-paper contradictions

## Evidence Summary

CCM is a validated, non-invasive, repeatable SFN biomarker with multinational normative data and cross-disease evidence (diabetic neuropathy, fibromyalgia, MS, PD, SLE, POTS, migraine, chemotherapy-induced). CCM detects SFN pathology with moderate sensitivity (~59% in FMS) and can track treatment response (preliminary). However, CCM and skin biopsy (IENFD) measure partially distinct aspects (moderate correlation). ME/CFS-specific CCM evidence is limited to Azcue 2025 (n=moderate, tortuosity as primary discriminator, AUC=0.720). Normative comparison against the Tavakoli database not yet performed for ME/CFS.

Strongest supportable claim: CCM provides a non-invasive, repeatable SFN assessment tool validated across multiple diseases. ME/CFS-specific normative comparison and longitudinal data are needed.

Speculative: disease-specific CCM signatures (MSA vs PD, non-length-dependent vs length-dependent) may eventually distinguish ME/CFS-associated SFN from other neuropathies, but current data are insufficient.

## Clinical Relevance
**MEDIUM** — Non-invasive alternative to skin biopsy; could reduce diagnostic delay for SFN in ME/CFS. Not yet validated as ME/CFS-specific diagnostic. Severity applicability: unknown.
