## Executive synthesis

The integrated literature points to a unifying frame: **the ISR is a context-dependent stress sensor that is hijacked from two opposite directions in ME/CFS**.

1. **HHV-6 miR-aU14** (Hennig/Prusty) drives DRP1-mediated mitochondrial fragmentation — bypassing or overwhelming the ISR's protective elongation response (Baron/Wiseman).
2. **SARS-CoV-2 N protein** (Aloise) suppresses PKR-ISR activation — preventing the protective response from initiating.
3. Once the acute insult resolves, **chronic low-grade ATF4 activity** persists and becomes net-harmful (Götz Drosophila), generating the metabolic/translational ceiling that produces PEM (Wang WASF3 link).
4. The system is **tissue-specific** (Omdal null peripheral result) — explaining why blood biomarkers consistently fail in ME/CFS.

This frame predicts that **the therapeutic window is not "activate" or "inhibit" the ISR but rather "restore context-appropriate dynamics"** — and that timing relative to PEM phase matters.

## Tier 1: Strong ME/CFS connection

### H1. Bidirectional ISR dysregulation hypothesis

**Mechanism:** Different ME/CFS subgroups have *opposite* ISR states depending on triggering pathogen. Post-HHV-6/EBV reactivation patients: ISR chronically activated (PERK↑, ATF4↑, WASF3↑). Post-SARS-CoV-2 patients (long COVID overlap): ISR suppressed during acute phase by N protein, then dysregulated rebound. This explains heterogeneity in trial responses to immunomodulators.

**Evidence link:** Wang 2023 (PERK↑ in muscle), Hennig 2022 (HHV-6 fragmentation), Aloise 2023 (SARS-CoV-2 N suppresses PKR-ISR), Christ 2023 (Omicron less suppressive — predicts milder long COVID phenotype with Omicron infections).

**Certainty:** 0.40

**Falsifiable prediction:** Stratifying ME/CFS patients by triggering pathogen will reveal opposite directions of phospho-eIF2α / ATF4 expression in muscle biopsy. ISRIB will worsen post-HHV-6 cases and improve post-SARS-CoV-2 cases (or vice versa). If ISR markers are random with respect to trigger → falsified.

---

### H2. PEM as biphasic ISR cycle

**Mechanism:** PEM is not a single state but a two-phase ISR cycle. **Phase 1 (0–24h post-exertion):** acute eIF2α phosphorylation — adaptive, translational pause, mitochondrial elongation (Baron/Wiseman). Symptom: immediate fatigue. **Phase 2 (24–72h):** ATF4-driven transcriptional reprogramming — maladaptive when chronic, suppresses OXPHOS subunits, induces CHOP. Symptom: delayed crash, cognitive symptoms. **Phase 3 (recovery):** GADD34-mediated dephosphorylation; if impaired → no full recovery, symptoms persist.

**Evidence link:** CostaMattioli/Walter 2020 (ISR temporal dynamics), Le 2025 (acute protection vs chronic harm), Götz 2025 (chronic ATF4 net-harmful).

**Certainty:** 0.45

**Falsifiable prediction:** Time-course muscle biopsy (CPET protocol: pre / +6h / +24h / +48h / +7d) will show distinct waves of phospho-eIF2α (early) and ATF4 target genes (delayed). Healthy controls will show resolution by 72h; ME/CFS will show persistence. If markers are flat or non-temporal → falsified.

---

### H3. WASF3–DRP1 axis convergence

**Mechanism:** Wang's WASF3 (ER stress → supercomplex disruption) and Prusty's HHV-6 miR-aU14 (→DRP1 fragmentation) converge on the same outcome (mitochondrial dysfunction) via parallel mechanisms. WASF3 may also promote DRP1 recruitment to mitochondria (currently unstudied). If true, ER stress and viral miRNA additively fragment mitochondria.

**Evidence link:** Wang 2023, Hennig 2022, Baron 2025 (ISR normally protects against DRP1).

**Certainty:** 0.30

**Falsifiable prediction:** WASF3 knockdown in HHV-6-miR-aU14-expressing cells will partially rescue mitochondrial morphology. DRP1 inhibitor (Mdivi-1) will rescue both WASF3-overexpression and miR-aU14-expression phenotypes. If WASF3 acts downstream of DRP1 only → falsified.

---

### H4. Tissue compartmentalisation explains biomarker failure

**Mechanism:** Omdal's null peripheral result is not a refutation of ISR involvement but evidence of compartmentalisation. ISR activation in ME/CFS is restricted to: skeletal muscle (Wang), CNS glia (Prusty postmortem), possibly endothelium and DRG sensory neurons. Peripheral blood mononuclear cells (PBMCs) are not the affected compartment. This predicts that **all PBMC-based ME/CFS biomarker studies systematically underestimate ISR involvement**.

**Evidence link:** Omdal 2026, Wang 2023 (muscle), Kasimir 2022 (brain), CostaMattioli 2020 (tissue-specific ISR).

**Certainty:** 0.55

**Falsifiable prediction:** Paired sampling (PBMC + muscle biopsy + skin biopsy + saliva) in same ME/CFS patients will show ISR markers elevated in muscle/skin (DRG-adjacent) but not PBMC. Effect sizes in tissue >> blood. If muscle and PBMC correlate → falsified.

---

### H5. Chronic ATF4 as metabolic ceiling

**Mechanism:** Götz 2025 shows GCN2–ATF4 suppression extends Drosophila lifespan; chronic ATF4 is net-harmful. In ME/CFS, persistent low-grade ATF4 activity creates a "metabolic ceiling": ATF4 represses mitochondrial OXPHOS subunit translation, induces aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases (amino acid stress response), and drives one-carbon/serine pathway flux away from energy production. This *is* the ceiling that PEM hits.

**Evidence link:** Götz 2025, Wang 2023, Le 2025.

**Certainty:** 0.40

**Falsifiable prediction:** ATF4 ChIP-seq in ME/CFS muscle will show enrichment at mitochondrial gene promoters (repressive) and amino acid metabolism genes (activating). Serum ATF4-target metabolites (FGF21, GDF15, asparagine, serine, glycine ratios) will be elevated. If ATF4 target signatures absent → falsified.

---

### H6. GDF15 as ISR readout in ME/CFS

**Mechanism:** GDF15 is a canonical ATF4 target, robustly elevated in mitochondrial diseases, and crosses tissue boundaries into serum (unlike most ATF4 outputs). It is the most likely *peripheral* biomarker of *tissue-specific* ISR activation. GDF15 also acts on hindbrain GFRAL receptors → nausea, anorexia, fatigue — symptoms shared with ME/CFS.

**Evidence link:** Le 2025 (eIF2α–ATF4), CostaMattioli 2020, mitochondrial disease literature.

**Certainty:** 0.55

**Falsifiable prediction:** Serum GDF15 will be elevated in ME/CFS vs. matched controls (effect size 0.4–0.7), correlate with PEM frequency, and rise post-CPET in ME/CFS but not controls. If no elevation → tissue-ISR hypothesis weakened. (Note: some ME/CFS GDF15 data already exists; needs systematic review.)

## Tier 2: Indirect connection, plausible

### H7. Sex-specific ISR thresholds

**Mechanism:** ME/CFS is ~4× more common in females. Estrogen modulates ER stress responses (XBP1 splicing, PERK sensitivity). Female cells may have a lower threshold for ISR activation post-viral insult, predisposing to chronic ATF4 persistence.

**Certainty:** 0.25

**Falsifiable prediction:** Sex-stratified PERK/ATF4 levels in muscle will show greater elevation in females. Estrogen withdrawal (postmenopausal) will modify ISR signature. Premenstrual symptom worsening will correlate with cycle-phase ATF4.

---

### H8. ISR and orthostatic intolerance via DRG

**Mechanism:** Dorsal root ganglion neurons are an unmyelinated, mitochondria-dense compartment vulnerable to ISR-driven energy failure. Small-fibre neuropathy (documented in ~40% of ME/CFS) may be a tissue-specific ISR readout. This connects ISR to POTS/OI mechanistically.

**Certainty:** 0.30

**Falsifiable prediction:** Skin biopsy (intraepidermal nerve fibre density) ME/CFS patients will show ATF4 target expression in DRG-derived fibres. ISRIB or salubrinal-type intervention will modify SFN-related symptoms before fatigue improves.

---

### H9. Mast cell activation as ISR amplifier

**Mechanism:** Mast cells are highly translationally active; tryptase release requires intact eIF2α function. Chronic ISR may paradoxically dysregulate mast cell granule maturation, producing the MCAS-like phenotype reported in subsets of ME/CFS.

**Certainty:** 0.20

**Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS patients with MCAS overlap will show ATF4 target signature in skin mast cells. ISR modulators will affect tryptase/histamine release dynamics.

---

### H10. ISR–autonomic loop

**Mechanism:** Brainstem nuclei (NTS, locus coeruleus) integrate visceral signals and modulate autonomic output. Local ISR activation in these nuclei (via HHV-6 reactivation, Kasimir) could explain autonomic dysfunction without peripheral nerve damage.

**Certainty:** 0.25

**Falsifiable prediction:** PET tracers for ISR markers (in development) will show brainstem signal asymmetry in ME/CFS. HRV abnormalities will correlate with brainstem rather than peripheral markers.

## Tier 3: Speculative cross-disease bridges

### H11. ME/CFS as "prodromal proteinopathy"

**Mechanism:** Chronic ISR activation is a feature of early Alzheimer's, ALS, prion disease. ME/CFS may represent a *reversible*, *early-stage* ISR dysregulation that, in some patients, progresses to neurodegeneration decades later. This would predict elevated late-life dementia incidence in ME/CFS cohorts.

**Certainty:** 0.15

**Falsifiable prediction:** Long-term cohort follow-up (20–30y) will show elevated AD/ALS incidence in ME/CFS. CSF p-tau/Aβ42 ratios in long-duration ME/CFS will trend toward AD direction.

---

### H12. Shared ISR signature: ME/CFS, fibromyalgia, long COVID, Gulf War illness

**Mechanism:** All four conditions share post-exertional symptom worsening, autonomic dysfunction, cognitive symptoms. Common upstream node = chronic tissue-specific ISR following diverse triggers (virus, chemical, trauma). This unifies "post-acute infection syndromes" as an ISR-mediated category.

**Certainty:** 0.35

**Falsifiable prediction:** Muscle biopsy ATF4/PERK signature will be present in all four conditions at similar magnitude, distinct from depression/deconditioning controls.

## Drug / medication ideas

### D1. ISRIB — context-dependent

**Mechanism:** Stabilises eIF2B decamer, blocks ISR translational suppression even when eIF2α phosphorylated. Restores protein synthesis, memory in animal models.

**ME/CFS rationale:** If chronic ATF4 is the metabolic ceiling (H5), ISRIB lifts it. BUT Baron/Wiseman shows ISRIB blocks protective mitochondrial elongation — risk in active viral phase.

**Certainty (benefit):** 0.30

**Risk:** ⚠️ HIGH if active HHV-6/EBV reactivation present (could unleash DRP1 fragmentation).

**Falsifiable prediction:** Open-label ISRIB pilot stratified by HHV-6 dUTPase antibody status: benefit in seronegative subset, harm in seropositive. Status: pre-clinical, not yet in human trials for ME/CFS.

---

### D2. Salubrinal / Sephin1 (Guanabenz derivatives)

**Mechanism:** Inhibits GADD34/PP1 dephosphorylation of eIF2α → prolongs ISR. Opposite of ISRIB.

**ME/CFS rationale:** If ME/CFS subgroup has *insufficient* ISR (post-SARS-CoV-2 N suppression, H1), prolonging ISR may help. Guanabenz is FDA-approved (antihypertensive) — repurposing pathway exists.

**Certainty:** 0.20

**Risk:** ⚠️ Could worsen chronic-ATF4 subgroup.

**Falsifiable prediction:** Trial in long COVID with documented low PERK signature; expect benefit. Trial in HHV-6+ ME/CFS; expect worsening.

---

### D3. Mdivi-1 (DRP1 inhibitor)

**Mechanism:** Blocks mitochondrial fission. Counters HHV-6 miR-aU14 effect directly.

**ME/CFS rationale:** Strong if H3 (WASF3-DRP1 convergence) correct. Mdivi-1 has off-target effects (complex I inhibition); newer DRP1 inhibitors (Drpitor1a) more selective.

**Certainty:** 0.35

**Falsifiable prediction:** Patient-derived muscle myoblasts treated with Mdivi-1 will restore supercomplex assembly and respiration. Status: animal-stage, no human trials.

---

### D4. Antivirals targeting HHV-6 reactivation

**Mechanism:** Foscarnet, ganciclovir, valganciclovir suppress HHV-6 replication. If miR-aU14 is the upstream driver, suppressing reactivation prevents fragmentation.

**ME/CFS rationale:** Subset analyses (Lerner, Montoya valacyclovir/valganciclovir) showed benefit in seropositive subsets. Prusty's miRNA mechanism provides molecular basis previously lacking.

**Certainty:** 0.45

**Risk:** Toxicity (renal, marrow) requires careful patient selection.

**Falsifiable prediction:** RCT in HHV-6 dUTPase IgG-positive ME/CFS subset will show benefit; seronegative subset will not. miR-aU14 in plasma exosomes will correlate with response.

---

### D5. GCN2 inhibitors (GCN2iB, IMPACT)

**Mechanism:** Blocks amino-acid-deprivation arm of ISR. Reduces chronic ATF4.

**ME/CFS rationale:** Direct test of H5. Götz 2025 Drosophila data: GCN2 suppression extends lifespan.

**Certainty:** 0.25

**Risk:** Untested in humans for chronic disease; cancer-stage development.

---

### D6. PERK inhibitors (GSK2606414, ISRIB-adjacent)

**Mechanism:** Specifically blocks ER-stress arm. Wang 2023 directly implicates PERK.

**ME/CFS rationale:** If WASF3/PERK axis is dominant, PERK inhibition addresses root.

**Certainty:** 0.30

**Risk:** Pancreatic toxicity (β-cells require PERK); narrow window.

---

### D7. Low-dose naltrexone (LDN) — mechanistic update

**Mechanism:** Beyond opioid-receptor effects, LDN modulates TLR4/microglial activation. Microglia are major CNS ISR responders. LDN may reduce chronic neuroinflammatory ISR.

**ME/CFS rationale:** Existing empirical use; ISR provides mechanistic frame.

**Certainty:** 0.30

**Falsifiable prediction:** LDN responders will show reduction in CSF GFAP and ATF4-target signatures.

---

### D8. Metformin — ISR angle

**Mechanism:** Activates AMPK, inhibits mitochondrial complex I (mild), induces protective mitochondrial dynamics. Recent data: metformin modulates ISR via ATF4.

**ME/CFS rationale:** Cheap, safe, well-tolerated. Mechanistically plausible for chronic-ATF4 subgroup.

**Certainty:** 0.25

**Falsifiable prediction:** 12-week metformin RCT in ME/CFS with ATF4-high muscle signature: expect modest PEM reduction.

---

### D9. Rapamycin / sirolimus

**Mechanism:** mTORC1 inhibitor. mTOR and ISR have reciprocal regulation; rapamycin can paradoxically activate ATF4 via 4E-BP1. Complex.

**ME/CFS rationale:** Anti-aging community uses; some ME/CFS patients self-experiment. Mechanistic interaction with ISR not well characterised in this context.

**Certainty:** 0.15

**Risk:** Immunosuppression — caution if HHV-6 reactivation possible.

## Supplement / nutraceutical ideas

### S1. Glycine + N-acetylcysteine (GlyNAC)

**Mechanism:** Restores glutathione; reduces oxidative stress that drives ER-stress ISR. Glycine also feeds one-carbon pathway downstream of ATF4.

**Certainty:** 0.35

**Falsifiable prediction:** GlyNAC trial (existing in aging literature) will show fatigue improvement in ME/CFS subset with elevated oxidative markers.

---

### S2. Urolithin A

**Mechanism:** Induces mitophagy; clears DRP1-fragmented dysfunctional mitochondria. Counters Hennig/Prusty mechanism downstream.

**Certainty:** 0.30

**Falsifiable prediction:** Urolithin A (Mitopure) RCT will show muscle bioenergetic improvement on 31P-MRS in ME/CFS.

---

### S3. Spermidine

**Mechanism:** Polyamine; induces autophagy; reduces eIF5A hypusination dysregulation in chronic ISR.

**Certainty:** 0.20

---

### S4. Methylene blue

**Mechanism:** Alternative electron carrier; bypasses complex I/III defects from supercomplex disruption (Wang).

**Certainty:** 0.25

**Risk:** SSRI interaction (serotonin syndrome).

---

### S5. Branched-chain amino acid (BCAA) restriction (counterintuitive)

**Mechanism:** GCN2 senses uncharged tRNAs. Paradoxically, *reducing* BCAA may not activate GCN2 if total amino acid balance restored. Methionine restriction (well-studied in aging) may suppress ATF4.

**Certainty:** 0.15

**Falsifiable prediction:** Methionine-restricted diet trial — measure plasma FGF21, GDF15, ATF4-targets.

---

### S6. NAD+ precursors (NR, NMN)

**Mechanism:** Sirtuin activation, PGC-1α induction, mitochondrial biogenesis. Counteracts ATF4 OXPHOS suppression at transcriptional level.

**Certainty:** 0.30 (but mixed ME/CFS trial data)

---

### S7. Coenzyme Q10 + alpha-lipoic acid

**Mechanism:** Supports complex I/III function; partially compensates for supercomplex disruption.

**Certainty:** 0.30 (existing ME/CFS use)

---

### S8. Lithium (microdose)

**Mechanism:** GSK3 inhibition; induces autophagy; modulates ISR via ATF4. Microdose (1–5mg lithium orotate) used in some neurodegen contexts.

**Certainty:** 0.15

**Risk:** Renal/thyroid monitoring even at low doses.

## Non-pharmacological ISR modulators

### N1. Pacing as ISR-state management

**Mechanism:** Reframe pacing not as energy budgeting but as preventing ISR-cycle entry (H2). Each PEM = ISR cycle that fails to resolve. Pacing = staying below ISR-trigger threshold.

**Certainty:** 0.50 (compatible with existing pacing data)

**Falsifiable prediction:** Wearable HRV + accelerometer thresholds calibrated to individual ISR-trigger point; adherence reduces PEM frequency.

---

### N2. Heat therapy (sauna)

**Mechanism:** HSF1 induction, HSP70 expression, ER chaperone support. Counters protein misfolding upstream of PERK.

**Certainty:** 0.30

**Falsifiable prediction:** Mild repeated heat exposure (Finnish-style, well below crash threshold) will reduce muscle PERK signature over weeks.

---

### N3. Cold exposure

**Mechanism:** Norepinephrine surge, HRI activation (if cold-induced eIF2α phosphorylation occurs in heart/muscle).

**Certainty:** 0.10

**Risk:** ⚠️ Cold tolerance frequently impaired in ME/CFS; could trigger PEM.

---

### N4. Time-restricted eating

**Mechanism:** Periodic GCN2 activation (transient amino acid limitation) may reset ISR-dynamic range — analogous to muscle conditioning. Avoids chronic activation.

**Certainty:** 0.20

**Falsifiable prediction:** 14:10 TRE (mild) trial — measure FGF21 dynamics; expect restoration of pulsatile pattern.

---

### N5. Hyperbaric oxygen

**Mechanism:** Reduces HIF-1α, ER stress in hypoxic tissue; modulates mitochondrial dynamics. Some long COVID data.

**Certainty:** 0.25

---

### N6. Vagal stimulation (tVNS)

**Mechanism:** Cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway; reduces microglial activation and CNS ISR.

**Certainty:** 0.20

## Mathematical model extensions

### M1. ISR state variable in PEM ODE model

Add to existing energy/PEM ODE system:

- `I(t)` = phospho-eIF2α level (compartment-specific)
- `A(t)` = ATF4 transcriptional activity (lagged from I)
- `dI/dt = k_kin · S(t) − k_dephos · G(t) · I` where S = stress signal, G = GADD34
- `dA/dt = k_atf4 · I − k_decay · A`
- Energy ceiling `E_max(t) = E_baseline · (1 − α · A(t))` — ATF4 lowers ceiling

**Falsifiable prediction:** Fit model to time-resolved muscle biopsy + symptom data; expect α > 0 and lagged A response matching delayed PEM onset.

---

### M2. Bifurcation analysis

Parameters: dephosphorylation rate (`k_dephos`) and stress influx (`S`). Predict bistability: healthy = monostable low-I, ME/CFS = bistable with high-I attractor. Therapeutic targets = parameters that collapse the high-I attractor.

---

### M3. DAG extension

Add nodes: PERK, GCN2, HRI, PKR (parallel ISR kinases) → eIF2α-P → ATF4 → {OXPHOS↓, AA-metab↑, FGF21↑, GDF15↑}. Add edges: HHV-6-miR-aU14 → DRP1 → fragmentation (parallel to ISR). SARS-CoV-2-N → PKR (inhibitory edge).

---

### M4. Two-population stochastic model

Subgroup A (ISR-high) vs Subgroup B (ISR-suppressed) with different transition probabilities to chronic state. Predicts trial response heterogeneity quantitatively.

## Diagnostic / biomarker ideas

### B1. Tissue ISR panel (muscle biopsy)

Phospho-eIF2α (Ser51), ATF4, CHOP, GADD34, ASNS, FGF21 mRNA + WASF3. Define ME/CFS-positive signature. Validate vs. depression/deconditioning controls.

**Certainty (diagnostic utility):** 0.50

---

### B2. Serum GDF15 + FGF21 ratio

Both ATF4 targets that cross tissue compartments. Ratio may be more specific than either alone (FGF21 also responds to ketosis, exercise). Cheap, scalable.

**Certainty:** 0.45

**Falsifiable prediction:** GDF15/FGF21 ratio elevated in ME/CFS vs. controls; rises post-CPET in ME/CFS.

---

### B3. miR-aU14 in plasma exosomes

Direct readout of HHV-6 reactivation. Stratifies patients for antiviral trials.

**Certainty:** 0.50

---

### B4. PBMC ex vivo ISR challenge

Even if baseline PBMC ISR markers are normal (Omdal), the *response dynamics* to ex vivo stress (thapsigargin, tunicamycin) may differ. Functional assay rather than static measurement.

**Certainty:** 0.40

**Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS PBMCs will show delayed eIF2α dephosphorylation (impaired GADD34 function) post-stressor.

---

### B5. tRNA charging assay

GCN2 activation requires uncharged tRNAs. Direct charging assay (microarray-based) could reveal subclinical amino acid stress.

**Certainty:** 0.20

---

### B6. PET imaging — emerging ISR tracers

[18F]-labelled tracers for ATF4 targets in development. CNS-specific ISR detection currently impossible non-invasively; this would transform ME/CFS imaging.

**Certainty:** 0.30 (pending tracer development)

---

### B7. Skin biopsy combined readout

IENFD (already used for SFN) + ATF4 IHC + mast cell tryptase. Three-marker skin biopsy may be the most accessible tissue panel for ME/CFS.

**Certainty:** 0.40
