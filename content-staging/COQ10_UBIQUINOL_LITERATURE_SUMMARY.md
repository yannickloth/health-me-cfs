## Evidence Overview

### Meta-Analysis (Highest Quality Evidence)
**Tsai et al. (2022)** - Systematic review and meta-analysis of 13 RCTs (n=1,126)
- **Primary finding:** CoQ10 significantly reduces fatigue (Hedges' g = -0.398, p = 0.001)
- **Dose-response:** Higher doses correlate with greater fatigue reduction (coefficient = -0.0017 per mg, p < 0.001)
- **Duration-response:** Longer treatment durations correlate with greater fatigue reduction (coefficient = -0.0042 per day, p = 0.007)
- **Formulation effect:** CoQ10-only formulations more effective than CoQ10 compounds
- **Safety:** Excellent (1 adverse event in 602 participants, 0.17%)
- **Certainty:** High (0.85)

### Bioavailability Evidence
**Mei et al. (2026)** - Novel cocrystal ubiquinol vs standard ubiquinone (n=12)
- **Primary finding:** Novel cocrystal ubiquinol shows 2.01-3.43x higher bioavailability
- **Pharmacokinetics:** Cmax ratio 2.20 (90% CI: 1.59-3.04), AUC0-t ratio 2.01 (90% CI: 1.51-2.70)
- **Safety:** No adverse events in either formulation
- **Clinical implication:** Higher bioavailability may allow lower doses with same effect
- **Certainty:** Medium (0.55)

### ME/CFS-Specific Evidence
**Castro-Marrero et al. (2021)** - CoQ10 + NADH in ME/CFS (n=207)
- **Primary finding:** Significant improvements in cognitive fatigue, overall fatigue, and quality of life
- **Dose:** 200mg CoQ10 + 20mg NADH daily for 12 weeks
- **Outcome:** Benefits persisted 4 weeks post-treatment
- **Certainty:** High (0.80)

**Maes et al. (2009)** - CoQ10 deficiency in ME/CFS (n=58 patients, 22 controls)
- **Primary finding:** 44.8% of ME/CFS patients have plasma CoQ10 below lowest control value
- **Correlation:** Lower CoQ10 correlates with fatigue, autonomic, and neurocognitive symptoms
- **Certainty:** Medium (0.65)

**Di Pierro et al. (2024)** - Comprehensive narrative review of CoQ10 for post-viral fatigue
- **Primary finding:** Strong evidence from ME/CFS RCT (n=207) plus open-label studies
- **Mechanism:** Electron transport chain restoration, ATP production, antioxidant protection
- **Formulation insight:** Bioavailability crucial—crystal-modified ubiquinone shows 75% better absorption
- **Safety:** No serious adverse events across all reviewed studies

### Fibromyalgia Evidence (Cross-Disease Insights)
**Peel et al. (2015)** - CoQ10 for post-polio fatigue (n=103)
- **Finding:** 100mg CoQ10 daily for 60 days did NOT reduce fatigue
- **Significance:** Null result, demonstrates dose/duration importance
- **Certainty:** Medium (0.60)

**Barletta et al. (2023)** - CoQ10 + alpha-lipoic acid for Long COVID (n=174)
- **Finding:** 53.5% treatment vs 3.5% control achieved complete fatigue resolution
- **Significance:** Supports CoQ10 for post-viral fatigue syndromes
- **Certainty:** Medium (0.55)

### Related Condition Evidence
**Deng et al. (2025)** - CoQ10 exercise performance meta-analysis (24 studies)
- **Finding:** CoQ10 reliably elevates circulating levels but provides at most modest exercise performance benefits
- **Significance:** Clarifies that CoQ10 reduces fatigue but does not enhance exercise capacity
- **Certainty:** Medium (0.60)

**Mantle et al. (2025)** - Gulf War Illness/Fibromyalgia/ME/CFS/Long COVID review
- **Finding:** Evidence for CoQ10 benefit across low-energy associated disorders
- **Significance:** Supports common mitochondrial pathology across conditions
- **Certainty:** Medium (0.65)

## Gaps and Limitations

1. **ME/CFS-specific pharmacokinetics:** No data on CoQ10 absorption in ME/CFS patients vs healthy controls
2. **Ubiquinol vs ubiquinone direct comparison in ME/CFS:** Bioavailability advantage not yet linked to clinical outcomes in ME/CFS
3. **Severe/very-severe patient data:** Limited evidence for dosing in most severe cases
4. **Long-term outcomes:** Most studies limited to 12 weeks, little data beyond 6 months
5. **Biomarker utility:** CoQ10 levels not validated as diagnostic or prognostic marker
6. **Combination therapies:** Limited evidence on CoQ10 + other mitochondrial support combinations beyond NADH
7. **Formulation standardization:** Wide variety of formulations with differing bioavailability
8. **Fed state pharmacokinetics:** Most bioavailability data from fasting conditions
