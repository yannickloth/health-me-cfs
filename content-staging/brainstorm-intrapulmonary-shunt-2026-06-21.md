## Category 1: Novel Hypotheses

### 1.1 Post-Exertional Shunt Window → Delayed PEM Trigger *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Mechanistic rationale:** IPAVAs open during exercise in nearly all adults (Lyne 2024). In health, HPV closes them promptly post-exertion, restoring normal Qs/Qt. If HPV remains impaired post-virally (Li 2024), IPAVAs may stay patent for hours after exercise, creating a *persistent shunt window* → ongoing arterial hypoxemia → delayed tissue hypoxia driving PEM at 24–48h. This would explain the temporal gap between exertion and peak PEM — a gap current mechanisms (lactate, inflammation, ion dyshomeostasis) don't fully cover.
- **Evidence link:** Farrow 2023 (Qs/Qt elevated during exercise post-COVID, cert 0.62); Li 2024 (HPV impairment persists post-infection, cert 0.48); Lyne 2024 (IPAVA prevalence ~30% rest, ~100% exercise, cert 0.42). No study has measured Qs/Qt *after* exercise in any post-viral population.
- **Preliminary certainty:** 0.20 — no post-exercise shunt data exists; all components are established but the temporal link is entirely conjectural.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS patients show Qs/Qt >5% at 1h and 6h post-exercise (vs normalization within minutes in healthy controls), with 48h Qs/Qt correlating with PEM severity. Falsified if Qs/Qt normalizes within 30 min of exercise cessation in all patients.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** If shunt stays open for hours after exercise in ME/CFS, it would mean oxygen delivery fails not just during activity but for the rest of the day — a mechanism that could explain why PEM hits with a delay rather than immediately.

### 1.2 RBC Deformability × IPAVA Routing Synergy *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Mechanistic rationale:** ME/CFS RBCs have impaired deformability (Saha 2025). Normal RBCs deform to squeeze through pulmonary capillaries (diameter ~5–7 μm vs RBC ~7–8 μm). Stiff RBCs that cannot deform will preferentially route through low-resistance IPAVAs (diameter ~25–50 μm), increasing shunt fraction. This creates a positive feedback: stiff RBCs → more IPAVA flow → less alveolar O₂ uptake → tissue hypoxia → further RBC damage (oxidative stress) → more stiffness. The synergy links two documented but unconnected ME/CFS abnormalities: RBC stiffness and tissue hypoxia.
- **Evidence link:** Guo 2025 (ME/CFS RBC deformability defect, cert 0.60); Saha 2025 (further RBC rheology evidence); Davis 2023 (IPAVA flow regulation by O₂ tension, cert 0.55). No study has measured RBC deformability and Qs/Qt in the same patients.
- **Preliminary certainty:** 0.20 — individual components established but interaction untested.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Within ME/CFS patients, RBC deformability (measured by microfluidic transit time) inversely correlates with Qs/Qt on 100% O₂ testing, independent of other variables. Falsified if R² < 0.10 controlling for age, sex, BMI.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** If stiffer red blood cells bypass the lungs' oxygen-exchange capillaries more readily, two separate ME/CFS findings (stiff blood cells + low tissue oxygen) may turn out to be two ends of the same problem.

### 1.3 Shunt-Predominant ME/CFS Phenotype *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Mechanistic rationale:** Not all ME/CFS patients will have elevated shunt — it likely defines a measurable subset. Clinical profile predicted: exertional dyspnea disproportionate to fatigue (vs fatigue-predominant patients who lack dyspnea), positional worsening (platypnea-orthodeoxia pattern), preserved lung volumes and spirometry, mild desaturation on CPET (SpO₂ drop ≥4% during submaximal exercise), normal/near-normal DLCO, normal CT chest. This phenotype would be *primary pulmonary vascular* rather than *primary microcirculatory* — important distinction because treatment targets differ.
- **Evidence link:** Espejo 2025 (platypnea-orthodeoxia post-COVID, cert 0.25 — case report but proves the phenotype exists post-virally); Grist 2022 (129Xe MRI gas exchange defect with normal CT/PFTs, cert 0.55); typical clinical descriptions of ME/CFS dyspnea.
- **Preliminary certainty:** 0.20 — phenotype defined from Long COVID data; ME/CFS-specific dyspnea patterns not systematically characterized for shunt.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Cluster analysis of ME/CFS patients on CPET (AT, SpO₂ nadir, VE/VCO₂ slope) identifies a cluster with: AT < 50% predicted + SpO₂ drop ≥4% + VE/VCO₂ < 30 (low dead space, favoring shunt over V/Q mismatch). This cluster shows Qs/Qt > 6% on 100% O₂ testing. Falsified if no cluster meets all three criteria.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** If a "shunt-predominant" ME/CFS subtype exists, it would mean some patients' energy failure originates in the lungs rather than the muscles — a distinction that points to different testing and potentially different treatment.

### 1.4 Shunt as Orthostatic Intolerance Mechanism Independent of POTS *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Mechanistic rationale:** Platypnea-orthodeoxia (dyspnea + desaturation on upright positioning) from intrapulmonary shunt can cause orthostatic symptoms independently of autonomic dysfunction. In supine position, basal lung regions receive more perfusion; upright, apical regions (where IPAVAs may concentrate) are gravity-dependent for flow. If HPV is impaired, upright posture increases shunt fraction via gravitational recruitment of IPAVAs in poorly ventilated apices. This would present as upright fatigue, dizziness, and dyspnea with measurable SpO₂ drop but *normal* heart rate increment — distinguishing it from POTS.
- **Evidence link:** Espejo 2025 (cert 0.25, single case proving the phenomenon exists post-COVID); Lyne 2024 (IPAVA location in upper lung zones, cert 0.42); general platypnea-orthodeoxia physiology from hepatopulmonary syndrome literature (indirect).
- **Preliminary certainty:** 0.15 — single case report; hepatopulmonary syndrome physiology well-established but never applied to ME/CFS.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** A subset of ME/CFS patients with orthostatic intolerance but (a) negative tilt-table for POTS (HR increment < 30 bpm) and (b) unchanged BP show SpO₂ drop ≥3% on upright posture, with Qs/Qt > 6% in upright but < 4% supine. Falsified if no patient meets all three.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** Some ME/CFS patients who feel worse standing up may have blood bypassing their lungs' oxygen-exchange areas when upright — a mechanism that would not show up on standard autonomic testing and would need a different treatment approach than POTS.

### 1.5 Shunt as HIF-2α Endothelial Deficiency Driver *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Mechanistic rationale:** The paper posits a β2AR-negative HIF-2α endothelial deficiency subtype (cert 0.55). HIF-2α is endothelial-specific and responds to chronic, mild hypoxia; HIF-1α is ubiquitous and responds to acute, severe hypoxia. If intrapulmonary shunt produces *chronic mild arterial hypoxemia* (PaO₂ ~70–80 mmHg instead of 90–100), this would preferentially activate the HIF-1α system over HIF-2α — explaining the shift toward HIF-1α-dominant metabolic pathology in ME/CFS. The shunt provides the chronic low-grade hypoxia signal that sets the HIF ratio imbalance.
- **Evidence link:** Hoel 2021 (tissue hypoxia metabolic phenotypes, cert 0.65); Scheibenbogen/Wirth HIF-2α model (β2AR-negative subtype); Farrow 2023 (persistent shunt at 6 months — chronic, not acute, cert 0.62). No study links shunt to HIF isoform balance.
- **Preliminary certainty:** 0.25 — HIF-2α endothelial deficiency model is established in the paper; shunt provides a parsimonious upstream driver; bidirectional causality also possible (HIF-2α deficiency → impaired pulmonary endothelial function → HPV failure → shunt).
- **Falsifiable prediction:** ME/CFS patients with elevated Qs/Qt (>6%) show lower HIF-2α / HIF-1α mRNA ratio in PBMCs compared to patients with normal Qs/Qt and healthy controls. Falsified if ratio does not differ by shunt status.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** A subtle oxygen deficit from blood bypassing the lungs could be what chronically biases the body's oxygen-sensing systems toward the wrong hypoxia response — a potential unifying bridge between a lung-level finding and the molecular subtypes already described in ME/CFS.

### 1.6 Shunt as Explanation for the "Low AT / Normal Peak VO₂" CPET Paradox *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Mechanistic rationale:** A subset of ME/CFS patients show normal peak VO₂ but *low anaerobic threshold* (AT) — oxygen consumption rises normally at high workloads but lactate accumulates early. This is paradoxical: normal peak O₂ delivery suggests no overall O₂ limitation, yet early lactate indicates O₂ supply-demand mismatch at submaximal intensity. Shunt resolves the paradox: at low-moderate workloads (where Qs/Qt matters proportionally more because FIO₂ is lower), shunt reduces arterial O₂ content, forcing early anaerobic metabolism (low AT). At peak exercise (100% O₂ breathing often used in testing), shunt is suppressed by hyperoxia (Davis 2023), "masking" the defect. This pattern would not be explained by microcirculatory dysfunction alone.
- **Evidence link:** Davis 2023 (hyperoxia reduces IPAVA flow, cert 0.55); Farrow 2023 (Qs/Qt worsens during room-air exercise, cert 0.62); general CPET literature in ME/CFS showing low AT + variable peak VO₂. No study has tested whether the low-AT/normal-peak-VO₂ subset specifically has elevated shunt.
- **Preliminary certainty:** 0.25 — the CPET pattern is documented; the shunt explanation is mechanistically coherent; the hyperoxia masking effect is testable.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Among ME/CFS patients with normal peak VO₂ but low AT (< 50% predicted), Qs/Qt is > 6% and correlates with AT (r > 0.5). In these patients, breathing 100% O₂ during submaximal exercise raises AT (shifts lactate curve rightward). Falsified if Qs/Qt is normal in this subgroup.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** This hypothesis offers a reason why some patients' exercise tests look "normal" at peak effort yet abnormal at moderate effort — the measurement method itself (high oxygen during testing) may temporarily hide a problem that is present during ordinary daily activities.

### 1.7 Viral Non-Specificity of HPV Impairment *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Mechanistic rationale:** HPV is mediated by pulmonary artery smooth muscle sensing alveolar PO₂ via mitochondrial ETC complex I and O₂-sensitive K⁺ channels. Any viral pathogen that causes pulmonary endothelial inflammation can impair HPV via: (a) endothelial NO overproduction (iNOS induction) → vasodilation overriding HPV, (b) endothelin-1 dysregulation, (c) complement deposition damaging endothelial HPV signaling. SARS-CoV-2's impairment is documented (Li 2024), but EBV, CMV, H1N1, and enteroviruses cause similar pulmonary endothelial inflammation. The mechanism may generalize — making shunt a *common pathway* for post-infectious ME/CFS rather than a COVID-specific curiosity.
- **Evidence link:** Li 2024 (COVID impairs HPV, cert 0.48); Bush 2022 (microvascular shunts in COVID, cert 0.50); general physiology: HPV mechanism well-characterized (reviewed in Dunham-Snary 2017, cert 0.70 — not in bib but widely established); indirect: EBV/CMV pulmonary involvement literature.
- **Preliminary certainty:** 0.20 — mechanism plausible but HPV impairment never studied in non-COVID post-viral ME/CFS.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** EBV-triggered and influenza-triggered ME/CFS patients show elevated Qs/Qt (>5%) compared to healthy controls, comparable to post-COVID ME/CFS patients. Falsified if only post-COVID patients show elevated Qs/Qt.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** If multiple different viral triggers can cause the same lung-level oxygen bypass, shunt could be a unifying mechanism across post-infectious ME/CFS regardless of which infection started it — rather than a unique feature of Long COVID.

### 1.8 Inflammatory Shunt: Complement-Mediated HPV Failure *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Mechanistic rationale:** ME/CFS involves chronic complement activation (microclots, immune complexes → classical/lectin pathway). Complement anaphylatoxins (C3a, C5a) induce pulmonary vasodilation, oppose HPV, and increase vascular permeability. If complement activation damages pulmonary microvascular endothelium or biases vasomotor tone toward dilation, HPV becomes ineffective → shunt worsens. This creates a vicious loop: complement activation → HPV failure → shunt → tissue hypoxia → more endothelial damage → more complement activation.
- **Evidence link:** Kell/coagulation microclot literature in ME/CFS; complement activation documented in Long COVID and ME/CFS; C5a-mediated pulmonary vasodilation in ARDS physiology (indirect, cert ~0.60 from non-ME/CFS literature). No study has specifically linked complement markers to shunt in any condition.
- **Preliminary certainty:** 0.15 — every step is individually plausible; the integrated loop is conjectural.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In ME/CFS patients, complement activation markers (C5a, sC5b-9) correlate with Qs/Qt (r > 0.4), and this correlation is independent of CRP or IL-6. Falsified if no correlation.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** The same immune system overactivation implicated in ME/CFS blood clotting may also damage the lung's ability to regulate blood flow — a connection that would link two separate findings (microclots and exercise desaturation) under one inflammatory mechanism.

## Category 2: Research Directions

### 2.1 Proof-of-Concept: 100% FiO₂ Shunt Testing in ME/CFS *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Rationale:** The single most decisive experiment. 30 ME/CFS patients (15 post-COVID, 15 non-COVID trigger) + 30 healthy controls. Measure Qs/Qt by 100% O₂ method (A-a gradient + arterial blood gas). Primary outcome: Qs/Qt difference between groups. Secondary: correlation with exertional SpO₂ drop (6-min walk), dyspnea (Borg), and PFTs. Tertiary: test-retest reliability. Identifies whether shunt exists in ME/CFS — no inference needed.
- **Evidence link:** Farrow 2023 protocol directly transferable; 100% O₂ testing is standard, approved, and used clinically.
- **Preliminary certainty:** n/a (research proposal)
- **Falsifiable prediction:** See primary outcome — hypothesis upheld if mean Qs/Qt > 5% in ME/CFS group.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** A simple breathing test (breathing pure oxygen through a mouthpiece while a blood sample is taken) could definitively answer whether ME/CFS patients' blood fails to pick up enough oxygen in the lungs — a question that currently has no answer because nobody has done the test.

### 2.2 Serial Qs/Qt During 2-Day CPET with PEM Capture *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Rationale:** Hypothesis 1.1 (post-exertional shunt window) is directly testable with a standard 2-day CPET protocol used in ME/CFS research. Measure Qs/Qt at 5 timepoints: Day 1 pre-exercise, Day 1 immediately post-exercise, Day 1 at 1h and 6h post-exercise, Day 2 pre-exercise (24h). Correlate delayed Qs/Qt with PEM metrics (ΔVO₂, symptom scores, lactate). Essential for determining if shunt contributes to PEM specifically.
- **Evidence link:** 2-day CPET is established in ME/CFS (Stevens 2018, Keller 2024); Qs/Qt measurement added as an additional gas exchange metric.
- **Preliminary certainty:** n/a (research proposal)
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Qs/Qt at 6h post-exercise is the best predictor of Day 2 PEM severity (measured by ΔVO₂ at ventilatory threshold), outperforming Day 1 peak lactate and heart rate.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** This study would answer whether the effect of exercise on lung oxygenation bypass in ME/CFS lasts for hours or days — not just during the activity — which would change how we understand why overexertion has such delayed consequences.

### 2.3 Supine vs Upright Qs/Qt with Simultaneous Head-Up Tilt *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Rationale:** Tests Hypothesis 1.4 (shunt-mediated orthostatic intolerance). Measure Qs/Qt supine and at 5 min, 15 min, and 30 min of 70° HUT in 20 ME/CFS patients with orthostatic intolerance (10 POTS+, 10 POTS−) and 10 healthy controls. Continuous SpO₂, HR, BP monitoring. Primary: ΔQs/Qt supine→upright between groups.
- **Evidence link:** Espejo 2025 case report; standard HUTT protocol; Qs/Qt measurement method.
- **Preliminary certainty:** n/a (research proposal)
- **Falsifiable prediction:** The POTS− orthostatic subgroup shows Qs/Qt increase of ≥2% on upright posture, while healthy controls show no change.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** If standing worsens the lung oxygenation bypass in some patients, it would offer a new explanation for why certain ME/CFS patients feel terrible when upright even when their heart rate is normal — and this would be detectable with existing clinical equipment.

### 2.4 Inhaled Nitric Oxide (iNO) Challenge as Shunt Probe *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Rationale:** Inhaled NO is a pulmonary vasodilator *and* HPV restorer — it reverses HPV impairment by providing exogenous NO to pulmonary vessels that have lost HPV due to endothelial dysfunction. If shunt in ME/CFS is driven by HPV failure, iNO at 20 ppm should *reduce* shunt (paradoxical response — opposite to the normal response where iNO worsens shunt by dilating already-dilated vessels). The direction of Qs/Qt response to iNO would diagnose the mechanism: increase = normal (vessels responsive, no HPV defect); decrease = HPV impairment confirmed.
- **Evidence link:** iNO physiology well-established in pulmonary hypertension testing; Farrow 2023 (shunt documented post-COVID); Li 2024 (HPV impairment).
- **Preliminary certainty:** n/a (research proposal)
- **Falsifiable prediction:** ≥50% of ME/CFS patients with elevated Qs/Qt show a paradoxical *decrease* in Qs/Qt of ≥2 percentage points during iNO inhalation. Falsified if < 10% show this pattern.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** This test uses a medically approved gas to diagnose *why* the lung bypass is happening — if inhaled nitric oxide paradoxically improves oxygenation, it proves the underlying problem is a failed protective reflex and points toward a specific treatment strategy.

### 2.5 Retrospective Analysis of ME/CFS CPET Datasets for Shunt Evidence *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Rationale:** Many ME/CFS CPET studies archive arterial blood gas data during exercise. The A-a gradient calculation requires only PaO₂, PaCO₂, and FiO₂ — all commonly recorded. A retrospective analysis of existing datasets (10+ studies with archived ABGs) could answer whether ME/CFS patients show elevated A-a gradient (surrogate for shunt) without a single new patient recruitment.
- **Evidence link:** Walitt 2024, Keller 2024, Stevens 2018, Nelson 2019, Vermeulen 2010 — all have CPET with ABG data.
- **Preliminary certainty:** n/a (research proposal)
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Pooled A-a gradient from existing ME/CFS CPET datasets is significantly higher than healthy control pools. Falsified if no difference.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** The data to settle whether ME/CFS involves a lung-level oxygen problem may already exist in research archives — no new patients or expensive equipment needed, just re-analysis of past studies.

### 2.6 Hyperoxia vs Normoxia Exercise Challenge: Behavioral Probe *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Rationale:** If shunt causes exertional hypoxia, breathing 100% O₂ during submaximal exercise should (a) decrease lactate at matched workload, (b) increase AT, (c) reduce perceived exertion (RPE) — and the effect size should correlate with baseline Qs/Qt. Single-blind crossover study: 20 ME/CFS patients, 2 CPETs (hyperoxia vs normoxia, randomized order). Measures: lactate at matched workload, AT, SpO₂, RPE.
- **Evidence link:** Davis 2023 (hyperoxia reduces IPAVA flow during exercise in health, cert 0.55); Farrow 2023 (shunt worsens during exercise, cert 0.62). Effects should be larger in shunt-positive patients.
- **Preliminary certainty:** n/a (research proposal)
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In patients with Qs/Qt > 6%, hyperoxia raises AT by ≥10% of predicted compared to normoxia; in patients with normal Qs/Qt, no effect.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** If breathing pure oxygen during moderate exercise measurably improves performance only in certain patients, it would provide a real-time test for who has the lung-level problem — and potentially identify who might benefit from portable oxygen during activity.

### 2.7 ¹²⁹Xe MRI Gas Exchange Imaging in ME/CFS *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Rationale:** Grist 2022 demonstrated ¹²⁹Xe MRI detects gas exchange impairment (reduced RBC:tissue-plasma ratio) in Long COVID patients with normal CT and PFTs. Performing the same imaging in ME/CFS patients — with and without post-COVID onset — would directly visualize whether (a) gas exchange is impaired, (b) the pattern is consistent with shunt (reduced RBC signal in alveolar capillaries) vs diffusion limitation (reduced uptake), and (c) whether non-COVID ME/CFS shows the same pattern.
- **Evidence link:** Grist 2022 (cert 0.55, directly transferable protocol); ¹²⁹Xe MRI is non-invasive (inhaled contrast).
- **Preliminary certainty:** n/a (research proposal)
- **Falsifiable prediction:** ≥30% of ME/CFS patients show RBC:tissue-plasma ratio below healthy reference range, with the deficit correlating with dyspnea severity and AT.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** A special MRI scan using inhaled xenon gas could directly show whether ME/CFS patients' lungs are failing to transfer oxygen into the bloodstream — providing visual proof that goes beyond standard breathing tests.

### 2.8 Qs/Qt Correlation with PEM Biomarker Panel *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Rationale:** Test the full mechanistic chain: shunt → reduced O₂ content → tissue hypoxia → metabolic consequences. In 40 ME/CFS patients, measure: Qs/Qt (100% O₂), resting lactate, O₂ extraction fraction (by near-infrared spectroscopy or PET), HIF-1α protein in PBMCs, plasma succinate (HIF-stabilizing metabolite), and VEGF. Correlate all with shunt fraction. If the chain holds, shunt-positive patients should cluster with: high HIF-1α, high lactate, low OEF, high VEGF, high succinate.
- **Evidence link:** Hoel 2021 (cert 0.65, metabolic phenotype); Walitt 2024 (impaired O₂ extraction, cert 0.60); HIF-1α/succinate literature.
- **Preliminary certainty:** n/a (research proposal)
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Qs/Qt explains ≥30% of variance in HIF-1α protein levels after controlling for age, sex, and deconditioning. Falsified if R² < 0.10.
- **Non-specialist consequence:** This study would trace the entire chain from a lung abnormality through blood chemistry to molecular markers — if the correlations hold, it provides the "smoking gun" evidence that shunt is a root cause of ME/CFS tissue hypoxia rather than a coincidental finding.

## Category 10: Reasons This Mechanism May NOT Be Relevant

### 10.1 Shunt May Be COVID-Specific, Not Generalizable to ME/CFS *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Rationale:** SARS-CoV-2 infects pulmonary vascular endothelium directly via ACE2 — a property not shared by EBV, enteroviruses, or H1N1 (the classic ME/CFS triggers). Direct endothelial infection may cause HPV impairment via cytopathic effects (endothelial cell death, denudation) that other viruses cannot reproduce. If so, shunt is a COVID-specific curiosity with zero relevance to the 70%+ of ME/CFS cases triggered by non-COVID pathogens.
- **Evidence link:** Li 2024 (COVID impairs HPV, cert 0.48 — but mechanism may be ACE2-specific); all 9 Long COVID shunt studies (Farrow, Harbut, Sandhu, Grist) are COVID-specific with no non-COVID comparison group.
- **Implication for paper:** If shunt is COVID-only, the ch14d cross-disease speculation (@spec:intrapulmonary-shunt-postviral, cert 0.35) would need to specify "post-COVID ME/CFS only" rather than general post-infectious ME/CFS.
- **Disconfirming evidence needed:** EBV-triggered ME/CFS patients show normal Qs/Qt (≤5%) while post-COVID ME/CFS patients show elevated >5%. This would prove viral specificity.

### 10.2 Shunt Fraction Is Too Small to Cause Symptomatic Hypoxia *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Rationale:** Normal Qs/Qt ~3%. Post-COVID Qs/Qt ~8% (Farrow 2023). An increase of 5 percentage points means ~5% of cardiac output bypasses oxygenation. In a 5 L/min CO, this is 250 mL/min of venous admixture — reducing PaO₂ from ~100 mmHg to ~80–85 mmHg. This mild hypoxemia is well within the flat portion of the O₂-Hb dissociation curve (SaO₂ remains >95%). The resulting drop in arterial O₂ content (~2 mL O₂/dL blood) is modest and should be compensated by increased cardiac output or O₂ extraction during exercise. Healthy individuals with this degree of shunt from hepatopulmonary syndrome are often asymptomatic at rest.
- **Evidence link:** Farrow 2023 Qs/Qt values; O₂-Hb dissociation curve physiology; hepatopulmonary syndrome literature (where PaO₂ often drops to 60–70 mmHg before symptoms).
- **Implication for paper:** The paper's tissue hypoxia model would not need revision — other mechanisms (microcirculatory, mitochondrial) would be sufficient. Shunt would be an epiphenomenon rather than a causative mechanism.
- **Disconfirming evidence needed:** In ME/CFS patients with elevated shunt, PaO₂ on room air is >80 mmHg AND supplemental O₂ during exercise does NOT improve AT or symptoms — showing the shunt is too small to matter.

### 10.3 Normal VO₂max in Shunt Studies Suggests No Limitation *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Rationale:** Farrow 2023 patients had normal VO₂max. If shunt were a meaningful O₂ delivery limitation, VO₂max should be reduced (because peak O₂ delivery depends on arterial O₂ content × cardiac output). The fact that post-COVID patients with ~8% shunt achieve normal VO₂max implies that (a) the shunt is compensated by increased cardiac output or O₂ extraction, or (b) the shunt is not present at peak exercise (hyperoxia in testing suppresses it). In either case, shunt is not the limiting factor for whole-body O₂ delivery. ME/CFS patients *do* have reduced VO₂max — but if shunt alone can't explain it, other mechanisms (microcirculatory, cardiac, mitochondrial) are the primary drivers.
- **Evidence link:** Farrow 2023 (normal VO₂max in post-COVID with shunt); Davis 2023 (hyperoxia suppresses IPAVA flow — may mask shunt during peak CPET on 100% O₂).
- **Implication for paper:** The paper's emphasis on microcirculatory and mitochondrial mechanisms (ch06) is well-justified. Shunt could be a minor contributor but not a primary cause of reduced VO₂max or tissue hypoxia.
- **Disconfirming evidence needed:** In ME/CFS, Qs/Qt at peak exercise (room air) correlates with VO₂max (r > 0.5) AND supplemental O₂ improves VO₂max — showing the shunt is functionally limiting.

### 10.4 Shunt Does Not Explain PEM — Wrong Time Course *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Rationale:** PEM peaks at 24–48h post-exertion. Shunt, if present, would cause hypoxemia *during* and *immediately after* exercise (minute-hour timescale) — not 24h later. The proposed "post-exertional shunt window" (Hypothesis 1.1) is speculative: there is no evidence IPAVAs or HPV impairment persist longer than minutes after exercise cessation. PEM's delayed time course is better explained by mechanisms with the right kinetics: (a) mitochondrial damage → ATP depletion → ion dyshomeostasis → calcium overload → further mitochondrial damage (hours-days cycle), (b) post-exertional immune activation → cytokine release → sickness behavior (hours-days), (c) HIF-1α-driven transcription (hours).
- **Evidence link:** PEM time course well-documented at 24–48h; IPAVA closure kinetics post-exercise in healthy individuals (Lovering 2008, cert ~0.60 — IPAVAs close within minutes of exercise cessation).
- **Implication for paper:** The paper's PEM mechanisms in ch15 (hibernation-mimetic, ion dyshomeostasis, HIF-1α ECM degradation) do not require shunt. Even if shunt is elevated, it would contribute to *acute* exercise intolerance but not to delayed PEM.
- **Disconfirming evidence needed:** Qs/Qt remains elevated > 6h post-exercise in ME/CFS (proving the delayed window exists). Without this, shunt cannot be a PEM mechanism.

## Category 11: Null Hypothesis Assessment

### 11.1 Zero Shunt in ME/CFS: What Would Change? *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Scenario:** Direct Qs/Qt measurement finds ME/CFS patients have normal shunt fraction (3–5%), indistinguishable from healthy controls. No correlation with PEM, exertional desaturation, or any ME/CFS measure.
- **Impact on paper:** The @oq:intrapulmonary-shunt-mecfs and @spec:intrapulmonary-shunt-postviral environments would need explicit falsification updates: "Measured in ME/CFS: normal. Shunt ruled out as mechanism." The ch28 citation insertion (mentioning shunt as candidate mechanism) would need removal or qualification. **However**, all other paper mechanisms (microcirculatory dysfunction, RBC deformability, HIF-2α deficiency, PEM hibernation, ion dyshomeostasis) remain unaffected — they were developed independently and do not depend on shunt. The paper would lose a candidate upstream mechanism but would not require substantive revision.
- **Which claims need revision:** (a) The inference that post-COVID shunt generalizes to ME/CFS — falsified. (b) The cross-disease bridge in ch14d — would need caveat: "post-COVID specific; not observed in general ME/CFS." (c) The suggestion that shunt could explain the "normal PFTs + dyspnea" paradox in ME/CFS — would be refuted.
- **Which claims are reinforced:** The emphasis on microcirculatory and mitochondrial primary dysfunction (ch06) would be strengthened — if shunt is normal, the O₂ bottleneck must be entirely downstream of the pulmonary vasculature.
- **Net significance:** Low for the paper as a whole (shunt is one of many candidate mechanisms), high for the shunt hypothesis specifically.

### 11.2 Shunt Normal but PEM Still Present: Mechanism Ruled Out *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Scenario:** Qs/Qt is normal at rest AND during/after exercise (including 24h and 48h post-CPET) in ME/CFS patients with documented PEM. PEM severity shows zero correlation with any shunt measure.
- **Impact on paper:** Shunt is definitively eliminated as a PEM mechanism. The paper's PEM model in ch15 (hibernation-mimetic, ion dyshomeostasis, HIF-1α ECM degradation) would be confirmed as independently adequate. The @oq:intrapulmonary-shunt-mecfs would be updated to note falsification. **No other paper claims affected.**
- **Revised claim needed:** "Intrapulmonary shunt does not contribute to PEM in ME/CFS. PEM mechanisms must be entirely microcirculatory, mitochondrial, or ion-homeostatic."
- **Net significance:** Modest — PEM mechanisms already robust in the paper.

### 11.3 Shift from "Unifying" to "Subgroup-Only" Mechanism *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Scenario:** Qs/Qt is elevated only in post-COVID ME/CFS patients (∼25% of the ME/CFS population), normal in all other trigger types (EBV, H1N1, idiopathic). Shunt is COVID-specific, not a general post-viral or ME/CFS mechanism.
- **Impact on paper:** The ch14d cross-disease speculation (positioning shunt as shared post-viral mechanism) would need restriction to post-COVID cases. The ch06 open-question would need a note: "Relevance likely limited to SARS-CoV-2-triggered cases." **No other claims affected.** The paper already has separate sections for post-COVID and non-COVID ME/CFS.
- **Revised claim needed:** "Intrapulmonary shunt is a SARS-CoV-2-specific mechanism; it does not generalize to other viral triggers of ME/CFS."
- **Net significance:** Moderate — restricts the scope of a cross-disease inference but does not affect the paper's core ME/CFS mechanisms.

## Category 12: Evidence Quality Concerns

### 12.1 Selection Bias in Long COVID Shunt Cohorts *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Concern:** Farrow 2023 recruited from a respiratory follow-up clinic — patients selected *because* they had dyspnea or abnormal gas exchange. The ~8% Qs/Qt may represent the severe end of the post-COVID spectrum and may not reflect all post-COVID patients. Harbut 2023 studied hospitalized COVID-19 patients (severe initial infection). Generalizability: the shunt fraction in mild or asymptomatic COVID (or in the general post-COVID population) is unknown. If unselected post-COVID cohorts show normal Qs/Qt, the shunt prevalence is much lower than the current literature suggests.
- **Implication for paper:** The claim "Qs/Qt ~8% persists at 6 months post-COVID" is true for the studied populations but may not hold for all. The PARTIAL integration decision already flags this indirectly via WEAK-EVIDENCE.
- **Certainty impact:** Farrow 2023 cert might be 0.62 → 0.55 after considering selection bias.
- **Mitigation in paper:** Already captures "all evidence from Long COVID" — but should note "recruited from respiratory follow-up; may overestimate shunt prevalence."

### 12.2 Small Sample Sizes and Absence of Pre-COVID Baselines *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Concern:** Farrow: n=26 COVID, 13 healthy. Grist: n=11 Long COVID, 9 healthy. Sandhu 2026: modest sample. All lack pre-COVID within-subject Qs/Qt measurements (impossible for a retrospective study, but crucial because some healthy individuals have baseline Qs/Qt of 5–6% — the "elevation" may partly reflect individual variation, not true disease effect).
- **Implication for paper:** The certainty in all individual studies is bounded by sample size. The "~8%" is a group mean; individual values and range are the better metric. If upper end of normal range is 5%, and some measured individuals have Qs/Qt of 5–7%, the overlap may mean many patients are within normal limits.
- **Certainty impact:** Modest downward adjustment for Grist (0.55 → 0.50) and Sandhu (0.55 → 0.50). Farrow's 0.62 is more robust (better powered).
- **Mitigation in paper:** The environments already note sample sizes implicitly via citations. Could add: "All post-COVID shunt studies are small (n < 30 per group)."

### 12.3 Measurement Limitations: 100% O₂ Method *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Concern:** The classic 100% O₂ shunt measurement (breathing 100% O₂ for 20 min → measure A-a gradient) is the gold standard for Qs/Qt but has known limitations: (a) Hyperoxia may itself open IPAVAs in some individuals (O₂ toxicity → pulmonary vasodilation), potentially creating false-positive shunt. (b) The method requires arterial cannulation — invasive, challenging in severe ME/CFS, and may select for healthier participants. (c) It measures shunt at a single timepoint with non-physiological FiO₂ — the shunt fraction during room air breathing or during exercise may differ. (d) Absorption atelectasis from 100% O₂ can artifactually increase shunt.
- **Evidence link:** Davis 2023 (hyperoxia *reduces* IPAVA flow during exercise — this is the opposite of concern (a), but the interaction between 100% O₂ at rest and IPAVA opening is debated). General pulmonary physiology literature.
- **Implication for paper:** If the 100% O₂ method is used in future ME/CFS studies, methodological caveats must be stated. The paper's @oq:intrapulmonary-shunt-mecfs already notes the test exists — but doesn't flag these specific measurement confounds.
- **Mitigation in paper:** Should add: "Shunt measurement by 100% O₂ method has known limitations: hyperoxia-induced atelectasis may overestimate shunt, and results may not generalize to room-air or exercise conditions." No effect on core paper claims.

### 12.4 IPAVA Detection Semi-Quantitative; Anatomical Fraction Unknown *(origin: brainstorm)*

- **Concern:** IPAVA detection relies on saline contrast echocardiography with a semi-quantitative grading scale (1–3 based on bubble appearance timing). This does not measure actual shunt volume — just presence/absence of anastomotic channels. The same grade can represent vastly different shunt volumes. ¹²⁹Xe MRI is quantitative but expensive and available in few centers. The anatomical fraction of pulmonary blood flow that can bypass capillaries via IPAVAs is unknown — the Qs/Qt of 8% measured by 100% O₂ includes both anatomical shunt (IPAVAs, bronchopulmonary anastomoses) and diffusion limitation effects. The true anatomical fraction attributable to IPAVAs may be lower.
- **Evidence link:** Lyne 2024 (reviews IPAVA prevalence but acknowledges limited quantification); Bush 2022 (bronchopulmonary anastomoses — multiple shunt channels, not just IPAVAs).
- **Implication for paper:** The neat "IPAVA → shunt → Qs/Qt 8%" causal chain in the environments may overstate the anatomical precision. The shunt could be from multiple sources (IPAVAs + bronchopulmonary anastomoses + potential diffusion limitation at the capillary level in damaged lungs).
- **Mitigation in paper:** The environments could specify "intrapulmonary shunt detected" rather than attributing all to IPAVAs. Current wording is largely appropriate (uses "intrapulmonary right-left shunt" as umbrella). Should add: "The anatomical substrate includes multiple potential shunt channels (IPAVAs, bronchopulmonary anastomoses), and their relative contribution is unknown."
- **Certainty impact on anatomical claim:** Lyne 2024 cert could be 0.42 → 0.38 (IPAVAs exist, but shunt attribution to IPAVAs specifically is less certain).
