## 1. Evidence Landscape

**Strong (certainty ≥ 0.60):**
- `raadsheer1994crh` (0.80), `bao2008stress` (0.80), `bao2010crh` (0.78), `meynen2007crh` (0.75), `purba1995crhms` (0.70) — Swaab-group PVN CRH-neuron quantification methodology and cross-condition data.
- `tak2011hpa` (0.72) — meta-analysis: consistent hypocortisolism in CFS, not explained by depression/medication.
- `sauve2023gnrh` (0.65) — GnRH-neuron death in Long COVID hypothalamus (Netherlands Brain Bank approach); precedent for viral, cell-type-specific hypothalamic neuronal loss.

**Weak (certainty < 0.40):**
- `dasilva2025iacfscrh` (0.30) — THE PRIMARY FINDING. Conference-only, n=7, unpublished, single centre, no replication.

**Medium (0.40–0.59):**
- `morris2017hpa` (0.55) — immune-inflammatory pathways drive central HPA hypofunction (mechanism for the driver).
- `debellis2021pituitary` (0.55) — competing autoimmune mechanism (anti-pituitary/hypothalamus antibodies).
- `ferrero2017cns` (0.40) — only prior peer-reviewed ME/CFS autopsy; gliosis/white-matter; no hypothalamic analysis.

**Null / contradicting:**
- Raijmakers 2022 (TSPO PET, no neuroinflammation in CFS; NOT added to bib — cite descriptively, not as `@`) — constrains the neuroinflammatory-driver hypothesis. Caveat: TSPO PET has limited sensitivity for chronic low-grade hypothalamic inflammation and unreliable cell-type attribution.

**Indirect biochemical:** CRH loss → ↓ACTH → ↓cortisol → immune disinhibition — consistent with documented hypocortisolism (tak2011, morris2017).

## 2. Internal Contradictions

1. **CRH direction reversal (fundamental, informative not disqualifying):** Depression (`raadsheer1994crh`) and MS (`purba1995crhms`) show *increased* PVN CRH neurons; Da Silva reports *decreased* in ME/CFS. Same brain bank, same region, same method. → The opposite direction argues ME/CFS is NOT simple "stress-system burnout" and is pathophysiologically distinct. Frame as a distinguishing feature, not a contradiction to suppress.
2. **Driver mechanism dispute:** neuroinflammatory/excitotoxic (Da Silva's proposal, morris2017) vs. autoimmune (`debellis2021pituitary`). Cannot resolve without autoantibody + histology in the same brains. → present both as `#open-question`.
3. **TSPO null vs. neuroinflammation driver:** partial contradiction; TSPO sensitivity caveat applies. → inline caveat.

Band rule: raadsheer(0.80) vs dasilva(0.30) is a >0.15 gap, but they measure *different conditions*, not the same claim — not a same-claim contradiction. The ME/CFS-specific claim rests solely on the 0.30 source.

## 3. Evidence Summary

- **Establishes:** ME/CFS is associated with hypocortisolism (strong, tak2011); viral hypothalamic cell-type-specific neuronal death has a precedent (sauve2023gnrh); a preliminary, unpublished autopsy series reports selective PVN CRH-neuron depletion in severe ME/CFS.
- **Strongest supportable claim:** IF replicated, selective CRH-neuron loss would locate ME/CFS hypocortisolism centrally (hypothalamus) rather than adrenally — a mechanistic relocation, not a new disease claim.
- **Must remain speculative:** the CRH-depletion finding itself (n=7, unpublished); its driver (neuroinflammation vs autoimmune); its generalizability beyond severe/very-severe cases.
- **Not supported:** any claim that this is established, replicated, or applies to mild/moderate ME/CFS.
- **Primary target chapter:** ch09-endocrine sec-01 (mechanisms of HPA dysfunction) — slots as a structural, central-origin mechanism alongside the existing pituitary-injury and adrenal-atrophy mechanisms.

## 4. Clinical Relevance

- MEDIUM (mechanistic context for treatment decisions). If central CRH machinery is damaged, low-dose cortisol replacement addresses symptom (low cortisol) not cause, and may be counterproductive; favors upstream anti-inflammatory/immunomodulatory strategies. This is a mechanistic caution, NOT a clinical recommendation.
- Severity applicability: **severe / very-severe only** (all 7 decedents were severe). Applicability to mild/moderate UNKNOWN — state explicitly.
