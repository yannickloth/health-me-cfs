**Date:** 2026-05-24
**Research Stream:** tissue-specific-autoantibodies
**Status:** Phase 1 Complete

## Papers Found

### 1. Anchor Paper: Tatai et al. 2026 (GeroScience)
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s11357-026-02286-9
- **Title:** Tissue-specific autoantibody signatures reveal immune alterations undetected by routine serology in long COVID
- **Sample:** n=114 long COVID, n=36 controls (tissue-specific); n=137 long COVID, n=81 controls (ANA cohort)
- **Key Findings:**
  - Tissue-specific autoantibodies: cardiac 54%, vascular 34%, pulmonary 34%
  - IgM-dominated (60% vs 36% in controls, p<0.05)
  - NOT detected by routine ANA HEp-2 testing
  - Associated with neurovascular manifestations: hypertension, headache, anosmia, ageusia
  - CRP only marginally elevated in seropositive individuals
  - Polyreactive pattern (up to 8 distinct bands per patient)
  - Follow-up (n=30, 141 days): persistent IgM and emergence of new autoantibodies
- **Certainty:** 0.65
- **Quality Notes:** Moderate sample size, peer-reviewed Springer journal, single study, not yet independently replicated. First study applying simultaneous multi-tissue screening to long COVID.

### 2. Freitag et al. 2021 (J Clin Med) - ME/CFS GPCR Autoantibodies
- **DOI:** 10.3390/jcm10163675
- **PMID:** 34441971
- **Title:** Autoantibodies to Vasoregulative G-Protein-Coupled Receptors Correlate with Symptom Severity, Autonomic Dysfunction and Disability in Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome
- **Sample:** n=116 ME/CFS patients
- **Key Findings:**
  - Autoantibodies to vasoregulative GPCRs: beta-adrenergic (β1, β2), muscarinic acetylcholine (M3, M4), endothelin A/B (ETA/B), angiotensin II type 1 (AT1)
  - Most AABs correlated with fatigue and muscle pain (infection-triggered onset)
  - Cognitive impairment correlated with AT1-R and ETA-R AAB
  - Different correlation patterns in infection vs. non-infection triggered ME/CFS
  - Provides evidence for role of AABs in disease pathomechanism
- **Certainty:** 0.50
- **Quality Notes:** Moderate sample size, peer-reviewed, single study, measures functional activity of autoantibodies.

### 3. Tölle et al. 2020 (J Clin Med) - Immunoadsorption Pilot in ME/CFS
- **DOI:** 10.3390/jcm9082443
- **PMID:** 32751659
- **Title:** Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome: Efficacy of Repeat Immunoadsorption
- **Sample:** n=5 ME/CFS patients (follow-up from previous pilot)
- **Key Findings:**
  - Modified IA protocol resulted in 80-90% decline of total IgG and β2AR-AB
  - 4/5 patients showed rapid improvement lasting 6-12 months
  - Treatment well tolerated
  - Provides proof-of-concept for autoantibody-mediated ME/CFS
- **Certainty:** 0.40
- **Quality Notes:** Very small sample (n=5), no control group, observational follow-up. Supports autoantibody hypothesis but limited by small n.

### 4. Pressler et al. 2024 (Trials) - IA-PACS-CFS RCT Protocol
- **DOI:** 10.1186/s13063-024-07982-5
- **PMID:** 38454468
- **Title:** IA-PACS-CFS: a double-blinded, randomized, sham-controlled, exploratory trial of immunoadsorption in patients with chronic fatigue syndrome (CFS) including patients with post-acute COVID-19 CFS (PACS-CFS)
- **Sample:** Planned n=66 (44 active, 22 sham)
- **Key Findings:**
  - Protocol paper for RCT testing 5 cycles of IA every other day
  - Includes both ME/CFS and post-COVID ME/CFS patients
  - Primary outcome: Chalder Fatigue Scale
  - Secondary: biomarker-related objectives, cognitive outcomes, physical parameters
  - Safety, tolerability assessment
- **Certainty:** 0.35 (protocol only, results pending)
- **Quality Notes:** Rigorous RCT design, sham-controlled, double-blinded. Results will be critical for validating autoantibody-mediated mechanism.

### 5. Anft et al. 2025 (Mol Ther) - Immunoadsorption in COVID-19-Induced ME/CFS
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ymthe.2025.01.007
- **PMID:** 39797400
- **Title:** Effect of immunoadsorption on clinical presentation and immune alterations in COVID-19-induced and/or aggravated ME/CFS
- **Sample:** n=12 post-COVID ME/CFS patients
- **Key Findings:**
  - ANSR autoantibodies (β1, β2, M3, M4) nearly eliminated after IA
  - Decrease in pro-inflammatory cytokines: IL-4, IL-2, IL-1β, TNF, IL-17A
  - Significant reduction in soluble spike protein
  - Strong positive correlation between cytokines and ANSR-AABs in spike protein-positive patients
  - Neuropsychological function improvement 30 days post-IA
  - Modest hand grip strength improvement
  - Rebound of removed proteins within 1 month
  - No significant improvement in self-reported symptoms or ME/CFS questionnaires
- **Certainty:** 0.50
- **Quality Notes:** Small sample (n=12), observational (no control), but detailed immune profiling. Shows biological effect but limited clinical benefit, raising questions about autoantibody primacy vs. downstream effect.

### 6. Wallukat et al. 2021 (J Transl Autoimmun) - GPCR Autoantibodies in Long COVID
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.jtauto.2021.100100
- **PMID:** 34582287 (Note: PMID needs verification)
- **Title:** Functional autoantibodies against G-protein coupled receptors in patients with persistent long-COVID-19 symptoms
- **Sample:** Long COVID patients (n not specified in abstract)
- **Key Findings:**
  - Detected functional autoantibodies against GPCRs in long COVID
  - Similar pattern to ME/CFS (β-adrenergic, muscarinic)
  - Suggests pathogenic mechanism linking long COVID to ME/CFS
- **Certainty:** 0.45
- **Quality Notes:** Sample size unclear from available data, single study, not yet independently replicated. Critical for cross-disease comparison.

## Key Themes Across Papers

### 1. IgM Dominance and Failed Class Switching
- Tatai et al. (2026): IgM-dominant tissue-specific autoantibodies in long COVID
- Suggests persistent antigenic stimulation and failed/atypical class switching
- Clinical implication: Ongoing immune dysregulation rather than resolved autoimmune response

### 2. ANA HEp-2 Diagnostic Gap
- Tatai et al. (2026): Routine ANA HEp-2 testing failed to detect tissue-specific autoantibodies
- 83% seropositive on Western blot vs. no discriminatory value on ANA HEp-2
- Clinical implication: Standard serology inadequate for long COVID/ME/CFS autoimmunity diagnosis

### 3. GPCR Autoantibodies in ME/CFS and Long COVID
- Freitag et al. (2021): GPCR autoantibodies correlate with ME/CFS symptom severity (n=116)
- Wallukat et al. (2021): GPCR autoantibodies in long COVID (pattern similar to ME/CFS)
- Clinical implication: Autonomic dysfunction mediated by receptor-modulating autoantibodies

### 4. Immunoadsorption as Therapeutic Approach
- Tölle et al. (2020): Pilot shows 4/5 responders with 6-12 month benefit
- Anft et al. (2025): Biological effect (antibody/cytokine reduction) but limited clinical benefit
- Pressler et al. (2024): RCT in progress (critical for validation)
- Clinical implication: Mixed evidence suggests autoantibodies may be epiphenomenon in some patients

### 5. Cross-Disease Overlap
- GPCR autoantibodies found in both ME/CFS and long COVID
- Similar receptor targets: β1/β2 adrenergic, M3/M4 muscarinic
- Clinical implication: Shared pathophysiology supports ME/CFS as a post-viral autoimmune syndrome

---

## Null Results and Contradictory Evidence

### Negative Trial Results (Awaited)
- Pressler et al. RCT (NCT05710770): Results pending as of 2026-05-24
- Critical for validating autoantibody-mediated mechanism

### Limited Clinical Benefit Despite Biological Effect
- Anft et al. (2025): Antibody/cytokine reduction but no significant self-reported symptom improvement
- Raises questions about autoantibody primacy vs. downstream effect
- Suggests patient heterogeneity: some may be autoantibody-mediated, others not
