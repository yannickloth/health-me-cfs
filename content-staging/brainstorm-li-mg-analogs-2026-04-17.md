# Brainstorm: Substances with Li/Mg-Analogous Relationships in ME/CFS
**Date:** 2026-04-17
**Trigger:** Analysis of Li/Mg ionic substitution, shared enzyme targets, and convergent mechanisms  
**Context:** Extends brainstorm-calcium-2026-04-18.md (S3: Mg as Ca²⁺ antagonist) and  
literature-lithium-ip3-calcium-2026-04-17.md (Li → IMPase → PIP₂ → Ca²⁺ chain)

---

## Framework: What Makes Li/Mg Analogous?

Five relationship types identified between Li and Mg that may generalize to other substances:

| Type | Li/Mg example | Generalizable to |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| Ionic mimicry at shared enzyme site | Li⁺ competes with Mg²⁺ at GSK-3β catalytic site | Zn²⁺, Mn²⁺ at Mg²⁺-dependent kinases |
| Upstream/downstream convergence | Mg is GSK-3β cofactor; Li blocks it | Berberine (AMPK → GSK-3β), valproate (inositol) |
| Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase substrate + expression regulation | Mg-ATP is substrate; Li upregulates pump expression via GSK-3β | Taurine (pump stability), K⁺ (co-transport partner) |
| NMDA block complementarity | Mg²⁺ blocks channel; Li reduces upstream drive + downstream damage | Zn²⁺, memantine, agmatine |
| Thyroid deiodinase axis (partial antagonism) | Li suppresses T4→T3; Mg supports deiodinase | Se (stronger deiodinase cofactor), Zn²⁺ |

## Tier 1: High-value analogs (strong mechanistic overlap, ME/CFS relevance, integration-ready)

### A1. Taurine — The Third Arm of the Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase / NCX Axis

**Category:** Supplement / endogenous osmolyte  
**Certainty:** 0.45 (mechanism solid; ME/CFS-specific data exists via metabolomics)

**Mechanism:**
- Taurine at 10–40 mM intracellular concentrations is the dominant organic osmolyte in cardiomyocytes and skeletal muscle — directly where ME/CFS calcium overload is proposed (Wirth NCX reversal model)
- Taurine stabilizes Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase α-subunit conformation, increasing pump Vmax without changing Km — mechanistically distinct from Mg-ATP (which is the substrate) and from Li (which regulates expression)
- Taurine reduces intracellular Na⁺ accumulation → less NCX reversal → less Ca²⁺ import: the same endpoint as Mg-ATP repletion, through a different mechanism
- Taurine chloramine (TauCl, formed when taurine reacts with hypochlorous acid from activated neutrophils) suppresses NF-κB and NLRP3 inflammasome at a different node from Li (TLR4) and Mg (IκB stabilization) → third parallel NF-κB suppression pathway
- Plasma taurine: significantly depleted in ME/CFS metabolomics (Germain2017) and fails to recover post-exertion (Glass2023) — direct disease-state evidence
- Taurine deficiency in aging Science 2023 (Singh2023): taurine replacement reverses multiple aging hallmarks in mammals — mitochondrial dysfunction, cellular senescence, inflammation — all overlapping with ME/CFS pathophysiology
- NCS-1 interaction: taurine modulates NCS-1 calcium sensor activity (documented in cardiac cells) — overlaps with Li's NCS-1/InsP3R1 disruption mechanism

**Relationship to Li:**
- Partially substitutable at the NCX/Na⁺ axis endpoint (both reduce intracellular Na⁺ → reduce NCX reversal)
- Non-redundant at NF-κB (different nodes: taurine via TauCl, Li via TLR4/GSK-3β)
- Non-redundant at mitochondria (taurine protects against Ca²⁺-mediated mPTP opening; Li induces mitophagy via IMPase)
- Synergistic: taurine reduces the Ca²⁺ overload that Li's downstream pathways try to repair — sequential complementarity

**Relationship to Mg:**
- Both support Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase but via completely different mechanisms (Mg = substrate; taurine = structural stabilizer of pump conformation)
- Mg depletion → TRPM7 dysfunction → intracellular Mg deficit → pump substrate shortage → Na⁺ accumulates
- Taurine depletion → pump destabilization → Na⁺ accumulates via different route
- Same endpoint, additive risk, independent correction

**Falsifiable predictions:**
- (a) ME/CFS patients with lowest plasma taurine should show highest post-exercise intracellular Na⁺ (23Na-MRI), independent of RBC Mg status — if both predict Na⁺ accumulation additively, combined taurine+Mg depletion should give worst sodium scores
- (b) Taurine 3g/day for 12 weeks should reduce post-exercise Na⁺ accumulation independently from Mg repletion
- (c) Li + taurine combination should show additive NF-κB suppression in ME/CFS PBMCs in vitro (different nodes: TauCl vs TLR4)

**Integration targets:**
- ch16-supplements: expand taurine rationale with Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase and NCX mechanism (not just "mitochondrial")
- ch06: Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase section — add taurine as third axis alongside Mg-ATP and Li
- ch14h: calcium cascade section — add taurine as NCX-targeting intervention

### A2. Selenium — The Thyroid Axis Counterweight to Lithium

**Category:** Essential trace mineral / deiodinase cofactor  
**Certainty:** 0.50 (deiodinase mechanism very well established; ME/CFS thyroid application is extrapolation)

**Mechanism:**
- All three deiodinase isoforms (DIO1, DIO2, DIO3) are selenoproteins — selenium is the catalytic residue (selenocysteine at active site), not merely a cofactor
- Without Se, deiodinase cannot catalyze T4→T3 conversion at all — it is the rate-limiting elemental requirement
- Mg supports deiodinase indirectly (enzyme stability, ATP-dependent iodothyronine transport); Se is the direct catalytic residue
- Li suppresses T4→T3 conversion; Se depletion eliminates it — they are additive risks on the same pathway
- Low T3 syndrome in ME/CFS is documented (fT3 low with normal TSH); Li + Se deficiency together could produce severe functional hypothyroidism even with normal TSH
- Se also regulates thioredoxin reductase (TrxR), glutathione peroxidase (GPx) — the primary antioxidant selenoproteins — whose depletion compounds oxidative stress in ME/CFS mitochondria
- ME/CFS patients show evidence of elevated oxidative stress; Se deficiency removes two major antioxidant enzyme systems simultaneously

**Relationship to Li:**
- Direct counterweight: Se repletion is the most effective physiological correction for Li-induced T4→T3 suppression
- Se monitoring should accompany Li monitoring — currently absent from documented Li protocol
- Prediction: ME/CFS patients starting Li with Se deficiency should show faster fT3 decline than Se-replete patients

**Relationship to Mg:**
- Both are cofactors for different steps in the thyroid axis (Mg: transport; Se: catalysis)
- Both are depleted by chronic stress and chronic illness
- Se and Mg depletion co-occur — both removed by excessive cortisol, chronic diarrhea, malabsorption
- Se + Mg combination supports the full deiodinase axis; either alone is insufficient

**Falsifiable predictions:**
- (a) ME/CFS patients on low-dose Li should show faster fT3 decline if baseline Se is in lowest quartile vs. highest quartile
- (b) Se repletion (100–200 mcg selenomethionine) in Se-deficient ME/CFS patients starting Li should attenuate the expected fT3 drop at 3 months
- (c) In ME/CFS without Li: baseline Se should correlate with fT3 level and inversely with fatigue severity (via deiodinase efficiency)

**Integration targets:**
- ch14a Li monitoring protocol: add Se assessment and repletion as part of Li safety protocol (not currently mentioned)
- ch16-supplements: Se section (if absent) — add deiodinase mechanism explicitly in ME/CFS context
- published/Li-SAMA letter: add Se as thyroid axis protectant when starting Li

### A3. Zinc — The Triple-Overlap Mineral (NMDA + GSK-3β + Deiodinase)

**Category:** Essential trace mineral  
**Certainty:** 0.42 (NMDA block well established; GSK-3β and deiodinase roles are secondary)

**Mechanism:**
- Zn²⁺ blocks NMDA receptors at the Mg²⁺ site on NR2A/NR2B subunits — voltage-independent block (unlike Mg²⁺ which is voltage-dependent). Zn²⁺ IC₅₀ on NR2B ≈ 10–20 nM (nanomolar — far lower than Mg IC₅₀ in mM range). Zn²⁺ is thus a more potent NMDA modulator than Mg at NR2B
- GSK-3β: Zn²⁺ inhibits GSK-3β directly (Zn²⁺ coordinates at the Mg²⁺ catalytic site, similar mechanism to Li⁺). Low-dose Zn supplementation reduces GSK-3β activity in neuronal models
- Deiodinase: DIO1 requires Zn²⁺ for structure (zinc finger domain in the enzyme complex); Zn deficiency impairs T4→T3 conversion independently of Se depletion
- Synaptic Zn²⁺ is released from glutamatergic neurons during activity and modulates NMDA tone in real time — this is a physiological endogenous NMDA brake, analogous to but distinct from Mg²⁺
- Zn²⁺ is rapidly depleted by chronic immune activation (zinc is sequestered by activated macrophages via metallothionein induction — part of the "nutritional immunity" response) — ME/CFS chronic immune activation could chronically deplete synaptic Zn²⁺
- ME/CFS: plasma Zn typically not measured; no direct ME/CFS Zn metabolomics data found — gap

**Relationship to Li:**
- Three convergent points: GSK-3β inhibition, NMDA modulation, deiodinase axis
- Zn²⁺ + Li could have additive GSK-3β inhibition — needs monitoring (excessive GSK-3β inhibition could impair insulin signaling, proliferation)
- Zn²⁺ NMDA block is voltage-independent and persistent; Li's NMDA effects are indirect (reducing NR2B expression) — different kinetics, non-redundant

**Relationship to Mg:**
- Both block NMDA but at different subunits and with different kinetics
- Both support deiodinase (Mg indirectly, Zn structurally) — additive for T3 production
- Mg and Zn compete for intestinal absorption (both divalent, same transporters) — taking high-dose Mg can deplete Zn. Clinically important in ME/CFS where both may be deficient

**Falsifiable predictions:**
- (a) ME/CFS patients with lowest plasma Zn should show greatest NMDA-mediated excitotoxicity markers (glutamate/GABA ratio in MRS)
- (b) Zn supplementation (15–25 mg/day zinc glycinate) should improve central sensitization symptoms independently of Mg repletion
- (c) Combined Zn + Mg supplementation should show greater T3 improvement than either alone in ME/CFS patients with low-normal fT3

**Integration targets:**
- ch16-supplements: Zn section — add NMDA block and GSK-3β inhibition mechanisms (not just "immune support")
- ch08-neurological: central sensitization section — add Zn²⁺ as endogenous NMDA modulator, depleted by chronic immune activation
- ch14a Li protocol: add Zn interaction note (Zn + Li additive at GSK-3β; absorption competition with Mg)

### A4. Potassium — Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase Co-Dependency with Mg

**Category:** Electrolyte / essential mineral  
**Certainty:** 0.55 (mechanism established; clinical sequencing implication for ME/CFS is underemphasized)

**Mechanism:**
- Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase requires BOTH Mg-ATP (substrate) AND K⁺ (extracellular binding site for the antiport step) — neither alone is sufficient for pump function
- K⁺ binds the E2 conformation of the pump extracellularly, triggering dephosphorylation and Na⁺ release. Without K⁺, the pump is locked in E1-P (phosphorylated) state even with abundant Mg-ATP
- Hypokalemia renders Mg-ATP supplementation functionally useless for pump restoration
- ME/CFS: fludrocortisone (common OI treatment) causes K⁺ loss as a side effect — directly undermining the Mg-ATP supplementation rationale
- Salt loading protocols (NaCl ± fludrocortisone) for POTS/OI in ME/CFS further dilute K⁺ without replacement
- Low K⁺ causes muscle weakness, fatigue, and arrhythmia — symptoms that overlap with ME/CFS and could masquerade as worsening ME/CFS in patients on fludrocortisone

**Relationship to Mg:**
- Sequential necessity: K⁺ must be adequate for Mg-ATP to support the pump
- Mg and K⁺ co-depletion common (low Mg impairs renal K⁺ retention — hypomagnesemia is a cause of refractory hypokalemia)
- Correcting Mg depletion often corrects K⁺ depletion simultaneously (Mg required for renal K⁺ handling)
- "Refractory hypokalemia" (K⁺ fails to correct despite supplementation) is a classic sign of unrecognized hypomagnesemia

**Relationship to Li:**
- Li competes weakly with K⁺ at the pump's K⁺ binding site (Li is a Na⁺ mimic and enters via Na⁺ channels but also weakly inhibits the K⁺ step of ATPase at high concentrations)
- At therapeutic Li doses this competition is minimal, but in a K⁺-depleted patient, even small Li-mediated pump interference becomes more significant

**Falsifiable predictions:**
- (a) ME/CFS patients on fludrocortisone with low-normal K⁺ should show worse Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase function (post-exercise sodium accumulation on 23Na-MRI) than those with replete K⁺ and same Mg status
- (b) Combined K⁺ + Mg repletion should restore pump function better than Mg alone in K⁺-depleted patients
- (c) Refractory fatigue worsening after fludrocortisone initiation should correlate with K⁺ decline

**Integration targets:**
- ch16: K⁺ section — add explicit note that K⁺ and Mg are co-requirements for Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase (not just two separate electrolytes)
- ch14a severe protocol: add check — before starting Mg for Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase support, verify K⁺ is replete; check K⁺ in fludrocortisone patients
- ch16: Mg section — add "refractory hypokalemia as Mg deficiency sign"

## Tier 2: Moderate-value analogs (2–3 mechanistic overlaps, more speculative in ME/CFS)

### B1. Berberine — Convergent GSK-3β + NF-κB + Mitochondria via AMPK

**Category:** Plant alkaloid supplement  
**Certainty:** 0.38 (AMPK mechanism solid; ME/CFS-specific data absent)

**Mechanism:**
- Berberine activates AMPK → AMPK phosphorylates and inactivates GSK-3β — indirect GSK-3β inhibition overlapping with Li's direct inhibition
- Berberine suppresses NF-κB (IKKβ inhibition) — different node from Li (TLR4/GSK-3β) and Mg (IκB stabilization), a third parallel suppression point
- Berberine inhibits Complex I of mitochondrial electron transport → triggers mitophagy (AMPK-dependent) — analogous to Li's mTOR-independent mitophagy induction
- Berberine reduces NLRP3 inflammasome activation — overlapping with Li's NLRP3 modulation
- Berberine has anti-dysbiotic effects (targets SIBO-associated bacteria) — relevant for ME/CFS gut-brain axis
- Notable: berberine poorly crosses BBB (P-gp substrate) — its central effects are limited vs. Li which has good CNS penetration

**Relationship to Li:**
- Shares 4 of Li's 5 ME/CFS-relevant mechanisms (GSK-3β, mitophagy, NF-κB/NLRP3, mitochondrial quality control)
- Missing: Li's IMPase → IP3/Ca²⁺ axis and glymphatic/aquaporin effects
- Could serve as a partial Li substitute with different safety profile (berberine: GI side effects, drug interactions via CYP3A4; Li: renal/thyroid)
- Not ionic mimicry — mechanism is via AMPK cascade, not direct ion competition

**Integration targets:**
- ch18-emerging-therapies: add berberine as convergent GSK-3β/NF-κB/mitophagy agent
- ch16-supplements: add berberine with explicit mechanistic comparison to Li's pathways

### B2. Inositol — Li's Upstream Antagonist and Mg's PIP₂ Ally

**Category:** Supplement / PIP₂ precursor  
**Certainty:** 0.40 (PIP₂ mechanism well established; ME/CFS-specific prediction from PIP₂ exhaustion hypothesis)

**Mechanism:**
- Myo-inositol is the head-group precursor for phosphatidylinositol (PI) → PIP₂ synthesis
- Li depletes inositol via IMPase inhibition — inositol supplementation directly reverses this depletion (Belmaker 1998: behavioral reversal of Li effects by inositol)
- TRPM7 is a channel for Mg²⁺ entry (DuPreez2023); TRPM7 also regulates PIP₂ levels via its kinase domain (TRPM7 kinase phosphorylates enzymes in the PI pathway)
- If TRPM7 is dysfunctional in ME/CFS (DuPreez2023), both Mg²⁺ homeostasis AND PI/PIP₂ metabolism may be impaired via the same channel — inositol supplementation could partially rescue the PIP₂ arm independently
- The PIP₂ exhaustion hypothesis (hyp:pip2-exhaustion, ch14h) proposes GPCR autoantibodies drive PLC to deplete PIP₂ — inositol could partially refill this pool
- MRS studies in ME/CFS: altered myo-inositol in brain parenchyma documented — suggests CNS inositol metabolism is perturbed

**Relationship to Li:**
- Direct antagonist at the IMPase/inositol step — predicts that inositol supplementation partially offsets Li's beneficial IP3 reduction (if the Li mechanism is via inositol depletion)
- This creates a paradox: inositol is beneficial for PIP₂ pool restoration (Mg/TRPM7 axis) but could undermine Li's mechanism (inositol depletion hypothesis)
- Resolution: if Li's benefit is primarily via GSK-3β (not IMPase), then inositol is compatible with Li therapy

**Integration targets:**
- ch16-supplements: add inositol with explicit note on Li interaction paradox
- ch14h: PIP₂ exhaustion hypothesis — expand inositol as treatment strategy (H5 in brainstorm-calcium already identifies this)

### B3. NAD⁺ Precursors (NMN/NR) — Mg-Dependent NAD Kinase Axis

**Category:** Supplement  
**Certainty:** 0.35 (Mg-NAD kinase link is real; ME/CFS-specific synergy is speculative)

**Mechanism:**
- NAD kinase (NADK) converts NAD⁺ to NADP⁺, which is required for glutathione recycling and the pentose phosphate pathway — NADK is strictly Mg²⁺-dependent
- Mg deficiency → reduced NADK activity → NADP⁺ depletion → glutathione recycling impaired → oxidative stress compounds
- NMN/NR supplementation increases NAD⁺ pool, but the conversion to NADP⁺ for antioxidant defense requires Mg-NADK
- Without Mg repletion, NMN/NR supplementation may raise NAD⁺ but fail to improve NADP⁺-dependent antioxidant defense
- Li: no direct NAD kinase connection; Li indirectly supports mitochondria via mitophagy (clearing damaged mitochondria that waste NAD⁺)
- Synergy: Mg (NADK cofactor) + NMN/NR (substrate) → maximal NADP⁺ production for antioxidant defense; Li (mitophagy) → reduces NAD⁺ wastage on damaged organelles

**Integration targets:**
- ch16-supplements: NMN/NR section — add Mg-NADK co-requirement note
- ch06: energy metabolism — add NADK as Mg-dependent step in antioxidant defense

### B4. Creatine — Phosphocreatine Buffer and Mg-ATP Sparing Agent

**Category:** Supplement  
**Certainty:** 0.40 (phosphocreatine mechanism established; ME/CFS creatine kinase relevance documented)

**Mechanism:**
- Creatine kinase reaction: phosphocreatine + ADP ↔ creatine + ATP — requires Mg²⁺ as Mg-ADP and Mg-ATP chelate
- Phosphocreatine buffer protects the Mg-ATP pool during rapid energy demand — acts as a rapid ATP resynthesis reserve
- In ME/CFS, creatine kinase activity is altered (documented in several studies); phosphocreatine repletion slower than controls post-exercise (31P-MRS data)
- Creatine supplementation restores the phosphocreatine buffer, reducing reliance on oxidative phosphorylation for burst activity
- Without adequate Mg, creatine kinase cannot function optimally (Mg required for the reaction)
- No direct Li interaction — creatine is an orthogonal energy buffer, not sharing Li's neuroinflammation targets

**Integration targets:**
- ch16-supplements: creatine section — add Mg co-requirement explicitly
- ch06: phosphocreatine buffer section — add creatine supplementation as mechanistically supported by 31P-MRS data

## Tier 3: Speculative analogs (1–2 overlaps, creative bridges)

### C1. Valproate — Li's Inositol-Depleting Sibling

**Certainty:** 0.25 (mechanism parallel to Li; clinical use in ME/CFS is not established)

- Valproate depletes brain inositol via different route: GSK-3β inhibition → reduced MIPS (myo-inositol-phosphate synthase) activity
- Both Li and valproate converge on inositol depletion and GSK-3β inhibition — the "inositol depletion hypothesis" applies to both
- Valproate additionally has HDAC inhibition (histone deacetylase) — epigenetic effects, potential for gene expression changes relevant to ME/CFS neuroinflammation
- Valproate used in small open-label series for ME/CFS-adjacent conditions (fibromyalgia, allodynia) but no controlled data
- Risk: hepatotoxicity, teratogenicity, significant weight effects — safety profile worse than Li for ME/CFS use
- Integration target: ch18-emerging — add valproate as Li-analog with overlapping mechanism but worse safety profile

### C2. Memantine — NMDA Block Analog to Mg²⁺ at Different Site

**Certainty:** 0.30 (NMDA mechanism established; ME/CFS application speculative but growing interest)

- Memantine is an open-channel NMDA antagonist — blocks the channel at a site distinct from Mg²⁺ but with similar functional effect (reducing Ca²⁺ influx during pathological NMDA activation)
- At therapeutic doses, memantine is "voltage-independent" and "use-dependent" — blocks only during excessive NMDA activation, sparing physiological transmission
- This is mechanistically superior to Mg²⁺ for neuroprotection: Mg²⁺ block is voltage-dependent and can be relieved by depolarization; memantine block is more persistent during excitotoxic bursts
- ME/CFS: central sensitization and allodynia suggest pathological NMDA overactivation; memantine could provide sustained protection Mg cannot
- Case reports and small series in fibromyalgia (cognitive improvement, pain reduction); no ME/CFS RCTs
- Li analog: memantine addresses the NMDA Ca²⁺ overload arm; Li addresses the upstream neuroinflammatory drive. Non-redundant, potentially complementary
- Integration target: ch18-emerging — add memantine as Mg-functional-analog at NMDA, with Li comparison

### C3. Agmatine — Endogenous NMDA Modulator and Imidazoline Receptor Agonist

**Certainty:** 0.25 (mechanism intriguing; clinical evidence sparse)

- Agmatine (decarboxylated arginine) is an endogenous NMDA channel blocker (at Mg²⁺/polyamine site) and NOS inhibitor
- Also an imidazoline I₂ receptor agonist — I₂ receptors regulate monoamine oxidase, potentially relevant to ME/CFS neurotransmitter dysregulation
- Agmatine has antidepressant, neuroprotective, and anti-inflammatory effects in rodent models
- Mechanistic overlap with Mg²⁺ (NMDA block) and Li (neuroprotection, inflammation modulation)
- No ME/CFS data; preliminary human data in neuropathic pain
- Integration target: ch18-emerging — add agmatine as speculative endogenous NMDA modulator

### C4. Manganese (Mn²⁺) — Functional Mg²⁺ Substitute at Some Kinases (Toxic at Excess)

**Certainty:** 0.20 (mechanistic interest; no clinical application)

- Mn²⁺ substitutes for Mg²⁺ as cofactor in several kinases (including GSK-3β, RNA polymerase, DNA repair enzymes)
- At physiological concentrations Mn²⁺ supports normal enzyme function; at excess → neurotoxic (manganism)
- Relevant to ME/CFS as disease analogy: Mn²⁺ transport uses TRPM7 (the same Mg²⁺ entry channel dysfunctional in ME/CFS per DuPreez2023)
- If TRPM7 is dysfunctional, both Mg²⁺ AND Mn²⁺ entry are impaired — could contribute to kinase dysfunction beyond Mg deficiency alone
- Not a supplementation candidate but important mechanistic insight: TRPM7 dysfunction is not just a Mg depletion story, it's a broader divalent cation transport failure
- Integration target: ch14h — TRPM7 section: note that Mn²⁺ and other divalent cations also require TRPM7, broadening the functional consequences of its dysfunction

## Summary: The Li/Mg/Taurine/Se/Zn Pentagon

The most clinically actionable insight is that five substances converge on overlapping but non-redundant mechanisms in ME/CFS:

```

                    │         GSK-3β Inhibition               │
```

**Clinical sequencing implication (novel, not currently documented):**
1. K⁺ — verify replete first (prerequisite for Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase)
2. Mg + Se + Zn — restore cofactor trifecta (deiodinase, NMDA, ATPase)
3. Taurine — stabilize pump and reduce NCX reversal
4. Li — add last, with thyroid protection already in place from steps 2–3

This sequencing is not documented anywhere in the current treatment chapters.
