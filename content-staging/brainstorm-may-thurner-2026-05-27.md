# Brainstorm: May Thurner Syndrome Integration into ME/CFS Paper
**Date:** 2026-05-27
**Context:** Phase 1 literature research revealed ZERO direct MTS-ME/CFS connections in literature; integration is entirely speculative and theoretical.

---

## Rank Definitions

- **Tier 1 (0.3-0.7 certainty):** Mechanistically plausible, grounded in indirect evidence, testable with existing methods
- **Tier 2 (0.1-0.3 certainty):** Logical extrapolations, requires new conceptual bridges, testable but methodologically challenging
- **Tier 3 (0.0-0.1 certainty):** Highly speculative, multiple inferential leaps, currently difficult to falsify

## 1. Novel Hypotheses

### Tier 1

**H1: MTS-induced venous congestion as primary preload failure driver**
- **Rationale:** MTS causes 50-70% LCIV stenosis → reduced femoral return → ~5-10% effective blood volume sequestration; matches ME/CFS hypovolemia magnitude
- **Evidence link:** Neglén2008 (anatomy), Wolpert2020/O'Sullivan2018 (fatigue prevalence), ME/CFS blood volume deficits (ch10)
- **Certainty:** 0.6
- **Prediction:** ME/CFS patients with MTS will show larger standing blood volume redistribution (>15% vs <10% controls) on plethysmography

**H2: Venous pressure gradient disruption → impaired glymphatic CSF-ISF exchange**
- **Rationale:** Glymphatic perivascular flow driven by arterial pulsation - venous pressure gradient; elevated venous pressure reduces gradient efficiency
- **Evidence link:** Patel2024 (venous stasis → glymphatic), Marshall2022 (cerebral venous outflow impairment), glymphatic dysfunction in 65% ME/CFS (ch14a)
- **Certainty:** 0.5
- **Prediction:** DTI-ALPS index inversely correlated with iliac vein compression severity in ME/CFS cohort

**H3: MTS as amplifier of orthostatic intolerance through IJV flow coupling**
- **Rationale:** Hartung2019 shows 36% IJV flow reduction upright in MTS; IJV stenosis already implicated in ME/CFS orthostatic intolerance (Marshall2022)
- **Evidence link:** Hartung2019 (IJV flow), orthostatic intolerance prevalence 34-58% in MTS (Ferreira2023)
- **Certainty:** 0.5
- **Prediction:** ME/CFS + MTS patients show synergistic upright IJV flow reduction (interaction effect > additive)

**H4: Chronic venous distension → endothelial glycocalyx shedding → capillary leak**
- **Rationale:** Sustained venous hypertension damages glycocalyx → increased capillary permeability → plasma extravasation → worsened hypovolemia
- **Evidence link:** Endothelial dysfunction in ME/CFS (ch6), glycocalyx markers (syndecan-1, hyaluronan) in venous disease, O'Sullivan2018 SF-36 vitality improvement suggests reversibility
- **Certainty:** 0.4
- **Prediction:** Plasma syndecan-1 elevated in ME/CFS + MTS; correlates with orthostatic symptom severity

**H5: Autonomic remodeling from chronic venous congestion**
- **Rationale:** Anderson2021 shows reduced HRV, elevated NE in MTS; chronic baroreceptor input from elevated iliac pressure may reset autonomic setpoints
- **Evidence link:** Anderson2021 (HRV/NE), sympathetic dominance in ME/CFS (ch6), Wolpert2020 fatigue improvement suggests autonomic normalization
- **Certainty:** 0.4
- **Prediction:** ME/CFS + MTS patients show blunted baroreflex sensitivity correlating with LCIV stenosis severity

### Tier 2

**H6: Venous congestion → interstitial fluid accumulation → compression of peripheral nerves**
- **Rationale:** Chronic venous stasis increases interstitial pressure → compresses small nerve fibers → neuropathic pain patterns common in ME/CFS
- **Evidence link:** Small fiber neuropathy prevalence in ME/CFS (ch4), venous edema in chronic venous disease, IJV stenosis-pain connection
- **Certainty:** 0.3
- **Prediction:** Skin biopsy small fiber density inversely correlated with venous stasis markers in ME/CFS

**H7: Iliac vein compression → altered lymphatic return → impaired immune surveillance**
- **Rationale:** Iliac lymphatics run parallel to iliac vein; venous stasis may compress lymphatics → reduced clearance of inflammatory mediators → neuroinflammation persistence
- **Evidence link:** Lymphatic dysfunction in ME/CFS (immune dysregulation ch3), neuroinflammation in ME/CFS (ch14a), Patel2024 glymphatic-neuroinflammation link
- **Certainty:** 0.25
- **Prediction:** ME/CFS + MTS patients show delayed lymphatic clearance on near-infrared lymphangiography

**H8: Venous stasis-induced metabolic reprogramming in skeletal muscle**
- **Rationale:** Reduced venous return → lower oxygen delivery → shift to glycolytic metabolism → lactate accumulation → post-exertional malaise amplification
- **Evidence link:** Metabolic inflexibility in ME/CFS (ch5), lactate elevation in venous stasis, PEM characteristic of ME/CFS
- **Certainty:** 0.25
- **Prediction:** Exercise challenge shows exaggerated lactate rise in ME/CFS + MTS vs ME/CFS alone

**H9: Intracranial pressure dysregulation from venous outflow impedance**
- **Rationale:** Iliac stenosis → elevated abdominal venous pressure → increased IVC pressure → impaired cerebral venous drainage → ICP fluctuations
- **Evidence link:** ICP abnormalities in ME/CFS (ch7), Marshall2022 cerebral venous outflow, headache prevalence in both conditions
- **Certainty:** 0.2
- **Prediction:** Transcranial Doppler shows elevated dural sinus pressure in upright position for ME/CFS + MTS

**H10: Mesenteric congestion → gut barrier dysfunction → endotoxin translocation**
- **Rationale:** Chronic venous hypertension in portal/mesenteric system → gut wall edema → increased permeability → endotoxin leakage → immune activation
- **Evidence link:** Gut dysbiosis in ME/CFS (ch3), endotoxin markers in ME/CFS, venous congestion effects on gut
- **Certainty:** 0.2
- **Prediction:** Serum LPS-binding protein elevated in ME/CFS + MTS; correlates with abdominal symptoms

### Tier 3

**H11: Intermittent venous collapse → microemboli formation → endothelial microthrombi**
- **Rationale:** Pulsatile compression could cause intermittent endothelial injury → microthrombi → capillary obstruction → ischemia-reperfusion injury
- **Evidence link:** Microclot hypothesis in ME/CFS, endothelial activation markers, Wolpert2020 high 3-year patency suggests ongoing risk
- **Certainty:** 0.1
- **Prediction:** Circulating endothelial microvesicles elevated in ME/CFS + MTS; reduced by anticoagulation in subcohort

**H12: Venous stasis → altered nitric oxide signaling → mitochondrial complex IV dysfunction**
- **Rationale:** Reduced NO bioavailability from endothelial dysfunction → impaired mitochondrial respiration → energy deficit; NO is complex IV regulator
- **Evidence link:** Mitochondrial dysfunction in ME/CFS (ch5), NO deficiency in ME/CFS, endothelial NO synthase uncoupling
- **Certainty:** 0.08
- **Prediction:** Peripheral muscle biopsy shows complex IV deficiency preferentially in ME/CFS + MTS

**H13: Pelvic venous congestion → autonomic ganglion irritation → dysautonomia amplification**
- **Rationale:** Dilated pelvic veins may compress autonomic ganglia (hypogastric, sacral) → dysregulated autonomic outflow
- **Evidence link:** Pelvic congestion syndrome overlaps with MTS, autonomic dysfunction patterns
- **Certainty:** 0.05
- **Prediction:** EMG of sacral nerve roots shows abnormal patterns in ME/CFS + MTS with pelvic symptoms

**H14: Venous compression syndrome network as connective tissue disorder manifestation**
- **Rationale:** MTS, Nutcracker, IJV stenosis share connective tissue laxity; MTS may be marker of systemic vascular wall weakness
- **Evidence link:** Connective tissue abnormalities in ME/CFS (joint hypermobility), EDS comorbidity, vascular wall integrity markers
- **Certainty:** 0.05
- **Prediction:** ME/CFS + MTS patients show higher prevalence of other compression syndromes vs ME/CFS alone

**H15: Chronic venous pain → central sensitization → pain amplification**
- **Rationale:** Persistent nociceptive input from venous distension → central sensitization → widespread hyperalgesia
- **Evidence link:** Central sensitization in ME/CFS (ch4), chronic pain sensitization, stenting pain relief suggests peripheral driver
- **Certainty:** 0.05
- **Prediction:** Quantitative sensory testing shows lower pain thresholds in ME/CFS + MTS correlating with pain duration

## 2. Research Directions

### Tier 1

**RD1: Cross-sectional screening study**
- **Design:** n=200 ME/CFS patients, CTV/MRV of iliac veins, stratified by orthostatic severity
- **Primary outcome:** Prevalence of >50% LCIV compression in ME/CFS vs age/sex-matched controls
- **Secondary outcomes:** Correlation with standing HR, SBP, stroke volume, orthostatic symptom scores
- **Power calculation:** Assuming 22% general population MTS prevalence (Neglén2008), detect 2× increase to 44% in ME/CFS (α=0.05, β=0.2)
- **Certainty of feasibility:** 0.8 (CTVR protocols established)
- **Prediction:** MTS prevalence 35-55% in ME/CFS, higher in severe orthostatic intolerance subgroup

**RD2: IJV-iliac flow coupling study**
- **Design:** n=50 ME/CFS patients, Doppler ultrasound of IJV (supine/upright) and iliac veins, simultaneous impedance cardiography
- **Primary outcome:** Correlation between upright IJV flow reduction and LCIV compression severity
- **Secondary outcomes:** Cerebral blood flow (TCD) during tilt, symptom provocation correlation
- **Certainty of feasibility:** 0.7 (ultrasound widely available)
- **Prediction:** Multiplicative interaction: MTS amplifies IJV flow reduction, synergistic CBF reduction

**RD3: Glymphatic-MTS imaging study**
- **Design:** n=60 ME/CFS patients, DTI-ALPS for glymphatic function, iliac CTVR, fatigue severity correlation
- **Primary outcome:** DTI-ALPS index difference between MTS+ and MTS- ME/CFS patients
- **Secondary outcomes:** CSF dynamics on phase-contrast MRI, glymphatic clearance of intrathecal gadobutrol (subcohort)
- **Certainty of feasibility:** 0.6 (DTI-ALPS validated, invasive clearance sub-study challenging)
- **Prediction:** Glymphatic impairment severity tracks with venous compression severity

**RD4: Interventional pilot: LCIV stenting in ME/CFS**
- **Design:** n=20 ME/CFS patients with >50% LCIV stenosis, randomized 1:1 stent vs sham, crossover at 6 months
- **Primary outcomes:** Change in orthostatic symptom score (COM-PA), 6MWT distance
- **Secondary outcomes:** SF-36 vitality, HRV, ICBV (impedance cardiography), tilt table tolerance
- **Safety monitoring:** Stent patency (Duplex at 1/3/6 months), thrombosis prophylaxis per venous stenting guidelines
- **Certainty of feasibility:** 0.5 (ethical concerns, recruitment, cross-over design)
- **Prediction:** Stenting group shows >20% improvement in orthostatic tolerance, maintained at 6 months

**RD5: Blood volume redistribution dynamics study**
- **Design:** n=80 ME/CFS patients, segmental bioimpedance during head-up tilt, stratified by MTS status
- **Primary outcome:** Time-course of leg blood volume accumulation in MTS+ vs MTS-
- **Secondary outcomes:** Thoracic impedance change (central blood volume), venous compliance metrics
- **Certainty of feasibility:** 0.8 (bioimpedance equipment available)
- **Prediction:** MTS+ shows faster leg volume accumulation (steeper slope) and larger total redistribution

### Tier 2

**RD6: Autonomic remodeling longitudinal study**
- **Design:** n=40 ME/CFS patients, HRV, baroreflex sensitivity, catecholamines at baseline, 6 months, 12 months; MTS imaging at baseline
- **Primary outcome:** Autonomic parameter trajectories by MTS status
- **Secondary outcomes:** Symptom progression, exercise capacity changes
- **Certainty of feasibility:** 0.6 (longitudinal retention challenging)
- **Prediction:** MTS+ shows progressive sympathetic dominance; stenting (natural intercurrent intervention) halts progression

**RD7: Endothelial glycocalyx biomarker study**
- **Design:** n=100 ME/CFS patients, plasma syndecan-1, hyaluronan, endocan; iliac CTVR; standing tolerance test
- **Primary outcome:** Glycocalyx marker levels by MTS status
- **Secondary outcomes:** Correlation with capillary leak (albumin extravasation test), orthostatic HR
- **Certainty of feasibility:** 0.5 (assays available but not standardized)
- **Prediction:** Glycocalyx markers elevated in MTS+, correlate with symptom severity

**RD8: Lymphatic clearance dynamic imaging**
- **Design:** n=30 ME/CFS patients, near-infrared lymphangiography with indocyanine green, MTS imaging
- **Primary outcome:** Lymphatic clearance half-time by MTS status
- **Secondary outcomes:** IL-6, TNF-α clearance kinetics post-exercise
- **Certainty of feasibility:** 0.4 (specialized equipment, technical complexity)
- **Prediction:** Delayed lymphatic clearance in MTS+, correlates with post-exertional symptom flare

**RD9: Skeletal muscle metabolic study with 31P-MRS**
- **Design:** n=40 ME/CFS patients, 31P-MRS of calf muscle during plantar flexion, MTS imaging
- **Primary outcome:** Phosphocreatine recovery time by MTS status
- **Secondary outcomes:** Lactate kinetics, fatigue severity correlation
- **Certainty of feasibility:** 0.5 (MRS access limited)
- **Prediction:** Slower PCr recovery in MTS+ despite same workload, suggests ischemic limitation

**RD10: Intracranial pressure monitoring sub-study**
- **Design:** n=10 ME/CFS patients, lumbar puncture with pressure transducer during tilt, MTS imaging
- **Primary outcome:** ICP change upright vs supine by MTS status
- **Secondary outcomes:** Headache provocation, optic nerve sheath diameter ultrasound
- **Certainty of feasibility:** 0.3 (invasive, ethical concerns)
- **Prediction:** Abnormal ICP elevation upright in MTS+ patients

### Tier 3

**RD11: Endothelial microvesicle characterization**
- **Design:** n=50 ME/CFS patients, flow cytometry for endothelial-derived microvesicles (CD31+, CD42b-), MTS imaging
- **Primary outcome:** Microvesicle concentration by MTS status

**RD15: Central sensitization pain mapping**
- **Design:** n=40 ME/CFS patients, quantitative sensory testing (QST), conditioned pain modulation, MTS imaging

- **Secondary outcomes:** Functional MRI pain network activation
- **Certainty of feasibility:** 0.4 (QST established, fMRI access limited)
- **Prediction:** Enhanced central sensitization in MTS+ correlating with venous pain duration

## 3. Drug/Medication Ideas

### Tier 1

**DM1: Midodrine (α1-agonist) for MTS+ ME/CFS**
- **Rationale:** Increases venous tone → reduces venous pooling → improves preload; already used in POTS
- **Evidence link:** POTS treatment literature (midodrine efficacy), Wolpert2020 fatigue improvement suggests preload-targeting works
- **Dosing strategy:** Start 2.5mg TID, titrate to 10mg TID; monitor BP supine hypertension
- **Certainty:** 0.6
- **Prediction:** ME/CFS + MTS patients show larger orthostatic HR reduction on midodrine vs MTS- (differential response)

**DM2: Pyridostigmine (AChE inhibitor)**
- **Rationale:** Enhances ganglionic transmission → improves sympathetic outflow → increases venous tone; enhances baroreflex
- **Evidence link:** POTS pyridostigmine trials, Anderson2021 autonomic dysfunction in MTS
- **Dosing strategy:** 30mg TID, titrate to 60mg TID; monitor cholinergic side effects
- **Certainty:** 0.5
- **Prediction:** Improves HRV and orthostatic tolerance preferentially in MTS+ subgroup

**DM3: Fludrocortisone (mineralocorticoid)**
- **Rationale:** Expands plasma volume → increases preload; mitigates venous pooling consequences
- **Evidence link:** ME/CFS hypovolemia (ch10), POTS fludrocortisone use, O'Sullivan2018 volume restoration benefit
- **Dosing strategy:** 0.05-0.2mg daily; monitor BP, K+, edema
- **Certainty:** 0.5
- **Prediction:** Larger stroke volume increase in MTS+ vs MTS- on fludrocortisone

**DM4: Ivabradine (If current blocker)**
- **Rationale:** Reduces HR without affecting BP/contractility → improves diastolic filling → reduces orthostatic tachycardia
- **Evidence link:** POTS ivabradine efficacy, inappropriate sinus tachycardia, Wolpert2020 symptom improvement
- **Dosing strategy:** 2.5-7.5mg BID; monitor bradycardia
- **Certainty:** 0.5
- **Prediction:** Improves standing HR control in MTS+ without compromising venous return (unlike β-blockers)

**DM5: Low-dose norepinephrine reuptake inhibition (bupropion SR 150mg)**
- **Rationale:** Mild α-agonist effect increases venous tone; improves central fatigue; Anderson2021 elevated NE suggests deficiency
- **Evidence link:** ME/CFS fatigue (bupropion benefit in fatigue syndromes), autonomic dysfunction
- **Dosing strategy:** 150mg SR daily; monitor anxiety, insomnia
- **Certainty:** 0.4
- **Prediction:** Improves both central fatigue and orthostatic tolerance in MTS+

### Tier 2

**DM6: Pentoxifylline (rheological agent)**
- **Rationale:** Improves red blood cell deformability → reduces blood viscosity → improves microcirculation despite venous stasis
- **Evidence link:** Venous stasis rheology, microcirculatory impairment in ME/CFS
- **Dosing strategy:** 400mg TID; monitor GI tolerance
- **Certainty:** 0.3
- **Prediction:** Improves muscle oxygen extraction in MTS+ despite venous congestion

**DM7: Spironolactone (aldosterone antagonist)**
- **Rationale:** Blocks aldosterone-induced fibrosis; may prevent venous wall remodeling; mild diuretic reduces edema
- **Evidence link:** Venous wall remodeling in chronic stasis, endothelial fibrosis pathways
- **Dosing strategy:** 25-50mg daily; monitor K+, gynecomastia
- **Certainty:** 0.3
- **Prediction:** Reduces venous wall stiffness progression in MTS+

**DM8: Statins (atorvastatin 10mg)**
- **Rationale:** Improves endothelial function; reduces endothelial glycocalyx shedding; anti-inflammatory
- **Evidence link:** Endothelial dysfunction in ME/CFS, glycocalyx preservation, stent patency improvement
- **Dosing strategy:** 10mg daily; monitor LFTs, CK
- **Certainty:** 0.25
- **Prediction:** Reduces plasma syndecan-1 in MTS+ ME/CFS

**DM9: Low-dose ACE inhibitors (lisinopril 2.5mg)**
- **Rationale:** Reduces afterload; improves endothelial NO production; antifibrotic
- **Evidence link:** Endothelial NO deficiency, venous wall remodeling, ME/CFS autonomic dysfunction
- **Dosing strategy:** 2.5-5mg daily; monitor BP, creatinine
- **Certainty:** 0.25
- **Prediction:** Improves endothelial function markers in MTS+ without exacerbating hypotension

**DM10: Sildenafil (PDE5 inhibitor, low dose)**
- **Rationale:** Enhances NO signaling → improves endothelial function; may improve venous tone via cGMP
- **Evidence link:** NO deficiency in ME/CFS, endothelial dysfunction, microcirculation

- **Certainty:** 0.2
- **Prediction:** Improves tissue oxygenation in MTS+ without compromising systemic BP

### Tier 3

**DM11: Direct NO donors (isosorbide mononitrate)**
- **Rationale:** Replaces NO deficiency; improves endothelial function; venodilation vs arteriodilation dose-dependent
- **Evidence link:** NO deficiency, endothelial dysfunction
- **Dosing strategy:** Very low dose 10mg daily (venoselective range); monitor headache, hypotension

**DM12: Venotropic phlebotonics (micronized purified flavonoid fraction - MPFF)**

**DM13: Low-dose anticoagulation (apixaban 2.5mg BID)**
- **Rationale:** Prevents microthrombi formation from endothelial injury; reduces endothelial activation

- **Certainty:** 0.08
- **Prediction:** Reduces endothelial microvesicles, improves microcirculatory flow

**DM14: Mitochondrial cofactor stack (CoQ10 + L-carnitine + riboflavin)**

- **Certainty:** 0.05
- **Prediction:** Improves exercise capacity preferentially in MTS+ if NO-complex IV mechanism valid

**DM15: Immunomodulatory agents (low-dose naltrexone 4.5mg)**
- **Rationale:** Reduces neuroinflammation; improves glial function; may break glymphatic-neuroinflammation loop
- **Evidence link:** Neuroinflammation in ME/CFS, glymphatic dysfunction, LDN off-label use
- **Dosing strategy:** 4.5mg at bedtime; monitor vivid dreams
- **Certainty:** 0.05
- **Prediction:** Reduces inflammatory markers, improves fatigue in MTS+ ME/CFS

## 5. Non-Pharmacological Interventions

### Tier 1

**NP1: Custom gradient compression stockings (20-30 mmHg)**
- **Rationale:** External compression → reduces venous pooling → increases preload
- **Evidence link:** POTS standard of care, O'Sullivan2018 volume restoration, venous stasis management
- **Protocol:** Thigh-high stockings, donned supine before arising, wear during daytime hours
- **Certainty:** 0.8
- **Prediction:** ME/CFS + MTS shows larger HR reduction on standing with stockings vs MTS-

**NP2: Abdominal compression binder**
- **Rationale:** Increases abdominal pressure → reduces iliac vein distension → improves venous return
- **Evidence link:** Abdominal compartment physiology, orthostatic management
- **Protocol:** 20-30 mmHg binder, worn during upright activity
- **Certainty:** 0.6
- **Prediction:** Improves standing stroke volume preferentially in MTS+

**NP3: Recumbent positioning protocol**
- **Rationale:** Reduces gravitational venous pooling → allows preload recovery
- **Evidence link:** POTS recumbent positioning, O'Sullivan2018 symptom relief
- **Protocol:** 20° head-up tilt during day (working position), supine rest 20min after activity
- **Certainty:** 0.7
- **Prediction:** Larger HR/BP stabilization during recumbence in MTS+

**NP4: Counter-maneuver training (leg crossing, muscle tensing)**
- **Rationale:** Activates muscle pump → forcibly returns venous blood → acute preload boost
- **Evidence link:** POTS counter-maneuvers, venous return augmentation
- **Protocol:** Leg crossing + gluteal squeezing 10sec every 2-3min during standing
- **Certainty:** 0.6
- **Prediction:** Acute HR reduction larger in MTS+ vs MTS- during counter-maneuvers

**NP5: Paced breathing (6 breaths/min)**
- **Rationale:** Enhances venous return via respiratory pump; improves HRV
- **Evidence link:** POTS breathing protocols, Anderson2021 HRV impairment
- **Protocol:** 6 breaths/min for 5min before standing, during prolonged standing
- **Certainty:** 0.5
- **Prediction:** Improves HRV, reduces orthostatic HR increase in MTS+

## 7. Mathematical Model Extensions

### Tier 1

**M1: Add venous compliance variable C_venous(t) to preload model**
- **Current model:** Preload P_pre = f(blood_volume, venous_return)
- **Extension:** P_pre(t) = f(V_blood(t), R_venous(t), C_venous(t), Q_iliac(t))
  - Q_iliac(t) = flow through iliac vein = function of stenosis severity S(t) and pressure gradient ΔP(t)
  - For MTS: S_MTS = 1 - (cross-sectional_area_stenosed / cross-sectional_area_normal)
- **ODE equation:** dV_venous_legs/dt = Q_arterial_in - Q_iliac_out - L_drainage
  - Q_iliac_out = (ΔP(t) / R_venous) × (1 - S_MTS) × C_venous_factor
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Model predicts critical S_MTS threshold (~0.5) where upright Q_iliac drops >30%; matches Hartung2019 IJV flow data

**M2: Add glymphatic-venous coupling to CSF dynamics model**
- **Current model:** CSF dynamics driven by production-absorption-pressure
- **Extension:** Glymphatic flow Q_glymph = f(arterial_pulsatility, venous_pressure_gradient)
  - ΔP_venous(t) = P_abdominal_venous - P_jugular_venous
  - For MTS: P_abdominal_venous elevated due to iliac stenosis
- **ODE equation:** dV_ISF_interstitial/dt = Q_prod_ISF - Q_glymph_clearance - Q_lymphatic
  - Q_glymph_clearance = k_glymph × ΔP_venous × (1 - α × S_MTS) (α = coupling coefficient)
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Model predicts glymphatic clearance reduction proportional to S_MTS × ΔP_venous elevation

**M3: Add autonomic remodeling state variable A(t)**
- **Current model:** Autonomic output as static parameters
- **Extension:** dA/dt = k_remodel × (P_venous_iliac - P_setpoint) - k_reset × A
  - A > 0 = sympathetic dominance, A < 0 = parasympathetic dominance
  - Chronic venous hypertension (P_venous_iliac elevated) drives sympathetic remodeling
- **Feedback:** A(t) affects R_venous(t) via sympathetic venoconstriction
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Model predicts progressive A drift over months if P_venous_iliac chronically elevated; reverses if stented

**M4: Extend baroreflex model with venous return sensing**
- **Current model:** Baroreflex responds to arterial pressure (carotid sinus)
- **Extension:** Add cardiopulmonary baroreceptor sensing of central blood volume V_central
  - HR = f(P_carotid, V_central, A_symp_state)
  - For MTS: V_central reduced disproportionately on standing
- **ODE equation:** dHR/dt = k_baroreflex × (P_setpoint - P_carotid) + k_cardiopulmonary × (V_central_setpoint - V_central)
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Model predicts exaggerated HR response in MTS+ due to blunted cardiopulmonary signal from reduced V_central

**M5: Add endothelial glycocalyx state G(t)**
- **Current model:** Vascular resistance static
- **Extension:** dG/dt = k_damage × P_venous_stress - k_repair × G
  - G ∈ [0,1], G=1 = intact glycocalyx, G=0 = fully shed
  - P_venous_stress = chronic venous pressure - normal pressure threshold
  - G affects capillary permeability L_cap and plasma volume V_plasma
- **Feedback:** Reduced V_plasma worsens orthostatic tolerance
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Model predicts progressive G reduction over years; halts/reverses with venous decompression

### Tier 2

**M6: Add interstitial fluid pressure P_interstitial(t) to microcirculation model**
- **Extension:** dP_interstitial/dt = (Q_filtration - Q_lymph_drainage) / K_compliance
  - Q_filtration = L_cap × (P_capillary - P_interstitial - σ × Π_osmotic)
  - For MTS: elevated P_venous increases P_capillary → more filtration

## 9. Diagnostic/Biomarker Ideas

### Tier 1

**D1: Standing Doppler ultrasound protocol for MTS screening**
- **Protocol:** Iliac vein Doppler in supine, then 70° head-up tilt for 5min
- **Metrics:**
  - Peak systolic velocity (PSV) change (upright/supine ratio)
  - Flow volume change (mL/min)
  - Vein diameter change (compression index)
- **Diagnostic criterion:** PSV increase >2.5× upright suggests >50% stenosis
- **Evidence link:** Hartung2019 IJV flow upright protocol, venous Doppler validation
- **Certainty:** 0.7
- **Prediction:** PSV ratio >2.5 in 35-55% of ME/CFS patients, correlates with orthostatic severity

**D2: Orthostatic impedance cardiography redistribution index**
- **Protocol:** Segmental bioimpedance during 10min head-up tilt
- **Metrics:**
  - Leg blood volume accumulation rate (dV_leg/dt)
  - Thoracic impedance change (dZ_thorax/dt)
  - Redistribution ratio (ΔV_leg / ΔV_thorax)
- **Diagnostic criterion:** Redistribution ratio >3.0 suggests MTS (>50% iliac stenosis)
- **Evidence link:** RD5 study design, bioimpedance validation in POTS
- **Certainty:** 0.65
- **Prediction:** Redistribution ratio >3.0 in 40-60% of ME/CFS+POTS, predicts MTS on imaging

**D3: Plasma syndecan-1 as glycocalyx shedding marker**
- **Protocol:** ELISA for syndecan-1 in plasma, standing vs supine samples
