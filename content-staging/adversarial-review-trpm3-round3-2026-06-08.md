=== SOPHIST-AUDITOR (Logic Attacker) — 4 findings ===

T1 (IL-11) — 2 findings:

1. False dichotomy: "Simplest explanation: generic inflammatory response correlating with age difference" reduces the explanatory space to a binary (age artifact vs. ME/CFS signal). Multiple alternatives unaddressed: storage degradation (>10 yr at -80°C), batch effects, selection bias, unmeasured confounders. The "simplest" framing excludes them without justification.

2. Non sequitur: The colon after "If replicated, the finding would be significant" implies the subsequent clause supports it, but "IL-11 is a master regulator of inflammaging (mouse)" does not entail that replicated human IL-11 elevation is significant *for ME/CFS specifically* — IL-11 could be elevated generically across chronic conditions without ME/CFS-specific pathophysiological meaning.

T2 (MMP-9 Ratio) — 2 findings:

1. Affirming the consequent (structurally enabled): "If hyperactive platelets → higher serum/plasma MMP-9 ratio" is If-P-then-Q. The text frames the ratio as a "functional platelet activation readout," structurally inviting observe-Q-therefore-P inference. The stated caveats sit in Limitations as post-hoc hedges rather than logical guards built into the claim.

2. Internal contradiction: The ratio is simultaneously proposed as a "functional platelet activation readout" and described as "confounded by platelet count, medications, exercise/meals" — factors unrelated to platelet activation state. A measure confounded by non-activation factors cannot serve as a specific activation readout unless those factors are controlled, which the excerpt does not establish.

=== DEVIL'S ADVOCATE-AUDITOR (Counter-Argument Builder) — 6 findings ===

T1 (IL-11) — 3 findings:

1. Undefended claims: (a) "Inflammaging model from mouse data only" — cross-species extrapolation of inflammaging to ME/CFS is undefended; mouse senescence models may not recapitulate human ME/CFS inflammatory profiles. (b) The age-mismatch null is called "co-equal" but the actual age delta is not quantified relative to the effect size, so the claim that age alone could account for the 2× IL-11 elevation is undefended.

3. Alternative explanations missing: (a) IL-11 could be elevated from a confounder (BMI, subclinical liver disease, undiagnosed comorbidity) rather than from ME/CFS-specific inflammaging; (b) "generic inflammatory response" is underdeveloped — no specific competing pathway (e.g., IL-6/CRP axis, NLRP3 inflammasome) is named to give the alternative explanatory force. Asymmetric scrutiny: Falsifiable predictions (a)–(d) are listed but none are assessed for whether they have been attempted or failed; the falsification framework is presented as armor without verifying it has been tested.

T2 (MMP-9 Ratio) — 3 findings:

1. Undefended claims: (a) "Precedent: chronic urticaria" — chronic urticaria involves mast-cell-driven pathology fundamentally different from ME/CFS; no argument is given for why a biomarker precedent from that disease transfers. (b) "Ratio concept" — why an MMP-9 ratio specifically indexes platelet activation rather than endothelial damage, neutrophil degranulation, or general MMP dysregulation is not defended.

2. Weakest link: The coagulation alternative ("differential clotting speed could produce spurious differences") is stronger than the text implies — if true, it does not merely "weaken" the biomarker; it fully explains the ratio as a pre-analytical artifact, reducing certainty below 0.35. The differential clotting hypothesis is testable (vary clotting time, add anticoagulants, measure MMP-9 kinetics) but the text does not indicate this has been attempted.

3. Alternative explanations missing: MMP-9 is also released by neutrophils, macrophages, and endothelial cells — a serum/plasma ratio difference could reflect differential release from any of these cell types, not specifically platelets. No argument excludes these alternatives. Asymmetric scrutiny: "Research-accessible, not primary care" addresses clinical actionability rather than validity, deflecting from the central question of whether the ratio measures anything real.
