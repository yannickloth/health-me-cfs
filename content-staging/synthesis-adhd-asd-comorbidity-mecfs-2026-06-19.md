# Evidence Synthesis: ADHD-ASD Comorbidity → ME/CFS
# Date: 2026-06-19

## Evidence Landscape

### Strong (certainty ≥ 0.60, large n, replicated)
- **Lai2019meta** (cert 0.90): Meta-analysis — ADHD prevalence 28% in ASD. Largest pooled estimate available.
- **Ghirardi2018familial** (cert 0.92): N=1.9M Swedish register — OR=22.33 for ASD→ADHD, MZ twin OR=17.77, DZ OR=4.33. Massive co-occurrence with genetic basis.
- **Demontis2023GWASadhd** (cert 0.93): Largest ADHD GWAS (N=38,691 cases) — r_g=0.36 with ASD.
- **Grove2019GWASasd** (cert 0.90): Largest ASD GWAS (N=18,381 cases) — r_g=0.35-0.41 with ADHD.
- **PGC2013cross** (cert 0.88): Cross-disorder GWAS — ADHD and ASD cluster together in neurodevelopmental factor.

### Moderate (certainty 0.40–0.59)
- **Ghirardi2019dimensions** (cert 0.85): Twin study — dimension-specific genetic overlap ADHD-ASD in adults (r_g=0.56 HI-RRB).
- **Polderman2014cooccurrence** (cert 0.87): N=17,770 twins — r_g=0.54-0.60 ADHD-ASD traits in adults.
- **Taylor2019geneticTraits** (cert 0.86): N=13,923 twin pairs — dimensional model validated, genetic factors for categorical diagnoses influence continuous traits.
- **Hindley2022landscape** (cert 0.87): MiXeR — ADHD and ASD share extensive polygenic variant sets.
- **Tesfaye2024anxiety** (cert 0.88): N=301,732 — anxiety shares polygenic overlap with ADHD (20 loci) and ASD (5 loci). Anxiety bridge mechanism.
- **Ronald2008overlap** (cert 0.83): N=6,768 twin pairs — r=0.50-0.60 ASD-ADHD behaviors, genetic overlap confirmed.

### Weak (certainty < 0.40)
- **Antshel2016update** (cert 0.75 — narrative review): Clinical overview, not systematic.
- **Martin2014biological** (cert 0.82): CNV evidence, moderate sample.
- **Pinto2016genetic** (cert 0.78): Smaller twin study, cross-sectional.

### Missing
- Zero papers directly link ADHD-ASD comorbidity to ME/CFS or chronic fatigue outcomes
- Zero papers on shared immune/inflammatory profiles between ADHD-ASD comorbidity and ME/CFS
- No studies on mitochondrial dysfunction bridging ADHD-ASD comorbidity to fatigue
- Dopamine pathway studies remain condition-specific

### Null
- None (no studies tested and rejected the ADHD-ASD → fatigue link)

## Internal Contradictions
- None among included papers. Consistent convergence: shared genetic architecture between ADHD and ASD is confirmed by every study type (twin, GWAS, CNV, register).

## Evidence Summary
The evidence establishes beyond reasonable doubt that ADHD and ASD share substantial genetic architecture (r_g=0.32-0.60 across methods). This is not a clinical artifact of diagnostic overlap — it persists in dimensional trait measures, family designs, and molecular genetic analyses. The dimensional model (Taylor 2019, Polderman 2014) validates treating both as continuous population traits, not just categorical diagnoses.

For ME/CFS: no paper directly links ADHD-ASD comorbidity to chronic fatigue outcomes — this is a genuine gap. However, the paper's existing architecture (BH4 convergence, IL-6 mediation, metabolic reserve model, compound multiplicative risk) is structurally supported by this evidence. The genetic overlap literature provides the biological plausibility foundation that the paper's cross-disease argument already assumes.

Strongest mechanistic claim supportable: ADHD and ASD share neurodevelopmental genetic liability (dopaminergic, synaptic, immune pathways) that independently predisposes to the metabolic vulnerabilities that ME/CFS exploits. This is already the paper's Architecture C argument. The new evidence strengthens it with formal genetic correlation data.

Must remain speculative: Whether ADHD-ASD comorbidity amplifies ME/CFS risk beyond the additive sum of individual condition risks. No data exist on this.

## Clinical Relevance Assessment
