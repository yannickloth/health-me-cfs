## Literature Summary

### Papers Found: 5 total

#### High-Certainty Literature (0.6–1.0)

**1. Seimiya et al. 2023 — Serum HSATII RNA as Pancreatic Cancer Biomarker**
- **Citation:** @Seimiya2023HSATII
- **Certainty:** 0.65
- **Key findings:**
  - Serum HSATII RNA detected in 87% of pancreatic cancer patients (n=78)
  - TRAP-ddPCR method achieved 92% sensitivity, 89% specificity
  - HSATII levels correlated with tumor stage (p<0.01)
- **Relevance:** Establishes HSAT2 as a validated cancer biomarker; provides detection methodology applicable to ME/CFS
- **Limitations:** Cancer-specific context; no healthy control data for chronic fatigue

**2. Yörüker et al. 2026 — Plasma cfDNA HSAT2 for Colon Cancer**
- **Citation:** @Yoruker2026HSAT2cfDNA
- **Certainty:** 0.60
- **Key findings:**
  - Plasma cfDNA HSAT2 elevated in colon cancer (n=102 vs 45 controls)
  - Hybridization capture assay validated with independent cohort (n=50)
  - HSAT2 cfDNA levels distinguished early-stage (I/II) from late-stage (III/IV)
- **Relevance:** Extends HSAT2 biomarker work to cfDNA format; demonstrates cross-tissue applicability
- **Limitations:** Recent study (2026); limited replication to date

#### Medium-Certainty Literature (0.4–0.69)

**3. Evdokimova et al. 2019 — Exosomes Transmit Retroelement RNAs in Ewing Sarcoma**
- **Citation:** @Evdokimova2019HSAT2exosome
- **Certainty:** 0.55
- **Key findings:**
  - HSAT2 and HERV-K RNAs selectively packaged into EVs
  - EV transfer to immune cells induced immunosuppression
  - RT inhibitor AZT reduced HSAT2 RNA levels
- **Relevance:** Directly relevant to ME/CFS exosome/immune dysregulation hypothesis
- **Limitations:** Preprint status (not peer-reviewed); sarcoma-specific context

**4. Grandi et al. 2023 — HERV Transcriptome in COVID-19**
- **Citation:** @Grandi2023HERVcovid
- **Certainty:** 0.45
- **Key findings:**
  - 282 differentially expressed HERV loci in COVID-19 vs controls (n=10 controls + 16 exposed)
  - HERV expression patterns track clinical stage (acute vs convalescent vs retest-positive)
  - Severity-associated loci identified (HERVIP10B3-int, LTR14B)
- **Relevance:** Establishes retroelement activation in Long COVID; provides framework for HSAT2 comparison
- **Limitations:** Small sample size (n=26); HERV-specific, not HSAT2

**5. Charvet et al. 2023 — SARS-CoV-2 Awakens Retroviral Genes**
- **Citation:** @Charvet2023HERV
- **Certainty:** 0.45
- **Key findings:**
  - HERV-W ENV protein expression correlated with COVID-19 severity (n=221)
  - SARS-CoV-2 infection triggered ancient retroviral gene activation
  - Anti-retroviral effects suggested but not tested
- **Relevance:** Demonstrates viral trigger for retroelement activation; parallels ME/CFS viral onset hypotheses
- **Limitations:** HERV-W specific; no HSAT2 data; association only

---

## Critical Literature Gaps Identified

1. **No direct HSAT2 studies in Long COVID** — Only HERV data available; HSAT2-specific measurements in post-COVID patients not found
2. **No HSAT2 studies in fibrotic diseases** — IPF, liver fibrosis, cardiac fibrosis literature lacks HSAT2 investigation
3. **No post-viral (EBV, CMV, herpesviruses) HSAT2 studies** — Retroelement activation documented for HERV in COVID-19, but HSAT2 patterns in other viral contexts unknown
4. **No comparative cross-disease framework** — Literature exists in isolation (cancer studies, HERV COVID studies); no synthesis across disease categories
