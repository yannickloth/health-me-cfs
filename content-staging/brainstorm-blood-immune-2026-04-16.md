## 1. Novel Hypotheses

### 1.1 Complement-Driven Perivascular Mast Cell Amplification Loop
- **Category**: hypothesis
- **Certainty**: 0.45
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Complement activation (C3a/C5a anaphylatoxins) triggers mast cell degranulation at perivascular sites. Mast cell mediators (histamine, tryptase, heparin) increase vascular permeability, allowing further complement protein leakage into perivascular tissue where alternative pathway amplification occurs on host surfaces. This creates a spatially confined positive feedback loop: complement activation → mast cell degranulation → vascular leakage → more complement in tissue → more mast cell activation. The Maya2026 complement pQTL subgroup (high C3/low Bb) may represent individuals genetically predisposed to this loop due to excess C3 substrate availability. The Bragée2026 CSF finding of complement enrichment in severe cases could reflect this loop operating at the blood-brain barrier, where mast cells are known to reside perivascularly.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Maya2026complement-genetics (complement pQTL subgroup), @Bragée2026csf-proteome (CSF complement enrichment in severe), existing paper mast cell model (@eq:mast-cell-dynamics, @sec:mast-cell-model), complement C3a/C5a mast cell activation mechanism (already cited in paper, ch07, ch14d)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) Skin blister fluid or interstitial fluid from ME/CFS patients will show higher C3a:C3 ratio than plasma, indicating local complement amplification; (b) complement-pQTL-positive subgroup will have higher serum tryptase and prostaglandin D2 than complement-pQTL-negative patients; (c) C5a receptor antagonist (avacopan) will reduce mast cell mediators in a crossover trial.

### 1.2 Extracellular Vesicles as Blood-Brain Barrier Ferrying Vectors for Immune Remodeling Signals
- **Category**: hypothesis
- **Certainty**: 0.35
- **Mechanistic rationale**: EVs from activated monocytes carry altered cargo (proteins, miRNA) that can cross the BBB via transcytosis, adsorptive-mediated transport, or paracellular leakage through compromised tight junctions. The Seifert2026 finding of altered EV miRNA cargo (reduced let-7b-5p) in post-COVID ME/CFS is significant because let-7b-5p regulates NF-κB signaling and TLR4 expression in microglia. Reduced let-7b-5p delivery to the CNS via EVs would disinhibit microglial TLR4, producing neuroinflammation. This provides a specific molecular mechanism for how peripheral immune remodeling (Shahbaz2026 monocyte activation) communicates with the CNS, bypassing the BBB model's cytokine-transport pathway. The Yu2026 NII white matter neuroinflammation findings could reflect the downstream consequence of this EV-mediated signaling.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Seifert2026extracellular-vesicles (EV let-7b-5p reduction), @Shahbaz2026single-cell-immune (monocyte activation producing EVs), @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (NII neuroinflammation), existing BBB model (@eq:bbb-permeability, @eq:bbb-transport)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) ME/CFS patient EVs applied to healthy human microglia in vitro will upregulate TLR4 and pro-inflammatory cytokines compared to control EVs; (b) let-7b-5p mimic transfection into ME/CFS EVs will normalize microglial response; (c) EV cargo profiling will differ between ME/CFS patients with and without NII white matter abnormalities.

### 1.3 Galectin-9 as the Molecular Bridge Between Immune Remodeling and Endothelial Dysfunction
- **Category**: hypothesis
- **Certainty**: 0.30
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Galectin-9 is produced not only by activated monocytes (Shahbaz2026) but also by endothelial cells under inflammatory stress. When endothelial cells produce Galectin-9, it has two effects: (1) it engages TIM-3 on passing immune cells, promoting T cell exhaustion and apoptosis of gamma-delta/MAIT cells in the vascular compartment; (2) it cross-links glycoproteins on the endothelial surface, directly impairing endothelial barrier function. This dual role makes Galectin-9 a candidate molecular bridge between the two convergent themes: it drives immune remodeling (via TIM-3 on immune cells) and simultaneously disrupts vascular integrity (via endothelial cross-linking). The Heng2025 finding of elevated thrombus formation and vascular reactivity proteins could partially reflect Galectin-9-mediated endothelial damage.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Shahbaz2026single-cell-immune (Galectin-9-TIM-3 axis), @Heng2025mecfs (vascular reactivity proteins), @heng2025mecfs (thrombus formation proteins), existing paper on endothelial senescence (Nunes2026) and BBB dysfunction
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) Serum Galectin-9 levels will correlate with both immune remodeling markers (MAIT/gamma-delta depletion) and endothelial dysfunction markers (von Willebrand factor, thrombomodulin, soluble VCAM-1) in the same patients; (b) in vitro, recombinant Galectin-9 will increase endothelial monolayer permeability in a TIM-3-independent manner (blocked by lactose but not anti-TIM-3); (c) Galectin-9 levels will be elevated in CSF of severe ME/CFS patients.

### 1.4 The Brainstem Autonomic Loop Hypothesis: Neuroinflammation as Both Cause and Consequence of Vascular-Immune Failure
- **Category**: hypothesis
- **Certainty**: 0.40
- **Mechanistic rationale**: The brainstem (medulla, pons) contains the autonomic centers (nucleus tractus solitarius, dorsal motor nucleus of vagus, rostral ventrolateral medulla) that regulate heart rate, blood pressure, and cerebral blood flow. Neuroinflammation in the brainstem (detectable by NII metrics, Yu2026) would impair autonomic output → reduced vagal tone → impaired cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway → peripheral immune activation → cytokines cross BBB → further brainstem neuroinflammation. Simultaneously, reduced autonomic output → hypovolemia/hypoperfusion → cerebral ischemia → metabolic stress → further neuroinflammation. This creates a three-node vicious cycle: brainstem neuroinflammation ↔ autonomic failure ↔ peripheral immune-vascular dysfunction. The Faro2024 finding of increased cuneiform nucleus–medulla FC in ME/CFS may reflect compensatory activation of this impaired circuit.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (NII white matter including brainstem), @Faro2024connectivity (cuneiform–medulla FC), existing paper ANS/brainstem content (ch08, ch10), vagal coupling model (@eq:vagal-coupling), BBB model (@eq:bbb-permeability), @Heng2025mecfs (vascular proteins)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) NII metrics in brainstem white matter tracts will correlate more strongly with autonomic function tests (heart rate variability, tilt table response) than with cognitive tests; (b) patients with POTS (Bragée2026 CSF neutrophil degranulation) will show worse brainstem NII metrics than non-POTS patients; (c) improving vagal tone (via transcutaneous VNS) will reduce peripheral inflammatory markers only in patients with brainstem NII abnormalities.

## 2. Research Directions

### 2.1 Multi-Stressor Exercise Study With Immune-Vascular-Blood-Brain Barrier Triangulation
- **Category**: research direction
- **Certainty**: 0.70
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Germain2025 showed proteomic PEM dynamics but sampled only plasma. An ideal study would triangulate three compartments simultaneously: (1) plasma proteomics (immune remodeling, complement), (2) CSF proteomics (neuroinflammation, complement), and (3) MRI with NII model (white matter neuroinflammation) — all before and at multiple timepoints after exercise (baseline, 1h, 24h, 48h, 72h). This would map the temporal cascade: does peripheral immune activation precede, coincide with, or follow BBB breakdown and CNS neuroinflammation? The Bragée2026 CSF finding of complement/coagulation in severe cases combined with Germain2025's plasma dynamics suggests a specific temporal ordering that can only be resolved with multi-compartment longitudinal sampling.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Germain2025proteome (longitudinal plasma PEM dynamics), @Bragée2026csf-proteome (CSF complement/coagulation), @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (NII model), @Che2025 (multi-omics post-exercise)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: In the multi-compartment longitudinal design, one of three temporal patterns will emerge: (a) peripheral immune changes precede CSF changes → peripheral-to-central propagation; (b) CSF changes precede or coincide with peripheral → CNS-initiated; (c) simultaneous changes → shared upstream trigger. The pattern will differ between severity subgroups.

### 2.2 Galectin-9 In Vitro Rescue Experiments
- **Category**: research direction
- **Certainty**: 0.65
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Shahbaz2026 identified Galectin-9-TIM-3 as a potential driver of gamma-delta/MAIT cell depletion via scRNA-seq. The critical next step is functional validation: does blocking this pathway actually rescue cell survival? This requires (1) measuring serum Galectin-9 in ME/CFS vs controls, (2) co-culture experiments where ME/CFS monocytes are cultured with healthy gamma-delta/MAIT cells with and without Galectin-9 neutralization, and (3) TIM-3 blocking antibody experiments. This directly tests the mechanism and determines therapeutic relevance.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Shahbaz2026single-cell-immune (Galectin-9-TIM-3 axis), existing paper's Galectin-9 hypothesis (ch07, @hyp:galectin-9)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) Galectin-9 will be elevated in ME/CFS serum; (b) neutralizing Galectin-9 in ME/CFS PBMC cultures will increase MAIT and gamma-delta T cell survival at 72h; (c) TIM-3 blockade will partially rescue but not fully normalize survival (suggesting Galectin-9 has additional TIM-3-independent targets).

### 2.3 Complement pQTL Subgroup Stratification Study
- **Category**: research direction
- **Certainty**: 0.60
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Maya2026 identified a complement pQTL-defined inflammatory subgroup but with a small discovery cohort (n=50 ME/CFS). A targeted replication and extension study should: (1) genotype the 6 identified pQTLs in a larger ME/CFS cohort (n≥300), (2) measure complement proteins (C3, Bb, C3a, C5a, C4d) at baseline and post-exercise, (3) correlate with PEM severity, autonomic function, neuroimaging, and treatment response. This would determine whether the complement subgroup has distinct clinical features and treatment needs.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Maya2026complement-genetics (complement pQTL subgroup), @Germain2025proteome (PEM proteomics), @Bragée2026csf-proteome (CSF complement in severe)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: Complement pQTL-positive patients will show: (a) greater complement activation (C3a, C5a) at baseline and exaggerated complement response post-exercise; (b) worse PEM severity independent of overall illness severity; (c) distinct neuroimaging profile (more NII white matter abnormalities) compared to pQTL-negative patients matched for severity.

### 2.4 EV Cargo as Dynamic PEM Biomarker: Pre/Post Exercise Time Course

A longitudinal EV profiling study (same timepoints as Germain2025: baseline, 1h, 24h post-exercise) would test whether EV miRNA/protein cargo tracks PEM onset and severity, potentially providing a minimally invasive window into compartment-specific pathology.

## 3. Drug/Medication Ideas

### 3.1 C5a Receptor Antagonist (Avacopan) for Complement-pQTL Subgroup
- **Category**: drug
- **Certainty**: 0.40
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Avacopan is an oral, selective C5a receptor antagonist approved for ANCA-associated vasculitis. In ME/CFS, C5a drives mast cell activation (via C5aR1), neutrophil chemotaxis, and endothelial activation. The Maya2026 complement subgroup (high C3/low Bb) likely has elevated C5a generation. Blocking C5aR1 would interrupt the complement→mast cell→vascular permeability amplification loop (hypothesis 1.1) and reduce neutrophil degranulation products found in POTS+ CSF (Bragée2026). Unlike broad immunosuppressants, avacopan targets a specific pathogenic mediator with an established safety profile.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Maya2026complement-genetics (complement subgroup), @Bragée2026csf-proteome (CSF neutrophil degranulation in POTS+), @Che2025 (complement activation), existing paper complement section (ch07)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: In a crossover trial of complement-pQTL-positive ME/CFS patients, avacopan vs placebo will: (a) reduce serum C5a, tryptase, and IL-6 at 4 weeks; (b) improve orthostatic tolerance (tilt table standing time) by ≥20%; (c) reduce PEM severity scores without causing immunosuppression (normal infection rates).

### 3.2 Low-Dose Naltrexone + Dextromethorphan Combination for Microglial Reprogramming
- **Category**: drug
- **Certainty**: 0.35
- **Mechanistic rationale**: LDN modulates TLR4 on microglia (reducing pro-inflammatory signaling). Dextromethorphan is a sigma-1 receptor agonist that suppresses microglial NLRP3 inflammasome activation. Both drugs have existing safety data and are inexpensive. The combination targets microglial activation (Yu2026 NII neuroinflammation, Nakatomi2014 PET evidence) from two complementary angles: LDN reduces the TLR4 activation signal, while dextromethorphan blocks the downstream inflammasome execution. This combination could theoretically reprogram microglia from pro-inflammatory (μ₁) to anti-inflammatory (μ₂) state per the existing microglial model (@eq:microglia).
- **Evidence anchor**: @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (NII white matter neuroinflammation), existing paper microglial model (@eq:microglia, @sec:microglia-model), existing LDN discussion in paper (ch28, @sec:immune-intervention-models), Bezisterim discussion (ch18)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) LDN+dextromethorphan combination will reduce CSF soluble CD14 and chitotriosidase (microglial activation markers) at 8 weeks; (b) NII metrics will improve (increased NII-HR, decreased NII-FF) at 16 weeks; (c) the combination will be more effective than either drug alone in a three-arm crossover (LDN, DXM, LDN+DXM).

### 3.3 TRPM3 Agonist (Pregnenolone Sulfate or Novel Agonist) to Restore NK Cell Calcium Signaling
- **Category**: drug
- **Certainty**: 0.35
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Sasso2026 confirmed TRPM3 ion channel dysfunction in NK cells. TRPM3 mediates calcium influx critical for NK cell degranulation and cytotoxicity. Pregnenolone sulfate is an endogenous TRPM3 agonist, but its bioavailability and blood-brain barrier penetration complicate use. A more targeted approach would be a peripherally-restricted TRPM3 agonist or positive allosteric modulator that specifically enhances NK cell TRPM3 function without CNS side effects. Restoring NK cell cytotoxicity could improve viral surveillance (reducing herpesvirus reactivation) and immune remodeling normalization.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Sasso2026trpm3 (TRPM3 NK cell dysfunction), existing paper NK cell model (@eq:nk-dynamics), existing paper viral reactivation model (@eq:viral-dynamics)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) Pregnenolone sulfate or a TRPM3-positive modulator will restore calcium influx in ME/CFS NK cells to control levels in vitro; (b) restored NK function will reduce EBV viral load in peripheral blood at 8 weeks; (c) patients with the most severe TRPM3 impairment at baseline will show the greatest clinical response.

### 3.4 Anti-Galectin-9 Antibody or Small-Molecule Inhibitor for Immune Remodeling Reversal
- **Category**: drug
- **Certainty**: 0.20
- **Mechanistic rationale**: If Galectin-9 drives gamma-delta/MAIT cell depletion (Shahbaz2026), blocking it could reverse immune remodeling. Anti-Galectin-9 antibodies are in preclinical development for cancer immunotherapy (where Galectin-9 suppresses anti-tumor immunity). Repurposing for ME/CFS would aim to release gamma-delta and MAIT cells from TIM-3-mediated exhaustion, potentially restoring barrier immunity and reducing the tissue-resident cell deficit that contributes to gut barrier dysfunction and infection susceptibility. However, Galectin-9 has pleiotropic functions (immune regulation, apoptosis of pathogenic T cells) so systemic blockade carries autoimmunity risk.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Shahbaz2026single-cell-immune (Galectin-9-TIM-3 mechanism), existing paper Galectin-9 hypothesis (ch07, @hyp:galectin-9)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) Anti-Galectin-9 treatment in ME/CFS PBMC cultures will increase gamma-delta and MAIT cell counts and reduce TIM-3 expression at 7 days; (b) in vivo, short-term anti-Galectin-9 will increase circulating MAIT cells without triggering autoimmunity (measured by ANA, anti-thyroid antibodies); (c) clinical improvement will correlate with MAIT cell recovery, not just with Galectin-9 level reduction.

## 4. Supplement/Nutraceutical Ideas

### 4.1 Modified Citrus Pectin as Oral Galectin-9 Binder
- **Category**: supplement
- **Certainty**: 0.30
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Modified citrus pectin (MCP) is a galectin-binding polysaccharide with established ability to bind Galectin-3 (used in cancer adjunct therapy). Galectin-9 shares the carbohydrate recognition domain structure with Galectin-3, and MCP has demonstrated in vitro binding to Galectin-9 at higher concentrations. Oral MCP could partially sequester circulating Galectin-9 in the gut lumen, reducing systemic Galectin-9 bioavailability and releasing TIM-3-mediated suppression of gamma-delta/MAIT cells. MCP is well-tolerated, inexpensive, and has existing safety data. This would be a low-risk first test of the Galectin-9 hypothesis before committing to expensive monoclonal antibodies.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Shahbaz2026single-cell-immune (Galectin-9-TIM-3 axis), existing paper Galectin-9 hypothesis (ch07), MCP galectin-binding literature (Nangia-Makker et al. 2002)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) MCP 5g/day for 8 weeks will reduce serum Galectin-9 levels by ≥15% vs placebo; (b) MAIT cell and gamma-delta T cell counts will increase in MCP responders; (c) MCP will not reduce Galectin-3 below normal range (monitoring for potential adverse effects on immune surveillance).

### 4.2 Sodium Butyrate for BBB Integrity and Microglial Reprogramming
- **Category**: supplement
- **Certainty**: 0.45
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Sodium butyrate is a histone deacetylase (HDAC) inhibitor that promotes microglial polarization from pro-inflammatory (M1/μ₁) to anti-inflammatory (M2/μ₂) phenotype. Butyrate also strengthens tight junctions in endothelial cells, potentially improving BBB integrity. The paper's gut-brain-immune axis model (@eq:gut-immune) already identifies butyrate depletion as a driver of intestinal permeability; extending this to the BBB is mechanistically consistent. Oral sodium butyrate (or butyrate-producing prebiotics like resistant starch + inulin) could address two convergent themes simultaneously: reducing neuroinflammation (via microglial reprogramming) and improving vascular integrity (via BBB tight junctions). Butyrate is already produced endogenously by gut commensals and has extensive safety data.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (NII neuroinflammation), existing BBB model (@eq:bbb-permeability), existing microglial model (@eq:microglia), existing gut-brain-immune axis (@eq:gut-immune), @Hsu2025gut (reduced butyrate-producing bacteria in ME/CFS)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) Oral sodium butyrate 600mg/day for 12 weeks will improve NII metrics (increased NII-HR, increased NII-RF); (b) serum S100B (BBB integrity marker) will decrease; (c) CSF inflammatory markers will shift toward anti-inflammatory profile (increased IL-10, decreased TNF-α).

### 4.3 Quercetin as TRPM3 Sensitizer and Complement Modulator
- **Category**: supplement
- **Certainty**: 0.30
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Quercetin has dual relevance to the two convergent themes: (1) it sensitizes TRPM3 channels, potentially partially restoring calcium influx in NK cells with impaired TRPM3 function (Sasso2026); (2) it inhibits complement C3 convertase, potentially reducing complement overactivation in the Maya2026 subgroup. Quercetin also stabilizes mast cells (inhibiting degranulation) and has anti-inflammatory effects via NF-κB inhibition. This multi-target profile makes it a plausible low-risk adjunctive supplement for patients with both immune remodeling and complement/vascular involvement.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Sasso2026trpm3 (TRPM3 dysfunction), @Maya2026complement-genetics (complement subgroup), existing paper mast cell model (@eq:mast-cell-dynamics), quercetin TRPM3 sensitization literature (Straub et al. 2020), quercetin complement inhibition (Shahzad et al. 2017)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) Quercetin 500mg BID will improve NK cell calcium influx in response to TRPM3 agonists at 8 weeks; (b) serum C3a and C5a will decrease in complement-pQTL-positive patients; (c) tryptase levels will decrease (mast cell stabilization).

### 4.4 NAD+ Precursor (Nicotinamide Riboside) to Break Energy-Immune Vicious Cycle
- **Category**: supplement
- **Certainty**: 0.40
- **Mechanistic rationale**: The energy-immune coupling model (@eq:energy-immune-coupling, @eq:immune-energy-feedback) predicts a bistable vicious cycle: immune activation drains ATP → ATP deficit impairs immune function → persistent infection → sustained immune activation. NAD+ is a critical cofactor for both mitochondrial ATP production (Complex I) and immune cell function (sirtuin-mediated DNA repair, PARP activity). NAD+ levels decline in ME/CFS (Naviaux2016 metabolomics). Nicotinamide riboside (NR) elevates NAD+ more efficiently than nicotinamide, and clinical trials in other fatigue-associated conditions (Long COVID, cancer-related fatigue) have shown mixed but promising results. If the energy-immune cycle is indeed bistable, NR could theoretically shift the system below the separatrix by raising the energy ceiling enough for immune recovery.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Heng2025mecfs (elevated AMP/ADP, reduced ATP/ADP in immune cells), @Germain2025proteome (glycolysis upregulation → mitochondrial stress), existing energy-immune coupling model (@eq:energy-immune-coupling), @Che2025 (impaired energy production)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) NR 1000mg/day for 8 weeks will increase immune cell NAD+ levels (measured by LC-MS in PBMCs) and improve ATP/ADP ratio; (b) the ATP/ADP improvement will correlate with reduced IL-6 and improved NK cell cytotoxicity; (c) responders will show lower baseline ATP/ADP ratio (more room for improvement).

## 5. Non-Pharmacological Interventions

### 5.1 Transcutaneous Vagus Nerve Stimulation (tVNS) Targeted to Brainstem NII-Guided Protocol
- **Category**: non-pharma
- **Certainty**: 0.45
- **Mechanistic rationale**: tVNS activates the cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway (reducing TNF-α via α7 nAChR on macrophages) and increases vagal efferent tone (improving autonomic function). The brainstem autonomic loop hypothesis (1.4) predicts that tVNS should be most effective in patients with demonstrable brainstem neuroinflammation (measurable by NII metrics). This suggests a precision approach: use NII imaging to identify patients with brainstem white matter involvement, then apply tVNS specifically to this subgroup. The existing vagal coupling model (@eq:vagal-coupling) predicts that increasing V_efferent will reduce TNF-α and improve the energy-immune cycle.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (NII model for patient stratification), @Faro2024connectivity (cuneiform–medulla FC), existing vagal coupling model (@eq:vagal-coupling), existing paper ANS content (ch10)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) Patients with brainstem NII abnormalities will show greater improvement in HRV, inflammatory markers, and fatigue with tVNS than patients without brainstem involvement; (b) tVNS will reduce CSF inflammatory markers (C3a, IL-6) in POTS+ patients; (c) functional connectivity between cuneiform nucleus and medulla will normalize with tVNS.

### 5.2 Intermittent Hypoxia Preconditioning for Cerebral Blood Flow Augmentation
- **Category**: non-pharma
- **Certainty**: 0.25
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Brief, controlled episodes of hypoxia (intermittent hypoxic training, IHT) stimulate upregulation of HIF-1α, which induces VEGF, erythropoietin, and nitric oxide synthase — all of which improve cerebral blood flow and angiogenesis. In ME/CFS, cerebral hypoperfusion is documented (Barnden2011, Novak2022) and NII findings of cerebral edema (Yu2026) may reflect impaired perfusion. IHT could theoretically improve cerebral perfusion without the metabolic cost of exercise, making it suitable for severe patients. The Heng2025 finding of vascular reactivity protein elevation suggests the vascular system is primed but dysregulated — IHT may help recalibrate vascular reactivity.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (NII-HR cerebral edema), @Heng2025mecfs (vascular reactivity proteins), existing paper cerebral hypoperfusion content (ch08, fig-cerebral-hypoperfusion-mecfs), IHT literature in cerebrovascular disease (Serebrovskaya 2012)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) 4-week IHT protocol (15% O₂, 5 min × 6 cycles, 3×/week) will improve cerebral blood flow velocity (transcranial Doppler) and NII-HR metrics; (b) improvement will be greater in patients with baseline cerebral hypoperfusion; (c) IHT will not trigger PEM (unlike exercise challenge), measured by post-session symptom scores.

### 5.3 Thoracic Eccentric Breathing Protocol for Glymphatic-Immune Clearance Enhancement
- **Category**: non-pharma
- **Certainty**: 0.25
- **Mechanistic rationale**: The glymphatic system clears metabolic waste from the brain during sleep via CSF convection along perivascular spaces, driven by arterial pulsation and respiration. Slow, deep breathing with prolonged exhalation (thoracic/diaphragmatic emphasis) maximizes respiratory sinus arrhythmia, which amplifies the arterial pulsation that drives glymphatic flow. If neuroinflammation (Yu2026 NII findings) is maintained in part by impaired clearance of inflammatory mediators from the brain, enhancing glymphatic clearance could reduce neuroinflammatory burden. The existing paper discusses the glymphatic system and mast cell-CSF flow axis. This protocol is cost-free and risk-free, making it suitable for all severity levels.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (NII neuroinflammation), existing paper glymphatic system discussion, Iliff et al. 2012 (respiratory modulation of glymphatic flow), Lundgaard et al. 2017 (sleep-dependent glymphatic clearance)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) A structured breathing protocol (4-7-8 breathing, 20 min BID for 8 weeks) will improve subjective sleep quality and reduce morning brain fog; (b) in a subset with accessible CSF (research setting), inflammatory mediators (IL-6, TNF-α) will decrease after 8 weeks; (c) the protocol will improve HRV, providing a measurable autonomic benefit even if glymphatic effects cannot be directly measured.

## 6. Combinations + Access

### 6.1 The "Triple Threat" Protocol: Complement Block + Immune Reset + BBB Stabilization
- **Category**: combination
- **Certainty**: 0.25
- **Mechanistic rationale**: For severe patients in the complement-pQTL subgroup, a multi-component protocol targeting all three convergent systems simultaneously: (1) avacopan (C5aR antagonist) to block complement-driven mast cell activation and neutrophil degranulation, (2) LDN + sodium butyrate for microglial reprogramming and BBB stabilization, (3) modified citrus pectin for Galectin-9 sequestration and immune remodeling modulation. Each component targets a different node in the complement→mast cell→BBB→neuroinflammation→autonomic failure→peripheral immune activation cascade. The combination rationale is that attacking the cascade at multiple points simultaneously may overcome the bistability of the energy-immune cycle (which predicts that single interventions may be insufficient to cross the separatrix).
- **Evidence anchor**: @Maya2026complement-genetics, @Shahbaz2026single-cell-immune, @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation, @Bragée2026csf-proteome, existing bistability hypothesis (ch30, @sec:energy-immune-coupling)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) The triple combination will produce clinically meaningful improvement (>20% reduction in fatigue scores) in complement-pQTL-positive severe patients at 12 weeks, whereas any single component alone will produce <10% improvement; (b) the combination will shift multiple biomarkers simultaneously (C5a, Galectin-9, NII-HR, HRV) rather than one; (c) response will be more durable at 6-month follow-up than with single interventions.

### 6.2 Severity-Stratified Access Ladder for Immune-Vascular Interventions
- **Category**: combination
- **Certainty**: 0.55
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Severe ME/CFS patients cannot access exercise studies, imaging protocols, or clinic-based interventions. A severity-stratified access ladder would map interventions to functional capacity: (1) Bedbound/severe: breathing protocols, oral supplements (NR, butyrate, MCP), LDN — all deliverable at home; (2) Moderate: add tVNS, IHT, add avacopan for complement subgroup; (3) Mild-moderate: add monitored exercise testing, targeted immune interventions (Galectin-9 blockade in clinical trials). This ensures the convergence findings reach all severity levels, not just the ambulatory minority that participates in research.
- **Evidence anchor**: All 12 studies; existing paper ch14a (severe ME/CFS management); Bragée2026 severity stratification
- **Falsifiable prediction**: Implementing the access ladder will: (a) increase treatment response rates in severe patients (who currently have no evidence-based options) from near-zero to measurable levels; (b) identify which interventions are most effective at each severity level; (c) reveal that certain mechanisms (e.g., Galectin-9 pathway) are more targetable at specific severity stages.

## 7. Mathematical Model Extensions

### 7.1 EV-Mediated Inter-Compartment Signaling Module
- **Category**: math model
- **Certainty**: 0.50
- **Mechanistic rationale**: The existing integrated model (@ch:integrated-systems) couples systems via soluble mediators (cytokines, neurotransmitters) and neural pathways (vagus). It lacks a formal treatment of extracellular vesicle-mediated signaling, which the Seifert2026 data suggests is relevant. A new EV compartment should track: EV concentration in plasma, EV cargo state (miRNA profile, protein cargo), EV production rate (by activated monocytes, endothelial cells), EV clearance rate (hepatic, renal), and EV transcytosis across BBB. The cargo state determines the effect of EVs on recipient cells: EVs with low let-7b-5p upregulate TLR4 on microglia; EVs with Galectin-9 engage TIM-3 on immune cells. This module connects peripheral immune remodeling to CNS pathology through a mechanism distinct from free cytokine transport.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Seifert2026extracellular-vesicles (EV cargo signatures), @Shahbaz2026single-cell-immune (monocytes as EV source), existing BBB model (@eq:bbb-transport), existing microglial model (@eq:microglia)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) Adding the EV module to the integrated model will improve fit to longitudinal PEM data (Germain2025) compared to the model without EVs; (b) the EV module will predict that interventions targeting EV production (e.g., GW4869 neutral sphingomyelinase inhibitor) would reduce CNS neuroinflammation more than interventions targeting free cytokines alone; (c) model simulations will reproduce the Seifert2026 observation that EV let-7b-5p correlates with clinical severity.

### 7.2 Complement Cascade Module Coupled to Coagulation and Mast Cell Systems
- **Category**: math model
- **Certainty**: 0.50
- **Mechanistic rationale**: The existing model mentions complement C3a/C5a as mast cell priming inputs (@eq:mast-cell-dynamics) but does not formalize complement cascade dynamics. A new module should track: C3, C3a, C3b, C5, C5a, C5b-9 (MAC), Factor B, Factor D, and properdin — the key components of the alternative pathway. The module should couple to: (1) mast cell degranulation (C3a/C5a → MC activation), (2) coagulation cascade (C5b-9 → tissue factor expression → thrombin generation), (3) BBB permeability (C5a → endothelial activation), and (4) neutrophil activation (C5a → chemotaxis, degranulation). The Maya2026 pQTL subgroup can be modeled as elevated C3 production rate with normal/low Factor B, producing the high C3/low Bb phenotype.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Maya2026complement-genetics (alternative pathway subgroup), @Bragée2026csf-proteome (CSF complement + coagulation), @Che2025 (complement activation), existing mast cell model (@eq:mast-cell-dynamics)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) The complement module will predict that C5aR blockade (avacopan) reduces mast cell degranulation more effectively than broad C3 inhibition (compstatin) in the Maya2026 subgroup; (b) model simulations will reproduce the Bragée2026 finding of co-complement and coagulation enrichment in CSF; (c) the model will predict a critical C3 level above which the complement-mast cell loop becomes self-sustaining.

### 7.3 Brainstem Autonomic Loop as Three-Node Bistable System
- **Category**: math model
- **Certainty**: 0.40
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Hypothesis 1.4 proposes a three-node cycle: brainstem neuroinflammation ↔ autonomic failure ↔ peripheral immune-vascular dysfunction. This can be formalized as a three-variable coupled ODE system: (1) brainstem microglial activation state μ_BS (from @eq:microglia applied to brainstem), (2) autonomic balance index A = V_efferent/S_sympathetic (from @eq:ans-balance), (3) peripheral inflammatory load I (from @eq:cytokine-general). The coupling terms: μ_BS impairs A (neuroinflammation disrupts autonomic nuclei), A modulates I (vagal anti-inflammatory pathway), I drives μ_BS (cytokines cross BBB). Analysis of this three-node system should reveal whether it exhibits bistability (two stable states: healthy and disease) and what the critical parameter values are for the transition. This extends the existing two-node energy-immune bistability to include the autonomic-brainstem axis.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (brainstem NII), existing vagal coupling (@eq:vagal-coupling), existing bistability hypothesis (ch30), @Faro2024connectivity (brainstem FC changes)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) Mathematical analysis will reveal a separatrix in the (μ_BS, A, I) state space; (b) parameter values estimated from patient data will place patients on the disease side of the separatrix; (c) the model will predict that interventions targeting the weakest coupling (identified by sensitivity analysis) will be most effective at pushing the system back to the healthy attractor.

## 8. Cross-Disease Bridges

### 8.1 ME/CFS-Systemic Sclerosis Overlap: Complement-Endothelium-Mast Cell Triad
- **Category**: cross-disease
- **Certainty**: 0.30
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Systemic sclerosis (SSc) involves complement activation, endothelial dysfunction, mast cell activation, and fibrosis — the same triad that the blood/immune convergence studies identify in ME/CFS, minus the fibrosis. The Bragée2026 finding of "extracellular remodelling" protein ratios in CSF, the Heng2025 vascular reactivity proteins, and the complement cascade enrichment in CSF all parallel SSc pathology at a molecular level. If ME/CFS represents a "smoldering" version of the same complement-endothelium-mast cell pathology without the fibrotic endpoint, then: (a) ME/CFS patients should show elevated circulating fibrocyte precursors (the cells that become fibroblasts in SSc) but without tissue deposition; (b) ME/CFS patients should share autoantibody specificities with SSc (anti-endothelial cell antibodies, anti-fibrillarin); (c) drugs effective in SSc (nintedanib, tocilizumab) might have rationale in ME/CFS subsets.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Bragée2026csf-proteome (extracellular remodelling), @Heng2025mecfs (vascular proteins), @Che2025 (ECM disruption), SSc literature on complement-endothelium-mast cell axis (van Bon et al. 2014)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) ME/CFS patients will show elevated circulating fibrocytes (CD45+Col1+ cells by flow cytometry) compared to controls, but lower than SSc patients; (b) a subset of ME/CFS patients will have anti-endothelial cell antibodies detectable by immunofluorescence; (c) ME/CFS patients will not show the same pattern of tissue fibrosis (measured by skin biopsy, HRCT chest) despite molecular overlap.

### 8.2 ME/CFS-Hereditary Angioedema Bridge: Kallikrein-Kinin System as Hidden Amplifier
- **Category**: cross-disease
- **Certainty**: 0.25
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Hereditary angioedema (HAE) involves uncontrolled bradykinin production due to C1 esterase inhibitor deficiency, producing episodic vascular leakage and edema. The complement and coagulation systems share C1-INH as a regulator. If ME/CFS involves chronic low-level complement activation (Maya2026, Che2025, Bragée2026), C1-INH consumption could reduce bradykinin regulation, producing chronic low-grade vascular leakage. This would manifest as: orthostatic intolerance (vascular leakage in lower extremities), cerebral edema (NII-HR reduction in Yu2026), and POTS (reduced intravascular volume). Unlike HAE's episodic dramatic swelling, ME/CFS would represent chronic subclinical bradykinin excess. Icatibant (bradykinin B2 receptor antagonist, used in HAE) could be tested.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (NII-HR cerebral edema), @Bragée2026csf-proteome (POTS+ CSF changes), @Heng2025mecfs (vascular reactivity proteins), HAE pathophysiology literature (Kaplan & Joseph 2010)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) ME/CFS patients will show elevated plasma bradykinin or kallikrein activity compared to controls; (b) C1-INH levels will be reduced in severe ME/CFS (especially POTS+); (c) subcutaneous icatibant will produce rapid (within hours) improvement in orthostatic symptoms in a subset of patients with elevated bradykinin.

### 8.3 ME/CFS-Neuromyelitis Optica Spectrum Disorder (NMOSD) Bridge: Complement-Mediated Astrocytopathy
- **Category**: cross-disease
- **Certainty**: 0.20
- **Mechanistic rationale**: NMOSD is an astrocyte-directed disease where anti-AQP4 antibodies activate complement, producing complement-dependent cytotoxicity (CDC) and membrane attack complex (MAC) deposition on astrocytes. The astrocyte-lactate shuttle is discussed in the existing paper's neurological chapter. If complement activation in ME/CFS (Maya2026, Bragée2026) produces subclinical astrocyte dysfunction (not overt cell death as in NMOSD), this could impair the astrocyte-neuron metabolic coupling that supports neuronal energy supply. The complement-microglia-mast cell cascade could damage astrocytes at a sublethal level, reducing lactate shuttle capacity and contributing to the brain energy deficit. This predicts: (a) ME/CFS patients should show elevated CSF GFAP (astrocyte injury marker) without the massive elevations seen in NMOSD; (b) complement-targeted therapy might improve astrocyte function specifically.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Bragée2026csf-proteome (CSF complement cascade), @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (white matter involvement), existing paper astrocyte-lactate shuttle discussion (ch08), NMOSD complement literature (Saadoun et al. 2020)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) CSF GFAP will be elevated in ME/CFS vs controls, correlating with NII metrics and fatigue severity; (b) astrocyte cultures exposed to ME/CFS CSF will show reduced lactate production, reversible by complement inhibition; (c) avacopan (C5aR antagonist) will reduce CSF GFAP levels in ME/CFS patients with elevated baseline GFAP.

## 9. Diagnostic/Biomarker Ideas

### 9.1 Composite Blood-Immune-Vascular Biomarker Panel: The "ME/CFS fingerprint"
- **Category**: diagnostic
- **Certainty**: 0.50
- **Mechanistic rationale**: No single biomarker has achieved sufficient sensitivity/specificity for ME/CFS diagnosis. A composite panel leveraging convergent findings from multiple studies could: (1) TRPM3 function in NK cells (Sasso2026, functional assay), (2) complement protein panel C3/Bb ratio + C3a/C5a (Maya2026, ELISA), (3) plasma EV let-7b-5p (Seifert2026, qPCR), (4) ATP/ADP ratio in PBMCs (Heng2025, LC-MS), (5) NII metrics from diffusion MRI (Yu2026, imaging). Machine learning classification on this multi-modal panel should outperform any single marker. The key insight from the convergence is that these markers span different pathophysiological systems — a patient positive on ≥3/5 systems is very likely to have ME/CFS rather than a mimicking condition.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Sasso2026trpm3, @Maya2026complement-genetics, @Seifert2026extracellular-vesicles, @Heng2025mecfs, @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation, @Germain2025proteome
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) A random forest classifier trained on the 5-marker panel will achieve ≥85% sensitivity and ≥85% specificity in distinguishing ME/CFS from healthy controls and disease controls (depression, fibromyalgia, Lyme); (b) the panel will classify ME/CFS patients into mechanistic subgroups that predict treatment response; (c) the panel will be stable over 6-month follow-up in untreated patients (test-retest reliability).

### 9.2 EV-Based Liquid Biopsy for CNS Compartment Assessment
- **Category**: diagnostic
- **Certainty**: 0.35
- **Mechanistic rationale**: CSF sampling is invasive and rarely available. Neuronal-derived EVs (found in peripheral blood) carry cargo that reflects CNS state. If neuroinflammation (Yu2026) and complement activation in CSF (Bragée2026) are real phenomena, neuronal- and glial-derived EVs in blood should carry molecular signatures of this pathology: complement proteins, inflammatory miRNAs, GFAP, neurofilament light chain. This would provide a "liquid biopsy" of the CNS compartment without lumbar puncture. The Seifert2026 finding that EVs carry disease-specific cargo supports this approach.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Seifert2026extracellular-vesicles (EV cargo specificity), @Bragée2026csf-proteome (CSF pathology), @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (CNS pathology), neuronal-derived EV literature (Shi et al. 2022)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) Neuronal-derived EVs (isolated by L1CAM immunocapture) from ME/CFS blood will show elevated GFAP, complement C3/C4, and inflammatory miRNAs vs controls; (b) neuronal EV cargo will correlate with NII metrics (Yu2026) in patients who have both blood draws and MRI; (c) neuronal EV cargo will change after exercise stress, tracking PEM dynamics.

### 9.3 Galectin-9 Blood Test for MAIT/Gamma-Delta Depletion Screening
- **Category**: diagnostic
- **Certainty**: 0.40
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Flow cytometry for MAIT and gamma-delta T cells requires fresh blood, specialized equipment, and expertise — impractical for routine clinical use. If Galectin-9 drives their depletion (Shahbaz2026), serum Galectin-9 level (measurable by standard ELISA) could serve as a surrogate marker for tissue-resident lymphocyte depletion. A high Galectin-9 level would identify patients with immune remodeling who might benefit from Galectin-9-targeted interventions (MCP, anti-Galectin-9). This is a practical diagnostic step-down from specialized flow cytometry.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Shahbaz2026single-cell-immune (Galectin-9-TIM-3 mechanism), existing paper Galectin-9 hypothesis (ch07)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) Serum Galectin-9 (ELISA) will correlate with MAIT and gamma-delta T cell counts (flow cytometry, r ≤ -0.6); (b) Galectin-9 level will be stable over 3 months (trait marker, not state marker); (c) Galectin-9 will differentiate LC-ME/CFS from recovered post-COVID individuals (Shahbaz2026 cohort).

### 9.4 Exercise Challenge Complement Activation Test for PEM Phenotyping
- **Category**: diagnostic
- **Certainty**: 0.45
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Germain2025 showed that proteomic PEM dynamics differ between ME/CFS and controls. Complement activation is a convergent finding across multiple studies (Maya2026, Che2025, Bragée2026). A focused exercise challenge protocol measuring complement activation products (C3a, C4a, C5a, sC5b-9) at baseline, 1h, and 24h post-exercise could serve as a functional PEM test: ME/CFS patients should show exaggerated and prolonged complement activation compared to sedentary controls. This extends the Sorensen2003/Polli2019 exercise complement findings with modern assays and the Germain2025 longitudinal framework.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Germain2025proteome (PEM dynamics), @Maya2026complement-genetics (complement subgroup), @Che2025 (complement activation post-exercise), @Sorensen2003complement, @Polli2019complement (existing paper citations)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) ME/CFS patients will show ≥2-fold greater C3a and C5a elevation at 24h post-exercise vs sedentary controls; (b) the magnitude of complement activation at 24h will predict symptom severity at 48h; (c) complement-pQTL-positive patients (Maya2026) will show the most exaggerated complement response.

### 9.5 Multi-Compartment Severity Score: Blood + CSF + Imaging Integration
- **Category**: diagnostic
- **Certainty**: 0.40
- **Mechanistic rationale**: Current ME/CFS severity scales are purely clinical (questionnaire-based). The Bragée2026 finding that CSF protein ratios correlate with severity, combined with Yu2026 NII metrics correlating with disability, and Germain2025 plasma proteomics correlating with symptom severity, suggests that a multi-compartment biomarker severity score could: (1) provide objective severity measurement, (2) identify which compartment(s) drive severity in each patient, (3) track treatment response more sensitively than questionnaires. The score would integrate: blood (complement panel, EV cargo), CSF (protein ratios, if available), and imaging (NII metrics). Even without CSF, the blood + imaging version should outperform clinical scales.
- **Evidence anchor**: @Bragée2026csf-proteome (severity protein ratios), @Yu2026diffusion-neuroinflammation (NII-disability correlation), @Germain2025proteome (symptom-proteomics correlation)
- **Falsifiable prediction**: (a) The multi-compartment severity score will correlate more strongly with functional capacity (6-minute walk test, actigraphy) than questionnaire-based severity scales (SF-36, Chalder); (b) patients with multi-compartment involvement (blood + imaging + CSF) will have worse outcomes than those with single-compartment abnormalities; (c) the score will detect improvement in clinical trials earlier and with greater effect size than symptom questionnaires.

## Cross-Cutting Connections

The two major themes (immune remodeling + blood/vascular) connect through three molecular bridges identified across these ideas:

1. **Complement system** — bridges immune activation (C3a/C5a chemotaxis, MAC) to vascular damage (endothelial activation, coagulation) to CNS pathology (BBB permeability, microglial activation via C5aR). Hypotheses 1.1, 1.3, drugs 3.1, diagnostics 9.4.

2. **Galectin-9** — bridges immune remodeling (TIM-3-mediated cell depletion) to vascular dysfunction (endothelial cross-linking, perivascular mast cell effects) to inter-compartment signaling (EV cargo). Hypotheses 1.2, 1.3, drug 3.4, supplement 4.1, diagnostic 9.3.

3. **Brainstem autonomic circuit** — bridges CNS pathology (neuroinflammation) to peripheral immune dysregulation (vagal anti-inflammatory failure) to vascular dysfunction (autonomic control of perfusion). Hypothesis 1.4, non-pharma 5.1, math model 7.3.
