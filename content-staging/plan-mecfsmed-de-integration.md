## Tier 1: New Knowledge (high integration value)

### 1.1 Amifampridin (3,4-DAP) for Post-COVID Fatigue
- **Source article:** Boehmeke T (2024) "Reduced Fatigue Symptoms in Post-COVID Syndrome With Amifampridine." *Cureus* 16(1):e52935
- **Content:** n=5 double-blind discontinuation trial; Bell Score improvement; normalized sleep requirement; 2/5 confirmed in blinded phase
- **Target:** ch18-emerging-therapies.typ (new subsection or addition to neuromuscular junction agents)
- **Why novel:** Not mentioned anywhere in our document. Amifampridin is a voltage-gated potassium channel blocker used for Lambert-Eaton — interesting NMJ crossover to ME/CFS fatigue
- **Caveat:** Very small n=5; must frame as preliminary case series, not evidence-based recommendation

### 1.2 Medical Gaslighting as Clinical Phenomenon
- **Source page:** mecfsmed.de/mecfs/medical-gaslighting/
- **Content:** Definition, systemic contributing factors (time constraints, bias, gender health gap), psychological consequences (medical PTSD, treatment avoidance), historical parallels (MS misdiagnosed as hysteria), ME/CFS-specific dismissal patterns
- **Target:** ch24-controversies.typ (new subsection under diagnostic barriers or psychologization)
- **Why novel:** We have only one passing "gaslighting" mention (sleep study interpretation). This deserves systematic treatment: it's a major patient experience theme with documented health outcome impacts
- **Integration scope:** ~300-400 words; tie to existing PACE criticism and diagnostic delay content

### 1.3 German Clinical Guidelines (DEGAM + G-BA)
- **Source pages:** mecfsmed.de/mecfs/leitlinien/ + G-BA article
- **Content:**
  - DEGAM 2022: Uses IOM for screening + CCC for confirmation; explicitly warns against "activating measures"; recommends pacing
  - NICE 2021 coverage (we have this)
  - G-BA LongCOV-RL: Statutory care guideline under §92 SGB V; GP-coordinated multi-level care; PEM-specific provisions; 3-year evaluation mandate
- **Target:** Existing guidelines section (likely ch24 or ch03 if we have diagnostic criteria chapter) — need to check exact location
- **Why novel:** German guidelines completely absent from our document. DEGAM is particularly significant: a major national GP society explicitly rejecting activation therapy and mandating pacing. G-BA guideline is landmark policy (statutory, not advisory)
- **Citations to add:** DEGAM 2022, Nacul et al. 2021 (EUROMENE), G-BA LongCOV-RL 2023

### 1.4 Serotonin Excess / 5-HT1A Receptor Dysfunction (mouse model)
- **Source article:** Lee JS et al. (2024) *Journal of Translational Medicine* 22:34
- **Content:** High-dose fluoxetine → elevated serotonin in dorsal raphe + hypothalamus → HPA axis dysfunction, fatigue, pain sensitivity, anxiety; mechanism is 5-HT1A functional loss (not quantitative change)
- **Target:** ch08-neurological.typ (existing serotonin/tryptophan section) or ch09-endocrine.typ (HPA axis)
- **Why novel:** We cover tryptophan pathway and kynurenine extensively, but this specific mouse model demonstrating hyperserotonergia → ME/CFS-like symptoms via 5-HT1A receptor dysfunction is a distinct finding. The 5-HT1A functional loss mechanism is not in our current coverage
- **Caveat:** Mouse model only; frame as preclinical hypothesis-generating

## Tier 2: Enhancing Existing Coverage (moderate value)

### 2.1 EBV → ME/CFS in Youth (Pricoco et al. 2024)
- **Source article:** Pricoco R et al. (2024) *Front. Pediatr.* 11:1266738
- **Content:** n=25 (12 adolescents + 13 young adults) post-EBV ME/CFS; 12-month follow-up; adolescents 45% recovered vs young adults 0% recovered; avg 27 symptoms per patient
- **Target:** ch12-genetics-epigenetics.typ or ch07-immune-dysfunction.typ (EBV section)
- **Why useful:** We cite pediatric prognosis generally but not this specific study. The stark age-dependent recovery difference (45% vs 0%) is a clinically important finding worth documenting
- **Citation:** Pricoco et al. 2024

### 2.2 EBV IgG Machine Learning Classifier (Fonseca et al. 2024)
- **Source article:** Fonseca A et al. (2024) *Medicina* 60:161
- **Content:** Super Learner algorithm on 3,054 EBV peptide variants; 26-antibody classifier → 100% training / 90% test accuracy for infection-triggered ME/CFS; fails for non-infectious triggers
- **Target:** ch20-biomarker-research.typ (diagnostic biomarkers section)
- **Why useful:** ML-based diagnostic classifier is novel approach not in our coverage; the trigger-specificity finding (works only for infection-triggered cases) supports subtyping paradigm
- **Citation:** Fonseca et al. 2024

### 2.3 Paxlovid Does NOT Prevent Long COVID (Durstenfeld et al. 2024)
- **Source article:** Durstenfeld MS et al. (2024) *J Med Virol* 96:e29333
- **Content:** n=4,684 from CCS cohort; nirmatrelvir 16.1% Long COVID vs control 14.0% — no significant reduction
- **Target:** ch18-emerging-therapies.typ or ch14d-cross-disease.typ (Long COVID section)
- **Why useful:** Negative finding is clinically important — refutes a common patient/clinician assumption. We have minimal Paxlovid coverage
- **Citation:** Durstenfeld et al. 2024

### 2.4 HDL Proteome + Statin/ARB Combination Therapy
- **Source article:** Grote K et al. (2024) *Int J Mol Sci* 25(8):4522
- **Content:** n=16 (8 PCS + 8 PVS); statin+ARB 6 weeks → reduced inflammatory cytokines/adhesion molecules; HDL proteome abnormalities (elevated hemoglobin, cytoskeletal proteins); upregulated FAM3C, ATP6AP2, ADAM10 post-treatment
- **Target:** ch18-emerging-therapies.typ (endothelial/cardiovascular treatments) or ch10-cardiovascular.typ
- **Why useful:** Novel therapeutic combination with proteomics rationale; ties to our extensive endothelial dysfunction coverage
- **Caveat:** n=16, no control group — very preliminary

### 2.5 ME/CFS Severity Classification (NICE 4-tier)
- **Source page:** mecfsmed.de/mecfs/schweregrade-von-mecfs/
- **Content:** Detailed 4-tier classification (mild/moderate/severe/very severe) with functional capacity descriptions; IQWiG distribution (25%/50%/25%); mortality case (Merryn Crofts)
- **Target:** ch02-core-symptoms.typ or existing severity section
- **Why useful:** If our document lacks a formal severity classification table, this fills that gap. The IQWiG distribution data and functional capacity descriptions are clinically actionable
- **Check needed:** Verify whether we already have NICE severity tiers documented
