## KEY INSIGHTS FOR OUR PAPER

### 1. Immune chapter (ch07) needs major revision signal
The immune overview article argues current evidence for low-grade inflammation is "weak and not in proportion to the disability." Multiple null findings across viral, autoantibody, and cytokine studies accumulating. Our ch07 should acknowledge this evidence gap honestly.

### 2. Psychosomatic history series — unique resource for ch03
The 15-part "dark psychosomatic history" series documents how MS, asthma, epilepsy, diabetes, autism, schizophrenia, cancer, heart disease, RA, and peptic ulcers were ALL once considered psychosomatic. The peptic ulcer/H. pylori story is the strongest parallel to ME/CFS. This series provides excellent historical context for our history chapter.

### 3. Comorbidity skepticism (ch14d)
The hEDS and POTS articles challenge the assumed ME/CFS-hEDS-POTS triad. hEDS lacks identified connective tissue defect; POTS threshold has low specificity/sensitivity/reproducibility. Our comorbidity chapter should present these critiques.

### 4. Exercise/deconditioning evidence (ch06, ch10)
Strong evidence against deconditioning hypothesis: NASA bed rest comparison, normal-range VO2max, boom-bust myth debunked by accelerometry. 2-day CPET has methodological problems limiting biomarker utility.

### 5. Treatment evidence quality (Part III)
FITNET-NHS failure, MAGENTA failure, Cochrane critique, blinding problems, GRADE limitations — all strengthen our evidence-based treatment assessment.

### 6. DecodeME genetics
Major new evidence pointing to brain/neuronal genes. Low heritability (9.5%) but consistent signal across independent studies.

### 7. Funding disparity
Quantified NIH funding decline ($10.1M → $7.4M, 2024→2025) while European investment grows. <0.03% of NIH budget despite disease burden.

### 8. Catastrophizing critique
Term should be abandoned; PCS measures pain not distortion; applied to ME/CFS patients inappropriately.
