# Brainstorm: CPET Null Replication (Mancini 2026) — ME/CFS Pathophysiology Implications

**Date:** 2026-05-26
**Context:** Null replication of 2-day CPET protocol (Mancini et al. 2026) contrasts with positive findings (Keller 2024). Key consistent signals: elevated RPE, chronotropic incompetence.

---

## 1. Novel Hypotheses

### H1.1: PEM as Phenotype-Dependent Phenomenon
**Mechanism:** The discrepancy between Keller (CCC patients, 5-8% decline) and Mancini (Fukuda patients, no decline) suggests PEM is not a universal ME/CFS feature but phenotype-dependent.
**Evidence link:** CCC requires PEM for diagnosis; Fukuda does not. This creates selection bias at enrollment.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.75
**Falsifiable prediction:** In a single cohort, the subset meeting CCC criteria will show Day 2 decline while those meeting only Fukuda will not, after controlling for baseline fitness.

---

### H1.2: RPE as Primary PEM Marker, VO₂ Decline as Epiphenomenon
**Mechanism:** Elevated RPE at all workloads (p < 0.001) is the only consistent finding across both studies. VO₂ decline may be downstream consequence of RPE-mediated effort termination rather than primary PEM.
**Evidence link:** Borg RPE elevation found in both positive (Keller) and null (Mancini) studies; VO₂ decline not replicated.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.70
**Falsifiable prediction:** RPE elevation on Day 2 predicts subsequent symptom exacerbation regardless of VO₂ change magnitude; VO₂ decline can occur without symptom exacerbation if effort is maintained.

---

### H1.3: PEM as Binary State Captured by Individual-Level Threshold, Not Group Mean
**Mechanism:** Group-average analysis obscures binary PEM states. Patients either cross exertion tolerance threshold (manifesting as decline) or do not. Mixing responders and non-responders dilutes group signal.
**Evidence link:** 22% of ME/CFS showed ≥1 mL/kg/min decline (similar proportion to controls); heterogeneity in response pattern unreported.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.65
**Falsifiable prediction:** Cluster analysis reveals distinct responder vs non-responder subgroups; within-group effects are large (Cohen's d > 0.8) even when group mean is null.

---

### H1.4: Chronotropic Incompetence as PEI (Post-Exertional Immune) Precursor Rather Than PEM Marker
**Mechanism:** 9/58 ME/CFS (15.5%) vs 1/25 controls (4%) showed chronotropic incompetence. This may be pre-existing autonomic dysregulation that predisposes to PEM rather than PEM consequence.
**Evidence link:** Chronotropic incompetence elevated in both studies; persists across Day 1/Day 2.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.60
**Falsifiable prediction:** Baseline chronotropic incompetence predicts Day 2 VO₂ decline or symptom exacerbation; treating CI reduces PEM expression.

---

### H1.5: Day 2 Effort Termination Criterion Mismatch Drives Apparent Discrepancy
**Mechanism:** Mancini excluded non-ACSM compliant tests (likely excluding CI patients). Keller included CRI failures, arguing CI is part of PEM phenotype. This analytical choice determines whether decline is detected.
**Evidence link:** Mancini's exclusion criteria vs Keller's inclusive approach.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.55
**Falsifiable prediction:** Re-analyzing Mancini data with Keller's inclusion criteria yields significant Day 2 decline; analyzing Keller data with Mancini's exclusion yields null result.

---

### H1.6: PEM Requires Cumulative Exertion Beyond Single Max Test
**Mechanism:** Two max tests separated by 24h may be insufficient to trigger PEM in Fukuda-defined patients. PEM may require repeated submaximal exertion or longer recovery intervals.
**Evidence link:** Patient reports describe PEM onset after prolonged activity, not single maximal effort.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.50
**Falsifiable prediction:** 3-day CPET protocol or submaximal serial exertion paradigm elicits decline in Mancini cohort where 2-day protocol does not.

---

### H1.7: CPET Protocol Captures Cardiopulmonary PEM, Misses Neuroimmune PEM
**Mechanism:** 2-day CPET detects cardiopulmonary deconditioning patterns. ME/CFS PEM may be primarily neuroimmune, manifesting as symptom exacerbation without VO₂ change.
**Evidence link:** Consistent symptom reporting in both studies despite divergent physiological findings.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.55
**Falsifiable prediction:** Biomarkers of neuroimmune activation (cytokines, microglial markers) correlate with Day 2 symptom exacerbation but not with VO₂ decline.

## 2. Research Directions

### R2.1: Head-to-Head Dual-Criteria Study
**Objective:** Resolve selection bias by testing same cohort under both Fukuda and CCC criteria.
**Design:** Recruit ME/CFS patients, apply both criteria sets in parallel. Perform 2-day CPET, analyze by subgroup.
**Power analysis:** Target 30 patients per subgroup (Fukuda-only, CCC+), 80% power to detect d=0.7.
**Timeline:** 18 months (6mo recruitment, 6mo data collection, 6mo analysis).
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.80

---

### R2.2: Individual-Level Trajectory Clustering
**Objective:** Identify responder subgroups masked by group-average analysis.
**Design:** Apply latent class growth analysis to Day 1→Day 2 change vectors (VO₂, RPE, HR, work rate). Identify clusters, validate against symptom exacerbation.
**Outcome:** Responders, non-responders, paradoxical responders (improvement).
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.75

---

### R2.3: RPE-Threshold Pacing Protocol Development
**Objective:** Derive individual RPE thresholds that predict PEM onset.
**Design:** EMA study with RPE monitoring during naturalistic activity. Identify threshold RPE levels predicting next-day symptom exacerbation.
**Sample:** 100 ME/CFS, 14-day monitoring period.
**Analysis:** ROC curves for RPE thresholds predicting PEM.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.70

---

### R2.4: Symptom-Contingent CPET Variant
**Objective:** Develop CPET protocol terminated by symptom exacerbation rather than volitional exhaustion.
**Design:** Incremental work protocol with continuous symptom monitoring. Terminate when pre-defined symptom threshold crossed. Compare termination points across days.
**Rationale:** Captures PEM even when physiological reserve exists.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.60

---

### R2.5: Chronotropic Incompetence Phenotyping Study
**Objective:** Determine if CI is primary driver of CPET findings.
**Design:** Stratify ME/CFS by CI status. Compare 2-day CPET outcomes. Test pyridostigmine in CI subgroup.
**Outcome:** Does CI subgroup show decline? Does treatment normalize Day 2 performance?
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.65

---

### R2.6: Longitudinal PEM Baseline Study
**Objective:** Determine if baseline recovery state moderates Day 2 decline.
**Design:** CPET protocol administered at 3 time points: rested state (7d post-exertion), post-PEM state (48h after triggering exertion), and recovery phase. Compare trajectories.
**Hypothesis:** PEM is detectable only when starting from vulnerable baseline.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.55

---

### R2.7: Cross-Modal Validation of PEM Measures
**Objective:** Identify objective biomarkers correlating with RPE elevation but not VO₂ decline.
**Design:** Simultaneous CPET + cytokine panel + HRV + actigraphy + metabolomics. Identify biomarker patterns tracking with RPE.
**Outcome:** Multi-modal PEM index outperforms single-metric CPET.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.70

## 3. Drug/Medication Ideas

### D3.1: Pyridostigmine for Chronotropic Incompetence
**Target:** Autonomic dysregulation, impaired HR response.
**Mechanism:** Acetylcholinesterase inhibition enhances ganglionic transmission, improves chronotropic competence.
**Evidence link:** Used in POTS; shown to improve HR response in autonomic disorders.
**Dosing:** 30-60mg TID, titrated.
**Trial design:** Crossover, 4-week arms, pre/post 2-day CPET.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.65

---

### D3.2: Ivabradine for HR Control
**Target:** If-mediated pacemaker current in sinoatrial node.
**Mechanism:** Reduces HR without affecting contractility or VO₂. May improve HR reserve utilization.
**Evidence link:** Used in inappropriate sinus tachycardia; improves exercise tolerance in POTS.
**Dosing:** 2.5-7.5mg BID.
**Rationale:** If chronotropic incompetence is pacing issue (HR ceiling too low), ivabradine paradoxically improves exertion tolerance by reducing resting HR.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.55

---

### D3.3: Modafinil for RPE Reduction
**Target:** Central fatigue pathways, perceived exertion.
**Mechanism:** Dopaminergic and orexinergic activation may alter RPE interpretation.
**Evidence link:** Improves perceived effort in fatigue conditions; mixed results in ME/CFS.
**Dosing:** 100-200mg daily.
**Outcome measure:** RPE at standardized workloads, not VO₂max.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.50

---

### D3.4: Low-Dose Naltrexone (LDN) for Neuroinflammation
**Target:** Glial activation, cytokine signaling.
**Mechanism:** Microglial inhibition reduces neuroinflammation, may improve exertion tolerance.
**Evidence link:** Anecdotal benefit in ME/CFS; reduces fatigue in fibromyalgia.
**Dosing:** 0.5-4.5mg nightly.
**Trial:** RPE change during submaximal exertion.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.60

---

### D3.5: Beta-Blockers for CI with Adrenergic Hyperactivity
**Target:** Excessive sympathetic drive, beta-receptor desensitization.
**Mechanism:** Beta-1 blockade may improve HR response efficiency in hyperadrenergic states.
**Evidence link:** Used in POTS; improves HRV in some patients.
**Caveat:** May worsen fatigue in hypoadrenergic subgroup. Phenotyping required.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.45

---

### D3.6: Mestinon (Pyridostigmine) + Beta-Blocker Combination
**Rationale:** Addresses both cholinergic deficiency (improving HR upstroke) and adrenergic excess (preventing overshoot).
**Evidence link:** Synergistic in complex dysautonomia.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.55

## 4. Supplement/Nutraceutical Ideas

### S4.1: Creatine Monohydrate
**Target:** ATP regeneration, muscular fatigue perception.
**Mechanism:** Increases phosphocreatine stores, reduces metabolic stress at given workload.
**Evidence link:** Reduces RPE in healthy adults; improves muscular endurance.
**Dosing:** 5g daily loading, 3g maintenance.
**Outcome:** RPE at standardized workloads, not VO₂max.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.60

---

### S4.2: L-Carnitine
**Target:** Mitochondrial fatty acid oxidation, cellular energy metabolism.
**Mechanism:** Improves fatty acid transport into mitochondria, reduces metabolic byproducts.
**Evidence link:** RCTs show benefit in ME/CFS fatigue; reduces RPE in athletes.
**Dosing:** 1-3g daily.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.55

---

### S4.3: Coenzyme Q10 (Ubiquinol)
**Target:** Electron transport chain, antioxidant protection.
**Mechanism:** Improves mitochondrial efficiency, reduces oxidative stress signaling.
**Evidence link:** Improves exercise capacity in heart failure; mixed results in ME/CFS.
**Dosing:** 100-300mg daily (ubiquinol form).
**Rationale:** If CI reflects mitochondrial inefficiency, CoQ10 may improve exertion tolerance without changing VO₂.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.50

---

### S4.4: Magnesium Glycinate
**Target:** Muscle relaxation, ATP utilization, HR regulation.
**Mechanism:** Cofactor for ATPases; improves muscle recovery; modulates autonomic tone.
**Evidence link:** Reduces muscle cramping, improves HRV.
**Dosing:** 200-400mg elemental Mg daily.
**Outcome:** HR recovery kinetics, RPE reduction.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.45

---

### S4.5: Ribose (D-Ribose)
**Target:** ATP synthesis precursor.
**Mechanism:** Bypasses rate-limiting step in de novo ATP synthesis.
**Evidence link:** Small RCTs show benefit in ME/CFS; improves cellular energy.
**Dosing:** 5g TID.
**Caveat:** Cost, mixed replication.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.40

---

### S4.6: Alpha-Lipoic Acid
**Target:** Mitochondrial antioxidant, glucose uptake.
**Mechanism:** Reduces oxidative stress, improves insulin sensitivity.
**Evidence link:** Improves neuropathic symptoms, reduces fatigue in some studies.
**Dosing:** 300-600mg daily.
**Rationale:** If neuroinflammation drives RPE elevation, ALA may modulate signaling.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.45

## 5. Non-Pharmacological Interventions

### N5.1: RPE-Guided Pacing Protocol
**Concept:** Replace HR-based or time-based pacing with RPE-threshold pacing.
**Protocol:** Identify individual RPE threshold (via EMA). Stay below threshold in daily activity. Gradually titrate upward as tolerance improves.
**Validation:** Track PEM frequency/severity vs baseline pacing.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.75

---

### N5.2: Symptom-Contingent Exercise Testing
**Concept:** Terminate exertion when pre-defined symptom threshold reached, not at volitional exhaustion.
**Protocol:** Standardized exertion with continuous symptom monitoring (pain, fatigue, cognitive fog). Stop when aggregate symptom score exceeds threshold.
**Rationale:** Captures PEM even when physiological reserve remains.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.65

---

### N5.3: Micro-Bout Pacing
**Concept:** Very short activity bouts (1-2 minutes) separated by extended rest.
**Protocol:** Activity periods capped at 2 minutes, minimum 10-minute rest between bouts. Monitor cumulative activity time vs PEM onset.
**Hypothesis:** Prevents cumulative stress while maintaining activity.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.60

---

### N5.4: Heart Rate Variability (HRV) Biofeedback
**Target:** Autonomic balance, CI mitigation.
**Protocol:** Daily HRV biofeedback sessions (10-15 minutes). Target increased vagal tone.
**Outcome:** HRV improvement, CI normalization, RPE reduction.
**Evidence link:** Improves autonomic function in dysautonomia.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.60

---

### N5.5: Cognitive Offloading During Exertion
**Concept:** Reduce cognitive load during physical exertion to prevent PEM exacerbation.
**Protocol:** Physical activity with cognitive simplification (no problem-solving, no multi-tasking). Compare to activity with cognitive demand.
**Hypothesis:** Cognitive-physical interaction drives PEM severity.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.55

---

### N5.6: Pre-Exertion Priming Protocol
**Concept:** Prepare autonomic system before exertion.
**Protocol:** 10-minute vagal priming (slow breathing, relaxation) before exertion. Compare to no-priming control.
**Outcome:** HR response, RPE, PEM onset.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.50

## 7. Mathematical Model Extensions

### M7.1: Individual-Level PEM Probability Model
**Current limitation:** Group-average VO₂ decline assumes uniform PEM response.
**Extension:** Bayesian hierarchical model with individual-level PEM probability parameters.
**Structure:**
- Level 1 (individual): `PEM_prob_i ~ Beta(α_i, β_i)`
- Level 2 (population): `α_i, β_i ~ hyperprior(phenotype, severity)`
- Observed: `VO₂_change_i ~ mixture(PEM_prob_i)`

**Benefit:** Estimates each patient's PEM probability from observed data.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.75

---

### M7.2: RPE-Integrated Exertion Capacity Model
**Current limitation:** Model focuses on VO₂, ignores RPE.
**Extension:** Two-dimensional exertion capacity model: physiological (VO₂) × perceptual (RPE).
**Equations:**
```
EffectiveCapacity_i = f(VO₂_i, RPE_i)
where:
  VO₂_i = baseline_VO₂ + Day_effect_i + error
  RPE_i = baseline_RPE + Day_effect_i + error
  Day_effect_i ~ mixture(PEM_prob_i)
```
**Interpretation:** PEM reduces effective capacity via RPE even if VO₂ unchanged.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.70

---

### M7.3: Chronotropic Incompetence as Moderator Variable
**Current limitation:** CI observed but not modeled.
**Extension:** Include CI status as moderator of Day 2 decline.
**Model:**
```
VO₂_change_i = β0 + β1*CI_i + β2*phenotype_i + β3*CI_i*phenotype_i + error
```
**Interpretation:** β3 quantifies interaction between CI and phenotype on PEM expression.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.65

---

### M7.4: Multi-Modal PEM Index Model
**Current limitation:** Single metric (VO₂) insufficient.
**Extension:** Latent variable model with multiple observed indicators.
**Structure:**
```
PEM_latent_i → VO₂_change_i
PEM_latent_i → RPE_change_i
PEM_latent_i → HR_change_i
PEM_latent_i → symptom_change_i
```
**Benefit:** Latent PEM score robust to measurement noise in any single metric.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.70

---

### M7.5: Temporal Dynamics of PEM Model
**Current limitation:** Binary day comparison.
**Extension:** Continuous-time model of PEM recovery trajectory.
**Model:**
```
PEM_severity(t) = PEM_peak * exp(-t/τ) * (1 + oscillatory_component)
where τ = recovery time constant (varies by individual)
```
**Application:** Fit to multi-day post-exertion symptom diaries to characterize recovery kinetics.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.60

---

### M7.6: Bifurcation Model with RPE Control Parameter
**Current limitation:** Bifurcation model (cert 0.40) doesn't include RPE.
**Extension:** Add RPE as control parameter driving system across stability boundaries.
**Model:**
```
State variables: [energy reserve, inflammation, autonomic tone]
Control parameter: RPE_threshold
Dynamics: d/dt(State) = f(State, RPE_threshold, external stress)
```
**Interpretation:** Individual RPE thresholds determine stress level triggering PEM bifurcation.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.55

## 9. Diagnostic/Biomarker Ideas

### B9.1: Composite PEM Index (CPEMI)
**Components:**
1. **Physiological:** VO₂ at VT change (0-3), HR reserve change (0-2), VO₂max change (0-2)
2. **Perceptual:** RPE at standardized workload change (0-3), symptom exacerbation score (0-2)
3. **Temporal:** Duration of symptom exacerbation (0-2), time to return to baseline (0-1)

**Scoring:** 0-15, threshold ≥8 indicates significant PEM.
**Advantages:** Captures multi-dimensional PEM, robust to single-metric noise.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.75

---

### B9.2: RPE-Threshold Diagnostic Protocol
**Concept:** Identify individual RPE threshold predicting PEM, use for diagnosis.
**Protocol:**
1. EMA RPE monitoring during naturalistic activity (7 days)
2. Identify threshold RPE predicting next-day symptom exacerbation (ROC analysis)
3. Threshold ≤12 (on 6-20 scale) = PEM-positive; >12 = PEM-negative
**Advantages:** No CPET required, scalable.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.70

---

### B9.3: Chronotropic Incompetence Screening
**Protocol:** Submaximal exercise test (6MWT) with continuous HR monitoring. Identify CI:
- Peak HR < 80% age-predicted max
- HR upstroke blunted (HR increase < 30 bpm at 25W)
- HR recovery delayed (>12 bpm drop in first minute post-exercise)

**Interpretation:** CI+ = higher PEM probability.
**Advantages:** Low-cost screening, accessible.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.65

---

### B9.4: Symptom Diary + Wearable HRV Composite
**Components:**
- Symptom exacerbation score (0-10)
- HRV drop magnitude (pre- vs post-exertion)
- Time to HRV recovery

**Algorithm:** Logistic regression predicting PEM status from composite features.
**Advantages:** Passive monitoring, longitudinal data.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.60

---

### B9.5: Cytokine Signature Alternative to CPET
**Rationale:** If PEM is neuroimmune, objective biomarkers may outperform CPET.
**Target panel:** IL-6, TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-10, CXCL10.
**Protocol:** Draw pre-exertion and 24h post-exertion. Compare fold-change.
**Threshold:** ≥2-fold elevation in ≥2 cytokines = PEM-positive.
**Advantages:** Objective, not effort-dependent.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.55

---

### B9.6: Near-Infrared Spectroscopy (NIRS) Muscle Oxygenation
**Protocol:** NIRS during submaximal exercise. Measure muscle oxygen extraction kinetics.
**Hypothesis:** ME/CFS patients show impaired oxygen extraction (slower desaturation, slower resaturation).
**Advantages:** Objective, peripheral measure, not dependent on maximal effort.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.50

---

### B9.7: Cognitive Exertion Test as PEM Trigger
**Concept:** Use cognitive stressor (n-back, Stroop) as exertion trigger, bypass physical limitations.
**Protocol:** 30-minute cognitive challenge with continuous monitoring (EEG, pupillometry, HRV). Measure post-exertion symptom exacerbation.
**Rationale:** Many ME/CFS patients report cognitive PEM. May be more sensitive trigger than physical exertion.
**Preliminary certainty:** 0.55
