### Internal Contradiction Detection

1. **Catastrophizing role:** Poort 2021 (catastrophizing does not mediate fatigue improvement) vs the broader CBT/GET literature assumption that catastrophizing is a key mechanism. This is not a contradiction between included papers — it's the included null evidence against an external literature assumption. **Framing:** The null evidence supports the paper's existing catastrophizing critique. No internal contradiction requiring resolution.

2. **Interoceptive accuracy direction:** The literature supports both elevated interoceptive attention (hypervigilance) AND reduced interoceptive accuracy. These are not contradictory — Garfinkel's tripartite framework resolves them: interoceptive sensibility (self-reported) can be elevated while interoceptive accuracy (objective performance) is reduced. This dissociation is the central mechanistic insight. **Framing:** Present the Garfinkel framework as resolution; the dissociation itself is evidence of disrupted interoceptive processing.

3. **Emotional reactivity:** Rimes 2016 (blunted autonomic activity + elevated subjective distress) vs Van Den Houte 2017 (negative affect amplifies somatic symptoms). Not a contradiction — different levels: Rimes measures autonomic output (blunted); VDH measures cognitive-emotional amplification (exaggerated). Both are consistent with a model where bottom-up interoceptive signals are corrupted and top-down affective amplification is exaggerated. **Framing:** Present as complementary findings describing different nodes in the same system.

4. **Alexithymia causality:** Whether alexithymia in CFS is cause, consequence, or correlate is unresolved. All studies are cross-sectional. **Framing:** Acknowledge as unresolved; present both models (predisposition vs acquired) as #open-question.

### Evidence Summary

The evidence establishes a coherent pathway from peripheral immune-autonomic dysfunction to altered interoceptive and emotional processing in chronic illness. The core mechanism: peripheral inflammation (via vagal afferents and humoral routes) modulates interoceptive brain regions (insula, ACC, brainstem), altering the brain's predictive models of bodily state. This produces the interoceptive accuracy-sensibility dissociation (patients report heightened body awareness but show objective interoceptive deficits), elevated alexithymia (difficulty identifying feelings — a downstream consequence of corrupted interoceptive signals), and emotional-autonomic dissociation (blunted peripheral autonomic reactivity with heightened subjective distress).

The strongest mechanistic claim supportable: inflammation-induced interoceptive prediction error is the computational mechanism linking ME/CFS peripheral immune dysfunction to its emotional, cognitive, and fatigue symptoms. This mechanism is well-established in general neuroscience (Harrison 2009, Savitz/Harrison 2018) and has been applied to MS fatigue (Manjaly 2019, Danciut 2024) and general fatigue (Greenhouse-Tucknott 2022) but has never been DIRECTLY tested in ME/CFS.

What must remain speculative: the specific application to ME/CFS without direct empirical testing in the ME/CFS population. The mechanism is plausible and supported by indirect evidence but unvalidated in ME/CFS specifically. The NICE NG206 context supports the directionality (biological → psychological, not vice versa) but doesn't substitute for direct evidence.

What the evidence does NOT support: catastrophizing as a clinically meaningful mechanism in fatigue (Poort 2021 null mediation; Sohl/Friedberg 2008 recall-bias finding). The paper's existing catastrophizing critique is supported by new null evidence. Emotional processing difficulties are real in CFS but reflect biological drivers, not maladaptive psychology.

**Null findings are positively informative:** The catastrophizing null results strengthen the paper's position, not weaken it. They are evidence against psychological maintenance models.

### Clinical Relevance Assessment

**Clinical relevance: MEDIUM** — mechanistic context for treatment decisions, not actionable interventions. Understanding that emotional/interoceptive symptoms are downstream of immune-autonomic dysfunction supports: (1) targeting underlying biology rather than treating emotional symptoms as primary psychological problems, (2) validating patient experience of emotional disruption as biologically real, (3) cautioning against treatments that assume psychological causation (GET, CBT as primary treatment), (4) directing research toward anti-inflammatory and autonomic interventions for emotional/interoceptive symptoms.

**Patient subset applicability:** Unknown — no studies stratified by severity. Broadly applicable across severity levels as it describes fundamental interoceptive processing, but very severe patients may not tolerate any interoceptive assessment tasks.

**Evidence type:** Mix of general-population neuroscience (Harrison, Seth, Garfinkel, Barrett, Zhang/Wager), cross-disease (MS — Manjaly, Danciut; FM — Valenzuela-Moguillansky), and CFS-specific (Maroti, Rimes, Bileviciute-Ljungar, Wortinger, Van Den Houte). No ME/CFS-specific mechanistic study. The model is robust in general neuroscience but requires validation in ME/CFS.

## Integration Decision: PROCEED

**Rationale:** ≥2 papers with certainty ≥ 0.40 (11 papers) AND 11 papers with certainty ≥ 0.60; evidence is not uniformly null (only catastrophizing studies are null, and these are positively informative); ≤50% of papers have certainty <0.40 (only ~10%). The evidence consistently supports biological foundation for emotional/interoceptive changes and provides null evidence against psychological maintenance models.

Clinical relevance: MEDIUM. Contradictions: none (apparent contradictions resolved by Garfinkel tripartite framework).
