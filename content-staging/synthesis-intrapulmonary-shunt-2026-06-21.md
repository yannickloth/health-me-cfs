# Evidence Synthesis: Intrapulmonary Right-Left Shunt in ME/CFS and Long COVID

**Topic slug:** intrapulmonary-shunt
**Date:** 2026-06-21

## 1. Evidence Landscape Summary

### Strong (certainty ≥ 0.60)
- **Farrow et al. 2023** (0.62): Qs/Qt ~8% (normal ~3-5%) in recovered COVID patients at 6 months, worsening with exercise. Normal spirometry/DLCO in most patients.
- **Harbut et al. 2023** (0.62): Quantified dual gas exchange defect (shunt + dead space) in COVID-19 pneumonitis and early recovery.
- **Durand et al. 2020** (0.60): Established intrapulmonary shunt as recognized mechanism of exercise-induced hypoxemia (general physiology, not ME/CFS-specific).
- **Hoel et al. 2021** (0.65): Metabolic evidence of tissue hypoxia/energy strain in ME/CFS (indirect — supports downstream consequence of shunt).

### Moderate (certainty 0.40–0.59)
- Sandhu 2026 (0.55), Grist 2022 (0.55), Davis 2023 (0.55), Bush 2022 (0.50), Li 2024 (0.48), Lyne 2024 (0.42)

### Weak (certainty < 0.40)
- Espejo 2025 (0.25): single case report

### Null/negative
- No papers that tested for and found absence of shunt in ME/CFS or Long COVID
- No papers that refute shunt as mechanism

### Missing
- **Zero direct ME/CFS studies:** No intrapulmonary shunt measurement (Qs/Qt) in any ME/CFS patient cohort
- No correlation between shunt fraction and PEM severity
- No exercise-effect on Qs/Qt in ME/CFS
- No studies on reversibility or intervention for post-COVID shunt

### Indirect biochemical/systemic links
- **3 papers** with certainty ≥ 0.40 linking tissue hypoxia mechanisms to ME/CFS:
  - Hoel 2021 (0.65): ME/CFS metabolic phenotypes compatible with tissue hypoxia
  - Grist 2022 (0.55): 129Xe MRI shows gas exchange defect in Long COVID with normal PFTs — consistent with shunt
  - Davis 2023 (0.55): IPAVA flow regulation by oxygen tension — mechanism exists and is dynamic
- The paper already extensively discusses HIF-1α pathway dysfunction, tissue hypoxia, microcirculatory impairment, and impaired oxygen extraction in ME/CFS. Shunt provides a *proximal mechanism* for why tissue hypoxia occurs despite normal pulmonary function.

## 2. Internal Contradiction Detection

**None found.** All papers are consistent: intrapulmonary shunt is elevated post-COVID, persists despite clinical recovery, and is measurable despite normal PFTs. No paper contradicts this.

## 3. Evidence Summary

What this evidence establishes:
- Intrapulmonary right-left shunt (Qs/Qt ~8%, normal ~3-5%) persists for at least 6 months after COVID-19
- This shunt explains hypoxemia in patients with normal spirometry, DLCO, and lung volumes — the "normal PFTs with desaturation" paradox
- Shunt worsens with exercise
- Anatomical substrate exists: intrapulmonary arteriovenous anastomoses (IPAVAs) present in ~30% of adults at rest, opening during exertion
- SARS-CoV-2 impairs hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction (HPV), increasing shunt vulnerability
- 129Xe MRI confirms gas exchange impairment despite normal CT/PFTs

Strongest mechanistic claim supportable:
- "Persistent intrapulmonary shunt is a demonstrated mechanism of exertional hypoxemia in post-COVID patients, providing a candidate mechanism for tissue hypoxia and exercise intolerance in ME/CFS-like post-viral syndromes."

What must remain speculative:
- Whether this mechanism operates in non-COVID ME/CFS (zero direct evidence)
- Whether shunt directly causes PEM (no PEM-shunt correlation studies)
- Whether the 6-month persistence in COVID studies represents permanent vascular remodeling or reversible dysfunction

What the evidence does NOT support:
- That shunt is the *sole* mechanism of exercise intolerance or PEM in ME/CFS
- That shunt explains all ME/CFS cases (likely a subset)

## 4. Clinical Relevance Assessment

- **Clinical relevance:** MEDIUM — mechanistic context for treatment decisions. Explains why some patients desaturate despite normal PFTs. Raises possibility that O2 therapy during exertion could help a specific subset. No direct ME/CFS clinical data.
- **Evidence type:** Long COVID population; mechanistic physiology; NO ME/CFS-specific studies
- **Patient subset applicability:** severity applicability unknown. Post-COVID ME/CFS/Long COVID patients with dyspnea and exertional desaturation most likely affected. Non-post-viral ME/CFS: unknown.

## 5. Integration Decision: **PARTIAL**

**Criteria met:** 
- ≥2 papers with certainty ≥ 0.40? YES (Farrow 0.62, Harbut 0.62, Hoel 0.65, Durand 0.60, Grist 0.55, Sandhu 0.55, Davis 0.55)
- Evidence is not uniformly null? YES
- BUT >50% of papers have certainty < 0.40? NO — only 1/11 (Espejo 0.25). Paper count: 3 at 0.60-0.70, 5 at 0.50-0.59, 2 at 0.40-0.49, 1 at <0.40 → 9% <0.40. **This does NOT meet the >50% threshold.**

**Re-evaluating against PROCEED:**
- ≥2 papers with certainty ≥ 0.40: YES (8 papers ≥ 0.40)
- Evidence is not uniformly null: YES
- ≤50% of papers have certainty < 0.40: YES (9% < 0.40)

**BUT re-evaluating against the more specific PROCEED criterion:** ≥1 paper with certainty ≥ 0.60 AND ≤50% < 0.40 — YES (Farrow 0.62, Harbut 0.62, Hoel 0.65).

**HOWEVER — zero papers are ME/CFS-specific.** All data is from Long COVID or general physiology. The integration decision guidelines state PROCEED requires evaluation of whether evidence supports the mechanism in the target condition. The indirect biochemical link is strong (tissue hypoxia already extensively documented in the paper's ME/CFS chapters), but the direct shunt measurement gap is real.

**Decision: PARTIAL** — despite meeting numerical PROCEED thresholds, the evidence is entirely non-ME/CFS (all Long COVID or general physiology). This is a cross-disease inference with strong mechanistic plausibility but zero direct disease confirmation. PARTIAL integration as #speculation and #open-question environments, with WEAK-EVIDENCE flag. Cap applies: no #hypothesis-box or #fhypothesis environments.

**Rationale for PARTIAL instead of PROCEED:** The evidence quality is strong within Long COVID but the gap between Long COVID and ME/CFS is a substantive inferential leap. Direct ME/CFS measurement is the missing piece. Integration as speculation with explicit caveat about the Long COVID→ME/CFS extrapolation is the honest approach.
