## 1. Novel Hypotheses

### H1. Mast cell as the cascade ignition node, not just an amplifier (certainty 0.50)

- **Mechanism:** In Septad patients, mast cell mediator release (tryptase, histamine, PGD2, heparin, chymase) acts upstream of: (a) endothelial NO/permeability changes driving POTS; (b) Cajal cell dysmotility driving GI burden; (c) collagen-matrix MMP activation worsening hypermobility tissue laxity; (d) microglial priming driving central pain and fatigue. The 75–91% response rate to MCAS therapy (Yao 2025) and the bidirectional amplification loop (Wirth/Löhn 2023) are signatures of an upstream node, not a parallel comorbidity.
- **Link:** Yao 2025; Wirth 2023; Rohrhofer 2025 (16.7% MCAS); Weinstock 2021 (LC=MCAS phenotype).
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In a randomized MCAS-first vs POTS-first vs pain-first sequential trial (n≥120 cascade-eligible patients), MCAS-first arm shows ≥2-component improvement at 8 wk in ≥60% vs ≤30% in either alternative arm. Failure to separate refutes the ignition-node hypothesis.

### H2. The "amplification ratchet" — irreversibility threshold (certainty 0.35, speculation)

- **Mechanism:** Each loop iteration of MCA → autonomic dysregulation → connective-tissue/barrier breakdown → mast-cell re-activation deposits long-lived priming signals (sensory neuron sprouting, mast-cell hyperplasia in gut/skin, persistent FcεRI upregulation). Beyond a critical loop count the cascade becomes self-sustaining without an external trigger. This explains why ME/CFS duration predicts MCAS treatment response negatively in clinical observation.
- **Link:** Wirth/Löhn 2023 bidirectional loop; Rohrhofer 2025 97.2% MCAS-after-ME/CFS pattern.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Disease duration is a stronger negative predictor of MCAS-treatment response (≥2-component cascade improvement) than baseline tryptase, MCAS severity, or POTS severity. Specifically: response rate drops by ≥15 percentage points per 5 years of duration.

### H3. Hypermobility as permissive substrate, not driver (certainty 0.55)

- **Mechanism:** 81% hypermobility prevalence (Eccles 2021) vs 15–18% strict hEDS suggests laxity is a permissive substrate that lowers thresholds for MCAS triggering (mechanically deformable connective tissue → repeated mast cell mechanostimulation → chronic degranulation), but is not sufficient. The contrast — most hypermobile people don't have ME/CFS — argues against laxity as a driver.
- **Link:** Eccles 2021; Vazquez 2022 (HaT in hEDS = general population); MCAS rates in hypermobile non-ME/CFS cohorts.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Among hypermobile ME/CFS patients, joint instability scores (Beighton dynamic, GJH+symptoms) predict MCAS severity ( tryptase, UMA score) more strongly than they predict fatigue or PEM severity. Effect size r≥0.30 for MCAS axis, r≤0.10 for core ME/CFS axis.

### H4. The "third trigger" requirement (certainty 0.30, speculation)

- **Mechanism:** MCAS+hypermobility alone produce a non-ME/CFS phenotype (e.g., chronic urticaria + joint pain). ME/CFS emerges only when a third event — viral infection, severe stressor, surgery, anesthesia — converts the substrate into the persistent fatigue/PEM state, possibly via vagal or HPA-axis reset.
- **Link:** Post-infectious ME/CFS literature; Long-COVID parallel (Weinstock 2021); the 97.2% MCAS-after-ME/CFS finding (Rohrhofer 2025) suggests the trigger that initiates ME/CFS often initiates MCAS.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In hypermobile MCAS patients without ME/CFS (a finable cohort), prospective 5-year follow-up shows ME/CFS incidence concentrated in those experiencing a defined "third trigger" (viral, severe stress, surgery) at HR ≥3 vs untriggered.

### H5. MCAS subtypes map to Septad cascade direction (certainty 0.40)

- **Mechanism:** Three MCAS phenotypes — (a) histamine-dominant (flushing, GI), (b) tryptase/PGD2-dominant (orthostatic, neuropsychiatric), (c) leukotriene-dominant (respiratory, fatigue) — predict which Septad arm activates first in a given patient. Mediator panel directs personalized first-line therapy (H1/H2 vs cromolyn vs montelukast).
- **Link:** Mediator-pattern heterogeneity in MCAS literature; differential receptor distribution across Septad target tissues.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Pre-treatment 24h urinary mediator ratios (N-methylhistamine vs PGD-M vs LTE4) predict which Septad component improves first under broad MCAS therapy (concordance ≥0.6 vs chance 0.33).

### H6. HNMT-mediated "histamine clearance failure" subtype (certainty 0.35, speculation)

- **Mechanism:** HNMT Thr105Ile reduces intracellular histamine catabolism by ~30%. In ME/CFS patients carrying this variant, even normal mast cell baseline release saturates degradation, producing functional MCAS phenotype with negative biochemical workup. Explains the 75–91% response rate "regardless of biochemical confirmation" (Yao 2025).
- **Link:** Yao 2025 response paradox; HNMT variant frequency 8–13% homozygous in Europeans; histamine clearance kinetics literature.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** HNMT Thr105Ile homozygotes are enriched among MCAS-therapy responders with normal tryptase/N-methylhistamine vs non-responders with same biochemistry (OR ≥2.5).

### H7. The cascade has a "lock-in latency" of 4–8 weeks (certainty 0.50)

- **Mechanism:** Mast cell membrane stabilization (cromolyn, ketotifen, H1/H2) requires 4–6 weeks for steady-state granule depletion and downstream barrier (gut, BBB, vascular) repair. The empirically observed 4–8 week confirmation window for ≥2-component cascade improvement reflects this kinetic, not placebo or regression to mean.
- **Link:** Pharmacokinetics of cromolyn membrane effects; gut barrier repair timelines (LPS reduction in 6–8 wk); operationalized criterion in integrated text.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Symptom trajectory in MCAS-first arm shows non-linear improvement with inflection at 4–6 wk, distinguishable from linear placebo trajectory by mixed-model (interaction p<0.05).

### H8. Septad as a single dysautonomic-mastocytic syndrome with five projections (certainty 0.45)

- **Mechanism:** Reframe: Septad isn't a comorbidity cluster but a single underlying "neuroimmune connective syndrome" (NICS) with five symptomatic projections (mast, autonomic, GI, joint, fatigue/PEM). Diagnostic criteria currently fragment one disorder into seven labels because each specialty sees its own slice.
- **Link:** Co-occurrence rates >>> chance; shared response to MCAS therapy; common embryologic origin (neural crest → mast cells, autonomic ganglia, parts of connective tissue).
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Latent class analysis on a deeply phenotyped Septad cohort (n≥500) yields a single dominant latent class explaining ≥40% of variance across all seven label scores, more than any 2-class or 3-class model adjusted for parameter count.

## 2. Research Directions

### R1. Sequential treatment RCT (cascade arm comparison)

- **Design:** 3-arm RCT, n=180 cascade-eligible patients (MCAS+POTS+pain). Arms: MCAS-first, POTS-first, pain-first 8-wk monotherapy then crossover. Primary: ≥2-component improvement at 8 wk. Tests H1.
- **Feasibility:** Moderate; requires multi-site MCAS-experienced clinics. ~$2M, 30 mo.
- **Falsifies:** Ignition-node hypothesis (H1).

### R2. Latent class analysis of Septad phenotypes

- **Design:** Cross-sectional deep phenotyping, n=500. Variables: tryptase, NMH, PGD-M, LTE4, Beighton, NASA lean, gastric emptying, HRV, fatigue/PEM scales, HNMT/TPSAB1/KIT genotype. LCA + factor analysis.
- **Feasibility:** High; existing biobank cohorts (UK Biobank ME/CFS, DecodeME) feasible.
- **Falsifies:** H8 (single-class structure) and H5 (mediator-direction mapping).

### R3. Prospective MCAS→ME/CFS conversion cohort

- **Design:** n=300 hypermobile-MCAS patients without ME/CFS, 5-yr follow-up, capture viral/stress/surgical events. ME/CFS incidence as outcome.
- **Feasibility:** Hard; long horizon. Could leverage allergy/immunology clinic registries.
- **Falsifies:** H4 (third-trigger).

### R4. HNMT genotype-stratified MCAS therapy trial

- **Design:** Pre-stratify by HNMT Thr105Ile homozygosity, treat all with H1+H2 8 wk, response rate by stratum.
- **Feasibility:** Moderate; ~600 genotyped, ~60 homozygotes needed.
- **Falsifies:** H6 (HNMT subtype).

### R5. Mediator panel as predictive biomarker

- **Design:** Pre-treatment 24h mediator panel + 8-wk response trajectory across 4 Septad components. Test predictive concordance.
- **Feasibility:** High; mediator panels available in reference labs.
- **Falsifies:** H5.

### R6. Live-cell connective tissue mast cell density

- **Design:** Skin/gut/joint capsule biopsies from Septad-positive ME/CFS vs hypermobile-only vs ME/CFS-only vs controls (n=15/group). Mast cell count, activation markers (CD117, FcεRI, tryptase IHC).
- **Feasibility:** Hard; biopsy ethics; but punch skin biopsy minimally invasive.
- **Falsifies:** Whether Septad ME/CFS is a tissue-mast-cell-hyperplasia disease vs functional release disease.

### R7. Tryptase-mediator decoupling in ME/CFS

- **Design:** Simultaneous serum tryptase + 24h urinary NMH/PGD-M/LTE4 + skin prick wheal kinetics in n=100 ME/CFS. Test whether the 16.7% formal MCAS rate underestimates because tryptase doesn't track tissue-bound activation in this cohort.
- **Feasibility:** High.
- **Falsifies:** Hypothesis that current criteria are insensitive in ME/CFS.

## 3. Drug / Medication Ideas

### D1. Cromolyn sodium (oral) — first-line trial standardization (certainty 0.60)

- **Mechanism:** Mast-cell membrane stabilizer; minimal systemic absorption; gut-targeted.
- **Link:** Yao 2025 response data; Long-COVID off-label use.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In standardized 8-wk trial at 200 mg QID, ≥2-component cascade improvement in ≥40% of MCAS-criteria-positive ME/CFS.
- **Risk:** Low. ✓

### D2. Ketotifen — H1 + mast stabilizer dual action (certainty 0.50)

- **Mechanism:** H1 antagonist + membrane stabilizer + mild antimuscarinic. Crosses BBB → may address central sensitization arm.
- **Link:** Pediatric MCAS literature; sleep-amplifying side effect potentially useful.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Ketotifen 1–4 mg HS produces sleep-quality improvement (PSQI delta ≥3) in MCAS-positive ME/CFS at higher rate than non-sedating H1 (cetirizine).
- **Risk:** Low; daytime sedation. ✓

### D3. Omalizumab (anti-IgE) — refractory MCAS subset (certainty 0.40)

- **Mechanism:** Reduces FcεRI surface expression and mast cell sensitivity. FDA-approved for chronic spontaneous urticaria.
- **Link:** Case series in MCAS; mechanism plausible for IgE-driven subsets.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In MCAS-positive ME/CFS refractory to H1+H2+cromolyn, omalizumab 300 mg q4wk produces ≥2-component cascade improvement in ≥30% at 16 wk.
- **Risk:** Moderate; black-box anaphylaxis. ⚠️ (paradoxical in MCAS)

### D4. Low-dose naltrexone (LDN) — Septad-specific framing (certainty 0.40)

- **Mechanism:** TLR4 antagonism on microglia and mast cells; reduces mast-cell degranulation in vitro at micromolar; central anti-inflammatory.
- **Link:** Existing ME/CFS LDN literature; mast cell TLR4 expression.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** LDN response rate is higher in MCAS-positive ME/CFS subset than MCAS-negative ME/CFS subset (≥15 percentage point separation).
- **Risk:** Low. ✓

### D5. Ivabradine — POTS arm without sympatholysis (certainty 0.55)

- **Mechanism:** Selective If-channel SAN inhibition; lowers HR without lowering BP, no central effects.
- **Link:** Several POTS RCTs supportive.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In Septad-cascade ME/CFS with POTS, ivabradine outperforms beta-blocker on PEM frequency (because it doesn't blunt exercise capacity centrally).
- **Risk:** Low. Belgian availability: ✓ (Procoralan/generic).

### D6. Pyridostigmine — vagal-tone restoration angle (certainty 0.40)

- **Mechanism:** Acetylcholinesterase inhibitor; enhances cholinergic tone → may dampen mast cell activation (α7nAChR on mast cells inhibits release) and improve gut motility.
- **Link:** POTS adjunct evidence; α7nAChR-mast cell axis.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Pyridostigmine 30–60 mg TID improves both POTS HR response and GI dysmotility scores (Steinsvik-style ultrasound endpoints) in Septad ME/CFS.
- **Risk:** Low–moderate; cholinergic GI side effects. ✓

### D7. Rupatadine — dual H1 + PAF antagonism (certainty 0.45)

- **Mechanism:** H1 + platelet-activating factor antagonist; PAF is a major non-histamine mast cell mediator implicated in vascular leak and orthostatic intolerance.
- **Link:** Spanish MCAS practice; growing PAF literature.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Rupatadine 10–20 mg/day outperforms cetirizine on orthostatic component specifically (Δ NASA lean tolerance ≥3 min).
- **Risk:** Low. Belgian availability: ✓ (Rupafin).

### D8. Famotidine — preferred H2 in ME/CFS (certainty 0.50)

- **Mechanism:** H2 antagonist with minimal CYP interaction (vs cimetidine); emerging Long-COVID interest with putative additional anti-inflammatory action.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Famotidine 40 mg BID added to H1 produces ≥10pp incremental cascade improvement over H1 alone.
- **Risk:** Low. ✓

### D9. Mestinon + midodrine combination — POTS arm under MCAS umbrella (certainty 0.45)

- Standard POTS combination; flagged here as **second-line within the MCAS-first protocol** rather than first-line.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Sequential MCAS→POTS arm outperforms POTS-only arm on PEM endpoint at 16 wk.

### D10. JAK inhibitors (long-shot) — refractory inflammatory arm (certainty 0.20, speculation)

- **Mechanism:** JAK1/2 signaling downstream of multiple mast-cell-derived cytokines (IL-6, IL-13). Tofacitinib used in refractory chronic urticaria.
- **Link:** Emerging case reports.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In severe refractory Septad ME/CFS, 12-wk tofacitinib produces ≥30% cascade response.
- **Risk:** High (immunosuppression, thrombosis). ⚠️ Research-stage only.

### D11. BTK inhibitors — hyperactivated mast cell variant (certainty 0.20, speculation)

- **Mechanism:** Bruton's tyrosine kinase central to FcεRI signaling. Remibrutinib (LOU064) phase III in chronic urticaria 2025.
- **Link:** Emerging trial data outside ME/CFS.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** BTK inhibitor produces tryptase decline and cascade improvement in MCAS-positive ME/CFS within 4 wk.
- **Risk:** Moderate; bleeding, infection. 🔬

### D12. Gepants (CGRP antagonists) — pain/migraine arm (certainty 0.30, speculation)

- **Mechanism:** CGRP released by mast cells and trigeminal afferents; gepants block receptor.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Atogepant reduces both migraine frequency and PEM intensity in Septad ME/CFS with migraine.
- **Risk:** Low–moderate. ✓

## 4. Supplement / Nutraceutical Ideas

### S1. Quercetin — natural mast-cell stabilizer (certainty 0.45)

- **Mechanism:** Inhibits PLA2, calcium influx, histamine release in vitro; bioavailability limited (use phytosome form).
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Quercetin phytosome 500 mg BID adjunct to H1 produces measurable urinary NMH reduction (≥15%) at 6 wk.
- **Risk:** Low. ✓

### S2. Luteolin — BBB-crossing flavonoid (certainty 0.40)

- **Mechanism:** Inhibits brain mast cell activation; central anti-neuroinflammatory; Theoharides group data.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Luteolin formulation produces specific cognitive arm improvement (PASAT, Stroop) over peripheral-only quercetin.
- **Risk:** Low. ✓

### S3. Vitamin C — mast cell mediator clearance (certainty 0.35, speculation)

- **Mechanism:** Cofactor for diamine oxidase; increases histamine catabolism; antioxidant.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** 1–2 g/day reduces 24h NMH by ≥10% in Septad ME/CFS.
- **Risk:** Low. ✓

### S4. DAO (diamine oxidase) supplementation pre-meal (certainty 0.30, speculation)

- **Mechanism:** Exogenous histamine catabolism in gut lumen; relevant for histamine-rich meal triggers.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Pre-meal DAO reduces post-prandial flushing/abdominal pain/tachycardia events by ≥30% in Septad ME/CFS with documented food triggers.
- **Risk:** Low. ✓

### S5. Magnesium glycinate — mast cell membrane + autonomic (certainty 0.40)

- **Mechanism:** Mg²⁺ stabilizes mast cell membrane; smooth muscle relaxation; vagal tone.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Mg glycinate 400 mg/day improves heart rate variability (RMSSD ≥10% increase) at 6 wk.
- **Risk:** Low. ✓

### S6. PEA (palmitoylethanolamide) — endocannabinoid-like mast modulator (certainty 0.40)

- **Mechanism:** Activates PPAR-α; downregulates mast cell activation; established anti-neuroinflammatory.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** PEA 600 mg BID reduces pain VAS by ≥2 points in Septad ME/CFS with chronic pain.
- **Risk:** Low. ✓ Belgian availability: ✓ (Normast).

### S7. Low-histamine diet trial (formal, time-limited) (certainty 0.40)

- **Mechanism:** Reduces exogenous histamine load on saturated catabolic pathway.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** 4-wk strict low-histamine elimination → reintroduction protocol identifies ≥1 reactive food in ≥50% of Septad ME/CFS, with consistent symptom score change ≥20%.
- **Risk:** Low if time-limited; moderate if chronic (nutritional deficit, eating-disorder risk). ⚠️ supervised.

### S8. Berberine + DAO combo — gut-mast axis (certainty 0.25, speculation)

- **Mechanism:** Berberine reduces gut bacterial histamine production; DAO catabolizes residual.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Combination reduces breath-test markers of histamine-producing dysbiosis.
- **Risk:** Low–moderate. ✓

### S9. NAC + glycine — mast cell GSH support (certainty 0.30, speculation)

- **Mechanism:** Mast cell oxidative stress potentiates degranulation; GSH buffer reduces it.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** NAC 600 mg BID + glycine 3 g HS reduces self-report flare frequency by ≥25% at 8 wk.
- **Risk:** Low. ✓

### S10. Vitamin D optimization — mast cell modulation (certainty 0.40)

- **Mechanism:** VDR expression on mast cells; deficiency associated with hyperreactivity.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** In deficient (<30 ng/mL) Septad patients, replacement to 50–80 ng/mL produces measurable mediator reduction at 12 wk.
- **Risk:** Low if monitored. ✓

## 5. Non-Pharmacological Interventions

### N1. Vagal nerve stimulation (transcutaneous auricular tVNS) (certainty 0.45)

- **Mechanism:** α7nAChR-mediated mast cell inhibition (cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway); autonomic rebalancing.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** 6-wk tVNS protocol reduces both HRV indices and mast mediator markers in Septad ME/CFS, with effect absent in MCAS-negative ME/CFS.
- **Risk:** Very low. ✓

### N2. Heat avoidance + active cooling protocol (certainty 0.55)

- **Mechanism:** Heat is a major mast cell trigger; cooling vests, ambient control measurably reduce flare burden.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Strict ≤21 °C ambient + cooling vest during PEM-prone activity reduces 4-wk flare count by ≥30%.
- **Risk:** None. ✓ Practical.

### N3. Mast cell trigger diary + n-of-1 elimination (certainty 0.50)

- **Mechanism:** Personalized identification of dominant trigger class (heat/food/chemical/mechanical/emotional).
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Structured diary identifies ≥2 reproducible triggers in ≥70% of Septad patients within 8 wk.
- **Risk:** None. ✓

### N4. Controlled compression garments — POTS+joint dual benefit (certainty 0.55)

- **Mechanism:** Venous return for orthostatic; proprioceptive feedback for hypermobile joints; reduces mechanical mast triggering.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Class 2 (20–30 mmHg) waist-high garments produce dual improvement on NASA lean and Beighton-related pain scores.
- **Risk:** Low. ✓ Belgian INAMI may reimburse with POTS diagnosis.

### N5. Pacing tied to mast-cell flare cycle (certainty 0.40)

- **Mechanism:** Mast cell activation peaks 2–6 h post-trigger; pacing prescriptions should anticipate refractory windows.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Mast-flare-aware pacing reduces PEM frequency more than time-based pacing alone (≥20% delta).
- **Risk:** None. ✓

### N6. Surgical/dental anesthesia protocol (certainty 0.55)

- **Mechanism:** Histamine-releasing anesthetic agents (atracurium, morphine, vancomycin) trigger crashes; pre-medication and agent substitution prevents.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Pre-procedure H1+H2+steroid + non-histamine-releasing agents reduces post-procedure flare incidence from ~50% to ≤15%.
- **Risk:** None — risk-reducing. ✓ High actionability.

### N7. Mold/VOC exposure reduction (certainty 0.35, speculation)

- **Mechanism:** Mycotoxins and VOCs documented mast cell triggers.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Among Septad ME/CFS in indoor-exposure-positive housing, remediation produces ≥1-component cascade improvement at 12 wk in ≥40%.
- **Risk:** None medically; cost. ✓

### N8. Bowen / gentle myofascial work — hypermobile-safe (certainty 0.30, speculation)

- **Mechanism:** Avoids high-velocity manipulation that triggers mechanical mast activation; gentle proprioceptive input.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Bowen produces lower flare-induction rate than chiropractic manipulation in matched Septad patients.
- **Risk:** Low. ✓

### N9. CO₂ rebreathing / capnography-guided breathing (certainty 0.40)

- **Mechanism:** Hypocapnia common in POTS/ME/CFS amplifies cerebral hypoperfusion; restoring end-tidal CO₂ via slow breathing improves orthostatic tolerance.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Capnography-targeted breathing (ETCO₂ 38–42) improves orthostatic standing tolerance by ≥3 min.
- **Risk:** None. ✓

### N10. Salt/fluid loading individualized to MCAS sensitivity (certainty 0.45)

- **Mechanism:** Standard POTS prescription, but high-histamine fluids (bone broth) may worsen MCAS arm; sodium load needs to be MCAS-compatible.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** Sodium chloride tablets + filtered water outperform bone broth on net Septad burden in MCAS-positive subset.
- **Risk:** Low. ✓

## 6. Mathematical / Formal Model Extensions

### M1. Septad as 7-node directed graph with weighted edges (certainty 0.55)

- **Formalism:** Nodes = {MCA, POTS, hEDS/HSD, GI dysmotility, pain/FM, fatigue/PEM, central sensitization}. Directed edges weighted by empirical conditional response rates. Adjacency matrix A; cascade dynamics x_{t+1} = f(Ax_t).
- **Prediction:** Largest eigenvector of A points toward MCA node — i.e., MCA has highest centrality. Empirically testable from response-correlation data.
- **Use:** Identifies optimal intervention node analytically.

### M2. Ratchet model with hysteresis (certainty 0.35)

- **Formalism:** Each Septad component as bistable variable (low/high activation) with state-dependent transition rates. Treatment lowers activation rate but does not symmetrically raise deactivation rate → hysteresis loop. Captures "irreversibility" intuition (H2).
- **Prediction:** Treatment-response landscape exhibits multiple stable points; some patients require prolonged therapy to cross barrier.

### M3. Bayesian network for diagnostic triage (certainty 0.55)

- **Formalism:** DAG with prior prevalences from integrated section (81/15/17/27%) as priors, conditional probabilities from response data. Patient-specific posterior P(MCAS-driven cascade | observed symptoms) drives MCAS-first decision.
- **Prediction:** Bayes-net-driven triage outperforms current clinical heuristic on cascade-improvement endpoint by ≥10pp.

### M4. Stochastic differential equation for mast-cell mediator dynamics (certainty 0.30, speculation)

- **Formalism:** dH/dt = -k_clear H + sum(trigger pulses) - therapy(t). Patient parameters k_clear (HNMT-genotype-dependent), trigger frequency, mast hyperplasia (fixed-effect amplitude). Predicts inter-individual response variance.
- **Prediction:** SDE-fitted k_clear correlates with HNMT genotype.

### M5. Latent class extension (certainty 0.50)

- **Formalism:** Standard LCA on phenotype vectors; compare 1-class (NICS hypothesis H8) vs k-class fit.
- **Prediction:** See R2.

### M6. Coupled oscillator model — flare cycles (certainty 0.25, speculation)

- **Formalism:** Mast cell activation, autonomic sympathetic, vagal tone modeled as Kuramoto-style coupled oscillators. Phase-locking under stress predicts flare clusters.
- **Prediction:** Continuous monitoring (Garmin-style HRV + symptom log) shows phase-locked flare clusters with measurable coupling constant K.

### M7. Information-theoretic biomarker selection (certainty 0.40)

- **Formalism:** Mutual information between candidate biomarkers (tryptase, NMH, PGD-M, LTE4, HRV, Beighton) and treatment-response outcome. Select minimal panel maximizing I.
- **Prediction:** A 4-variable panel achieves ≥80% of full-panel predictive AUC.

## 7. Cross-Disease Bridges

### X1. Long-COVID as "natural experiment" Septad induction (certainty 0.60)

- **Bridge:** Weinstock 2021 — LC symptom profile = pre-treatment MCAS. LC offers a time-zero post-trigger Septad cohort.
- **Prediction:** Prospective LC patients meeting Septad criteria respond to MCAS-first therapy at higher rate than chronic ME/CFS patients matched for severity (less ratchet lock-in, H2).

### X2. Mastocytosis (systemic) — ME/CFS-like fatigue arm (certainty 0.40)

- **Bridge:** Systemic mastocytosis patients report disabling fatigue separable from skin/GI burden. The fatigue arm responds to KIT-targeting (avapritinib).
- **Prediction:** In ME/CFS subset with KIT D816V, avapritinib produces fatigue-specific improvement.

### X3. Chronic spontaneous urticaria (CSU) — omalizumab template (certainty 0.40)

- **Bridge:** CSU is mast-cell-driven; omalizumab effective in ~70%. Septad ME/CFS may inherit subset of this responsiveness.
- **Prediction:** See D3.

### X4. POTS without ME/CFS — Septad-without-fatigue arm (certainty 0.45)

- **Bridge:** Some POTS patients fully recover with treatment; ME/CFS doesn't. The differentiator may be the "third trigger" (H4) and the ratchet (H2).
- **Prediction:** POTS-only patients with hypermobility+MCAS markers but no infection history recover at higher rate than POTS+ME/CFS matched cohort.

### X5. Fibromyalgia — small-fiber neuropathy + mast bridge (certainty 0.50)

- **Bridge:** SFN documented in both FM and ME/CFS; mast cells closely apposed to small fibers. Skin biopsy mast-cell-fiber colocalization could be a unifying biomarker.
- **Prediction:** Skin biopsy IENFD + mast-cell-to-fiber distance correlates with Septad burden across both diagnoses.

### X6. IBS-D / functional dyspepsia — gut Septad arm (certainty 0.50)

- **Bridge:** Mast-cell-targeted therapy (cromolyn, ebastine) effective in IBS-D subsets. Steinsvik 2023 ultrasound dysmotility may reflect same biology.
- **Prediction:** Septad ME/CFS with prominent GI arm responds at IBS-mast-positive subset response rate.

### X7. Hypermobile dancers / athletes without ME/CFS (certainty 0.45)

- **Bridge:** Hypermobility ≠ illness; many elite performers thrive. Resilience markers (vagal tone, baseline mast cell density, training-induced barrier strengthening) identify protective phenotype.
- **Prediction:** Cross-sectional comparison reveals 1–2 protective biomarkers (e.g., higher RMSSD, lower baseline tryptase, higher T-reg) absent in Septad ME/CFS.

### X8. Kounis syndrome — acute mast-coronary bridge (certainty 0.30, speculation)

- **Bridge:** Mast cells trigger coronary spasm in Kounis. Cardiac chest pain in Septad ME/CFS may share mechanism at sub-clinical level.
- **Prediction:** Septad ME/CFS with chest pain has higher rate of microvascular dysfunction on PET/CMR.

### X9. Histamine intolerance literature (DAO deficiency) (certainty 0.45)

- **Bridge:** DAO-deficiency phenotype (post-prandial flushing, headache, GI) overlaps with MCAS. Histamine intolerance may be undertreated overlapping diagnosis in Septad cohort.
- **Prediction:** Pre-meal DAO improves post-prandial component of Septad cascade specifically (S4).

### X10. ARFID/eating-disorder boundary (certainty 0.30, speculation)

- **Bridge:** Restrictive eating in MCAS-driven food-trigger avoidance can spiral into ARFID. Recognition prevents misdiagnosis.
- **Prediction:** Structured low-histamine reintroduction protocol reduces ARFID-trajectory rate in Septad cohort vs unstructured avoidance.

## 8. Diagnostic / Biomarker Ideas
