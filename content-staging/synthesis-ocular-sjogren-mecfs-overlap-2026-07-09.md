**Indirect biochemical/systemic links (critical for this topic):** STRONG.
- Tear IL-6 / IL-17 / MMP-9 / BAFF track systemic SjS activity (`wu2024sjogrendryeyediagnostics`); same cytokine family elevated in ME/CFS serum → ocular surface may mirror the systemic inflammatory milieu.
- Corneal subbasal nerves are unmyelinated C-fibres; CCM-quantified CNFD tracks SFN in SjS (`luzu2022cornealinnervation`, `wang2025ivcmsjogren`), FM (`verges2025fibrodryeye`, Kubat, Sommer), long COVID (Cañadas, Azcue). CCM is **already used in ME/CFS SFN work** (Azcue group).
- Lacrimal functional unit is parasympathetically innervated (CN VII) → ME/CFS dysautonomia plausibly disrupts it, contributing to sicca downstream of autonomic pathology.
- ≥3 indirect papers ≥0.40 intersecting documented ME/CFS mechanisms (SFN, cytokine elevation, dysautonomia).

## 2. Internal Contradiction Detection

No fundamental contradictions among the 6 papers. All converge: tear proteomics and CCM detect ocular-surface neuro-immune pathology in SjS and FM. The tear-proteomics reviews (George, Wu) and the LC-MRM discovery study (Lépine) are consistent. CCM studies (Luzu, Wang, Vergés) are consistent in direction (reduced/altered corneal nerves in autoimmune/fatigue dry eye). No weighting or open-question framing needed on contradiction grounds.

One epistemic caveat (not a contradiction): no direct ME/CFS ocular-surface study exists — every ME/CFS-specific claim is by *analogy* from SjS/FM/long COVID. This bounds all new content to speculative/inferential framing.

## 3. Evidence Summary

- **Establishes:** the ocular surface (tear film + corneal subbasal nerve plexus) is a validated, non-invasive readout of neuro-immune and small-fibre pathology in Sjögren's and fibromyalgia. The measurement toolchain (LC-MRM tear proteomics; corneal confocal microscopy) is mature and already applied to ME/CFS-adjacent cohorts.
- **Strongest supportable claim:** CCM-detected corneal small-fibre loss is a shared, cross-disease readout spanning SjS, FM, long COVID, and (per Azcue) ME/CFS — making the corneal nerve plexus a candidate common SFN observable.
- **Must remain speculative:** that the ME/CFS *tear proteome* mirrors the SjS tear signature; that ocular-surface testing stratifies ME/CFS subgroups. No direct ME/CFS tear data exist.
- **Not supported / not found:** no evidence that ocular-surface findings distinguish ME/CFS from its mimics; no null data (a gap, possibly publication bias).
- **Primary integration targets:** ch14d (cross-disease — extend existing SjS block with ocular-surface subsection); ch29 biomarker-research (tear proteomics + CCM as accessible window); hypothesis-registry; appendix-h.

## 4. Clinical Relevance

- Direct patient-care implications: LOW-to-MEDIUM. No actionable ME/CFS ocular test exists today; the value is a *research* diagnostic-window proposal. Corneal confocal microscopy is non-invasive and feasible even for severe/bedbound patients (chair-side, ~minutes) — a genuine accessibility advantage if validated.
- Evidence type: cross-disease / mechanistic-inference for ME/CFS; direct only in SjS/FM.
- Patient subset applicability: **all severities** in principle (CCM and tear collection are low-burden); of particular value for **severe/very-severe** where invasive skin-biopsy SFN testing is impractical. Severity-specific validation data: **unknown**.
- **Summary: Clinical relevance = LOW–MEDIUM (research-diagnostic window; not yet actionable). Subset: all (special value severe/very-severe); validation unknown.**
