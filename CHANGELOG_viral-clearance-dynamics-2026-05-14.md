## Motivated By

This integration was motivated by the negative PoCoVIT methylprednisolone trial in Long COVID, which revealed critical gaps in understanding viral clearance dynamics. The trial's failure, combined with the clinical similarities between Long COVID and ME/CFS, suggested that:

1. Different post-viral syndromes may represent different stages or phenotypes of the same underlying process
2. Viral clearance mechanisms may distinguish between recovery and chronicity
3. Early intervention during a critical window may prevent ME/CFS development
4. Immune phenotype differences (activated vs exhausted) may explain divergent treatment responses

Key motivating sources include:
- Adam et al. (2024) PoCoVIT methylprednisolone trial @Adam2024PoCoVIT
- Eaton-Fitch et al. (2024) ME/CFS vs Long COVID immune phenotype comparison
- Kol et al. (2026) FIPV lymphocyte persistence model (comparative virology)
- Dubbo cohort post-infectious fatigue studies (pathogen-agnostic failure rates)
