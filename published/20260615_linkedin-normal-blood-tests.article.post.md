Ferritin 22. Reference range 10-200. "Normal."

Your mitochondria need ferritin above 50 — arguably 100 — before their iron-dependent enzymes run at capacity. The reference range was set to catch anaemia, not to detect cellular energy starvation.

This pattern repeats across every micronutrient ME/CFS patients depend on:

Iron: functional depletion begins 15 years before the lab flags it. Randomised trials show fatigue improves with IV iron at ferritin levels the lab calls normal.

Magnesium: serum levels reflect less than 1% of body stores. ATP only works when bound to magnesium, and no standard test measures Mg-ATP.

B12: neurological damage starts at "normal" serum levels. Methylmalonic acid catches the problem. It's rarely ordered.

Vitamin D: "sufficient" at 30 ng/mL for bone. Immune optimisation needs 50-60. The lab says fine. The immune system disagrees.

For a patient already at the edge of energy sufficiency, four "normal" results each representing a sub-optimal input to the same energy production system is not fine. It's four treatable problems being ignored because no single one crossed an arbitrary threshold.

The tests that catch what standard panels miss cost a few hundred euros and identify treatable contributions to fatigue. They should be routine.
