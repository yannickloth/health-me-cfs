Cyanide kills by binding Complex IV of the electron transport chain — the final step of ATP production. Hydrogen sulfide binds the same site. Same enzyme. Same mechanism.

The difference is dose. Cyanide arrives acutely and kills. H₂S, produced chronically by gut bacteria, arrives continuously and impairs. Not enough to kill cells. Enough to reduce ATP output by 10-30% — silently, for months, without a measurable biomarker in standard panels.

The source: sulfate-reducing bacteria (Desulfovibrio, Bilophila) that overgrow when gut motility is impaired — which it is in ME/CFS, because the vagus nerve that controls the gut cleaning cycle is dysfunctional.

The cruelest part: these bacteria are fed by "healthy" foods and supplements. Garlic, broccoli, cauliflower, eggs. NAC, alpha-lipoic acid, MSM. A patient following an anti-inflammatory diet and taking mitochondrial support supplements may be feeding the bacteria that are poisoning the mitochondria they're trying to support.

Clinical signs: rotten-egg gas, sulfur food intolerance, diarrhoea, fatigue that worsens with "healthy eating."

Treatment exists: dietary sulfur restriction, bismuth subsalicylate, targeted probiotics, rifaximin. If Complex IV is chronically inhibited by gut-derived H₂S, no amount of upstream cofactor support will restore ATP. You have to stop the poison before you can fix the engine.
