Peeling potatoes shouldn't leave someone in pain for three days.

But for people with ME/CFS, it can — and the reason isn't that they're weak, deconditioned, or exaggerating. It's that their muscles are producing a chemical environment that activates pain receptors at rest-level activity, their nervous system is amplifying every signal that arrives, and their immune system treats mild movement the way a healthy body treats significant physical stress.

This is not fatigue. This is nociceptor activation, microvascular ischemia, mast cell degranulation, and a centrally sensitized nervous system — all running simultaneously, in a body that's already energy-constrained.
