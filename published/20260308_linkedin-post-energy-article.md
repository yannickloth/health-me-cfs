New article: What happens in the body when energy production fails in ME/CFS?

Your cells produce roughly 50 kg of ATP every day. In ME/CFS, that process breaks down — not like a power outage, but like a power grid running at half capacity where the equipment degrades every time you push it.

This article walks through the biology and math behind ME/CFS energy failure:

- How the electron transport chain's nonlinear threshold creates a "cliff effect" where moderate mitochondrial damage causes disproportionate ATP loss
- Why damaged mitochondria can't be cleaned up (the cleanup process itself requires the energy the damaged machinery fails to produce)
- The mechanism behind post-exertional malaise — and why the crash comes 12-72 hours later
- Why mathematical modeling predicts that single treatments are unlikely to work

Every quantitative claim is traced back to its equation with step-by-step arithmetic anyone can follow — no math background needed.

Based on the formal ODE models from our open-access ME/CFS documentation (CC-BY 4.0): https://zenodo.org/records/18905326
