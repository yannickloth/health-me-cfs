Always cold, even under two blankets. Or suddenly drenched in sweat — then cold again minutes later. Or fingers turning white in a mildly chilly room. Or completely unable to tolerate a warm bath.

Temperature dysregulation is one of the most common and least explained symptoms of ME/CFS. And once you understand why it happens, a whole cluster of other "weird" symptoms start to make sense too.

I wrote a detailed article explaining the biology:

→ Why the autonomic nervous system loses control of thermoregulation — and the autoantibodies involved
→ Why heat generation costs energy that isn't there
→ Why blood doesn't reach the extremities reliably
→ Why heat is also a problem — through the same broken system, failing in the opposite direction
→ And why these same failures explain breathlessness with normal oxygen levels, vision that varies by day, cognitive symptoms that worsen when standing, extreme light and sound sensitivity, and the "hypoglycemia" that isn't hypoglycemia

These aren't unrelated symptoms. They are a coherent physiological pattern — autonomic dysfunction, mitochondrial energy deficits, reduced blood volume, and cerebral hypoperfusion — producing consequences across every system the autonomic nervous system touches.
