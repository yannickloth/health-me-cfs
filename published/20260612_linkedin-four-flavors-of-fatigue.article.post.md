"I'm tired" means four biologically unrelated things. Medicine hears one word and reaches for one differential.

Peripheral fatigue: muscles can't generate force. Measurable with a dynamometer. Resolves with rest.

Fatigability (PEM): systemic collapse 24-72 hours AFTER exertion. The defining feature of ME/CFS. Does NOT resolve with rest. Worsened by exercise.

Sleepiness: propensity to fall asleep. Responds to stimulants and sleep.

Amotivation: body works, brain won't issue the command. Dopaminergic. Depression, ADHD.

The treatments are incompatible. Exercise helps amotivation and harms fatigability. Stimulants help amotivation and mask fatigability. Sleep resolves sleepiness and does nothing for PEM.

When a clinician prescribes graded exercise to a patient with fatigability because their assessment tool measured "fatigue" on a 0-10 scale, the tool failed — not the patient.

Four questions separate them: Does your body fail under load? Do you crash days after activity? Do you fall asleep involuntarily? Can your body perform but you can't make yourself start?

Five minutes. Four questions. Completely different diagnoses, treatments, and prognoses.
