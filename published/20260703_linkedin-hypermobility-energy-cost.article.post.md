Her doctors called her "flexible." Nobody told her that her collagen doesn't hold her joints together properly — and that this costs her body three times the energy for every movement a normally-jointed person does without thinking.

In hEDS and hypermobility spectrum disorder, ligaments are too elastic to stabilise joints. Muscles compensate continuously. Standing still isn't effortless — it requires active contraction to prevent subluxation. Sitting in a chair costs more ATP than it should. The proprioceptive system works overtime processing noisy position data from unstable joints. The energy tax is invisible and permanent.

Then the cascade:
- Vascular laxity → blood pooling → POTS → compensatory tachycardia (50% more cardiac work, all day)
- Autonomic dysfunction → impaired gut motility → SIBO → malabsorption
- Defective connective tissue → abnormal mast cell signalling → MCAS → recurrent immune flares
- Chronic pain → sleep disruption → impaired restoration

Each step has an energy cost. The costs are additive. A patient paying all of them is running a permanent energy deficit before getting out of bed.

The cluster — hEDS + POTS + MCAS + ME/CFS — isn't coincidence. It's one substrate. Defective collagen creates a body that was never structurally equipped for normal energy efficiency. The buffer between managing and failing was always thinner. The cliff was always closer.

The Beighton score takes two minutes. The question takes ten seconds. The pattern recognition changes everything.
